telegram that refuses to be brief

Date: 1942-09-01
Recipient: Mother
Location: Desert Training Center, California
Year: 1942
Themes: family, training, heat, humor, duty

dear mother STOP

the desert is a yellow drum and we are the sticks STOP dust climbs into our teeth and learns our names STOP the lizards salute with eyelids because hands would be foolish here STOP

we run until the sun forgets us then keep running for the moon STOP canteens knock our ribs like polite neighbors STOP i think of your kitchen where water comes with a simple turn like a hymn you hum without noticing STOP

sergeant says keep your head down i say yes sir and keep my heart up where you can see it from the porch STOP my rifle sleeps beside me like a thin stubborn dog STOP i oil it the way you butter pans with love and necessity STOP

there is a rumor of overseas like a train you hear before you believe in tracks STOP i am not brave i am busy which is the same uniform from far away STOP tell mrs halpern the tomatoes would have won the fair this year STOP

i am fine do not send cookies i am already a bakery of heat STOP send postcards of rain STOP

kiss the little radio for me when it plays our song STOP tell it we are learning to walk in time with bigger music STOP

your son STOP (who will come home as safely as a period) STOP
