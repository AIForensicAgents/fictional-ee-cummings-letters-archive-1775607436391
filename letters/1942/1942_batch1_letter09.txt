code garden

Date: 1942-10-28
Recipient: E.
Location: Bletchley Park, Buckinghamshire
Year: 1942
Themes: secrecy, codebreaking, duty, nature, connection

E,

we plant alphabets and harvest silence. the Hut smells of pencil shavings and tea that has decided to be brave. rotors mutter to themselves like difficult bees. a page of nonsense sits down beside me and gradually becomes a chair. i sit. it creaks. we make a kind of music.

i bike in through a morning that has been lightly ironed by fog. the gatekeeper nods as if we share a conspiracy with the hedges. small things are loyal: the squeak of my chain, the biscuit i have hidden, the fountain pen that remembers rain. we do not say what we do. instead we talk about weather as if it were a dear aunt.

your last letter brought a leaf pressed flat as a coin. i keep it near the switchboard. when the room fills with wires like a field of very thin snakes, i touch the leaf and for an instant the forest is fluent again. then the lamps blink their Morse and the room forgets trees.

at lunch we tell jokes that make no sound. sometimes i imagine the men at sea, the convoy you once described as a procession of small moons. i work quickly then. i undo knots tied by hands i will never meet. i like to think the ocean hears me untying.

if we are ever allowed to speak, i will take you to the pond and tell you everything except the names of angels. until then, keep the leaf. i will keep its shadow.

yours in the arithmetic of mercy,
Margaret
