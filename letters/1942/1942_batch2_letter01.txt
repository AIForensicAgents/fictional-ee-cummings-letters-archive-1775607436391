small moon in a brownout envelope

Date: 1942-03-05
Recipient: M.
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1942
Themes: love, wartime, blackout, rationing, longing

dear m,

tonight the city pulls a dark sweater over its bright shoulders and pretends it is asleep. the harbor swallows its mirrors. i write by a lamp whose bulb wears a paper hat, and every word tiptoes because the wardens have asked the windows to keep secrets.

i have learned the arithmetic of sugar: one spoon is a sunday, none is a kiss saved for later. so i sweeten this letter with breath instead, and stir courage in the cup, and it is enough (it must be).

the typewriter clicks like tiny boots across a parade somewhere far and not-far. when it rests, the quiet grows fur and curls on my lap. i think of your hands, their map of small honest scars, and how they will touch the map of places you have yet to go.

i walked past the Common, where winter keeps one last white hand on the grass. a boy flew a paper airplane with his eyes shut, as if faith were a breeze, as if the world would catch what we launch from it. i wanted to tell him: yes.

i have your last postcard folded to the size of a wish. each time i open it, the sky folds out too. the stamp tastes like a promise. i will kiss you where your name begins and ends (which is everywhere).

sleep under the careful moon. i will hang my heartbeat on the doorknob so you can find our room in the dark.

yours without ration,

l.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
