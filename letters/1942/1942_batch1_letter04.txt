snowletter to a red scarf

Date: 1942-02-18
Recipient: Tanya
Location: Leningrad
Year: 1942
Themes: siege, endurance, love, winter, hunger

tanya,

snow arrives with its quiet grammar and edits the streets into commas. i queue with my neighbors and the ration card waltzes slowly in my mitten. a woman ahead of me hums an aria that used to be bread. the ice on the window reads us, and sometimes looks away.

your scarf waits on its nail like a faithful flame. i imagine it walking about the room, searching for the neck that taught it warmth. when i boil water it remembers rivers. i pour some for the geranium, which refuses to learn how to surrender. i tell it stories of may; it lifts one leaf like a tiny hand.

you at the factory, you among sparks. your voice in my ear telling the day not to be cruel, and for a moment the day listens. i mend my gloves with thread the color of cranberry jam, and think of your laugh falling down the stairs, never breaking.

the radio is a small square door that opens on storms and bravery. sometimes it coughs, sometimes it sighs. i listen anyway. i keep your last postcard in the sugar tin, the one with the crooked stamp. i take it out and the room grows another heartbeat.

come home when the machines have learned to sing without you. bring your fierce hands. i will boil potatoes like a love song and we will wear the scarf together, as if our necks were a single promise.

i am waiting inside the whiteness,
Masha

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
