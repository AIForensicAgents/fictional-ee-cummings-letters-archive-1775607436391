notes from a theater dark as a hat

Date: 1942-11-22
Recipient: Miss Ada R.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1942
Themes: art, blackout, performance, war bonds, friendship

dear ada,

the stage is a quiet street where footsteps rehearse being footsteps. we have painted the flats the color of midnight, so when the wardens knock we can simply turn the world sideways and call it scenery.

last night the pianist wore mittens between songs and the audience applauded warmer as if hands could make weather. i read my new piece (a clock falls in love with a siren; they never meet except in the ear) and a sailor in the third row cried politely into his sleeve. perhaps we are not entertainment but bandages with glitter.

the manager says sell bonds with every encore; i put liberty on my tongue and it tastes like brass and apples. after, in the alley, fog stitched itself to our coats and the moon signed its name on the puddles.

come monday if you can. bring your red shoes that walk like opinions. we will rehearse the scene where a window learns to open itself. i have written you three parenthetical kisses (one for the left cheek, one for the right, and one to keep in your pocket until the lights go up).

faithfully ludicrous,

p.
