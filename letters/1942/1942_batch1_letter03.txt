blackout honey

Date: 1942-03-27
Recipient: Ruth
Location: London, England
Year: 1942
Themes: blackout, intimacy, resilience, war, domestic

ruth of the candle-flame laugh,

tonight we pour the city into a teacup and sip carefully. the wardens shuffle by with their gentle thunder, tapping on shutters like patient metronomes. the sky has put on its mitten, and searchlights stitch white letters across a dark envelope no one dares to open.

you asked how you look in darkness; i answered by touching your wrist. the pulse knows everything the sirens do not. we counted to sixty and the seconds were not afraid. in the cupboard, two apples pretend they are a summer orchard. in the hearth, coal invents a small miracle and then another.

we learned to whisper with our shoes. we learned to butter bread with shadows. when the all-clear arrives it is always a little embarrassed by its own music, like a sparrow caught in a cathedral. still, we breathe as if first-time lungs, and the room grows taller.

i have saved the last spoon of honey. i press it to your tongue and for a moment the war forgets us. we sit by the radio and let the announcer fold names and places into the air. we do not unfold them. we keep them warm between our ribs, where at last the quiet finds a chair and removes its hat.

should we survive, let’s promise to go to the sea and not speak for three hours. after that we will say only yes for a week.

love, like a lamp with a brave shade,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
