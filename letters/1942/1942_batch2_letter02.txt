to my brother with the salt on his boots

Date: 1942-06-14
Recipient: Elias
Location: Norfolk, Virginia
Year: 1942
Themes: siblings, sea, convoys, anxiety, home

dear elias,

i pack this letter with dry socks and gulls, even if it must arrive naked as paper. the pier keeps the smell of you, a good rope smell, the kind that knows both knot and trust. i stand where the water says maybe and the sky says wait.

mother polishes her worry like the good spoon; it shines and will not be put down. i tell her your ship is an iron prayer and you are its amen. convoy, that slow impossible word, moving across the wet page where some invisible hand keeps trying to erase.

i send you a pocket of land: a photo of the dog asleep on the porch, paws twitching at dream rabbits. i send you june, and peaches, and the way our street goes quiet when the siren tests are over and even the leaves seem relieved.

if the ocean speaks, listen for the part that is our father laughing. he taught us to float with our chins up and our mouths open to the sun because joy is a kind of breathing. remember the compass of his hand on your hair.

write when you can. fold the letter around a sliver of horizon so i can measure how brave the blue has been to you.

your sister,

r.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
