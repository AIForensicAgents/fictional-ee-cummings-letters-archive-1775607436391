train-window orchard

Date: 1942-09-11
Recipient: Miss Adams
Location: Union Pacific, Nebraska Plains
Year: 1942
Themes: travel, teacher, coming of age, landscape, gratitude

dear miss adams,

i promised to write when the world began to move. consider this my handwriting learning to sway. fields unspool like green ribbon, then brown, then a sudden silver where a creek keeps its promises. every telegraph pole is a tall thin yes.

you used to say travel is the mind’s rehearsal for astonishment. i practice by naming the clouds. that one is library. that one is choir. that small torn one is the shrug a soldier gives before sleep. in the dining car i met a woman with a hat full of feathers who said she was going west to sew parachutes. she ate pears like good news.

i carry the book you gave me, the one with margin notes that look like polite lightning. on page ninety there is a coffee stain that remembers last winter. i read aloud when no one is in the berth and the train pretends to listen. even the whistle seems to turn a page.

the porter calls me sir though i am a tangle of nerves in a borrowed jacket. at night little towns appear and vanish, polite ghosts who could teach cities how to greet stars. i count windows lit against the great velvet, like buttons on the coat of a kindness i have not yet met.

thank you for making the map look less like a wound and more like a dance floor. i will send postcards from the mountain, from the desert, from the exact middle of myself when i find it.

your student still practicing astonishment,
Cal

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
