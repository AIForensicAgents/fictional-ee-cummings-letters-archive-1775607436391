sailors & matches

Date: 1942-02-05
Recipient: Brother Eli
Location: Norfolk, Virginia
Year: 1942
Themes: brotherhood, navy, anticipation, family, faith

eli,

in the pocket of my pea coat i found two wooden matches and a crumb of last week. i am keeping both for luck. the pier smells of rope and dusk. ships huddle like big-throated animals learning a new hymn. the chief says we will be ready by dawn and i believe him because his hands look like the truth.

i keep thinking of that creek behind the church where minnows practiced being silver. i think of our mother’s apron, her spoon beating sugar into storms. everything ends up in the water, they say. even fear chooses a tide eventually.

nerves are small birds and i am a generous sky. still, at night i pull the blanket up to my chin the way a field does when frost remembers its name. the guys play cards with faces nobody taught them, and cigarettes behave like brief saints. we laugh. we train. we shine boots until they hold an extra horizon.

if you see old mr. Marsh tell him the wrench he lent me is turning the whole ocean. i will bring it back, salt-polished, grateful. tell our sister i have learned exactly three knots and also a fourth, which is the kind your voice makes when it breaks into song.

keep the porch light. keep the porch. keep the quiet after a storm for me.

i’ll write again when the sea signs its name on me,
Tom

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
