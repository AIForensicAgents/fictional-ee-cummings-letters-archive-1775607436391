moonlight envelope

Date: 1942-01-12
Recipient: M.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1942
Themes: longing, homefront, winter, letters, hope

dear you,

tonight the river wears its thin white glove and suddenly the bridge remembers footsteps we have not yet taken. the streetlamps shiver a little, as if they are small saints. i keep the window cracked so the cold can edit my breath into something braver.

i have folded a piece of moon into this paper. it is a selfish moon, but it will travel without complaint. perhaps it will rest in your coat pocket and make the brass buttons less lonely. perhaps it will illuminate a ration line, a corridor, your shadow standing up to be counted.

the typewriter coughs like an engine and the keys dance their earnest dance. between letters, your name stays lit in the dark theater of my head. there are rumors in the newspaper like rain that has lost its way; there are maps that act like mouths. everyone speaks of moving, nobody says where the heart can sleep.

i went to the library and borrowed silence. it smells like old paper and rescue. i stitched your initials to my mitten with clumsy red thread and walked past the bakery that cannot remember sugar. even so, the alley cat performed its soft opera and the baker’s boy laughed, which is how the world refuses to surrender.

take this: a sprig of invisible lilac, a vow that blinks but never closes, the thinnest promise which is also the strongest string. i will tie it around my wrist and you tie it around yours; in between us, invisible kites will learn the wind.

stay safe enough to be reckless with joy someday.

always, and then again always,
L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
