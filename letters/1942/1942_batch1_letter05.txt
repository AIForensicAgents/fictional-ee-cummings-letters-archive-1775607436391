factory music

Date: 1942-05-16
Recipient: Ma
Location: Detroit, Michigan
Year: 1942
Themes: homefront, industry, women, pride, family

dear ma,

the clock whistles us into morning and our hands report for duty before we do. aluminum blooms on the line like a new sort of flower; we teach it to fly with rivets and stubbornness. the supervisor’s pencil taps time like a metronome that learned to smoke. by lunch my palms shine bright as grammar in a schoolbook.

we are a choir of hammers. sallie from bay three tells jokes that make even the drill press blush. a girl named Ora kisses her good-luck locket before each shift, as if the war can be persuaded with jewelry. we tie scarves over our hair and write our names inside the bandanas like prayers that will not be lost.

when the siren for break sounds, the whole factory exhales and i swear the walls lean back a little, grateful. i eat an apple that thinks it is july and a sandwich wrapped in newspaper headlines. in the margins i draw wings, ugly but determined.

you should see the way light behaves on the airplane skins. by afternoon it is silver rain, by evening a quiet moonrise in pieces. when we clock out, the sky is a large blue shoulder and i want to lay my head on it.

if my pay envelope looks heavier, it is only because i have filled it with fatigue and a letter to you folded very small. tell dad to oil the back gate. tell the lilacs to wait one more week. tell yourself i am safe, which is true when you say it.

all my love balanced on a rivet,
June

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
