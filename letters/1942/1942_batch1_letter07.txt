small prayer stitched in seawater

Date: 1942-08-19
Recipient: Nurse Sal
Location: Guadalcanal
Year: 1942
Themes: pacific, care, memory, nature, survival

sal,

rain comes in with its sleeves rolled up and the palms applaud it. the jungle does not whisper; it breathes out loud. last night the path to the aid tent grew teeth, but this morning it is only mud again and the frogs rehearse their green sermons.

i am learning the difficult alphabet of dawn in a place that forgets to end. somewhere out there a ship pretends to be a cloud and chooses us anyway. the boys write home on soggy paper that smells like courage and mildew. i bandage a shoulder and your name hides in the gauze. it is a habit i choose.

i think of your laugh lifting the corners of the ward until even the beds look hopeful. i think of the way you said bite down while the bullet watched, gentle as a baker. when the generator hiccups, the dark jumps into our pockets and we have to pat ourselves down to find the light.

i keep a shell with a small doorway of pink. i put it to my ear and it gives me a city of waves, which is to say, it gives me your hand on my forehead. send nothing if you cannot send a lie about winter. i would like to remember breath fogging the air without thinking smoke.

if i come home with a scar, i will name it for the day you taught me to dance by not moving. if i do not come home, forgive the ocean for trying to keep what it loves.

until the bright ambulance of peace,
Mateo

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
