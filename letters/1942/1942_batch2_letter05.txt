winter letter with borrowed light

Date: 1942-12-31
Recipient: Father
Location: Minneapolis, Minnesota
Year: 1942
Themes: home front, factory, winter, grief, hope

dear father,

the year leans its tired back against our door and asks to come in. i let it warm its hands on the stove and promise we will step carefully into the next one, boots first.

on the swing shift the factory hums like a box of bees. bolts roll under my palms, small cold planets behaving themselves. when the whistle blows, we stack our halos on the hooks with our coats, and walk out into snow that tastes like static and church.

mrs linden's window shows two blue stars now; we leave flowers made of paper because the real ones are busy somewhere else, making medicine for sleep. i think of you by the river measuring ice with your boots and telling me it sings when it breaks, which is another way to say change has perfect pitch.

if i have been brave, it is because the city keeps lending me its light: the streetcar's patient gold, the bakery's dawn, the little radio's soft geography of far-away. i carry them in my pockets like matches that promise a future flame.

may the new year be a door that remembers our names. i send you my hands through the post; they are tired but not tired of hope.

love,

e.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
