desert telegram (never sent)

Date: 1942-07-04
Recipient: A.
Location: Near El Alamein, Egypt
Year: 1942
Themes: war, desert, distance, memory, yearning

A,

if i could staple the wind to the map i would know where i am. the desert is a blank page that insists on being written with footprints and smoke. our trucks move like beetles learning a hymn. at night the stars punch small holes in the black tent and something vast looks in.

i have sand in my letters, my ears, the hinge of my jaw. we eat from tins that remember other lives. i drink water as if it were a rumor of the sea. sometimes, in the blue before dawn, a jackal spells its long name across the ridge.

i keep your handkerchief in the breast pocket, which keeps my heart from wandering. when the engine stalls i tell it a story about you and usually it forgives me. today a boy from Leeds taught me how to swear quietly. later he sang a lullaby for a tank and it fell asleep like a large obedient child.

there will be a moment on a road without a name and a horizon that never completes itself. i will think of your hair unrolling like a flag. i will think of your stubborn mouth inventing a country i can live in. i will think of how you taught me to pronounce mercy.

if this never finds you, if it becomes only another grain under a shoe, know i wrote it with both hands and also with my breath.

yours from the silence between shell and sky,
R.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
