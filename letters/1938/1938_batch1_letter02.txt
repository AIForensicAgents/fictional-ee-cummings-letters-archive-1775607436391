Lantern at the Kitchen Sink

Date: 1938-01-10
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Grundy County, Iowa
Year: 1938
Themes: family, rural life, winter, memory, resilience

thomas,

the barn creaks like a fiddle someone forgot to tune, and the snow keeps its quiet promise across the fields. mother leaves the lamp by the sink and the flame makes of our spoons small suns. father fell asleep with the Gazette folded over his chest—he dreams of rain probably (he always does when it is a crown of ice instead).

i measured the wind against my sleeve this afternoon and it was exactly the width of a wish. the cattle steamed like tea, patient as engines. i swear the radio knows more than it says—it clears its throat before speaking of the world beyond our fenceline, then changes its mind and gives us a hymn.

we have learned to mend what breaks, except for the hour between four and five when the past comes in for coffee and never pays. still, january is honest as a palm, and the stars indulge us: they pretend we are taller than we are.

keep the knife sharp and your laughter sharper. i send with this letter two buttons and a small pocket of courage (do not spill when opening). tell ruth her pie stitched winter together for at least an evening.

i am your brother and will be again tomorrow,

eli
