bridge of wheat

Date: 1938-06-09
Recipient: My brother Henry
Location: Near Dodge City, Kansas
Year: 1938
Themes: dust, brotherhood, land, resilience, machines

Original fictional letter in a modernist, experimental, emotionally vivid vein; not written in the style of any specific author.

Brother—

Morning came in on its knees, dust on its cuffs. The fields are a book the wind keeps dog-earing; the pages scratch and shrug and pretend at green. I walked the fence line where the posts lean like tired uncles, and a meadowlark stitched music into the hot seam of air.

We greased the combine with yesterday’s stubbornness, and it answered in a cough that might, with luck, become a song. The men talk rain as if it were a cousin late to supper—expected, forgiven in advance. A truck from town went by with sacks like sleeping children in the back, and the driver lifted two fingers to the world. That is how we bless things here.

Do you remember when we jumped the creek and it pretended to be a river and we believed it? I keep that belief in my pocket with the jackknife and a folded newspaper clipping about a dam somewhere far away. They say the river there will behave because men have taught it manners. I wonder who will teach the sky.

Last night I lay on the porch and listened to the radio glow. A dance band far away looped bright ropes of sound, and for a minute the prairie wore a tuxedo. Then a wheat beetle began its plodding sermon, and the stars took their hats off, and we were ourselves again.

If you come by harvest, bring your good boots and that wicked grin. We will make a bridge of wheat between now and better. I have saved the last sip of coffee in the thermos for the miracle we keep half-expecting.

Your brother (sunburned but standing),

Sam.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
