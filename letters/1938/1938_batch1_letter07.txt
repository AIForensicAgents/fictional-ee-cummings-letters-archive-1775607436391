If the Moon Had a Pawnshop

Date: 1938-11-05
Recipient: Mr. Lionel Greene, Proprietor
Location: Cincinnati, Ohio
Year: 1938
Themes: time, objects, trade, music, identity

mr. greene,

i am returning the watch that returned me to too many hours. it ticks like a neighbor who knows my secrets. please give me, in exchange, something that does not measure me—perhaps the harmonica on the top shelf, the one that looks as if it once swallowed a river and learned to sing it back.

i say this respectfully: your shop is a museum of almost. violins with their dreams carefully dusted. rings that confess to every finger but one. cameras that remember faces no one names aloud. today, the light leaned in to admire your window and refused to leave, so the street looked richer for a moment.

my coat is thin and bravado is a poor tailor, but i am warm with the thought that every traded thing keeps a soft map of its journeys. i hope you sometimes hold an item to your ear (after the bell has gone to sleep) and listen for the sea of its previous owner.

wrap the harmonica in yesterday's news; i like the way headlines stop shouting when music begins.

regretfully, gratefully,

p.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
