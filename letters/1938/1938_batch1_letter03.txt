Wire Night

Date: 1938-09-30
Recipient: Ruth Mendel
Location: Lower East Side, New York City
Year: 1938
Themes: uncertainty, city, friendship, radio, courage

dear ruth,

tonight the radio's throat is velvet but the words scratch. names cross the ocean on thin wires, shivering like moths against a bright uncertain bulb. the street below is all zippering footsteps and the sweet-bruised smell of fruit stands closing their eyes. i counted three sirens and one laugh the size of a birthday candle.

in the window glass i make two faces (mine and a future i cannot pronounce). the headlines argue with themselves and lose; i pour tea into the argument and it steams less terribly. you once said a city is a choir of locked doors humming in their sleep. there are keys on my table i do not recognize and how they glitter.

i will not pretend: a thin fear walks the hall, polite as a landlord. but i have the poem you tucked into my coat, and it is a small stubborn lantern. i hold it at the stairwell and the dark steps up, surprised it was not the only thing here.

if you come by tomorrow bring your pockets—i have saved for you three marbles, a spoon that believes it is silver, and a story about a girl teaching rain to read. we will eat apples until the map of worry surrenders.

yours under the humming wire,

anna
