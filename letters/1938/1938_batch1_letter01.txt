Spring Envelope (with Streetcar)

Date: 1938-03-21
Recipient: Eleanor (Lark) Archer
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1938
Themes: spring, love, city, longing, playfulness

dear lark,

the city unbuttons its gray coat and (oh) shyly becomes april-green. i rode the streetcar with my pockets full of wind and the conductor's bell kept saying yes yes like a sparrow learning grammar. there are tulips drilling up through the common, small red grammophones turning sunlight into brave music. i bought a stamp that tasted a little like peppermint and the future.

your last note slept under my pillow and made the night breathe easier. i woke in the middle (a sudden heart) and the radiator was clicking a typewriter poem none of the books know. if i could fold the charles and fit it into this letter i would; you could let it out in your room and the river would lean against your wrists, shy as a first kiss.

i am learning the piano backwards (i begin with silence and arrive at sound). the professor frowns but the windows approve. today i saw a boy drop a nickel into a gutter; he watched his face go down with it and did not cry. i wanted to hand him a sky instead, but the sky was busy being enormous.

do you remember the cracked blue teacup at the thrift? i bought it. i drink air from it when the kettle is stubborn. (one must practice for miracles.)

come soon, or send your hands ahead; i promise to keep them warm in my pockets until the rest of you arrives. spring runs on small feet—if we do not listen we will step on her.

always your ridiculous,

m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
