A Room Measured in Shoes

Date: 1938-05-27
Recipient: Miss June Ortega
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1938
Themes: city, work, music, friendship, hope

june,

the el throws its ribbon of iron past my window and ties the day together whether it wishes or not. i have become the seamstress of footsteps—every customer a paragraph, every heel a punctuation. mr. feld says leather is a language and i am beginning to conjugate it: i mend, you mend, we dance.

last night at the regent, the trumpet blew a moon into the ceiling and we all pretended gravity was negotiable. i thought of your hair pin that escaped during the fox-trot and hid like a fishbone in the floorboards. such small rebellions save us.

i pay the rent with exact change and the hope that no one asks for more. see how the linoleum keeps its promise to shine? i keep mine too: i will not mistake survival for surrender. there are geraniums in the window, scandalously red, and a lump of sugar i refuse to use because someday a stranger will arrive who needs sweetness larger than his coffee.

write me on payday (or any day that wants forgiving). if you find a pair of blue shoes smaller than an apology, save them—they will fit summer.

love from the eighth stair up,

clara
