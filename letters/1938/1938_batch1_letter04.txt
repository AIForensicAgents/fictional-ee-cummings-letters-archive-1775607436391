The Small Courage of Tea Cups

Date: 1938-02-14
Recipient: Mother
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1938
Themes: family, care, art, home, tenderness

dear mother,

i brought home a chipped saucer from the thrift and it stands with its chin up among the better plates. it reminds me how you taught us to carry on with a crack or two, to pour sweetness anyway. valentines are everywhere in the shop windows—lace hearts making the cold blush. i do not send you a heart cut from paper because you already have mine (folded into your apron pocket for safekeeping).

art school is a carnival where the rides are made of light. our instructor says a line must earn its keep; i try to make them honest, even when they are stubborn as mules. my hands come home smudged with charcoal, as if i shook hands with evening.

how is your cough? i miss the sound of spoons meeting the bottom of your blue bowl; i miss the peppermint you hide for guests and give to me anyway. i have learned that loneliness wears perfume in the afternoon and pretends to be a visitor; i politely show it the door and put water on for tea.

when the kettle sings i will think of you opening the window a little so the steam can tell the street your name.

with a daughter's foolish, everlasting affection,

rose
