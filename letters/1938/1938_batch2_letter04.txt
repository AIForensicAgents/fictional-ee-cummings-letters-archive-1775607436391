a small republic of rain

Date: 1938-09-30
Recipient: Marta
Location: Rue de Seine, Paris
Year: 1938
Themes: Europe, rain, news, anxiety, friendship

Original fictional letter in a modernist, experimental, emotionally vivid vein; not written in the style of any specific author.

Marta—

This afternoon the sky folded its gray napkin over the city and every café window became a theater. I sat with a cup that pretended to be courage and watched the street learn rain. Even the pigeons rehearsed solemnity. Newspapers shouted in black breath; everyone read them like mirrors they could not put down.

A boy sold matches under my awning—each small fire a brave vote. He smiled and the day coughed. I wanted to buy the whole bundle and light every dark stairway in Europe. Instead I bought two, tucked one in my pocket for you, and let the other tremble in my hand until the rain forgave it.

What can I say? The air is crowded with maybe. Trams bow at corners; soldiers stroll with the casualness of furniture. In the gallery, the new canvases are all arguments no one wins: angles with bruised light, faces that have misplaced their mornings. Yet in the market a woman hummed while weighing plums; the tune made a little country where no boots have ever been loud.

I keep thinking of your letters from the coast, how the sea ate the horizon like bread. Here the Seine slicks its stones and mutters; bridges practice tenderness badly and get better by midnight. When I crossed Pont Neuf, a man with a violin broke the weather in two, and for a measure I believed in secret treaties signed by music.

If you write back, tell me what your hands are learning these days. Tell me if the gulls still write their crooked lines of joy. Tonight, when the streetlamps lift their gold coins into the rain, I will spend a wish on each for you.

Yours, in this small republic that issues its own passport of drops,

Aline.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
