November Sends Little Telegrams

Date: 1938-11-22
Recipient: Aunt Beatrice
Location: Asheville, North Carolina
Year: 1938
Themes: convalescence, autumn, memory, kindness, family

aunt b.,

i am writing from a room that smells like starch and courage. the nurse walks as if balancing a secret on her head; i admire her very much. the doctor says my lungs have been rehearsing too hard and must learn to whisper again. as if the body were a choir and could be told hush kindly.

outside, the trees practice telegrams: stop. leaf. stop. cold. i have been making maps of the ceiling cracks—italy appears at noon if the light is certain. a boy brought in with a sprained ankle laughs like a stovepipe (hollow and amazed). we share oranges; the peelings look like fragments of a saint's sunset.

i remember your porch swing that never kept a straight face and the lemon cookies you rationed until i learned to steal only one. please send instructions for folding a napkin into bravery; i have forgotten step four.

by christmas i expect to be a small bell someone can ring for tea. until then, know i am held up by quiet kindnesses, which are stronger than rope.

your niece with the stubborn pulse,

edith

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
