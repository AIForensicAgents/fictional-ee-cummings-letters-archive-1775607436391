Warehouse Hymn

Date: 1938-08-16
Recipient: Sara Lenox
Location: Newark, New Jersey
Year: 1938
Themes: labor, city, music, dignity, change

sara,

the warehouse keeps noon in its pocket and takes it out only to check the time. we pack the day's breath into crates, seal it with a slap, and send it toward cities that will forget to thank us. the fan wobbles like a prayer with a stutter—still, it gives us wind enough to remember our names.

manny from line three hums a hymn that never lands; the sound brushes the boxes and blesses them unconsciously. at lunch we sit on an unambitious staircase and eat sandwiches that taste like thrift and mustard; you tell a joke that requires two hands and a forgiveness.

today a leaflet fluttered in through the loading dock and pretended to be a bird. we read it together and folded it back into something smaller than its words. sometimes change arrives sparingly; we give it water and a window.

if i could quit i might, but there is rent and the neighbor's baby learning to say light; one must finance such miracles. after shift, rain courteously erased our footprints. i took the long way home because i like the way evening waves at me from factory windows, as if i were its favorite mistake.

write soon. include news of your mother and whether the geranium has forgiven you for turning it to the dark wall (even for a little while).

yours with tape on every knuckle,

jo

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
