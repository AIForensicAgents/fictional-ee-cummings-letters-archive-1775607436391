Lighthouse Letter

Date: 1938-07-08
Recipient: Captain Ezra Pike
Location: Rockport, Massachusetts
Year: 1938
Themes: sea, solitude, memory, navigation, friendship

captain,

the fog has a soft jaw tonight; it chews the bells until the sound is only an aftertaste. gulls write their crooked signatures across the sky, as if the day were a contract we should read more carefully. the keeper let me climb the spiral and i watched the lens breathe. every rotation the world understands itself, a little—then forgets.

there is a boy who sells postcards by the wharf; he writes his own tide tables on the back in pencil (erratic, yes, but his courage is precise). i bought three and put them in a pocket i cannot easily reach. some treasures must require the contortion of gratitude.

i am well, though my loneliness salts everything. it keeps kindly—like cod. i have learned to sleep with the window open to the rope-voiced wind; it says goodnight the way old sailors do, with their eyes already half on tomorrow.

send me the story of the storm you said had teeth. send me also your right glove; i found its partner hiding under my chair, smelling faintly of tar and stubbornness.

faithfully adrift,

miriam
