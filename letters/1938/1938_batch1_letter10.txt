Snow Envelope For Two

Date: 1938-12-24
Recipient: Julian Cross
Location: Minneapolis, Minnesota
Year: 1938
Themes: winter, companionship, home, holiday, hope

julian,

i walked home inside a snowfall the size of forgiveness. every flake said hush and the street obeyed. the baker's window was a church of cinnamon; even the coins in my pocket smelled like saints. i have lit the last of the crooked candles and arranged the chairs as if neighbors might suddenly occur.

do not bring a gift; bring your particular silence (the kind that braids with mine without arguing). if you must, tuck a sprig of something green in your hatband; the room reacts well to small, impossible summers.

i made a soup that understands poverty and a cake foolish with nutmeg. the radio is trying to invent joy from a thin station—it nearly succeeds. at midnight we will open the window and let in one cold star each. they will not mind sharing our roof a little while.

what i want is simple, which does not mean easy: let us be two chairs that remember the shape of their sitters. let us be a window someone trusts.

if the snow deepens, stay anyway. i have an extra blanket and the moon owes me a favor.

with a night made gentler by your name,

a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
