threshing & thunder (your name)

Date: 1898-08-19
Recipient: Sister Ruth
Location: Osage County, Kansas
Year: 1898
Themes: grief, harvest, weather, sisterhood, endurance

Ruth dear,

the wheat has a thousand copper throats and they all sing when the wind pays them a visit. i ride the binder like a sober queen, dust for a crown and sun for a decree. we piled the straw into improbable kingdoms. in the afternoon a sullen thunderhead came walking over the prairie with its pockets full of nails; i told it politely we have roofs, thank you, but it hammered anyway. the child clapped at each lightning stitch.

in the small silence after supper i took your last letter to the porch steps and let it breathe with the cool. you wrote his name (do you see? i write around it, like a cautious horse around a hole). i keep it on my tongue like a bitter candy—what won't melt must be carried. sometimes at the pump i almost say it, and the water flinches.

i am not the weeping kind; the stove taught me to stand close to pain and turn it into heat. still, when night fastens its black buttons across the sky, i permit a softness: i make a little room beside me where he might have stretched his long laugh, and i smooth the sheet as if smoothing time.

send me, if you can manage, a hymn that has forgotten the words but not the tune. i will hum it to the rows, and they will understand; plants are clever in ways people are not.

your stubborn and loving sister,

May
