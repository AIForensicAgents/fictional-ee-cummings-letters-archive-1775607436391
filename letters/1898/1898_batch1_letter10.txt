white sheets, red noon

Date: 1898-07-24
Recipient: Dr. Margaret Cole
Location: Santiago de Cuba
Year: 1898
Themes: war, compassion, exhaustion, heat, mercy

Margaret,

i write in the peel of afternoon when the flies hold a parliament over everything that breathes. the ward is a geography of heat and whisper. we launder our hands until they forget their sins, then begin again. the wounded arrive folded into themselves like unanswered letters; we open them carefully, and sometimes they answer back.

a boy from Ohio asked if the sea is the same everywhere; i told him yes, and also no, which satisfied his morphine and his wonder. another insisted on gripping a spoon as if it were a promise. i have learned to nod at superstitions; they hold the tent up when the rope of reason frays.

outside, the palms wag their serious green. inside, the sheets are flags of truce we change and change again. when the fever climbs, time becomes a tall ladder no one should have to carry. still, there are mercies: a letter finds its soldier, a joke limps across three cots, a sleeping brow cools. oh, the extravagant relief when a pulse remembers itself.

i have not cried today, only once yesterday (behind the water barrels, which keep secrets). i will sleep with my boots on and the lamp trimmed for a dream small and clean. write me something useless and beautiful—a description of your cat's habitual rudeness would do. beauty here is medicine we cannot requisition.

from the littlest edge of strength,

Rose

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
