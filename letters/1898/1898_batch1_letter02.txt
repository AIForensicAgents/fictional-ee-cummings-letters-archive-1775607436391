letter with salt

Date: 1898-05-02
Recipient: My Brother Thomas
Location: Rockland Harbor, Maine
Year: 1898
Themes: family, sea, labor, memory, resilience

Brother Tom,

the sea keeps writing the same soft sentence over the stones and then forgetting it. i stand ankle-deep in the skim of yesterday and hear our childhood running—those broomstick ships, the brave puddles, Mother calling supper like a lighthouse. funny, how the gulls still sound like our laughter bent by the wind.

work is a rope from morning to night. we mend nets (which are really the town's stockings) and tell the truth to herrings: we mean them no harm until we do. sometimes the fog leans its enormous shoulder upon the wharf and we all become smaller—only hands, only voices. i like that: to be reduced to usefulness.

still, in the cramped lull between barrels i allow a thought of you (big as a sail). are your fields greening without my noisy advice? i imagine you squinting at a cloud and legislating rain. remember the oak behind the shed that cracked in that one wicked storm? sea is exactly that storm, elongated into a life. but i am learning to listen: to the heave, the hush, the secret hinge on which horizons turn.

i send you a pocket of salt in the corners of this paper. if the post brings you the taste of it, know i am unafraid and honest-tired, and that at night when the lamp discovers my face, i think a prayer that resembles your name.

your brother,

Nathan

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
