spring (in my pocket)

Date: 1898-03-17
Recipient: Miss Eliza Winthrop
Location: Beacon Hill, Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1898
Themes: love, spring, city, anticipation, tenderness

Dearest Eliza,

today the lilacs rehearsed a very small thunder and my coat learned to be lighter. all the cobbles on Mount Vernon Street clicked their clear little tongues—oh, approving the slender sun—while I walked with your letter folded against my heart like an extra beat. (how a paper can awaken the blood astonishes me.)

i confess i have been conducting secret experiments with happiness. i set a teacup in the window and it fills with brightness (no spoon required). i let my thoughts stroll without shoes, and they return tracking the perfume of your name. and if the wind asks who taught me this, i answer: a certain young lady who knits silence into music and places it upon my shoulders when the evening grows shy.

Boston is all stairs and bells and gentle brick, but since you, every stair becomes a shoreline, every bell a boat. i am ridiculous, i know; the dignified lamps shake their heads. yet dignity never kept anyone warm.

i have tucked two pressed violets here (if the postmaster doesn't nibble them en route). let them keep a blue secret for you. when next we walk by the river, i shall say what the violets already know—that time, startled, runs faster when your hand is in mine, and also (curiously) slower, as if trying to stay to watch us.

until then, think of me as a pocket full of April, foolish and entirely yours.

E.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
