snow-tunnel arithmetic

Date: 1898-02-09
Recipient: Father
Location: Truckee, California
Year: 1898
Themes: labor, nature, endurance, machines, family

Father,

the mountain multiplies its whiteness by itself until we are small figures upon a black slate, chalking tunnels with our breath. the rotary plow chews a logic through drifts that would rather be cathedrals. i stand on the cut where wind and iron disagree, and my beard becomes a modest icicle.

we house the locomotives in wooden sleeves; they sleep like tired bears and wake smoking opinions about grades and curves. last night a coyote recited hunger at the edge of camp while the men recited cards. i won a pocketknife and lost my patience with a foreman who believes the Almighty invented frost solely to keep us honest.

i think of your hands (quiet thunderstorms) teaching me the patient sum of nails + boards = shelter. out here the equation is snow + time = humility. a blue noon can suddenly break into knives; a friendly slope becomes a sly stair. yet at sunrise the peaks pinken like apology, and all is forgiven.

tell Mother i eat sufficiently and sleep in a coat heavy enough to remember four winters. tell the small pear tree that when April arrives, i shall consider it a personal letter.

your son who listens to rails,

Jonah
