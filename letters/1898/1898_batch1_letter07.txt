the sun suddenly remembers night

Date: 1898-01-25
Recipient: Professor L. Harrington
Location: Bombay, British India
Year: 1898
Themes: science, awe, travel, cosmos, empire

Professor,

your letter followed me like a proper English shadow all the way to a shoreline where the January heat argued with my collar. i confess i traveled for the science and arrived for the astonishment. when the eclipse took the day in its sharp teeth, an immense hush gowned the world—crows mid-wing bewildered into punctuation, men with turbans pausing their mercies of trade, the sea itself reconsidering its blue.

we made our measurements (cautious as accountants of fire), but i kept disloyal notes in the margins. at totality, the corona flared like a saint's last rumor. i thought: if truth is this bright, perhaps all our instruments are polite lies. forgive me—i am drunk on shadow.

my companions are clever and sunburnt. one swears he could hear the spheres debating in Sanskrit; another sent a cigarette up like a tiny comet and christened it Reason. as for me, i saved a piece of stunned daylight on this paper. hold it to your cheek upon receipt; i believe a cool astonishment will persist.

i return by steamer next month, humming with the engine. send word to my mother that i have eaten curries, made friends with a goat, and found the universe capable of blushing.

yours under an altered sun,

Henry A. Pike
