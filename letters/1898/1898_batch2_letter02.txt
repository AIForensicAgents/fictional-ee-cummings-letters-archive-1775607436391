On the Station Platform (after the whistle)

Date: 1898-04-19
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Omaha, Nebraska
Year: 1898
Themes: departure, trains, brotherhood, technology, restlessness, prairie

Tom,

After the whistle the air is not air but nails, bright and quick. My trunk is a little brown animal trying to follow me; I drag it tenderly. Cinders hop into my sleeves like black snow. The prairie sky is our kitchen table overturned—the legs of cloud reaching, reaching.

I am taking the north train to Chicago. They promise a school where wire sings. Imagine me at a desk with ear pressed to the hush, catching messages that arrive like migrating geese: dot dash dash dot (a ragged V against the weather). Between the couplings of cars my heart performs its sharp arithmetic, and I confess I miss already the slow addition of our evenings.

Tell Mother I carry her thimble, though I shall never sew; it rests in my pocket like a small moon to govern my tides. Tell Father his watch keeps his stern seconds; I obey most of them. If I fail, it will be in the bright direction.

Do not guard the fence too fiercely. Let the tall grass argue its case. In August I will send money, and if I learn the language of lightning perhaps I will send that too: a wire that says simply—Tom, I am here, and the distance is only a long breath.

Your brother,
Caleb

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
