camp, with trumpets made of dust

Date: 1898-06-12
Recipient: Mother
Location: Tampa, Florida
Year: 1898
Themes: war, duty, fear, faith, heat

Dearest Mother,

i write beneath a tent that keeps the stars outside (yet somehow invites their fever in). the men are a choir of canteens and coughs; boots bruise the ground into a drum. we practice marching—left, right, left—until even our shadows salute. Tampa's heat is a brass instrument; it plays us all day long without resting its lungs.

there is talk of Cuba like a far red apple fallen beyond a fence. some pluck it with their words, some sharpen their knives on the idea of it. as for me, i polish my rifle as if it were a childhood mirror: i keep seeing a boy who once believed summer meant blackberries only.

you will say pray, and i do, but my prayer is a telegram to a heaven whose operator is sometimes on break. still, i send it: keep my mother from worrying, keep my feet from foolish stones, and if bullets are ideas traveling too fast, please make me uninteresting to them.

i keep your handkerchief; it is a white bird folded in my pocket. when the bugle invents morning, i touch its wing and think of your bread, your violets lining the fence, the remarkable patience of your clock. should i be allowed a letter from Santiago, i will write with an ocean's honesty.

kiss the porch for me. kiss Father's chair (even though it creaks opinions). tell Ruth that the world is larger than a map and twice as confusing, but that i am steady as i know how to be.

your son,

Elias
