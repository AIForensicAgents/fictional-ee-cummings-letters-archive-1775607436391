A Winter Letter to the River

Date: 1898-12-17
Recipient: Jasper (who refuses winter)
Location: Hudson, New York
Year: 1898
Themes: winter, friendship, migration, river, acceptance, parting, home

Jasper,

The river has taught the shore a narrow patience. Ice breaks from itself in blue plates and drifts like a cupboard of broken china. Fishermen test the edge with broom handles and jokes; both are braver than they sound. I walk the quay and learn the cold by degrees, as if it were a lesson in Latin I had shirked and must now decline properly.

You say you cannot forgive winter. I understand. The white fields make honesty too easy; everything admits what it is. Come sit with me where the pilings hum and the smoke from the brickworks sketches our faces without asking. I will bring the tin cup; we shall sip something stern and let our breath practice vanishing.

There is under the frost a thin blue that does not agree to end. Even at noon, a quiet vein shows itself between floes and travels with an unembarrassed purpose. If you must follow summer, write me from the first orange blossom you find; I will hold the paper to my nose and tell you the news of snow. If you stay, we will learn together how to be still without becoming stone.

Stay if you can. If you must go, be carried.

Samuel

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
