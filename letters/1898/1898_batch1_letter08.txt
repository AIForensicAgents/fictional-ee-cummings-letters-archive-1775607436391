ink (votes sewn into the hem)

Date: 1898-11-14
Recipient: Miss Beatrice Hall
Location: Bloomsbury, London, England
Year: 1898
Themes: rights, friendship, persistence, city, strategy

Beatrice,

this afternoon we copied addresses until the streets swam—a city is only letters walking arm in arm toward a signature. the kettle in the tiny office boiled itself into righteous speech, and yes, we agreed with it. i pricked my finger on a pin and signed the petition in red (accident is sometimes the most eloquent orator).

i wore my plainest dress and the bravest ribbon i own. a gentleman outside the hall informed me the world is arranged like a chest of drawers with women in the narrow one at the top for handkerchiefs. i told him i am a shawl now, but i mean to be a coat. he laughed a little too easily; we allowed him the old music, while teaching a new tune to the floorboards.

when i am tired i imagine your handwriting marching up Whitehall with two umbrellas and a wicked grin. we must be more stubborn than December and kinder than Parliament. if we cannot open the door, let us remove it from its hinges and use it for a table upon which to plan the next door.

bring your needle Thursday; we will sew the notices into our hems where no nervous constable can confiscate them. one day the vote will be folded into our pockets like ordinary sunlight and we shall wonder how we ever did our washing without it.

in fellowship (and ink),

Agnes
