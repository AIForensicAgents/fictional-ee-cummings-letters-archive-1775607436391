A Quiet Reckoning with Paint and Rain

Date: 1898-07-12
Recipient: Miss Adelina Moreau
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1898
Themes: art, rain, music, desire, apology, city, river

Adelina,

Rain loosens its soft drums upon the roof and the studio smells of turpentine, bread crust, and the green patience of mildew. Your glove remains on the chair where you forgot it—one quiet hand refusing to leave. I have placed beside it a saucer of water to catch the sky; both have kept their silence admirably.

Down in the Quarter a cornet climbs a narrow stair of notes and looks from the balcony into the street; a piano answers from a saloon in little square windows of ragtime. The mules strike sparks from the stones; the sparks argue with the rain, then disappear into a wet hush. The river pretends to sleep (it never does), rolling its shoulders under the bridges.

I was severe last week, a palette knife where a brush would do. Forgive me. Your quiet is not refusal; it is another color I have not yet learned to mix. When you turned your face away I saw on your neck a crescent like the new moon where the locket chain rubbed—so delicate I feared any paint would bruise it. Magnolias here are large as saucers and almost the same shade as sorrow when evening climbs them.

If you return tomorrow I will ask nothing of you but a chair and your patience. The canvas may yet decide for us. If it refuses, we shall drink coffee strong enough to carry two small hearts across the narrowest night.

Ever your painter,
Lucien

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
