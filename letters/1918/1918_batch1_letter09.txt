to a city i keep arguing with

Date: 1918-03-01
Recipient: Baltimore (my difficult friend)
Location: Baltimore, Maryland
Year: 1918
Themes: city, contradictions, affection, suffrage, rain

dear city—

you cough soot into my collar and then kiss my ear with a streetcar bell. the shipyards stamp their iron syllables and the gulls translate badly. when it rains you invent thirteen kinds of sadness and sell them on the corner two for a penny. i buy three and go home humming.

we are not good for each other and yet here is the coffee i drink every morning on pratt street, here is the woman who waters her geraniums with a patience that could heal whole alphabets. last sunday a parade of women carried their hunger like banners; the wind took notes. a policeman tipped his hat to a girl with mud on her hem and no apology anywhere on her face. i wanted to learn her handwriting.

i saw a piano being lifted through a window and thought, that is how music enters a life: dangerously, with rope burns, accompanied by four men grunting the word almost. a boy traced the shape of a battleship in chalk and then sailed it down the gutter while the real harbor shrugged.

city, we fight at breakfast and reconcile by dusk. you bruise easily and pretend you don't. so do i. let us keep teaching one another the art of the next step.

still arguing, still staying,
—c

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
