armistice with bells & salt

Date: 1918-11-11
Recipient: Anna
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1918
Themes: armistice, grief, joy, homecoming, bread

anna—

at eleven the air unclenched. every bell practiced its miracle and even the pigeons forgot their schedule. a woman I had never seen before pressed a loaf of bread into my hands and said, take, as if i might know what to do with victory. we tore it open in the street and the steam inside was a history i could breathe.

men climbed statues and became temporary saints. a child beat a pot with a spoon until his mother laughed and then cried and then laughed again. the soldiers—those who are left—walked as if they did not trust the ground to be still. i saw one kneel to button a boot and remain kneeling, not from prayer but from the fatigue of being allowed to stand.

i thought of the ones who will not write, the chairs that have learned to wait forever, the letters that will keep arriving with their shoes full of snow. i carried your name slowly across the bridge, counting the ribs of the river. it seemed important to be careful with it today.

tonight i will cook onions until the room forgives me. i will open the window and let the crowd's song become our ceiling. come as soon as you can, with your fierce laugh and your winter hands. there is so much mending to begin.

always (and more now),
—p
