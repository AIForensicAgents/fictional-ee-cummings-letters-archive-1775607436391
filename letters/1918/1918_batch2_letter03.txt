between syringes & skylight

Date: 1918-09-18
Recipient: Eleanor Gray
Location: Ambulance Depot No. 3, Rouen, France
Year: 1918
Themes: caregiving, exhaustion, hope, friendship, war

nell,

i have learned the sweet mathematics of pulse: how a wrist divides fear by breath, how a bandage multiplies by quiet. mornings arrive with wheels and water; evenings leave in a soft braid of iodine and lamp smoke. our hands speak a language faster than our mouths (the grammar is pressure and release).

a boy asked if he was still beautiful; i told him yes because his eyes were busy being summer. another asked for his mother and then remembered the ocean and slept. there are minutes when the ward is a field of birds, flapping and flapping, and then—the settling—feathers of silence everywhere.

through the skylight an honest square of sky leans down and listens. once a sparrow came to inventory us, hopping bed to bed, a very stern quartermaster. he approved of the laughter we keep hidden in our pockets for emergencies. you would have given him crumbs and he would have written you a commendation.

i cannot say what day it is without counting it on my apron. but i can say that your letter warmed me through like a cup held in both hands. keep writing. every envelope you send is a door i can walk through when the night names itself too loudly.

whenever this ends (and it will end, because even rain eventually dries its hair), i shall borrow your shoulder and sleep a whole orchard of sleep. until then, i stitch, i wash, i hum, i borrow tomorrow from the kettle.

always with ink on my fingers,
—t
