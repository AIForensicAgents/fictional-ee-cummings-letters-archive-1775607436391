from a window that keeps running west

Date: 1918-06-10
Recipient: Mother
Location: Union Pacific train, between Chicago and Omaha
Year: 1918
Themes: travel, family, america, observation, affection

mother dear—

the window is a patient horse and i have been riding it all afternoon. fields unzip themselves; small towns lift their hats of smoke. i waved to a boy standing beside a scarecrow shorter than his father. his smile ran after our car for a quarter-mile and then sat down to catch its breath among the thistles.

in the dining coach a corporal taught me how to butter bread without making the knife weep. he said the prairies are the ocean learning to stand up. we passed a river that had forgotten its name and leaned against its own bends like a tired woman.

i am carrying the roll of your mending thread; it holds my ticket together and, i think, most of me too. please do not worry if you read in the newspapers that the world is misbehaving—it is—but there is still the hush between telegraph poles and the way wheat leans when a red-wing blackbird decides to be the only song.

i will write again from denver where i am told the mountains sleep in formal attire. kiss the houseplants. give mr. lemons next door my regards; his dog nodded to me last tuesday and i am certain it meant something.

i remain your wandering envelope,
—r
