note to the stranger i will become

Date: 1918-07-29
Recipient: My future self
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1918
Themes: self, forgiveness, sea, art, time

dear you—

the dunes today were a paragraph blown loose from the book of noon. i walked until my feet forgot their birthdays and the wind put salt where my thoughts had been. gulls made their clumsy miracles overhead. a boy with paint on his hands told me he was painting the taste of the ocean and i believed him.

if you are reading this from some stricter weather, remember that forgiveness moves like tidewater—doesn't arrive, simply keeps being here. you are permitted to be unremarkable, to hang your coat on the hook of a quiet day. you are permitted to laugh at your own jokes when nobody is listening. practice it now: say the word home until it becomes a mouthful of light.

the town smells of bread and kerosene. a sailor whistled a tune that stood on one leg, then the other. i wrote a poem that was mostly a mistake and loved it for that reason. please keep every clumsy thing you make; the future will need them as matches.

if war is the great noise, be the small silence that outlasts it. keep a pebble in your pocket from a beach you trusted, and when the newspapers begin to shout, hold it. it will remember you when you forget you.

with salt (and yes, the sun),
—h
