a packet of poems, and a city like a drum

Date: 1918-04-27
Recipient: Mr. Whitlow
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA
Year: 1918
Themes: art, publication, rebellion, modernity

sir,

i am sending, bound in brown string and a little foolishness, a handful of poems that have been troubling my coat pockets. they are not properly disciplined; they prefer to sit sideways in their chairs and whistle at noon. please forgive their posture. i tried marching them, but they confess a taste for dancing.

if your press has room for a few unbuttoned thoughts, let these try on the city. i imagine them in type—ink still damp—walking down a street where brick learns to sing and the lamplighter combs twilight with his thin ladder. i imagine a reader stopping (left foot surprised, right foot conspiratorial) to listen.

the poems don’t salute well. they shake hands like weather. but they are honest about their astonishments, and i have taught them to hold a door for anyone carrying grief. were you to find them unruly beyond what the public can endure, send them back by the earliest post and i shall teach them to bow (once or twice) without giggling.

if accepted, i will be grateful in installments. if refused, i will continue anyway, because accidents of the heart require documentation. thank you for whatever attention you can lend these small rebellions.

yours in paper and breath,
felix hart
