regarding certain unruly commas

Date: 1918-05-21
Recipient: Editor of The Small Review
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1918
Themes: art, experimentation, publishing, courage, modernism

dear editor—

forgive the insubordination of my commas; they are young colts and refuse to trot in parade. i have sent you a small herd of poems which, while ill-mannered, possess a curious honesty. their vowels are unshaven. i suspect they drink gin when my back is turned and kiss inappropriate syllables in stairwells. nevertheless i commend them to you as one commends a wayward nephew: not without dread, but with affection.

i aspire to that grammar the wind employs in early evening when the laundry surrenders its flags. the war has taught us the poverty of polite sentences; one must say it plainly: breath is precious and so are the mistakes of breath. a line should break where the heart does.

if you require credentials i offer only this: yesterday i watched a girl tie red ribbon to a lamppost because her sweetheart has been gone a year. the knot she made could teach every school its letters. i feel certain that poem already exists and has asked me merely to deliver its groceries.

should you find these pages unprintable, do send them back at once; they have a habit of getting lost and i fear for them among strangers. but if they earn a small corner in your august room, know that i will sweep that corner daily with a broom made of astonishment.

respectfully & impertinently,
—a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
