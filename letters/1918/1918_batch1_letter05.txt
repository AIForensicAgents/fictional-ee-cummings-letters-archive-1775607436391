apple-skin light over the harbor

Date: 1918-09-18
Recipient: L.
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1918
Themes: illness, caution, love, harbor, hope

l—

the news prints its gray mouth every morning and the city listens with a feverish ear. on tremont a policeman sneezed into his sleeve as if folding up a storm. the hospital windows look like long spelling mistakes. people swallow worry like aspirin and walk quickly away from their own footsteps.

yet the harbor had the audacity to be beautiful at dawn. i went to watch the fishing boats button the water with small, determined stitches. the sun peeled itself like an apple and the gulls argued over the peels. an old woman sold me a sprig of thyme for luck; i tucked it above my door and the room began to remember summer.

i have put your last handkerchief, pale as a promise, beside my pillow. i am boiling every cup i kiss; i am learning to keep my laughter at arm's length, then offer it only to open air. please write that you are drinking broth and standing at your window ten minutes each afternoon so the sky can recognize you.

if you do not come back to me this week, come in the next—but do come, even if only as a letter wearing your perfume. in the meantime i will be cautious and brave in equal parts, like a cat crossing a street with its whiskers full of bells.

love in careful handfuls,
—e
