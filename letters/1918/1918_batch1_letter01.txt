larkspurs in a coat pocket

Date: 1918-02-14
Recipient: M.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1918
Themes: love, winter, memory, city, tenderness

dear m—

this morning the river wore a thin breath of sugar, blue and brittle as a whisper you forgot to finish. i walked past the brick houses with their buttoned-up windows and thought how our names, stitched poorly by winter, keep coming unraveled whenever the light remembers us. in my coat pocket i found two paper larkspurs from last june (faded as the back of your hand after laughing too long). they have survived all my forgettings and are, i suspect, more faithful than my gloves.

you always call february a small room with a slow clock. i have decided to sit very still in that room and let the clock find me. last night i dreamed your hair was a tide and i was the moon, not pulling but listening. it is possible, i think, to be filled by what one cannot hold; perhaps that is what the heart was made for—those impossible errands.

i bought oranges from a woman with tired boots. i peeled one on the bridge and the air erupted into sudden summer; a saint of peel and pulp. a man passing smiled as if i had performed a kindness. imagine: the city blessing itself with a citrus prayer.

i will post this near dusk when the lamps decide to become small brave suns. if the envelope tastes of salt, forgive the tongue of the river which has been practicing my name for weeks. today it almost got it right.

yours (quite)
—n
