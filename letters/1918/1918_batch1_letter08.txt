fog teaches the wings patience

Date: 1918-10-31
Recipient: Elias
Location: London, England
Year: 1918
Themes: aviation, weather, friendship, waiting, imagination

elias—

the aerodrome has swallowed its lamps and the sky is a wall refusing introductions. mechanics move like chess pieces with oil for blood. the fog is not a curtain but a thought that won't complete its sentence. i watched a biplane taxi in quiet circles and felt sorry for its hope.

you would have laughed at the tea—boiled to firmness, a sort of edible dusk. a boy from kent offered me a cigarette and told me his sweetheart knits propellers into every sleeve. perhaps that's how they work: a sweater's secret whirl.

you remember the orchard behind my mother's house? the one that smelled like schoolbooks and bees. sometimes when the engines start, i imagine the trees taking off in formation, apples buttoned to their jackets, leaves saluting with such green seriousness. don't tell the flight sergeant; he'll put me on potato detail for the offense of poetry.

if the weather decides to lend you its hands tomorrow, fly kindly. not bravely, not brilliantly—kindly. as if your shadow were a small animal keeping up. return it to me unscorched. i will be waiting near the hangar door with a pocketful of peppermints and a story i haven't spoiled yet by telling.

the clock insists but the fog disagrees. tonight the disagreeing wins.

with bone-deep fondness,
—j
