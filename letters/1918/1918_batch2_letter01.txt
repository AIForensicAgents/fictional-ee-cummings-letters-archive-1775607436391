small thunder of your name

Date: 1918-07-03
Recipient: Clara Whitman
Location: Neuilly-sur-Marne, France
Year: 1918
Themes: love, war, distance, night, longing

dear clara,

today our tin cups rang like thin bells and the sky wore its careful bandage of cloud. your last letter sleeps against my chest; it rustles whenever the guns forget themselves. men here speak in compass and rumor, but i keep your name like a small disobedient country where the grass refuses to learn winter.

at dusk a mule sneezed and the world laughed—how strange and honest!—then somewhere a star broke its ankle and the horizon limped red for a minute. i pretended my boots were polite. you are not here to scold their muddy grammar, but the poppies argue you are near (they blush so loudly).

if the moon inquires, tell her: yes, i am eating; yes, i am sleeping; no, i am not brave. i am only very careful with the pieces of morning. i save the brightest for you. when i close my eyes, the road to boston curls like a cat and purrs on the windowsill of my mind.

we practice the alphabet of silence between shells. it goes: a is for after, b is for between, c is for come back. in a stolen minute i wrote your initials on the back of my hand; they looked like a door. i knocked. the knuckles answered with your laugh.

i have seen cherries stitched to trees by the sturdiest birds. so i believe in us, in how your wrists can tie wind to patience, how my heart (awkward, stubborn) still learns to pronounce home.

always, always,
—m
