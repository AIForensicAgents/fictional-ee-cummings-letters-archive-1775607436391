coal stove mornings

Date: 1918-01-12
Recipient: Mother
Location: New York City, USA
Year: 1918
Themes: family, art, winter, city, uncertainty

my dearest mother,

the stove ticks like a small black cricket and the windows have grown their annual frost ferns—such patient gardeners, januarys. i mix paint and light until my hands forget which is which. the city breathes in a white scarf; even the trolleys cough politely.

i bought three oranges (the grocer polished them like sunrise) and placed them in the blue bowl you sent. they look like a promise that accidentally learned how to sit still. sometimes i read the news and it sounds like a cracked bell from very far away. the word europe keeps spilling down the page.

how is your violets-pot? does it still insist on flowering to the left, as if listening for father in the parlor? i dreamt last night that i was a window and the world kept happening through me. i woke with my cheek against a sketch, and there, on the paper, my breath had drawn a harbor.

send, if you can, a little thread of home—one of your folded recipes, the way you sign your name without lifting the pen. i will pin them above my easel and let them drip into the canvases. i promise to pray carefully and keep my hat dry.

your son, in too-big gloves and a good wool heart,
arthur
