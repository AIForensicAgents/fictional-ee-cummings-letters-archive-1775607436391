mud becomes sky when i close my eyes

Date: 1918-04-03
Recipient: Sophie
Location: Somewhere near Amiens, France
Year: 1918
Themes: war, hope, sibling, mud, dreams

sophie—

the earth here is an unbuttoned coat and we keep looking for the pockets. some mornings the boots are heavier than the men wearing them and we take turns pretending to be clouds. it helps. last night the guns rehearsed their terrible opera and a rat walked across my diary to sign its name in rain. the lads say the spring is coming; i say the mud has already learned to bloom, it grows stars at night where the shells have written commas in the ground.

i carry the thought of your stained teacups like a chapel that fits inside a glove. you once told me sleep is a small boat with no oars; we push it out and the dark does the rest. tell the cat i have learned to purr through clenched teeth. tell father i am practicing silence with every letter i do not send.

i have pressed a leaf from a hedge that forgot to die in this cold. it is folded here beside this page and if it reaches you still green then the world is not lost. when i close my eyes i can smell the bakery on the corner of home street (you know the one) and for a full minute the horizon is butter.

be the spring i can put my boots into. be well; be ferociously well.

your brother who keeps learning the names of the moon's many sorrows,
—t
