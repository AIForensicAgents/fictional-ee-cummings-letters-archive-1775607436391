telegram of lilies

Date: February 14, 1937
Recipient: Mother
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1937
Themes: family, gratitude, memory, flowers, home

dear mother,

the florist wrapped the lilies so carefully that even their perfume whispered. i wanted to send a telegram but could not trust a wire with our silences, so i send these flowers instead, each petal a dot-dash of the heart. do not answer at once; let the kitchen consider them until even the kettle nods.

i remember your hands rinsing sunday from my hair, making of me a better monday. i remember the winter we learned how to warm rooms with soup and forgiveness. this city is a snow globe lately and i shake it when lonely just to watch the small storms behave.

neighbors think valentines are for sweethearts. i think they are for the oldest tenderness, the one that taught my first shoes to be kind to sidewalks. if i owed you every sunrise i have borrowed, i would be paying forever, contentedly, my pockets rattling with light.

keep the biggest lily for the table and place the small one by the mirror so it may admire your stubborn bravery. tonight i will drink cocoa to your health and pronounce your name in the steam until the window invents spring.

with a heart buttoned properly,

your child,

emma

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
