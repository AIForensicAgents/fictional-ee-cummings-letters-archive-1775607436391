bridge-light, gutter-boat

Date: 1937-06-01
Recipient: Sebastian
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1937
Themes: art, war, modernity, friendship, city

dear Sebastian,

paris today was a pocket of polished rain. bridges kept their wrists out, elegant and sure, and the seine wore yesterday’s conversations like soft jewelry. i walked to the exposition where the century is learning to pronounce its own astonishment.

in a pavilion a painting the size of an argument—black and white like a piano forgetting its song—unfurled a town the moment after the sky screamed. bulls, mothers, a broken lamp trying to become a moon. we stood, you would have too, with our cleverness emptied into our shoes. there are rooms where silence runs out of places to hide; this was one.

outside, a boy launched a paper boat into a gutter river and the boat sailed like a treaty no one would sign. cafés bubbled; typewriters clicked their steel rosaries. i bought chestnuts and burned my tongue (the price of warmth).

if you come—and you should—bring your camera but forget your certainty. we will inventory the doves on pont neuf, watch them rehearse belonging. i want to show you a lamppost that leans as if listening to someone’s invisible violin. i want to be that sure, to tilt toward what i can’t yet hear.

until then: write. your commas arrive like small bridges; i cross them carefully.

with an immodest amount of missing you,

l.
