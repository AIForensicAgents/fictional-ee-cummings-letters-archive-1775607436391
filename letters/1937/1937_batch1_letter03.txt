guernica in my tea

Date: July 15, 1937
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1937
Themes: art, war, empathy, exile, memory

ruth,

i went to the exposition and found a room where the air had teeth. walls arguing in black and white. a horse that could not finish screaming. the light inside the canvas had the violence of noon. my tea afterward would not cool; it kept remembering flames it had never met.

perhaps it is vulgar to send you a letter that smells of turpentine and grief, but this is all the language i have for today. in the pavilion i saw a woman holding a child as if holding time by the throat could slow it. men with raised arms like broken telegraph poles. a door opening into a mouth of shadows. everywhere the word no was trying to become a yes and could not find the hinge.

i walked home along the seine. a barge nudged the bank like a large animal ashamed of its size. paris wore rain with intelligent posture. in a cafe a boy played a tango on a tinny piano and made the sugar bowls blush. i thought of your wrists, small countries that declared independence the moment they learned to pulse.

forgive me if this letter is too many weather changes. i keep moving from thunder to blue in a single breath. send me news of your garden. does the cat still sleep with his paw over his face as if too much moon is bad for his reputation. i send you three pebbles from the river and the promise that our evenings will be soft as buns when i return.

holding your name carefully,

m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
