the typewriter dreams of oceans

Date: November 19, 1937
Recipient: Nadia
Location: Portland, Maine
Year: 1937
Themes: writing, sea, solitude, desire, return

nadia,

the lighthouse combed the night’s hair while i fed a ribbon through the old typewriter and promised it a storm of vowels. gulls argued their white arguments and the docks answered in ropes. i believe my machine wishes to be a boat; each key is an oar, each page a small brave water.

i tried to write common sense and only achieved weather. the sentences kept rolling their shoulders like waves, salt sneaking into the margins. i thought of your mouth inventing summer in a city of coats, how you made tea taste like travel. here, the wind insists it was born inside a bell and is merely doing its duty.

this morning i watched a fisherman mend a net, his fingers slow as forgiveness. he told me the ocean never says please, only thank you. i wrote that down and fed it to my ribbon and the letters came out darker, as if finally convinced of something.

soon i will return, my pockets full of polite pebbles and a new seriousness about sweaters. keep the window cracked for the arrival of our better selves. when the train delivers me, we will fold the map until the corners kiss and walk wherever that crease decides.

yours, salt-kneed and awake,

dan
