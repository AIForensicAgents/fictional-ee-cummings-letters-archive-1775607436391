after the silver lung

Date: 1937-05-12
Recipient: Mr. Cartwright, Editor
Location: Newark, New Jersey
Year: 1937
Themes: catastrophe, media, flight, witness, grief

sir,

for your paper (if paper can hold smoke), a witness:

the airship came down the way a promise remembers it is only breath—huge, patient, then suddenly a lesson in falling. a silver lung crumpling into a cough of flame. people ran like commas escaping an impossible sentence. the wind tasted like pennies and fear.

a voice on the field kept trying to be larger than fire and kept breaking. we were all made of listening for a moment, our eyes busy with red and our ears full of the future stuttering. i pressed my hand to the fence and the fence pressed back, hot as a mistake.

later, cinders stitched themselves to my sleeve: tiny autographs from a brief, violent alphabet. one man carried a child the way dawn carries its first noiseless bird; neither of them cried. i kept thinking how hydrogen is a dream that forgets to wake.

if you print this, do not let the headline be larger than the names. let the type breathe. let the blank spaces say what we cannot.

yours in ash and accuracy,

an evening passenger

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
