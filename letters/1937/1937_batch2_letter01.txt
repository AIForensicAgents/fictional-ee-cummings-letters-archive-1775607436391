a small thunder of you

Date: 1937-02-14
Recipient: M—
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1937
Themes: love, winter, city, longing, intimacy

dear M—,

i woke before the radiator found its breath, and the streetcar rang a thin tin halo over sixth avenue. snow keeps nibbling at the curbs (a white animal with patient teeth). somewhere a baker opens the warm mouth of morning and feeds it light, but here the room is all your absence, unbuttoned.

your last letter taught the alphabet to my pulse. i read it aloud into the teacup until the steam remembered your name. forgive this scrawl (ink is just winter learning to melt). if i could post you a sky i would tie it with rain and promise it wouldn’t break in transit. i carry your laughter folded twice in my coat; when i cross the street the wind unfolds it and the city listens.

come at dusk when the ashcans glitter and the stoops practice their piano of footsteps. bring your red coat and that reckless way of holding silence between thumb and forefinger. i will bring oranges, the small bright planets we can peel into sunrises, and the secret grammar of wrists.

until then i button the night from collar to hem and climb into it like a certainty. your kiss—somewhere between a chapel and a match—still happens to my mouth when i’m not looking.

i am (unsent, unspent, undeniably),

yours,

a.
