addressed to the somewhere

Date: 1937-07-10
Recipient: Amelia (no address)
Location: Oakland, California
Year: 1937
Themes: flight, disappearance, courage, public grief, wonder

dear amelia,

the map is a polite liar and the ocean is its conspirator. you went to find the exact seam of courage and the radio dwindled to a small coin rolling under a bed where even hands cannot reach. all week the newspapers arrange their questions into neat squares, and still: there is an empty chair that refuses to sit down.

i am writing to the somewhere you taught us to believe in, the pulsing between islands, the bright corridor a propeller makes out of otherwise. at the field a girl rides her bicycle in tight green circles, stitching the grass; when she looks up, the clouds consider applause. she says your name the way a ship says harbor.

what is a horizon but a promise the world keeps at arm’s length? if you can hear us—any us at all—tilt the day by one degree and we will set every kitchen compass spinning. if you cannot, know this: the sky has your handwriting now. it signs its evenings with your initials, somewhere above the laundry lines and the loosened hearts of strangers.

i keep a thimble of blue in my pocket for brave weather. it is heavy today.

with unafraid longing,

a citizen of your sky

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
