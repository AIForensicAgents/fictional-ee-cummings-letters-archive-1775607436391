bridge of red syllables

Date: May 28, 1937
Recipient: Elias
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1937
Themes: modernity, awe, city, freedom, engineering

elias,

yesterday the bridge opened its red ribs and we walked inside a heartbeat the size of a world. fog kept pretending to be wise, laying its white hand on us and saying hush, hush while below the bay unbuttoned a hundred silvers. we were a pair of commas on a long sentence of steel.

i counted the cables like rosary beads, each bead a prayer to the ordinary bravery of rivets. sailors whistled, a child released a red balloon which did not so much fly as remember a different weather. i told a stranger that the wind here is a polite thief; it took my hat, then gave me back my scalp tingling like applause.

from the center, the city looked like someone had spilled their cup of buildings and apologized with light. i wrote your name with my shoe on the new paint where no one could see, a private graffiti of the ankle. it will evaporate into the official red of things but i will know it is there, a stain of joy under the uniform.

when the last foot left the span last evening, cars began to pour themselves carefully like tea. the horn of a ferry spoke one long syllable and i thought: we are all only verbs, crossing. come west if the east grows too polite. bring only your laugh and that three-button coat. i will meet you halfway, on a bolt with the ocean on both sides, and we will learn to be light enough for wind.

with pockets full of fog,

L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
