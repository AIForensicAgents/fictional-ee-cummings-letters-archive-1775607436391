late orchard gospel

Date: September 12, 1937
Recipient: Pastor Ellison
Location: Asheville, North Carolina
Year: 1937
Themes: faith, harvest, doubt, reconciliation, nature

pastor,

i am writing from the orchard where the ladder has opinions and the bees sing work into my wrists. the apples are small planets willing to be held. evening wrings a gold handkerchief over the hills and i, an unbeliever with leanings, am nearly persuaded by light.

i have misplaced the word amen but found several good substitutes: the thud of fruit into a basket, the clear yes of a child rinsing dust from her knees, the way a tired man uncaps his quiet at the door and offers it to the room. perhaps faith is only the body agreeing to go on, and style is how we make the agreement beautiful.

you asked if i still hear the sermon after the hymn dissolves. lately i hear it in the pockets of my jacket, where two coins argue softly and a bruise keeps its promises. i hear it in the kitchen when the kettle begs for a small audience and gets one. on sundays i practice gratitude by sweeping, an altar of crumbs.

if i come to church next week will you meet me afterward by the sycamore and allow doubt to sit with us like a respectable cousin. i have questions that sound like birds but cannot yet fly. i promise to bring cider and the honest intention to listen while the grass explains its green.

with muddied boots and a courteous heart,

isaac

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
