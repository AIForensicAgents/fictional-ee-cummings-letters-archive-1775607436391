dust is a difficult music

Date: April 3, 1937
Recipient: Brother Owen
Location: Dodge City, Kansas
Year: 1937
Themes: family, dust bowl, survival, land, hope

dear owen,

the wind was a black piano again today, its keys full of grit. we pack damp towels at the windows and still the cupboards sing our throats raw. even the hymns in mother’s book have grown a shade browner. i keep finding wheat in my shoes that was never planted.

this morning we walked the field and talked to the stubborn geometry of furrows. it answered in coughs. the north fence sank another inch into the stubborn past. i patted the cow, who chews as if she were rehearsing patience for the rest of us. your old harmonica tastes like a roadside, so i play it anyway, a thin tune to keep the kitchen from remembering it is tired.

news from town: tommy has found work rolling barrels at the depot; he says he prefers circles to straight lines because circles come back for you. the church ladies are sewing masks for the littlest ones and their needles make the only rain we get. the radio talks about far-off wars and nearer elections in a crisp voice that has never eaten dust.

i dream of a wide green that does not flinch when the sky coughs. until then we keep boiling coffee as if bitterness were a vitamin. write soon and tell me if the city still has edges that do not move. if you cannot send money, send your laugh wrapped in newspaper. i will trade it for beans and the belief that every storm must, out of sheer boredom, learn to leave.

yours in grit and stubbornness,

clare
