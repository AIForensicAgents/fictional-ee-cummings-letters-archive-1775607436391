on the train between two everythings

Date: March 21, 1937
Recipient: Miss A.
Location: Pullman Car, Pennsylvania Railroad
Year: 1937
Themes: movement, time, longing, machines, anticipation

miss a.,

this car hums like a patient bee and i have given it my knees, my hat, my small trust in schedules. telegraph poles tick past like punctuation, writing a very long, very skinny poem that only the horizon can read. i press my forehead to the glass and the world becomes a rumor of itself.

the porter taught me to fold a bed from a wall, which is a magic i intend to keep. i slept between cities and woke with a map on my tongue, each station a sweet salt. across the aisle a woman mends a sock with the concentration of a saint; each stitch a treaty. a man in a gray suit sells quiet to the rest of us by opening his newspaper very large.

we pass a shack, then a steeple, then a puddle pretending to be the sky. i am traveling toward you with the seriousness of a small storm. in my pocket a ticket whispers, soon soon. i practice your name on the window and the condensation applauds by disappearing exactly on time.

what shall we do when i arrive. eat oranges and their little suns. count the pigeons, then set them free again, a book suddenly learning how to fly. i bring you nothing but this rattling/wantedness, this word that keeps leaning forward. if the train overshoots, i will throw myself to a slower geography and walk the last mile in my socks just to hear our hello land softly.

yours almost here,

r.
