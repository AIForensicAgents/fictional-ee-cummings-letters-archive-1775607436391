small manifesto to a violin

Date: October 5, 1937
Recipient: Irena
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1937
Themes: music, work, city, resilience, affection

irena,

i watched you in the empty hall where the ceiling keeps secrets and the chairs are tidy little unbelievers. your bow was a thin moon held between careful fingers and the air, obedient, became a river at your insistence. even the janitor stopped flirting with his broom.

you told me the supervisor pays in envelopes that smell faintly of starch and good intentions. take it. take this city too, with its lake that refuses to be only water. practice on the wind that sprints down michigan avenue like a telegram with legs. when your wrist trembles (mine does too) consider it the signature of honesty.

last night the streetcar shook a lullaby out of its metal bones and i thought of your G string speaking to my spine in full sentences. i am not a believer in much, but i believe in the way your shoulder makes room for sorrow then asks it to sing instead of weep. if there is a nation for such transformations, you are its quiet flag.

come by the cafeteria after rehearsal; the coffee is righteous and the pie understands loneliness. i will save you a seat by the window where the evening builds itself like a polite cathedral. bring your instrument unlatched so it may breathe. bring your laugh. i have two nickels and a plan to make them rhyme.

in open-throated admiration,

ben
