after the dirigible burned

Date: May 10, 1937
Recipient: Harold
Location: Newark, New Jersey
Year: 1937
Themes: technology, mortality, news, friendship, vulnerability

harold,

the newsreels at the theater stuttered flame and the room held its breath like a bad swimmer. a silver whale undone by sky. we walked out into evening and everything looked suspiciously mortal: streetlamps, our hats, the way the ash in my cigarette insisted upon being light.

someone said the word disaster as if chewing ice. a boy asked if a thing that floats can still be called a ship when it is on fire. i wanted to answer him with a better century. a woman prayed with her hands curled under her chin, two small lifeboats that did not know where to go.

i have always loved the brag of machines, their confident pulse. tonight i loved them less and people more. the way an old man steadied his wife at the curb as though she were a glass of water he intended never to spill. the way a stranger held the theater door because the world had clearly forgotten its manners.

come by tomorrow and we will walk along the river where the ferries practice not being miraculous. we will buy a paper that gets the dates right and the feelings wrong, then correct both with coffee. i will tell you a new plan i have been brewing, which is mostly this: to hold lightly what burns bright and hold tightly what burns slow.

your friend in smoke and dawn,

jules
