ink breathing like a small bird

Date: January 9, 1937
Recipient: Marta
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1937
Themes: love, winter, city, intimacy, art

dear marta,

tonight the radiator hiccups like a tin heart and i sit stitching sentences to the dark. snow keeps sighing against the stoop, each flake a scrap of paper no one sent. the city wears its hush like a scarf and somewhere a saxophone leans its warm shoulder into the cold.

i kept your last note under the tea tin so it would learn patience. even paper needs schooling. when i unfold it, the room becomes a small summer: your g, your k, the curl of an s that could hold a moth. i swear the ink still breathes if i lean close enough; it smells of bread and daring.

i walked to the corner for stamps, boots chewing the whiteness. a drunk was arguing with a lamp about who was lonelier. i wanted to tell them: loneliness is a fine old chair; it just wants a coat thrown over its arms. the post office clerks clicked like chess pieces. i mailed you my hands in the form of words.

if i had a ladder tall enough i would climb into the snow and rearrange it, spell your name along the rooftops. i would borrow the moon the way one borrows sugar (apologizing, grinning) and roll it to your window so your dreams could see by it.

be the city in my pocket. be the door that forgets to lock. i will be the small bird of january, tapping our yes against your sill until morning opens.

love (without mittens),

A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
