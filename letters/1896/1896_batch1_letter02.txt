ink & bicycle bells

Date: 1896-02-23
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Brooklyn, New York
Year: 1896
Themes: modernity, family, freedom, humor, city, movement

dear Thomas,
i have learned the apostrophe of balance: two wheels, a spine, and the grammar of falling. the bicycle is a bright insect under me, clicking, and the cobbles of Fulton Street argue every syllable. the boys who sell papers shout the day into rags; i refuse to purchase, i am already full of turning.

i fell twice (do not inform Mother). the second tumble was almost music—brakes a violin scream, my hat a small black moon rolling into a puddle. and yet when i rose the city lifted too, the ferry whistle stitched a horizon and the river wore a silver grin.

do you remember the apple orchard? the hill we dared by sled? this is that, only the snow is made of dust and sunlight, and the sled is a little machine that believes in me. every road opens like an envelope. inside: wind; inside: a word that feels like tomorrow.

come visit when your books loosen their grip. we shall ride at dusk where the streetlamps practice being stars, and race the trolley without being unkind. i will lend you the bell—its small bright syllable says yes yes, as if it knew the question before we asked.

your laughing sister,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
