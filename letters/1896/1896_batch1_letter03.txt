in the seam of rain

Date: 1896-03-30
Recipient: Mother
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1896
Themes: home, weather, faith, distance, tenderness, south

dear Mother,
the rain here is a long seam and the city is a dress that fits the river too tightly. magnolia breath presses through the screens; it is the kind of perfume that remembers other summers. i draw the curtains and the day sits beside me like a good patient girl.

there is a boy who sells figs from a broken crate. he calls each fruit by a different name—mercy, prayer, hush—and i, unruly, buy them all. when i peel their purple darkness my hands look almost devout. do not worry: i attend church with my better self, which is to say i light the little candle of attention and it does not refuse to burn.

your last letter carried the breath of starch and lavender. i kept the envelope under my pillow two nights; dreams came tidily folded. sometimes i am sorry for my distance, as if i had become a long porch no one can quite cross. but listen: when the rain stops, the street shines like a hymn between verses, and i am certain you stand at its far door, apron full of sunlight, ready to wave me in.

i send coins for the coal-man and a kiss for the cat. tell Father i have learned to sew a button with steadier hands—each stitch a small oath kept.

your daughter, trying to be good,
R.
