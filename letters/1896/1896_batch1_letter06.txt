regarding the thunder that never arrived

Date: 1896-06-14
Recipient: Miss Ada Penrose
Location: London, England
Year: 1896
Themes: travel, anticipation, music, urban, silence, fog

dear Ada,
London fog is a glove that fits everyone at once and no one entirely. i have worn it along the Strand where omnibuses clatter like over-argued thoughts. in the music hall a soprano stitched lightning through the smoke; the audience held its breath as if a new century were a bird about to choose a shoulder.

i waited for thunder that never arrived. perhaps expectation is its own storm, a silent one that rearranges furniture. i found myself smiling at a lamppost (it blushed, i swear) and at a newsboy whose headline was simply the afternoon, wet and continuous. in the museum a Roman mouth spoke without lips; i understood enough to nod.

if you were here we would count umbrellas and declare certain ones brave. we would drink tea the color of patience and talk, in whispers, about the architecture of pulse—how a hand finds a hand, and the bridge does not fall. instead i write you, and this paper becomes a small pier where letters tie their boats and wait for your harbor.

send me a scrap of your day. even a pin from your hat, carrying a little domestic thunder. i remain (between fog and almost) your hopeful conspirator.

as ever,
J.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
