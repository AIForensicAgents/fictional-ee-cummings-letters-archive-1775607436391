first frost, last lantern

Date: 1896-11-15
Recipient: Father
Location: Burlington, Vermont
Year: 1896
Themes: seasons, work, inheritance, reconciliation, quiet, rural

dear Father,
the first frost has written its thin autograph upon the field, and the pumpkins keep their orange like a good secret. i took the lantern out before dawn; it made a small cathedral around my hand. inside that light i remembered your boots, their honest grammar with the soil.

i have learned the hinge-song of the barn door and the way silence gathers where the threshing ceased. there is a mercy to work—how it erases the mirror and leaves a window. i stack wood and the day agrees to be carried, one rough syllable at a time. when the sun finally unbuttons the mist, the river shows its spine.

you asked, in your last letter, if i intend to stay when winter sharpens. yes. the road glittered a little with leaving but i have chosen the steadier verb. perhaps i am no longer traveling toward a grand city of lanterns, but i find here a smaller empire: a kettle that keeps its word; a dog who forgives without footnotes; a sky that signs everything with geese.

sleep well. i will bank the stove so it remembers morning. if i am any kind of man, it is the kind your silence taught me to be—careful with nails, extravagant with bread.

your son,
H.
