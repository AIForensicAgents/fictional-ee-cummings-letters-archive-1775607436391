on lilacs & probability (a courteous disturbance)

Date: 1896-05-17
Recipient: Professor Aurelia Knox
Location: New Haven, Connecticut
Year: 1896
Themes: intellectual curiosity, spring, mentorship, chance, self-discovery

esteemed professor knox,—

i return your kind notes annotated by spring; forgive the purple fingerprints (the lilacs, extravagant tutors, insisted). after lecture today, when the chalk-constellations still trembled upon the blackboard, i walked beneath the elm arcade and found that bernoulli had colonized the birdsong. every chirp tried its odds upon the air and—miracle of bravado—won.

you spoke of chance as a gentleman who removes his hat upon entering but declines to sit. i, poor hostess, set out the best china and watched possibility sip from every cup. in each sip i tasted more of myself than i expected; what arithmetic is this, where unknowns confess the known by almost-music?

i wish to say: your theorem about the honest coin is persuasive; yet in the pocket of a young woman walking home with lilacs and several futures, the coin warms to preference. it learns to fall toward what listens hardest. forgive my impertinence; i mean only that the hand (being human) is an instrument of weather.

a moth has spent the past hour interrogating my lamp—pursuit both foolish and exact. i recognize the experiment: light refuses to be finished, so the world is stitched again by our bewilderment. you have taught me to welcome that needle. i will go on pricking the vast cloth with questions until stars appear where i have not put them.

if this semester consents, i intend to present a small paper on the statistics of blossoms—how a lilac branch (being apparently simple) is occupied by an unruly democracy of buds toward bloom. my method is imprecise and (to be honest) happily so; it seeks the limit at which counting becomes love. your correction, should it arrive, will be a kind weather over my earnest errors.

i remain, with chalk on my sleeve and lilac in my hair, your student who has just now learned to spell wonder with variables.

respectfully & irreverently,

margaret elden
