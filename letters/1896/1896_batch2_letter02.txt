field report (homefront, magnolia)

Date: 1896-04-30
Recipient: Captain Rowan Fitch
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1896
Themes: family, distance, home, nature, yearning

brother,—

the magnolias have commenced their scandal again, white flags hoisted from green citadels, and every afternoon the river forgets its discipline to preen in the sun. i am required (by myself, and a small conspiracy of sparrows) to tell you that the house remains standing though mother leans on its walls with her listening. she polishes the bannister until our childhood goes sliding down, thump by remembered thump.

your last letter carried sea-salt in the fold. i opened it, and the room acquired a tide. such obedient currents! the chair lifted one leg to consider a voyage, and our old clock, that grammar-master, allowed its ticks to wander out of order (still arriving at the hour, but drunk with detour). you spoke of charts, of careful angles, of uniformed distances; i heard instead the slow acrobatics of loneliness.

here, a regiment of bees inspects the azaleas. i watch, and learn command by their hum. the grocer's boy salutes no one in particular (which is to say, everyone), and farther down on bull street the preacher has installed a bell so exuberant it forgets to be obedient to sunday alone. last evening, some fiddles met a porch and argued themselves into a dance. the fireflies adjudicated fairly.

i send you a pocketful of provincial news: mrs. ellery's goat has given birth to a goat precisely herself; the telegraph at the corner sulks in rain; and in the newspaper a poem tried to be a sermon and succeeded only (beautifully) in being a poem. i clipped it and put it under the bread tin because we must keep certain hungers honest.

rowan, if your compass grows tired, lay it on your palm and let your pulse persuade it. remember the true north we learned in the sound of oars when father rowed at dusk (that hush-braided certainty). write me your latitudes of heart, not merely the naked numbers; i will answer with longitude of home, measured in porch steps and the length of mother's sigh.

go safely through the foreign syllables. i have stored an extra summer for you beneath the porch, beside the rake. upon your return we shall release it, and august will stand applauding in its shirtsleeves.

your ridiculous brother,

thomas
