the lighthouse writes back

Date: 1896-04-18
Recipient: Captain Elias Ward
Location: Portland, Maine
Year: 1896
Themes: sea, guidance, friendship, danger, hope, vigil

dear Captain Ward,
the lighthouse wrote to me last night in its alphabet of light—short, long, long, a patient blinking grammar upon the wet page of fog. i answered by polishing the lens until my arms were sailors and the cloth a tide.

the gulls are inventors of weather; today they built a wind out of their white decisions and the sea agreed. i stacked wood; the stove answered with a red mouth. there are moments when the starboard in my chest lists toward doubt, but then the bell on the harbor jawbones a kindness and i am kept.

you would laugh at the cat, who believes herself a constellatory beast, pacing the parapet as if the night were a soft-collared thing. she and i share the same profession: we tend what trembles. the wick, the ship, the human thought crossing a dark season—each wants a hand around it that will not flinch.

come ashore when the weather gives back your decks. bring stories damp with salt; i will pour coffee that remembers Brazil and we will pretend the table is a chart where two old fools locate a warmer latitude by heart alone.

until then, trust the blinking—my yes to your return, repeated.

your friend on the rock,
M.
