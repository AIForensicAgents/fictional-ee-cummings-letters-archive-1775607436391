Recipient: Miss Eliza Hart
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1896
Themes: love, winter city, anticipation, intimacy, letters

dearest eliza,—

tonight the snow stitches the street to its breath; every lamp is a small astonishment buttoning the avenue. the postman (a solemn wind-figure) has the look of a necessary saint, and i—poorly buttoned myself—peel the cold from the railing and think of your hands, which i would learn the grammar of, fingertip by gentle fingertip.

i have bought a single violet from the woman near park street. it came wrapped in yesterday's words, all their sturdy little squares of news dazed by purple fragrance. do you know how a violet behaves in winter? (as if the world were merely a darker room to be illumined by its hush.) i have placed it on my windowsill where the glass invents a second blossom, an obedient dream.

i write to say everything and only this: if you breathe there, i hear it here. if you look at the moon, she adjusts her lace and leans nearer my window as if addressed to us both. the city, which pretends hard edges, is tender enough to bisect itself for our meeting (sometime, soon) and be sewn again afterward with our footsteps for thread.

forgive the uneven heartbeats of this ink; the nib stammers when i remember your laughter tilting against the air—oh that cheerful anarchy, that confetti of sound. i keep it in my coat, folded, to open when the streetcar is too dutiful and men speak loudly of coin, of coal, of clocks; i open your laughter and become, briefly, another century.

i am learning (awkwardly) the practice of patience. it is an instrument requiring more breath than i imagined. but when you arrive, i shall be fluent: the smallest minutes will blurt into astonished forever, and even my boots will understand waltzing.

be warm for me. keep the lamp low and the book higher. let your shadow walk before you like a soft assertive bell. and write—do write—your words are a room i know by heart though i have never entered its door.

mine (incorrectly and always),

n.
