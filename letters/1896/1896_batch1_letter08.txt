telegram of peaches

Date: 1896-08-21
Recipient: Mr. August Finch
Location: Asheville, North Carolina
Year: 1896
Themes: harvest, love, technology, sensory, playfulness, rural

dear August,
your telegram said simply PEACHES STOP MANY STOP HURRY and i did, tripping over the afternoon. how can such small brass words carry that orchard’s round astonishment? ink is a poor mouth for juice.

the hillside is reckless with fruit. the trees bow as if courtesying to bees, and i, greedy parliament of one, vote for abundance. we ate beside the packing shed, our elbows perfumed, and the sun kept adding its signature to our wrists. you laughed when a wasp admired your thumb; it fled, convinced by your calm.

is love perhaps an agriculture—the patient gamble of weather, the pruning that feels like harm but promises sweetness later? i think of the wire singing above us, messages winging like invisible birds, and i am glad our news pretends to be business while meaning kiss.

send the crates north by Friday; i will sell them to the city with a straight face. privately i shall admit that every peach contains a syllable of your name, and that my hands, even now, refuse to be clean of it.

yours in syrup and in sense,
L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
