a small thunder in a teacup

Date: 1896-01-12
Recipient: Miss L. Hartwell
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1896
Themes: winter, longing, courage, intimacy, city

dear L.,
this morning the snow makes a white hush of the streets, and every hoofprint is a small dark heartbeat. i walked beneath the gaslamps (their pale halos a little tired, like eyelids at dawn) and thought of the tea steam that curls from your cup—how it learns to be sky and forgets its kettle.

if courage is a teaspoon it trembles, yet carries sweetness. i have tried to be such a spoon today: to lift the ordinary toward the mouth of wonder. an old woman on Tremont fed the sparrows with her glove removed, and i believed, absolutely, in the afternoon.

this city speaks in muffled bells and bootbuttons; my own not-quite words keep rustling in the coat lining. will you let them out? when i stood at the window the hansom turned the corner and for one bright foolish second i thought it was you, your laugh scattering the quiet like salt.

consider this my modest rebellion: i am choosing to be glad (with you) in advance. pin this line inside your sleeve where it may burn lightly all day. yes, we are small—smaller than a snowflake’s window—but we drift together, and that is a kind of weather.

ever your sometimes-broken, always mended,
E.
