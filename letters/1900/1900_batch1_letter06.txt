salt and violet

Date: 1900-06-30
Recipient: Lydia Reyes
Location: San Juan, Puerto Rico
Year: 1900
Themes: love, island, storm, change, resilience

Lydia querida,

today the sky was a scarf the ocean could not keep. mangoes fell like small suns in the yard and my niece laughed in a language older than her mouth. the trades came quick, put their fingers in the shutters, rattled a music that made even the teapot dance a little.

i have been brave in the showy way and quiet in the important way. change knocks as if it were polite, but its feet are muddy. we mop and mop. still, once the floor dries, there is a shine we did not own before. i think that is how i will choose to name this year.

you asked if i still dream in spanish. i dream in salt and violet. the salt for my grandmother's kitchen where every question was answered with a handful of cumin and a story. the violet for the time between afternoon and evening when all faces become tender and the streets forget to be loud.

come when the moon fattens. bring the song you keep in your pocket. my windows are foolish with listening.

con todo lo que tiembla y permanece,

Isabel

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
