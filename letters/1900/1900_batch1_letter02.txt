harbor breath

Date: 1900-02-03
Recipient: Brother Samuel
Location: New Bedford, Massachusetts
Year: 1900
Themes: family, sea, work, faith, weather

Dear Sam,

the masts are spines against a milk colored sky and the gulls write their rude alphabets over our heads. i have been mending nets with hands that remember our apple tree ladder, the splinters that taught us small sermons. work tastes of salt and iron and the old prayer we learned from Father, the one that fits in a pocket like a stone.

men here speak in knots. i listen, and sometimes their stories untie themselves in steam as the kettle fusses. at night the pier boards creak like a sleeping ark and i count the creaks instead of sheep. mother would laugh to know my sleep is a ship that leaves me behind most nights.

i am learning the patient grammar of weather. a good wind says go. a hard wind says stay and do not be brave about it. i write you because you understand the ache under the ribs that is not hunger, not entirely, but a kind of map folded too many times.

when spring opens its green mouth, come down. we will walk the length of water and bargain with tomorrow.

your sister,

Ruth
