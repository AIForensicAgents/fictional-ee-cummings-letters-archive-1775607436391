on the quiet engine of night

Date: 1900-04-22
Recipient: Mr. Tobias Finch
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1900
Themes: friendship, industry, insomnia, city, yearning

Dear Tobias,

it is late enough that even the clocks seem to whisper through their teeth. the trains drag sleep across the yards in great metal blankets and i lie wakeful, counting the soft punches of distance. somewhere a whistle finds the perfect ache of a blue note and i forgive it everything.

you asked how the city feels in the ribs. it is a stove you stand too near because the cold behind you is a wall. sparks lift from the factories and attempt a clumsy starlight. the river wears a black sleeve and keeps its hands to itself.

i think of our small schoolroom where chalk dust rose like snow from your cuffs and the map of the world curled at its edges like a cat. once you said the future is not a sermon, it is a question that cannot stop wagging its tail. i am trying to pet that question without getting bitten.

send me any softness you can spare. a word. a sentence with boots off. i will trade you a city of windows for a single green hill.

affectionately,

Evan
