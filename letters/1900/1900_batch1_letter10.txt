last orchard letter

Date: 1900-12-12
Recipient: Nathaniel B.
Location: Montpelier, Vermont
Year: 1900
Themes: seasons, friendship, home, aging, silence

Dear Nat,

the orchard is a book that has turned to its winter chapter. ladders lean and dream. the trees hold their arms like old men practicing sermons to a small fond wind. i have counted the barrels and the checks and the thin coins and find the math both honest and unpersuasive.

do you remember when we believed the apples were lanterns we could eat. i bit and the juice made an argument i could not answer. now the rows are a hymn of sticks and the ground is busy inventing sleep.

my body has a new whistle in the knees when i climb the hill. i am not complaining, only noticing. noticing has become my trade. sunrise takes longer to make up its mind and i forgive it. the stove is a bishop with bright red lungs.

come for Christmas if the roads behave. bring the fiddle. we will play the thin silver music that lets the cold listen without envy. if you cannot come, write. the house has room for your handwriting.

yours in frost and cider steam,

Abel
