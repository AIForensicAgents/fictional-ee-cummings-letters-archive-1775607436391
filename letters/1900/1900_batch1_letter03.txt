lantern between raindrops

Date: 1900-03-18
Recipient: Miss Colette Duval
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1900
Themes: art, rain, longing, city, awakening

Chere Colette,

today the city wore pearls that the sky forgot to fasten. i crossed the bridge and the Seine rehearsed a hundred small applauses against the quay. umbrellas budded everywhere, sudden flowers with black petals, and i felt unreasonably tender toward each stranger moving like a note along the staff of the boulevard.

your studio, with its murmuring canvases, has taken root in me. colors climb my thoughts like ivy, insisting there are doors i have not opened. i fear and love the door called now. my hand shakes when it knocks. my hand is a lantern between raindrops.

you told me last night that a face is a city and the eyes are its windows and the mouth a narrow square where festivals begin. since then every passerby is a procession. even the baker, dusted with flour as if with new snow, looked capable of revolution and mercy.

if i could, i would paint your name on the inside of weather, so it would fall around me in a quiet storm that does not wet the coat but soaks the heart.

forgive the excess. my english misbehaves when it is happy.

avec ferveur,

M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
