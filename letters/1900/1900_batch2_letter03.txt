Lilacs by the Window

Date: 1900-05-12
Recipient: Mother
Location: Concord, New Hampshire
Year: 1900
Themes: home, spring, domesticity, loneliness, tenderness

Dearest Mother,
this morning the lilacs leaned in like old friends gossiping through the window latch. Their breath is sugar and rain. I set your blue bowl where the light collects, and it filled itself politely, as if accustomed to company finer than mine.

I mend by the clock and by the birds. Needles are a kind of quiet thunder; they take storms apart and resew them into sleeves. I have patched Father's cuffs with a patience that is half prayer, half arithmetic. It comforts me to count, to gather raveled minutes and knot them again where they slipped.

There was a brass band on Main Street, and a boy too young for his drum. He tumbled along behind the music like a hat with its own wind. We followed until the bakery, then surrendered to the honest arguments of cinnamon. I saved the freshest roll for you, which is to say I ate it tenderly and told it your name.

Do not be alarmed by my small loneliness. It is a bird I carry, not a stone. At night it folds itself under my ribs and warms me; sometimes it sings. When I am bold I sing with it, softly, so the lamp will not suspect.

Send a line when you can. Tell me whether the lilac by the gate forgives the frost. Tell me whether you have slept entirely through a night yet. I will practice the art of being exactly here until you answer.

Your daughter,
Elinor

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
