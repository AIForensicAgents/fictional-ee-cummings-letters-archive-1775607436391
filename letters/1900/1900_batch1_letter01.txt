a small thunder in afternoon light

Date: 1900-01-11
Recipient: Ada Merriweather
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1900
Themes: love, winter, city, longing, intimacy

Dear Ada,

the city has buttoned its gray coat and even the cobbles seem to think slowly. i carry your last letter against my breast like a shy bird, feeling that soft little thunder of ink when the wind turns a corner. horses exhale lace. shop windows practice being moons.

i told the day i would not live without you and the day shrugged, slipped one pale sun across the harbor, then put it away again as if it were a brooch for better weather. still, somewhere between the telegraph's tin heartbeat and the snow's careful handwriting, your name rises like a lamp behind my ribs.

what i want is scandalously small and frighteningly large. to pour tea for you, and spill some, and laugh. to open a window with you and let the afternoon climb in, boots and all. to say simplest words like bread and chair and home until they bloom into cathedral bells.

bring your glad gloves when you come. bring your forgettings and your little storms. i will sweep a path through these narrow rooms until even the dust says your syllables.

until then, i am

yours, terribly awake,

J.
