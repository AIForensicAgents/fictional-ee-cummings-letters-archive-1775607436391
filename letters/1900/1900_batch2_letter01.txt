Small Rain for Lydia

Date: 1900-03-21
Recipient: Lydia Marsh
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1900
Themes: longing, rain, city, distance, intimacy

Dear Lydia,
today the rain graduates from whisper to violin and every street wears a gray shawl. I have walked beneath the heavy-lidded sky until my boots learned the puddles by name, and the city, for once, remembered it has a pulse.

I think of your window on Beacon Hill, the modest plants you feed with teaspoons of talk. Here the lamplighter wades, wick by wick, into evening; his pole is a thin mast and each flame hoists a flag of warmth. The East River is a sleeve of moving pewter; gulls stitch little commas in the damp air, as if they would pause the world so I might write you, then breathe.

What foolish hope, that two hands and a pen might carry the weight of you. Yet hope insists, a bright stubborn button at my throat. When I close my eyes I hear your laugh like a small bell in a pocket. Between us lie miles of timetables and respectable weather, and also this: the soft ungovernable country where your name becomes mine to keep.

Let us be extravagant in quiet ways. Keep a cup on the sill to catch the first clear drop. I will send you a sprig of rain folded into an envelope; when you open it, stand close, and if the room trembles a little, know it is my heart arriving on tiptoe.

Ever your N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
