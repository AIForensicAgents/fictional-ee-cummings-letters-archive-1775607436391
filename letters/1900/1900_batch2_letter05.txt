Midwinter Letter to a Future Home

Date: 1900-12-28
Recipient: Elsbeth Rooke
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1900
Themes: future, love, sea, hope, homebuilding

My Elsbeth,
the Pacific keeps the clock in its lungs and lets it out in fog. Nights, the horn speaks one syllable over and over until the hills agree to sleep. I have learned to answer with a candle, a spoon, a patience that tastes like salt.

I walk the wharf where ropes dream of ladders. Men with faces the wind has already read lift crates of oranges that smell like small suns. The city surprises itself with gold at four o'clock; every window is a confession. I stand quite still and let the light say what I cannot yet build.

We will have a house with a door that laughs, and the sea will borrow it on Tuesdays. In the yard, an apricot that remembers snow. Inside, a table broad enough for all of our maps and any sudden mercy. I will place your chair where morning begins; you will hide your thimble in the hems of my silence and find it there every time.

There is work here for honest hands and a future that wears its hat at a cheerful tilt. Come when the year turns. Bring your winter; we will teach it to swim. Until then, I send you this city folded twice, with a piece of my breath between the paper and the truth.

Yours as the tide is,
Martin

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
