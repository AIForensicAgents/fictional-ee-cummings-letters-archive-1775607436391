garden with a clock inside

Date: 1900-05-14
Recipient: Mother
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1900
Themes: family, spring, home, memory, time

Dear Mother,

the azaleas are rehearsing their blushes and every porch has a rumor of lemonade. mornings begin with the polite racket of birds discussing eternity. i sweep the steps as if welcoming a parade that may or may not arrive, and there is satisfaction in the small minister of bristles and dust.

you would like the way the camphor trees keep their green promises while the live oaks dangle long syllables of shade. time, in this house, is a gentle housemaid who hums and forgets to wind the clock until noon. i forgive her because i, too, forget. your thimble remains in the saucer where sunlight practices grace.

my life is made of chores with a soul. i polish the kettle and it behaves like a moon. i fold linens and they recall wind from a summer we thought would not end. sometimes i sit by the window doing nothing that can be measured and yet my heart is busy as a bee that has misplaced its map.

if your bones ache, send me the ache and i will write over it with spring.

your daughter,

Martha
