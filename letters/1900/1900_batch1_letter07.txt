steam and lilacs

Date: 1900-07-19
Recipient: Editor at Large
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1900
Themes: ambition, art, city, summer, audacity

Sir or Madam,

i send you this narrow parcel of words from a tenement that sweats like a horse at noon. the city has put its mouths to the hydrants and children invent rivers barefoot. above us the clotheslines preach democracy to shirts of every station. i am not trying to be novel. i am trying to be awake with my pen.

some sentences arrive with their cuffs properly buttoned. others burst in like cousins after a long ride and kiss everybody twice. i keep both kinds because the heart does. if my pages misbehave, it is out of love for the world which keeps leaning too far out of the window.

you are busy measuring columns and taming commas. i ask only a narrow corner of your day to let these small animals run. if they break a teacup, i will pay with a better teacup made of breath.

in exchange i offer a city seen from underneath, where the steam rises and the lilacs attempt a stubborn perfume against the soot. print them if you dare. refuse them if you must. either way, i remain a citizen of the sentence.

with reckless respect,

P. Alcott

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
