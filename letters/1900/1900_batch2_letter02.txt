Between Trains (a hinge of smoke)

Date: 1900-06-07
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1900
Themes: travel, family, restlessness, trains, uncertainty

Dear Tom,
the station is a cathedral of iron where shoes pray in sparks and the clock, stern as any bishop, blesses every late soul with a frown. Smoke writes its dark cursive up the great ribs of the roof. I sit between departures, a suitcase warming my knees like a small obedient animal.

Do you remember the creek behind the mill, the way we taught leaves to race and called it science. The same river now hides itself in these tracks, slick with yesterday, shouldering west. I am not brave, Brother, but I am restless, which is a cousin of courage and easier to feed.

Men speak of business in square voices; a porter hums a hymn that remembers somebody's kitchen. A child counts pillars as if to keep the world from coming apart. In such arithmetic I locate our family, bead by bead. Mother pretends not to watch the door; Father pretends to trust the weather. We are all practicing.

When my train arrives I will try not to stare at the future as if it might flinch. If I fail, forgive me. If I succeed, forgive me also. Write to me at Omaha or any town that has a hotel and a stubborn moon. Pack this blessing for me: that what I find will not be what I fear, and what I keep will be small enough to carry home.

Your sister,
Ada

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
