windmills of dust

Date: 1900-10-27
Recipient: Dr. Hassan al-Nuri
Location: Cairo, Egypt
Year: 1900
Themes: travel, wonder, letters, desert, learning

Honored Doctor,

i have been apprenticed to the desert for three weeks and remain ignorant in charming ways. the wind here is an alphabet that refuses to sit still. dunes rise and bow like scholars of a stern and generous school. at night the stars embroider the tent with a patience that makes my breath slow until it almost learns their craft.

your note advised me to drink shade as if it were water. i have done so under a fig tree that remembered a story it would not tell. camels regard my urgency with the old humor of mountains that move.

in the market today i bought a small brass astrolabe whose metal contains yesterday's thumbprints. it reminds me that we are always mapping, always a little lost and happily so, for the map is the excuse to go on.

when i return i will bring dates, and a jar of the sweet grit of this place, and a new quiet i am practicing between sentences. it is a good quiet that does not argue with footsteps.

with gratitude for your guiding ink,

Thomas L.
