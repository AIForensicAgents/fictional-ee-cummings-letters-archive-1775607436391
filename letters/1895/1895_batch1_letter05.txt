a candle argues with night

Date: 1895-05-30
Recipient: Reverend Elijah Ward
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1895
Themes: faith, doubt, hope, light, introspection

Reverend Ward,

I write from a room where the wallpaper has long ago decided to be vines. The evening presses its shoulder against my window, and my one candle stands like an idea that refuses to sit down. I am trying to pray without telling God what to do; it is difficult. My wants have large hats and push to the front of the line.

On Sunday your sermon said that the heart must be a harbor for mercy. I went home and found only small docks, listing, shy. Yet tonight something opened, perhaps the hinge on a quiet door. I remembered the day the river carried a branch past us, and how you said even driftwood is on its way to usefulness.

If faith is a house, mine has too many windows and no latch. The wind comes in gossiping and leaves with my composure. Still, when the choir reached that low soft note (where every sorrow hides its face), I felt inside me what I can only call a yes without edges.

Forgive the clumsy metaphors. Language behaves rowdily when asked to carry the soul. I mean simply this: the dark is not winning. The candle argues, and the argument is beautiful.

Humbly,
Ruth

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
