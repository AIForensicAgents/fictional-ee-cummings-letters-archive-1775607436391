the trembling of a first book

Date: 1895-10-04
Recipient: Mr. Jasper Hale, Bookseller
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1895
Themes: art, ambition, nervousness, city, letters

Mr. Hale,

Enclosed please find a manuscript that has kept me awake like an immoderate moon. I have sanded its rough places with ordinary courage and polished a few sentences on my sleeve until they agreed to look like themselves. The title may be too earnest; tell me and I shall dress it in subtler shoes.

I have watched customers in your shop lift a book and listen a moment (as if testing a seashell for inland oceans). That is what I wish of this little volume: that it whisper of large weather, that it turn a stranger's pocket into a small geography.

If you will consider it, I can promise punctual poverty, enthusiastic revisions, and a gratitude that breeds more of itself. The city is kind today: magnolia insisting upon perfume, a trumpet practicing on Dauphine Street, a child convincing a puddle it is a mirror. It seems mean not to meet such kindness with a try.

If you decline, kindly do so quickly so I may continue to love your windows without embarrassment. If you accept, write soon and I will bring you a basket of figs and my best blush.

Your obedient and hopeful,
Clara Beaumont

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
