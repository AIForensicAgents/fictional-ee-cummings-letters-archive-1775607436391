address left blank (for the road)

Date: 1895-12-12
Recipient: Self (left behind)
Location: Reykjavík, Iceland
Year: 1895
Themes: journey, solitude, north, identity, acceptance

Dear You,

I have come as far north as my questions allow. The wind rehearses its single word upon the harbor and the mountains keep a grammar of stone I will not pretend to master. There is a room where the stove teaches red to the dark, and I sit with a cup that is only steam and listening.

Today I walked upon a road that preferred to be a silence. Footprints arranged themselves behind me like an argument I had already lost. Even so, I felt lighter with each step, as if certainty were a coat I could put down for weather to borrow.

Do you remember the list we made of all we could not carry? It was longer than our arms and shorter than our courage. I have carried only this: a pencil, a name that fits some days, and the habit of stopping when light feels fragile.

If you receive this, it means I have not forgotten where to place my own voice. Forgive the long delay. Some distances are not in miles and do not own maps. When I return, I will bring a small rock that believed itself eternal and then agreed to be pocket-sized. We will set it on the mantle and call it compromise.

Be kind to the mirror. Stand where the window says morning and practice yes.

With the warm truth of cold air,
I
