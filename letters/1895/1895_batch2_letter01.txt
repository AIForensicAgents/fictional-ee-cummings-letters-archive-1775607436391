Snowlight, or the way your name opens

Date: 1895-02-17
Recipient: Aurelia Hart
Location: Brooklyn, New York
Year: 1895
Themes: longing, winter, intimacy, urban night, promise

My aurelia—

tonight the snow is a quiet alphabet teaching the streets how to whisper, and i, your most unruly pupil, keep mispronouncing your name with my breath against the window. every flake proposes a different spelling. some are bold & crystalline, some melt as if their one desire were to become the little river between us.

i walked beneath the gaslamps, and each lamp made a small cathedral of its halo (i knelt, briefly, to the ordinary miracle of a horse steaming like a samovar). in that warmed cloud i hid a syllable of you and carried it home: your laugh, the belltone one, which turns the narrow room broader than any boulevard. you are my avenue of lanterns; i stroll, glove to glove with the idea of you, and (oh) my heart breaks into applause like a parlor of startled fans.

the neighbors are playing a hymn with shut windows. i prefer your little pagan wrists, their bird-quick tremble when surprised; do you remember the rain two mondays past? how your parasol became a moon, and you, moon-keeper. i would write to that rain to return at once, but snow is the present king, and i, i am loyal to any weather that sends you letters in hush.

say yes to me when the crocuses make their small brave inventions. say yes not as a cathedral but as a pocket (where i may reside, lint-happy, coin-impatient, a button among secrets). i will build for you a season of kindly mischief: pastry for breakfast & sunlight smuggled into drawers, and foolishness every evening at seven o'clock sharp.

i close my eyes to see you better. (see how you open them for me.)

faithfully, wildly,
josiah
