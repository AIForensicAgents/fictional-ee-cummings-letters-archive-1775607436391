Cinders of the aurora (and other private combustions)

Date: 1895-12-01
Recipient: Professor Artemisia Crane
Location: Reykjavík, Iceland
Year: 1895
Themes: science, awe, nature, loneliness, discovery

Esteemed Professor—artemisia if i may—

this island breathes audibly. when the kettle sings, the ground answers from its own hidden lungs, and i, twice warmed, attempt science with mittens on. last night the sky unbuttoned herself northward and walked about the fields in green scandal. i had promised the villagers a lecture on magnetism, but we all forgot our grammar and stared until our eyes learned to swim.

you asked for observations, not rhapsodies. very well: the compass wandered as if gossiping, then stood at attention when the wind changed minds. the snow, rewritten by light, refused to remain white—i recorded olives, limes, and once a theatrical pink. a fox crossed the slope without consulting me; his tail made punctuation i struggle to translate. i believe it was a semicolon of hunger.

i have fallen slightly in love with the postmaster’s daughter, which, you will grant, is acceptable fieldwork; the heart is an instrument, too, though it seldom keeps time. she laughed at my awkward boots and taught me the word for lava cooled into patience. say it with me: hraun. i carry it in my mouth like flint.

if you can spare a coil of better wire, send it north by the slowest possible miracle. my battery is indecisive and the telegraph key sulks. still, when i tap gently, Reykjavík flies along the line like a sparrow who has seen the sea and forgives it for being enormous.

i hope your lectures are attended by both skeptics and believers, those excellent twins who argue us toward a brighter lantern. keep a chair for me by the radiator; i will return when the dark finishes its long poem.

obediently (and otherwise),
august
