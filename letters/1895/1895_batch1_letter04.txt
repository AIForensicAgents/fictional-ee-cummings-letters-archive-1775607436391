green grammar of a leaf

Date: 1895-04-15
Recipient: Mr. Lin Bao
Location: Kyoto, Japan
Year: 1895
Themes: nature, botany, friendship, patience, letters

Dear Lin,

The ginkgo in the temple courtyard has taught me a politeness the dictionaries forgot. Each leaf bows in a language I almost understand: a thin yes against the morning, a small no against the storm, and a perfect maybe when the wind can not make up its mind.

I carry your last letter between my notebook pages and now everything I write smells faintly of tea. You asked how the moss arranges itself upon the stone. Patiently, like a monk dressing. The stone does not argue. I tried to argue less today and felt myself grow.

I am sending, wrapped inside this paper, the smallest fan of bamboo (plucked from the edge of a path where the stream confesses) and a sketch of the garden in which clouds practice stillness. If it arrives broken, keep the pieces. Some things want to be plural to speak clearly.

You once said botany is only love with better posture. I smiled at the time; now I bow back to your wisdom. When we meet again, let us trade specimens: I will give you the leaf that chose my pocket, you will give me the seed that learned my name.

Until then, breathe like the tree breathes (deep, unhurried, and entirely now).

Your friend,
Sato
