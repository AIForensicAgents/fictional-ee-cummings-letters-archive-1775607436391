harborheart (unmoored)

Date: 1895-03-23
Recipient: Captain Mireille Dupré
Location: Marseille, France
Year: 1895
Themes: sea, departure, desire, waiting, promise

Mireille,

The harbor keeps your footprint the way a shell keeps thunder. Ropes dream of your hands and wake as knots. I stand where the gulls stitch the air with their ragged needles and try to read a sky that cannot be taught to sit still.

You sailed at noon, precise as a violin beginning. The quay applauded with salt and rumor. I swallowed a coin of sun and it keeps turning in me; perhaps this is courage, perhaps merely the sea imagining itself inland.

Do you remember the map we drew upon the table, our coffee the ocean, our spoons, ships? I laughed and you said hush: storms listen for their names. Now I hush because your name is a compass. It turns without touching.

When night arrives I will let the dark teach me longitude. I will practice the grammar of waves: if, then; if, then. If you return, then I am a harbor. If you do not, then I must learn to be my own coastline.

Bring me home a pocket of wind. Or a story. They weigh the same, and either will lift me.

With a mouth of salt and an armful of sky,
Henri
