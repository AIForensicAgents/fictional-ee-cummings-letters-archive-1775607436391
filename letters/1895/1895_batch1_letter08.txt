monsoon measures in G minor

Date: 1895-08-28
Recipient: Miss Sushila Mukherjee
Location: Calcutta, British India
Year: 1895
Themes: music, rain, yearning, city, friendship

Dear Sushila,

The rain arrived with a conductor's stern nod and the whole afternoon obeyed. Rickshaws turned into small boats with wheels and the veranda stretched its spine to listen. I set the metronome beside the open window and found the monsoon already keeping time. We worked together: I struck G minor; the sky supplied the drums.

Your last visit left jasmine in the stairwell, and it lingers (the way a kindness lingers when no one admits to needing it). I practiced the étude you marked with that brave circle; my fingers stumbled, then remembered they had knees. We curtsy to the difficult so it knows we are not afraid.

Tell your aunt that the ceiling fan still refuses ordinary behavior, insisting on its personal philosophy of motion. I like its sincerity. When I grow stubborn, I pretend to be a ceiling fan and continue anyway.

The street boys have made small navy fleets from paper and are colonizing puddles with delighted flags. If I were less respectable I would join their war and lose gloriously.

Return soon with your green umbrella and the laughter that opens as easily. We will play until the rain becomes an audience and the kettles applaud.

Always,
Ananya

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
