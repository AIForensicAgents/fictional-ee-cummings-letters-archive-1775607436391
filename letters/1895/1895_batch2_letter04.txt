The river writes in moonmilk

Date: 1895-09-10
Recipient: Captain Eli Booker
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1895
Themes: river, labor, music, loyalty, forgiveness

Captain—

the levee is a choir of ropes and patience. tonight the river has exchanged her bustle for a white nightgown and wanders, barefoot, past the warehouses. how shall i report her behavior? she is guilty of every gentle crime: persuading a barrel to dream of orchards, borrowing the moon without permission, forgiving men who do not deserve such mercy.

i sorted the manifests at dusk. your handwriting, which i have known longer than some prayers, goes stern at the corners when whiskey is mentioned, then softens around coffee and salt beef as if the letters had teeth satisfied by chewing. i confess a mischief: i tucked, inside the crew’s packet, a yellow leaf that resembles a small map of our failures. perhaps when they open it, the men will consider where not to return.

the band plays somewhere behind me—cornet blushing, drum considering its future. i do not dance; i practice motion by watching water. you taught me that: a captain’s first waltz is with current, second with wind, third with luck if she will have him. have you noticed how late summer moths carry lanterns so tiny only sorrow sees by them?

you asked if i will sign on again when the fog behaves. i answer with my thumb, stained by tar: yes, provided we carry something light as forgiveness. i am tired of freight that insults the back. let us move nothing heavier than a widow’s letter, a parcel of biscuits, a violin in a narrow box. failing that, let us at least sing at the work until it begins to believe it is joy.

keep the bow pointed tenderly.
jabez

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
