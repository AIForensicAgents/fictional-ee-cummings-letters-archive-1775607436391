our hearts ride the new electric

Date: 1895-06-18
Recipient: Madame Celeste Armand
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1895
Themes: modernity, love, city, art, electricity

Celeste,

This afternoon the trolley took me from the gray to the bright in a single spark. The city now speaks in wires and blushes in lamps; everything believes in a swifter morning. I kept your scarf knotted at my throat and thought: we are both inventions, perfected by motion.

In the gallery a woman stood before a field made of bruised blue and called it wheat. I adored her insolence. A boy with ink on his fingers explained perspective to the air. I bought apricots that tasted like a rumor of August and ate them in the square while a violinist stitched the day together with thin gold thread.

I want to make a life with you that hums the way the new lines hum beneath the street, invisible yet undeniable. Let us be as improper as possibility. Take my hand at the crossing where the traffic forgets its manners and trust that my body remembers how to lean toward you at exactly the speed of our courage.

Tonight, when the lamps conduct their private orchestra, stand by the window and think of how every current seeks its home. I am a wire singing your name. Come Monday, bring paint under your nails and laughter in your pockets. We will scandalize the ordinary.

Your reckless Auguste
