snow writes us slowly

Date: 1895-01-07
Recipient: Miss Ada Montgomery
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1895
Themes: love, winter, city, longing, letters

Dear Ada,

The snow is writing the city very slowly tonight, letter by slow-falling letter. Every lamp becomes a small moon in its own puddle and the streets keep their breath like children hiding in the dark. I walked to the corner where your laughter last happened and, yes, it is still warm there; someone must have dropped a promise and forgot to pick it up.

Your last note arrived with the morning coal, smudged with its own urgency. I read it by the stove and felt my ribs opening like a small theater curtain; I think a bird lives there, and your words were seed. If you place your hand upon the window just now you will feel mine (thin as a candle, brave as a flame). We touch through glass, and glass is a kind winter.

Let us be scandalously patient. The world uses hurry like a cheap umbrella, but you and I may prefer to be rained upon. When spring rehearses, we will walk to the Common and let the thaw teach us mercy; two citizens of sunlight, astonished to be nearly ourselves.

Do not be lonely in your red shawl. Fold it twice and imagine that the extra fold is my arm. Breathe deliberately. Pin this cold to the door and call it January; we have outlived worse names.

Ever your J., who listens for your step inside the ticking of the clock

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
