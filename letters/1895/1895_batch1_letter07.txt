pocketful of comets

Date: 1895-07-21
Recipient: Rowan (my small astronomer)
Location: Edinburgh, Scotland
Year: 1895
Themes: parenthood, imagination, distance, night, comfort

Dear Rowan,

Here is how to fall asleep when your father is away: first, put the moon in your pocket. It is smaller than it looks when one is brave. Second, teach your toes to count sheep in their own language (baa becomes hush becomes yes). Third, remember that inside your left shoe there lives a very polite comet that knows the road to my door.

I saw a train today run like a silver fox along the hillside. I waved my hat and it whistled back, which proves that machines have manners when greeted properly. The heather wore its purple like a secret it could not keep. I brought you a pebble that thinks it is a mountain; if you listen closely, you can hear it practicing thunder.

Your mother says you took apart the clock and put it together wrong on purpose so the mornings arrive at noon. That is a magician's wisdom and I approve. Time needs our help to be interesting.

When the wind complains, tuck it under your blanket. When the dark grows too large, lend it a small story. I will return before your tallest yawn. Until then, sleep with your fists opening slowly like small brave flowers.

Kiss the dog for me, and do not forget the comet.

All my love,
Papa

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
