A small rebellion sewn in blue thread

Date: 1895-06-21
Recipient: Madame Estelle Rivière
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1895
Themes: independence, craft, city life, mentorship, subtle defiance

Madame—dear estelle—

i am writing between the hem and the heartbeat. the shop is a pond of silk; our needles are dragonflies. madame lamotte is away at luncheon, flirting with her veal, so the mannequins stand unchaperoned, and the mirrors practice honesty. i have taken the liberty—a little one, blue as a pocket—of adding to the Countess’s bodice three stitches that mean freedom. they are invisible unless you look with hunger.

you taught me to cut on the bias, to bid satin keep its temper, to measure twice the circumference of a woman’s secret wishes. what instrument is more faithful than a tape that begins at zero and ends at breath? men will say dresses are obedient; dresses know better. see how they learn a woman’s courage from the inside out, and carry it (button by bright button) into the cruel afternoon.

the city clatters, but up here swallows write cursive above the courtyards. i practice their alphabet with my scissors. snip: a vowel. snip: an argument. i confess, today i refused a gentleman’s order. he wanted a hunting jacket cut against the grain of kindness. i told him our cloth is brave, not violent. he blinked—how frenchly—and left his coin like a small apology on the counter.

tonight i will walk along the river and attempt to stand as beautifully as old bridges do. perhaps i will purchase a peach, and mistake its perfume for a future. estelle, if your health allows, come Sunday and sit by the window. we will drink tea too hot for politeness and mend the world in increments, which is to say, in seams.

yours in prickered thumbs & unthimbleable hopes,
claire

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
