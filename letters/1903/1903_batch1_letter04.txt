Ticket Stub Psalm

Date: 1903-05-29
Recipient: Mr. Amos Reed, Railway Clerk
Location: St. Louis, Missouri
Year: 1903
Themes: travel, yearning, work, chance, machines

Dear Amos,

today the depot was a hymn with a thousand brass throats. i have never seen so many departures trying on the same hat. the timetable fluttered like a tame thunderstorm, and your window, with its little square of authority, shone as if polished by miles.

you clicked your punch as though mending the sky. each hole a small eclipse the paper would recover from in a better shape. i envied that certainty. my days are more like unscheduled stops, a cow on the tracks, a poem in the boiler.

do you ever listen to where a ticket wants to go? i held one between my fingers and felt kansas ache through the pulp, wheat whispering its tall equations. another spoke in a fast boston syllable and asked, very politely, for a clean handkerchief.

i did not ride. i took home only the smell of metal and a stubborn hope that even standing still may someday arrive. on my mantel is a stub for a journey not yet named. i pray over it, mildly and without a church.

should you find a misprinted destination, keep it for me. i would like to visit the town called maybe, platform seven and a half, where goodbyes are returned if the fit is poor.

faithfully,

ruth
