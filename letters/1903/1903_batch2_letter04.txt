letters are small boats

Date: 1903-10-28
Recipient: Mr. August Hart (never sent)
Location: St. Louis, Missouri
Year: 1903
Themes: forgiveness, estrangement, pride, vulnerability, reconciliation

Mr. Hart,

i am practicing how not to begin with anger (though it wears my coat, though it knows where i keep the matches). you taught me the straight back and the hard bargain; also how to fasten my mouth until it squeaks. but a son is not a ledger and balance is not love, however perfectly the columns behave.

yesterday a man at the foundry called me August as if the month were a fist. i answered to it without thinking, as a dog answers to the chain not the name. forgive me: i am cruel because i am afraid of the ordinary tenderness that would unmake me if i let it near. i walked home counting bricks—as if endurance could be arithmetic—then uncounted them one by one until the street remembered how to be a road, not a wall.

what i wanted to say (small as a button, stubborn as one) is that i have kept the pocket-watch you broke and i mended in secret. it stutters, yes, but faithfully. i wind it mornings and pretend i am turning toward you instead of away. some days this works.

should you ever be tired of your throne of silence, there is a chair by my window that forgives every weight. come when the town forgets its noise. we will invent a language with few numbers and many doors.

unposted and still arriving,
Thomas
