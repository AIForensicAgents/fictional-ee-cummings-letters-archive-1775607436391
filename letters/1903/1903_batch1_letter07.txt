Chalkdust & thunder

Date: 1903-09-03
Recipient: Miss Ada Bell, Schoolteacher
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1903
Themes: education, storm, order, admiration, childhood

Miss Bell,

the afternoon arrived carrying a storm like a violin case. inside, thunder practiced its scales while your pupils recited the multiplication table (a separate weather entirely). chalk marked the board in little drifts, and your sleeve became a pale coastline against the dark.

i sat at the back pretending to be a tall question. the children, brave ships, sailed between your commas and came safely to equals. one boy sneezed the angel out of him and you said bless you with the authority of a lighthouse.

what i mean is: you performed a small miracle with fractions while lightning tried to divide the sky in half. no one screamed. a girl with a seashell ribbon solved her problem so gently i believed in the mercy of numbers.

when the bell released everyone, the rain forgot rage and remembered garden. i walked home with a white thumb from the chalk you lent me. already the mark fades, but the hand remembers obedience, the soft click of truth when a sum agrees.

please keep my pencil on your desk, the bitten end forward. it longs for more answers and perhaps for your voice when you say almost and mean try again.

gratefully,

thomas

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
