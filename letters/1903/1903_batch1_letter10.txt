The Thread and the Needle

Date: 1903-11-05
Recipient: Seamstress at the east-facing window
Location: Calcutta, British India
Year: 1903
Themes: craft, observation, city, respect, chance encounter

Madam,

i pass your window each morning when the sun rehearses its gold and the trams conduct their patient clavichords. you do not know me, but i have learned your needle's breath: in, out, a small heartbeat domestic and exact. the cloth rises to meet you as if it had prayers tucked into its hem.

there is a bird that watches from the awning and approves. sometimes the street unrolls a red carpet of dust, sometimes a blue of shade; always your hands are two clear rivers going somewhere important. you stitch a city together and no one stops to thank you because the seam is so perfect it disappears.

i carry a tear in my coat that refuses money. it wants your attention, which is worth an empire when concentrated. i could leave the coat; i could leave my day; i could leave this letter and return with the courage to say please.

the markets here speak seventeen fragrances at once. i grow dizzy and must sit on a thought until it quiets. you seem immune, anchored by thimble and will. teach me this. i am tired of unraveling.

if the monsoon comes early, close your window and let rain perform its fever outside. you will still hear the rhythm. your needle has its own weather and it is kind.

with respect from a stranger,

d.
