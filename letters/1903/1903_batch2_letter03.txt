the shore writes your name with gulls

Date: 1903-07-19
Recipient: Sister Nell
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1903
Themes: family, grief, sea, memory, longing

dear Nell,

the shore keeps writing your name with gulls, then letting the tide revise it into foam. perhaps this is how memory prays: not with certainty, but with a thousand small surrenders. i brought the old shawl; it still holds a weather of lavender, and when the wind turns inland i swear mother is almost here, nearly at the porch, her key already forgiving the lock.

i am learning the dialect of boats. each rope says wait; each bell says come; and between them the harbor breathes like a patience we might trust. last night i sat on the damp steps and told the dark our stupid brave plans: how we will mend the roof, how we will teach the piano to forgive our clumsy hands, how we will set a place at supper for what is missing and pour it tea.

do you remember the fair where we spent our courage on a tin lark that refused to sing? i keep that silence in my pocket now, as if it were a coin i might give the future when it is poor. i am not sad, exactly. more like a window the rain forgets to leave.

write soon, with your crisp, sensible ink that never smudges. tell me you have found a way to sleep without bargaining. tell me the ache has learned its letters and can be sent, correctly addressed, to the sea.

with all my weather,
R.
