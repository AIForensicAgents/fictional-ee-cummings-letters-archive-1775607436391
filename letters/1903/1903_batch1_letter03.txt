The small rebellion of violets

Date: 1903-04-15
Recipient: Mother
Location: Cincinnati, Ohio
Year: 1903
Themes: spring, family, home, economy, language

Dearest Mother,

today the yard invented spring without asking anyone's permission. i stepped onto the porch and a committee of violets had already passed the motion. the thumb of sun pressed its warm seal upon our doorknob; i held it and felt signed.

you should see how the laundry declares its white flags from the line, surrendering to a breeze that knows no war. the neighbor's baby has a laugh like sleigh bells misplaced in april, and the postman carries rain on his cap as if it were a medal.

your last letter touched my cheek with its careful grammar. you have always stitched the world into sentences that do not come undone. i, meanwhile, make small rebellions with commas and whisper too many parentheses (they are only little rooms where tired thoughts may sit a while).

do not worry at the thinness of my purse. the pantry is inventive, and the grocer lets me pay with stories now and then. sometimes i give him the one about father and the apple barrel, and he charges me two pennies less for remembering well.

take care of your blue glass vase. it keeps the winter bottled, and when you lift it to the window the whole sky pours through like milk.

your wayward and devoted son,

henry
