Lamplight bends across our kitchen

Date: 1903-02-11
Recipient: Mara Ellison
Location: New Bedford, Massachusetts
Year: 1903
Themes: love, domesticity, winter, hope, intimacy

dear Mara,

tonight the kettle invents a little weather of its own; steam climbs the window and writes brief alphabets before melting into our ordinary breath. the lamp bends across our small kingdom—table, crust of bread, your sleeve rolled like a moon at the elbow—and everything important happens quietly.

if the world is a great brass engine grinding days into coins, i will spend them here, one by quiet one, passing each warm minute into your palm. outside, the street is brittle with February, horses etch frost with iron hooves. but you, with your spoon turning sugar into courage, you soften the cold until it remembers how to be water again.

forgive my hurried heart; it knocks as if the door were forever almost opening. when you smiled over the chipped cup, time misplaced its spectacles and could not read the clock. i thought: let us be foolish and keep this lost hour, tuck it with the receipts and onion-skins, brave as a sparrow.

say we will grow the spring in windowboxes, coaxing green from dirt like a secret. say we will argue tenderly over where to hang the calendar, as if months could be persuaded to stay longer by a nail. say your name (softly) and watch the room widen, the walls learn kindness.

yours, which is to say: mine becoming better,
E.
