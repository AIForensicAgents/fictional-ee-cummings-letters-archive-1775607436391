Between timbers & tide

Date: 1903-03-02
Recipient: Mr. Nathaniel Pike, Lighthouse Keeper
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1903
Themes: sea, solitude, work, guidance, modernity

Dear Mr. Pike,

the ocean keeps leaving its handwriting on the pier, and the gulls misread every word. i envy your lamp whose body is a patient star caged in brass. to live where wind erases yesterday and the sea writes it back (only bluer) seems a sturdy sort of prayer.

i have come to think the city is a house that forgot its door. we hammer at windows with our eyes, while some far tower hums a steady vowel of mercy. is that your work, that humming yes which shepherds boats across their private midnights?

last evening i walked the strand and counted the thoughts i did not think. each slat of boardwalk held one, smelling of tar and maybe of forgiveness. the moon was extravagant, a coin too bright to spend, so i hid it under my hat and went home taller by one silver inch.

please teach me how to tend a light without burning my fingers. i can offer in exchange a pocket map of sorrow's shallows and where the driftwood of laughter piles up after a good storm. also, a thermos of coffee that tastes like a harbor remembers.

if the sea coughs, wrap it in your coat. if the sky forgets its name, remind it softly.

with salt in my boots and hope in my sleeve,

marion
