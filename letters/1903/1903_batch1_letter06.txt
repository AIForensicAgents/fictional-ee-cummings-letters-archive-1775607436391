A Quiet Blue Engine

Date: 1903-07-21
Recipient: To the Printer at No. 5
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1903
Themes: printing, art, modernity, craft, voice

Sir,

your presses speak a language i almost understand. when the rollers take their ink, the room inhales; when the form kisses paper, the city briefly behaves. i stood this afternoon in the hot romance of machinery and felt alphabet dust settle on my wrists like a practical blessing.

i have brought a thin sheaf of unruly pages. they refuse to stand in straight military lines. some words insist on leaning, others on whispering, and one scandalous line will not be capitalized even at the start. i ask only that you do not cure them entirely; let their fevers remain readable.

stereotype me lightly, if at all. i want the letters to remember they were once noises inside a human mouth. if they must be caged, let it be in a font with windows. and please choose a blue for the cover that understands rain without becoming it.

in payment i can offer cash of the ordinary sort and also, if you accept barter, a promise that no page will be ashamed of its silence. there are blank spaces which know more than paragraphs. we will not crowd them.

if the press should cough, drape it with the proof and give it tea.

with hopeful ink,

agnes
