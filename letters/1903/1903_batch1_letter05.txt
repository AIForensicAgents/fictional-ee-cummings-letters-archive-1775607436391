On the Lens of Morning

Date: 1903-06-11
Recipient: Sofia Nikolaevna
Location: Odessa, Russian Empire
Year: 1903
Themes: photography, dawn, harbor, patience, love

Sofia,

dawn here arrives on soft wooden feet, careful not to wake the harbor. the cranes bow like tall gray women and lift the sun in polite spoonfuls over the water. i have borrowed a camera whose eye is gentler than mine and have taught it to drink light without spilling.

people stand for me and become suddenly true, as if the pose releases a secret rope and all their days ring from an invisible bell. a fishmonger hid his smile behind a beard of salt; the plate caught it anyway and set it like a coin where i could not spend it.

your letters arrive with the itinerary of swallows. inside them i find shawls and thistles and, once, a small argument with god neatly folded. i wear them all. the argument fits best when it rains.

if you were here we would walk the steps that descend like a polite waterfall from the city to the sea. calves would scold, lungs would applaud, and at the bottom we would buy cherries that taste like a first oath said under a window at midnight.

i am learning the noble patience of exposure. too quick and the day is a blur; too long and the heart burns a hole in itself. pray for my shutter finger.

ever,

lev
