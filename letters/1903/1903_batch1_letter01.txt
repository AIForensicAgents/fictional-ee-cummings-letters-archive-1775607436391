An Envelope of Rain

Date: 1903-01-07
Recipient: Miss Eliza Hart
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1903
Themes: longing, winter, urban, intimacy, memory

Dear Eliza,

this morning boston stooped under a gray shawl and called it weather. i walked to the post with shoes full of yesterday and pockets full of your name (it rustled like folded birds). the streetcars hissed their little steams, and the baker's window rained sugared moons upon the glass. i remembered the first afternoon we pretended not to meet, and how that pretending unbuttoned the world.

i have kept your letter in my left coat, near the bewildered heart. when i take it out the paper warms, as if it had its own small stove and a chair beside it for my breath. words lean toward me, thin as needles, but gentle: so i let them sew me together.

everyone says january is tidy with its cold, but i think it spills everywhere. even in my tea the spoon rings a tiny bell of distance. what an orchestra longing is (piccolo of wrists, drum of spine, cymbal of sudden silence) and i, poor conductor, wave a glove at empty air.

write to me with the rain you keep. tell me the color of the lamplight on your desk. i will answer with the exact sound my window makes when the wind decides to be your voice.

ever your unbuttoned,

m.
