Paris postcard without a picture

Date: 1903-10-18
Recipient: Monsieur Lucien Moreau
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1903
Themes: art, city, friendship, autumn, bohemia

Lucien,

the boulevard wears its autumn like a velvet bruise. painters argue with their brushes on every corner and the cafes steam up the glass with philosophical cattle. i carry a notebook that keeps pawing the ground and refuses to be fed anything but accidents.

yesterday we watched a woman peel an orange so slowly the day fell asleep and did not notice night take its chair. the peel came off in one operatic breath; somewhere a violin bowed agreement. i wrote nothing of it and yet my pockets smelled the color of hunger.

you ask about work. it advances, then hides. i throw a sentence into the air and a sparrow sits on it until both are too astonished to fly. somewhere a printer will suffer me. somewhere a reader will forgive.

meet me by the bridge at seven when the lamps pretend to be domestic stars. bring your ridiculous hat; it teaches the river a useful arrogance. we shall drink something brown and cheap and listen to boats pronounce their slow consonants.

if you see the moon, tell her i cannot tonight. i am spoken for by an alley cat and a tune that refuses to end at the right place.

affectionately,

estelle

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
