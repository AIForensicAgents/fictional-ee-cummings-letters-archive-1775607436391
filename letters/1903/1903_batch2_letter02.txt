a train invents morning

Date: 1903-05-03
Recipient: Mr. Ezra Pike
Location: On the 7:10 to Chicago
Year: 1903
Themes: travel, modernity, anticipation, motion, industry

Mr. Pike,

the morning unbuttons itself as the locomotive yanks us out of night. telegraph poles stride the fields like lean punctuation, commas of blackbirds rearranging the sentence of sky. smoke braids the wind; our windows collect it like thumbprints of a hurried god.

i had meant to rehearse figures—quantities, prices, the disciplined march of sums. instead i am counting miles in heartbeats. every whistle loosens another knot in the clock. we thunder across wooden bridges and they answer in ribs, a hollow drum you could dance to if the aisle permitted.

a boy with a newspaper has confessed the headlines are all elbows and no hands. i buy one anyway and fold it into a pillow for my thoughts. wheat, wheat, wheat—an ocean wanting a name; farmhouses blinking as if just woken by the rumor of cities.

Chicago is gathering itself (i can feel the pull), a high collar of smoke and ambition. when i step down i will pretend i am not small. i will polish my shoes with tomorrow and say the numbers clearly; but for this jostled instant i am simply a passenger, briefly perfect, stitched to the world by rails.

expect me near noon; with contracts in one pocket and a new noise in the other,
J.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
