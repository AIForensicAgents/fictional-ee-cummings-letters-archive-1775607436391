Two hands in winter

Date: 1903-12-01
Recipient: My Brother the Astronomer
Location: Flagstaff, Arizona Territory
Year: 1903
Themes: astronomy, winter, family, wonder, distance

Brother,

how is your cold cathedral of glass and mathematics. does the dome open its dark mouth obediently when you ask and close again like a prayer done properly. i picture you with red fingers, one on the sky's pulse, one on the ledger's calm throat.

here the winter is a slower wolf, padded and polite. the river makes a careful music against its own bones. at night i try to count the stars and always stop at the one that reminds me of you when we were ten and certain.

people talk of mars as if it were a neighbor with a loud clock. i prefer to think it shy, calling across the fence in dusty syllables. if you hear anything, answer with generosity; even planets deserve a kind yes after so much silence.

i have made you mittens from the idea of warmth and the memory of our mother's red yarn. they will not fit but they will try. put them on when the telescope forgets you are human.

write when the comet pauses to change shoes. i will be at the window setting out a saucer of milk for the long night.

your sister,

clara
