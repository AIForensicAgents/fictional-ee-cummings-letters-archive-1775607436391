winter's pocket has a star

Date: 1903-12-14
Recipient: The Future (care of Myself)
Location: Cleveland, Ohio
Year: 1903
Themes: time, self, resilience, wonder, future

to the Future (care of Myself),

snow rehearses the streets into whiteness; even the lamplight speaks softly, as if not to wake what is sleeping underneath. i have had a year like a pocket with a hole: brave coins vanished, and yet my hand keeps finding a warm lint of miracle.

a boy fell on the ice and laughed before he cried—the order matters. a woman sang to a pot of potatoes and they boiled quicker (i swear). i saw a stray dog refuse a thrown crust and choose instead a child’s mitten, carrying it home like a vow. these are my studies, your curriculum.

i do not know if you will own a room with a rug or a sky with an elevator; i do not know if the machines will learn how to hum us to sleep. but i suspect the heart remains analog, wound by touch. if you can, love someone with the recklessness of bread: daily, warm, and disappearing.

i send you a map drawn on the inside of my ribs. if you get lost, listen: the house with laughter in its windows is yours. do not knock. the door has been hoping for you all day.

faithfully in draft,
M.
