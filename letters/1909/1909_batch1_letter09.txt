winter proofs

Date: 1909-12-05
Recipient: Miss Adele Browne
Location: Baltimore, Maryland
Year: 1909
Themes: art, winter, memory, gratitude

Miss Browne,

i received the photographs—those pale arguments with time—and set them along the mantel. each one whispers of a door just out of frame. your hand in the corner of the third print (accident, ornament) is the bravest light.

the season settles with an editor's firmness: margins of frost, sentences of bare branch. i am learning to print my days with clearer inks, to let the shadows be footnotes and not the text. thank you for teaching me where to stand so that ordinary becomes articulate.

i send you a scarf woven by a widow on Pratt Street; there is a patience in it that does not require proof. if you walk by the water wear it loose, and the wind will sign your name with a flourish.

should you desire new plates, i can procure a cheaper silver, though i know how economy bruises art. still—i trust your eye more than my caution.

respectfully (and warmly),

—R.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
