quiet arithmetic of leaves

Date: 1909-11-10
Recipient: Editor of The Lantern
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1909
Themes: art, submission, autumn, hope, vulnerability

esteemed editor,

consider this a small suitcase of poems placed on your doorstep before the city is awake enough to argue. autumn has been explaining subtraction to the trees; i am merely taking notes. each leaf solves itself by letting go, simple and catastrophic as an excellent idea.

i set type by day — a humble ceremony of squares and commas — and by evening i loosen the collars of words until they breathe differently. what i send is not fashionable, perhaps; it wears its pockets inside out so the moonlight can find its change. but fashion never kept anyone warm at three in the morning when the heart insists on counting backwards.

if you must refuse, please do it the way november does: with clarity and a brief, convincing wind. if you can accept, allow the pages to keep their small stammer; it is the only honest sound i make. i have trimmed the manuscripts with care, though one or two lines escaped and are hiding in the margins like shy foxes. do not pursue them too sternly. they will come when called by kindness.

should you require biography: i was born, i am learning to listen, and someday i hope to deserve the word citizen. for now i throw my voice into the air and hope it returns with company.

with respect and a pocket of improbable hope,

a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
