a pocket of steam

Date: 1909-05-03
Recipient: Mr. Samuel Reed
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1909
Themes: city, ambition, friendship, modernity, work

sam,

the city whistles itself awake at five and does not apologize. up rumbles the elevated, iron syllables clanging their early grammar over the heads of newsboys and bakers and my poor coffee which trembles like a small opinion. i stole a ride to fifty-ninth and counted smokestacks for courage, then traded a nickel for a seat in the library where ceiling-fresco cherubs supervise us sternly (one winked, i swear it).

i have found work setting type in a printshop that smells of lead and possibility. each letter is a brick-sized bee and i am the soft field it refuses to obey. by noon my fingers are black with alphabets; they look like cautionary tales, but they open doors. yesterday i held a proof and the paper burned cold in my hand — the way a horizon does, when you realize it is a decision not a distance.

come visit. bring your stubborn laughter and that hat you insist is lucky. we will chase the afternoon to the battery and buy oranges with the last of our bravery. afterward we will stand along the rail and practice not knowing what to say, which i am told is a species of wisdom.

tell me whether the town misses you or only believes it does; tell me how the lilacs in your landlord's yard keep their vows. send along a scrap of quiet if you can spare it. i will return it improved, or at least rumpled with admiration.

your friend under the great uneconomical sky,

n.
