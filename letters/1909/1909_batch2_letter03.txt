moth-prayer for daylight

Date: 1909-06-21
Recipient: Mother
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1909
Themes: family, healing, home, summer, nostalgia

dear mother,

the doctor assures me it is nothing but an argument between my lungs and the weather and that both will soon grow polite. i have chosen to believe him because the morning chose to be beautiful — which seems the better authority. the window is a bright wound that nobody minds; it lets in a summer so green it invents its own grammar. a breeze troubles the curtain and forgives it immediately.

when i close my eyes i am in your kitchen counting raspberries into the blue bowl, each a small confession. you hum that tune with no name while the kettle tries to remember how to sing. do you recall the maple that leans over the lane like a sleepy uncle? i think of its hands cupping shade and the hot dust kneeling there like a prayer come true. if i am mended anywhere it is at that threshold where your apron collects sunlight the way a field gathers bees.

forgive this swerving page. convalescence has a way of teaching words to walk like toddlers, lurching toward an unreasonable joy. i am trying to eat more, to sleep less sadly, to hold time like an egg i must not drop. please do not worry (i have decided not to), and tell father that the river here behaves with suitable decency, although i suspect it dreams of floods.

kiss the cat. keep the jam jar breathing. and leave the back door unlatched for luck.

your child, almost brave,

l.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
