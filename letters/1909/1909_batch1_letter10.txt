after the strike: bread & bells

Date: 1909-11-19
Recipient: C.
Location: Lower East Side, New York, New York
Year: 1909
Themes: labor, hope, solidarity, city

C.,

the streets, today, wore their serious shoes. girls with needles in their pockets turned their throats into banners, and the air learned a new syllable (enough). i watched from a doorway where the rain argued with my hat; even umbrellas felt inadequate to the weather of voices.

there is a breadline that smells like hymnals. a boy offered me a match and said, sir, keep it for when the dark forgets your name. i am ashamed of how my hands shook. courage is a small currency; we spend it mostly on ourselves. but when a bell rang three o'clock, the sound untied a knot somewhere under my ribs and light came out, not politely.

if you are safe, write me. if you are not, write twice. i have two ears and a clumsy pair of shoes that will find you. let us learn a hunger that feeds rather than consumes.

—A. (who believes in the common table)
