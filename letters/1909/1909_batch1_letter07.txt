lantern republic

Date: 1909-09-03
Recipient: Father
Location: Keene, New Hampshire
Year: 1909
Themes: family, work, guidance, light

Father,

i finished sanding the table; its surface now remembers trees in a kinder tense. i think of your hands—those two quiet commandments—lifting a hinge into its future. at dusk the porch becomes a parliament of moths; they vote for light with their soft bodies (unanimous).

you once said a man should carry a lantern inside his chest in case the world becomes a cellar. i have tried to warm my lantern with work, with books too heavy for their ideas, with the simplest argument: that bread and mercy are cousins. some evenings i fail and the match goes out before it is even struck. then i recall your whistle (a bright rail), and the dark is less sure of itself.

when i return, we will go to the millpond and practice being quiet until the heron decides to trust us with its shadow. please keep the plane sharp. i am trying to build a chair sturdy enough for the thought of love.

your son,

E.
