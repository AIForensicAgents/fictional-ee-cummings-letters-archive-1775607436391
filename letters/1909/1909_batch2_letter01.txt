ink-thrush midnight

Date: 1909-02-11
Recipient: Eleanor Thorne
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1909
Themes: longing, winter, memory, intimacy, letters

dear eleanor,

this evening is a slow violin of snow and gaslight, a hush so entire the street forgets its name. i have placed your last letter (petal-breath, lampwarm) beside my ink, and the page leans toward it like a moth to a kindly fire.

how is it that your handwriting turns the plain air into a room with windows open to june, even in february where roofs wear their pale burdens and the river holds its breath under glass? i walked by the common and counted footprints as if they were stars misplaced upon the earth; some belonged to a dog, some to a boy who kicked the sky into the snow, and the smallest were surely yours (wild guess, dear heart), because i needed them to be.

if you stood here (if you dared) i would show you how the clock in our hall forgets a minute every hour and the house forgives it, kindly. i would fold your name into my coat so no wind could find it lonely. and if words are bridges (stubbornly, beautifully impractical) then step across this one i am building now: plank after plank of nonsense and truth.

know this: my day learns to breathe correctly only when i imagine your laugh traveling the wire, a warm coin spent into my palm. until the thaw, i will practice the bright arithmetic of your absence, solving for you and always arriving at you.

be more than careful. be you.

yours (unmended and unwilling to be),

r.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
