a room measures its light

Date: 1909-10-11
Recipient: Self (address left blank)
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1909
Themes: self, travel, art, wonder

Sir (and also child),

i rented a room that believes in windows. the street is fluent in rain; it speaks against the sill with a silver tongue. i have put a pear on the table to teach the air about sweetness. the pear is patient. the air—less so.

i keep a ledger of small braveries: asking directions without apology; declining a second coffee because the first was sufficient; forgiving my pen for stammering. museums are afternoon churches; i kneeled before a painting until my knees learned the word enough. night arrives in cuffs of blue and lets me go without a bribe.

what shall we do with our astonishment? wear it, perhaps, like a scarf we refuse to own. tomorrow i will buy a stamp and mail this to the future. if it arrives with a smudge, know that it is only my thumb, insisting that we existed.

—yours sincerely (and experimentally),

you
