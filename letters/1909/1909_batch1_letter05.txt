seaside arithmetic

Date: 1909-06-18
Recipient: Rowan (friend of the shore)
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1909
Themes: sea, friendship, listening, play

Rowan,

the sea has a pocket full of answers; it spends them on the sand and asks for change. i went down barefoot (to be paid in salt) and counted how many blues the day could afford. gulls practiced their rude grammar, correcting my solitude with sudden commas of wings.

there is a boat named Tomorrow that never leaves the harbor. i admire its ambition—to remain. an old man told me he keeps his heart in a net and casts it every noon; sometimes he brings up a shoe, sometimes an apology, once a violin. i asked what song the tide understands. he said: any song that doesn't insist on being right.

if you were here we'd build a fort from abandoned promises and sit inside until the ocean forgave us our laughter. bring a pencil that can write on wet light. bring yourself, which is to say, every silence that ever learned to sing.

i send you a shell: hold it to your ear and hear me learning how to listen.

—A.
