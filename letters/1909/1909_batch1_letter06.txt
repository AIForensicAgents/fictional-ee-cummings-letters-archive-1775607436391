grammar of thunder, or: i keep your letter in a shoe

Date: 1909-07-23
Recipient: Lydia
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1909
Themes: love, storm, city, desire

Lydia,

this city climbs itself—brick by breath—while streetcars braid sparks into evening hair. i found your letter folded like a shy bird and hid it in my shoe; walking has been sweeter ever since. every step pronounces your name with a soft accent of leather.

today a storm rehearsed its drums above the stockyards; the sky learned fast from smoke how to darken. i could not decide which thunder to admire—the one outside, or the boldness in my ribs when i remember the freckle beside your left eye (that small planet). if i am improper it is because you invented a new etiquette of wonder and failed to warn me.

come when the air tastes of pennies and rain. i will meet you where Halsted meets tomorrow. we will read shop signs aloud until they turn into poems and then into kissing and then, perhaps, into a long honest quiet (parentheses of breath).

wear your blue dress; the town needs a horizon.

—Yours (unpunctuated),

M.
