in the small thunder of trolleys

Date: 1909-01-21
Recipient: M.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1909
Themes: longing, winter, city, distance

M.,

tonight the trolley rings its bell like a thin moon and the streets wear a sugar of frost. i walked home with my breath making white telegrams (unposted) to your ear. every lamp is a careful star and the wires hum a little hymn to the cold. will you read this when your window is asleep? will you hold it against your cheek and let it warm, a small animal of paper?

your name moves through me the way milk warms in a kettle—quietly, with a brave little steam. the neighbors close their curtains; inside, spoons speak to cups. i keep my hands in my coat like a secret i am almost brave enough to tell. if i said come, would the river move aside? would the iron bridges kneel?

i do not know the arithmetic of distance; i only know the sum of footsteps: one plus one plus one (plus you). and i am a foolish scholar of winter, learning by heart each flake that writes itself against my sleeve, learning that the street remembers us even before we pass.

be well (which is to say be near),

—yours, a little breathless
