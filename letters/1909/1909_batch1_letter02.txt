a quiet revolt (ink & buttons)

Date: 1909-02-07
Recipient: Mr. Halberd, Editor
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1909
Themes: art, modernity, rebellion, city

Sir,

i send you a pocket of fresh words—please return the pocket if the words misbehave. the city operates in clacks and clicks: elevators rehearsing their metal prayers, typebars kissing paper with small exact violences. i have watched the noon pour out of Broadway, all brass and shoe-leather, and have found inside it a little republic of breath where punctuation refuses to salute.

you may say these sentences are not properly dressed. but buttons are not the heart. if i (buttonless) stand in a gust of meaning, who will deny the wind its grammar? the poems i send have not combed their hair. they have, however, learned to walk alone at dusk and to bow to lamplight. is that not manners enough?

i want a place for a voice to be ungentle to itself until it becomes kind. if your pages can be such a country, i pledge my ink to its flag of impossible white.

faithfully (and otherwise),

—A. L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
