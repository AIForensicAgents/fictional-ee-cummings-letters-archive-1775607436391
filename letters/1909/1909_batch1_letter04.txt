night-train with small courage

Date: 1909-05-29
Recipient: Aunt Eugenia
Location: Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
Year: 1909
Themes: travel, courage, family, night

Dear Aunt,

i write from a seat that trembles politely; the car window collects my face and gives it back in passing—so many borrowed versions. the conductor punches holes in our paper names and we become a constellation of tickets, necessary as stars to a sailor.

the countryside drifts by in folded darkness, a book i will never finish. somewhere a farmdog is informing the night of us. my valise snores. i am carrying a small courage in my waistcoat pocket (it rattles with the keys), for you told me: tell the world your throat is a stair and climb it.

i am coming to sit with you in the parlor where the fern keeps time, to drink that tea you refuse to sweeten because sadness must be tasted, you say, before it can be named. i shall bring lemon and a story that chooses you as its porch.

respectfully and ever oddly yours,

—Nathan
