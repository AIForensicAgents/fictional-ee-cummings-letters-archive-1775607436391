river-letter (with lilies)

Date: 1909-08-30
Recipient: Miss Beatrice Hale
Location: St. Louis, Missouri
Year: 1909
Themes: love, travel, water, distance, night

beatrice,

the river is a very long thought that the shore keeps trying to finish. tonight the paddlewheel stitches moonlight into the brown silk water, and each stitch sighs. men speak in rope and coal; a boy runs with a lantern so earnestly he becomes one. above us the courthouse is a serious cake with too much frosting and not enough birthday.

i bought lilies with the small coins your last note made appear in my pocket. they smell like a white answer to a question i have not asked correctly. i keep them by the berth where sleep attempts its narrow miracles. when the boat turns, they nod: yes dear, yes dear, as if they know all about distances and how they pretend to be maps.

if i could parcel this night, i would wrap it in a newspaper scandal and tie it with the cleanest string. i would send it upriver to your window where the curtains lift their shy wrists. you would open it and the sound inside (a soft machinery of frogs, the laugh of water against wood) would set your books swaying like contented trees. then i would step out of the envelope uncreased, and we would drink lemonade manufactured by the moon.

please remember to be the sort of beautiful that startles sidewalks into better behavior. i am practicing your name until it learns to walk on water. the bells talk midnight. i must answer.

always onward (and slightly toward you),

t.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
