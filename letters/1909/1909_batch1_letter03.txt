april invents a green you

Date: 1909-04-12
Recipient: Sister Clara
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1909
Themes: spring, family, memory, forgiveness

Dearest Clara,

our street has grown a thousand small wings; they call themselves leaves and applaud the wind. i walked to the Common where boys launched boats (churches of pocket-size) across the puddles, and i thought of your hands mending a hem—such weather inside a thimble.

mother sent a jar of plum jam; it tastes like yesterday agreeing to stay a minute longer. do you remember the old lilac that watched us pretend to be royalty? it is rehearsing its purple crown. oh and the cat has learned to spell bread with her paws on the doorstep—brave punctuation.

april has written your name in a shade of green the tailor cannot find. i carry it pinned to my coat, a leaf-letter. come sunday, if your cough is polite, meet me by the iron fountain. we will count the swans and forgive ourselves for last winter's small sorrows.

your brother (rain on his collar, sun in his shoes),

—J.
