two cents and an orange

Date: 1953-09-05
Recipient: The woman with the green umbrella
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1953
Themes: urban encounter, strangers, rain, whimsy, observation

Madam,

you do not know me. i was the fellow on State Street balanced between a newspaper headline and a hotdog, caught in the gallop of the El above. you paused, umbrella like a small green planet with its own private weather, and the rain had the courtesy to applaud us both.

it is brazen to write a stranger, but the city encourages brazen things. the shoeshine boys coin jokes on the curb, and a saxophone upstairs combs its hair straight back until midnight. when you tipped your umbrella toward the streetlight, the drops brightened and i swear a tiny orchestra tuned itself in the sheen.

i was carrying two cents and an orange, which is to say i was well-armed. i meant to eat the orange on the steps of the Art Institute and pretend i owned every painting of lions sleeping. instead i watched you navigate puddles like an inventor of quiet bridges. it did something to the day; it rearranged the furniture of my hurry.

if this note reaches you (tucked perhaps between the pages of a library book or the pocket of a coat you almost buy), keep your planet. keep its weather. i will keep the peel and learn its grammar.

respectfully,

A passerby with citrus
