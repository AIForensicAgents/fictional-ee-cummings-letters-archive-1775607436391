the porchlight listens

Date: 1953-04-30
Recipient: Mrs. Rivera, my next-door neighbor
Location: San Antonio, Texas
Year: 1953
Themes: neighborliness, gratitude, community, dust storm, music

Dear Mrs. Rivera,

tonight your radio learned to drowse and sang low in Spanish, like a lamp with a secret. i sat on my steps with a lemon soda and the dusk braided stray dogs into the street. you waved your dish towel, which looked exactly like a small flag for a country of kitchens and kindness.

i wanted to thank you for the beans and the advice tucked in with the steam: take the day by the hand, you said, and teach it to cross the street. when the wind went yellow with dust last week and the alley lost its edges, your porchlight leaned forward and listened to everyone's fear until it got bored and wandered off. that's a trick i'd like to learn—how to keep a bright face and a compassionate ear without losing the night entirely.

my job at the store is a stack of tins and a bell that blushes each time it is rung. men come in with their hats like polite moons, and women shop with worry knotted to their wrists. i wonder what becomes of the worry when it leaves the wrist. perhaps it sits on rooftops, counting the laundry below.

if your son writes from El Paso, tell him a stranger next door hopes the road keeps his thirst at arm's length. if you need sugar, just tap the wall twice and i will answer.

sincerely,

Lupe

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
