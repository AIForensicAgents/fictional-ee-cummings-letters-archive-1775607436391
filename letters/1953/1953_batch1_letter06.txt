lullaby for a blacked-out piano

Date: 1953-07-28
Recipient: Ruth (at the lake)
Location: Oshkosh, Wisconsin
Year: 1953
Themes: music, summer, domestic life, power outage, love

Ruth,

the storm teased the town, then flipped the switch—everything dimmed into a campfire version of itself. our piano became a large asleep animal in the parlor. i touched its flank and it sighed a little c—somnambulist note that wandered into the hallway and lay down.

i lit two candles and set them like punctuation on the lid. the keys looked like evening teeth. i played by guesswork, which is to say by faith, and the room kept nodding along. you're at the lake counting dragonflies on your knee while the moon balances itself on the pier-posts like a cautious acrobat. i tried to imagine the sound your hair makes when it decides to be wind.

neighbors came outside with tin radios and a bag of cherries; the pit-spit made a polite rain upon the stoop. someone said the outage was a blessing, the way silence sometimes is a teacher you never paid for. i agreed and shook the cherry bowl until a small red galaxy corrected itself into our mouths.

come home when you have taught the water your name again. bring me a stone the size of a laugh, and i'll trade for this candle wax that remembers our music.

love,

Paul
