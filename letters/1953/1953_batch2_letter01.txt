a small telegram of sky

Date: 1953-02-14
Recipient: L.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1953
Themes: love, longing, city, winter, intimacy

Dear L,

today the snow forgot its own whiteness and learned the blue of your last letter. i carried it through Thompson Street with my coat unbuttoned on purpose, so February could listen.

i cannot say I miss you without the world of windows shivering. the jukebox at Carmine's coughs out a slow song and i swear the needle leans closer when i write your name. the steam from the bakery makes a tender weather of the corner and still, your absence is a door i keep opening, just to be certain the room still exists.

i bought violets that looked like small telegrams of sky. they are practicing your perfume on the sill, rehearsing breath. the landlady's radio talks about senators and loyalty and i, disloyal to distance, pledge only what my hands can hold: the invisible of you, bright as a match struck under water.

last night a streetlight blinked itself into the shape of your wrist. i kept time against it, irregular and true. if you come back by Easter, i will teach the wineglass how to remember your lipstick. if you are delayed, i will take a pencil and break it in half, so each of us may write at the same time.

come soon, or anyway, arrive inside this sentence. the bed is a map folded to your initials.
yours, a little louder than silence,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
