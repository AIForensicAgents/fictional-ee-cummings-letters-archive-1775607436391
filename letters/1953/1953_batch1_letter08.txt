after the train leaves baltimore

Date: 1953-10-10
Recipient: Mother
Location: Washington, D.C.
Year: 1953
Themes: city life, work, restraint, family, yearning

Mother,

i am writing from a boardinghouse whose staircase wakes itself at every step, as if each footfall were an idea the house wishes to remember. the landlady arranges doilies like small snowdrifts and keeps cough drops in a jar that believes in discipline. the city hums in necktie tones; even the pigeons look employed.

at the office, my desk is an obedient rectangle. i file names that belong to people i will never meet, and the papers make a white weather around my hands. on lunch break i walk to the river and let the bridges proofread my thoughts. the radio in the corner mutters about committees and questions and the importance of saying the right nothing. i practice silence until it is fluent.

i saw a boy flying a brown paper kite shaped like a government building; it rose and trembled and finally decided to be sky instead. it felt like a lesson i can't yet write down. don't worry about my meals—there's a diner that puts a fried egg on top of everything, and i am learning to be sunny-side.

i miss the porch at home where the night is not so polite. give my best to the maple tree; tell it i am brave in the small ways, and working on the large.

your son,

Edmund
