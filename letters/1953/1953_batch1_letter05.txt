translation of a thunderstorm

Date: 1953-06-12
Recipient: Professor Hale
Location: Iowa City, Iowa
Year: 1953
Themes: writing, weather, rural life, craft, teacher-student

Professor,

i have taken your advice and given the poem a wider field. last night the sky rehearsed its hooves above the barn, and the tin roof learned a dozen synonyms for hurry. i sat at the kitchen table with the typewriter like a stubborn mule and tried to bridle the weather into sentences.

you said: do not name the heart unless you can show it at work. so i gave it chores. it pumped the well-handle, counted hailstones into a clean jar, erased a window with the back of its hand and wrote another on the fog. between strikes of light, i could see the corn arguing politely with the wind. the dog pretended to be brave until it wasn't.

my ribbon is near the end; the letters come out thin, as if they were already ghosts of themselves. still, i like the way the e's fade first—as though the poem were learning economy. i keep thinking of the road after rain, how it wears the farm upside down on its back. if reflection is a kind of confession, then maybe a poem is a road after weather.

yours truly,

Annie (who keeps listening for commas in the thunder)
