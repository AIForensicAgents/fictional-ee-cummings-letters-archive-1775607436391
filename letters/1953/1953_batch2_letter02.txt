regarding the quiet room mother left

Date: 1953-04-09
Recipient: My sister, Rose
Location: Cleveland, Ohio
Year: 1953
Themes: grief, family, memory, home, spring

Dear Rose,

this morning i opened Mother's closet and the mothballs made a small snowstorm. her Sunday hat, a moon with a pin in it, tried to rise. it's April and the house keeps pretending to be full, rehearsing the sounds she left behind: the kettle's soft argument, the radio's patient gospel, the stairs clearing their throats.

I found a jar of buttons — countries of their own — and sorted them until my palms knew the borders. a dress hem caught on my cuff and would not let go; perhaps it mistook me for someone braver. the mirror at the end of the hall continues to hold her breath. when i pass, i hand it mine.

the neighbors brought cake, which we cut in squares like careful fields. i ate without tasting, then tasted without eating. grief is a housecoat that fits everyone and no one; i fasten it wrong each time and still it holds.

come when you can. bring the little radio that only finds Cincinnati at night. we'll let the hum live here, between the sofa and her slipper. we'll sort the photographs until our faces remember how to be faces; we'll open the windows and let the clematis explain its green grammar.

i am learning to speak to absence. it answers in clean sheets and a chair pulled out but not sat in. if i write again tomorrow, forgive me the repeating — echo is the only instrument i was given.

your brother in the quiet room,
Samuel
