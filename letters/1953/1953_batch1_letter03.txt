postcard i never send

Date: 1953-03-15
Recipient: Eleanor
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1953
Themes: parting, memory, rain, restraint, urban life

Eleanor,

it is raining a very deliberate rain—the kind that writes its name on every brick and then underlines it. i took my hat off to listen better. the Common is a green lung full of wet footsteps; carriages of umbrellas go by as if the city had decided to be a slow parade.

i would have said: the room we shared still leans toward your shadow. the mirror wears your leaving like lipstick. but sentences are noisy thieves and you always preferred the quiet bravery of gestures: the teacup warmed in both palms, the tilted book, the scarf rope that taught my throat a softer knot. so i am practicing blankness, the clean postcard unscribbled, as if not speaking could learn to be a kind of mercy.

still: when a trolley bell stutters, i think it is your laugh rehearsing my name. when the stairs crack, i think you are returning with the precise gravity of April. (perhaps this is foolish. perhaps all rain is a school for fools.)

i will fold our last afternoon into my wallet and let it age among the tickets and crumbs. if i pass you in a street made of other seasons, i will not wave. i will stand very still and hope my stillness is eloquent.

with a care that forgets itself,

j.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
