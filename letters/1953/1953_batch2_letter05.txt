winter letter to a future me folded in a coat pocket

Date: 1953-12-27
Recipient: My someday self
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1953
Themes: time, self, desert, hope, winter

Dear future me,

the desert carries winter like a violin it has decided to play slowly. piñon smoke writes a small alphabet against the dusk and i read what i can: stay. breathe. look. a silver train stitches far mountains to nearer ones and unthreads them again, patient as a grandmother.

i walked Canyon Road with a thermos, and the lid kept a warm moon in my palm. the adobe holds light after sundown, as if the day left its coat and will return. i think of the year as a corridor with many doors, some broom closets, some starlight. we opened what we could; a few opened us.

you will forget parts of this. that is fine. carry the forgetting gently, like an egg that refuses to hatch but is smooth and necessary. remember instead the exact color of this evening, the not-quite-blue that confesses to the first star. remember how the wind untied your name and let it fly around your head like a slow bee that meant no harm.

when the calendar rinses itself and begins again, do not count the days; teach them to sing. buy the small bread, share it. write letters you do not send, and one you do. if the world asks for your certainty, hand it your wonder, already warm from your pocket.

until soon,
you.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
