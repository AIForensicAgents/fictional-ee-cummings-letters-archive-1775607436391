on the back of a matchbook

Date: 1953-02-21
Recipient: My Brother Thomas
Location: Cleveland, Ohio
Year: 1953
Themes: family, nostalgia, working-class life, winter, humor

Tom,

i'm writing from the diner on Lorain—the one where the waitress calls everybody sailor and the jukebox coughs quarters into small storms. Dad's whistle let the mill out early, and the whole street wore a tired kind of glitter: snow tamped flat by boots, breath like cheap cigars. you asked if the city is any different. it isn't, not really, only the pigeons look older and the lake keeps forgetting its name under the ice.

remember our trick of balancing spoons on our noses? i tried it tonight and the cook clapped once, very solemnly, as if i'd played Chopin with my face. the paper by the register is all detectives and scores and sighs. the waitress says the radio keeps talking about faraway hills with smoke on them. i don't know if it's bravery or boredom that sets a person walking that direction. maybe both.

Mom still folds her prayers small as receipts and tucks them in cookbooks. she sent along your wool cap; i wrapped it around the sugar dispenser and imagined your head was that sweet and that stubborn. if you do come home for a weekend, we'll carve our initials in the alley snow again (it always melts into a hopeful puddle, doesn't it?).

until then, keep your breath warm and your pockets loud.

your brother,

Sam
