christmas inventory of miracles (penciled)

Date: 1953-12-24
Recipient: No one in particular
Location: Burlington, Vermont
Year: 1953
Themes: winter, grace, poverty, holidays, solitude

Dear whoever is awake,

tonight the town crochets itself a silence and hangs it on every fencepost. i walked to the corner store for matches and came back with a loaf of bread that smelled like an apology accepted. the clerk wrapped it as if tucking in a child.

what we have: a kettle willing to talk above its station; a postcard from a cousin who spells wonder with three o's; boots that remember all my wrong turns and forgive them; a window that refuses to stay out of the snow's business. what we do not have: certainty, the kind of faith that clicks like a lamp, a choir of money. but i lit a candle stub and it overlooked my inventory with gracious eyes.

on the radio a crooner practiced the art of not hurrying. in the alley, a cat negotiated the terms of midnight. i wrote a list of names and let a crumb of light touch each one. some were lost, some only misplaced by time. a bell somewhere discovered it could be itself and not an advertisement for anything.

if you are awake, drink something warm and permit your breath to be a modest star. wrap the year in butcher paper, write fragile and mean it, then open it anyway.

cordially,

H.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
