the committee is listening (so whisper)

Date: 1953-10-12
Recipient: A fellow playwright
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1953
Themes: censorship, art, fear, resistance, community

Dear C,

i have taught my typewriter to cough instead of speak. the ribbon has learned to unsay itself. somewhere in a room without windows, they are auditioning words for loyalty, weighing jokes on a yellow scale. my pages return with thumbprints like bruises; my commas, suspect for leaning.

yesterday a man asked me if i loved my country. i told him yes, like a window loves the light it cannot keep. he wrote something down that didn't look like yes. we ordered coffee we did not drink. the waitress refilled truth until it was cold and shining.

if they come for me, let them find only half a sentence and a coat with all the buttons misnamed. leave verbs in the hallway, leaning. hide the stage directions in your hem. teach your actors to whistle their lines between their teeth. the audience understands more than the stage permits; they have been rehearsing all year with their radios turned low.

still, i am not frightened, exactly. fear is a sharp hat and i wear a softer one. i will meet you Thursday by the river where the leaves change opinions out loud. bring the play we do not sign, the one about a country where birds are not subpoenas and doors open like honest fruit.

in erasures,
M.
