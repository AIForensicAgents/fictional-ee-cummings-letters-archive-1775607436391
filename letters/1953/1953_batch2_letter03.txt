after the whistle stopped (korea)

Date: 1953-08-01
Recipient: Private J.D.
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1953
Themes: armistice, friendship, ocean, survival, homecoming

Dear J.D.,

the armistice is five days old and the newspapers print quiet in heavy black, as if peace were a funeral they were not invited to but cover anyway. the bay keeps its fog like a promise folded twice. i drink coffee that tastes of ship paint and condensed milk and watch the bridge disappear, appear, disappear, like breath practicing to stay.

i imagine your boots asleep under a cot in some barracks of dawn. is the silence there new or only louder than the other noises. last night the docks played cards with their shadows; a gull won and flew away with the pot of light.

your last letter carried a pebble in its pocket, it rattled when i opened it. i keep it on the sill. when trains go by (their iron syllables), the stone hums yes, yes, yes. i answer maybe, maybe. not because i doubt, but because maybe is a door that opens without knocking.

if you come through here wearing the soft miracle of being intact, we will go to the Palace of Fine Arts and let our voices climb the ruin. i will show you the bench where i told the water your name and the water, polite, forgot at once. we can forgive it. the ocean is busy unlearning warships.

write again. even one line, or the breath that escorts it. i will hold the paper to my ear until your handwriting makes a sound.

—A.
