appendix for a dream

Date: 1953-11-16
Recipient: Dr. Lin
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1953
Themes: dreams, insomnia, city, therapy, autumn

Doctor,

last night the fog practiced handwriting against my window until the room believed it was an envelope. i dreamed again of the staircase that narrows to a pencil line and then to a thought too thin to climb. when i wake, my lungs try to read the air backwards. perhaps this is only November doing its good work.

you said: keep a ledger of the small awakenings. so—today the streetcar rang a kind of gentle alarm and an apricot vendor called out in a voice the color of his fruit. a child kicked a bottle cap across the sidewalk and it kept deciding to be a moon. i ate noodles with chopsticks like careful lightning and the soup regarded me as if i'd just told it a secret.

i wish for sleep that does not audit me. i wish to be surprised by morning, not negotiated into it. still, the city teaches mercy: laundries letting go of ghosts in warm clouds, the bay repairing the day's cracked glass with one uncomplicated glitter.

i will try the breathing exercise you wrote on your pink prescription pad. if i learn to be a better envelope, perhaps the dream will agree to be a letter i can open slowly.

with respect,

Marian

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
