a small telegram of breath

Date: 1953-01-07
Recipient: M.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1953
Themes: love, winter, city, longing, art

Dear M,

tonight the city buttons its gray coat to the throat and every window is a candle with somewhere to be. i am practicing the ordinary miracle of breathing (in the pantry of my ribs, where the beans tick like stars). outside, the milk bottles wear white collars and the stoop pretends to be a ship in snow. i think of your hands, how they correct the light when it falls crooked on a page; how a match struck near your voice becomes a door.

we are poor in all the usual currencies, but rich in the small ridiculous ones: a wobbling chair, a pocket of pencil shavings, the clatter of the radiator translating winter into steam prayer. i typed your name and it shivered the ribbon. how little a letter must do, to carry the throat of a person across the clanging night!

i have nothing new except this: the cats have learned my footsteps; the moon decided to be a bread crumb; my heart keeps addressing envelopes to your absence, and every stamp is a thumbprint bruised with hope. if you return, leave the door ajar enough for February to peep in—i will teach the month to whisper.

until then, i fold this breath twice and post it to you.

yours (with frost on the period),

A.
