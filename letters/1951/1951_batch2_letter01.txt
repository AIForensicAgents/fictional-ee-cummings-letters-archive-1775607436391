airmail (with blue edges) to miriam

Date: 1951-02-14
Recipient: Miriam A.
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1951
Themes: love, winter, distance, city, longing

Dear Miriam,

the airmail envelope (blue-edged like sky wearing socks) waits shyly on my desk.
outside, winter powders the trolley tracks, and every breath is a small telegram—stop—
sent from chest to night.

i bought carnations (pink, brave) from a woman whose hands were maps of warmth.
she winked as if to say: love is a streetlamp that forgets to be afraid.

i keep hearing the soft arithmetic of your footsteps, how they add me to myself.
in the library, a boy coughed semicolons; in the bakery, sugar snowed; i was
everywhere and somehow only where you might be.

if i could fold this city into an envelope, i would crease the Common, tuck the river,
lick the stamp of moon, and send you all of boston, addressed to your throat.

until then, take this pocket-sized weather: my mouth spelling your name
like sunday bells; my heart, a woolen glove turned inside out to dry on the radiator.

hurry toward april. i will be the first green rumor under the snow.

always, not always,
i

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
