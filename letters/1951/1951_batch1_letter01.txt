april telephones bloom (manhattan hum)

Date: 1951-04-13
Recipient: Lydia
Location: New York City, NY
Year: 1951
Themes: love, city, spring, longing, intimacy

dear lydia,
this afternoon the avenues flung their hats into the wind and every traffic light behaved like a pulse. telephone wires learned a new bird and sang it down the block. i bought a paper cup of coffee that steamed like the inside of a star and thought of your wrist lifting a curtain, of your mouth being the small and necessary mercy by which a day forgives itself.

the city keeps trying on mirrors. i walk until the pavements turn to vowels and even my shoes begin to pronounce a tenderness. somewhere above 14th a piano is falling very quietly up the stairs. that is how i remember you, astonishment moving the wrong way and being right about it.

last night i mailed you my breath, folded into a telegram i never sent. it said come soon in the grammar of rain. i imagine the envelope arriving unaddressed, its stamp a little moon, the clerk asleep and dreaming of your name.

when i stand on the fire escape the sky leans close as if to borrow sugar. the new leaves are the green of unsaid things. i am so full of them, lydia, small elastic syllables, a pocketful of sky that will not stop lightly leaping.

if you were here i would place my ear to your shoulder and listen to the quiet engine of us. no one would suspect how entirely the streetlamp understands.

night loosens its tie. i am awake and harmless and held together by the fact of you.

yours in the plural of yes,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
