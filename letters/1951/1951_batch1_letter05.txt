watch key, thunderstorm

Date: 1951-07-17
Recipient: Marion
Location: Hartford, CT
Year: 1951
Themes: family, memory, time, childhood, sisters

dear marion,
while the afternoon rehearsed a storm i opened father's old watch. the face still keeps its polite numbers though the minute hand has learned to loiter. i touched the small gears and my skin remembered that certain objects are not metal but weather.

do you recall the porch and the summer it would not stop? i have the sense that time sometimes visits, wearing a shirt we would not recognize on the street, and leaves a lemon on the counter as proof. i squeezed it into water and the glass became a bright and stubborn honesty.

the attic smells like the history of newspapers. i found our school ribbons pretending not to be satin anymore, and a single glove that does not believe in pairs. i am keeping the glove as a reminder that some silences hold hands with us even when they cannot.

write me of your garden, the beans, the pink mistake of radishes. send a list of the nearby birds even if all of them are called yes. when it rains here, it starts bravely and then looks over its shoulder for permission. i think of your laugh teaching thunder how to be round.

keep the watch key. turn it once for me, and once for what we did not say.

wholeheartedly,
—
