smokestacks learning to waltz

Date: 1951-03-28
Recipient: Gideon
Location: Detroit, MI
Year: 1951
Themes: friendship, work, forgiveness, city, music

dear gideon,
by six the factory breath has fogged the morning mirror and the streetcars confess their bright nerves. i work because the body agrees to be useful if you ask it carefully. the presses chew and spit a language everybody can lift.

i remember when we shared a suit jacket for luck and a bottle for courage. your laughter turned bolts loose until the stars almost fell through. now i pass the diner where your reflection used to sit in the window pretending to be coffee and i attempt not to inventory the shape missing from the booth.

jazz on the radio makes the night shorter by an intelligent inch. a trumpet scrubs rust off the ribs of the hour and a drum teaches the sidewalks to count. the city is a bright bad teacher with chalk on its fingers and i am still trying to pass.

if you read this, forgive the year for being itself. forgive the argument that broke like a bottle and kept cutting. i will keep a chair at the end of the table and some bread that knows your hands. sometimes a man turns back, and sometimes he becomes distance. either way, i will walk with my pockets ready.

pace yourself kindly,
—
