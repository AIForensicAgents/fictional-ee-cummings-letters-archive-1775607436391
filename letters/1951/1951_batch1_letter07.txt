desert envelope with a sky inside

Date: 1951-09-12
Recipient: Night Clerk, La Fonda Hotel
Location: Santa Fe, NM
Year: 1951
Themes: solitude, travel, strangers, night, kindness

dear night clerk,
forgive a stranger who keeps borrowing the lobby's quiet as if it were a book. you pass me the key with a look that understands sleeplessness better than any dictionary. the bell above the door rings like a coin tossed into a well and the well is the whole dark.

the adobe walls hold heat the way a secret holds breath. from my window a streetlamp studies its own shadow very earnestly. i wish to thank you for the glass of water that arrived as a clear sentence when my mouth had forgotten its alphabet.

i came here to learn the color of empty. it turns out to be full, like a pocket after you think you have spent everything. the desert asks nothing but answers kindly. even the lizards write their names in quick cursive and leave them for the sun to read.

if i leave before you return to your book, please know your nod at 2 a.m. stitched me back into my outline. we who move between rooms need small certainties. yours was a hand on the switch that did not hurry the dawn.

gratefully awake,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
