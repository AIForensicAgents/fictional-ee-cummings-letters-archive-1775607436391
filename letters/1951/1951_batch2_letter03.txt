after the matinee (you, umbrella)

Date: 1951-09-21
Recipient: Eva
Location: Manhattan, New York
Year: 1951
Themes: heartbreak, urban, cinema, rain, memory

Eva,

we stepped out of the matinee into a rain that had been rehearsing all morning.
your umbrella was a small theater where we did not quite fit; i leaned,
you did not; taxis practiced their yellow vowels; the marquee kept blinking maybe.

the newsreel inside said the world is complicated (as if we hadn't noticed).
our hands, backstage, missed their cues. i said coffee; you said silence; the street said hush.

i keep a ticket stub in my pocket like a thin bone. the film already forgets us—
actors bowing to the dark, a kiss that solved nothing except its own hunger.

if love is a city, ours lost the map. still, a wet newspaper tried to fly,
a dog shook rain into confetti, and for a moment you laughed (small thunder).

in the diner, a jukebox begged nickels to be brave. i studied your reflection
in the window where everything was backwards and therefore arguably true.

it is evening now. i walk past neon letters trying to spell forgiveness
and failing beautifully. should you need me, i will be where the avenue
turns into river and all names become weather.

be well. keep the umbrella. keep the sky it borrowed.
not yours, still somehow,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
