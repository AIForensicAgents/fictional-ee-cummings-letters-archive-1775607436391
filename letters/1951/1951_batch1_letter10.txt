westbound, a pocketful of wheat light

Date: 1951-05-04
Recipient: Conductor of Car 7
Location: Santa Fe Railway, Kansas
Year: 1951
Themes: travel, america, observation, transit, gratitude

dear conductor,
thank you for the ticket that made distance behave. the prairie unrolled like a faithful bandage and the sky kept tapping on the window for permission to enter. when you punched the small moon into my card i understood that travel is a precise and cheerful surgery.

in the dining car the salt and pepper bowed to each curve and the coffee remembered all my late nights at once. a woman in a blue hat cradled a white box that certainly contained a miracle or a cake. an old man counted telegraph poles, which are the country's thin astonishments.

you walked the aisle with the gravity of a good sentence. i have been rescued by such sentences before. when the brakes sang their brief iron psalm i almost wrote something important on the condensation but the window kept moving.

if i fall asleep, wake me at a station named after a river. i promise to step down carefully and carry my suitcase as if it were a sleeping animal. there is a town ahead where i am expected by a chair, a porch, a single lamp with the talent for patience.

with respect for your whistle and its tidy horizon,
—
