Factory windows at five

Date: 1951-05-03
Recipient: My brother Thomas
Location: Detroit, Michigan
Year: 1951
Themes: labor, brotherhood, machines, uncertainty, hope

Dear Thomas,

five whistles make a day—one to wake, one to start, three to remember there is an outside.
the line moves like a prayer nobody quite believes but keeps saying anyway.
my hands learn each bolt's vowel and consonant; by dusk they are fluent in steel.

there is talk (always talk) about Korea, about lists with our names
folded into someone else's envelope. i do not know which is heavier:
the hood we lift together or the question under it.

on lunch break, the radio on a nail hums ballgames and weather. someone laughs
with his whole spine. a pigeon studies us from the rafters, decides not to enlist.

i remember you in the field behind our old house, throwing a rust-red ball
until the light forgot us. you said: a man is the work he carries home under his nails.
maybe. but tonight when the plant exhales us (warm, sooty, ordinary stars),
i carry a small extra: the thought of you reading this, your brow a careful bridge.

tell Ma the rent is paid, the thermos does not leak, and i have not misplaced hope.
it rides the bus with me, standing room only, pretending to be a coin.

i will write again when the whistle learns a different song.
your brother,
L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
