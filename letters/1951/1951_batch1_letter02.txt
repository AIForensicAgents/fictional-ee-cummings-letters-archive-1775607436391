carbon paper, blue fingerprints

Date: 1951-02-21
Recipient: Mr. Harrow, Editor
Location: Cambridge, MA
Year: 1951
Themes: art, doubt, ambition, letters, publication

dear mr harrow,
forgive the undiscipline of these sentences, they keep slipping out of their collars. i have enclosed a manuscript that smells faintly of midnight and the elbow of a lamp. it may also contain a train or two, some rope ladders, and a moth which refuses to be anything but itself.

i have not learned how to use the respectable voice. it puts its shoes on and my tongue runs away. still, i imagine your office with its calendar like a square jaw, and you leaning into a window of smoke to listen for what does not yet have a place on the shelf.

last week a professor told me the heart should mind its margins. i nodded as one must, but afterward i watched a boy chalk a circle on the sidewalk and step inside, perfectly unafraid. it seemed to me that literature is this, a room you draw around an ache until the ache can sit down.

if you will consider the pages, do not straighten them too much. let the stray commas breathe. the paper is cheap and thin but holds a sky that is heavier than i could carry alone.

with gratitude and a pencil chewed down to its music,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
