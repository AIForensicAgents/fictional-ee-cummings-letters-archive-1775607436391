pacific postcard with no stamp

Date: 1951-08-03
Recipient: Elias
Location: San Francisco, CA
Year: 1951
Themes: war, separation, hope, ocean, friendship

dear elias,
this ocean is the largest pocket i have ever turned out. everything spills from it, including sleep. the evenings are fog with a heartbeat and somewhere under it a ship coughs politely. i practice not seeing headlines because they bruise too easily.

when i write your name i keep dropping it, it rolls under the table like a coin that will not be found until morning. i imagine you somewhere east of here, the ground too loud with boots, horizons folded like maps into the soldiering of days. i have no instructions for courage except this: try to keep your hands around a small and excellent silence, something like an old harmonica that fits exactly.

they sell postcards on the pier with girls whose smiles could mend bridges. i choose instead a card with a lighthouse too far away to be useful. it looks like a throat clearing. i meant to send it but it would only arrive after you have already forgiven me for sending it.

i am ordinary, i eat soup, i walk out to the end of the wooden ribs and watch pelicans misunderstand gravity. if you do not sleep, pretend the night is a coat with pockets deep enough to keep you warm. i am here, stitching air to air, until you return.

always, and always yet,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
