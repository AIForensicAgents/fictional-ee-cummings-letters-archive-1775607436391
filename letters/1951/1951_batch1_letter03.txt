kitchen radio, soft thunder

Date: 1951-06-09
Recipient: Mama
Location: Chicago, IL
Year: 1951
Themes: family, home, nostalgia, city, everyday

dear mama,
this morning your postcard arrived with a smudge of kansas wheat in it. i rested it on the stove while the kettle learned to whistle and the radio told us, politely, about the world. the announcer's voice had that sunday suit feeling, careful as new shoes. my eggs were brave and did not mind being ordinary.

i am well, the apartment holds together with stubborn cheer. the neighbors argue sweetly through the wall, their syllables braided like laundry on a line. i have made friends with the woman at the corner grocer who keeps apples in a bowl like patient planets.

your advice to sleep early found me late again. i walk the river after work where cranes make their lean alphabets against a mauve sky and somewhere a barge hums the low note of enough. i am learning to fry potatoes without burning their small suns, to sew buttons back into their weather, to keep coins for the telephone in a jar that pretends to be a lighthouse.

i think of the kitchen at home, your hands coaxing quiet from dough. i think of the apron pocket where secrets kept lint company. i think of your laugh starting at the window and fetching air.

i miss you in the soft way that makes a day taller. consider this letter a warm plate left on your table.

love from your stubborn child,
—
