to a small future person (folded into a dresser drawer)

Date: 1951-12-01
Recipient: the child we might meet someday
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1951
Themes: longing, future, desert, tenderness, home

Dear you,

the desert keeps its quiet the way a hand keeps a secret—warm, careful, sure.
at dusk, adobe houses blush and coyotes stitch the hills with their thin bright thread.
we light a lamp shaped like a pear and the room becomes an orchard of gold.

i do not know your face yet (will your eyes be morning or late afternoon?),
but already you rearrange the furniture of our sleep. your mother hums while folding
towels; i pretend it is for me. the radio breathes a lullaby through its small, brave ribs.

we saved a drawer for you: wrapped blankets, a spoon with a star engraved,
a book that smells like patience. outside, piñon smoke writes slow cursive
over the dark; the moon practices being a cradle.

if you come to us, we will teach you thunder and how to answer it.
we will count the trains that pass without stopping and love them anyway.
we will show you the atlas and the backyard and the difference between them.

and if you do not come, we will keep this tenderness walking the house
like a quiet dog, feed it early, let it sleep late, forgive it for barking at shadows.

whoever you are, wherever you are (now, later, never):
know that tonight two people held a space open for your name,
and the stars, polite as librarians, nodded.

waiting without waiting,
us

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
