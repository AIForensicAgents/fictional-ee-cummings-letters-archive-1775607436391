Date: 1951-11-19
Recipient: Myself
Location: Brooklyn, NY
Year: 1951
Themes: self, resilience, urban life, introspection, gentleness

dear you,
this is a small appointment with tomorrow arranged by the timid courage of tonight. the window keeps practicing moonlight and almost succeeds. i have placed my shoes side by side to remind them of companionship.

do not spend the morning apologizing to clocks. noodles will boil, a letter will not write itself, and the roof will be a roof whether or not you admire it. touch the banister as if it could forget you. it will not.

you have learned to speak softly to fear until it believes itself a houseplant. remember water, remember sun. also remember the hard-to-pronounce word for getting through the day, which is you.

should a sadness ring the bell, feed it soup and let it watch the radio. do not hand it your coat. when you leave the apartment, tuck a brave napkin in your pocket in case of weather. be polite to stray dogs and to your own shadow, which has been doing its work quietly for years.

if the poem will not come, make a bed for it. if sleep will not come, let the bed be a boat and your breath the oars. arrive somewhere simple and say thank you.

i am, faithfully and with ordinary astonishment,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
