montparnasse with ink on its fingers

Date: 1951-10-05
Recipient: Ada
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1951
Themes: art, exile, city, friendship, aesthetics

dear ada,
paris keeps dropping little coins of light into the gutter and picking them up again as if embarrassed by wealth. i go to your studio at the serious hour when windows look like unopened letters. your canvas leans like a conspirator. a woman inside it is almost an orange, or maybe a cathedral about to believe in afternoon.

you taught me that color is not a color but a verb with a pulse. today the zinc bar held my elbows and we listened to a trumpet invent an alley. the waiter wore a sorrow that kept its shoes polished. i did not mind being a foreigner because the air here remembers how to say please to the wind.

i have been careful with loneliness, it bruises if you breathe on it too hard. i walk the river until the bridges become ideas. the news from home arrives in envelopes that smell of starch and distance. i answer with a postcard that says see how the moon rehearses itself on the seine.

paint me a chair we can both sit in. paint me a night that does not end, only becomes softer. when you laugh, the easel stops pretending to be wood and becomes a tall animal learning to dance.

yours in the grammar of pigment,
—
