ink breathes like midnight

Date: 1927-01-12
Recipient: M.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York City
Year: 1927
Themes: love, winter, city, art, longing

dear M,

snow edits the streets into hush and every lamp is a slow yellow heartbeat. i have been teaching my hands to speak you: thumb against forefinger, a tiny cathedral, the bell invisible but ringing just the same. upstairs the radiator exhales a tin garden of roses and i am their only bee, drunk and lonely and glowing.

the painting refused to behave today. it went on being a winter mouth, closed. so i walked it to the window and let it taste the alley. a cat rearranged the trash into a sort of opera, and the sky applauded with very thin blue gloves. i made the mistake of thinking i could be sensible, and immediately the day poured out of its glass.

M, sometimes language is too coat-and-hat for what we do with our breaths. what i mean is: the city has your wrist in its pocket. what i mean is: somewhere behind my ribs a small unschooled bird eats clocklight and learns your name by hunger. i never pass the bakery without believing that sugar remembers you (the way streetcars remember sparks).

if you come tomorrow i will build a cup wide enough for two twilights and a little cinnamon. if you do not, understand that i will kiss the stairwell banister with such concentration the wood will grow blushes. i am trying to be brave, which is to say i am trying to keep my mouth from writing you all over the fog.

always (and a little after),

i
