night telegram to tomorrow

Date: 1927-09-08
Recipient: Myself (tomorrow)
Location: Paris, Montparnasse
Year: 1927
Themes: time, city nights, self, art, wander

dear you who wake up,

do not scold me for this midnight contraband. i have been stealing hours from sleep and teaching them to dance on the sill. the cafe closed its eyes at two and kept talking. someone drew a city on the table with spilled wine and invited boats to anchor in the crumbs. the saxophone was a rumor traveling from mouth to mouth until it believed itself.

i walked the crooked seam between languages and found that both sides were wearing the same perfume: coffee with a decisive heart. a girl rode a bicycle through my chest and left two bright tire marks along the ribs. consider forgiving her. consider sending thanks. the moon rehearsed being an egg and broke over the rooftops with exemplary manners.

tomorrow, be kinder to your knees. they have been negotiating with these old stairs and might strike. if a stranger asks you the way to anywhere, tell them two wrong turns and a bridge. they will arrive better for the detour. work when the window blinks, not when the clock shouts. and should loneliness knock (he is punctual, that fellow), offer him a chair with one wobble so he cannot stay long.

sleep now. i have put a small apricot of joy in the pocket of your coat. find it at noon. eat it slowly.

yours across the thin wall of hours,

me

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
