midnight prayer for a cloud

Date: 1927-09-30
Recipient: Clara R.
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1927
Themes: distance, performance, landscape, reinvention, yearning

Clara,

Desert midnight is an alphabet arranged by silence. The coyotes spell a question I cannot answer and the mesas keep their knuckles on the table. I came here to unlearn the city’s busy mirror, to drink the wide air like a clean habit, but the sky is too large to fit behind my ribs without a little ache.

Your last note said the costume pearls snapped in the middle of Act Two and scattered like soft hail. I almost heard the applause change its weather. You always had a way with collapse—turning it to confetti and asking the broom to dance. Do you still pin roses to your wrist and pretend they are watches that tell your true time?

At noon I paint adobe with a crew of men who have names the sun chews tenderly. The walls take the color of warm bread. In the shadows, lizards write small impatient scripts. One of the men gave me a turquoise bead as if he were returning my lost eye. I wear it around my throat, a patient punctuation.

I have not touched a piano in three months. Sometimes I set my hands on the cool sill and wait for the tune that used to arrive—the one that made rain hurry toward us from the blue far edge. Perhaps I will trade these hands for hands that repair roofs; perhaps I will keep them for your shoulder when your dress misunderstands gravity.

If the touring company crosses the plains this winter, write me a date and I will hitch my heart to it. Until then I tie my prayer to the smallest cloud and let it wander toward you, a white button seeking its coat.

Ever your accomplice in whatever refuses to be sensible,

Julian

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
