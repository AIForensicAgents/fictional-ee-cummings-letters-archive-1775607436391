harbor salt and the lowercase moon

Date: 1927-07-30
Recipient: Captain of the Morning Ferry
Location: Gloucester, Massachusetts
Year: 1927
Themes: sea, morning, gratitude, travel, quiet

Captain,

i am writing to thank you for the way your boat remembered dawn before the sun did. we leaned out over the rail and the sea kept its promises, shaking hands with our shoes. gulls performed their white arithmetic above us (divide fish by sky, multiply by hunger). the town unrolled like a patient rope and tied itself to our ankles, gentle.

your voice knows how to talk to fog. i watched it back down politely, revealing islands like shy teeth. if i owned a hat worth doffing, i'd have doffed it. the engine mumbled an old sailor prayer that did not mind being half asleep. even my city thoughts (those quick-talking devils) lost their breath and had to sit awhile on a coil of rope.

i have packed a tin of the morning to take inland. i will open it when the streets misbehave and let the salt run along my tongue until the clamor agrees to be rhythm. you said every harbor is a pocket where the world keeps its spare light. i believe you. when night fell like a careful curtain, i noticed the moon had chosen small letters for itself, modest and perfectly legible.

should i return in september, keep a space on the bench where the wind learns politeness from your hands.

with both feet still swaying,

T.
