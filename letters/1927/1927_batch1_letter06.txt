desert envelope (with a blue lizard)

Date: 1927-06-21
Recipient: Rosa
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1927
Themes: landscape, love, heat, silence, travel

Rosa,

the desert kissed my eyelids twice and the second time i agreed to forgive its honesty. everything here tells the truth slowly: adobe learns sunrise by fingertip; junipers knit shade with patient green needles; a river that forgot how to be a river whispers its old name to stones at night.

i am lighter than i have any right to be. perhaps it is the high air tugging moths from my chest. perhaps it is the blue lizard who owns this letter. he rests upon my page and blinks punctuation. when i write your name, he nods in the native language of heat. my shadow has undergone a change—she walks a little ahead, scouting for water, returning with handfuls of sky.

i bought a small pot, unglazed, the color of lips that haven't decided to speak. the woman who made it smiled as if her teeth were the last snow on the mountains. she said: hold it with two hands or it will dream of thunder and leave. so i hold it. i hold your absence the same way (careful, like weather).

come if you can. pack nothing but thirst and the courage to be slow. i will carve us a home from afternoon, with a roof of lapis silence and doors that open before we knock.

with dust and a star folded into this envelope,

J.
