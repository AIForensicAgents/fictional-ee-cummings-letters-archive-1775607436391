automobile lullaby

Date: 1927-12-01
Recipient: Helen
Location: Detroit, Michigan
Year: 1927
Themes: industry, love, winter, work, motion

Helen,

the factories have tucked their metal children into rows and the whistle reads a bedtime story made of steam. snow introduces itself to the windshield politely then insists on staying, as snow does when it is most itself. i finished my shift with grease in the lines of my palms like itinerant music. it refuses to leave even with fierce soap, so i let it be a map.

the city hums the key of iron and i hum along, off by a forgiving half-note. nights here are a thousand quiets hiding beneath one very loud dream. on the tram some men sleep with their mouths open like small garages, doors up for moonlight repairs. a woman carries violets through the cold and the whole car warms its eyes by them.

i wish you were here for the ridiculous glory of headlights spelling snowflakes at thirty miles per hour. for the way a new car exhales and believes itself a beast until a child touches the hood and tames it. i am earning not much and it is almost always enough. when i close my eyes, the belts and pistons keep working in my chest, and your name is the drop of oil that makes the impossible smooth.

send a scarf. or send a letter heavier than wool. i will wrap both around my throat and drive the long soft mile to morning.

affectionately rattling but sound,

P.
