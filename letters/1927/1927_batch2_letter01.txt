an envelope of skylight

Date: 1927-02-14
Recipient: Marjorie Ellis
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1927
Themes: romance, urban life, longing, winter, intimacy

Marjorie,

Tonight the street is a thin violin and I am the bow, slow as a sleeve sliding down a wrist. The tobacconist shuts his small brass door with a sigh that tastes of roses and smoke. I have walked the block twice to watch the upstairs baker blow sugar to snow on a cake, which I pretended was the moon. Forgive the theater of it—your name keeps tugging the curtain rope in my chest until the stars come out coughing.

It is the holiday of foolish red hearts and I have none to pin, only a teacup of rain by the window and a coat shiny with other people’s weather. When the trolley rattles, the whole room agrees with your laugh—yes; and my hands, two conspirators, fold and unfold a blank postcard as if a pigeon might happen upon it and carry me to your wrist.

Today I passed a florist who arranged chrysanthemums like grammar, fierce and exact, and found myself promising to speak to you without commas, to bring you my breath as a single sentence that does not end. Even the elevator in this shabby building knows the vow, rising with a private hush and falling with a grin that says dare.

Come down the stairs of the day you are in. I will wait by the lamplight that spools itself into gold thread, and I will stitch a city from it small enough to button in your pocket. If you cannot come, send a word. Send the paper shadow of your palm. Send that brief, impossible yes which I wear already, a flower you forgot to deny me.

Yours in ink and a little courage,

A.
