river letter with thaw

Date: 1927-02-28
Recipient: Brother Elias
Location: Cincinnati, Ohio
Year: 1927
Themes: family, river, work, change, machines

Brother,

the river is a long gray muscle today, flexing its February and showing us teeth of broken light. barges move like patient whales with square backs; men on the piers trade signals with their hats. i stand with the cool iron of the railing teaching my pulse to march in a straight line and it laughs, taking alleys.

Father says we were born with silt in our pockets and that is why we sink so easily into work. i have not told him that every gear i touch remembers rain and tries to rust a little prayer into my knuckles. it is a handsome fatigue, to lift and lift until the world agrees to be lighter. still, i keep a scrap of sky in my coat lining for luck (blue cloth covered in fingerprints of small clouds).

they are laying new track along the far bank, a steel sentence spelling something modern. the whistle writes commas across the afternoon. i do not mind machines when they are honest, when they groan like oxen and sweat beside us. it is the smooth perfectly smiling ones that frighten me, as if they will marry our hours and never once ask our names.

i hope your cough has wandered away and lost itself in a field somewhere. when the thaw finishes its slow arithmetic, come down and we will teach the water to toast us with foam and old stories.

your brother who is mostly mud and a little star,

L.
