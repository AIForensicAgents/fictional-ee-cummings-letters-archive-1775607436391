cornfield arithmetic

Date: 1927-10-14
Recipient: August
Location: Iowa City, Iowa
Year: 1927
Themes: harvest, friendship, nostalgia, rural life, autumn

August,

the fields are doing their wide golden multiplication and even the scarecrow looks like a professor of light. your postcard arrived wearing dust and thumbs. i held it to my ear and heard the patient chewing of America. do you remember how we used to test the wind like coins, flipping handfuls of dry husks until they landed heads-up with summer?

now i am a city pocket full of nails and streetcar stubs and still the corn teaches me to count properly. one sky plus one sky equals a bigger silence. boots plus road equals a kind thought. loneliness divided by a dog equals something survivable. Mother says i am sentimental. i tell her the combine is just a faster hymn.

the boys at the elevator talk of prices as if they were weather; the weather talks of God as if He were a neighbor who borrows sugar and returns with pie. at dusk, barns remember their red the way old stories remember their laughter. i stood there and forgot to stand there and the crickets stitched me back into place.

come visit when the last wagon sighs home. i will make coffee that tastes like sleep surrendering. we will sit on the porch and solve nothing with excellence.

your friend who keeps a kernel in his throat for luck,

H.
