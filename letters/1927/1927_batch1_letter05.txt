april funeral without umbrellas

Date: 1927-05-03
Recipient: Mother
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1927
Themes: grief, family, memory, spring, ritual

Mother,

we put her in the good earth without umbrellas because she would have scolded the rain for worrying. Aunt Elise always believed weather was a polite visitor if you introduced yourself properly and offered milk. the preacher's voice wore through its coat and stood there shivering, true. i kept my hands in my pockets to keep from fixing anything (i have this useless talent for mending what is not broken).

a bird's black needle stitched the sky into a kind of sleeve and for an instant i thought of her wrists, those small busy rooms where buttons felt important. do you remember the pie she burned and refused to apologize for, claiming that even mistakes want their birthdays? i let the dirt fall soft, syllable after syllable, until it spelled: thank you for socks, for ironed silence, for the laugh that sounded like kitchen light.

at home i tried to be good with the kettle. i failed the way steam fails—beautifully, quickly. i hope you slept. grief is a chair in the hallway you bump into every time, until finally you keep your hips to the wall and learn. i am learning. i keep finding her in the way windows keep their breath on chilly mornings, surprised to be alive.

i will come sunday. i will bring oranges. we will peel them like little suns and eat without speeches.

your child (earnest, a little frayed),

E.
