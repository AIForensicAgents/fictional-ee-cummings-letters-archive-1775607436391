Sunday With Unfinished Sky

Date: 1923-06-24
Recipient: Mother
Location: Boston, MA
Year: 1923
Themes: family, faith, nostalgia, summer, gentleness

Dearest Mother,

Sunday takes off its hat and sits in the kitchen. bells are rinsing the air. I have boiled two eggs and thought of you exactly eleven times—no, a dozen, which seems more honest for eggs and mothers.

Today I walked by the Common where children were busy teaching grass to be surprised. a man played hymns on a cornet that kept remembering ragtime and blushing. I did not go inside a church; I let the street be my pew, and the sparrows were a choir with untrained but relevant faith.

Do not worry about my shoes; they have learned the city very gently. as for my heart, it is the sort that stops at corners and looks both ways, then crosses anyway. I keep my promises to you folded in the pocket where I would keep handkerchiefs, were I a better son.

Send news of the lilacs. I swear I will write more often, as if often were a country and I its visa.

Your boy,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
