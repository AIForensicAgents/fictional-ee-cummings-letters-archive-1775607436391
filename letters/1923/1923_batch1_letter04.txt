A Letter Folded Like a Bird

Date: 1923-04-28
Recipient: Lydia
Location: Santa Fe, NM
Year: 1923
Themes: landscape, transformation, solitude, desire, wind

Lydia,

the desert is a grammar that refuses conjunctions. everything here is and. and sky and bone-blue noon and cholla and the sound of your name refusing to be thirsty. adobe holds the day's heat like an animal with a secret. nights are sewing needles, cold and honest.

I have been learning to walk more slowly until the wind catches up to the news I carry for you, which is this: something small inside me has unbuttoned. I keep finding footprints where there were only shoes. I keep finding time where there was only schedule. perhaps you sent this. perhaps the mountains did.

If you unfold this letter carefully it may fly once; do not be alarmed. a letter must practice leaving, to arrive. I kissed the margin on the left, in case margins read from right to left in this climate.

Write me of the color of your kitchen in late afternoon. tell me if the kettle sings in a key you like. I will listen from here, holding my breath until it is yours.

Always (and always),
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
