Harvest of Small Fires

Date: 1923-10-31
Recipient: Eleanor
Location: Hartford, CT
Year: 1923
Themes: grief, autumn, ritual, memory, tenderness

Eleanor,

October loosens its bright buttons into the street. children, costumed as permission, tumble door to door; the moon pretends not to know them. I walked past three porches lit by careful flames and thought of the candle you kept in the kitchen for storms—how it made a church out of a spoon.

Grief is industrious tonight, but not unkind. it stacks its wood properly. it sharpens the match on its sleeve and waits for my consent. I give it sparingly, like sugar. the house takes a breath, holds it, and lets it go.

I have not put your ribbon away. it has become a compass that points to various sorts of north: the old laughter under the lilac, the letter you almost mailed, the rain that discovered both our names at once. you are not gone so much as shifted into a place my language cannot frame without leaning.

If you can, visit my dream by the back gate. I will leave it unlatched. there is tea in the cupboard that forgives everything.

With a gentleness I did not know I had,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
