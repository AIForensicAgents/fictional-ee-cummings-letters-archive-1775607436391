streetlamp sonata (for A.)

Date: 1923-02-14
Recipient: A.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1923
Themes: love, city, night, yearning, modernity, music

dear A,

four streetlamps have been practicing moonlight on the corner and the snow keeps forgetting to be cold whenever i say your name under my breath. the el coughs its bright iron syllables and the saxophone upstairs keeps falling in love with the wrong note (which, of course, is the right one for tonight).

i am learning the city by the taste of it: coffee bitter as true things, printer's ink running like a startled river across my hands. your last letter lives in my coat pocket where my fingers pretend to be brave. i read it between traffic-lights and pigeons, breathing the commas you left for me like small ladders.

this morning i saw a seamstress pin daylight to a dress and it fit. later, a boy sold me a paper and his grin said headlines are only rumors until the heart believes them. please believe this: i am clumsy with happiness and it keeps breaking open around you.

the room is a narrow ship; rain taps the windows in morse; i answer by moving nearer to the desk, which moves nearer to the lamp, which throws its warm coin on the page, which buys me one more minute of you. the typewriter does a little dance and misses a step and i forgive it (the way you forgave me for walking too fast past the bakery where light is baked with bread).

i will come tomorrow if the day has a door. if it does not, i will build one out of breath, out of sidewalks, out of every unmailed kiss.

keep the ribbon from your hair where the wind can see it, so the world will learn a better way to move.

ever,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
