Postcard Without a Stamp

Date: 1923-02-07
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Cambridge, MA
Year: 1923
Themes: family, ambition, memory, education, war-shadow

Thomas,

I write this on the back of a day I forgot to pay for. if it limps to you, forgive the ink for being winded. the Yard is a tidy puzzle of snow and brick, undergraduates buttoned like envelopes. I haunt the library where words keep their warm coats on hooks and the silence is a reliable clock.

You asked how a man earns his keep by courting air: poorly, gloriously. there are cheaper infinities (coal, for example), but none so faithful as a clean page. when the radiator ticks like an old insect I remember the thin tents and the fat stars and how nothing learned our names, not even the sky. I am not sad, only built with a hinge.

If Father asks, tell him I am industrious at last: I sweep the attic of my chest each morning, put all the dust into sentences, sell them for bread, and then eat the broom.

Write what the river looks like this week. I miss the way it pretends to be going somewhere.

Your brother,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
