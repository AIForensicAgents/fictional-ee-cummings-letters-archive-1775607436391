Streetlamp Psalm

Date: 1923-01-11
Recipient: Ada
Location: New York, NY
Year: 1923
Themes: love, city, winter, jazz, longing, modernity

Dear Ada,

tonight the streetlamps button the dark with pale coins, and I walk under their small astonishments, thinking of your wrists. snow learns my name softly (once, twice) and disappears into the hush before it can say it right. I have been good with my typewriter, terrible with my heart. the keys obey; I do not.

In the window of Katz's a trumpet is kissing the air into gold. jazz is a ladder the moon keeps forgetting to climb. will you write and tell me how your laughter is growing these days? sometimes I put a cup on the sill and it fills with invisible music; sometimes with quiet.

I have rented a room with a ceiling like an eyelid. when it blinks, the train shivers the bones of the block. I am busy inventing a grammar that knows your name. I am busy failing at it, joyfully.

If you come Saturday I will buy two oranges and a newspaper we won't read. bring your breath; I will bring mine; and between them, maybe, a city.

Yours in the weather between,
—N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
