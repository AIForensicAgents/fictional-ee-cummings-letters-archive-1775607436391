Night Telegram to the Sea

Date: 1923-08-03
Recipient: Samuel (aboard the Dorothea)
Location: Coney Island, NY
Year: 1923
Themes: travel, friendship, sea, carnival, distance

Sam,

Writing as if the wire were salt and the alphabet a flock of gulls. the boardwalk laughs too loudly, but I am faithful to the darker surf beyond the lamps. the Ferris wheel keeps proposing to the stars and being turned down, politely, again and again.

Are you well, ship-friend? do the ropes remember your hands? does the engine dream in a language you can sleep inside? I keep hearing the sea insist on its difficult yes. it washes the footprints off bravely made plans and then offers the same sand back, new, as if we hadn't tried already.

Coney sells sugar to the night until the night forgets itself. I bought none. I kept my hunger for your stories—the whale of a wave you saw, the storm with its wet teeth, the calm that made you suspicious.

Send me a word from the horizon. any word. I will fold it into a paper boat and float it in my sink.

Affectionately and awake,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
