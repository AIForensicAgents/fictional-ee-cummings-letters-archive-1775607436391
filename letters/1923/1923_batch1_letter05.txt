Concerning Rain on the Factory Roof

Date: 1923-05-16
Recipient: Mr. Halpern, Editor
Location: Chicago, IL
Year: 1923
Themes: industry, art, negotiation, modernity, sound

Dear Mr. Halpern,

Enclosed—forgive the superstition of that word when there is no envelope inside an envelope—are three poems which arrived after listening to rain articulate a factory roof. it spoke fluent tin, dialect steam. somewhere in the hush between shift whistles I heard the small republic of words vote yes.

You asked for something with a pulse you could sell to men who wear their hours like wrenches. perhaps this is it; perhaps not. the city is a furnace that burns the name off things, then asks them to answer roll call anyway. I keep trying to smuggle their true names past the guard in my throat.

If you cannot use them, send back their shadows at least. I am fond of my failures and prefer them home by supper.

I remain, industrious and unmanageable,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
