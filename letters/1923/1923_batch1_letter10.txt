Snow at the Threshold

Date: 1923-12-22
Recipient: Self
Location: Providence, RI
Year: 1923
Themes: introspection, renewal, winter, patience, self-compassion

N.,

It has begun to snow as if the year were erasing itself delicately, as if to spare our feelings. doorways grow their white beards. the street forgets where it was going and turns back, smiling.

You have done poorly and well, both faithfully. you have kept odd promises to the moon and broken sensible ones to the bank. you have loved unreasonably, which is the only arithmetic that adds up. when frightened, you put a chair in front of the door; when brave, you opened the window instead. this is acceptable.

Here is a plan for tomorrow: put on yesterday's boots and go watch the river think. carry a pocket of bread for the birds and another pocket of silence for yourself. tell no one what you are forgiving.

When the kettle sings, answer it. when the page stares, look back kindly. when the year knocks, let it in with an almost-shy welcome and call it by a simple name: begin.

—N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
