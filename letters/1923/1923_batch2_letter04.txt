vinegar and moonlight

Date: 1923-11-03
Recipient: Mireille
Location: Montparnasse, Paris
Year: 1923
Themes: romance, city, exile, aesthetics, hunger, invitation

Mireille,

At the café the glasses sweat like nervous dancers and the waiter writes our hunger in a secret alphabet of wrists. I have discovered a genius of vinegar in the salad and a moonlight in your sleeve; between the two a revolution occurs and I am politely overthrown.

You told me the boulevards are not roads but stages. Tonight a baker's window sighed at me with a hundred warm breathing loaves, and somewhere a violin unbuttoned the dark. Painters argued colors into submission and then set them free again. Words here have pockets; I hide foolishness in mine and pay for your laugh with it.

Do you remember the bridge where the river tried to hold the moon like a coin and kept dropping it delightedly? Meet me there tomorrow when the barges quit pretending to be planets. I will bring a paper boat for the wind to read and we will give our quiet to the water. If rain comes, good; I have always wanted to be translated into weather.

You say I write too many parentheses. Perhaps. They are only spare arms for when I cannot resist holding the world closer. If I have been abrupt, forgive the foreignness of my hope; it arrived ahead of my passport.

I will leave this letter under your cup, where the dregs will bless it with a constellation of grounds. Walk slowly so the city can keep up with you.

À toi,
L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
