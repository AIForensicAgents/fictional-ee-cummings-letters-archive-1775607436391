Ink & Bread

Date: 1923-03-19
Recipient: Miriam (the landlady)
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1923
Themes: poverty, art, language, expatriate life, humor

Madame Miriam,

The walls are learning my French faster than I am. they nod at my verbs and shrug at my nouns. please forgive the rent arriving in syllables; I am paying in advance with a small promise that glows—two poems nearly finished, one loaf not yet.

Montparnasse is a workshop of sudden faces. painters drag daylight up the stairs like a stubborn piano. I keep company with the smell of turpentine and coffee and that eternal bread which is somehow both yesterday and tomorrow. in the morning the baker's bell rings and the idea of hunger becomes very polite.

You asked if I hammer my typewriter at night. I do, but softly, as if the paper were a door to somewhere kinder, and I must knock without frightening it. some lines arrive with their hair wet, as if they swam the Seine; others with dusty shoes, from the catacombs of my pockets.

If I am late by two days, consider this letter a curtain drawn back: see, the room is full of work. consider also my cheeks, confident with shame.

With affection and the sincerest francs I can gather,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
