On the Edge of the Typewriter

Date: 1923-09-15
Recipient: Ruth (stenographer on the third floor)
Location: Detroit, MI
Year: 1923
Themes: work, intimacy, machines, hope, city

Ruth,

I have watched your hands domesticate thunder. keys and carbons and those small neat cages you build around men's unruly sentences. outside, the factories count to ten in smoke and then begin again. it is a counting with a paycheck, a pulse that sometimes forgets it has a heart.

Forgive this letter for climbing the stairs to you. it knocks without appointment. I only meant to say: when you tuck a stray hair behind your ear, time is briefly well-behaved. the office clock looks away. the boss drinks his coffee more carefully. the paper, pitying itself, grows softer.

I have no automobile (shameful confession in this city of tires), only a bicycle that believes in me on good days. but if you wish, I will ride beside you at the hour when whistles unclench, and we can watch the river unbutton itself from the bridges.

Consider this a blueprint for a small room called maybe, with two chairs and the bright idea of evening.

Yrs,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
