Postmark: Montparnasse Wind

Date: 1924-04-10
Recipient: Aunt Louisa
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1924
Themes: travel, family, art, hunger, modernity, hope

dear aunt louisa,

how well you taught me to sew a straight seam; now i stitch days together with crooked blue thread. paris is a dress that never stops rustling. cafes bloom along boulevard raspail; chairs open like brass flowers and people sit inside them, bright bees of talk.

i rent a room where the ceiling slopes as if bowing to an invisible violin. bread is a serious religion here; we break it and clouds of hymn rise. i paint a little, i write a little, i hunger a little (but not unhappily). at night the bands try to teach the air new hips. the trumpet is a small sun with a loud idea.

today an old woman sold me tulips from a cart the color of patience. she wrapped them in yesterday's news; the headlines bled politely onto my fingers. i carried them through wind that smelled like rainkeeping promises. i thought of your kitchen: the lemon-curd light, the clock who believed in us.

i am safe. my french improves in sudden blushes. when i say miss, the word finds its knees and prays for you. send news of the back garden: does the lilac still lean against the fence as if listening for the neighbor's secrets

i hope i am becoming the sort of niece whose days make a small, brave sound when opened.

with devoted rumpledness,
n.
