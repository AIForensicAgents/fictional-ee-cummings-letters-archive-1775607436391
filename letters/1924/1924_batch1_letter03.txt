blue envelope from the port

Date: 1924-03-03
Recipient: Brother Elias
Location: Marseilles, France
Year: 1924
Themes: travel, faith, sea, restlessness, observation

Brother Elias,

the harbor smells of a thousand decided lives—tar, salt, lemon, diesel—each odor a heavy coin pressed into the palm. gulls draw white parentheses around the mast-trees and inside them the water re-says itself until even doubt grows gills.

i am writing from a café with tables like small red stages. a boy smokes as if it were prayer and the waitress polishes glasses into crystal convictions. your last letter told me to look for the holiness of ordinary breath; today i found it billowing from the bread man's bicycle, warm and precise.

at night the ships light their ribs, gentle skeletons, and i am not lonely so much as wide—like the sea practicing forever. do you remember when we were children and we believed the horizon was the place the sky kept its promises? i wrote that in chalk on the quay and the tide has accepted it into its archive of dissolving truths.

i will not join any monastery but i have begun to bow (inwardly) to the quiet between waves. bless me with a scrap of psalm if you think it will fit inside my pocket; i am nearly out of pockets.

with salt-stiff affection,
A.
