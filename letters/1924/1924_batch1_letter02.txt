ink and snow

Date: 1924-02-14
Recipient: Clara
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1924
Themes: snow, letters, affection, study, domestic quiet

Clara,

the university bell shook morning out of its sleeve and the yard wore a soft astonishment of snow. i brought the typewriter to the window; its black teeth stalled on your name, which prefers to be written in the warm hush of a pen. so i give in to ink, the old companion—how it hums along the paper like a low river, how it forgets to be straight when the heart bends.

there is a small coffee steeple on my desk, steam making a congregation of the air. people pass the window with buttons bright as little planets, and each footprint is the modest confession of a boot. it is valentine's by calendar, but by weather it is candle and mitten and your scarf around my throat, though you are not here to knot it.

i am a practical dreamer today: i sharpened pencils, i balanced coins, i memorized the curve of your ear from memory (a foolish arithmetic). if you come, i will read you the margin-notes the snow leaves on the world. if you do not, i will mail you a handful anyway, gathered in a handkerchief of breath.

keep warm. keep impossible. keep me.

Yours,
E.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
