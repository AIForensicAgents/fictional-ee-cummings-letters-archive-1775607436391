desert arithmetic

Date: 1924-09-15
Recipient: Isamu
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1924
Themes: friendship, landscape, art, silence, craft

Isamu,

the desert counts differently: one sky plus one sky equals more sky. my rooms are made of adobe truth and shadow argument. i put a stone on the table and the table softened. today i learned how quiet has bones.

i visited a pueblo where the ladders lean into the past as if asking for forgiveness or tea. a woman with hands like weather made bread from the same mathematics as the mountains: water + patience + fire = the mouth’s best idea. i ate and understood a little.

you would love the edges here—how a line is a negotiation, how light behaves like a courteous knife. i tried to carve the afternoon and it carved back, lifting a curve out of my stubbornness. send me the rumor of your latest sculpture; i need to know what the wind said when it met your chisel.

nights are a long animal breathing. coyotes embroider the darkness with thread you can almost hear. i lie awake adding distances: chicago to here + my name to yours. the sum refuses to be sorrow. it is a new tool i am learning to hold without fear.

come when the moon is thin and brave. we will make a grammar from stones.

faithfully,
R.
