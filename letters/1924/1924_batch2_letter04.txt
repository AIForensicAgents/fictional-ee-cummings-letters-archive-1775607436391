Studio Window with One Stubborn Star

Date: 1924-10-02
Recipient: Marta
Location: Berlin, Germany
Year: 1924
Themes: city, longing, loss, music, change, art, night

marta,

i keep the window cracked to let the night practice its piano. trams approach and depart like deliberate insects, their bellies lit with faces. sometimes i pretend i can read the future in their windows—every stop a new punctuation, every passenger an unfinished sentence.

your chair is still here: lean, aloof, waiting for the secret weight of you. i talk to it because the city is expensive with its listening. the radio murmurs a waltz that remembers how to limp. money flakes in my pockets like old paint; i pay the baker in confetti and he gives me bread shaped like forgiveness.

i would tell you i am brave, but bravery has moved into a cheaper district. so i am only awake. i am awake when the lamps go out and a single star nails the sky to itself. i am awake when the downstairs couple argues in cracked porcelain. i am awake when the kettle sings the word almost.

you were the blue that stayed behind my eyes after i looked at the lake too long. go on, say you are a continent away; maps are only shy paper. the body keeps an outlaw geography. yours is a country my hands still remember to name.

if a letter could cross a room, step softly, take your sleeve, it would. instead it must become this small whisper that fits in the mouth of the post. place it on your tongue a moment before opening; i have salted it with yes.

from the corner where paint dries slower,
l.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
