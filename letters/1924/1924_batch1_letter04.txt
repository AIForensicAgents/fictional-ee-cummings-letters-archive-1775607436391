in the green room of thunder

Date: 1924-04-19
Recipient: Madame Aurelia
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1924
Themes: art, performance, spring, desire, modernity

Madame Aurelia,

your theatre is a body rehearsing its own astonishment. behind the curtain tonight the rain applauded first, then the audience remembered their hands. i watched your actress uncork a silence and pour it over us until our throats shone with it. you have taught the stage to breathe in cursive.

after, in the green room, the mirrors were full of weather. rouge became weather. pins became weather. the understudy cried a beautiful comma and went on smoking. i wanted to tell you how your direction makes the furniture volunteer for peril, but your cigarette already knew; its ash fell like deliberate snow on the piano.

we are making a century that walks on pointes in boots. the boulevards clatter with umbrellas like black beetles promising news. i have brought you a ribbon pulled from a postcard vendor's mouth; he recited every city as if it were a girl he'd almost kissed.

teach me to open light with the small knife of gesture. teach me to throw my voice into the wings and have it return wearing pearls. and if you see the thunder again, let it borrow my coat; i no longer need certainty when rain is speaking.

Always your devoted conspirator,
L.
