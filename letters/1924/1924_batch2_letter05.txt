Between the Stations (a letter folded like dawn)

Date: 1924-12-29
Recipient: Father
Location: On the Lake Shore Limited, en route east
Year: 1924
Themes: reconciliation, travel, winter, family, memory, forgiveness

father,

the train sews america together with a hot needle. sparks fall outside and are stepped out by the snow. i sit between a man who sleeps like a question and a window that keeps answering: fields, barns, a river loosening its cold grammar.

i have rehearsed the apology so long it has learned to walk on its hind legs. perhaps let us try something simpler. when the conductor punched my ticket, the tiny moons fell in my lap; i saved them. here they are. i mean to say i have kept small evidences of belonging.

do you remember the winter we fixed the fence by lantern the wire bit our gloves and still we laughed because the cows mistook us for sunrise. i have been carrying that light like a folding knife.

the whistle bends the dark until it almost breaks. every station is a name i could have been. i think of your hands: blunt, correct, teaching wood to confess its grain. i think i am finally learning to be quiet in the useful way.

when i step off, if you are there, let the first word be bread. i will answer with coffee. afterward we can speak exactly as much as the table requires.

your son with his ticket halves,
j.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
