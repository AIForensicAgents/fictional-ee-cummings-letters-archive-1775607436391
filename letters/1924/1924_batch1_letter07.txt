eight postcards i didn't buy

Date: 1924-08-02
Recipient: Mr. Quinn
Location: Berlin, Germany
Year: 1924
Themes: art, economy, modernity, humor, postwar city

Dear Mr. Quinn,

you asked for curious things and i have a pocketful: 1) a postcard of the Brandenburg Gate wearing a sky of nervous blue. i did not buy it because the sky was doing all the work. 2) a dancer with legs like scissors for ribbon, cutting the air. i did not buy her because she was already signing my breath.

3) a factory by the canal swallowing coal like complicated bread. not purchased; the smoke had my name on it. 4) an alley with a gramophone at its mouth, promises at 78 revolutions. i left it for the cat who sells time by the saucer.

5) a boy selling newspapers with his elbows. he stood inside inflation as if it were a coat two sizes too large. i touched the card and it touched back. i did not buy it because our commerce is already tender. 6) a cabaret doorway opening like an eyelid. i prefer doors that blink first.

7) a statue repaired with wire and hope. 8) a woman in a hat that forgave the century. i did not buy either; my suitcase is allergic to nobility.

i am fine, uneasy, electric. send any money you do not need and i will turn it into soup, tickets, and possibly a painting no one has the courage to like yet.

yours in exchange and experiment,
K.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
