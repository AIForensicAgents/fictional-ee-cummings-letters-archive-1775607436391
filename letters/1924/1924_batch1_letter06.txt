riverboats & violets

Date: 1924-06-12
Recipient: Sylvie
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1924
Themes: jazz, summer, yearning, river, sensuality

Sylvie,

night in this city invents a fresh tongue and teaches it to the trumpet. the horn leans out of the window of a throat and the alley answers with its most accurate hips. we danced until the floorboards learned our names; even the ceiling fan kept time with a sleepy blade of mercy.

the river is a long black sentence stroking the levee until it purrs. men with hats like private moons talk low about shipments, about bourbon in brown paper prayers. i bought violets from a girl who kept them in a blue bucket and i am sending you their smell folded carefully between these lines.

we were not careful with our joy, you and i, and therefore it was honest. i have a table at a café where the sugar is damp and the clock forgets noon. come sit with me before summer evaporates into rumor. bring your laugh and that dress the color of forgiven sins.

p.s. in the mirror i found a boy who believed in forever. i told him forever is a riverboat: loud, vain, shining, and somehow always departing. he forgave me and asked for violets.

with sweat and sweetness,
T.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
