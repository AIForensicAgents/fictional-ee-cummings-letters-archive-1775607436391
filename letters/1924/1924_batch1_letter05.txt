cornfields telegram (never sent)

Date: 1924-05-27
Recipient: Father
Location: Des Moines, Iowa
Year: 1924
Themes: family, home, forgiveness, rural life, change

Father,

the wheat stands like a congregation listening for your cough. every fencepost keeps the old hymnal in its grain and the barn's bent back remembers my first lie, small and brave as a grasshopper. i have come home by train and silence; the station master's watch had the same stubborn tick as your jaw when a storm organizes itself over the fields.

i want to telegram you because cities taught me the speed of wanting, but i know the wire would thin what must be said. so i write with dust on my sleeves and a swallow scratching the sky into ribbons.

do you remember the summer the well went dry and we learned how to fold our thirst into patience? i am thirsty for your forgiveness without asking for it. i have a new suit that makes me a stranger and a hole inside it where your advice would fit.

there is talk of tractors with hearts of fire, of roads poured like milk from town to town. progress has a beautiful handwriting and still i miss the way your boots argued with the porch. if i post this, the letter will betray me; if i burn it, the smoke will tell you everything.

your son, standing but not tall,
J.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
