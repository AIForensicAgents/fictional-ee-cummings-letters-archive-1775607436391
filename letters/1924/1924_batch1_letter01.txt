streetlamp sonata

Date: 1924-01-08
Recipient: M.
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1924
Themes: city, longing, winter, music, night

Dear M,

this city plays its piano with teeth of light and each street is a throat full of yellow chords. tonight i walked until the cobbles remembered our feet. the wind kept small change in its pockets and paid the moon to stay a little longer.

i promised myself not to write and here are my hands explaining you to the paper—how your laugh knits a scarf around my ribs, how your name breaks into stars when i close my eyes on the subway. the train makes a long hush (like a cat arranging itself) and all the strangers rearrange into the idea of us.

forgive the way my sentences lean. i have been drinking from the neon violin outside the bakery on sixth; the loaves were warm enough to understand, the baker dusted me with flour and blessing.

i want to walk with you until morning is a pocket we have turned inside out. i want to be reckless and useful as a match. the snow, shy and exacting, has begun and every flake is a small permission.

be where you are, perfectly. i will fold this letter into your absence and send it toward you like a sparrow who has learned a new word: home.

—A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
