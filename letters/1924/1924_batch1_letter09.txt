harvest telegram with rain

Date: 1924-10-10
Recipient: Lina
Location: Trieste, Italy
Year: 1924
Themes: distance, harvest, rain, language, memory

Lina,

rain has taught the harbor to whisper its consonants. i write you from beneath an awning that believes in umbrellas the way the sea believes in returning. the cranes make a slow alphabet against a sky that keeps forgetting the word for blue.

i should telegraph but the wires are crowded with weddings and ships. so here is my message at the speed of ink: the grapes were gathered and the barrels sleep like fat monks. your letter arrived folded as a gull; i unfolded it into weather.

i remember your hands sorting olives, exact as prayers. mine are clumsy with the season but generous—i keep dropping small kindnesses and they bruise into sweetness. do you ever taste a word before speaking it? today i tasted departure: a copper coin dissolved on the tongue.

when the sun remembers itself we will walk the moles and count the boots. bring the scarf that smells of fennel and soap. i will bring the joke that refuses to be told, so we must live it instead.

until then, keep your window open to let the rain practice. it must learn your name.

con affetto e una tasca di vento,
M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
