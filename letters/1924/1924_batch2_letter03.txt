Wheat & Quiet (a small thunder)

Date: 1924-07-18
Recipient: Brother Elias
Location: Near Wichita, Kansas
Year: 1924
Themes: nature, work, machines, family, memory, rural life

eli—

the fields have learned the wide language again. wheat speaks in brown syllables and the wind answers with its many mouths. i walk the fencerow early, boots sugared with dust, and the sun lifts like a pale coin someone will spend all day.

the new tractor shivers through the furrows, a tin beetle with stubborn light in its belly. it coughs and the crows rearrange the sky. i miss the old team but i am learning to love the noise: it is the century clearing its throat.

last night the storm rehearsed at the horizon, a dark theater with excellent lighting. when it arrived, the house grew small around our names. i wrote yours on the fogged window and it sailed away when the pane cooled. afterward, the frogs practiced their green arithmetic from the ditch and everything added up to enough.

mother keeps the table honest. she folds the cloth so exactly the corners stop arguing. she asked about you, how is the city, does it still wear its hat indoors. i said yes, and sometimes it remembers to tip it.

come when the bales stand like square suns fallen to earth. come and we will drink from the pump and be filled with the very old water.

your brother under the generous sky,
thomas

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
