Ink Snow Over Second Avenue

Date: 1924-01-21
Recipient: Clara
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1924
Themes: love, winter, city, longing, art

dear clara,

the snow is teaching second avenue to whisper; each flake a small apology that melts before it finishes. my room steams lightly, like a teacup of breath. the radiator repeats its warm alphabet (hiss s s s) and i pretend to understand.

today i wrestled the typewriter until it purred a little poem. the keys are black pebbles skipping the white river. when i miss you (which is my occupation) the subway lifts its great iron shoulder, carries me through a necklace of stations, and returns me to the one without you.

i keep your ribbon in a book the color of midnight. sometimes i open to the ribbon and the whole room smells like your unbuttoned laugh. it is a small weather, private and sudden; it thunders yes in the quiet.

if you are cold, cup this letter in your hands. i have hidden pockets of august between the lines. listen: the streetcars ring their thin bells. a man shouts oranges. snow answers softly.

i love you not like a trumpet but like a window opening at three in the morning; the city steps inside barefoot.

yours (until ink forgets to be dark),
sam
