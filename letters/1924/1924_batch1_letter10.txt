small prayer for midnight trains

Date: 1924-12-21
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1924
Themes: winter, motion, hope, solitude, trains

Ruth,

the solstice lays a cold hand at the base of the city’s neck and the city purrs despite itself. i am at the station where the benches hold more secrets than any church. snow worries the tracks into lace and every whistle translates itself into almost.

i bought a ticket to somewhere sensible and folded it into the wrong pocket. perhaps i will go to you by accident—my favorite method of faith. the porters move with the grace of men who have seen too many goodbyes and still take your suitcase gently as if it contained a sleeping finch.

i have been saving a small prayer for midnight trains: let them carry our bad decisions into smaller towns where they can grow gardens. let them return with coal and forgiveness. let the windows be kind to our reflections, which are tired and brave and learning how to be both.

if you have a candle, light it near the clock and tell me when the shadow reaches the frame. i will drink coffee exactly then and we will be together in the empire of timing. until i arrive, open your book to any page and read my name beneath every comma.

from the platform’s tender edge,
S.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
