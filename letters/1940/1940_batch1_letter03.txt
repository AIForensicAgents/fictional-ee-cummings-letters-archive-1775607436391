blackout alphabet

Date: 1940-09-13
Recipient: Lark
Location: Bloomsbury, London
Year: 1940
Themes: war, night, courage, love, city

dear lark,

when evening whistles, all windows swallow their stars and become well-behaved blanks. we practice not shining. streets learn to memorize their own feet, and the moon slips a shawl over her silver shoulders as if she, too, were instructed to say hush. sirens chew the air; we answer by opening our chests and tucking the small bright animals of our hearts into our pockets.

i make tea, a fragile steam of bravery you can hold without burning if your hands know how to ask. the cups clink like two coins paying for a quiet room. in that hush i hear your voice crossing the channel of distance, not a word, just the breath between syllables where the truest letters live.

i do not pretend at courage, only at togetherness. we sit under the table when the sky growls, knees touching like conspirators. i have learned to kiss the dust off a photograph and to tell joke-stories to the kettle until it laughs. the streetwears its dark like a good coat; under it, hands find hands.

i slip this letter under the door of the night. perhaps it will crawl to you, a small faithful animal with ink on its paws. keep your pocket a little open.

lightly, with both eyes,
i.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
