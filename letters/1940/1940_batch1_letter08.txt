salt suitcase gospel

Date: 1940-12-02
Recipient: Esther
Location: Marseille, France
Year: 1940
Themes: waiting, sea, exile, bureaucracy, hope

dear esther,

the port is busy with shoes and salt. every step here squeaks a little with brine and hope. offices chew papers slowly, then spit out tomorrows that must be rinsed and tried on again. i have learned to wait with my spine; it stands like a narrow pier no storm can convince otherwise.

there is a man who sells ink by the thimble and news by the rumor. i buy the ink. each stamp i press is a small red prayer that remembers longer than i do. our suitcases grow fluent in the language of benches. a gull stole the word tomorrow from my hand and then dropped it back on my head with a laugh; i took this as an omen with feathers.

at night the sea practices breathing for us. we sleep between the pages of our documents and dream of doors. if the number is called we will not run; we will float, because the heart has its own harbor and it opens like a mouth that knows your name.

keep your scarf. keep your song. when the ink dries we will be paper-boats that do not drown.

pocketing a horizon for you,
r.
