harbor with thickened breath

Date: 1940-03-18
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Halifax, Nova Scotia
Year: 1940
Themes: family, sea, waiting, memory, work

dear thomas,

fog rummages through the harbor like a grandmother through buttons, patient, choosing the one that fastens everything. ships come and go with their iron prayers sealed in the throat; docks creak a little hymn and gulls paint quick commas on the sky. i found a room above a chandlery, wallpaper full of faded ships that refuse to sink even in paper seas.

i keep the pocketknife father left us; it opens fish, letters, and sometimes afternoons. with it i carve time into smaller times and feed them to the stove. the tea here tastes of salt and waiting. on the wharf a boy counts ropes for luck; he gets to thirteen and begins again. i understand him.

they say convoys gather like dark bees and that the ocean has teeth. i say the ocean has ears too, because when i speak your name the tide inches closer as if to overhear. do you still walk the ridge behind the mill when weather behaves? imagine me there, boots full of sky, pockets rattling with anchors of rain.

write to me with lighthouse handwriting. tell me the farm has kept our footsteps where the mud remembers. i will send you a length of harbor wind, knotted three times for courage.

your brother,
a.
