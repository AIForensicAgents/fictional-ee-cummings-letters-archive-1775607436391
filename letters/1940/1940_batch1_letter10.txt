lighthouse with a heartbeat

Date: 1940-08-18
Recipient: Etta
Location: Pemaquid Point, Maine
Year: 1940
Themes: nature, love, distance, domestic, resilience

dear etta,

the lighthouse blinks its patient morse and the rocks answer with foam that sounds like yes typed a thousand times. jars on the shelf breathe summer; peaches hold their tongues in syrup and pretend they are suns in captivity. potatoes sleep like planets under a thin quilt of dirt.

your last letter smelled of laundry and cinnamon. i read it on the porch until the mosquitoes joined the punctuation. you wrote that distance is a stubborn mule; i agree, but i have a carrot of belief and a rope of jokes. sometimes the fog comes on all at once and steals every line from the shore; then the horn says i am here i am here i am here and i believe it.

i mend a net with clumsy grace and think of you mending the hem of a day so it does not unravel. if winter is a question i will answer with matches and tea. when i sleep, the tide climbs a little up my dreams and leaves shells of laughter on the pillow.

kiss the dog for me if he consents to diplomacy. i send you a stone warmed in my pocket all afternoon; place it where your cold begins.

always, with salt and light,
h.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
