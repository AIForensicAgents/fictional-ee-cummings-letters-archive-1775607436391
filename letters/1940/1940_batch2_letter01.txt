a small valentine with city snow

Date: 1940-02-14
Recipient: Marta
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1940
Themes: love, city, winter, intimacy, hope

dear marta,

tonight the el brushed the rooftops with its iron eyelashes and every window shivered a little (as though a piano were quietly clearing its throat). i walked home with all the sidewalks lifting their white shoulders, the snow a patient hush that made our corner seem almost country—as if horses might turn from the river and ask for sugar by the door.

there are words i keep stitched under the lining of my coat. when the wind slips its small cold hand between my buttons i can feel them move, restless little birds. they refuse to be arranged like soldiers and i don’t blame them: you are not a parade, you are a lamp burning in a glove.

we could pretend (just for tonight) that the city is a single room, and that room our breath. the radiator gives its soft sermon, the kettle complains, the neighbor upstairs practices a wilted fox-trot, and i—i am learning your name in several dialects of silence. each syllable a pocket of warmth: mar—ta. when i say it the streetlight leans closer.

please forgive the cheap candy and the envelope addressed twice; the snow kept rearranging the stamps. i would rather send you my left mitten, the one that carries the smell of hot chestnuts and ink. or the blank side of this evening’s sky, which i have folded carefully between these pages (don’t unfold all at once; the stars have a habit of spilling).

if you open the door i will hang my wet scarf over your chair and the city can do its worst beyond the latch. we will be two small conspirators of warmth, sharing an apple, a story, the stray thread that tethers tomorrow to now. and should the trains howl or the wind frown, we will practice the oldest trick: i will touch your wrist and it will be spring in three heartbeats.

yours from the corner where dusk keeps blushing,
—a
