arrivals/ departures (our ribs for tracks)

Date: 1940-10-05
Recipient: N.
Location: Union Station, Chicago
Year: 1940
Themes: travel, love, parting, modernity, memory

dear n.,

this station is a stomach full of trains. the roof swallows smoke and the smoke forgets to be sky. boards flicker their alphabet of leaving; each click is a small hammer building a bridge i am not ready to cross. i carry your name like second-class luggage under my tongue, scuffed, brave, determined to arrive.

on platform four a porter hums a hymn that might be about shoes or about god or both. i understand. every suitcase is a little chapel; we kneel to fasten them and pray not to forget the toothbrush of our courage. a child waves a red mitten at the idea of elsewhere and the idea waves back.

if time were polite it would let us loiter in its vestibule. instead, the whistle edits even the word stay. i will meet you in the seam between clocks, where noon kisses noon and nobody apologizes. send a telegram with only the word stay and i will press it beneath my wrist so the pulse learns its spelling.

my train coughs. the conductor tears a moon-shaped bite from my ticket. i am going but also i am staying, because i have folded you into the smallest pocket of my coat.

with every mile and none,
k.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
