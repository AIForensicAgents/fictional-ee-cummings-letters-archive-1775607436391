desert letter with thunder

Date: 1940-07-22
Recipient: Mother
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1940
Themes: family, landscape, art, becoming, weather

dear mother,

the clouds move like slow cattle over the mesa and when they finally gather themselves the thunder lays its broad hand on every adobe shoulder. the air smells of dust learning to sing. i have found a studio with a crooked window; it frames a slice of sky so blue it makes my brushes whisper please.

i mix colors with water from the pump and my fingers discover the grammar of clay. the radio from albuquerque coughs dances and local baseball and a preacher who loves the word mercy as if it were his last coin. i turn the knob until the station is a soft ghost; then i can hear the lizards practicing their quick cursive along the wall.

i am less afraid of failing when the desert watches. its eyes are so patient, mother, one feels every mistake will be sand soon enough and look at the sand: it shines. i eat beans with green chiles that teach my mouth new prayers. at night the sky finds its freckles and refuses modesty.

kiss the garden for me, and our old dog if he allows ceremony. keep the kettle singing. i send you rain wrapped in paper; open it carefully over the basil.

your child with paint on every pocket,
r.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
