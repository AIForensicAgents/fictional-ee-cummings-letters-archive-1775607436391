river teaches the horn to breathe

Date: 1940-04-11
Recipient: Leon
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1940
Themes: music, friendship, night, cities, joy

dear leon,

tonight the river undresses to its muddy bones and the horn writes its bright mistakes across the wet skin of the air. we drank a weather of sugar and you kept time with your shoulder the way only saints and drummers do. the alley cats signed their names on the dumpsters with cursive yowls; i clapped for every vowel.

in the square an old man swallowed the moon in slices and fed it back to us through a trumpet. the lamp over the bar blinked out and then remembered itself; i took this as permission to be a little forgiven. we walked home with our coats unbuttoned to prove we trusted the night.

if you come north bring the horn and a pocket of heat. i have a room that believes in echoes and a neighbor who keeps bees in his sentences. we can practice until the windows sweat and then let them cool like pies on a sill. the tune you showed me still walks around my ribs looking for a door; i will leave one unlocked.

ps: tell bessie the moon owes her a nickel and i will collect it in proper change.

with a backbeat and a grin,
d.
