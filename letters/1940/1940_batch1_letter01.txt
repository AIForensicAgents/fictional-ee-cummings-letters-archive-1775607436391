snow writes the city in small letters

Date: 1940-01-03
Recipient: Avery
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1940
Themes: longing, winter, city, art, intimacy

dear avery,

tonight the snow decides every rooftop should be a soft hat and my window is a patient eye. the radiator hisses like a very old cat learning a new lullaby. i am typing you into the room; each key is a bootprint crossing the white margin of the night. outside, streets curl into comma-shapes and the lamplight is a yellow vowel that refuses to end.

i went walking (my scarf a warm question) and the city answered with footfall-riddles. a saxophone dripped from the basement of thompson street, slow honey, and your name rose with it. i carry your name like a small match i am not yet brave enough to strike. perhaps the match knows better; perhaps it is saving us for the precise second when frost changes its mind.

the news on the radio breathes cotton and whispers distances i cannot pronounce. i turned it down until even the announcer seemed to be sleeping. meanwhile, my cup fogs its own poem and the windows do arithmetic with breath: one, two, two-and-a-half (i fail at counting when you are not here).

send me your heartbeat folded twice. send me the map of your winter hands. i will meet you where the avenue forgets its number and the snow remembers our footprints are a grammar of staying.

yours in frost & ink,
m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
