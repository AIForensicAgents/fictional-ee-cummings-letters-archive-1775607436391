spring with a stammer

Date: 1940-05-08
Recipient: Celeste
Location: Montparnasse, Paris
Year: 1940
Themes: uncertainty, spring, love, departure, city

dear celeste,

apricot light leaks over the rooftops and down into the cups at the cafe, sweetening even the spoon-handles. the chestnuts are blooming as if the world had signed a treaty with bees. yet the air has pockets of newspaper; each headline is a bird that cannot quite decide where to land. i buy a violet from a woman whose hands remember gardens; she pins it to my lapel like a promise with a small cough.

i walk past the station and the trains sleep with their eyes open, dreaming tickets. voices braid themselves into a language of leaving, not quite french, not quite anything. if i say come, is it a boat; if i say stay, is it a passport. i have practiced both words in the mirror until they fogged and forgave me.

you laugh in italics. write soon, even if your sentence has more petals than grammar. we can pack the sky into a handkerchief and tie it at the four corners and call it ours. bring nothing heavy, only your crowded pockets of sun.

until the lilacs finish their oath,
j.
