bridge becomes a verb

Date: 1940-10-31
Recipient: Professor Ravenel
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1940
Themes: art, apprenticeship, city, ambition, guidance

dear professor ravenel,

the bridge winks its red eyelid through the fog and suddenly verbs are easy. to span, to lift, to risk. the bay chews the edges of the city but politely, as if tasting before asking for seconds. i have rented a small room with a window that believes in tomorrow and a door that argues but finally agrees.

in the school studio i stretch canvases the way sailors prepare faith. i measure, i pull, i knot. paint behaves like weather; some mornings it rains exactly where i wanted a sun. you once told me accidents are just punctual miracles with no paperwork. i repeat this when my lines revolt and form their own parliament.

at night the neon makes a soft jungle. streetcars hum an elegant threat and the hills learn to lean out of respect. i sketch until the pencils sigh. please write me margins wild enough to wander in. send your criticism folded into a paper crane; i promise to set it flying over my desk and obey the angle of its wings.

i remain your stubborn pupil with pockets full of bridges i am not finished crossing.

faithfully ink-stained,
s.
