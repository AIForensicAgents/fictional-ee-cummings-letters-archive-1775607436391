a harbor folded in fog

Date: 1914-06-02
Recipient: Father
Location: Gloucester, Massachusetts
Year: 1914
Themes: sea, work, inheritance, silence, memory

Father,—

the fog is a book that forgot its letters and so became a harbor. men move in it as if taught by sleep; the boats rub their sides together like shy animals. i watched a gull write a question on the air and leave before the wind could answer. the nets (those wrinkled hopes) came home shining like poor saints, and we poured their silver grammar onto the planks.

i remember your hands translating rope into sense, the way a knot is a sentence that does not change its mind. i try to speak with mine, though they are still learning the dialect of salt. there is comfort in work that holds. the town bell coughed twice at noon; someone laughed as if absolved. even the barrels appear to listen.

when i close my eyes i hear your steps on wet wood, sure as the moon’s pulse. i have not yet become the man who does not look back—perhaps i shall never be—but i am braver with the smell of tar for a coat. if i have done anything right today it was to keep quiet when quiet was wiser.

send news of the garden, and whether the beans have forgiven last year’s early frost.

your son,
A.
