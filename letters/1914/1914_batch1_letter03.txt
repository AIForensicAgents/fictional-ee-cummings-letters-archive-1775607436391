Aeroplanes Over Longchamp

Date: 1914-05-28
Recipient: Lydia
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1914
Themes: travel, modernity, flight, longing, city life

Lydia,
this city is a pocket mirror which refuses to close. it keeps catching little heavens and handing them to me like coins i cannot spend. morning is a procession of aprons and milk pails. afternoon leans against a balcony and pretends not to notice itself. at dusk the Seine practices being time.

yesterday two aeroplanes scribbled the air above Longchamp, and the crowd below forgave gravity for its habits. i thought of you each time the wings tilted and the earth forgot its seriousness. the painters on the Left Bank argue whether blue is honest. i say yes; it kept my pulse safe when the motor coughed and the pilot laughed into the wind.

i walked without destination until my feet discovered a bookshop that smelled like a rescued thunderstorm. the books, shy as sparrows, hopped from shelf to hand. i chose a slim volume with the spine loosened like an old friend and read at a table no larger than a hat.

send news of your streets, how their stones fit the hour, whether rain arrives politely or leaps the railings. if you were here the bridges would become slower, patient with our leaning. write me your breath in smaller and smaller letters until the page is only heartbeat.

aurally yours,
—
