Library Quiet, Cannon Loud

Date: 1914-11-11
Recipient: Edwin
Location: New Haven, Connecticut
Year: 1914
Themes: friendship, war at a distance, moral choice, academia, comfort

Edwin,
this library has perfected the art of breathing through its nose. students carry their futures the way one carries an armful of glass—deliberate, dazzled, afraid to sneeze. meanwhile across an ocean, the papers say the ground makes its own thunder and keeps none for the sky.

i have stacked your letters on the sill where the sun congratulates them. you use your words like lanterns (practical, portable) and i am guilty of using mine like weather (unreliable but occasionally spectacular). still, we light the same dark differently and call the difference friendship.

you wonder should you go. i cannot name your courage for you. i can hand you only what i have: a morning in which the janitor hummed a waltz to nobody and the dust kept time; a view from the bridge after rain when every brick acquired a memory; a pear, eaten standing, which forgave my teeth.

i know the world is turning harder. still, on Chapel Street a child practiced the alphabet against a shop window with his finger and he got to G without fear. i wanted to frame the glass.

whatever decision is a door you open carefully—take my hand for the first step. i keep a chair here with your name embroidered by patience.

affectionately,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
