postcard of thunder

Date: 1914-07-19
Recipient: Mr. Henri D., painter
Location: New York City
Year: 1914
Themes: art, storm, anticipation, mentorship, distance

sir,— or friend (if paint allows),

this afternoon thunder visited my window and left wet fingerprints on the sill. i stood at the glass like an obedient canvas waiting to be told where light ends. it did not tell me; it only counted slowly to somewhere and departed. the street answered with umbrellas, each a small idea of night.

i have been thinking of your letters about color—how a blue is a fact until it remembers to be a feeling. in the papers europe is described as a table being set in a dim room; one can hear knives but not see the faces. i listen for color among the clatter. is there a shade for the breath just before a decision? i would like to learn it before the sky forgets how to be ordinary.

in my room the plaster peels into maps of impossible countries. i travel by looking. if you could lend me a compass made of brushes (one good stubborn hog bristle and a soft that forgives), i might arrive at a quiet that does not bore.

if you answer, teach me how a line forgives a trembling hand. i will pay in attention and a little stubbornness.

respectfully,
a stranger-who-would-be-friend
