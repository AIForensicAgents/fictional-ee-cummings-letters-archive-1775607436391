Snow at the Edge of the Year

Date: 1914-12-27
Recipient: Mother
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1914
Themes: family, winter, home, tenderness, passing time

Mother,
the first snow made a new alphabet on the roofs and we are all first-day students. the city walks more carefully when it is writing. i have tied my scarf around the cold with moderate success. Christmas was a handful of candlelight and oranges, which felt like a promise people can eat.

i carried your parcel across the Common and opened it beside a man who fed the winter birds. your knitting taught my hands how to be less foolish. i have learned to brew a seriousness out of tea leaves and a joke; it keeps me through the darker afternoons when the streetcars forget to smile.

they say the year will end whether we approve. i approve in advance, on the condition that January be gentler than its reputation. last night a choir untied the silence on Beacon Hill and let it sail down the slope into the lamps. i walked after it like a child follows a paper boat until the river explained patience to me.

i am working, i am eating, i am not anyone's hero, which is relief and absence at once. hold a place at the table for my lateness. i send you the city folded small enough to sit in your pocket, warming your hand against the weeks.

your son always,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
