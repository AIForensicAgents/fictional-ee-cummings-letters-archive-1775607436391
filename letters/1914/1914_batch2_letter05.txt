apples and smoke: an october confession

Date: 1914-10-21
Recipient: Clara
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1914
Themes: love, city, art, poverty, promise

Clara,—

i am writing with an apple for a paperweight and smoke for punctuation. the window shows a street that keeps losing and finding itself under horse-hooves. somewhere a piano argues with its own memory and then forgives itself. the lamp makes a small lake of yellow on the table and i swim in it by sentences.

today i sold a drawing to a man whose moustache was more confident than his eyes; with the coins i purchased a red pencil and a long walk. the walk cost more and was worth it. i discovered that autumn smells like a library that has stepped outside for air. trees loosen their buttons; leaves rehearse falling until they cannot help themselves.

what i am trying to say is simple and (therefore) difficult: when you said my name, it did not sound like a word; it sounded like a place i had been waiting to arrive at. if you come by when the lamplighter stumbles, there will be tea, a chair warmed by the piano’s stubborn shadow, and my necessary clumsiness.

do not answer quickly. let your yes or no invite its cousins; i will wait among their coats like a gentleman.

yours, if courage consents,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
