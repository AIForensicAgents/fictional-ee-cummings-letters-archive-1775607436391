North Atlantic, Between

Date: 1914-09-13
Recipient: Harold
Location: North Atlantic Ocean
Year: 1914
Themes: travel, friendship, sea, isolation, anticipation

Harold,
shipboard time is a soft deckchair that slides an inch with each wave. the gulls follow until even their certainty is tired, and then we are left with an ocean invented anew every ten seconds. the funnels speak smoke into a grammar i cannot translate.

a steward calls me sir as if we were both pretending to be someone else's cousin. i walk circuits of the promenade and invent a modest exercise for courage: look outward without blinking. sometimes the sky obliges by being enormous; sometimes it becomes a pocket you could misplace.

a woman with a fox collar told me her husband sleeps with one ear lucid, even on land. i said i sleep with one hand listening, which is approximately the same. we agreed that letters are simply rooms that travel better than people.

last night the dining room flirted with chandelier laughter, but afterward the sea practiced truth in the corridor and we all understood our feet. i went to the dark end of the deck and counted the ship's pulse. i send it to you now, folded in the napkin of this page.

when we make harbor i will bring you a piece of horizon, trimmed to pocket size. keep my place at the window. tell the city to breathe twice for me.

yours,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
