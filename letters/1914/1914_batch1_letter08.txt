Smoke Arithmetic

Date: 1914-10-22
Recipient: Miss Ruth Abbott
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1914
Themes: industrial city, courting, labor, modernity, self-reflection

Miss Abbott,
forgive the impertinence of my address; the city insists we all be formal with our tallness. factories do their multiplication in the open air—one beam plus one beam equals a street that never looks tired until midnight. the river moves like a man with too many errands.

in the yards the animals teach us the shapes of our hunger and we graduate daily. i ride the elevated to learn how rooftops confess, and in the morning the newspapers stampede through themselves. i keep a small notebook for the number of times a whistle can mean come home.

you asked in your last note whether i am well. yes and no, the formula that fits most weather. i have been accused of optimism by a machinist with carbon on his fingers; i took it as a handshake. when the sun leans against a brick wall at four o'clock, it smooths its hair and winks at me. the soot pretends not to notice.

i send you a pocket of Lake wind, left-aligned. i ask in exchange the syllable you save for late afternoons when your work is done, the one that unbuttons a silence without bruising it. do not be kind to my mistakes; be accurate.

respectfully and somewhat otherwise,
—
