Bells and Footsteps

Date: 1914-08-05
Recipient: Clara
Location: Antwerp, Belgium
Year: 1914
Themes: war, civilians, tenderness, fear, hope

Clara,
what can a hand do but name the air it enters. i write from a room that remembers lace. the street has grown narrower with every hour because fear is a seamstress and she likes her stitches close. still, a child chalked a sun on the curb this morning and the curb obeyed.

there are bells at odd times and boots in multiples. a cart of potatoes tried to look brave; it failed wonderfully and we loved it for that. your last letter was all sky, so much blue that my pocket lightened. keep sending sky. we are sharpening it carefully.

the station is full of questions wearing coats. people carry their houses in string and their names in their mouths. i held the elbow of an old man made entirely of maps and we crossed a crowd without spilling. he told me that in his village even the weeds hold hands.

i cannot say how this will end. i can say that a woman in a doorway sang to her baby as if night itself could be persuaded to take smaller steps. i stood under the window and the song found me. i will keep it folded against what is coming.

with all the rooms of my heart open to you,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
