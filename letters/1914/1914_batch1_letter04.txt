Moon Salt and Footlights

Date: 1914-06-16
Recipient: Theo
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1914
Themes: theater, summer, friendship, coast, artistic process

dear Theo,
the stage is a wharf this week and we are all small boats pretending to be storms. the rehearsal hall is so full of summer that props grow leaves when we look away. we have painted a door which opens onto a lamp, and sometimes the lamp pretends to be a voice.

the ocean comes and goes with the discipline of a metronome and still manages to surprise me. i sit on the sand with my shoes among the reasonable stars and consider how a gull decides the shape of its errand. last night the moon unwound itself like a spool of thread and stitched a road across the water. i nearly walked it, pockets stuffed with applause.

our troupe eats late and poorly and laughs exactly on time. an actress with a freckle at the edge of every word told me i move like a misplaced comma. i took this as blessing. we are learning that the smallest gesture can empty a theater of silence, and how a whisper, if carried carefully, can weigh more than a shout.

write me your window, how the city breathes against it. send any stray midnight you find; i will trade three kinds of tide.

yours in footlights and salt,
—
