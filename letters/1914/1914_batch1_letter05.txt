Fog with Headlines

Date: 1914-07-31
Recipient: Father
Location: London, England
Year: 1914
Themes: family, impending war, city observations, uncertainty, resilience

Father,
streets here wear their rain like a proper jacket. i walk early to hear the wheels announce themselves and the shopkeepers lift their shutters as if unveiling a quiet orchestra. tea is a method of telling time. so is the paper.

this morning every headline learned a new, sharp word. people read standing still (a rare posture), as if the pavement might tip beneath more syllables. i do not pretend to know what will happen, only that each clock now ticks like a small drum. there is a rumor of trains practicing night.

yet, in a bakery, a boy powdered with sugar stumbled and grinned. a woman in a green hat retrieved his dignity with a napkin. a cat invented sleep in a window. the ordinary keeps insisting and i am relieved to be insisted upon.

i spend hours at the museum where colors hold their breath. Turner ferries me from storm to storm and deposits me calm on the far bank. outside, the buses cough like stubborn ships. i have brought my pen to the river and asked it what one person can do. it dragged its feet through the ink and said: begin, then begin again.

please write your steadying hand across this uncertainty. i am well, which is an almost. i am yours always, which is the rest.

your son,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
