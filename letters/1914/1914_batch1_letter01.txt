Lamplight Between Leaves

Date: 1914-03-21
Recipient: M.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1914
Themes: love, spring, student life, yearning, urban quiet

dear M,
this morning the elm above the yard unbuttoned a few green thoughts and let them fall onto my notebook. i remembered your glove (dropped at the foot of the library steps like a small winter) and carried it in my pocket as if it might warm a future.

a lecture on Latin drifted past me like a thin boat. i did not board. i sat by the window where the air is brighter than any grammar, and the typewriter borrowed from Henry stuttered out my name until it became only sound. is that not what we are made of—sound and breath and a scarf suddenly untying itself, a light out of a darker light?

there is a lamplighter who comes at dusk and teases every street into a row of candles. when he passes, even the bricks remember they are red. i follow his flame half a block (like a child follows the moon by accident) and whisper your initials into it, which is foolish, but foolishness is simply courage wearing a soft hat.

if you are well, if the wind does not turn against your eyes, if the thin bird who lives in your wrist still sings—then i am braver than i have any right to be. send me the frost you keep in your handwriting; i will send back a thaw.

i will come Sunday if the sky agrees. the river keeps practicing how to be silver for you.

affectionately,
—
