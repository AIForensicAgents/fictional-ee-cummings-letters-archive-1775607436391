lighthouse at the end of the typewriter

Date: 1954-11-09
Recipient: Myself at 3 a.m.
Location: Portland, Maine
Year: 1954
Themes: self, memory, sea, writing, resilience

dear 3 a.m. me,

the harbor is chewing its dark bread, and the gulls are commas in a sentence the sea keeps revising. the typewriter watches me like a small square lighthouse and i am the ship that pretends to be lost so i can see it shine.

i keep father's boots under the bed as if the floor might snow. sometimes i put them on and walk the apartment, and the rooms arrange themselves like towns along a track i cannot quite remember. tonight the coffee tastes of raincoats and resolve. i have a handful of words whose faces i know but not their names.

be kinder to the first draft of morning. it will come with hair uncombed and ask foolish questions you should answer. do not edit the tenderness out. there is a rumor of frost in the hallway; invite it in and make it tea. learn the slow bravery of letters you do not send and the quick courage of the ones you must.

when the lighthouse of this machine grows tired, let the page go dark without apology. sleep is a harbor with room for all your small stubborn boats. tomorrow the paper will be clean as a bandage and you will need it.

sign this when the sun remembers us,
 you.
