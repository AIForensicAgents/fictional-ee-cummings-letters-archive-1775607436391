static and honey (a midnight to mr. benny)

Date: 1954-07-28
Recipient: Mr. Benny Lear
Location: Harlem, New York
Year: 1954
Themes: music, yearning, city night, improvisation, radio

mr. benny,

your trumpet last night unscrewed the moon and let a little good trouble spill onto 125th. the radio in the kitchen tried to steal it but the static only made it sweeter, like sugar with a burn. i leaned on the windowsill and the city leaned back, all elbows and grin.

the drummer was a locksmith, the bass a soft engine. somewhere between the second chorus and the spilled whiskey my ribs remembered a door. you opened it and the hallway wore midnight like a silk tie. somebody laughed in C sharp. somebody kissed the wrong cigarette and didn't mind.

i kept wanting to send you a telegram that said simply keep going, but i was busy dancing with the floor (she is not light, that lady, but she is faithful). when you bent a note the street bent with it and the taxis agreed to be small yellow miracles for one whole song.

take this as permission you do not need: make the air misbehave again tonight. let the horn find the part of morning that forgot it was morning. if you tire, hold the note out and let it carry you like a smart river.

a woman who signs her letters with smoke,
 s.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
