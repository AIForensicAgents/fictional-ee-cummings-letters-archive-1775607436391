science is a blue dress drying on a line

Date: 1954-09-18
Recipient: Miss Temple, my high school teacher
Location: Pasadena, California
Year: 1954
Themes: gratitude, science, aspiration, mentorship, wonder

miss temple,
the sun here is a diagram i keep failing and loving. i write from a room that smells of graphite and orange peel. the mountain keeps its shoulders polite above the smog.

you once told me curiosity is a ladder thrown against the blank wall of night. i have splinters in my hands and a view worth bleeding for. the lab wears its quiet like a halo. even the beakers have a posture.

they are talking about satellites as if a metal heart could learn to pray the way we do. i believe it might. i believe prayer is simply attention paid without change left over.

i bought a blue dress at the thrift store. i washed it in the sink and hung it on the line and the wind solved an equation i could not, turning fabric into flight. i thought of you holding open the classroom door to let my questions out.

thank you for teaching me to love every wrong answer that points with both hands. if i ever touch a star, i will write your name on my sleeve in chalk and not brush it off when it smears.

still adding up the sky,
juniper
