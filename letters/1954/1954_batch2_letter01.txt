snow-syllables on your window (chicago letter)

Date: 1954-02-14
Recipient: Iris
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1954
Themes: love, winter, city, intimacy, anticipation

dear iris,

the snow is practicing its quiet on the el tracks while the storefronts yawn warm, and inside the diner our two coffees make small eclipses on the table. your laughter keeps time with the radiator's soft complaint; the night wears its best humble shoes.

i walked back to your window once (twice) after leaving, because the glass kept a smudge of evening shaped like your wrist. even the streetlamp stuttered a little. there is a city made entirely of breath between us, and i am learning to cross it without maps, by pulse and foolish courage.

sometimes a trolley bell rings like a bright mistake and i think of the way you tuck a strand behind your ear as if saving it for later. i have hidden a nickel under your doorstep; spend it on a wish the size of breakfast. spend it on tomorrow's vowels, round and brave.

if anyone asks what we are doing, let us tell them: inventing a room out of weather. let us tell them: building a future out of small thunders (your palm, my name). tonight the alleys are narrow poems and every brick says stay.

write soon or sooner. if you cannot, send me the ash of a match you breathed on; i will light a city with it.

yours, always almost and almost always,
 n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
