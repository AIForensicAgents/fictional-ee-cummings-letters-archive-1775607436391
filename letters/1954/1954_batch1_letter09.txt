two rooms and an orchard of radios

Date: 1954-10-30
Recipient: The Landlord
Location: Cincinnati, Ohio
Year: 1954
Themes: humor, domestic life, noise, urban living, negotiation

sir,
this is a formal complaint written on an informal night. the neighbors have planted an orchard of radios and they are all in bloom. each fruit is tuned to a different argument. the walls pick them and feed them to me without asking.

i do not mean to be ungrateful; the rent is almost fair and the window learns moonlight with decent speed. but last night a preacher married a baseball game while a weather report threw rice, and i have never been so widely wed.

the wiring hums like a polite bee that forgot why it entered the room. when i switch the lamp on, it coughs out a senator. when i switch it off, it swallows a saxophone.

i request a visit from a man with a screwdriver and a tender heart. perhaps the building needs less sermon and more sleep. perhaps the ghosts of old antennas are restless and require a lullaby in screws.

i will pay the November rent in full and bring you a pie if silence ripens on the branch above my bed. i bake best when the oven can hear itself think.

with respect and ringing ears,
agnes on the third floor

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
