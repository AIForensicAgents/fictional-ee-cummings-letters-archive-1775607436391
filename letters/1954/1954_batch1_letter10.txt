holly and hallelujahs in lowercase

Date: 1954-12-24
Recipient: Sylvia
Location: Burlington, Vermont
Year: 1954
Themes: reconciliation, winter, forgiveness, ritual, love

sylvia,
this morning the town buttoned its white coat and pretended to be holy. i walked to the post in snow that forgave every footprint as soon as it could.

the shop windows practiced faith with tinsel and small electric stars. i bought an orange the color of august and put it in my pocket like a sun i could afford.

i have rehearsed our argument until even the chair knows its lines. perhaps we were right and wrong at the same time like a coin spinning between histories. i am tired of gravity choosing for us.

if you come by after service, i will make cocoa and not mention yesterday. the kettle remembers how you held it with a mitten and laughed at its small volcano. i would like to borrow that laugh for a minute, to warm the places the radiator cannot reach.

there is a pine branch in the window, refusing to be anything but green. i admire that. i am trying to be a person who does not flinch at joy. i am trying to say sorry without spraining my pride.

bring your wool scarf that smells like cider. bring your patience. leave the sharp words on the porch where the snow can blunt them.

still here, still us, if you will,
thomas
