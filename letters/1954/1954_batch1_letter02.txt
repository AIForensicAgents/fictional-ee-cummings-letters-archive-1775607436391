telegram-less sky

Date: 1954-02-18
Recipient: Brother Eli
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1954
Themes: family, travel, desert, memory, freedom

eli,
this morning the desert stitched a horizon with wire and posts, then forgot to send anything along it. a sky so clean even my doubts feel dusty.

i have traded east-coast hurry for a hat full of blue. the sun here does not knock; it walks in and sits at the table, eats the last orange, leaves the rind like a thin smile.

how is father’s stubborn radio? does it still sleep with its one eye open, listening for a war that already ended? tell him i am fine, which is to say the road keeps a hand on my shoulder and says keep going.

i paint postcard saints on motel envelopes: saint of mile markers, saint of low gas, saint of coyotes crossing when the moon forgets itself.

the adobe walls breathe cool as a well. sometimes i think our childhood was just a long hall lined with closed doors; out here, the doors are the whole sky and someone misplaced the hinges.

write me a letter as long as a train. send news of the sap waking in the maples, and i will send you a handful of this sand that sifts time like a cheap magician but still makes me believe.

your brother with two blisters and one very large horizon,
jack

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
