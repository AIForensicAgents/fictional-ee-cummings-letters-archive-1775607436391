drafts for a small revolution (kitchen table edition)

Date: 1954-05-27
Recipient: Myself in five years
Location: Oakland, California
Year: 1954
Themes: self-address, hope, political climate, domestic life, courage

dear future me,
this is a test of the emergency courage system. if you are reading, the world did not end, only turned its usual corners in its usual stubborn shoes.

the kettle clicks and the radio whispers about committees with sharp teeth. i turn it down and write you louder. remember how the cat walks the fence at dusk like a tightrope poet and never falls.

do you still keep your books under the bed, not from fear but because you like the idea of sleeping on a thousand pages of possibility. did you ever fix the chair that limps. did you learn how to say no without closing a door on yourself.

i have a job that pays in nickels and a life that pays in blue mornings, sweet as laundry on a borrowed line. when i get scared, i draw a map of the bay and label the fog mercy.

promise me you will love someone who laughs like a new penny and does not apologize for being bright. promise you will write back even when your hands are full of groceries and weather.

i am saving for a typewriter with a spine like a boxer. i am saving for the kind of day that does not ask permission to glow. i am saving myself, which is to say spending myself, which is to say i am here.

in wet ink and crooked teeth,
rosie

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
