station platform prayer

Date: 1954-06-13
Recipient: Armand
Location: Chicago Union Station, Illinois
Year: 1954
Themes: departure, forbidden love, travel, yearning, secrecy

armand,
platform 8 keeps swallowing minutes and giving back only whistles. i stand under the clock’s stern face and try to behave.

the suitcase sulks. the ticket is a small rectangle of good-bye pretending to be paper. i carry your name in my coat like contraband, safe and shining.

do you remember the hotel with the timid wallpaper. the way the hallway light blinked yes no yes and we believed it. my hands still remember the geography of your shoulders; they could find you in a blackout.

there is a brass angel who patrols the ceiling and keeps score of hearts departing. i bribe her with a cigarette and she winks smoke. she promises nothing and i forgive her.

tell me the river is behaving, that the city has not closed its fist around you. i am crossing state lines with a secret stitched under my tongue. when they ask for my destination, i say summer and mean you.

the train’s breath is on my face. i step forward. if the world were kinder, i would not have to write this in code. read the space between these words the way you once read the hollow at my throat.

whistling yes,
marcel

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
