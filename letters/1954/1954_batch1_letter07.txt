lighthouse arithmetic

Date: 1954-08-01
Recipient: Aunt Beatrice
Location: Portland, Maine
Year: 1954
Themes: grief, sea, family, ritual, memory

aunt bea,
i count the waves the way you taught me: out loud until the sea grows embarrassed and changes the subject. gulls rehearse complaints and the bell buoy speaks fluent patience.

your kitchen keeps its salt in a blue jar that remembers storms. i stand at the sink and the window makes a postcard of the horizon, creased where grief bends it.

since uncle went, the house has leaned a little more toward the water as if listening. you said the light must keep speaking even when no one answers. so we polish the lens until our arms ache and the beam goes on, a steady arithmetic against the dark.

this morning i found his pipe in the pocket of his coat. it held one last ghost of tobacco and a thumbprint that refused to fade. i pressed my own thumb there and said stay, as if that were a thing the living could order.

i will go up the stairs tonight and keep watch with you. we will name each ship by its sound and let the names float back, like bottles that know the shore.

with sea in my shoes and love in my sleeves,
ellen

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
