when the snow listens

Date: 1954-01-05
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1954
Themes: love, winter, longing, city, waiting

dear ruth,
tonight the city wears its hush like a borrowed coat; sleeves too long, cuffs brushing the cobblestones.
my window invents a moon out of streetlamp milk. i put on the kettle and your name rises with the steam, fragile and stubborn as january.

i am practicing the grammar of waiting. commas of footsteps, the period of a door closing two floors below, sudden parentheses of laughter i am not inside of.
when i breathe, the glass trembles and for a moment i think it is you knocking from the other side.

send a postcard if you cannot send yourself. draw me the crooked tooth of your smile, the soft geography of your hands.
i will answer with an envelope full of warm pockets and the smell of cinnamon, which i am surely learning for your sake.

there is a small bird that keeps insisting on dawn though it is still very night. darling, i am that foolishness. somewhere under all this winter, a sentence begins.

yours in mittened hope,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
