desert-letter with gasoline halo

Date: 1954-05-03
Recipient: Brother Eli
Location: El Paso, Texas
Year: 1954
Themes: family, journey, desert, forgiveness, restlessness

eli,

the road unspools like film hungry for light, and our truck coughs its little hymn westward. i bought oranges from a boy whose shadow was taller than him; we ate them in the heat and our hands became sudden suns. the radio slips between preachers and cowboys, both promising a horizon you can fold into a pocket.

this border wind is an unscrupulous translator, turns every sign into maybe. at a gas station a jukebox planted its neon flag in my chest and played a song that sounded like you skipping church. remember the field where we taught the scarecrow to shrug; i swear i saw it again, only dustier, and it forgave me for leaving first.

mama's letter found me at the post restante, smelling like starch and thunder. she wrote in her tidy storms, asked if i am eating, if the nights behave. i wanted to answer: the coyote teaches the moon to whistle; i am fine. but i wrote: yes. yes. yes.

say to the boys that the sky out here is roomy enough to mend a person. there is a motel that forgets your name kindly, and a girl with a chipped tooth who calls everyone sweetheart like she's repairing glass. i am learning the census of my own bones.

when the tires cool i can hear them whisper home. tell me if home hears back.

your sister working on a better map,
 m.
