A small letter to the apple-colored moon

Date: 1932-01-12
Recipient: Clara
Location: New York, NY
Year: 1932
Themes: love, city, winter, longing, imagination

dear Clara,

tonight the city is a stove of blue iron; the el trains clang like polite meteors and the streets steam as if the pavement were remembering summer. I am listening (quite seriously) to the radiator breathe. it hiccups at the moon. meanwhile your name walks the ceiling in its stocking feet.

I tried to behave bravely at the typewriter, but the keys kept tossing sparks and whispering no no no until I said your shoulders. then they all leaned close and said yes in a shy loud way. the ribbon stained my fingers. now I carry two smudged truths: it is winter, and you exist.

this afternoon I bought an apple for five cents; it sat in my palm like a small, patient planet—its skin shining with the idea of you. I ate it slowly on a stoop where an old man folded a newspaper into a ship and sailed it down the gutter river. a girl laughed and the sky shivered open.

forgive this letter its tilted hat. every sentence wishes to take your hand (gently, without insisting). if you are awake, place your ear on the windowpane; I am sending footsteps. if you are asleep, keep them; I will borrow your dream in the morning and return it warm.

yours until the streetlights forget to blink,

A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
