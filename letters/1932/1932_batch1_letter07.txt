Telegram length, ocean depth

Date: 1932-08-03
Recipient: Elias at sea
Location: Halifax, Nova Scotia
Year: 1932
Themes: travel, sea, farewell, courage, yearning

Elias,

this is not a telegram though I am trying to keep it thin as a wire. the harbor coughs fog into its sleeve and the gulls fling their opinions against the sky. I have a pocket of coins that all smell like salt and goodbye.

they say the ocean is a workplace for horizons. who punches the clock, I wonder, and who measures the lunch break of waves. when your ship lifts its shoulders over the bruise colored water, remember that the compass is only a well behaved dream.

I packed in this envelope an invisible sweater knitted from my patience, a map that refuses to be flat, and a word that means come back but also means go bravely. if it does not fit, wear the sweater and eat the map.

tell the engine to sing in a key your bones enjoy. teach the night watchman a joke so the midnight does not become too proud. and when the moon begins deleting the borders of things, write my name into the mist with your breath.

with a shore tucked carefully into my heart,

L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
