a small astonishment addressed to tomorrow

Date: 1932-12-31
Recipient: Future Daughter
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1932
Themes: anticipation, parenthood imagined, winter, city at night, tenderness

dearest tomorrow—

perhaps you will be small enough to wear the dawn like a ribbon. perhaps not. either way i am writing across this winter midnight to a you that may arrive by the gentlest train. the seine keeps its dark promises and the bridges learn the names of people passing until they shine with vocabulary.

i want to tell you how a baker in the sixth arranges loaves like sleeping commas and how the streetlamp outside my window baptizes snow into confetti. when i do not know what to do, i listen to the river. it has practiced leaving longer than anyone and still loves its banks.

if you come to us, we will teach you the arithmetic of wonder: one streetlamp plus one flake equals cathedral; two hands and one heartbeat make a country; three deep breaths open a window wider than the room. you will have your own arithmetic, i am sure, and it will correct us tenderly.

please do not hurry. being is a shy animal. if you arrive late we will set an extra place for lateness. if you never arrive we will love the possibility of you until it becomes another kind of you—no less real, only lighter on the feet.

tonight we will walk along the quai and count the new year as if it were laundry on a bright rope. somewhere a piano is practicing kindness. somewhere someone opens a letter and finds their name written correctly for the first time. this is what i wish for you: that the world remembers how to pronounce you.

with all the carefulness i can hold,
—l.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
