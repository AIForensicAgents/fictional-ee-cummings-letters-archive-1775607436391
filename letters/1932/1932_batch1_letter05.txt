Concerning the theater of umbrellas

Date: 1932-05-21
Recipient: Miss Evangeline Pierce, Dramaturge
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1932
Themes: theater, rain, surrealism, art, cities

Miss Pierce,

it rained with such disciplined improvisation yesterday that the boulevards produced a ballet of umbrellas, each a dark petal with its own opinion. I watched from a cafe where the spoons converse in fluent reflection. the waiter balanced three cups and a secret in one hand and still managed a bow to a dog.

the new play refuses to be reasonable and I love it for that. Act One opens with a door that remembers every knock it ever received. the chorus is four chairs who have no patience for propriety. a hat falls in love with a coat and learns regret. if this seems implausible, consider the city itself where bridges marry fog before breakfast.

I am writing the dialogue with a pencil that keeps losing its temper and sharpening itself against my pocket. the lines come like swallows late to an appointment; they arrive suddenly and make being late the point.

I propose we stage it in a room without a stage. let the audience sit inside the actors and the actors sit inside an umbrella and the umbrella sit inside a silence we pass around like an excellent pear. the ticket will be a question mark you can fold in half and wear as a tie.

please send word if you still believe in thunderstorms.

faithfully impractical,

M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
