Letter to a river with your handwriting

Date: 1932-04-07
Recipient: Ana
Location: Santa Fe, NM
Year: 1932
Themes: landscape, art, love, distance, silence

Ana,

the desert pretends to be empty in the most extravagant way. everything is a whispering absence full of tricks. today a lizard wrote a quick poem on a rock and vanished before I learned the alphabet of its feet. the river here is a bilingual thread: one side speaks thirst, the other side hums wet syllables.

your last letter smelled of cedar and the memory of thunder. I read it aloud to the mesas; they kept their dignity but allowed a shadow to smile. there is something in your voice that persuades noon to be merciful. even the sun removed its hat for a minute and let me breathe.

I am painting with dust and water and a foolish heart. some colors refuse prediction. a blue arrived this morning that could not be told apart from silence; I named it your breath when you wake. forgive me for the liberties I take with truth; it has more rooms than anyone suspects.

walk to the river at evening and press your ear to its elbow. if it mentions me, nod yes. I will answer by lighting every small star in the coffee tin above the stove and opening the window so the night can count them.

wideningly,

S.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
