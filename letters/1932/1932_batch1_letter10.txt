Christmas rumor at the back of the world

Date: 1932-12-24
Recipient: Father
Location: Bangor, ME
Year: 1932
Themes: family, winter, holidays, memory, humility

Father,

the streets carry snow like a careful waiter balancing white plates. the shop windows practice small miracles: oranges glowing as if the sun had been persuaded into circles, a toy train that travels bravely but never leaves home, paper stars that make their own sky within arms reach.

this morning I walked to the river where the ice argued politely with the current. the air had a blue taste. I thought of your boots by the door, the way you shook them out as if releasing yesterday’s mud back to history. I tried it with my coat; several old worries leapt out and froze midair, which is what they deserved.

I have bought nothing grand. a scarf for Mother that pretends to be spring, matches for the stove to teach it generosity, and a book of blank pages for you, because your hands know how to build sentences the way they build shelves: sturdy, with a sly flourish.

dinner will be humble but radiant. we will place an extra chair for any wandering grace that finds us. when the bells begin their soft arithmetic, stand by the window and breathe slowly; I will answer from mine and the night between us will feel like one long, patient hallway.

with a heart wearing mittens,

T.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
