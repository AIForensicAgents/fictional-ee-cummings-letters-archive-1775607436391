letter with rain stitched to it

Date: 1932-09-03
Recipient: Miss Eloise Hart
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1932
Themes: coast, yearning, weather, memory, waiting

eloise—

i write in a room that smells like damp wood and the kind of patience only late summer understands. the rain is thimbling the roof, small delicate dents on the night. your last note carried the salt arithmetic of gulls; i kept hearing rope speak fluent harbor.

i think of that narrow pier we borrowed from the morning. remember the boards, how they spelled maybe with each step. we had a map that refused to be folded and a sky that refused to be still. you put your hand on my sleeve and the tide forgot its manners.

the lighthouse up the coast winked all afternoon, as if it knew something that would not fit into daylight. i am not jealous of it; i am only learning to stand that tall when it’s dark.

i will come when the storms stop naming things after us. until then tie my name to the rail. let it season there with rope and wind, a modest talisman of splinters and believing. if anyone asks, say we are practicing the future and it requires both of us to listen hard enough to hear a fish turn.

close your shutters against the rain that thinks in parenthesis. keep your throat warm. even now i am sending you a scarf the color of first light—which is to say, confused but brave.

faithfully,
—e.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
