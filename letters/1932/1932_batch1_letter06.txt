To my future self (who is surely laughing)

Date: 1932-06-30
Recipient: Myself
Location: Portland, OR
Year: 1932
Themes: self-reflection, time, domestic, humor, hope

dear Future,

the woodsmoke writes its cramped postcard across the yard and the blackberry bushes rehearse anarchy with admirable thorns. I am wearing yesterday’s resolve and today’s socks. somewhere a train unbuttons the hillside with a slow delicate clatter and I remember that arrival is a hobby not a duty.

you will want to know what I learned: that loneliness is a room with generous acoustics; every small step is a drum. that coffee, even when poor, conducts lightning safely through the body. that one should keep a pencil by the sink because water insists on ideas.

I have forgiven the clock for being square. it continues to apologize in ticks. I have not forgiven the mirror, which refuses to lie persuasively. when you get here, please teach it discretion.

if you have found some light, wrap it in newspaper and mail it back to me with no return address. I will recognize it by how it warms my pockets and scolds my doubts.

until then, keep a spare sun beneath the bed and let the broom dance now and then. be kind to the creak in the stair; it is only the house remembering to speak.

affectionately provisional,

R.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
