A field report from the carnival of dust

Date: 1932-09-18
Recipient: Cousin Ruth
Location: Tulsa, OK
Year: 1932
Themes: drought, resilience, community, ritual, humor

Ruth,

the wind has been hiring itself out as a sculptor again. yesterday it carved a barn from a cloud and then emptied it on our porch. everyone carries a handkerchief for the weather inside their lungs. we tie our hats like respectful hostages and do our errands anyway.

down by the fairgrounds a carousel of tumbleweeds decided to go into business for itself. it accepted nickels and whistled as it spun. the children applauded the dust horses because they never get tired. a radio somewhere lost the end of a sentence and has been looking for it ever since.

we invent a ceremony for everything blown away. Mrs. Avery pronounced three blessings over a fencepost and a promise. at suppertime the stew tasted of maps. I could feel the county lines on my tongue.

still, there is a trick to happiness: we boil water as if it were a miracle, and often it is. we wipe the table until it becomes a small, dependable moon. even the dog, who knows more about scarcity than we do, sleeps like a philosopher.

send seeds if you can find any with a talent for stubbornness. my pocket keeps rehearsing rain.

all my dust and all my love,

H.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
