Postscript from a secondhand dawn

Date: 1932-02-29
Recipient: Brother Julian
Location: Chicago, IL
Year: 1932
Themes: brotherhood, poverty, music, resilience, city

Julian,

the lake has been sharpening knives against the March in our bones, and I keep finding nickels staring at me like small pale eyes at the bottom of my pocket. I stand in lines that carry their own weather; steam hovers above our coats like a weak halo. a man with a cornet plays what I swear is a staircase: up three notes, stumble, up two, down one, up. it arrives somehow at morning.

you asked after work. there is the kind with brooms, the kind with boxes, the kind with not yet. I have practiced all varieties of polite no. I keep my hat in my hands so it learns humility faster than I do.

in the afternoon I walked past the stockyards where the wind invented a new word for hunger and taught it to my stomach. yet there is a trick: look long enough at a puddle and it gives back a sky more faithful than the original. the city’s broken windows still collect stars; someone has to count them.

remember our father’s coat? Mother patched it into an itinerary. when I wear it, my shoulders discover far places. come when you can; the kettle behaves heroically at four o’clock. I have a chair for your tiredness and a joke saved in a matchbox.

unbrokenly yours,

J.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
