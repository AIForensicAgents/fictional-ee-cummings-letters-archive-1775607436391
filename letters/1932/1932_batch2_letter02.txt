On the Business of Breathing

Date: 1932-06-27
Recipient: Brother Caleb
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1932
Themes: work, brotherhood, resilience, music, economic hardship

caleb—

i stood where wabash leans over the river and let the wind inventory my coat. the hiring line was a slow animal, patient and hungry, and we were teeth inside its jaw. the foreman turned my name over like a coin without a country and set it back on the table.

across the street a boy coaxed a cornet until the bricks remembered they were warm once. that sound stitched us together for a minute: men with rain in their shoes and bread on their minds, suddenly a small parade nobody planned. if breath can be work (and i think it is), we earned our supper standing still.

i have been mending machines that no longer believe, teaching belts to move again by telling them stories of wheat and thunder. when the whistle blows at five and doesn’t mean anything, i carry the silence home wrapped in last week’s newspaper. somehow it still leaks.

write of the fields if you can. tell me the corn still stands proud as a choir, or even if it sulks, i will forgive it everything. give my love to ruth and the small storm of her steps in the hallway. tell her i have saved a red marble for the first day she names the sky correctly.

we will manage, brother. it is the long art of breathing: in, out, in, out—until the lungs learn mercy. i keep a photograph of the two of us holding nothing and calling it a kite. even the wind appeared.

yours,
m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
