tiny hymn for a typewriter ribbon

Date: 1939-10-14
Recipient: Agnes the Bookseller
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1939
Themes: art, language, commerce, fog, letters

dear Agnes,
the fog arrived on trolley wheels and shelved the city in its softest aisle.
i thought of your shop with its books breathing like polite animals, paper-scent
that makes the afternoon kneel and listen.

my typewriter, faithful grump, has started to stutter vowels; the ribbon is more ghost
than ink. send me a new one if you can—the red-and-black spool that gives words
a choice of blood or whisper. i promise to spend them carefully, to coin small prayers,
to purchase quiet with the exact change of commas.

yesterday a boy came to the counter asking for a map that does not show borders.
if you find two, keep one under the till for me. i am writing a story in which a window
learns to open itself and the wind applies for a library card.

do the bells still wring the evening out of north beach. walk me to the back room
in your next letter; tell me which spine you trusted today, which moth found a poem
warm enough to sleep on.

ever gratefully,
p.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
