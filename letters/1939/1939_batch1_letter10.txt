for the future you, found between the bells

Date: 1939-12-30
Recipient: My future self
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1939
Themes: time, self, jazz, hope, river

dear future me,
tonight the river nurses its slow thunder and the brass down on rampart street
teaches midnight how to walk before it runs. i am writing to the version of us
who will wake somewhere else with pockets rearranged by time. listen: keep the coins
that pay for wonder, spend the hard ones on rent, tip every trumpet.

a magnolia leaned over the balcony at dusk and italicized the air. a girl in a yellow
dress erased a block of sorrow with her hips. i learned three new ways to say yes
without using my mouth. the moon rehearsed tomorrow and found it good enough.

if the coming year insists on being large, meet it with small things: the rescue
of a button, the temperature of bread, the honest ache after laughing too long.
write names on the backs of photographs, especially your own. forgive the river
for never arriving, it is busy being everywhere at once.

when the bells go to work and the clocks loosen their ties, say our name out loud
and mean the oldest part of it. i am not afraid. you are not alone. the band
will take it from here.

as always,
y.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
