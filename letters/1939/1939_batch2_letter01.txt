small vowel of spring

Date: 1939-03-21
Recipient: L.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA
Year: 1939
Themes: spring, longing, urban quiet, tenderness, awakening

Dear L,

today the sky forgot its capital letters and went softly blue as if a shy mouth learning how to say yes. the wind rummaged the river like a polite thief and somebody whistled a tune i could not place, maybe the first tulip trying on its red.

i walked past the library and the bricks wore sunlight like a warm coat; my hands were ungloved and felt scandalous. on a bench a boy fell in love with a sandwich and the pigeons held their parliament of crumbs. i thought (too loud) of your hair as unbuttoned weather, and the way your laugh nudges the world until it remembers to swing.

last night the train coughed and hurried north; i imagined you somewhere between lamplight and paper, your name traveling the wires of me in tiny warm sparks. please forgive this uncareful heart which keeps misplacing its silence: every time i sit at the typewriter a small spring crawls out between the keys and spells your absence in green.

L, we are the secret punctuation of a season. i will meet you on the corner where winter exhales, and we will cross the street holding our breath like two bright coins. if the day wobbles, let it. if the future shrugs, i will button it up. the bees will revise the air; we will be their footnotes of honey.

let me be the silly shoe of your walking, the pocket your hands forget to empty, the rain that writes yes yes yes across the town and then dries into light.

faithfully and foolishly,

r.
