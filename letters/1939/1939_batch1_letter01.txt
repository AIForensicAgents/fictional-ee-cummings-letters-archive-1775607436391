ink snow (telegram without wires)

Date: 1939-01-12
Recipient: Lena
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1939
Themes: love, winter, distance, city, longing

dear Lena—
this morning the harbor wore its cold like a thin wedding-dress and i,
late again, married my coffee to the window. snow kept spelling your name
in an alphabet of falling quiet; i tried to answer (my breath, a soft telegraph)
but the panes stuttered back only winter winter.

your last card still naps in my pocket, creased as a smile.
i unfold it at crosswalks and the city pauses—briefly—like a violin
forgetting whether to weep or sing. what am i to do with all these minutes
that arrive barefoot and bright and find you not here.

i would send you my pulse by parcel post if stamps could carry
such clumsy cargo. instead i practice the art of almost:
almost touching your sleeve on a crowded street,
almost hearing your laugh as a streetcar rings,
almost finding your hand in the coatroom dark of afternoon.

keep the lamp lit. keep your mouth a harbor.
i am learning the grammar of arrival (subject: us. verb: breathe).
when the weather remembers itself to be spring
i will walk to you on roads of melted bells.

ever yours,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
