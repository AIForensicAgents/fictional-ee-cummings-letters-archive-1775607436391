blackout lullaby

Date: 1939-10-02
Recipient: Mira
Location: London, England
Year: 1939
Themes: war, courage, domestic intimacy, night, hope

Mira dear,

the city has learned to keep its eyes shut. by seven the curtains grow their serious faces and we sip our tea in a room shaped like listening. i have never loved a match so fiercely; the frail astonishment it makes is a saint among shadows. even the kettle whistles with a finger to its lips.

in the afternoon i watched an ARP warden teaching a child how to tie the night to a window. he said there you are then and patted darkness like a good dog. we practice breathing in small squares of air and even the clocks seem to tiptoe. somewhere the Thames remembers sunlight, and i swear i hear it smiling under the bridges.

don't be frightened for me. i have a tiny courage that fits in my coat pocket, stitched by your hands the sunday we argued about nothing at all and then forgave the world for being the world. if sirens arrive, i will step politely into the stairwell with my neighbors, an orchestra of shoes. Mrs. Adler always brings a lemon to pass around, for smelling. it makes the dark taste of warm yellow.

your last letter slept beneath my pillow and undid my dreams. your words touched my eyelids and i woke full of your light, as if a window had opened inside my chest. when i think of you i think of rain deciding to be music, of a streetlamp practicing patience, of two spoons that do not mind being the same.

when this present grief grows tired, we shall make a garden on the roof and invite the sky for tea. we will plant beans with quiet thumbs and let the birds proofread our laughter. until then, be my secret lamp. i will send you a kiss each night at nine; please open the dark a little and let it in.

hushed and wholly yours,

n.
