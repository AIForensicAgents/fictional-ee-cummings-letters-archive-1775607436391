telegram of moonlight

Date: 1939-05-10
Recipient: Brother Eli
Location: Chicago, Illinois, USA
Year: 1939
Themes: brotherhood, work, city life, music, perseverance

Eli,

i promised myself to write a proper letter but the night has a telegraph key and keeps tapping your name against my ribs. chicago has taken off its hat for summer and shows the hard shine of its head; the river smells of machines and bread, a useful sorrow. i am learning a new alphabet at the plant: rivet vowel, hammer consonant, the grammar of sweat. we conjugate steel until it sings.

you asked if i am lonely. i am, but in a friendly way. the city offers me a coat of noises and it fits, almost. the radio in the boardinghouse gargles swing and gin, and during ballgames the landlady leans forward like prayer. last sunday a girl sold violets on the el platform; the wind tried to blow her small garden into the lake, but she smiled and every petal obeyed her mouth.

don't worry your good knuckles about my rent. i have a slice of sky over the bed and a window that hatches pigeons and rain. when the whistle blows at dusk, the streets yawn open and we stumble into evening like coins tipped from a pocket. i count what i have: two shirts, one promise, three jokes, a picture of you scowling on the farm, the known fact that our mother keeps a tiny weather for us in her apron.

if the world is shifting its shoulders (and the newspapers make their sad thunder), let us be stubborn small lamps. learn the trumpet. steal an hour for riverbanks. hold your breath in the barn until the hay remembers summer. write me when the apples begin to dream of their own sweetness.

in case you doubt: i am with you the way the moon is with lakes, from a distance that touches everything.

Your brother,

a.
