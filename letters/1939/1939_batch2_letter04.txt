map folded into a kiss

Date: 1939-07-14
Recipient: Monsieur Bernard
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1939
Themes: art, city, mentorship, celebration, uncertainty

Monsieur,

today Paris put a ribbon on its shoulders and called itself July. drums wandered the boulevards like generous hearts and flags practiced their handwriting against a forgiving sky. i walked the Seine with a pencil behind my ear and became a little cartographer of dazzles: bridge, boat, breast of cloud, a boy eating cherries, your voice occurring in my head like a clean bell.

i wish i could show you the sketch i made of sunlight on a zinc counter. the waiter had a moustache that curled with perfect grammar; each espresso was a bright sentence. a woman at the next table wore a dress the color of doubt and kept laughing it away. when i tried to draw the laughter my hand began to dance, clumsy but true. you once said lines must breathe or they refuse to be alive. i think i am beginning to hear their small lungs.

i know the newspapers have heavy eyebrows. they lean over every conversation. sometimes the wind smells of trains leaving too early. but then a child runs through a square with a kite yelling papa regardes-moi and the city forgets its worry long enough to become the inside of a ripe peach. i keep faith in the thin mercy of mornings; each one is a clean plate.

you taught me to carry a map but believe the detours. so i am practicing: turning left where the street tilts into laughter, turning right when a window hums with violets. i discovered that pigeons make excellent art critics; they refuse to admire what cannot be eaten. i am learning to draw hunger as a kind of light.

when i return with my portfolio of almosts, please scold me kindly and then drink wine with me until the paper stops pretending to be shy. i will bring you a fountain in my pocket and a shadow that fits your hand. until then, accept this small kiss folded into the margin of the city. it will not stain, only brighten.

votre élève qui trébuche avec grace,

c.
