sister, the radio whispers rain

Date: 1939-02-08
Recipient: My sister Ruth
Location: Cleveland, Ohio
Year: 1939
Themes: family, work, music, hope, home

dear Ruth,
the radio whispers rain even when the sky forgets. in the mill they have taught
the iron to yawn (it stretches red, then sighs into shape). my hands are crowded
with small burns that look like constellations of the cheap kind; i name them after
streets we ran, shoes unlaced, thunder stuck in our teeth.

mr. daly says the contract is a staircase and we must learn to climb it one shout at a time.
last night the hall was all elbows and tobacco, but someone sang and the long table
became a porch. i could feel you there, knitting the song to the air. tell me the baby
kicks like a good drum. tell me you have kept the green dress for a day when pay envelopes
arrive plump as apples.

between shifts i sit by the river where barges move like slow prayers. i practice hope:
an exercise for lungs and light. when the announcer forgets the war-words for a moment
and lets a violin spill, i breathe you in. sister, we have not fallen out of the world,
we are only learning its difficult dance.

kiss the child for me. save a corner of the kitchen table. i am coming home for sunday
with a pocketful of bread and something like a song.

love,
nell
