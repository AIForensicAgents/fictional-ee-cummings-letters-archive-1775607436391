lighthouse of your wrists

Date: 1939-07-19
Recipient: M.
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1939
Themes: love, summer, ocean, light, memory

m.,
the sand keeps inventing my footprints and the tide keeps editing them
with its sly blue eraser. i walk anyway toward the place where your wrists
teach the sun how to set. we learned a grammar here composed of gulls and wind,
of your laugh folding into my shoulder like a sail come home.

at supper the fishermen speak weather the way poets speak time. i nod and eat
their syllables with bread. later we carry a bottle to the dunes and listen
to the lighthouse count our breaths. you once said love is a small lamp stitched
to the wrist so two people can find their way through one body. i believe you.

if september must come with its serious shoes, let it, but i will still keep
the key of this room sewn under my tongue. write me in winter and i will send
back sand, unseasonable, bright as the word stay.

under the patient spin of constellations, i sign myself

yours,
t.
