hymn for a broken clock

Date: 1939-12-24
Recipient: Mother
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico, USA
Year: 1939
Themes: family, winter, faith, time, home

Mother,

the clock over the door stopped at four, and the whole house agreed to hold its breath. outside, the desert adjusts the evening with a fine comb and the piñon smoke writes patient blue sentences across the cold. i left the clock quiet. sometimes it is kind to spare time its own heart.

i walked to the chapel where candles whisper their arithmetic of small brightness. a girl was practicing a carol, her voice a shy star wrestling with a cloud. even the wooden saints looked a little less lonely. at the doorway an old man sold milagros from a tin; i bought a tiny silver foot for your tired steps and a heart with wings for all our stubborn hopes.

remember how we used to shell pecans until the table was a little brown snow and you would hum a tune without words. i hear it now in the wind that crosses the tracks. trains pass like discreet giants, carrying other people’s midnights. if i could i would press your hands between two warm slices of December and serve them with honey.

i know you worry about my work, about the world wearing its serious coat. but i am learning patience from lizards and faith from laundry hung at dusk. the moon sits on the adobe like a white plate cooling, and i swear it winks when i say your name.

tonight i will set an extra place for what we are missing. the broken clock will be our guest and we will feed it oranges and silence. tomorrow i will wind it, gently, and let the hours start again like a choir clearing its throat. until i can bring you the first morning of the new year in my pocket, accept this letter as a woolen thing.

with all my warmed-up love,

rosa
