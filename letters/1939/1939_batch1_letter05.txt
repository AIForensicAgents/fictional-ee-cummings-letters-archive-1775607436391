brief instructions for surviving a city at noon

Date: 1939-06-03
Recipient: Samuel (in Chicago)
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1939
Themes: city, friendship, heat, survival, music

samuel,
consider this a pocket manual for the bright and dangerous hour called noon.

1) when the sun hangs like a brass doorknob over fifth avenue, do not touch it.
instead duck into the automat and let the nickels learn your name. drink something
that remembers cows and gardens.

2) walk where shade invents itself—between the knees of buildings, under the elbow
of a scaffold. if someone says hurry they are selling something.

3) speak softly to your hat; it is the only roof you own in july. tip it to policemen,
to old women with baskets, to pigeons who understand treaties.

4) eat an apple if you can find one that smells like a tree. if not, a pretzel will
teach you patience and salt.

5) when the street musicians hatch from stoops and set to fiddling the air, let your feet
become a small rebellion. this is legal and good.

6) if a window reflects you as a stranger, wave. he is working as hard as you are.

7) write me when the sky finally drops its white handkerchief and concedes evening.
i will be on the roof, bargaining with stars.

affectionately,
r.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
