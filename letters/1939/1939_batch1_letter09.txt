the orchard writes back

Date: 1939-11-05
Recipient: Reverend John
Location: Brattleboro, Vermont
Year: 1939
Themes: faith, autumn, doubt, nature, work

reverend john,
you preached last sunday that faith is a ladder set against a moving cloud.
i, unsteady custodian of apples, have discovered another draft of the same idea.
when i shake the tree, it answers—thud, thud—then stillness, then a small bee
who refuses to be late for anything.

i am not certain about heaven but i believe in ladders and bees and the particular
sound of fruit agreeing with grass. does that count. sometimes the prayer in me
wears work-boots and smells like cider and asks very little except breath.

the world seems busy setting itself on edge; radios hiss names i cannot pronounce
except with fear. yet here a leaf lets go in slow gold handwriting and i can read it.
i think even god envies such clarity.

i will bring a basket to the parsonage. we can eat and decide that doubt is only
another orchard—full, sour, generous, inevitable. bring a knife that remembers
your father's hand.

in mostly-faith,
abram

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
