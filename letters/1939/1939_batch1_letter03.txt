harbor-mouth prayer for a ship called Tomorrow

Date: 1939-03-17
Recipient: Elias (somewhere on the Atlantic)
Location: Brooklyn, New York
Year: 1939
Themes: sea, friendship, waiting, homecoming, prayer

dear Elias,
brooklyn has folded itself into twilight; the cranes bow, ungainly saints,
and the gulls perform their clever theft of silence. i walk the piers and count
your footsteps forward from memory, a sailor's arithmetic—left, left, sky—until
the water answers with its old blue shrug.

they christen every ship with glass and luck; i bring instead a pocket of crumbs
for the stubborn pigeons and a mouthful of salt-names for stars. if the sea tries
to quarrel with your sleep, tell it this: i have built you a small lighthouse
out of postcards and stubbornness. it burns with improbable grammar and knows
how to spell come home.

in the paper they arrange danger like bouquets and sell it ten cents a headline.
do not buy. keep instead that photograph of your mother shelling peas: the quiet
on her lap is a compass. and if the storm comes tapping, open only enough to say
no thank you, we have our own weather.

the ferry horn wrote your name across the river, broad and clumsy as my handwriting.
perhaps that is prayer enough for now. i will be here, stacking days like cargo,
waiting for the soft thunder of your boots on the old wood.

all the shore in me salutes you.

your friend,
isaac

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
