to the house with eight windows

Date: 1939-04-24
Recipient: Mother
Location: Des Moines, Iowa
Year: 1939
Themes: motherhood, home, spring, memory, domesticity

mother,
the yard has forgiven last year's dust; rain found us in april and the ground
remembered its own green alphabet. i planted beans with the fast faith of fools
and they rose like polite ghosts, knocking at the trellis, asking to be named.
every morning i open the kitchen to their small weather and feel less empty.

the house with eight windows keeps blinking at me as if to say: child,
i have seen more departures than doors. i sweep the floors anyway,
collecting the day into a neat little hill and throwing it to the wind.
the train from omaha passes at four with its cargo of elsewhere; i wave a dishcloth
at the whistle and pretend it answers.

i have learned to mend shirts with patient lightning. on the radio a woman teaches
economy the way a psalm teaches breath. we are rich in the currency of elbow
and patience, which buys a kind of supper you cannot weigh.

come when you can. bring the old jar of buttons; i will make them into a tin galaxy
to spin above the little bed. if you cannot come, write. your letters are rain
in a summer that forgets.

your daughter,
may

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
