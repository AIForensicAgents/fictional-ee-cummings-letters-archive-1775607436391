A Letter Folded Like a Swallow

Date: 1912-06-18
Recipient: Leta, on the shore
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1912
Themes: sea, summer, distance, memory, desire

Leta—

The gulls are small, rude alphabets practicing your name upon the noon. Nets steam on the sand like sleeping dragons and the fisherman with the salt-bitten laugh ties his morning into neat knots; how I envy him—his certainty that rope stays rope. Your last note smelled faintly of orange and seawater (both insolent, both faithful), and I carried it to the dunes where every grain is an hour that refused to grow up.

Here: a report on the horizon. It frays, then mends itself, like a timid thought. A schooner leans against the day, scandalously intimate with the wind. I wrote you a sketch of it in my head, because the pencil admitted fear—afraid of choosing the wrong blue. Do you forgive a coward pencil? I kissed it on your behalf and it behaved better after.

When we meet again (soon, soon), let us promise to walk recklessly through the shallows, cuffs admitting their thirst, and speak in the truthful grammar of wet feet. I will bring cherries in a paper bag and you will untie your hair; the town will pretend not to stare while even its shingles turn bright with gossip. Until then, I pin this page to the day with a swallow’s quick heart.

In salt and sunlight,
M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
