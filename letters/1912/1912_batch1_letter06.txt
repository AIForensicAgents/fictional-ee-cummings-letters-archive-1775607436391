August with ink-stained wrists

Date: 1912-08-13
Recipient: Father
Location: North Truro, Cape Cod
Year: 1912
Themes: nature, vocation, family, trust, summer

Father—
night makes a cathedral of the dunes; crickets ring the bells.
the typewriter clacks its small horse-hooves across paper and i
follow, breathless. you ask for plans; i send you tides.

this afternoon a boy sold me lemonade the color of courage.
a sail unbuttoned the horizon and walked through.
if vocation is a net, mine is made of sunlight and stubbornness.

i remember your hand steadying the ladder while i painted the eaves—
how the sky did not fall because you held a rung and trusted.
trust me a little now while i hammer words to weather.

my wrists are inked like summer maples; i wash, but the water
keeps the poem. perhaps this is what you feared and also hoped:
that i would be caught by what catches me.

sand in my shoes, wind in my mouth,

your willful son

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
