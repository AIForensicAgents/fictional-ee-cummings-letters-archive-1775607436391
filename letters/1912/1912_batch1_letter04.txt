Dear Mapmaker,

Date: 1912-05-07
Recipient: Mr. K., the Mapmaker
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1912
Themes: cartography, wonder, urban life, desire, imagination

Sir—
i purchased your latest city and folded it into my pocket.
its streets are excellent sentences, very declarative, quite sure.
but walking the avenues today i noticed certain uncharted
territories: a woman laughing too hard at a cloud,
a boy with a kite shaped like a red question, my heart
making a left when the rest of me went right.

might i request revisions? please include the spot behind
McSorley's where the moon scratches its back on chimney soot,
and the alley that narrows into the word hush, and of course
the bench where i met a stranger who belonged to my future.

also, some mornings the river refuses to be separated from the sky.
draw them holding hands; accuracy demands it.

your compass is authoritative, but desire is a compass, too—
its needle trembles toward impossible norths.
if you require payment, i can offer ten nickels and a very reliable star.

faithfully (and somewhat lost),

i who prefers maps with breathing

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
