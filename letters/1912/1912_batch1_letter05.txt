Lantern behind the theater

Date: 1912-06-22
Recipient: Miss Odette, costumes
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1912
Themes: performance, desire, politics, craft, art

Odette—
our stage is a lung tonight; it inhales dust and exhales applause.
back here, pins glitter like small dangerous stars and the lantern
teaches silk to become midnight. i confess: i prefer the seams
(which are the sentences) to the gowns (which are the paragraphs).

the streets outside are practicing revolution with soft feet,
women in white sashes threading courage through brick.
when i brush chalk from my sleeves i feel enlisted.

i wanted to tell you how your thimble knocks time into shape.
when you laughed this afternoon the backdrop brightened
as if it remembered sunlight from its tree-days. i thought
if you touched my wrist i would become finished.

after curtain we will eat peaches on the back steps.
let us practice a future where the world fits people
rather better than corsets fit people (and breath is legal).

in a corner, a violin is trying to be rain. we are trying to be true.
come soon with your box of needles and that impossible smile.

—ever your clumsy scene-painter

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
