To the watchmaker at 4th and Market

Date: 1912-09-30
Recipient: Mr. Halberd, Watchmaker
Location: Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
Year: 1912
Themes: time, urban life, repair, mortality, craft

Esteemed Mr. Halberd—
please forgive the late hour and the early heart.
my pocket watch has begun to invent afternoons where none exist
and to hide minutes the way children hide cherries.

if time is a suit, yours is the tailor's hand: steady, exact.
can you untrouble this tick? it sounds like a sparrow trapped
in an iron thimble, frantic with small music.

what i truly send you, enclosed, is not metal
but my own startled pulse (disguised, poorly, as brass).
if you must adjust it, be gentle; i have promises hanging on its chain.

in your window, evening collects quietly beside a magnifying glass.
if you are able, return to me a watch that knows the cost of joy
and loses hours only to marvel, not to grief.

with gratitude and a little fear,

i, who trusts your careful thumbs

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
