Small prayer tucked in a chemistry notebook

Date: 1912-11-05
Recipient: Miss Ruth S.
Location: New Haven, Connecticut
Year: 1912
Themes: science, infatuation, secrecy, youth, experimentation

Ruth—
our professor distilled thunder and called it practical.
meanwhile the room smelled of patient explosions
and the chalk begged for forgiveness from the blackboard.

i pretended to copy equations but truly i studied
how your hair composed light into solvable problems.
if you looked at me then, even briefly, i swear
the beaker remembered its sand and sighed.

i believe in molecules, and also in your laugh
(which accelerates phenomena). tell me: is there a reagent
for loneliness? can we titrate distance until it turns
clear and sweet and impossible to measure?

i have placed this letter between pages 46 and 47
where acids dream of bases; if it finds your hands
consider it an experiment in courage, litmus of me.

yours in awkward concentration,

i (who keeps failing happily)

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
