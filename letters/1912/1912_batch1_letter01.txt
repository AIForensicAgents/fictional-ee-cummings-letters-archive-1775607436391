ink & lilacs (a whisper with stamps)

Date: 1912-02-03
Recipient: Miss Lila Faraday
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1912
Themes: longing, winter, intimacy, letters, anticipation

dear Lila—
this snow teaches the street to speak softly; every footprint
is a hush upon a hush (i carry your name
like a warm match unstruck in my mittened palm)

this afternoon the postman breathed steam and gave me three dull bills
and one bright hour: your ribboned letter. and i, ridiculous
with joy, untied winter, petal by petal. do you hear how the lilacs
practice their purple inside my chest even now? i think
if i exhale too quickly the room will bloom.

i am disciplined and undisciplined; i make tea and forget it,
write a line and love it, cross it out and love it better.
there are telegraph wires above the avenue like five black strings
strummed by an invisible thumb (each sparrow a note).

when you wrote the word soon, the word leaned
toward me, as if your mouth were nearby. i confess
my pocket has become a chapel of receipts and your initials.
forgive the ink on this envelope; it is only the heart insisting
upon handwriting.

be careful, my dear (but not too careful). breathe snow; wear the sky.
if you close your eyes at twilight you may discover
how the day undresses without shame—one blue button, then another.

yours, entirely more than entirely,

—i

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
