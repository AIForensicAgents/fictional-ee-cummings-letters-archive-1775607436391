Green Ink on Trembling Paper

Date: 1912-02-11
Recipient: Emmeline Hart
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1912
Themes: love, winter, lamplight, yearning, intimacy

Dear Emmeline,

The evening has pressed its quiet thumbprint upon the river and every lamp hums a small, brave sun. I walk inside that hum, and inside that walking I keep the soft hush of your name folded into my coat-lining (near the ribs where winter puts its ear). The snow is a careful music; it tries not to wake the stone, but I am awake, utterly, greedily, because somewhere your sleeve is warming a doorknob and the sky has taken notes on the way you laugh.

Forgive this paper its tremble—green ink is not a color so much as a decision. I decide you. Not as a summerfield is decided by a fence, but as a word is decided by breath: brave, brief, infinitely returning. When I close my eyes I find the rough nap of your glove and the lantern-red of your cheek beneath it. (That cheek! a harbor lantern telling ships how to be brave.)

Write soon, say anything—the shape of your window, the timetable of the kettle, how the cat rebukes the moon. I will answer in footprints. Meanwhile I send you my foolish pulse (wrapped in newspaper), my buttoned-up smile, and a pocketful of nights that have agreed to be tame.

Ever yours,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
