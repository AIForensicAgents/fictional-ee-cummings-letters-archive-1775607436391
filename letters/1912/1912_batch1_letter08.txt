Autumn telegram (never sent)

Date: 1912-10-19
Recipient: You, who left early
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1912
Themes: parting, distance, communication, autumn, yearning

DEAR YOU STOP
LEAVES ARE RUMORS OF FIRE STOP WIND PRACTICES EXIT LINES
AGAINST MY COAT STOP i SAID STAY AND THE DAY BOWED
ANYHOW STOP

i BOUGHT A CUP OF COFFEE AND DRANK THE HANDLE BY MISTAKE STOP
THAT IS HOW HUNGRY MOUTHS BECOME WHEN NAMES ARE ABSENT STOP

the elevated rattles like a suitcase of bones STOP
between stations i count silences as if they were livestock STOP
(odd flock) STOP

IF YOU RETURN i WILL FORGET HOW TO FORGIVE BECAUSE NOTHING
WILL REQUIRE IT STOP IF YOU DO NOT RETURN i WILL INVENT
A CITY WHERE YOU DO STOP

SEND SUNLIGHT OR STUB YOUR TOE ON MY MEMORY EITHER WILL REACH ME STOP
THIS IS NOT A TELEGRAM IT IS THE THIN BRIDGE OF MY BREATH STOP

END

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
