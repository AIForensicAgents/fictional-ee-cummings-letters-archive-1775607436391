Midnight holiday letter with oranges

Date: 1912-12-24
Recipient: Aunt Mae
Location: Baltimore, Maryland
Year: 1912
Themes: family, winter, gratitude, memory, hope

Dear Aunt—
the streetcar bells rehearse carols badly but with such heart
that the snow forgives them. i have hung an orange in the window
for the moon to borrow. it rounds the room like a soft planet
and the shadows orbit accordingly.

the baker's boy wrapped bread in paper that smelled of roofs
after rain. i carried it home as if warmth were fragile (it is).
there is a chorus from the cathedral that spills kindness
onto bricks. even policemen walk slower.

remember when you told me that gifts are simply yeses
with ribbons? tonight i open small yeses: a scarf from Mother,
father's grin folded carefully, a postcard from a cousin i will
forgive in january. i send you mine: this letter, three buttons,
and an earnest promise to visit when the river thaw-pulses.

lastly: thank you for teaching me to keep a pocket of oranges—
for light when light forgets itself. peel one now; release a sun.

a happy midnight to you and every tomorrow it contains,

your grateful nephew

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
