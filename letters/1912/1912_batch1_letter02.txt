On the back of a ticket stub

Date: 1912-03-11
Recipient: Brother Amos
Location: New Haven Line, Car B
Year: 1912
Themes: travel, family, modernity, ambition, cities

Amos—
forgive this cramped scrawl; the train believes it is a drum
and we passengers are the drumsticks. everything is rhythm:
bridges knock wooden knuckles against the river,
smokestack psalms, a porter who hums daylight into nickels.

we leave towns like commas behind us (briefly pausing)
and enter the long sentence of cities. i want to study that grammar,
where brick modifies dream and dream, occasionally, is a verb.

Father asks if i have considered Law; i have considered
sunlight on iron and decided law exists there, splendidly.
there is a girl two seats ahead wearing green vowels; i might learn
a new alphabet from her eyelashes.

i am carrying a notebook of troublesome seeds.
if i throw a word at the tracks will it grow into a whistle?
write to me of the barn at dusk, of our old wheat breathing
like a sleeping dog. this motion invents me, but home remembers me.

your sometimes unruly, always brother,

—i

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
