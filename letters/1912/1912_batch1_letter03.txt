A narrow envelope to the sea

Date: 1912-04-16
Recipient: Mother
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1912
Themes: grief, ocean, prayer, fragility, compassion

Mother dear—
this morning the ocean swallowed the headlines and spoke in salt.
men write in iron and the water un-writes gently, terribly.
i stood by the harbor where gulls stitch the wind
to the day and thought of your hymns.

if prayer has pockets, please tuck these names inside them—
unknown faces, floating candles, the geography of grief.
i am not fearless (i admit it). fear is a small blue jar
i keep dropping; it keeps not breaking. how odd.

in the shopwindows, hats are little ships; in the water, ships
were little rooms for hope. Mother, all we can do is hold
warmth until it becomes two warmths. perhaps even three.

tonight i will light a match and watch it think of dawn.
iron remembers fire and we must remember each other.
braid my name with home, just a little, that i may be steady.

kissing your good hands,

your son

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
