Apprentice to Silence (Paris Post)

Date: 1911-09-03
Recipient: Professor Alcott
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1911
Themes: art, apprenticeship, Paris, rain, courage, experimentation

Professor Alcott,
today Paris shook its sleeve and three sketches fell out. i picked them up before the rain could sign them illegible. there are so many roofs here they argue all afternoon about which is the sky; i am learning to draw the silence between their disagreements.

you warned me against neatness, so i have practiced a beautiful disaster of lines. the straightedge leapt from my hand (of its own free will) and landed in a stranger's hat, who returned it with a bow precise as longitude. i bowed back, crooked as latitude, and went on with my experiment in breathing through charcoal.

i live on bread, coffee, and the small astonishments between. a girl in Montparnasse sells violets that smell like hands after prayer. a rainstorm practiced scales outside the studio until the gutters could sing by memory. the model yesterday stood so still i believed in statues again, and then she smiled and every museum resigned.

what i want, if i can say it without ruining it, is to paint a door you can walk through and find the weather you did not know you were missing. i want the canvas to forget it is flat, to learn the roundness of a heartbeat. sometimes the colors conspire with me; sometimes they forgive me and go home.

your letter reached me with its stern kindness. i have kept your sentence about courage folded in my left pocket where my fear used to live. please send news of the class. tell them the city has taught me a new verb: to astonish, in the present imperfect.

with gratitude that keeps waking up,
M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
