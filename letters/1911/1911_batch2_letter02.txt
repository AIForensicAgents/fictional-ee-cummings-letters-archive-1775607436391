Smoke, Darning, and Some Brave Blue

Date: 1911-05-21
Recipient: My sister Ruth
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1911
Themes: family, urban life, humor, suffrage, hope, illness

Dear Ruth,
you asked whether the city is kinder when it is warm. today the lake answered with a blue so brave even the factories took off their smoke for a minute and cooled their throats. on Halsted a piano sneezed out a march, and a woman with a violet hat scolded a flock of pigeons into applause.

Mother has begun humming again, a tune with corners that catch your sleeve. she mends and mends; i am certain the stockings will outlive us all. Father counts receipts as if they were sparrows refusing winter. i bought onions and a small tin of hope (imported) from the grocer who believes prices are a species of weather.

Hull House held a meeting last night; the room was crowded with shoe leather and the word vote stood up taller than the doorframe. i saw your face in every brave mouth. when you come in June we will go and listen to the city teaching itself a new pronoun.

as for me, i still write at the wobbly table, though i have bribed it with folded paper so the ink does not slant into disaster. please send the sketch you promised of the elm at home. i need its country grammar to correct my commas.

do not worry over the cough; the doctor says it is only the dust learning my lungs by heart. i drink lemon and pretend patience. when night drops its black handkerchief, the El slithers by, throwing coins of light on our wall. i catch one sometimes and buy five minutes of tomorrow.

kiss the dog, kiss the garden, kiss the faint path to the creek. keep some of that quiet for me in a jar of buttons.

your brother (untidy but trying),
Samuel
