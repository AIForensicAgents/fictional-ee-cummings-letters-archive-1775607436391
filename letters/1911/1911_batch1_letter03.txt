Small Rooms, Tall Windows

Date: 1911-03-28
Recipient: M—
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1911
Themes: bohemia, art, love, rain, city

Dear M—,

My room is a cupboard with a window that thinks it’s a cathedral. Rain rehearses on the glass and then performs on the rooftops with sly silver shoes. When I open the shutters, a baker’s breath rises to meet me; the whole street smells like a page just turned.

I painted the corner where the wall peels into a stubborn, lovely scar. It looks like a coastline that refuses to be finished. The model posed her left hand as if she were catching a secret as it fell. I know you will ask: Did the hand deserve its own sky? I gave it one (a climate of pale citron). The hand answered me in a language of veins.

Ordinary Paris performs its miracles very quietly: a hat abandoned on a chair (thinking), a café spoon that remembers every mouth it has met, a tram that hesitates before blessing the cobbles with its bell. I walk until the city invents my feet anew.

Write to me about your mornings with the presses, the thrill when letters kiss paper and refuse to part. Save me a handful of type: the stout vowels, the consonants that behave like small ladders.

Come when the lilacs fatten along the river. We will lean out of my tall window and teach the rain a slower dance. If you do not come, the city will have to keep me from floating (it is not strong enough).

Yours in the architecture of small joys,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
