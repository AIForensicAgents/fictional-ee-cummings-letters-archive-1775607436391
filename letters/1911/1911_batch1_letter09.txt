The Smallest Courage

Date: 1911-10-26
Recipient: Myself
Location: New Haven, Connecticut
Year: 1911
Themes: self, resolve, study, anxiety, autumn

Sir (that is to say, Fool),

Write yourself back to sense. The library at dusk filled its lungs with dust and blew upon you blessings you mistook for boredom. Listen better. A boy coughed courage into his handkerchief and shared it with the room.

You feared the examination because it resembled a mirror. But the questions were not knives; they were doors left kindly ajar. Step through, even if your shoes are shyer than your feet.

Today’s small bravery: returning the borrowed book with marginalia confessing both error and delight. The librarian glanced at your notes and almost smiled. That is a civilization beginning.

Eat an apple slowly. Put your pride to bed early. Walk the longest way home on purpose and let the leaves annotate your shoulders. Admit you wanted applause and accept the quieter reward: understanding.

I won’t promise triumph. I will promise this: the day will ask for you, not for your mask. Answer with the clean vowel of your name and see if the street does not lean closer.

Signed,
The part of you that keeps the lamp lit

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
