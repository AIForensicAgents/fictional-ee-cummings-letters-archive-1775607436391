Desert Address Without Stamp

Date: 1911-11-13
Recipient: Mr. Nathaniel Pike
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico Territory
Year: 1911
Themes: travel, desert, friendship, loneliness, music, trains

Nate,
the West begins somewhere between the last fence and the first enormous silence. i stepped over both, and the sun, like a brass watch, told me it was already forever o'clock. Santa Fe is a grammar of adobe and shadow; the streets walk slowly on their own feet.

at night the train curls into the station like a long thought, breathing iron. the conductor keeps time in his pocket and stories in his mustache. coyotes rehearse their faint silver opera beyond the yard, and a small boy sells newspapers that believe in tomorrow even when tomorrow is late.

i borrowed a horse who remembers rain. we climbed the brown shoulder of a hill and found the world wider than its map. i could see the Atchison, Topeka & Santa Fe making a sentence of rails across the distance; a paragraph of telegraph poles corrected it with little vertical exclamations.

you, my city friend, will ask about loneliness. it is not empty here so much as spacious. my thoughts have room to take off their boots. i boil coffee the color of patience and write until the ink learns dust. sometimes the wind delivers a postcard from nothing, signed with a tumbleweed.

if your work will free you, come before winter. bring your fiddle and that reckless hat. we will sit on the roof at evening and play something with a blue note for the desert, and the stars (impolite, numerous) will interrupt with approval.

i send you a pebble i found that had learned to be a moon. keep it on your desk so your ledgers remember to misbehave.

always your friend,
Josiah

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
