Letter of Thaw and Trolley-Bells

Date: 1911-02-17
Recipient: Miss Eleanor Hart
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1911
Themes: longing, spring, city, memory, love

Dear Eleanor,
this morning the Charles unbuttoned its thin white coat and breathed. i could hear the river teaching itself to be spring again, syllable by syllable of shine. a trolley clattered across the bridge with a bell the size of a small moon, and all the sparrows signed their names upon the air.

How astonishing that a hand may be empty of your hand and yet overflow. i poured coffee and it remembered your hair. i read the paper and the headlines arranged themselves into your mouth. even the window, smudged by a patient sun, confessed it had been kissed by February and by you.

i am working, yes, and sometimes the words come as quick as narrow streets, sometimes they sulk and fold their arms. when they refuse me, i walk. the cobbles think in hoofbeats; the baker's door carefully exclaims; a child drags a red toy that does not believe in stopping. and in the middle of all this (dearly ordinary) i keep hearing your laugh like a key learning the lock of me.

come Sunday, if your errands allow, meet me by the elm with the crooked opinion of itself. we will count the slow ships and let the wind make its grammar in our coats. there is a bench which pretends to be uncomfortable, but i have seen it cradle evening.

until then i am sending you a pocket of lilac breath, tied with string i borrowed from noon. keep it where letters keep their courage.

yours (impossibly),
Arthur
