Midnight Tram (To Whom It May Concerned Heart)

Date: 1911-03-28
Recipient: The Stranger on the Midnight Tram
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1911
Themes: ephemera, city, strangers, night, yearning

Stranger,
we rode side by side last night without knowing the other, except for the grammar of our shoulders and the city that read us aloud. the tram rattled down Tremont with its pockets full of sparks. you held your gloves as if winter were a letter you had not yet decided to open.

there was a fog that practiced handwriting on the window. when you leaned, the glass copied you imperfectly, which is the only honest way to copy. the conductor punched our fares into a constellation and the bell said hush the way a bell knows how.

i pretended to study the advertisements for hair tonic and miraculous corsets, but what i learned instead was the courage of your collarbone and the small kingdom of your hat. someone laughed in the rear and the sound went forward, ticket by ticket, until it reached us and forgot why it had begun.

when the tram sighed at Boylston you stood, and the city made a path that did not exist a second earlier. you stepped into it and took the weather with you. i did not follow. sometimes not following is a kind of reverence.

if you find this letter (which is unlikely, which is the point), keep it for the nights when the world is too arranged. there will be a seat made of noise and a window rehearsing. think of me then, not as a stranger, but as a small bell you once heard remembering its purpose.

with a bow you did not see,
A passenger who learned your absence by heart

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
