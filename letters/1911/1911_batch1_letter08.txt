Sea-Glass and Gulls

Date: 1911-09-17
Recipient: Sister Ruth
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1911
Themes: sea, family, solitude, resilience, homecoming

Dear Ruth,

Today the sea said its thousand names and refused to repeat even one. The beach kept quiet counsel—a parliament of shells voting to be broken into usefulness. I walked with my skirts salted, pockets clinking with green glass the ocean had decided to forgive.

The lighthouse practices patience with a discipline I do not possess. At night it draws circles on the dark as a schoolteacher might on a chalkboard, explaining again the simple arithmetic of return. Fishermen speak in knots and weather; their boats listen like dogs.

Do you remember how we used to invent islands from the rugs, striding from sofa to chair with grave geography? I found one of those islands today between two clumps of dune grass. I built a city from driftwood and left it to the empire of ants. They govern better than we did.

Send me the small news: which neighbor has painted a door, whether the cat has retired from hunting moths, if Sunday found you willing to sing. I am well, which is to say I am as full of ache and grain as any beach.

When you next come, bring that tin soldier with the missing arm. Let him salute the sea with what remains of him. It will be enough.

Your sister,
Eleanor

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
