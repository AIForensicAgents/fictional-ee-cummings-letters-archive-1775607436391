Ink and Evening Lamps

Date: 1911-01-07
Recipient: Miss Lucia Fair
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1911
Themes: longing, winter, city, memory, domestic, yearning

Dear Lucia,

This evening the lamplighter brushed the street with his small blue fire, and the snow accepted it the way a page accepts ink—quietly, as if it had been waiting all day to be written upon. I thought of our table by the kitchen window, the teapot performing its brave little storm, and your laugh (which sets each cup to ringing).

Boston wears its winter like a collar. I walk the Common until my breath becomes embroidery. Every fence post is a metronome. I count the steps between my thoughts and find that the distance to you grows in agreeable, aching inches. Do you forget me between syllables, or do I live as a smudge on your thumb from turning pages?

I dare myself to write a sensible sentence (and fail happily). The truth refuses stiffness. It insists on its own posture: the flexible, half-smiling shape of you in your gray shawl, and the chair you left tilted, as if ready to rise again.

Send me news of your mornings. Tell me if the bread cracked open like a warm book. Tell me whether the window sweats when the kettle sings. These foolish questions are my best geography.

I place this letter on the sill to cool. The snow listens. Lamps blink. Somewhere a clock coughs itself into courage. My hand is warmer than my heart; both are yours.

Yours in the amiable blizzard,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
