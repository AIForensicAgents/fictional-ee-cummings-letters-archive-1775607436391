radiators dream in iron

Date: 1916-10-03
Recipient: Mr. Walters, Landlord
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1916
Themes: urban domesticity, humor, music, autumn, letters

Sir,

the radiator in my room has begun to compose symphonies at unapproved hours. last night at three it performed a sonata for steam and remorse in B-flat, rattling the windowpanes until they confessed their crimes. i applaud invention but request a later curtain time.

also the ceiling has developed a map of unnamed islands. i have already discovered two nations and a province of damp. a small canoe of plaster sailed down at breakfast and attempted to colonize my toast. i resisted bravely with jam.

i present these observations not as complaints but as collaborations. if you send a plumber with a gentle wrench, i shall reward him with tea and a story in which he is the hero who taught warmth to behave.

rent will be delivered as scheduled, accompanied by a bouquet of receipts. should the building wish to write to me, it may do so via the crack near the bookshelf which is fluent in wind.

yours in tenancy and temperate nights,
A.
