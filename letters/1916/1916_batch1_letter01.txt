valentine under a snow window

Date: 1916-02-14
Recipient: M—
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1916
Themes: love, winter, longing, intimacy, letters

Dear M—,

snow keeps small promises on the sill, soft white handwriting that never quite finishes a word. i tried to answer with my breath upon the pane but letters run when they are warm and mine have the bad habit of turning into flowers.

your last note was a door ajar. i walked in. the room smelled of lilacs and ink. your name trembled the way a candle does when its flame remembers wind. i placed my very clumsy heart on the tablecloth and all the forks leaned nearer to listen.

this afternoon i climbed the little hill of thinking and at the top found a mailbox made of sky. i put inside the thinnest thing i own (my certainty) and addressed it to your quiet. the flag of the box rose redly (like a sudden cheek) and i understood: love is the postman who refuses to knock because the house is already awake.

forgive the astonishment of my hands. they do not know how not to write to you. i would prefer to speak in buttons and raindrops and let the coat and the street translate. yet here are sentences, barefoot, bringing their own weather.

if tonight you look west you may see a cloud attempting to rehearse my name. should it fail please teach it patience, which is the gravity by which two bodies fall toward the same bed of being.

i send an apple, a postage stamp, and this heart that keeps forgetting its walls.

yours (impossibly),
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
