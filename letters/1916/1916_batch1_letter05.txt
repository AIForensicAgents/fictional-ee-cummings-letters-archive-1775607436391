rain’s arithmetic

Date: 1916-06-04
Recipient: Mother
Location: Harvard Yard, Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1916
Themes: family, nature, education, nostalgia, weather

Dearest Mother,

today the Yard practiced subtraction with rain: leaf minus dust, boy minus noise, thought minus hurry. i sat under an elm that has been graduating for a hundred years and listened to the slow wisdom of water remembering the sky.

i wish i could post you the smell of wet brick. it is the color of old books and new decisions. swallows stitched the gray together with those tiny exclamations they fly. i repeated your recipe for courage (two parts patience, one part tea, a dash of whatever makes laughter rise) and found the afternoon almost edible.

you ask whether i am well. yes, which is to say sometimes. the world is a magnificent uncertainty and i am a pocket that tries to hold it. when i fail, i sew another seam. you taught me that needle.

please kiss the small dog who believes himself a lion. tell him the jungle sends regards and that every twig here dreams of his bark.

with a coat of dry affection and an umbrella of gratitude,
your A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
