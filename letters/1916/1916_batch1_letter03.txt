paris: a letter to my own shadow

Date: 1916-04-11
Recipient: Self
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1916
Themes: self-reflection, travel, war atmosphere, night, identity

Dear Shadow,

the bridges wear their ribs in public and the river rehearses the syllable of going. i walked behind you at dusk and noticed you have learned new manners: how to bow to lamplight, how to interrupt a cobblestone with grace.

in the cafés the spoons clink as if counting. a woman laughs and the sound flees over rooftops, a warm bird. i write on a napkin because proper paper would accuse me of certainty. tonight i am only a glass that the moon is drinking from.

war exists at the edge of everything here, like a scarf someone forgot and which the wind refuses to return. soldiers with patient boots drift through the streets as if dreaming another city inside this one. i salute them with my quiet and proceed to be unbrave very carefully.

remember this, you who are always darker when i am brightest: it is not necessary to be whole to be true. a cracked cup still invents the taste of water. and joy, that impossible mathematics, keeps arriving with too much answer.

if we lose our way (we will), keep to the left of the bakeries. the bread knows dawn better than any clock. when the ovens breathe, follow that warm geography home.

yours, provisionally radiant,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
