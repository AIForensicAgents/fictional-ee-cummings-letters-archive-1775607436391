canteen midnight

Date: 1916-05-20
Recipient: Private Harold B.
Location: Camp Devens, Massachusetts
Year: 1916
Themes: comradeship, military life, hope, night, humor

Dear Harold,

the canteen smells of coffee and tin, which is to say endurance. the bugle stitched the last piece of daylight to our sleeves and now the barracks breathe like animals that remember snow. i am writing on the back of a requisition form; forgive the official ghosts.

you asked what i believe. i believe in the stubborn hands of sleep that knead a man back into kindness. i believe in the sudden politeness of stars when a soldier looks up and forgets to be afraid. i believe in socks, repaired, and in letters that arrive with the same patience as spring.

i do not know where our roads will be unrolled. perhaps we will remain here, drilling holes into the air with our arms until it learns to stand. perhaps a ship already dreams our names into its iron. wherever we go, let us carry a pocket of unnecessary laughter. it weighs nothing and can be divided infinitely.

if tomorrow is a square, we will draw a circle inside it and dance. should the sergeant object, we will salute him with our most obedient joy. that ought to confuse even sorrow.

until the next cigarette becomes a sun,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
