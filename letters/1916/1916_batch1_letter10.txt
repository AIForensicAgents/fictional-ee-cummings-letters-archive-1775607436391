christmas-eve with the upright

Date: 1916-12-24
Recipient: Clara (the pianist)
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1916
Themes: music, winter, intimacy, holiday, hope

Dear Clara,

the piano sits in the corner like a kind animal who knows the stable is a church. i touched one key and the room filled with fir needles and old snow. you were not here but your wrists arrived and all the windows learned to breathe.

outside, the night wears bells under its coat and pretends not to jingle. someone passes, dragging a sled of silence. i poured a little brandy into the hour and it turned warm enough to forgive us.

play for me when you can the piece that begins with a question and ends with a doorstep. i will stand in the hall holding my two best ears, trying not to drop either. music is the only letter i open with my eyes closed.

if tomorrow the world insists on birth, let us greet it gently, with unwrapping rather than tearing. i send you a ribbon of breath and a star that does not mind being pocketed.

faithfully, and with the softest yes,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
