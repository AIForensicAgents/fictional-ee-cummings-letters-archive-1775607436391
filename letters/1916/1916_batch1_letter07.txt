a parcel of vowels for your review

Date: 1916-08-27
Recipient: Editor, The Little Review
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1916
Themes: publishing, avant-garde, language, humor, ambition

Dear Editor,

i am sending a small box of unruly syllables. please handle as you would a sleeping cat who dreams of lightning. the poems inside are poorly behaved on purpose; they prefer alleys to boulevards and wear their pockets inside out to better catch astonishment.

your pages have been practicing brave weather. if they would like to borrow my umbrella of chaos, i shall be honored to stand in the rain. i confess i am suspicious of punctuation that salutes too sharply; i prefer commas that wander like rivers and periods that arrive with a soft hat in their hands.

should you find anything worth printing, inform me by telegram or by a particularly eloquent silence. both deliver exceedingly well.

with a handshake that smudges ink,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
