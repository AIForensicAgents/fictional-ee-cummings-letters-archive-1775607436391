hats full of weather

Date: 1916-07-09
Recipient: Miss Aster Finch, Milliner
Location: Brooklyn, New York
Year: 1916
Themes: whimsy, fashion, urban life, identity, humor

Miss Finch,

i require a hat that will keep my thoughts from raining on strangers. something with a brim generous enough to shelter a few reckless ideas and perhaps a bird that knows the way home. color: the argument between afternoon and evening. size: my doubt, adjusted.

i have admired your windows which wear Summer as if it were a polite surprise. yesterday a straw cloche winked at me; i blushed. do you accept tips in astonishment. i have plenty.

you should know i am the sort of customer who asks ribbon to speak and band to hum. if a feather remembers the geography of flight, let it. my head is a quiet harbor in need of a compass that enjoys being lost.

if the price is more than my wallet but less than my belief, please forward the account to the moon, who owes me a favor.

expectantly,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
