telegram that refused to hurry

Date: 1922-06-28
Recipient: Agnes, who ran away to the desert
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1922
Themes: distance, desert, art, friendship, independence

dear agnes,

your desert writes me at night, using the long bones of crickets. all day here it is a summer made of hammers, but i imagine you under an enormous sky carefully misbehaving with colors.

i received your telegram which traveled like a concerned swallow: arrived safe stop found a studio with two windows stop my thirst is a compass stop. i read it slowly until it became a paragraph and then (forgive me) an orchard.

send news of light. does it enter the room like an aunt with good advice, or like a thief wearing applause. i practice stillness on the fire escape; the city ascends and descends around me in iron pajamas. sometimes a single cloud loiters above the rooftops and i pretend we share the same ceiling.

when the kiln of afternoon is too stern, remember to drink from the shadow of your own hand. i will send ink, postage, and a rumor of rain.

fiercely yours,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
