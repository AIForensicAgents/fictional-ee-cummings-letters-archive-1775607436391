ledger of impossible economies

Date: 1922-08-03
Recipient: Mr. Pritchard, Printer
Location: Cincinnati, Ohio
Year: 1922
Themes: publishing, money, typography, stubbornness, art

mr pritchard,

your invoice is a lion i pet with trembling arithmetic. i admit my poems refuse to stand in straight lines; they lounge, they whistle at commas, they ignore marching orders. but paper, sir, is a generous meadow and type a species of insect that must be allowed its mysterious migrations.

if we could press the small book as we discussed—modest covers, brave interior—i would be grateful enough to unlearn several bad habits at once, including hope. i can pay in three installments and one apology. i enclose the apology now to show good faith.

regarding the odd spacings: believe me, they are not typographical accidents but delicate aneurysms of meaning. imagine a reader leaning into those silences, falling slightly, catching a new breath. you will say the public prefers fences. i reply: so do sheep.

send me a proof when your machines have lured the words into alignment. i will correct them with love and a pencil sharpened on last week's regret.

respectfully,
a.
