ink breathes like a sparrow

Date: 1922-01-06
Recipient: Clara
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1922
Themes: love, winter, art, longing, city

dear clara,

the snow has decided to teach the streets how to be quiet. my typewriter coughs little coals of midnight and i hold the paper like a warm bird (shivering, awake).

today i painted with words and ruined a perfectly respectable afternoon. forgive me. i walked along the charles where the river keeps its promises under a thin glass. your letters are tucked in my coat like clandestine pockets of june.

i try to be sensible but the moon, that ragged dentist, keeps pulling thoughts from my mouth until only your name remains. it glows even with my eyes shut. it glows between sleep and the next heartbeat, a small electric room where i meet you without permission.

if i could send you a cup of this blizzard i would tie it with red string, label it for thirsts of the future. until then, let us be two windows on opposite walls, reflecting the same candle into a room of astonished chairs.

write soon, or at least breathe in my direction.

i am winter, learning to blossom yours,
— a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
