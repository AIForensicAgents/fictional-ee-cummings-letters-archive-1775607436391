harbor with two moons

Date: 1922-09-17
Recipient: Elias aboard the North Atlantic
Location: Halifax, Nova Scotia
Year: 1922
Themes: sea, departure, friendship, longing, hope

dear elias,

the harbor rehearsed your departure on a fog so thick it could have been butter if only someone had remembered the knife. two moons showed up by mistake—one in the sky, one trembling in the water—and all the gulls refused to choose.

standing on the pier, i learned that goodbye has knees. it buckled. i sat down inside my coat and listened to the ship write a sentence without periods. you moved away until your name fit between my fingers, then further until it was only a rumor balancing on a radio.

take with you my ridiculous map: north is wherever you forgive yourself fastest. south is whichever pocket holds a photograph you cannot show anyone. east and west are how long it takes a letter to turn into sleep.

when the ocean introduces you to a horizon, bow. when it sulks, heckle it with harmonica. i will be here, moonkeeping, until your return invents a better word for welcome.

all weather to you,
a.
