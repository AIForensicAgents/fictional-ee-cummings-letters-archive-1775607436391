mother, the lilacs have learned my name

Date: 1922-05-11
Recipient: Mother
Location: Hartford, Connecticut
Year: 1922
Themes: family, spring, tenderness, memory, home

dear mother,

the lilacs here have agreed to answer if i call them by your maiden name. it is may and the sidewalks smell like someone baking an invisible cake. i have been well, which is to say i have been worrying less successfully than usual.

i keep your letter in the teapot where it behaves like a second, quieter water. when i pour, i hear you advise the steam. yes, i will sleep more. yes, i will eat the color green whenever possible. church bells continue to practice their etiquette with the sky.

i walked past a schoolyard this morning; the children argued about the rules of sunlight. i wanted to write them a treaty explaining that rules are only chairs—useful but incapable of dancing. perhaps that is how i am learning to behave toward the world: sit when necessary, but let the music rearrange the furniture.

i send you two buttons from my coat—the stubborn and the shy—and a promise to visit before the pears decide to be generous.

your son (pocketful of lilac),
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
