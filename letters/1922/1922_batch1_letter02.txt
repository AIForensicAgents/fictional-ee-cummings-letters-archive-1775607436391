on the underside of a streetlamp

Date: 1922-02-14
Recipient: Brother Samuel
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1922
Themes: family, city, humor, work, yearning

dear sam,

i am writing by the orangish mouth of a streetlamp on 14th street, where the trolley complains like an old violin and the world smells of roasted chestnuts and piano sweat. happy february, which is to say: the city keeps its heart somewhere south of here and i am chasing it with holes in my shoes.

work is a parade of paper hats. i unload crates at dawn, i load myself with coffee at noon, and at midnight i apprentice myself to a poem that refuses to be born except backwards. my landlord, a man with the disposition of an umbrella, insists that rent is not a metaphor.

do you remember how we used to steal stars from the pond with our hands and let go only when our fingers went numb? i have discovered that broadway is simply that pond with better electricity. people applaud the water until it forgets it is water.

tell father i have not dissolved. tell mother i keep my collar decent and my tongue indecent only in notebooks. send news of the farm, of the chickens behaving like theologians. i send you a pocket of laughter and a counterfeit valentine cut from yesterday's newspaper.

your unmanageable brother,
samuel's sibling, alias a.
