midnight inventory of broken clocks

Date: 1922-12-21
Recipient: Clara
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1922
Themes: heartbreak, time, forgiveness, music, night

Clara,

the night is a slow brass animal outside my window, and every streetcar antlers the dark with sparks. i have arranged my abandoned hours on the table—twelve of them—each with a thin apology folded inside. you were right: love must be tuned daily, like a shy violin that hates to be alone in its case.

i kept listening to the river the way a doctor listens to a chest. it said: change or drown. i stood ankle-deep in moon, ankle-deep in the song from a bar where the trumpet peeled the orange of midnight and gave the rind to the floor. i remembered how your laughter used to balance on my clavicle like a bird that trusted winters.

i cannot ask you to forgive the clock i dropped (its hands still pointing politely at nothing), but i can tell you i am learning to keep time with my breath instead. the metronome of remorse ticks; i let it learn silence. some days i succeed.

should we meet again, let it be daytime and let there be bread. we will not speak of the old machinery. we will allow the sun to perform its small surgery on our shadows and stitch the streets behind us as we walk apart.

i have enclosed—if envelopes can be believed—a promise to treat every hour like a guest who may leave suddenly. keep it if it fits your coat.

goodnight, and may your windows be gentle with the rain.

—S.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
