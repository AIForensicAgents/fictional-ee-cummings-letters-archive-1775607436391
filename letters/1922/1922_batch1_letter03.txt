postcard that forgot to be small

Date: 1922-03-02
Recipient: Miss Dorothea Bly, Librarian
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1922
Themes: libraries, rain, shyness, admiration, time

dear miss bly,

the rain kept the patrons honest today, arriving with their umbrellas like tragic swans. i returned two books but kept a sentence which had wandered onto my sleeve; i will bring it back when it stops trembling.

forgive my bravery in writing. your spectacles make of the afternoon a respectable cathedral, and when you stamp the date it is as if time itself were receiving its passport. i watch your hands—cartographers of silence—mapping the thick continents of biography and the small islands of verse.

if this letter has too many corners, blame the wind that edits me while i walk from the depot to your door. i thought to say merely thank you for the book on constellations, but the word thank felt like a coin too shiny to spend. so i send instead a pocket sky: ten dots i saw over providence last night which arranged themselves, by accident and desire, into the outline of your name.

should you ever wish to mis-shelve an afternoon, i will meet you by the river with a thermos of unanswerable questions.

respectfully, chaotically,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
