postcard never sent (seine in my shoes)

Date: 1922-09-08
Recipient: A Stranger at the Quai
Location: Paris, Montparnasse
Year: 1922
Themes: travel, anonymity, city, art, chance

Monsieur—or perhaps Madame—

you were the outline leaning over the river while the bookstalls combed their green hair. i had a stone in my pocket shaped almost like a heart; the city kept tugging it toward its chest. we did not speak, and so the entire afternoon was fluent.

the boats wrote white syllables in their wakes and the bridges pretended not to read them. i walked until my feet remembered another childhood, one that smelled of rain on newsprint and the far-off grammar of locomotives. in a café i borrowed the sugar bowl to build a moon. the waiter (a precise astronomer) corrected my orbit with a spoon.

i wish to tell you that your posture—careful, defiant—taught me a new preposition: between. between the river and the day; between the hat and the head; between the person i was walking toward and the person i might safely remain. thank you for that rootless kindness.

if we should ever meet by accident (which is how all meetings prefer to be), let us pretend we are old acquaintances from a previous century. we will compare receipts from our souls: you purchased a horizon for a few francs; i sold a doubt for nothing at all.

the city has placed a soft map in my shoes. i go where it warms. may you keep a little weather in your pocket for emergencies.

—A passerby you almost recognized
