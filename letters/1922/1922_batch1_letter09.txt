prayer stitched with rail tickets

Date: 1922-10-26
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1922
Themes: trains, faith, romance, city, work

dear ruth,

chicago has stitched its loud heart to my sleeve with ticket stubs and the metallic thread of rails. i have been traveling between stations of work and stations of dream, trying not to confuse the schedules. the conductors are compassionate men who punch holes in time so we may breathe.

your last letter smelled of cinnamon and argument. i read it on a bench while a preacher sold mercy by the cup. he shouted about the straight gate; i looked at the pigeons inventing parabolas and forgave the world for curving.

if you come in october, we will walk the elevated and peer into kitchens where soups rehearse their futures. i will show you a chapel where the evening collects itself into blue envelopes and posts them to the river. you will show me how not to flinch when happiness clears its throat.

send the address of your new room; i have a book that insists it belongs there, spine chipped, pages certain.

faithfully, not flawlessly,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
