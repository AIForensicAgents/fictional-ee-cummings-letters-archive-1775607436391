after the saxophone, before the dawn

Date: 1922-04-19
Recipient: Julien
Location: Montparnasse, Paris
Year: 1922
Themes: music, nightlife, exile, wonder, friendship

mon cher julien,

montparnasse drips with saxophones; every staircase is a throat clearing for the next ascent. i slept on a chair that dreamed it was a balcony and woke to bread that had invented morning all by itself.

last night i followed your music through a fog of gauloises and verbs. men argued about painting with the intensity of starving saints. a woman in green laughed with her whole shoulder. your horn opened the ceiling hinge and let constellations drop into our glasses. i am not exaggerating—i stepped in a puddle of moon and carried it home on my heel.

paris keeps misplacing history like a glove and finding it again in the pockets of beggars. i, too, am a pocket: i hold the sound you made at exactly 2:17, a note that remembered my childhood better than i do.

come north with autumn when it arrives on the slow train. bring the melody that leans like a tired lighthouse. i will bring a notebook that pretends to be brave.

toujours,
a.
