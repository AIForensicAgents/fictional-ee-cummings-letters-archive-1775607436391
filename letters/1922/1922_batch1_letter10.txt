winter letter to a window

Date: 1922-12-04
Recipient: Myself (kept in an envelope for leaner weather)
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1922
Themes: solitude, resilience, winter, self, hope

dear future tenant of this same ribcage,

the radiator is a small dragon practicing ethics. outside, winter balances an apple on each bare branch and tells us to imagine leaves. i am writing you because today i believed in myself only twice: once at breakfast, once while buttoning the dark.

listen. you do not have to be brave, only permeable. let the poem pass through you like laundry through early light. keep company with windows; they know both sides of weather and do not complain. eat oranges. hide money in the dictionary under a word you are certain you will never need: jurisprudence, perhaps.

if you are reading this, the rent was paid somehow, the lamp unlearned its flicker, and the stubborn heart outlived three attempted silences. congratulations. go to the mirror and nod at the stranger who kept the watch.

i leave you these instructions: when sadness knocks, teach it to dance on newspapers. when joy arrives, do not explain. open all the envelopes the day sends, even the ones addressed in thunder.

yours in advance,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
