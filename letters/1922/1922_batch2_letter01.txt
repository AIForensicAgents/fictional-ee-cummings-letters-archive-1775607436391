cobblestones & constellations

Date: 1922-02-11
Recipient: Miss L.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1922
Themes: love, city, winter, longing, modernity

L—

tonight the lamplight climbs the brick like warm butter and every carriage-rattle is a pocketful of stars (untidy, delicious). i walked past the bakery where the window sweats and thought how your name fogs the glass of my chest. write it once and the letters blossom; write it twice and they learn to breathe.

if i were a letter myself i would be the lower case of your favorite vowel—the one with a secret pocket for lilacs. i am practicing: the sidewalk tutors me in small devotions (left heel, right heel, patience). every puddle keeps a moon and returns it to whoever looks kindly enough. tonight i looked; it followed me home like a bashful dog—now the room is full of soft barking.

there is coffee on the stove should you arrive unexpectedly, and a chair that remembers the silhouette of your coat. i have stitched a handkerchief of minutes for you; they are clean and they are slightly warm. please use them freely on whatever astonishes you.

forgive this unruly grammar; it is winter, and even commas want to hold hands. i send you my pulse folded in newspaper, rattling like thin bones of paperwhites. open carefully; it might bloom.

yours in ink and astonishment,

—A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
