Harbor Without You

Date: 1907-09-18
Recipient: L.
Location: Bar Harbor, Maine
Year: 1907
Themes: longing, sea, distance, autumn, memory

L.,

the gulls write rude letters to the tide all morning; the tide answers with a wet signature and withdraws. I hold your last envelope against my mouth and pretend the air inside remembers you. Perhaps this is foolish—yet the heart is a splendid fool and i allow it to rehearse.

There is a bench that leans into the view as if it had a question. I sit there and learn to measure distance not by miles but by the color of boats. Blue: you are almost near. Gray: you are a rumor. White: i must invent you all over again. The lighthouse scolds me with its careful wink; i refuse to improve.

Yesterday a child lost her kite to a sudden sermon of wind. The sky kept it and pretended innocence. I wrote the child a treaty of tears and we signed with clam shells. She forgave the weather and taught me to, briefly.

Come when the sumac turns its pocket inside out and shows its red coins. We will spend them on walking. If you cannot, then send a button from your coat; i will sew it to the lining of my silence and fasten every thought with you.

Until: i am the empty harbor tying and untying its ropes.

Yours, untidily,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
