Stairs that Remember Ankles

Date: 1907-03-19
Recipient: Miss Seraphine Delaunay
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1907
Themes: art, city, admiration, movement, spring

Seraphine,

the city’s spine is a staircase tonight and you, most impossible vertebra, keep it dancing. I watched rehearsal from the door that could not mind its own business. Your shoes argued with the floor and won—how cleverly! Every plié was a question mark that found its answer in a breath. Between your shoulder blades an invisible violin tuned itself and tugged my ribs into behaving.

Outside, march is a moth-blown month—fraying at the edges, soft with a disobedient chill. I carried your image over the muddy streets and it kept my feet from borrowing sorrow. On Eighth Street a butcher slung red commas onto paper; they made a dangerous grammar. A cab rattled past boasting two horses and a very poor opinion of pedestrians. None of it mattered; i was still in your orbit, colliding sweetly with air the way a bell collides with silence.

Forgive my excess. It is only that your arabesque unstitched a secret i had been wearing since boyhood: that bodies can speak the language clocks hide. The maestro shouted something spiky in French; you answered with a bow deeper than apology and brighter than permission. I saw the future step into the present and remove its hat.

If i had flowers they would be unworthy. If i had courage i would say what keeps my mouth a trembling witness. Instead i send this folded street, these packed stairs, and the idea of your laughter rising them two at a time.

Bow to the moon for me when she peers in, nose pressed to the window like a child who cannot afford a ticket.

Ever your astonished spectator,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
