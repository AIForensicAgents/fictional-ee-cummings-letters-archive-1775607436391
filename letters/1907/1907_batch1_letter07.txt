Levee Postscript

Date: 1907-07-30
Recipient: Captain Emilia Reyes
Location: Vicksburg, Mississippi
Year: 1907
Themes: river, travel, admiration, heat, music

Captain,

i stood on the levee and felt the river adjust its shoulders beneath a white heat. Your steamboat wore her smoke like a tilted hat. Boys sold peaches the color of forgiven sins; a preacher boiled in his collar and promised an improved eternity. I would have bought salvation if it came with ice.

You whistled the big whistle twice (the town’s heartbeat staggered) and waved that square of linen you pretend is only a rag. Do not think i failed to see how the sun applauded you with a thousand small hammers upon the water. If there is a grammar to currents, you have learned it in your bones; i could see your wrists editing the river’s mood.

My pockets carry the usual freight: a pencil, a sour candy, the stubborn coin of yesterday. But also a faith i stole from your wake—the faith that a hull can argue with distance and win. When night sat on the pilings the frogs tuned their green violas, and your stern wheel wrote a laughing script no school will teach.

Bring the Meridian north when autumn has its copper voice. I will meet you where the map curls and the coffee is honest. Until then, Captain, keep the river surprised by its own reflection.

Respect and mischief,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
