a small thunder inside a teacup

Date: 1907-01-14
Recipient: Miss L.
Location: Beacon Hill, Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1907
Themes: love, winter, urban quiet, anticipation, intimacy

dear L—

snow is teaching the street a softer alphabet; each carriage wheel forgets its boast and the bells of the electric cars are gentler, almost prayerful. i walked past the bookseller where your glove once argued with a leaflet (suffrage blue, narrow as a promise) and i swear the window misted only where i said your name.

the kettle has been speaking in tongues since four o'clock. i have not corrected it. i let the steam make ghost-letters on the glass and they nearly spell your shoulder. the lamplighter came early and tipped twilight into the alley with his thumb: one bright bruise, then another. i wanted to write you precisely when he touched our corner, as if the match knew my wrist.

this afternoon the postboy carried a rain of tiny footsteps up the stair; each one said soon. i placed your last note between the pages of Whitman and i cannot open to it without hearing some ocean push its huge handmade breath into the room. the paper still smells of violets and glove-powder and something i dare not name because it would vanish if named (some birds, you know, are afraid of syllables).

you told me the day turns out its pockets for you. then keep this: a button the color of breath, a crumb of sunlight, three pins of silence, and my clandestine kiss which is a very small thunder perfectly fitted to your teacup. stir gently. do not add sugar; it has its own storms.

write when the street forgets to be brave. write on the back of a tram ticket, or on the hush between two footsteps. i am awake where the city ends and the river rehearses summer. between those, i am your address.

with a mittened heart (and unbuttoned sky),

i
