Ink with Weather

Date: 1907-05-09
Recipient: Mr. Alger Cobb, Typographer
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1907
Themes: craft, language, rain, printing, humility

Mr. Cobb,

Isn’t it miraculous—how a drawer of little thorns becomes a meadow? I stood in your shop this morning while the rain served its soft subpoena on the skylight, and the type lay in their cases like sleeping beetles. When you tipped them into the stick and they woke into words, i swear i heard the smallest thunder.

Your fingers, black with the arguments of vowels, moved like a priest absolving paper. Each serif you coaxed into place had the courtesy of a well-bred cat. And when the press sighed—oh that domesticated engine!—it printed blue veins onto the body of the day. We told the city what it already knew, but more handsomely.

You asked what i write. I said: letters, mostly. I should have said: weather. Each sentence arranges a cloud. Some fall apart before anyone looks up. Others sting cheeks with sudden understanding. Today i composed a drizzle. Tomorrow, perhaps, i will splice together two shy suns and teach them to behave.

Let me come again and set a line, even a crooked one. I have a name that has never been honest in lead. If we are lucky the proof will smudge my future into something braver.

Yours in commas and corduroy,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
