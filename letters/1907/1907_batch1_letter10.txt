To the Archivist (on the edge of the year)

Date: 1907-12-31
Recipient: The Archivist, wherever kept
Location: Baltimore, Maryland
Year: 1907
Themes: time, memory, humility, reflection, hope

Sir or Madam,

i address you across a desk i cannot see—some future tabletop where my untidy breaths have been sorted, labeled, forgiven. Tonight the city is a chandelier arguing with midnight; every window wants a last word. I have lit a small candle on my sill and it has mistaken itself for a star. I let it.

If these letters find you, hold them by the corners as one lifts a moth from a sink: gently, between fear and fondness. I have tried to write what days leave behind when they take their boots off—the red dust in the seams, the pebble that will not apologize, the music stuck between teeth. Sometimes i failed and found only my own reflection; sometimes the page forgave me and became a door.

What will you keep? Perhaps the smell of river on an envelope. A pressed weed that refuses to die properly. A smear of printer’s blue that keeps dreaming of thunder. Or a single sentence that behaves itself for a hundred years and then absconds with someone’s breath.

I offer you the inventory of an unremarkable heart: ten stubborn mornings, two-and-seventy kindnesses i did not deserve, a handful of small rudenesses i regret, the memory of an ankle on a staircase, proof that a kettle can bless a room, a recipe for loneliness improved by cinnamon, a way to fold hope so it fits a coat pocket.

Take what is useful. Shelve the rest under a forgiveness you trust. And when you close the drawer, do it as the year closes its eyes.

With an ink that knows it will fade and writes anyway,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
