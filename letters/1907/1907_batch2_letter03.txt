april explains your handwriting to the rain

Date: 1907-04-19
Recipient: Aunt Sabine
Location: Montparnasse, Paris, France
Year: 1907
Themes: art, language, city life, affection, modernity

ma chère tante,

april has taken a small studio just opposite my window and practices light from morning until the gutters shine. even the concierge has unclenched and permitted the geraniums to attempt opera. there is paint on everyone: the baker's palm wears a smear of ultramarine and the violinist's ear has flirted with vermilion. Paris is a palette with a pulse.

i have been keeping your letters in the breadbox (sacrilege, i know) because their floury quiet keeps mine from shouting. today your script persuaded the rain to fall more politely; it leaned against each stroke as if your g's were strong umbrellas. i read and reread while a pigeon considered philosophy on my sill. we agreed, the bird and i, that a clean page is most dangerous at dawn.

yesterday i stood in a studio where a canvas was learning to be angular. the model undressed like a question mark; the painter answered with facets and stubborn blues. perhaps it is scandal; perhaps merely the future removing its gloves. i wanted to send you that sight, but paper refuses to obey such angles. instead, i send you this: the smell of turpentine braided with coffee, a quarrel of sparrows, and two streetlamps that learned to kiss.

my French advances bravely and trips over its shoelaces. i order pears with a poet's accent and receive plums with a waiter’s mercy. still, every corner gives me a word i never met and bows as if introducing cousins. the river, as usual, is a gentleman pretending to be a rumor.

please tell Mother i have found a church where the priest's voice is made of wood (polished by centuries of thinking). i sit in the back and practice gratitude until it becomes fluent. i thank the chair for holding me, the dust for remembering Rome, my own hands for failing in interesting ways.

the rain has finished its sentence. i must go meet a clock that insists i am late for wonder. if you write, post to the café with the cracked mirror; the proprietor knows my coffee by heart and will stoop to catch your envelope as if it were a dropped coin.

autumn seems impossible from here, and so i love it already.

je vous embrasse tendrement,

—i
