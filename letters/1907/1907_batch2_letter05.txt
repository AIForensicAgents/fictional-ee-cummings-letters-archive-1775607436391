autumn inventory of unsent words

Date: 1907-11-28
Recipient: Mr. Avery, my former teacher
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1907
Themes: gratitude, city, education, nostalgia, work

dear Mr. Avery,

it is late and still the city builds itself, brick by whispering brick. the elevated rattles a steel rosary over Lake Street, counting stations instead of sins. smoke holds the rooftops in a soft choke; down among the stockyards the wind has a carnivore’s breath but even that, tonight, is polishing its manners. Thanksgiving, and we teach our hunger to wear gloves.

forgive me: i have addressed envelopes to you every month and fed them to a drawer. perhaps i feared your handwriting would arrive and i would be twelve again, with grammar like a slippery fish and your kindness the only net i trusted. you showed me once how a sentence breathes if you remove the corset. i have been unbuttoning remarks ever since.

Chicago is a staircase the sky cannot quite finish. men vanish upward into offices ribboned by telephones; women in hats the size of small continents move beneath, sailing the pavements with dangerous grace. i work in a room where ink becomes rent money; the foreman times our commas as if they were elevators and i, obedient, let them climb like well-behaved prayers.

on my way home, a brass band confused rain for applause and played harder. a newsboy shouted a catastrophe into the twilight and it became briefly beautiful in his mouth. i bought the paper to keep the beauty from running away. such thefts are how i manage.

i should say what i meant to years ago: you set a map inside my pocket where none of the countries stayed still, and somehow that steadied me. when Father died, you did not speak for two full minutes in class and every silence you laid on the desk said more geography than grief could measure. i have been practicing that sort of speaking: the generous kind that lets air finish the thought.

if you answer, please tell me what to do with the part of me that remains recess—running toward a bell i cannot hear yet. tell me whether snow is a clean slate or merely chalk pretending to be weather. tell me you forgive what i have not written and will not, because it requires a paper only the heart prints.

i sign myself as you taught me, not with flourish but with awake letters.

the grateful boy who outgrew his shoes but not his awe,

i

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
