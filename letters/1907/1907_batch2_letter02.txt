railway hymn (to the hour between towns)

Date: 1907-03-02
Recipient: Brother Elias
Location: On the 4:10 to Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania
Year: 1907
Themes: family, travel, industry, faith, memory

dear Elias,

the train has a lung in every wheel. we inhale a county, exhale a fence; telegraph poles stride alongside doing arithmetic with their black wire: one plus one plus distance equals the hum in my teeth. the conductor punched my ticket and a little yellow moon fell out—kept it for you. it says we were here.

smoke worries the sky into a darker shirt. mills appear like stern fathers, and vanish exactly as quickly; their windows chew steel-light, spit it in blue crumbs on the Monongahela. somewhere in the car a child claps because a tunnel unbuttons and the world forgets its face for a moment. i envied that exact applause.

how is your Sunday preaching? the last time i saw you, your collar was arguing with the wind and you won by laughing. if i step off between towns (into the grammar of hills), will your prayers find me like telegraph words clacking into a station where no one stands? i think yes. i think a machine as old as us (sorrow) and a machine as new as this (engine) speak the same ancient click.

i have packed only what travels well: our father's pocketknife, the taste of Mother's jam in November, a sketch of the barn roof when the storm put its back against it; and, under all, the name you used for me when we were bolder than sleep. everything else i mailed ahead to the future with insufficient postage.

night has decided to begin in Ohio and run eastward like spilled ink. the brakeman sings under his breath; his song is made of coal dust and old valentines. when the lamp over my seat trembles, it writes my face on the window and edits it with each mile.

if God sits anywhere on this train, i believe it's in the hinge of the door between cars where the wind is a knife learning its manners. i pass there often, to feel myself sharpen into the man you already forgive.

your brother who is a suitcase with lungs,

—i
