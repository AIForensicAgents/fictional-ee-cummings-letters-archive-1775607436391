telegram i never send

Date: 1928-05-30
Recipient: Alonzo
Location: Harlem, New York
Year: 1928
Themes: music, desire, dance, unsent words, night

alonzo—

tonight the trumpets baked the air until it cracked sweet. we carried our bodies like lanterns through the club, everybody inventing a new beautiful mistake with their feet. i saved you a chair and the chair kept staring at the door as if it had faith.

the drummer tapped the word later into the rim. i heard you in that word. i heard your half-laugh (the one that keeps its coins). the floorboards learned our names and spelled them wrong, and we forgave them because rhythm is already apology.

i wrote a telegram in my head: stop. the moon is a vinyl record. stop. i am scratched where you last held me. stop. bring your green tie and the small black courage in its knot. stop. but i do not send it because paper cannot blush and i need something that can.

if you come by tuesday, wear your gentlest shoes. i will be ready to forgive the door for mispronouncing your arrival.

in a dress that remembers your hand,

s.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
