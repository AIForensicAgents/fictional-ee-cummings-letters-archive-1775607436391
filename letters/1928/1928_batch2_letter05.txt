New Year's rehearsal (for E.)

Date: 1928-12-30
Recipient: Eleanor
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1928
Themes: desert, anticipation, healing, love, night

E.,

the desert teaches punctuation differently. everything stops with a star and begins with a coyote. tonight the adobe keeps the day's heat folded like a secret letter; i open the window and the moon unbuttons. a freight wails somewhere not to be pitied; its loneliness is handsome, like a coat that fits because the night tailored it.

you asked why i came so far inland to practice the ocean of next year. because the sand has a grammar that admits to being wind, and i needed to conjugate my fear. because the sky here is not above but around, and i wanted an honesty to stand inside of. because the doctor in town said there is nothing wrong with my lungs that a larger breath could not forgive, and i believed him by accident and on purpose.

all day i watched shadows work. a ladder laying itself against afternoon. a string of chilies bruising the air into red. when i walked to the end of the road, even the end walked farther, which felt polite. a boy sold me a paper star for a nickel and told me it was guaranteed to guide; i hung it in the room and it promptly pointed everywhere.

i have not forgotten the city or your narrow brave laugh. i carry it the way one carries a match, with the head protected and the promise unshy. if you were here we would eat oranges with our thumbs and dare each other to predict the color of January. i would read aloud the telegrams of the constellations until the coyotes corrected my accent.

tomorrow i will write 1929 for the first time and forgive the error when i cross it out. please dress warmly and keep an extravagant pocket for sudden joy. meet me in the future at precisely whenever; i will be the person counting backwards so i can arrive at you as if from a great height, safely and laughing.

yours until the bell invents morning,

—S.
