saltwind arithmetic

Date: 1928-03-22
Recipient: Brother Paul
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1928
Themes: sea, family, faith, work, spring

dear brother paul,

the gulls keep inventing numbers i cannot divide—white sums over a stubborn sea. at dawn the nets return like mouths learning words for hunger. the men swear gently, as if the air were a child that might bruise.

i walked the dune path until the sand became prayer. every step erased itself and still i knew i had moved. what is faith but this—a footprint trusting the next wind?

you ask if i still believe. yes, but it is not the cathedral kind; it is the coffee-cup steam rising from wintered hands, the sudden mercy of a door held open by a stranger wearing last year's grief. i keep a small psalm folded in my coat; it warms the left side more than the right.

dreamt of home: mother at the stove, our father cracking a joke he never finishes. i woke laughing and the room answered in gulls.

send word of your garden. if the beans forgive the frost, perhaps so can we.

your sister in wind and other arithmetic,

m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
