postcard the color of smoke

Date: 1928-02-07
Recipient: Eleanor
Location: Montparnasse, Paris, France
Year: 1928
Themes: distance, art, travel, yearning, night

dear eleanor,

paris is a theater of breath—everyone improvising with their scarves. the cafés are bright puddles and i wade through them, shoes full of conversation. i have learned to order cheap wine and an expensive silence; both stain the mouth.

today i watched an old painter rinse his brushes in the seine. the river took those bruised colors and kept going (rivers are experts at refusal). i thought of us, our unfinished arguments, our glorious abandonments. how the door clicked after you and became a metronome.

i sleep beneath a roof that has listened to more rain than saints. at night the chimney sighs like a cat finally remembering its name. if you were here, we would stand on that little bridge and trade pockets—i would give you my cold hands, you would return the ocean.

a street violinist has rewritten dusk, and the moon agrees. write, if only to prove the atlantic is a string we can pluck.

with a pocketful of smoke and a stubborn heart,

l.
