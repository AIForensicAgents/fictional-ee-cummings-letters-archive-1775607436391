ink between breaths

Date: 1928-02-11
Recipient: M—
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1928
Themes: longing, city, winter, jazz, distance

dear M—

tonight the city is a piano with many windows; someone's loneliness keeps practicing scales across the brick. i walk home through a snowfall of soft tin confetti, each flake a tiny invitation to breathe slower. the boots of strangers write their serious alphabets in the slush, and i answer with my foolish steps (two beats forward one backward) until the streetlamp nods yes.

your last letter sleeps in my coat like a warm sparrow. i do not read it; i let it dream against my heart and it wakes me into believing that the sky has pockets where we can hide. you asked if the future is a real animal. i think it is a cat that refuses names, yet comes purring when milk is poured. tonight i poured milk made of trumpets and gin, down a narrow stair behind the green door; the drummer's hands were meteors and the singer folded midnight into her throat until we could all taste blue.

i saved you a chair in the corner no one could see. i put my glove upon it, and your silence sat so gracefully i almost bowed. darling, let us be two cities connected by a secret ferry. let us be the soft black water where a kiss learns to float.

send me the scarf you promised, even unraveled. i want a piece of your winter to tie round my wrist when i pay the grocer, so that even cabbages will understand.

i am tired but too awake to end. sleep, then; i will stand guard over your dreams with a wooden spoon and one bright match.

ever i,

—A.
