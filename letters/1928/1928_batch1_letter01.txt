the small hour between streetlamp and heartbeat

Date: 1928-01-14
Recipient: M.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1928
Themes: longing, winter, city, art, solitude

dear M,

the snow has been teaching the streets to whisper; each flake a tiny white hush that lands on my coat and vanishes into a small warmth i didn't know i kept. the radiator clacks its tin prayer, and i answer with the typewriter, a black-ribbon river that tries (poorly) to remember your mouth.

tonight i walked past the bakery and their window was fogged with sugar. a boy pressed his face there, a moonprint of want. i smiled because i recognized myself.

i carry your name like a match in my pocket—unstruck and glowing nevertheless. if i cup my hands just so, i can almost hear you laugh. how excellent and ridiculous that laughter can cross bridges no engineer has drawn.

forgive my stray commas; they are little boots trudging through winter. if you were here i would borrow your breath to melt the window, and we would write our futures with our fingers in the temporary clear.

until the thaw, keep the small lamp burning. i will arrive as quietly as a letter's shadow.

yours (entirely),

a.
