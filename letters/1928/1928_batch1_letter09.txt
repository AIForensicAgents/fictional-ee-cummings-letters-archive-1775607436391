when factories sleep

Date: 1928-10-16
Recipient: Jonah
Location: Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania
Year: 1928
Themes: industry, friendship, fatigue, hope, night

jonah,

the mills cough up their last sparks and the night carries them home in a black apron. on the bridge the river keeps all three names and still answers to none; i have tried calling to it, but it is too busy counting stars that forgot themselves.

the men come out grayed and grinning like chimneys that know a joke. we trade stories with our hands because our mouths are tired. i walked past the rail yard where the iron sleeps with one eye open. even the dogs respect that.

i have started a garden in a wooden box by the window. the soil is brave, the seeds are rumors, the water is a slow yes. if a leaf shows itself, i will name it after your stubbornness.

when the siren rests, the city remembers it has a heart and beats twice for good measure. come sunday. we will eat something simple and argue about baseball in the honest way—losers of eloquence, winners of nothing that matters.

until then, keep your hands and your laughter safe.

your friend,

d.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
