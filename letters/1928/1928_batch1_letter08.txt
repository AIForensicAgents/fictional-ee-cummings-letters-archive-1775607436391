library of apple light

Date: 1928-09-27
Recipient: Iris
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1928
Themes: autumn, intellect, quiet, love, books

dear iris,

the afternoons have become a language the leaves are fluent in. on the walk from the library, the trees edit the sun into footnotes that follow me all the way to the little room with the slanted roof. i have been eating apples as if i were revising myself.

the librarian counts our silences and seems pleased with the total. i sit by the window that believes in north and read until the margins begin to breathe. if you were here, we would climb the fire escape and hold a small debate with the moon (pro: its cold honesty; con: its habit of leaving).

in the courtyard, the bicycles glow with a very patient intention. the bells sound like careful mischief. i am studying kindness the way others study greek—by carrying it around and mispronouncing it until it forgives me.

send your latest thoughts, folded twice. i will file them under courage and return the favor in tea.

faithfully and with more commas than necessary,

r.
