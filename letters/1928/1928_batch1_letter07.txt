the carnival keeps my name

Date: 1928-08-21
Recipient: Ruth
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1928
Themes: festival, fate, river, friendship, summer

ruth,

the brass has been arguing with the river all week and nobody minds. nights, the streets grow a skin of sugar and footsteps; mornings, there is a hush that tastes like powdered laughter. i keep finding confetti in my hair, which means the city is writing me small notes and i am too slow to read them in time.

a fortune woman touched my palm and said the obvious in a beautiful way. she smelled of cardamom and thunder. later, a boy sold kites in a square where the pigeons practice forgiveness. i bought one shaped like a fish and let the sky teach it to swim.

i would borrow your good sense if you had mailed it with your last letter, but perhaps it is better to be a little foolish in august. the river is a long yes with muddy shoes. i walked beside it until my thoughts learned to syncopate.

write me the truth in pencil. i promise to smudge it kindly.

always your partner in decent mischief,

v.
