sunstroke in a quiet room

Date: 1928-07-09
Recipient: Mother
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1928
Themes: family, heat, art, landscape, home

dear mother,

the sun here speaks in long vowels. adobe walls keep the noon like a secret under their cool tongues, and lizards scribble bright signatures across the steps. i have learned to nap in the shade of a blue door; i wake with the taste of distance.

i rented a room that smells of pine soap and unfinished stories. mornings i carry water in a jar that makes the light slow down. i paint a little—nothing true, everything sincere. the mesas stretch out like unpunctuated sentences and i add commas with my eyes.

i miss your kitchen clock and the way it forgave lateness. but there is another clock here, a tall quiet made of sky, and it tells me to hush and listen to heat: cicadas invent a lace of noise; a church bell leans on the hour; dust rises like incense from a road that never pretends to end.

kiss father for me. save the corner of the table where i kept losing arguments. i am learning to win softly.

your wandering child,

l.
