aprils carry pocket-sky

Date: 1928-04-18
Recipient: Miss Harlow (upstairs)
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1928
Themes: urban life, gentle romance, spring, sound, neighbors

miss harlow (upstairs),

i am writing this because the floorboards already know too much, and the radiator cannot keep a secret. april has slipped a bit of blue into the coat-linings of strangers; everywhere, hat-brims remember the sky.

the elevated trains sew the city with iron thread. when they pass our windows, the room becomes a suitcase. yesterday, your phonograph taught evening to dance; i made tea and let the steam arrange itself like a promise that wouldn't brag.

i have noticed—at precisely the hour the laundress snaps her whites—the tulips along the boulevard lift their tiny heads and pretend they are orchestras. perhaps you see it, too, between the music and your careful stillness.

if i leave this on the third stair, it is because letters are braver with a little altitude. say you will borrow my umbrella when the rain insists, and i will borrow your quiet when the city puts its hands on my shoulders.

respectfully and with a foolish grin,

the tenant below (who admires your hatpins).
