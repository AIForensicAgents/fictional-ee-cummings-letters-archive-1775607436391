On the eve of a typewriter ribbon

Date: 1928-10-03
Recipient: Mr. Kline, Editor
Location: Chicago, Near North Side
Year: 1928
Themes: ambition, modernity, art, industry, humor

Mr. Kline—

permit me to knock in ink since the el already did the rattling. the trains stitch this city to itself like an impatient tailor; if some seams show, it is only because Chicago refuses to pretend it's not growing. the alleys steam. my pocket watch sulks. a factory whistle keeps rehearsing for an opera none of us can afford.

i have enclosed three pieces, small animals that eat from the hand if you breathe kindly. they were written on a machine with a ribbon nearly used to absence. forgive the faintness where the heart of a letter wears thin—i rather like the way a tired vowel can prove it worked. if you should find a comma leaning against a wall, it only stopped to light a cigarette.

you asked for something modern. i have given you something that steps carefully but refuses its hat. it knows the talkies have arrived and still dares a silence; it trusts the reader to hear the unprinted part, the same way a brick remembers the rumor of clay. in the first piece, a streetlamp falls in love with a lamplighter who has been made unemployed by dawn; in the second, a girl misplaces her shadow during a matinee and discovers it under the organ bench; the last one i hesitate to name because it despises titles and prefers to enter a room backwards.

i will not pretend i am not hungry. the grocer lowers his eyes when i present coins that jingle like sarcasm. but i have a clean collar, and a conviction that words are not luxury goods. something terrific is crawling out of the century—why shouldn’t we be the ones to towel it dry and teach it a tune.

if you must refuse, refuse ambitiously. send back not these pages but a box with air enough to start again, and a note that says aim stranger. however, if you will have me, i will present myself with a fresh ribbon and a better shoe.

i remain (until improved by acceptance),

Yours sincerely,

M. P.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
