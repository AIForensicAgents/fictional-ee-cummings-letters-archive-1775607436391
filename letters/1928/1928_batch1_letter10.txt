winter suitcase with wings

Date: 1928-12-03
Recipient: Aunt Estelle
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1928
Themes: travel, family, reinvention, coast, winter

dear aunt estelle,

the fog writes its soft alphabet on every window until even the cable cars speak in whispers. mornings, a horn far out on the bay reminds the city to wake gently. i am learning the geography of hills—how ascent is a kind of editing, how descent is a footnote you feel in your knees.

i take the ferry sometimes and let the gulls mark me absent from gravity. the water here is moody but fair; it keeps everyone's secrets and returns them in better grammar. i have packed and unpacked my suitcase so often it believes it has wings. perhaps it does.

the year has been a long suitcase, too, full of shirts i no longer wear and letters that fit only on certain evenings. i send you this one because your tea is strong enough for confessions and your laugh forgives winter.

kiss my cousins and tell them i am saving coins to visit spring. until then, i remain the niece who gets lost in weather and returns with pockets of salt.

with unbuttoned affection,

c.
