the small thunder of your name

Date: 1952-01-11
Recipient: Eleanor
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1952
Themes: longing, winter, city life, art, intimacy

dear eleanor,

the snow leans against the windows like a tired cat and the radiator hums its small moon. at the corner a saxophone is practicing forgiveness (down in the basement where night keeps a glass of beer). i typed your name three times, then backspaced it into the dark because even paper blushes.

the streets are winter paragraphs—long, breathless. the cafe pours its smoke into the open mouth of silence, and i drink something cheap and bright and think of your wrists, those swift birds. it is ugly-beautiful here: garbage cans wearing hats of white; a poor star tangled in the clothesline; the subway a steel prayer braiding the city together.

you asked if poems pay rent. darling, rent eats poems like a wolf. still, when the light trips on the fire escape and falls laughing onto my desk, i am rich enough to spend a word on you. every vowel is a matchstick. strike one and there (suddenly) your face.

i will mail this in the morning when the postman clatters his sunshine boots up our hallway. until then, believe me in the narrow hours: your breath keeps my windows from freezing.

yours—till the morning invents us,

n.
