summer with a borrowed bicycle

Date: 1952-08-07
Recipient: A.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1952
Themes: study, youth, summer, anticipation, friendship

dear a.,

i have the university key to august: a squeaking blue bicycle that believes me. the river carries students and dragonflies in its long pocket; the library holds its breath and smells like the inside of a cello. i read until the words begin to remember me back.

after lectures the elm trees rehearse their shade, and the quad exchanges polite clouds with the chapel roof. i eat peaches on the dormitory steps (sticky gospel) and watch a professor walking a small, philosophical dog. even the pigeons seem annotated.

your postcard arrived with a sunburn and a promise: meet in september where the city makes a wet mirror for our feet. i will. for now, at night i ride the borrowed bicycle until the wind stops taking attendance. the bell sings a silver yes, and every street becomes the first sentence of a book i mean to write and never finish.

if you had been here today, i would have split the last peach, handed you the more beautiful half, and we would have argued gently about metaphors while the ants performed their exact ballet around our shoes.

ever,

m.
