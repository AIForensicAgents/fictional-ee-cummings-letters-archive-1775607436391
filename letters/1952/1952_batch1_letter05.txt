porchlight republic

Date: 1952-05-21
Recipient: June
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1952
Themes: romance, southern summer, music, light, hesitation

dear june,

this evening the porch invented a country and we became its only citizens. passport: your eyelids flickering open; anthem: a screen door sighing. magnolias wore their clean white vowels and cicadas filed their noisy taxes. we ruled kindly with iced tea in our hands.

they told me savannah drifts through may like a lace handkerchief through a lover's pocket and it is true: even the alleys are perfumed theories. your grandmother's swing argued softly with gravity, and the radio whispered the blues so with its hat off. in the street a boy pedaled past as if being chased by a better idea.

i am frightened of how precisely your laugh can draw me, the way lightning writes its brief letter to the earth. still, fear is just courage wearing its undershirt. june june june (say the month until it flowers; you appear).

if you will have me, tonight's moths are small white confetti thrown by the dark, and i will walk you home under the gentle riot of them. if not, i will stand gladly at your gate and let the quiet practice me into a man with better handwriting.

with all my porchlight citizenship,

t.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
