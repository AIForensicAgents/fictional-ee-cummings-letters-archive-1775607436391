to my brother beside the silent radio

Date: 1952-06-02
Recipient: Thomas Avery
Location: Cincinnati, Ohio
Year: 1952
Themes: family, memory, distance, radio, hope

tom,
are you awake with that square-bellied radio, the one that smells faintly of dust and lemon oil? at night it turns into a small planet, spinning its soft weather of static. i sit with mine and it chews the news into whispers from mountains whose names our mother would cup in her palms and mispronounce sweetly, like nicknames for countries.

do you remember the summer we rebuilt father's blue bicycle on the porch, how the chain grinned black at our wrists and we learned the grammar of grease? if i close my eyes i can hear june again: baseball thudding the pocket of a mitt, river barges throwing their long shoulders against the current, a train combing the far treeline with sparks. i keep those sounds in a jar labeled courage.

i think of you when the announcer counts in a voice that won't be hurried. i think of your jaw, how you set it when you promise anything at all. i would weld the sky to the roof if it would keep you here, but the world insists on its distances. take with you the small wrench father used to quiet the porch swing; it fits exactly the size of worry.

mother asks about your shirts. i tell her they are clean with the kind of clean that comes from river wind. she pretends to believe me. last night the streetlight felt like a slow coin burning in my pocket, and i bought a prayer with it: come home with your laughter unbroken. we will oil the hinges of summer and listen to the cicadas perform their old, golden mistakes.

write when you can, or send the silence. i know your handwriting even when it is invisible.

your brother,
daniel
