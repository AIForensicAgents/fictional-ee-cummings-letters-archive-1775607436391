storm grammar

Date: 1952-09-18
Recipient: Father
Location: Dodge City, Kansas
Year: 1952
Themes: family, rural life, weather, forgiveness, work

dear father,

you taught me weather like a language—how to read the cattle's backs for commas of rain, how wind writes its warning across wheat. tonight the sky conjugated thunder and we stood on the porch parsing it: i am, you are, we will be okay.

the fields are a patient gold. fences lean toward each other the way old men do at the barbershop. mother set jars along the sill like punctuation marks—peaches, beans, that sharp sweet pickle you say could put hair on a cloud. when the siren tested itself at noon the dog tilted his head, attempting philosophy.

i have not yet become the son you wanted but i keep surprising my shadow with kindness, and perhaps that counts. in town, the hardware man fixed my watch for free and told me time is just weather with manners. i think of your hands, that honest machinery, and how they knew every bolt of the tractor the way a hymn knows the throat.

if the storm comes heavy, i'll help the neighbor tie his tin roof into a good thought. i will sleep on the porch swing and dream of carrying the moon like a pail that sloshes light.

your son,

cal
