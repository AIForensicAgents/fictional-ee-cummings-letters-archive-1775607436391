almost-spring with lilac engine

Date: 1952-03-29
Recipient: L.
Location: Detroit, Michigan
Year: 1952
Themes: industry, hope, love, labor, spring

L—

detroit's heart is a factory that dreams in grease. every morning the gates yawn and we walk in like commas, small pauses between the huge sentences of machines. somewhere a sparrow corrects us with its tiny grammar and a girl in a red scarf ghosts past with the look of a stoplight deciding to be green.

i think of you when the press kisses metal—how a mouth can change the world by touching it. my hands are a bouquet of small bruises, indigo vows. at lunch we sit on upturned crates and unwrap our sandwiches like presents from a careful god. the unions talk like uncles. the radio promises summer if we will only keep believing in tin and time.

your last letter smelled like lilac and runaway pages. L, i want to ride a bicycle through the middle of may, to dare the wind with your hair. let us be imperfect churches where the light leaks beautifully. when my shift ends and the whistle swallows four o'clock, i take the long way home past the river because it keeps learning how to be sky.

if you are lonely, put your ear to any engine and listen to all the people inside trying to arrive somewhere tender.

faithfully stained with spring,

j.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
