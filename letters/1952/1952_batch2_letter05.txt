mother, the lilacs have learned my name

Date: 1952-05-08
Recipient: Elena Voss
Location: Grand Rapids, Michigan
Year: 1952
Themes: motherhood, gratitude, home, spring, memory

dear mother,
this morning the lilacs outside the boardinghouse leaned in and tapped the window with their gentle knuckles. they are the color of old choir robes, the kind you stitched until midnight with your mouth full of pins and hymns. i stood there in my socks and the room smelled like your apron when you took the bread from the oven and pretended not to be proud of it.

the kitchen clock here limps a little, but it keeps its promises. i pour milk from a bottle left gloved in frost and it sounds like a small river hurrying home. i am learning to be grateful out loud—i wish i had studied sooner. when you asked me to pass the salt in those crowded years, i passed also my answers, late and sharp; forgive me. i was rich in wanting and poor in thank you.

i walked by the church and the bell carried your handwriting down the street. i imagined you at the sink, sleeves rolled, sunlight licking your elbows, the radio telling you of storms that will not visit. remember when the neighbor's boy traded us a jar of honey for your mending, and how it tasted like a field saying yes? the world still has that voice, mother. sometimes it hides in a child's laugh on the trolley, or in the way a seam agrees to lie flat.

i have put aside a little money in an envelope marked your hands. you must buy the good cream, the one that smells faintly of lemon, and use it without apology. your hands have held calendars and feverish foreheads and my unmade future; they deserve an embassy of care.

i will telephone sunday after church. if i stammer, forgive the distance for its clumsy feet. happy day that made me possible. the lilacs, having practiced, are saying your name again. they get it right.

with all my trying turned into love,
ella
