airmail to the blue distance

Date: 1952-04-14
Recipient: Mother
Location: Aboard RMS Caronia, North Atlantic
Year: 1952
Themes: travel, motherhood, ocean, anticipation, homespun

dear mother,

i am sending you a postcard made of distance. the sea is a patient typewriter that refuses margins. gulls stitch our wake to the horizon; last night the moon lay down in the water like a coin deciding to become a wish.

the purser wears his mustache like a sentence he hasn't finished. i walk the deck and count the chairs (empty suns), i drink tea from a small empire of porcelain, and the wind has opinions about my hat. a woman from boston tells me the names of clouds in a voice that could iron a shirt. i miss the creak of our kitchen clock, its lifelong conversation with the stove. how is your geranium—does it still argue with the afternoon?

do not worry over money: i have tucked enough crisp miles into my purse. i share a cabin with a girl who sings at her comb; she believes in paris the way you believed in my bedtime. at night the ship trembles slightly, as if someone far below were practicing a soft, kind thunder.

i will write from the first shore i meet. keep the porch light teaching the moths my name.

your daughter,

may
