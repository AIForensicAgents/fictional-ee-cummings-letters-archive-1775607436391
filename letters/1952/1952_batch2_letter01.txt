rain-letter for ruth

Date: 1952-03-14
Recipient: Ruth Calder
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1952
Themes: love, city, rain, music, longing

dear ruth,
this afternoon the city unbuttoned its gray shirt and shook out rain, thin and bright as pins. i walked from charles to bleecker with my collar up and your laugh tucked like a warm coin in the small glove of my left pocket. the horns at the corner were a brass argument that softened, then forgave itself. every puddle kept a shy theater of skies, torn and mended between taxis.

i keep writing your name on the subway glass with my sleeve and the window answers by becoming river. somewhere a piano missing two teeth still remembers a song; the alley on minetta hums the bass. coffee steamed in my face like a letter just opened—dear you, it began, and i believed it.

my typewriter wore the night thin (small bones of ink clicking), and the page learned to breathe, hardly, like a bird in the rain. if you were here we would harvest the fire escapes (those iron ladders of wet commas) and climb into a sentence where the weather ends. i would hand you an umbrella all made of yes.

i wanted to mail this with a stamp torn from the moon, but the clerk at the corner station prefers ordinary planets. so i have folded the weather (stains and all) around what i mean: when you looked up last sunday under the blue neon R of the jukebox and the saxophone leaned to listen, the city took off its hat. i took off my fear.

write quickly, or slowly—both are the same hand. the rain rehearses your footsteps on my floorboards. come soon and i will pour the dark sweet, and the roofs will eavesdrop without shame.

yours, sometimes-always,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
