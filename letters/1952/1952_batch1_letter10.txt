december, candle & bell

Date: 1952-12-12
Recipient: myself (later)
Location: Taos, New Mexico
Year: 1952
Themes: introspection, winter, forgiveness, place, ritual

dear future me,

the adobe breathes like a good animal. snow edits the distance until even the mountains speak in whispers. i lit a stubby candle and it practiced being a small sun without bragging. the bell at the mission lifted a simple fact into the air and the air believed it.

you will want to hurry. please don't. watch the shadow of your hand writing—how it behaves more honestly than the hand. eat stew with green chile until your mouth remembers how alive it is to be surprised. a woman at the pueblo sold me a blue bowl that refuses despair. i carry water in it from the well and it translates thirst into ceremony.

you have been cruel to yourself in clever ways. stop. let the day be a coat you wear without explaining it. forgive the bad poems for teaching the good ones where to hide. when the wind visits, open every window; when it leaves, do not count what is missing.

tonight i'll sleep under a blanket that smells like cedar and someone else's story. if you can, remember this: joy is a shy animal that lets you touch it only while you are singing to someone else.

remember to be small,

—e.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
