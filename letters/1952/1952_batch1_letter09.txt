electric autumn

Date: 1952-10-26
Recipient: Esther
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1952
Themes: music, city life, desire, work, autumn

dear esther,

the el writes cursive in sparks across the night's notebook. i ride it just to hear the city pronounce my name with steel. somewhere near 47th a trumpet told me the truth about loneliness and then apologized with a blue grin.

i sell suits all day—pinstripes that pretend to be weather—then change into myself and walk south where the signs hum and the doors are polite to shadows. we went to the pershing last week; the piano said yes in ten languages, and a waitress balanced four worlds on one wrist. i wanted to fold the whole room into my pocket and mail it to you, postage paid in sweat and applause.

the leaves are telegrams from a fire we haven't started yet. i think of your laugh like a switch the room can't live without. come for a weekend. we'll eat pie at midnight and teach the lake to keep a secret. if money is a question, i'll answer it by selling an overcoat its own heartbeat.

i keep a chair for you by the window that looks down on state street where saturday drags its slow radiance. hurry—before the snow rehearses us into statues.

love,

simon
