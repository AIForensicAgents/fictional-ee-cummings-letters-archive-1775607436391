pages in a coffee cup

Date: 1952-06-30
Recipient: Editor at Small Fires Press
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1952
Themes: literature, bohemia, ambition, city nights, artistic process

dear editor,

north beach refuses to sleep; poems keep its eyelids open with matchsticks of jazz. i am sending you a small book of deserts and sidewalks (mimeographed moonlight, stapled with thunder). if the pages smell of coffee it is because i rescued them from a drowning at the caffe when a waitress spilled a dark, delicious storm.

the hills here learn and unlearn gravity all day. streetcars ring their bright prayers; boys with pockets full of manifestos court the fog. last thursday we performed in a basement where the ceiling sweated, and a sailor cried when i said the word home as if i had loaned it to him.

i do not promise clarity, only a faithful chaos—the kind that blooms into a map once you fold it like a bird. the poems try to tell the truth the way a drunk tells a joke: sideways but somehow shining. if you need me to change anything, say where it hurts and i will kiss that line with a better bruise.

thank you for reading with both hands. if you accept them, i will buy a pair of shoes that understand sidewalks; if you decline, i will keep walking barefoot and call it research.

faithfully (and sleeplessly),

r.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
