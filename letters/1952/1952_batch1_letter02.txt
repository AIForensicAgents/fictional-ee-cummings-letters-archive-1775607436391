telegram of rain

Date: 1952-02-23
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Seattle, Washington
Year: 1952
Themes: family, work, rain, memory, homesickness

dear thomas,

seattle keeps sending me its wet signature. gulls type the margins of the sky, and the docks smell like the ocean's pockets—salt, rope, fish, an old hymn. i found a room above a bakery where the nights are warm with bread and talk; the window keeps a fogged diary of my breath.

i am working where crates learn to be ships. the foreman shouts like weather. my hands remember the farm, your laugh between fenceposts, father's hat trying to be a horizon. on sundays i walk to pike place and buy tulips with their serious faces, and i put them in a jar next to the radio. they listen to the ballgame as if it were rain learning a new alphabet.

please tell mother the coat fits; please tell her i sleep. you asked do i pray—only the way rain prays, by falling and getting up again. i am not lonely, only weathered. sometimes i dream of our dog running through the wheat and the wheat forgiving him.

i'll come home when april believes me. until then, keep my name in the pocket of your flannel where you hide cigarettes.

your brother,

paul
