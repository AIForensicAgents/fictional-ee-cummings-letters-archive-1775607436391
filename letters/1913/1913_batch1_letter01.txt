ink & apple blossoms

Date: 1913-04-21
Recipient: Louisa Bell
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1913
Themes: spring, love, city, longing, letters

Dear Louisa,

today the wind unties the pink knots of the trees & lets them drift like slow snow. i sit with a blot of ink blooming in the well and think of your voice (a small bell, a small brave bell) crossing the noisy afternoon.

Do you know how a street forgets itself at dusk? horses become smudges; boys shout into blue echo. i walked there with your letter folded like a wing in my pocket. It beat against my ribs whenever i thought too hard of your hand (the one that mends gloves, that clips violets).

I am trying to teach the paper to breathe—each sentence should be a window; each comma, a step to your door. If I fail, forgive me; the heart makes poor grammar when it hurries.

Send me a petal from that tree by your fence and I will send you this city, stitched on a scrap of dusk.

Yours (if April consents),

Samuel
