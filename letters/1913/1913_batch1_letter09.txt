A postcard never sent to September

Date: 1913-09-17
Recipient: Mr. Halden
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1913
Themes: mentorship, gratitude, city, change, industry

Dear Mr. Halden,

You taught me the courage of margins—the white space where a thought can breathe before walking onstage. I have been extravagant with that courage lately, leaving room for accidents, which sometimes wear the faces of miracles.

Chicago stands like a fist unclenching into boulevards. The stockyards officiate their brutal wedding to noon; above them, a sky of steel buttons. I look, write, look again, and my pen argues with what is acceptable.

Forgive your student for the insolence of changing his gait. I remember your chalk, a snowy weather that fell even in June. I keep a flake of it pressed in this letter.

Gratefully rebellious,

Samuel

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
