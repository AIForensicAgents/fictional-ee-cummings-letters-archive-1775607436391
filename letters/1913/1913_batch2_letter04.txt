A House of Paper Moons

Date: 1913-04-15
Recipient: Miss Aurelia Finch
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1913
Themes: work, women, resilience, commerce, friendship, hope

Dear Miss Finch,—

Our little shop has become a theater at sundown. Under the gas the straw brims go lunar; the ribbons tilt their faces and pretend a tide. Yesterday a girl came in with a white sash that simply said VOTES. She wanted a hat that could argue without speaking. We tried the one with the navy feather and it did: that feather wrote a sentence in the air the cautious morning had to read twice.

Do you remember last July’s storm that tried to take our awning downriver? We sewed the canvas back to itself with stubborn thread; I find that same thread in myself more often now. Rent has grown teeth again, but so has laughter, and it keeps biting back. I let the pins shine in the pincushion like little suns teaching the cloth daylight.

When the magnolia comes early, I shall set a bloom in the doorway as a kind of bell. Come down for the fair if you can; we will eat spun sugar and watch the Ferris wheel stitch the night to itself, round upon round, a seam you cannot find unless you love it.

With loyalty—and a pin through the heart of each plan—
Lila

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
