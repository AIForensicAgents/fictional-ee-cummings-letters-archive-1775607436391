Two stamps of Paris rain

Date: 1913-10-12
Recipient: Mother
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1913
Themes: travel, homesickness, art, city, rain

Dear Mother,

the stamp was damp when I pressed it—Paris keeps its weather in small bottles and spills one each afternoon. Streets pour their perfumes: bread, varnish, lilac, and the sweet elbow of a stranger.

I am learning the accent of roofs. They rhyme with pigeons. Painters argue with their hats on; a boy sells newspapers like he is throwing snow. I spend coins on museums and come away poorer, richer, wonderfully confused.

Do not worry about my meals (though I forget them). Worry only that I may grow too fond of windows. Every pane is a stage, and I a loyal audience of light.

When homesickness chews, I walk along the Seine. The water mothers everything—it scolds softly, it forgives. I send you this city folded twice, with rain to keep it pliable.

Kiss Father. Put an extra button in my memory of him.

Your son,

Samuel

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
