Small Prayer for a Station Platform

Date: 1913-10-03
Recipient: M——
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1913
Themes: yearning, travel, waiting, time, uncertainty, devotion

Beloved M,—

At Back Bay this morning the steam made a white chapel that vanished before any hymn could remember itself. I stood with my ticket folded small as a secret, and a porter whistled as if the day were a dog to be called to heel. A child counted the metal syllables of the wheels. What a strange arithmetic: if you are north and I am east, at what hour will the ache arrive?

I prayed a little to the timetable (it is a strict angel) and also to your hands (which keep truer time than any clock). A newspaper boy shouted the word Europe as if it were a ship you could jump from the curb; for a moment I bent my knees.

If courage were a coat I would button it to the throat, but today I wore hesitation and the wind went straight through me. When the express shouldered the track and left, the silence that arrived tasted of coal. I took it like communion.

Write me the color of your Tuesday. I will learn it by heart, a psalm recited low in the pocket.

Ever yours,
C.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
