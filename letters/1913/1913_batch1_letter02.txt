on a railing above the charles

Date: 1913-05-03
Recipient: Thomas Avery
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1913
Themes: friendship, ambition, art, rebellion, river

Dear Thomas,

we leaned on the cold railing and the river rehearsed its silver sentence below us. Rowers stitched straight thoughts through it; we, meanwhile, unbuttoned a few ideas and let them air.

You said the professors want their poems tidy as a parlor on Sunday. I said the world hiccups and stammers and suddenly sings—so why must we dress its mouth in cuffs?

I confess: doubt gnaws prettily. At night I feel like a cracked teacup that somehow holds the dark. Still, when the moon lifts its pale shoulder and the bridges blink, a stubborn courage crawls into my coat pocket.

Let us try, then, to write the way water remembers stone—patiently, with bright mistakes. If we are laughed at, we will laugh first.

Meet me tomorrow by the elm that leans like an italic. Bring the notebook that smells of rain.

Yours in necessary imprudence,

S.
