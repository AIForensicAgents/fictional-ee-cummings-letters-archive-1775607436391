Chant for a tenement staircase

Date: 1913-03-29
Recipient: Brother Joel
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1913
Themes: poverty, compassion, city, humility, faith

Brother Joel,

The staircase at 3rd and Orchard knows more prayers than the church. It creaks in Yiddish, sighs in Italian, coughs in Irish; sweat makes an ecumenical polish on the banister.

I climbed today carrying a loaf, and two doors argued about soup. A baby trumpeted victory over milk, and an old violin tuned the hallway to dusk.

Sometimes I fear my words wear good shoes to visit poor rooms. Forgive that. I am trying to learn the right scuff.

Let the mission keep boiling its coffee. Let the hymn be a ladle, not a fence. I will send more stamps and another pair of gloves when the month remembers money.

Yours in the awkward kingdom of mercy,

S.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
