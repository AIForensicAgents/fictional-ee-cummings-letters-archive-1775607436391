harbor grammar

Date: 1913-08-02
Recipient: Aunt Ruth
Location: Gloucester, Massachusetts
Year: 1913
Themes: sea, craft, discipline, family, metaphor

Dear Aunt Ruth,

the harbor keeps its own grammar: gulls are the commas, slow and necessary; nets, the parentheses that hold what thrashes; masts, emphatic exclamation.

I hired a week of sea to correct my unruly sentences. It slaps my cheeks into sense. Yesterday the fog came walking on soft shoes and the lighthouse cleared its throat all afternoon.

Uncle's old knife opens letters better than oysters. I have nicked my thumb and signed three pages in red by accident (or fate, which is the editor everyone fears).

If you see the boy who sells lemons near the pier, tell him I envy his yellow trade. Brightness is a currency I spend too slowly.

I send you one shell that listens politely, and a sprig of thyme for the soup that will keep the house believing in itself.

With salt in my pockets,

S.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
