machines dreaming in daylight

Date: 1913-02-17
Recipient: Editor of The Small Review
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1913
Themes: modernity, manifesto, experimentation, publication, urban

Esteemed Editor,

permit an unruly submission from a most courteous disobedience. I send you a handful of poems that have refused their collars. They prefer to go barefoot across the page, tracking mud and sudden light.

We live among machines that dream in daylight—elevators that hum private hymns, telephones that practice intimacy through wires, typewriters learning to bite. Shouldn't our language twitch and spark accordingly?

If my lines break where a breath breaks, if a verb misbehaves and loves two nouns at once, it is because the street has taught me generosity. I do not wish to fix the world; only to hear it more exactly.

Reject them if you must; but send back a margin smudged with your fingerprints, proof that a human wrist once turned these leaves.

With impolite hope,

Samuel North

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
