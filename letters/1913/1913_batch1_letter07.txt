August with bicycles

Date: 1913-08-23
Recipient: Eleanor Hart
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1913
Themes: summer, affection, freedom, nature, hesitation

Dearest Eleanor,

August uncurls like a cat on the porch and pretends to be sleepy; we know better. Your bicycle left two thin moons in the sand and the tide, greedy child, licked them away.

We pedaled until the dunes were rumors. You sang into the wind and it kept the tune, rough but faithful. I stowed your laughter in my shirt pocket where the sun could not spend it all at once.

If I were brave, I would ask you to winter beside me where sidewalks freeze and books warm their paws by kerosene. Instead I offer this earnest wheel of words.

Ride carefully through evening. The road grows suddenly philosophical after supper.

Affectionately (and a little foolish),

Sam

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
