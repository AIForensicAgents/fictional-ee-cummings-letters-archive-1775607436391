winter telegram (unmailed)

Date: 1913-01-05
Recipient: Future Self
Location: Concord, New Hampshire
Year: 1913
Themes: time, self, winter, doubt, resolve

Dear Future Self,

I write in a room wearing its breath like a gray scarf. The window has rehearsed snow into a flawless paragraph. I am nineteen and winter is a telegram that says STOP more than it says GO.

If you are reading this, you must have survived my doubts. Do they molt? Do they learn new tricks? Tell me you have kept a pocket for astonishment. Tell me language still burns your tongue slightly, like cinnamon too near the stove.

I promise to send you courage wrapped in ordinary acts: sharpening a pencil, lacing a boot, nodding to a stranger at the depot. Please send back the secret of mornings.

Unmailed, but not unlived,

S.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
