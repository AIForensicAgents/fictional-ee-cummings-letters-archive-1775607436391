red poppies under coal-smoke

Date: 1913-11-30
Recipient: Mina
Location: Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania
Year: 1913
Themes: industry, love, distance, resilience, city

Dear Mina,

the mills keep midnight folded in their furnaces, and every morning they open it a little. Soot freckled my cuffs today until I looked almost believable as a citizen of smoke.

At the market a woman sold poppies, improbable as small fires that forgot to be dangerous. I bought two and pinned them to my coat like exclamation points that meant your name.

If I could, I would roll the river back like a rug and sweep our footprints into the raw floor of distance. Instead I keep walking, and the city keeps teaching me its rough lullaby.

Write me about your window. Does it have the good habit of catching late sun? I require the measurement of your evening.

Always (beneath this ash-red sky),

S.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
