Night Psalm with Two Windows

Date: 1913-12-21
Recipient: Father
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1913
Themes: family, faith, winter, memory, reconciliation, introspection

Father,—

The year leans like a ladder against the last dark. I have kept the shop ledger straight enough to satisfy even your strict light, but when St. Martin’s bell untied midnight I set down my pen and just listened. There is a faith built of brick and sermon, and there is another that steps barefoot into the snow and finds not an enemy but an instruction.

Do not worry: I still nod to the catechism. Yet I confess that a second choir opens when I am alone—the stove ticking; window frost inventing cities; the moon practicing its pale handwriting on the floorboards. I remember your hands hammering the frame of this house: each nail a note, each beam a measure. If I am wayward it is only because music is, stumbling toward truth the way we stumble toward each other after work, awkward and glad.

When I think of your silence I hold a seashell to my ear and hear the whole Atlantic packed inside it. It sounds like “well done” and also like “try again.” Mother has hidden oranges for tomorrow; I will peel one in a single generous strip, to prove that the round world can be unbroken for a moment.

Your son,
Nathan

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
