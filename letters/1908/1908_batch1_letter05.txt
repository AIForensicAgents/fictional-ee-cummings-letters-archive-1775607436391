Room with Bare Electric

Date: 1908-05-12
Recipient: Mother
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1908
Themes: independence, art, family, city, longing

Dearest Mother,

My room is a clean poverty—one chair, one window, one hopeful bruise of paint on the wall pretending to be a garden. The lamp is not a lamp but a naked bulb, a white fruit with its peel missing. At night it hums like a bee that has mislaid its hive.

I promise I am eating: bread with a left-handed crust, coffee that tastes of Spain and smoke. I paint in the mornings until color forgets its manners and insists upon my sleeves. Afternoons I walk the river, which has learned to be both mirror and road. The bridges hold up the sky with the modesty of laundresses.

You asked if I am lonely. I am a small choir. There are alto hours and soprano ones; sometimes the bass of hunger clears his throat. But then a boy in a cap whistles toward the sun, and all four voices agree I am alive. Tell Father I read the newspapers and nod gravely, so he will be proud. Tell the cat that Paris invented the sunbeam and it is on my floor at this very moment, stretching.

I kiss your name upon the envelope. If it blushes, forgive me.

Your devoted daughter,
Clara

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
