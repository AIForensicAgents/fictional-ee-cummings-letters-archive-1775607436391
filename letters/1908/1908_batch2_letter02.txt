Lanterns & Locomotives

Date: 1908-05-21
Recipient: Julian Hart (at sea)
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1908
Themes: friendship, travel, harbor, machines, longing

Julian—

Tonight the harbor is a pocket full of lanterns and the moon (impatient coin) keeps trying to buy the water. Somewhere beyond those black buildings your ship is learning the grammar of distance; i stand on the pier and conjugate hope—i sail, you sail, we will be sailed by whatever wind knows our names.

New York clatters like a magnificent watch with too many hands. Elevated trains race their shadows; newsboys auction small thunders; a biplane stitched a brief seam in the afternoon sky and every throat became a question. I kept thinking how your laugh could outshout all that iron.

I have taken to drinking coffee as if it were advice. Each cup says go, go. My shoes agree and wander me into neighborhoods where laundry hangs like flags of surrender. There i discover that strangers can be kind for no reason but the reason, and this comforts what the ocean has borrowed from me—your chatter, your easy daring.

Send me a note from the middle of nowhere (which is to say the exact center of everywhere). Describe the color of the gulls' bad manners, the way the deck complains, the salt that turns language pure and impure at once.

Until your return i shall keep a small republic of patience. Its anthem is your name.

all haste and affection,

Thomas

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
