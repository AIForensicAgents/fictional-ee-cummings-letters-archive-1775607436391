On the Matter of Time and Other Mechanical Consolations

Date: 1908-10-12
Recipient: Mr. Ambrose Whitcomb, Mechanic
Location: Detroit, Michigan
Year: 1908
Themes: time, technology, forgiveness, modernity, friendship

Dear Ambrose,

I visited your shop today and found noon ticking from every corner—clocks dismantled like puzzles, springs coiled in their private forevers. A boy outside shouted about a new motorcar for ordinary men, and the street performed its usual miracle of becoming the future one cobblestone at a time.

The trouble is not the machine but the hour. I am forever losing and finding it, like a nickel with a hole bored through and tied to a string. When your hands disappear into a broken watch, you return with something more than gears. Faith, perhaps, wound tight and offered to the wrist.

Teach me that. Teach me to listen to the soft congregation of teeth turning teeth, to the sermon of balance wheels forgiving gravity again and again. I will pay in praise and in the honesty of my late arrivals. If you can fix the pocketwatch my father left—its minute hand limps as if nursing an old hurt—I wonder if some small forgiveness will begin in me as well.

Should we go hear the new engines race the river wind next Sunday? I would like to see a century unfold its collar.

Yours in grease and wonder,
Paul

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
