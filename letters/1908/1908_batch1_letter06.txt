Concerning a Rumor from the Forests

Date: 1908-07-05
Recipient: Tatiana Sergeyevna
Location: Saint Petersburg, Russia
Year: 1908
Themes: awe, news, nature, uncertainty, tenderness

Tatiana,

The newspaper stumbled into our breakfast like a startled bird. They say in Siberia a light opened its mouth over the trees and sang them flat. Men found day at midnight, and the deer learned geometry on the ground where the pines fell in their strange equations. A noise like ten ironsmiths hammering a cathedral, then silence that rang for miles after it was finished.

You know how city people are with distance—how we tame it with rumors and sugar. Still, I cannot help it: all morning my teacup trembled on its saucer as if the spoon remembered thunder. I thought of you under the tall ceilings where you keep your secrets in blue vases. I wanted to take your hand and stand under something unbroken.

What can explode a forest? The paper offers answers with polished shoes—comet, gas, divine arithmetic. I only know my own small sky, where your name moves like a patient star. If we are algorithms of breath and bread, promise me we shall be solved by kindness.

Write soon. Tell me how your garden is arranged, and whether your lilacs survived the rain. We must be careful with the world; it appears to be breakable.

Ever,
Lev

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
