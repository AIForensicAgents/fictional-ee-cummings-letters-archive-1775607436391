After the Meeting

Date: 1908-11-02
Recipient: Miss Eliza Hart, Temperance Society
Location: Kansas City, Missouri
Year: 1908
Themes: temperance, struggle, honesty, community, self-control

Miss Hart,

I walked home under a hat full of your courage. Your words went straight and clean as a church aisle, and for an hour I believed I could place my thirst at the altar and walk away unaccompanied. But the night has a way of un-buttoning resolutions. The saloon on Tenth Street breathed out its golden fog and my feet, those old rascals, remembered the door.

I did not enter. I counted the floorboards in the pavement instead, found a rhythm that forgave me for wanting what I did not take. Outside the window a man laughed the way men laugh when they are not listening to themselves. A piano misremembered a hymn, then improved upon it.

I am writing to tell you I failed in spirit but not in deed. Tomorrow the deed may fail and the spirit hold. You said to find a handhold in the ordinary—soap, bread, a kind sentence—and so I have washed the day from my wrists, sliced the loaf, and written this letter, which tastes like both apology and bread.

If your meeting requires chairs set or lanterns trimmed, send word. Hands save mouths.

Respectfully,
Jonas Pike

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
