Valentine in Brown Ink

Date: 1908-02-14
Recipient: Miss Adele March
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1908
Themes: love, winter, urban, anticipation, intimacy

Dear Adele,

Today my coat gathered the smell of snow and streetcars, and I thought of your glove buttons—how they keep a small winter obedient around your wrist. The city circles itself in lantern-light, and I stand in it (quietly unruly) whispering your name into my scarf as if the wool could keep a secret.

I bought a stamp and pressed it to my tongue; it tasted of musk and simple faith. The envelope fluttered in my hands like it wanted a different sky. I told it you would provide one. Every hour has rung itself thin, bell to bell, but the small minutes keep their private silk: that is where you wait for me, I think—in the minute between the second and its echo, where the heart kneels to tie its own shoe.

Shall I come to you on Tuesday, bringing the shy violets the florist hides behind his laughter, or will you come to me by trolley, counting the sparks under the wire as if each were a promise? I am not brave, only burning. Yet if you say the word, I will learn the alphabet of your hands and spell courage with my lips.

Yours (pressed flat in the atlas of longing),
Samuel

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
