Snowlike Silence

Date: 1908-12-24
Recipient: Reverend Elias Grey
Location: Montpelier, Vermont
Year: 1908
Themes: faith, winter, doubt, ritual, peace

Reverend Grey,

Snow has entered the village with its gentle authority, laying law on the fenceposts and the roofs without a single raised voice. The clock in your church has forgotten to be exact; its hands wander like shepherds among white hills of minute and hour.

I mean to speak plainly. Faith has been a house where I sometimes cannot find the door. Yet tonight the world performs belief without complaint: the horses accept their steam, the lamplighter devotes his flame to the street, and a child carves a name into frost as if it would last until summer.

I walked to the river and found it keeping a hard silence that did not frighten me. Perhaps this is prayer—standing beside a frozen thing and hearing it speak in the grammar of restraint. I will come to service, not because I am certain but because certainty has grown heavy and I wish to carry something lighter, even if it is only a hymn shared by strangers.

If you will have me without answers, I will bring a pocket of cedar shavings. The smell is an honest sentence.

With respect, and a hope I do not trust but keep,
Nathaniel Reed

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
