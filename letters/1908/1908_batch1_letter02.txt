On a Station Platform at Dusk

Date: 1908-03-03
Recipient: Mr. Edwin Clarke
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1908
Themes: travel, choice, friendship, time, yearning

Dear Edwin,

Dusk smudges the edges of the trains until they look like moving promises. I lean against the cold iron of the railing and watch a porter chalk a number on a car with the patience of a saint who has learned to bless departures. The air is a black bread of cinder and steam; I chew it, swallow it, become it.

You said once that a station is a question, and the tracks are two long answers which do not agree. Tonight they argue beautifully under my boots. People move like parentheses around a single sentence—farewell—that is always about to end and never does. Somewhere a violin falls into a waltz that nobody claims, and a child offers the platform his small and serious footprints.

I am on the verge of a decision so narrow it might be a knife. To leave is to gather myself into a suitcase; to stay is to unpack the weather in your window. Before the conductor calls this twilight to order, tell me: if I arrive without warning, will your lamp learn my face? Or shall I stand here until every timetable in the world forgets my name?

Faithfully, and impatient,
Thomas

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
