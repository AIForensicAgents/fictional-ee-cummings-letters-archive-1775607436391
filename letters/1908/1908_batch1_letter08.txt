Midnight Letter to the Future Daughter

Date: 1908-08-23
Recipient: Future Child
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1908
Themes: hope, motherhood, night, vulnerability, place

Little one who is not yet a handprint on any wall,

It is midnight and the shutters hold back the river with modest heroism. Crickets have organized themselves into a precise democracy. My chair rocks like a small boat, and I pour you this letter the way a woman pours water from a blue jug—steady, careful, believing the cup will be there.

If you ever come to me, I will show you how the magnolia unbuttons itself in heat, how the streetcar writes its name in sparks along the wire. We will count the dragonflies patrolling the garden and feed them rumors we wish to forget. You will learn that the moon is a patient clerk keeping your secrets in an iron ledger, and that even the most trembling heart can sign its name.

I am afraid of not deserving you. I am afraid of deserving you. Between these two harbors the river does its old, immortal work. Sleep honestly, wherever you are. I place my palm on my belly and speak plain: come when you are ready, or not at all. Either way, I will keep a small chair empty for you.

Always, your possible mother,
Ruth

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
