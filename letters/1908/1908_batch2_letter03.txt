Your Handwriting, Like Quiet Thunder

Date: 1908-08-03
Recipient: Eliza Morrell
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1908
Themes: love, summer, absence, music, domestic quiet

Dearest Eliza,

i am sitting in a window that believes in August. The curtains breathe like peaceful animals; the street hums its lazy bee. Providence has mislaid its urgency and i, being an honest thief, have pocketed the afternoon to write you.

Your last letter was the sound of rain taught to be handwriting. I read it aloud to the geranium and it blushed extravagantly. Do you remember the meadow near your father's mill—the one reckless with fireflies? Night there is a cathedral built by insects and sarcasm of stars. When you walked beside me your silence invented a new moon each step.

I have been practising not missing you and failing with method. At meals the spoon forgets its business and listens for your foot upon the stair. Even the gramophone sounds like a seashell—full of distances. I pass the tailor's window and every needle points toward you with absurd authority.

All this to say: the world is entirely too vivid without your shoulder, and yet i applaud it. Because you exist, peaches taste like explanations. Because you exist, i can forgive the mirror its exactness and the clock its stubborn pulse.

Send me a secret (the smaller the better). I will keep it warm between two commas until winter, then let it fly.

Yours in all weather,

Margaret
