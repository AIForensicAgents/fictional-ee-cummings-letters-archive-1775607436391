Sea Mary, Salt Mary

Date: 1908-04-21
Recipient: Mary (aboard S.S. Belgravia)
Location: Liverpool, England
Year: 1908
Themes: farewell, sea, memory, devotion, distance

My dear Mary,

The gulls have been schooling me in farewell all morning, making figure-eights of white upon the brown harbor air. I write from the quay where the ropes speak rope-language to the bollards, heavy syllables of stay. You move north by compass and payroll; I remain south by sentiment and poor luck.

Do you remember the coins we pressed into the ship’s flank for safety? I pressed one with my thumbnail until my breath left a small fog upon the metal. I wanted the ship to know me by that fog. Silly—yes—but the sea is an audience that claps in waves and forgets by noon.

Carry with you the little book of psalms (torn at the corner where I laughed too hard) and the photograph where my hat sits badly upon my head. When the boilers hum like a hundred stern uncles and your berth elbows your ribs, open to the middle and look where my thumb smudged the paper; that is the compass. When you cannot sleep, count the knots not in the rope but in your hair. Untie them with patience and a whisper to the ocean, which understands women better than men ever do.

Return if you can. If not, write how the horizon pronounces your name.

Ever yours,
Evan

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
