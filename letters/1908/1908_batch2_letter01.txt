A Small Astonishment in Ink

Date: 1908-02-09
Recipient: Clara Ames
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1908
Themes: sibling bond, winter, study, wonder, electric modernity

Dear Clara,

this morning the snow learned my name with its thousand white tongues (hush hush) upon the sill, and even the teapot listened. I walked beneath the electric wires—each one humming a thin winter hymn—and thought of your hands turning pages in that stern library where dust rehearses eternity.

Cambridge wears February like a shy bonnet. Streetcars bell their little certainties and boys carve constellations into the drift with their boots. I am trying to behave, which is to say i am trying to be astonished politely; it is difficult. The river is a sheet of breath; i wrote your initial upon it with my glove and the wind corrected my spelling.

You asked whether my lectures are improving. They are, the way dawn improves upon night—slow and pale and then suddenly extravagant. The professor's moustache recites history with more conviction than his mouth, but i forgive both. I carry a notebook whose paper seems to grow a spine whenever i whisper a foolish hope; sentences walk out of me with red cheeks.

Please keep the scarf. I have trained it to remember the exact geography of your neck. When you wear it, perhaps you will hear the same discreet music the wires insisted upon—thin, brave, nearly invisible. Write soon. Your envelopes are small rooms where i can live without apology.

i send you my pocket sky folded into this letter.

your brother,

Nathan

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
