Salt Telegrams

Date: 1902-03-19
Recipient: Captain Roebuck
Location: Portsmouth, New Hampshire
Year: 1902
Themes: sea, work, friendship, waiting

Captain,

It is a quiet morning and still my cuffs are salted—proof that the sea speaks even when one is ashore. The gulls behave like small postmen, flapping white envelopes above the pier, dropping their messages on the water where only the wind can read them. I imagine your deck taking those short, stubborn steps toward the horizon, your hands a pair of maps that know the way by bone.

I have been selling nets to men who dream of weight. They touch the mesh as though it were a future, tight and shining. Some ask after you. I say you went to borrow a compass from the distance and will be back when it has finished telling you its secrets.

There are nights when the tide loosens every knot inside me. I lay awake, counting the small, brave lanterns bobbing on the black—a city that learned to breathe salt. If I have a religion, it is the sound of a cleat accepting a line at dusk. Forgive such talk. On land, a certain poetry leaks out of a man like tar from a warm rope.

Send word when the current grows conversational, when the fo’c’sle laughs at your stories, when the morning cooks itself on the galley stove. Until then I will carry your weather in my pockets, and shake it out whenever the day appears too sure of itself.

With a shoreman’s salute,
Evan

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
