Map with One Blue River

Date: 1902-07-21
Recipient: Marcel
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1902
Themes: travel, art, friendship, city

Cher Marcel,

I have folded Paris into a pocket-sized geography: bridges like ribs, cafés like freckles, and the river—that patient blue sentence—unwinding under a sky that pretends not to be watching. I walk from shade to shade as if the day were a book too bright to read all at once.

A boy near the Luxembourg painted violets that smelled of turpentine and rain. He looked at his hands as though they were two small theaters, each with its own curtain. In the gallery on the Rue de Seine I stood before a portrait that glanced back with more forgiveness than I have earned. Forgiveness, here, is an outdoor custom—shared in bread, pronounced by waiters, stitched into the napkins like a white prayer.

At night the lamps are arguments I do not wish to win. The streets converse longer than I can keep awake. I am learning to order coffee without trembling and to let the spoon cool in its saucer like a calm idea. If you were beside me, you would listen to the river and claim it wrote your first sentence.

I send you an apple from an American stall near the station. It wore its shine like a uniform; I dulled it with my sleeve so that you might taste the orchard and not the parade. Meet me in late August by the bookstalls. I will be the one pretending not to read the spine of your face.

À toi,
Esther

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
