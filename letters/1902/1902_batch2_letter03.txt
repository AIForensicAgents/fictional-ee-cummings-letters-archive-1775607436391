After Midnight on the Butte of Painters

Date: 1902-10-08
Recipient: Madame Lucienne V.
Location: Montmartre, Paris
Year: 1902
Themes: art, music, night, desire, urban life

Chère Madame Lucienne,

After midnight the hill remembers every footstep. The cobbles refuse to forget; they keep small biographies of heels and rain and sudden embraces. I climbed slowly tonight, paint still under my fingernails like a second, truer skin. We had been arguing downstairs about light—Davide insisting it can be owned if you pay enough attention, Marceau swearing it is a scandal that refuses to keep modest hours. Your song solved us both. It moved up the stair like silk listening to itself, and I am ashamed to confess I nearly applauded the air.

At Le Chat, absinthe made unreasonable promises in the glasses. A woman at the next table wore sorrow like a fashionable hat; a poet tried to borrow it and was politely refused. I sketched my own hand because it would not be still. This is vanity perhaps, or desperation: to draw the instrument when the music is elsewhere. But I am learning your lesson—to praise the object by understanding its ache.

Tonight the moon worked for us unasked, laying silver across the roofs so that even the laundry became opera. When you sang La Fille aux cheveux de lin, it seemed the stairwell leaned closer and the dust rose in a kind of reverence nobody taught it. If I were faithful to my discipline I would go home and mix a grey that remembers how that note rounded the corner. Instead I am writing this. Forgive me; letters are another pigment when the canvas refuses to hold so much heart.

You speak of leaving for Nice when the season turns. I cannot tell you not to go—artists are migratory, and even the best cages rust. But if you must, leave me your shadow to rehearse departures with; leave me one ribbon from your dress to prove velocity can be tied to the simplest thing. I will send you my ruined brushes, loved to failure, as proof that ruin can be exquisite if it finds the right object.

When the morning arrives it will break itself against the cathedral and fall in warm shards through the studio windows. I will sweep carefully, while thinking of your voice in last night's light. Should you write, please write recklessly. The postman has such a solemn face that he needs to carry wildly beating things.

Toujours à vous (in a way paint cannot conceal),
Étienne
