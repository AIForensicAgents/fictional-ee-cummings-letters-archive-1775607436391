Midnight Carriage Receipt

Date: 1902-02-27
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1902
Themes: urban life, family, progress, nostalgia

Thomas,

I have taken to riding in the last carriage, where the draft is honest and the lamps shiver in their shoes. The horses strike sparks from the stones, brief comets that die before they have a chance to name themselves. I keep a notebook on my knee and try to sew the city into sentences, though the thread breaks whenever a whistle interrupts to explain its opinion of progress.

This town hums like a hive even in snow. Electric bulbs bloom from the dark with that stern white flower, and the elevated track scribbles steel across the sky. Yesterday I saw a woman selling violets in a fog of horse-breath; she arranged them in such a way that the street seemed suddenly to remember spring. I would have bought them, but my coins had already sworn allegiance to bread.

Write of home. Is Mother still mending by the stove, counting beads of heat with her thimble? Tell her I have not forgotten the smell of starch or the stubborn grace of our table. I keep my collar brushed. I speak carefully to strangers, who are everywhere, even in my pocket after I pay the fare.

I have enclosed the receipt from my first night ride—a small proof that I am here, moving. Keep it in the red book. If you tilt it in the lamp-light, you will see a smear that is not ink but the city itself, shaking my hand as if we had just been introduced.

Your brother,
James

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
