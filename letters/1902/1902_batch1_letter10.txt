Winter Letter with Lantern

Date: 1902-12-22
Recipient: Beloved M.
Location: Baltimore, Maryland
Year: 1902
Themes: reunion, winter, love, home

My dear M.,

The train window has invented a geometry of frost—ferns, ladders, little cathedrals trembling under the breath of strangers. I press my thumb to the glass and borrow its cold. Outside, fields lean into their sleep; a single horse writes its patient signature in the snow.

I have brought a lantern for the walk from the station. Its light is small but certain, like an honest coin. I think of you opening the door with your shawl half-tied, the house making its soft arrangements for my return—one extra cup, an elbow of soup, the clock deciding to be kind. If I arrive late, do not wait by the gate; winter likes to tell long stories to the feet.

There is a jar of preserves in my bag—the summer we forgot to finish. We will break it open on Christmas morning and commit the red to our tongues. Say you will forgive the weeks I kept too much of myself at work. I am coming with both hands this time, and all the pockets they insist upon.

Until I knock, keep a chair near the stove and a question ready. Ask me what the moon said over Delaware. I will answer truthfully: it said come home, and chose a road of ice to point the way.

Yours, quite entirely,
Nathan

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
