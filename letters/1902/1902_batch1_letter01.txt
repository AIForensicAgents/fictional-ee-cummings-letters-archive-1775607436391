A Small Weather of the Heart

Date: 1902-01-11
Recipient: Miss Eleanor Hart
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1902
Themes: longing, winter, memory, intimacy

Dear Eleanor,

Your handwriting arrived today like a sparrow on the sill—light, a little out of breath, full of surety despite the cold. I kept it under the tin where the tea leaves sleep, for fear the kettle would steam it to ink-rain. Do not scold me; the kettle and I quarrel when the world is this pale.\n
The river has chosen pewter for its winter dress. It moves nevertheless, just enough to prove the idea of moving. I walked along it at dusk and thought of you untying your hairpins, each one a brief, bright star falling behind the horizon of your ear. What a calendar the body keeps: I count the days since I saw you by the faint ache near my wrist where your farewell pressed like a ticket stub.

I have learned the house’s soft machinery—the stair that protests like an elderly violin, the door whose breath is a long wooden hush. When the clock insists on its opinions, I set a chair near the window and let the night do its numbers on me: one bell, two, three—until the dark stops pretending to be complicated and becomes simply itself.

Write again soon. Tell me if the sparrows there sing a different grammar. Tell me if you are taking milk, and whether the hem of your blue dress still catches on the wicker basket as it did last market-day. If you are cold, I will send you the small weather of my heart—a shawl no one sees, but you, perhaps, will know how to wear it.

Yours in a careful key,
Arthur

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
