five candles inside a blackout

Date: 1944-08-31
Recipient: Aunt Estelle in London
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1944
Themes: family, resilience, transatlantic, fear, light

dear aunt estelle,

the lake tonight is a stovetop left on low. boys fish for meteors with their eyes and respectable women become navigators by accident. i imagine you across the ocean with the light politely turned down, listening for a siren, or for good news dressed in bells.

i have five candles and a habit of faith. we eat supper without wasting shadows. the grocer s wife gives me the green apples with bruises because hunger is not picky and neither is hope. i polish the radio s knob with my sleeve as if clean hands could make the world say kinder things.

your last letter said the flying bombs bark at noon and at midnight and in between and still you clip roses in the yard as if the hedge were a spine that must not unlearn its posture. i read that sentence again when the wind fusses at the windows. it stands my shoulders up straight.

on the el today a soldier slept with his mouth open like a small door everybody was careful not to slam. somewhere, i thought, estelle is reviewing the weather with god. if there is thunder, you will lend it a cup of sugar.

write soon. tell me your new recipes for courage. i will send you this pocketful of lake wind and the idea of a street that never forgets how to be morning after a long night.

from your niece, improvising light,

ida.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
