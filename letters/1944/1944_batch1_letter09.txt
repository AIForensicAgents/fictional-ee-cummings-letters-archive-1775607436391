after the bells of paris

Date: 1944-08-27
Recipient: Colette (if your streets are yours again)
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1944
Themes: liberation, joy, radio, city, celebration

chere colette,

i heard them here across an ocean and a city of radiators the bells tumbling out of the radio like apples freed from the cart. people opened their windows at once and the night stood up. even the pigeons on the rooftop corrected their posture as if hats had been returned to heads.

the announcer s voice stumbled with happiness and we forgave him everything. i went into the hallway and danced with mr. pagliacci who plays the cornet and with miss ruth who wears grief like a silk scarf that will not wrinkle. on the third floor a baby hiccuped victory.

i imagined you on a street pronounced by sunlight, the great cursed boot lifted, the cobbles remembering their own names. i saw bread with crusts bigger than rumors, cafe chairs loosened from their chains of caution, a river widening its pockets for laughter. forgive my arrogance; i love the idea of your sidewalks too much.

when the music ran out we clapped for the radio as if it had hands. i carried a loaf past midnight and shared slices with strangers whose teeth glowed like sudden moons. we ate standing up, a communion improvised of crumbs and tears and the word enfin.

write to me when you can. send the recipe for the sound the bells made, if such a recipe exists. i will try it here, on this stubborn island, until the walls remember how to be windows forever.

embrasse la ville for me,

lucy.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
