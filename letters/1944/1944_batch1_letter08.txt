unaddressed (open at the edges)

Date: 1944-10-03
Recipient: To the one I haven’t met yet
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1944
Themes: destiny, waiting, desert, trains, tenderness

dear stranger whom my heart already recognizes by the way the wind stops to listen,

today the desert folded its wrists under its chin and watched me cross. the sky was a laboratory of unblued fire. freight trains stitched the horizon to itself with black thread and a whistle that knew where it was going before it arrived.

i work mornings where numbers pretend to be constellations and men read equations the way farmers read weather. they walk out with chalk on their fingers like frost and a look that belongs to maps. i bring them coffee and the word careful. sometimes it is enough, sometimes it is only a broom against the tide.

i do not know your hair, or your laugh, or how you prefer your eggs when the rain comes honest. but i believe there is a door i have been walking toward since my mother taught me how to tie my shoes. the knob is warm already.

if you are far, may the miles behave. if you are close, ring once and i will step out carrying my small suitcase of yes. the desert teaches thirst like a gentle tyrant and then gifts an evening that abolishes every law but blue. in that dusk i saved you a pocket of air, and a seat on the adobe wall where the stars rehearse their shyest names.

until your footsteps know my threshold,

m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
