a small brave cup of tea

Date: 1944-02-23
Recipient: Mother
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1944
Themes: family, home, wartime, hope, domestic

dear mother,

the kettle remembers how to sing even when i do not. i sit with a chipped cup and the light that comes in under the curtain like a thin soldier. the tin can on the sill grows one pale geranium as if to say see, we may be out of almost everything except the business of trying.

i have stitched the blackout curtains a little tighter; the needle liked the cloth and the radio liked the news less. mrs. callahan down the hall keeps her bread in the icebox now and her son s in his letters. i saved two orange peels in the drawer for their bravery and because they smell like trains to summer.

you asked if i am eating and if i am praying. yes to both, though my prayers are not tidy. some arrive barefoot. some forget their names and stand in the doorway listening to the clock. i do not mind. i am learning to pour silence into a cup and call it tea.

the stove ticks after its work like a good heart. i thought i saw you today in a woman s hat turning the corner. it followed me upstairs, that turn, as if i had borrowed a little of your steadiness and put it in my coat pocket. i will keep it safe there with the buttons and the bus schedules.

please send the recipe for the sweet rolls you used to make when snow pretended to be forever. i will substitute patience for sugar and see if the dough believes me.

your child, practicing comfort,

l.
