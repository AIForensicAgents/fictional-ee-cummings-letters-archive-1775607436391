ink breathes like midnight

Date: 1944-01-14
Recipient: Clara (in the upstairs room)
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1944
Themes: love, winter, city, rationing, longing

dear clara,

tonight the radiator sings its cracked bronze lullaby and january climbs the window like a pale cat. i press this paper to the table and the table to the floor and the floor to the city and the city to your name (which keeps the whole stack from falling).

on the corner a boy traded his snowball for a cigarette and the world seemed perfectly possible for a second. i carry coffee that has already lived three lives through the grounds. i watch the words in my cup rise, sink, rise. so does my heart when your steps flower up the stairs and vanish at our landing as if day itself were checking its ration book.

i keep your red scarf behind the pipes to warm the letters. it smells of rain and small, perfectly honest lies, the kind we tell each other when we are trying to be brave. your hands which know all the answers even when they are only searching for buttons, for the shy mouth of the dark, for me.

someday we will buy sugar without counting. someday there will be grapes fat as laughter. until then i have your sleeve in my pocket and a window that refuses to stop inventing new kinds of blue.

love is a lightbulb we unscrew carefully and carry warm in our fists across the cold room. hurry down if you cannot sleep. the moon has been writing your initials in the steam and i am pretending not to notice.

yours in frost and fire,

a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
