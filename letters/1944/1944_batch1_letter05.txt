the moon folds itself into an envelope

Date: 1944-06-07
Recipient: Mr. Halvorsen, the Night Janitor
Location: Detroit, Michigan
Year: 1944
Themes: war news, labor, night, solidarity, hope

sir,

the plant let out a long metallic breath at two this morning and for a second the whole city leaned toward the radio. the foreman turned the dial until europe arrived like a storm that had been practicing our names. men stepped onto beaches we have never seen and all the fluorescent saints in the ceiling flickered their uncertain hallelujahs.

i watched your hands, ordinary miracles with keys. you unlocked the broom closet as if courage were something one could sweep after, one corridor at a time. i am twenty-three and tell myself i am a bolt in a wing in a plane in a sky that does not forget. even so, i have never been so glad to see someone push a mop like a prayer.

the vending machine blinked hunger and we fed it nickels for comfort. lard sandwiches wrapped in yesterday. someone swore softly for luck. i thought of the moon, which has signed so many night shifts without complaint. she should be paid in oceans.

when the shift ended the whistle stitched us to morning. i walked out with the others and our faces were the color of new maps. if i could, i would write a letter to every floor you have made brave enough to hold our weight. this will have to do.

if tonight you find a small silence under the stair, it is mine. please carry it with the dustpan and throw it to the moon. she will know what to do.

respectfully and awake,

a fellow custodian of hours.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
