letter with a borrowed sky

Date: 1944-03-19
Recipient: Lieutenant James Avery, Somewhere in Italy
Location: Cleveland, Ohio
Year: 1944
Themes: brotherhood, distance, spring, courage, home

dear jim,

i found a postcard of lake erie pretending to be the ocean; i am sending it because even lies can steady a hand if they are blue enough. the snow is tired of being important and has begun to leave in soft shoes. factory whistles unwrap the morning at six and at six again and someone s lunch pail clangs like a bell that forgot how to pray quietly.

mrs. horowitz stands in her doorway counting children and loaves with the same fingers. your bicycle is in the cellar leaning against june. i oil the chain to keep the summer inside it from rusting. the dog has learned to sleep on your boots like a map.

i listen to the news and fold the names into a drawer because there are too many to leave lying around. when it is very late i practice saying your name the way the wind does when it slips around a corner and thinks no one is listening. james avery. then just james. then only the idea of returning steps.

the lake is grey today with ambition. it wants to be your safe harbor, a letter that knocks and is answered. i am sending socks, chocolate, the foolish conviction that spring knows your address. do not be too brave. be precisely the amount of brave that returns.

from your sister under a sky on loan,

m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
