spring writes your name across bridges

Date: 1944-04-11
Recipient: Marisol (who dances when nobody watches)
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1944
Themes: love, music, city, yearning, fog

dear marisol,

this morning the fog climbed down from the bridge like a quiet acrobat and put its cool hand on my hair. streetcars rang their coins against the air and i thought of you feeding nickels to the jukebox until it laughed.

i have six sugar stamps and a plan for music. the baker keeps his radio turned low so the bread can rise without politics. later at the civic center the pigeons rehearsed a ballet called we are not afraid of elegance. i watched and learned something about shoulders.

the bay is a pocket where ships lose and find themselves. when you dance your ankles write letters on the floor and my feet, bashful and clumsy, try to reply in a language not yet invented. i am not good at most things but i can carry your coat tenderly and warm the lining with my mistakes.

when the fog lifts, the hills become chandeliers and every cable car is a small cathedral with open doors. i walked past the cannery and the smell of tin and peaches argued sweetly. i almost bought you a can of tomorrow but settled for lilies bruised by the sidewalk and a promise.

meet me where the bridge learns to begin again. i will bring a record tucked under my coat and the audacity to hold you as if the night were a trustworthy street.

always rehearsing the steps to your name,

r.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
