snow rehearses our names softly

Date: 1944-12-24
Recipient: Benjamin (back from the Pacific, asleep on the couch)
Location: Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania
Year: 1944
Themes: homecoming, tenderness, winter, healing, quiet

ben,

you are home and sleeping with your boots on the floor like two animals finally tamed. the tree blinks kindly, as if practicing semaphore for a peaceable ship. snow has been standing at the window for an hour learning how to say our names without waking you.

i have boiled milk with cinnamon the way your mother did when the world needed to be smaller and warmer. the kettle does its round breathing, the house answers with the tick of familiar bones. i sit close enough to your chest to learn its new grammar and promise to study without argument.

your hands, even relaxed, remember storms. they open inside my own like parachutes testing the air. we will not speak tonight of islands or orders or the geometry of distances. there will be bread, and sleep, and the slow untying of knots we did not ask to learn.

when you stir i will bring you a cup and the orange i have been saving since last tuesday because victory must taste like something. i will peel it in one brave ribbon and wear the rind as a crown until you laugh. then i will take your boots to the hallway and whisper to them that their marching is finished.

if morning wants carols we will hum. if it wants silence, we will be mountain and valley in one, snow between us rehearsing yes.

welcome home, dear heart,

h.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
