prayer with salt and thunder

Date: 1944-07-22
Recipient: The Atlantic, addressed from a pier
Location: Coney Island, New York
Year: 1944
Themes: sea, faith, absence, summer, prayer

dear ocean,

i am writing to you because nobody else can keep secrets as big as ships. the roller coaster behind me keeps practicing the heart s bad habits. gulls invent impossible alphabets and refuse to translate. i bought a paper cone of hot clouds they called fries and burned my lip into honesty.

someone i love is somewhere between here and a city with a different alphabet of bells. i have a button from his sleeve and the promise that waves keep making even when they are tired. teach me, old salt, how to go forward without forgetting to breathe on the way.

i will trade you three dimes, one fear, and a memory of his laugh for a tide that brings home. the boardwalk sings through its nails. beneath the planks the sea rummages for the moon and finds my reflection instead wearing a life i pretend fits.

i know you have eaten bravely and given back sometimes. i have seen the way you leave the sand generous with shells, little rib cages empty of shame. if you are a god, be clumsy with mercy. if you are only water, be tender with astonishments.

when the thunder sets its shoulder against the sky i will stand here and lean with it. if he returns, let him taste this letter as salt on his lip and think only of kisses. if he does not, teach me a way to carry the absence that does not drown the rooms.

i remain, foolish and sincere,

the girl who believes in blue.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
