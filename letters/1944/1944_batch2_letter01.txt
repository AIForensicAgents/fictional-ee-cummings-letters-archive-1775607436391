ink & thunder (for your pocket)

Date: 1944-01-19
Recipient: Marta
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1944
Themes: wartime love, homefront, winter, rationing, longing, intimacy

dear marta,

today the snow unbuttoned its white coat over cambridge and the trolley sang like a thin cup. i carried our ration book like a small domesticated thundercloud—quiet only because it is folded. every stamp is a window; i press my face to them and see soup, wool, a little blue of tomorrow.

here the radiators do a polite, old-world humming. the newspaper leaves its black pollen on my fingers (headlines marching as if certainty were a uniform). i prefer your name: it is the one parade i will follow barefoot, even in january.

our victory garden sleeps under its quilt of frost; seed packets lean in a drawer like postcards from the future. i tried on my coat and discovered the lining learning to speak spring. maybe because your hand once slipped into that pocket—(a lighthouse with a secret password)—and every corridor since keeps a green breath.

if the sirens come (which i doubt; the sky here is mostly domestic, it boils its cloud-egg at six), i will count the cellar steps by your syllables: m-a-r-t-a. five bright candles and one shy echo. i keep a candle anyway, impudent and small, in case the electricity forgets its manners.

some nights i type your letters on this old machine and the ribbon coughs up dusk. the keys are tiny bridges: i cross to you, lightfoot and lowercase. there are so many commas between us, and yet the sentence holds.

write when the streets stop shivering. or before, because i like to warm your words between my palms. meanwhile: i am sweeping a narrow room in my heart and setting out the tea, for your return; the cups are chipped, the sugar thin, the love—unsuspecting and enormous.

always (which is not a clock but a door),

n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
