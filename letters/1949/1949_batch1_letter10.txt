small prayer for the morning after election

Date: 1949-11-09
Recipient: A stranger on the trolley
Location: Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
Year: 1949
Themes: civics, kindness, urban life, morning, renewal

dear unknown with newspaper ink on your thumb,

the city yawns its headlines and i sit across from you counting the brave wrinkles of ordinary people. someone has won, someone has not, and the trolley cares only for sparks. your hat tilts as if listening to gravity's sermon.

i want to tell you that hope is not the ballot or the banner but the old woman at the corner who keeps feeding pigeons into belief. last night my radio argued with itself while a kettle decided everything was still survivable. i slept in pieces and woke to a sky the color of erasers.

you do not know me. still, when the trolley bell bothered the intersection into order, you lifted your hand (reflex, kindness) and the driver nodded. it looked like a tiny democracy between strangers, the best kind, invisible and daily.

i am writing a small prayer disguised as a letter disguised as a thought: may we learn to lose without meanness and win without forgetting our shoes squeak like anyone's. may potholes be patched before winter, and may we forgive the city for not being a miracle while still asking it to try.

my stop is next. if you find this folded under the seat, keep it. if not, let the next passenger inherit the idea that morning is repairable.

in transit,
claire

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
