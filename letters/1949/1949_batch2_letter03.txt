port of moonlight: marseille to you

Date: 1949-07-06
Recipient: Aline, poste restante
Location: Marseille, France
Year: 1949
Themes: travel, postwar, yearning, cities, chance

aline—

no address of yours obeys me, so i point this letter at the post office window, where the clerk keeps the afternoon in a drawer beside the rubber stamps. the harbor smells of rope and oranges—the honest sweat of distances. sailors braid their cigarettes with songs i've never met; a gull with a hole in its left wing writes pale alphabets on the sky and no one corrects them.

i walked the old quarter where laundry makes flags of small countries (kingdoms of soap, republics of salt). an old woman sold me an apricot and a story; both were sweet and a little bruised. she said the war left teeth in the city's gums, but see, we smile anyway. she meant the stones. perhaps also herself.

i am learning to count in a currency of echoes. your footsteps around the hotel corner; your laughter folded under the bedspread of noon; your hand absent and everywhere, like wind that has decided to be generous. the boat horns are low animals calling to the night. i answer with a postcard of the basilica: dear aline, the hill makes the sky kneel, but gently.

sometimes in the cafe the radio leans across the room with american jazz and knocks over everyone's sorrow. glasses clink into new grammar. a girl in a red scarf invents a dance that forgives tuesday. i add sugar to my coffee like a sailor learning tenderness.

if you arrive, wear something foolish so i can recognize the courage it takes to be silly. i will bring you a paper cone of hot chestnuts for your impatient hands. we'll let the streets teach us their crooked alphabet; we'll speak marseille by walking.

until your pocket learns my name, i remain

yours between tides,

n.
