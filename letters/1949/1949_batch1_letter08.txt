on the roof with comets (or laundry)

Date: 1949-10-13
Recipient: Self, thirty years from now
Location: Brooklyn, New York
Year: 1949
Themes: time, hope, daily life, self-reflection, future

dear future me (who is practicing to be a constellation),

it is october and the city wears a jacket of brick. i have climbed to the roof with a basket of socks and questions. small stars mind their own shining while the radio downstairs misremembers a waltz. i am trying to keep faith with warm clothespins and the sky.

if you receive this, please confirm: does the heart keep its instructions folded under the tongue, and do we ever learn to read them without stuttering? i am less afraid of dying than of forgetting which keys open rooms i haven't met yet.

tonight the moon is a careful coin. i want to spend it on our ordinary life: morning eggs, a neighbor who sings off-key, the stubborn chair that never agrees with the floor. if the future offers you machines that speak, ask them to whisper. we have enough shouting.

in the street a boy bounces a rubber ball made of pure echo. i am suddenly eleven, scab-kneed and invincible. i do not know who taught me doubt, but i would like to return the lesson unopened.

promise me you will keep a foolish part, the one that believes umbrellas can escort miracles and that a good sentence can feed three people. also write to someone every week. letters are bridges that do not mind the distance.

with laundry and comets,
— you, still practicing

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
