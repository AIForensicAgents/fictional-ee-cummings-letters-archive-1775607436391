night train between towns

Date: 1949-06-17
Recipient: The conductor whose name I forgot
Location: Somewhere between Omaha and Cheyenne
Year: 1949
Themes: travel, night, anonymity, america, yearning

sir,

you punched my ticket as if time were obedient. the car shivered like an animal learning a new country of rails. coffee smelled like a brave mistake (i drank two). the sleeper swayed and i counted telegraph poles until mathematics forgave me.

out the window, america practiced distance. barns (modest red commas), windmills (shy parentheses), and stars — oh those indiscreet typewriters tapping light on the dark.

you walked the aisle like gravity's best friend, kindly and invisible. i wanted to ask your name and never did. perhaps you were called miles, perhaps you were called morning. you said goodnight to no one in particular and everyone heard it.

somewhere a coyote stitched a sentence from its own throat. somewhere a farmhouse lamp refused to sleep. somewhere my heart, traveling opposite the train, slipped back to a porch where i am always almost arriving.

if we meet at another platform, let me buy you a coffee the color of patience. i owe you that small warmth for not waking me when the moon leaned in to read my dreams.

respectfully,
a passenger with a pocket of westward

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
