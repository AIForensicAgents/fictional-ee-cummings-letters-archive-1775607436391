to my mother (with lilacs)

Date: 1949-04-30
Recipient: Mother
Location: Madison, Wisconsin
Year: 1949
Themes: motherhood, spring, healing, nostalgia, domestic life

dear ma,

i brought home a carton of lilacs and the whole apartment remembered the backyard of 1932. you, with clothespins like bright wooden teeth, your apron a sail the wind meant to marry. the sheets lifted, our names inside them, full of sun like two white lungs.

you ask do i eat enough. yes (enough to argue with loneliness and enough to flatter my bones). the grocer knows to save me the bruised peaches. they taste like summer telling the truth.

i wanted to tell you that i passed the old hospital today. the windows blinked in april language. there are no more stretchers on the lawn, only sparrows inventing aerobatics. i sat on the steps and counted to fifty for no reason except to arrive gently somewhere that didn't hurt.

i keep your letters in the breadbox. nothing molds there, only softens. i read the last one aloud and the kettle nodded. you said, remember to pray. i did not know what to say back, so i boiled water for tea until the room felt spoken to.

if i could, i would ship you spring in a hatbox: lilacs, the foolish rain that apologizes while happening, the small courage of early grass, and a rabbit that believes in both speed and vanishing.

love you always, in lowercase and uppercase,
annie

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
