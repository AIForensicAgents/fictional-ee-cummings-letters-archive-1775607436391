apartment windows (third-floor)

Date: 1949-01-12
Recipient: M.
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1949
Themes: longing, winter, city, intimacy, modernity

dear m,

snow edits the streets into hush and commas; our window (a rectangular heartbeat) fogs and clears like a shy lung. the radiator coughs its iron lullaby. i am wearing your sweater which remembers your elbows better than my ribs remember air. downstairs the grocer argues with oranges in a language of winter and coins.

i wish i could mail you the exact steam of my tea, the lipprint, the warm astonishment. i wish i could fold the skyline (crease it carefully along the river) and tuck it in this envelope, so you might open the folds and the bridges would arise like gentle machinery of wanting.

this morning the typewriter startled a poem out of me — its little teeth clacked, a tiny machine of courage. i have decided to trust the lowercase of my heart; capital letters make promises louder than they can keep.

last night i saw two jitterbug shadows thrown against a tenement wall. they kissed like electricity learning a softer word for itself. i thought of your hand (absurdly accurate compass) and the way it finds my wrist as if wrist were a country and your name were geography.

in this brand-new atomic hour, everything pretends to be certain, but the snow is honest. it falls and falls and never pretends to be anything else. please write before it melts.

love,
— a
