april letter with lilacs (to the piano)

Date: 1949-04-17
Recipient: Mira
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1949
Themes: longing, city, music, spring, intimacy

dear mira,

the lilacs have been rehearsing your breath outside my window—small purple lungs of april (every petal says hush). ninth street smells of rain, and the milkman leaves two bottles like pale moons at my door; i keep one for coffee, one for the song you left humming in my sleeves.

tonight the piano is a streetlamp: i lean against its hammered ribs, strike a match of chord, and the dark inhales. a slow note walks downstairs, fumbles for its hat, and goes out to look for you. how many keys must i open before your shadow hangs its coat on my shoulder.

i took the streetcar to the river and back, because staying still felt like a lie. a boy practiced whistles on the platform; a woman mended her glove with blue thread; the city kept losing its balance and catching itself with a laugh. i wrote your name on the fogged window. it stayed only as long as breath does, which is to say—long enough to matter.

(i am learning the grammar of absence: commas for waiting, dashes for the sudden door, parentheses to keep the small animals of hope warm.) if you come north, i will tune the day to your steps. i will pin a ribbon of sky in your hair and call it afternoon. i will buy oranges from the italian grocer who believes in luck and spends it freely on strangers.

there is a rumor of sun climbing the stairwell. there is a rumor of your hand in mine. tonight i will set a cup on the sill for whatever weather your name becomes, and sleep with my shoes politely ready.

be gentle with the trains, be brave with your sleep.

always (and then some),

L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
