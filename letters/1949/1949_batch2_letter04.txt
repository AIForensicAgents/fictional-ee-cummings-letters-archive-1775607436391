on the rehearsal of a life (third act, no curtain)

Date: 1949-10-13
Recipient: Etta—backstage
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1949
Themes: theater, work, friendship, city, vulnerability

dear etta,

the stage manager keeps time in his fist like a polite storm; when he opens it, minutes scatter across the boards and we go fishing with our faces. the ghost light burns its single unafraid eye on center; last night i whispered into it and it answered by refusing to blink. i think this is what courage sounds like: a bare bulb saying yes to darkness.

your dress for act two remembers some other century. it teaches your shoulders the quiet grammar of rain. when you step into the spotlight, the theater leans forward—rafters, dust, everyone—and the city outside becomes an orchestra of traffic, clarinets of streetcars, the grand drum of the el.

i've painted flats all day, blue upon blue, until the sky felt like a room we might afford. someone brought sodas; someone cursed at a box of nails; someone cried near the prop trunk and pretended it was allergies. we are a family that keeps inventing its last name. maybe that's why i stay: to practice belonging in costumes i won't own.

there's a man in the second row who never laughs but always breathes with us. i want to thank him for trusting the invisible machinery: cue lights, whispered counts, the inkwell of silence we dip our hearts into before a line.

i dreamt we took our bows to an empty house and it was the truest ovation—pure air clapping. you said, this is what it will be like when we are old, and i said, then let's make the emptiness busy with love.

as for me: i'm afraid of the morning you miss your entrance and the script refuses to forgive. i'm also afraid of not being afraid enough. between those doors is a hallway we keep sweeping. meet me there after curtain. i'll have two paper cups of coffee, and a pocketful of names for the moon over wabash.

break a leg is too violent for you, so—unbreakable silence to your breath, unshatterable light to your step.

with paint on my hands and you in my next line,

ada
