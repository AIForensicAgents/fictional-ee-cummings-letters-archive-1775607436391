the lake writes in moonlight

Date: 1949-11-02
Recipient: Lydia
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1949
Themes: love, regret, lake, reconciliation, night

lydia,

i walked to the water that refuses to be only water. the moon wrote its white telegrams and the waves tore them open without reading, like unruly cousins at christmas. i thought how we too open and open.

you left your scarf on my chair, an uncomplicated ghost. it smells of cedar and a laugh you gave me last spring. i wanted to return it, but returning is complicated and the scarf seems content to put quiet around my neck.

i have been rehearsing apologies in lowercase so they can fit through the keyhole of your anger. perhaps the real mistake was thinking love prefers clarity. it might be a fog that finally learns our names and then leaves anyway, out of politeness.

a gull shouted the wise stupidity of wind. i counted seven lights on the far pier and added us to them (unlit, but not absent). if you insist we were a mistake, let us be the kind that teaches the hand to hold water better next time.

should you wish, meet me where the breakwater starts to forget which side is shore. bring no verdicts. i will bring coffee in a thermos that cannot tell past from future. we will practice being weather together, just briefly, before the city wakes its iron into morning.

still here,
jacob
