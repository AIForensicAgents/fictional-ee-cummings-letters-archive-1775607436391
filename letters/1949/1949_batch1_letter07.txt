letter folded into a paper crane

Date: 1949-09-22
Recipient: Takeshi (who taught me the word tsuru)
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1949
Themes: friendship, resilience, cultural exchange, forgiveness, craft

dear takeshi,

i am folding this on the kitchen table that still keeps its elbows from 1942. the paper resists then remembers, like any body asked to fly. when i press the wings, your careful voice says mountain valley mountain, and there (suddenly) breath happens at the corners.

i brought persimmons from the market. their skins are small suns with patience. i think of the garden your mother carried home in her pockets when the world said no; how seeds outwait locks. sometimes we walk past the fence and the wind leaves respectful fingerprints.

we are not good at talking about certain years. still, last week you laughed so hard tea climbed out of its cup to listen. i decided then that silence can have furniture in it, and we can sit together on it without freezing.

in the afternoon japantown smells like newspapers warming their Japanese in the sun. we stood at the corner where the bus sighed and you taught me the difference between crane and heron (the patient bend of neck, the democracy of puddles). i wanted a word for the way light forgives windows and settles inside, but perhaps there isn't one and that is better.

here is the crane i promised: it contains a small apology for all the times language was a door locked from both sides. it also contains the map to tonight at eight, where noodles and laughter will be served by two ridiculous people who keep surviving on purpose.

with care,
mina

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
