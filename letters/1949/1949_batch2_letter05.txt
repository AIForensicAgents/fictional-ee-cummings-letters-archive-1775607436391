winter milk for a small star

Date: 1949-12-24
Recipient: My daughter-to-be
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1949
Themes: parenthood, winter, anticipation, tenderness, home

little comet—

the adobe keeps the night like a soft pocket, and the radio carries carols through the walls in a language even the kettle understands. outside, the sand has cooled into a quiet hardness (a desert is a hymn missing its loud parts). i have placed a bottle of milk on the sill to catch any starlight you might forget to pack. fathers are collectors of forgotten light.

your mother sleeps with a hand on december; each breath rearranges the room into anticipation. i arrange tiny shoes beside the stove (they haven't learned your feet yet, but they study). on the table: a bowl of cinnamon, a pencil with its mouth chewed from thinking, paper snowflakes that refused to be symmetrical and thereby told the truth.

you will arrive in a year that is learning to be less afraid. people are painting the ruins into houses. trains remember how to sing without carrying ghosts louder than luggage. when you ask about the world i will tell you it is an unfinished picture we hang anyway because we need something to look at when we're brave.

i have bought a small bell. when you like the morning, ring it. when you don't, ring it twice. we will honor both kinds of dawn. i have also learned a few lullabies, though i hum them poorly; forgive me in advance for all the ways i will be an amateur at love except trying.

if you are moonlight, i will be window. if you are thunder, i will be counting quietly until you finish speaking. if you are nothing i expected, i will still say yes with all the furniture of my heart.

come when you can. the sky has set an extra plate. the far mountains wear a shawl of blue, and i have a story about a coyote who traded his howl for a pocket watch and then had to learn time like everyone else. i think you'll like him.

waiting the wide way,

your father
