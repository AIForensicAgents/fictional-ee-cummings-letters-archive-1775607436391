music that walks home with me

Date: 1949-08-05
Recipient: Eloise
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1949
Themes: music, summer, desire, city nights, friendship

eloise,

the horn tonight peeled an orange of sound and handed me a quarter-moon slice. i put it on my tongue and for a second the river reversed. perspiration performed delicate cartography down the trumpet player's wrist; the map led everywhere at once.

the club smelled of smoke and sugar and last chances. someone laughed in B-flat. a woman in a red dress kept turning the room on its hinge until the door of midnight opened properly. i stood where the ceiling fan could keep my doubts from sticking.

when we stepped outside, the humidity introduced itself with perfect manners. even the lamplight sweated. the cobblestones told stories about hooves and heels, and somewhere a screen door apologized six times for being a screen door.

i wanted to write you immediately because the tune refuses to stop walking beside me, arm around my shoulders like a friend who doesn't ask if i am sad, only improves the sidewalk. if you were here, i would give you the chorus and keep the bridge, for i have always liked crossing things.

please come down before summer forgets our names. we will eat beignets and powder our grief with sugar, and the river will pretend to be slower while we learn its real speed.

yours in syncopated honesty,
sam
