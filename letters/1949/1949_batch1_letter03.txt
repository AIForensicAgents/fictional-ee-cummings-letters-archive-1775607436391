telegram i never sent

Date: 1949-03-09
Recipient: The Editor at Tide & Ember
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1949
Themes: art, publication, humor, form, patience

dear editor who arranges the weather of words,

i have prepared a handful of small storms (poems) for your desk. they arrive without umbrellas and disobey margins that never loved them. please do not correct the moon with a blue pencil. it has been publishing itself for centuries without permission.

i cannot promise capitalization. letters prefer to bow to each other here; the tallest are not the truest. i cannot promise nice endings either — the age creaks with unfinishedness. still, a sparrow leaped off my windowsill this morning and landed on air. i am willing to follow that editorial policy.

my typewriter has developed an affection for the letter e; i suspect it is common. the ribbon is a red river learning black. if you accept these little storms, know they were written by a person who keeps losing his shoes under music and finds them again under silence.

i will not send this as a telegram because urgency makes lies of adjectives, and i would rather be slow and accurate than quick and spectacularly mistaken. however, if you must have a telegram: STOP

please wear patience STOP the poems will be late but true STOP

respectfully, rebelliously,
— a contributor who trusts your margins more than his

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
