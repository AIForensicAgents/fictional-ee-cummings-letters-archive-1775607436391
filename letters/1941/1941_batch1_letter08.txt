Convoy syllables

Date: 1941-10-21
Recipient: Eli
Location: Halifax, Nova Scotia
Year: 1941
Themes: sea, friendship, work, waiting, sound, dawn

dear Eli,

morning lifts its gray shoulder over the harbor and the fog answers in slow grammar. ropes sleep on the pier like coils of difficult sentences; men step over them careful as editors. i keep tally for the office—numbers that smell of tar and newsprint—and dream of sails i never had the salt to grow.

a woman in a red scarf knits while the ships drink coal. each stitch looks like yes. the gulls argue on behalf of hunger, which is their occupation and perhaps ours too. sometimes, when the convoy horns speak in that deep alphabet, my chest replies without permission.

you once taught me to listen to engines the way some listen to rain. i try, and between the pistons and the patient clank i hear your laugh polished by distance. write soon and tell me if the sea is behaving; i suspect it is still that magnificent liar.

may the moon be a small lamp in your pocket,

— sam
