Desert with a hinge

Date: 1941-09-14
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1941
Themes: art, landscape, silence, love, weather, craft

dear Ruth,

a door in this desert opens on sky and then another sky, hinged only to breath. the adobe keeps its cool like an old story; lizards punctuate the margins with swift commas of green. i paint slowly because the light refuses to be rushed—it sits, it thinks, it becomes a hand.

your last note brought rain. i folded it twice and the thunder arrived exactly on the crease. after, the earth smelled as if it had remembered being a fruit. i walked to the arroyo and listened to stones practicing silence together (a symphony of almosts).

i miss the fuss of your hair escaping pins—small rebellions that taught my fingers politics. send one stray curl by post, if the post will consent. i would tie it to a nail in the studio and let the wind learn to write your name.

from a town made of whisper and sun,

— e
