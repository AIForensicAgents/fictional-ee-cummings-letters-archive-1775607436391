Blue ink on rush hour

Date: 1941-08-03
Recipient: Brother Theo
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1941
Themes: work, music, family, city, hope, labor

dear Theo,

the city shaves with a factory blade at five sharp. by six the avenues have their collars on and the lake throws a cuff of glitter at everyone without asking who we are. i ship crates by day (nails, bolts, whispers) and by night the saxophones on Maxwell Street repave my skull with good trouble.

you asked if i still keep church. i keep rhythm instead: two beats of doubt, one of grace, repeat. there is a diner with stools swiveling like small decisions; the waitress pours coffee the way a saint might pour light if she were tired and had rent due.

sometimes the trains write the horizon in metal handwriting and i imagine you walking out of that sentence, hat bent, grin corrected by weather. bring your old harmonica. the land here is very flat and could use a little curvature of brothers.

be stubbornly alive,

— j
