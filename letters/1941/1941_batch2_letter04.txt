a small green sunday in a war-colored year

Date: 1941-10-05
Recipient: E.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1941
Themes: autumn, study, tenderness, work, hope, invention

E—

today the elm outside the library rehearsed its golden grammar; leaves unspelled themselves one by one and landed correctly. i sat inside the gray thought of afternoon while the desk lamp admitted it was really a star trained to live indoors. students drifted past my table like quiet parades of hunger—books under arms, autumn in their collars.

on the river a crew cut the Charles into ribbons clean enough to dress a wound. i watched until the bruise of cloud learned how to forgive the sun. this is how the day decided to be: a question written in low light, and me, mispronouncing answers with a pencil.

there is a rumor across the bridge of a laboratory that teaches lightning to behave. it comforts me that someone is instructing the future in manners while i (clumsy tailor) try to fit words to the body of what i feel for you. your last note carried the smell of apples under it like a secret pocket. you wrote that the factory where you work has a bell which sounds like a kind of honest future. keep listening. every job, if loved properly, becomes a machine for making courage.

i keep seeing us in the smallest theater: a streetcar, your hand next to mine, a newspaper laughing and refusing to stay folded. when the wind remembers itself and comes for this town with winter teeth, i will send you a scarf the color of a fresh beginning. and if the year decides to be darker than sense, we will be our own lanterns—two stubborn rooms of light.

until i can count your eyelashes,
R
