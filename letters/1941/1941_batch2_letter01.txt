two a.m. window with a sparrow of steam

Date: 1941-01-27
Recipient: Mara
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1941
Themes: longing, city, winter, intimacy, silence, anticipation

mara—

tonight the radiator hums that miniature opera and the pipes remember how to be a river. outside, the street has put on its softest boots; snow teaches the city to whisper (every taxicab a little yellow thought, careful as a moth). i have been practicing the old trick of not breathing your name, yet my chest keeps writing it in white chalk on the dark of me.

i keep your scarf on the chairback where dawn can find it first. it knows the exact geography of your throat and refuses to forget. i boiled coffee until it turned into a small prayer, then let the cup go cold—because some prayers are better after they fail. on the sill, two letters from you lie like swans unlearning water; i do not open them, i only feel their weight which is a kind of reading.

this room has one light (a clumsy moon that forgot how to be sky and learned instead to be bulb). i have let the shade half-down so the neighbors cannot see the way your absence arranges my hands—how they make and unmake the bed as if invention could be a quilt.

the latest paper says the world is a match about to be struck, but here the matchbox is shut, and your laughter, remembered, is a lake with its own weather. if you were here, i would turn every knob of silence until music happened; we would listen to the radio not for news but for the shape of a voice that believes in us.

until your footsteps choose these stairs again, i will keep this night patient, like a violin unplayed and shining.

yours (if the dark agrees),
N
