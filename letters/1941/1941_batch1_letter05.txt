Neutral harbor, unruly heart

Date: 1941-06-12
Recipient: S.
Location: Lisbon, Portugal
Year: 1941
Themes: exile, sea, longing, travel, waiting, identity

dear S.,

lisbon is an ear pressed to the ocean. trams ring their bright coins along streets that tilt toward forgiveness, and the river wears a patient mouth. in cafés people fold their accents carefully as napkins; passports flutter like considerate moths around the lamps of rumor.

i rented a narrow room with a blue window. every dawn a fish market wakes it like a bell, and i am pulled from sleep by silver scales inventing noon. sometimes the wind carries a ship into my chest and ties it there with ropes of iodine and salt. i pretend to be a harbor but, beloved, i am mostly tide.

i have not posted the telegram i wrote yesterday: three words (come, come, come) arranged like a small staircase. i climb them alone and step back down, embarrassed at the view. still, if your feet remember this city, there is a café with two chairs conspiring under a jacaranda. i will be the late cup, the one that waits.

in the street a child is spinning silence on a stick as if it were sugar. i taste it and find your name hidden, bright and unreasonable.

with all the passports of my breath,

— r
