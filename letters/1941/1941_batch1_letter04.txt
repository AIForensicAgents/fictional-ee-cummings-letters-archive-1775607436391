Magnolia grammar

Date: 1941-05-27
Recipient: Aunt Livia
Location: Charleston, South Carolina
Year: 1941
Themes: family, music, south, news, memory, heat

dear Aunt Livia,

charleston writes itself this month with a fat white pen: magnolias dictating slow syllables to the bees, verandas correcting the punctuation of heat. i have taken to walking in the late afternoons when the humidity loosens its tie and even the cobblestones consider dancing.

i heard a trumpet slip out of the Blue Goose and lean against the streetlight smoking a blue idea. a boy, twelve at most, made the notes grin. he winked and the evening forgave itself for being ordinary. on my way back the radio inside a barber shop mentioned a place across the ocean where the maps are in bandages. the barber nodded to the static, as if it were an uncle who drinks too fast.

i wear your cufflinks sometimes, though they are too proud for my wrists. they say behave; i say perhaps. please send a recipe for the cake that rises like a hymn—i want to show these rooms how bread can believe in itself.

kiss the photograph of mother once for me; twice if the dust complains.

your unmanageable nephew,

— p
