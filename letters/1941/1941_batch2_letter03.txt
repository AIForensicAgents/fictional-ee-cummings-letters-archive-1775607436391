harbor letter with oil and gulls

Date: 1941-07-23
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Halifax Harbor, Nova Scotia
Year: 1941
Themes: sea, brotherhood, risk, work, quiet, home

tom—

the ships sleep like iron animals, tails of smoke tucked against their flanks. every rope has a story and none of them end in knots; they end in hands. my palms have learned the alphabet of tar and salt—if you pressed your ear to my skin you could hear the slow argument of the tide.

i am berthed on a freighter with a name too big for her paint. the mate says names should be whispered to water or it will take offense. we load barrels that smell like lightning kept in a cupboard, and crates that look ordinary until you notice how carefully the men walk around them. gulls edit the sky with their bright commas and the harbor answers in gray.

they tell us we will go east in a herd, ships nudging ships, escorted by patience and a few fast shadows with guns. at night the lamps wear their hats low. i have been told to keep my voice small, my cigarettes cupped, my worry folded. it is strange to be this young and already fluent in the quiet that danger teaches.

if you were here we would sit on the pier and let the boards speak splinters into our trousers. we would make bets on the weather and lose on purpose. i have a deck of cards and a book about whales. i will trade both for the sound of our mother's laughter in the kitchen, a kettle behaving like a miracle nobody needs to explain.

write to me at the agent's office and use fewer words than your heart wants. the sea prefers brevity. i will carry your letter under my shirt like a saint's small bone, careful as prayer.

keep the garden. tell the beans to climb brave.

your brother,
Sam
