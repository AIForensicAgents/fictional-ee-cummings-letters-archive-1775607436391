Semaphore of Peaches

Date: 1941-02-23
Recipient: Sister Alma
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1941
Themes: family, south, yearning, ritual, sea, memory

dear Alma,

from the porch the river lifts its green shoulder and winks at the moon. the ship horns talk in low syllables (a, o, o) like monks who forgot to grow old. i have nailed two palm fronds to the wind to make a signal you could read from boston: come.

today i shelled the afternoon like a pecan. inside the tough january of me there was a small february, and in that, your laugh (a clear bicycle bell). the grocer stacked canned peaches in a tower of syruped suns. i bought one—unscrewed—and let a yellow summer walk through the kitchen barefoot.

you asked whether the hymns still fit my mouth. they do, but differently: i hum them sidewise, with pockets full of salt. the radio tells a weather of far countries; even the gulls practice silence between wings. yet i am happy in the exact way a match is happy before it knows fire.

if you come, bring the shawl that forgets to be formal. we will sit with the moss and teach it scandalous words like joy.

with the impolite affection of tides,

— n

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
