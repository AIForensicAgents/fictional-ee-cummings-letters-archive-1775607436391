Sirens over tea

Date: 1941-04-19
Recipient: Isabel
Location: Bloomsbury, London
Year: 1941
Themes: war, resilience, love, city, sound, night

dear Isabel,

the siren returns the sky to its beginnings; we finish our tea before the sound finishes with us. a kettle knows discipline—boils, breathes, becomes a hush. we practice the same: cup, saucer, candle, down we go.

the warden tapped on the door with a knuckle the size of england. he peered in like a moon that forgot where to set. i smiled because your smile was busy in my pocket, unfolding light. belowground we trade names with shadows and someone tells a joke with both hands. even fear must laugh to prove it has a throat.

later the all clear arrived green and soft, like a verb with its shoes off. we climbed the stairs, each step a page. there was the small brilliant mess of morning on the table: crumbs, a wet spoon, your lipstick remarkable as a rose that learned arithmetic.

keep your mirror wrapped in a scarf when the blasts go wandering. i need it whole, for the day my face remembers your face the way a key remembers a lock.

faithfully unfinished,

— d

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
