Snow telegram (New York to the river of your hands)

Date: 1941-01-14
Recipient: Marian
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1941
Themes: winter, longing, city, distance, letters, imagery

dear Marian,

tonight the streets learn the alphabet of snow; each lamp pronounces an oo of astonishment and the el trains write their narrow thunder on the sky. i walk home with a pocket full of your name, which keeps escaping like a bird inventing air.

the radiator clicks three small prayers and the typewriter pretends to be a lighthouse; i press one key and a room leans toward you. if i look east (toward the river, that cold wrist) i can almost hear your wrist answering. distance is only a coat—buttons come off; we step through.

i bought oranges because the grocer smiled as if a sun were a plain thing. i peeled one and found inside the calendar of april. i will save it for your mouth. do come quickly, before the snow forgets its own whiteness.

meanwhile i fasten this letter to the night with a pin of breath. open carefully—there is a small weather inside.

always,

— a

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
