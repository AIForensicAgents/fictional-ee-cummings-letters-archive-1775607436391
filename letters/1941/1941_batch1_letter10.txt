After the radio broke open

Date: 1941-12-15
Recipient: M.
Location: New Haven, Connecticut
Year: 1941
Themes: grief, resolve, winter, hope, history, faith

dear M.,

on sunday the radio split open like a clock struck by thunder and all the hours ran out shouting. the street stood still; even the sparrows listened with their entire bones. since then the ordinary has worn a different hat—milk bottles at the door seem to volunteer for something bigger than milk.

there is fear, yes, folded in with the winter socks. and there is also an iron thread running through the breath of strangers on the trolley. i held the strap and thought: we are taller together, even if our hands shake.

i went to the chapel without a reason and came away with one. candles are only disciplined fires; they teach the air how to hope in small, repeated lessons. i will wait, i will work, i will not let your name unravel in the dark.

if the night grows too large, write me a room inside your pocket and i will climb in, knees and all. until then—the window keeps a little snow just in case you arrive suddenly and need to see my footprints learning your door.

always and always,

— a

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
