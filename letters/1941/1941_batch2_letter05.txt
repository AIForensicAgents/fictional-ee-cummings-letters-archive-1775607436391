after the sirens, sugar and quiet

Date: 1941-12-12
Recipient: Mother
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1941
Themes: wartime, family, fear, resolve, home, news

ma,

the radio poured out its black honey and the street answered with sirens learning our names. we turned the house dark the way you once turned a pillow cool—quick, certain, trying not to wake the part that dreams of being safe. even the canary learned to whisper.

the harbor blew a cold breath against the hills and every window remembered how to pray. i could feel the city practicing its balance, one foot in fear, the other flat in ordinary. after, when the lights were allowed back, the kitchen became a small republic of relief. you set the sugar bowl in the center and it shone like a white idea nobody could bomb.

all day men at the pier spoke to each other with their shoulders. the paper boys made their arms into metronomes. someone chalked a flag on the bricks and it looked like a door you could run through. there is talk of enlistments and ration books and my name hanging in the air like a coat waiting to be tried on. i am not brave yet, but i am practicing. bravery is just fear that decided to go for a walk while the kettle boils.

please do not let the garden obey winter too strictly. cover the young things with last month's newspapers; teach the earth to read headlines and still believe in carrots. i will come by on sunday and fix the fence so it no longer sounds like a tired mouth.

if the sky becomes a ledger, let our love be the line that refuses arithmetic.

your son,
Eli
