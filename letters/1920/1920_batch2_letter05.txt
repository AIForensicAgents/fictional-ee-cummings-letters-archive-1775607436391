when leaves invent silence

Date: 1920-12-01
Recipient: Josephine
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1920
Themes: nostalgia, lost love, winter, cityscape, yearning

Josephine,
this city walks on its own music and sometimes lets me borrow a measure. The chestnuts along the boulevard are busy remembering how to be fire. Cafes sip their customers and the waiters draw parentheses with their wrists. I am a winter hat among winter hats, yet the air keeps pronouncing your name with a soft French astonishment I cannot teach it to stop.

Do you recall the afternoon we grew taller by leaning toward each other, how the room became a narrow echo and then a cathedral. The bell still rings when I open certain drawers. In the mirror I practice the face of a stranger who has learned to be brave at the exact speed of a snowfall. He almost convinces me.

There is a letter I write on the back of my eyelids when sleep refuses. It begins with your particular shoulder as geography and moves quickly to weather reports no paper will print: sudden tenderness north northwest, scattered forgiveness by evening. I seal it with the old stamp of our laughter and wake to find my mouth full of cancelled kisses.

If you are well, the moon here owes you its tip. If you are not, I am sending a small army of minutes to hold your hands in shifts. Should we meet again, let the silence arrive first and place its leaf on the table; we will understand without translation.

Always arriving,
the citizen of our secret country

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
