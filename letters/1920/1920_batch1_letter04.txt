for Mother, with lilacs unbloomed

Date: 1920-04-27
Recipient: Mother
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1920
Themes: family, healing, spring, grief, forgiveness

Mother,

Today the market smelled of damp rope and lilacs that have not yet decided to be born. I bought nothing and came home with both hands full of April.

I have been untidy with my silences. You ask after church, after a sensible future, after that boy who did not return; I carry those questions like plates stacked too high, clattering whenever a door slams. Forgive my clatter.

The doctor says the lungs remember what the mind lets go, and sometimes at night I hear the ghost of a fever walking the stairs. But the window knows another story: sparrows rehearsing, ink drying without a breeze, the street-wagon clanging milk into morning.

If you will sit with me under the maple, we can invent together a language made only of nods and small cakes. I will bring a ribbon to tie around any sadness that refuses to behave.

Your daughter, trying to grow neat around her wildness,

E.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
