a small revolution of teacups

Date: 1920-04-23
Recipient: Mother
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1920
Themes: city life, hope, poverty, family, self-making

Dearest Mother,
I have found a room that is mostly windows and courage. The bed is admirably honest and the wallpaper is attempting lilacs with mixed success. The rent is a stern aunt but I pay her in folded bills and apologies. I have put our old blue teacup on the sill; when the sun arrives, it bathes there like a shy dancer. If home has a face, perhaps it is round and porcelain and slightly chipped.

You would laugh at the noises that practice downstairs: a piano with romantic intentions, a couple quarreling like two cathedrals, and occasionally the elevated train announcing that speed is a new god. I write at a small table that believes in work. Words come like pigeons, not especially elegant until they fly all together and suddenly the sky remembers how to be astonished.

I eat simply, and sometimes extravagantly (bread that cracks like a joke, oranges that bruise me with sunlight). Money is a cautious animal and must be approached sideways; I am learning. Do not worry. I keep a clean shirt for the future and a wild one for the present. When it rains, I read your letters aloud to the lamp, and it nods in all the right places.

Be well. Let the garden forgive me for my absence. I promise to send seeds of something impossible that nonetheless blooms here, between bricks and bravado.

Your child, with ink on every intention

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
