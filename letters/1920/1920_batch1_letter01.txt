a small address to the moonlight over Seventh Avenue

Date: 1920-01-09
Recipient: Clara
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1920
Themes: longing, city, night, anticipation, modern love

dear Clara,

this city invents midnight like a child invents a secret: with a palm over the mouth and a throb under the skin. i walked Seventh Avenue until the streetlamps leaned like tired lilies, and the el stitched iron syllables over my head. soon the bars will fold their bright wings and the polite thirst will pretend to be water; i think of you, sturdy as a lighthouse at the edge of Boston, unbuttoning the day from your shoulders.

i have painted you an absence on cheap paper. it is the color of a window after a train has already gone. if you tilt it toward the moon the emptiness shines.

write me a small word, any word, and i will grow it into a garden between my ribs. until then i carry your name where a handkerchief ought to be, pressed and ready for a sudden weather of tears or laughter.

ever your somewhat rumpled,

M.
