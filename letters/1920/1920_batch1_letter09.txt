dust and starlight from the High Desert

Date: 1920-10-03
Recipient: Esme
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1920
Themes: landscape, solitude, art, transformation, night

Esme,

The desert is a quiet grammar that refuses extra commas. Sky, earth, breath. That is the sentence. Yet within it, every stone revises me.

I went out at dusk and the mountains took off their distance and stepped nearer, like relatives arriving without suitcases. A coyote stitched laughter into the low brush and the moon unrolled its pale rug across our doorway. Even my shadow forgot to hurry.

I am attempting a canvas of thunderheads. They move like cities with their roofs undone. When it dries I will send it east in a crate of peaches, because all storms ought to arrive sweetly.

With dust on my cuffs and peace in my pocket,

Rafa.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
