river note with brass and thunder

Date: 1920-08-29
Recipient: Thomas on Lenox Avenue
Location: Harlem, New York City
Year: 1920
Themes: music, friendship, city night, rebirth, invitation

Thomas,

Lenox is a river and the brass boats keep coming. Tonight a cornet cut the smoke into ribbons and we tied them to our wrists. Miss B. sang a note that made the ceiling blink; even the bartender forgot the arithmetic of bottles.

I thought of you, your hands practicing a new geography across the piano, maps that will never be printed because they are meant to be traveled, not explained. Come north soon. There is a club behind a bakery where dough rises all evening and so does the room.

Bring your stubborn sorrow and let the drummer argue with it. He is persuasive. I am beginning to believe that rhythm is a kind of citizenship.

Yours in blue midnight and bread,

Cal.
