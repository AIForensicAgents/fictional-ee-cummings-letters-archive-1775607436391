minute of June collected in a pocket watch

Date: 1920-06-18
Recipient: Nora of the cornfields
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1920
Themes: city vs country, music, friendship, summer, yearning

Nora,

Chicago has learned to breathe in syncopation. At the corner, a trumpet teaches the night how to step sideways, and even the streetcars seem to blush. I keep your last letter in the pocket nearest my pulse; the prairie fell out of it when I opened it, a soft avalanche of wheat syllables.

I tried to explain to a cabman that fields are not emptiness but a choir; he laughed and said the city is a better song. He has not heard July move across your county like a slow gold train. He has not felt the polite hand of wind arranging a girl's hair as if it were a bouquet.

Come visit. I will set a chair on the roof. We will watch smoke write its shy alphabet against the moon and we will not be certain whether we are the poem or the paper.

Always your conspirator in weather and words,

J.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
