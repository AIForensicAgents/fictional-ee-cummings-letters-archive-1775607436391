from an upstairs window (midnight's postcard)

Date: 1920-02-11
Recipient: Eleanor
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1920
Themes: longing, winter, city night, intimacy, distance

Eleanor,
this room invents a hush the way snow invents a city, soft and deliberate. The lamp is a small tame sun and the paper listens, hoping I will not betray it with any honest word. Your name keeps testing the sill like a sparrow (urgent, stammering) and every time I reach to touch it the sparrow becomes weather.

Outside, the river lies awake with a kind of complicated dreaming; even the bridges have heartbeats tonight. I think of your laugh entering a room before you do and staying later than we should, how our hands are a conversation no dictionary will publish. The night coughs a passing trolley and the rails make a bright confession. My pulse answers, foolish and true.

I have been learning the grammar of distance, which is mostly a series of parentheses around a breath. When I walk, I carry you like a pocket watch ticking under my ribs. When I stop, you open and the hour spills into my shoes. If there is a religion I can keep, it is the brief brave prayer of your mouth against mine.

Sleep, if you must, between the commas of clouds. Leave a margin for my arrival. I will write myself smaller and smaller until I fit, a whispered letter folded into your palm.

yours (if stars permit),
the window's timid conspirator

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
