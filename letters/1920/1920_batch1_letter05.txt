harbor-salted love letter at dawn

Date: 1920-05-30
Recipient: Samuel (at sea)
Location: Boston Harbor, Massachusetts
Year: 1920
Themes: love, sea, distance, ritual, return

Samuel,

Dawn tipped its salt into the harbor and the gulls stitched white parentheses above a tug. I stood where the pilings remember every goodbye and practiced the bright foolishness of waving to no one.

When your letters arrive they smell of rope and mirrors. I hold them to my ear and hear a deck rolling, hear the punctuation of boots, hear the little church of the engine counting. If you could see me read, you would think I was praying. Perhaps I am, to a god of longitude and return.

Bring me back an ordinary thing: a pebble, a song half-hummed by a cook, a nail that used to be straight. I will pin it to the map of our kitchen wall and name it Harbor After Rain.

All love, braided and unbraided,

L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
