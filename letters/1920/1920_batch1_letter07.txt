Date: 1920-07-12
Recipient: Aunt Mabel
Location: Cleveland, Ohio
Year: 1920
Themes: suffrage, determination, family, civic hope, women's lives

Aunt Mabel,

They say Tennessee may be the hinge. I carry the headlines rolled like a telegram inside my sleeve, and every meeting feels like a kitchen where yeast is learning to rise. We pass around pins and biscuits, and arguments as warm as either.

At the last gathering, Mrs. Winter spoke of a ballot the size of a leaf and the size of a door. I thought of your hands doing the arithmetic of laundry for thirty years and wanted them to hold a small, stubborn oval that could tilt a country.

If the vote arrives, I will wear Sunday shoes on a Tuesday. If it does not, I will lace them tighter. Either way, I am learning the clean pronunciation of my own name.

Your niece, with ink on her cuff and hope in her hatpin,

May.
