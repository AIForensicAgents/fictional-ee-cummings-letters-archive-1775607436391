letter tucked inside a paintbox

Date: 1920-03-12
Recipient: Miss L. Durant, Fifth Street Gallery
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1920
Themes: art, ambition, city light, experimentation, invitation

Miss Durant,

I enclose this letter the way a painter hides a wild color beneath gray: for luck, for heat. The canvases lean in my room like sailors listening for tide. Their surfaces argue like clever children and I am only their slightly baffled teacher.

If you find a thin afternoon, come to the studio on Cornelia Street. The light there has a habit of confessing everything; dust performs its tiny theater and the chairs forget to be furniture. I will show you a series of small storms that dry into faces, and a portrait of the Hudson pretending to be an arm.

I do not promise goodness; I promise weather. And the honesty that happens when a brush forgets its manners.

With earnest pockets and ink on my fingers,

A. LeFevre.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
