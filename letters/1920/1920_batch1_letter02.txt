postscript carried by a snowbound train

Date: 1920-02-04
Recipient: Brother Eli
Location: Onboard the New York Central between Albany and Utica
Year: 1920
Themes: family, winter, travel, postwar quiet, hope

Dear Eli,

The conductor coughs the hours into his glove and we are stuck in a white paragraph between Albany and Utica. Outside, the farms are erased and rewritten by wind; inside, a baby practices the grammar of sleep while a stovepipe ticks like an old pocket watch.

I have your last postcard tucked near my ticket. You wrote that the soil is shy this winter and the plow refuses to be fooled. Come spring, you said, the ground will loosen its fist and return our dead buttons, our lost nails, and perhaps even a small courage we mislaid in 1918.

Brother, I am bringing you a sack of coffee and a joke not yet funny. When the snow lets us go I will step down with both. Save me a corner at the stove and the sound of the collie dreaming.

Your sister in transit and thaw,

Rose.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
