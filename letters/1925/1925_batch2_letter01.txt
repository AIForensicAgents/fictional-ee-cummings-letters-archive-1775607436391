the hush between streetlamps

Date: 1925-02-11
Recipient: M.
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1925
Themes: city nights, longing, winter, memory, intimacy

M,

this evening the avenues behaved like a choir that has misplaced its hymnal and must sing from memory. the snow (a careful whisper) powdered the hats of strangers until everyone was briefly kind. you would have smiled at the stoops, all stitched with little white commas, making the long sentence of the block pause and breathe.

inside me, a small elevator rises and falls whenever your name strikes the bell. forgive the peculiar machinery; i was not born with it, i had to invent it from rain and yesterday and whatever courage a thin cup of coffee can lend. i rode downtown and watched the windows invent new gold on the faces of trolleys. a boy in a paper cap carried roses that were learning to be dangerous. he counted them twice and the roses pretended innocence.

is it unseemly to say i miss the way you argue with light, as if it were a coat to be altered. you once trimmed the afternoon to fit our shoulders and we walked in it, precisely tailored, the sleeves correctly shy.

tonight i keep a lamp on the table and allow it to think it is the moon. i practice your name until the room acquaints itself with its echo. if i slept, the pillows would dream of your wrists; i remain awake for their dignity. somewhere a streetcar inhales, bells a small prayer, and becomes distance. i keep it.

if you are warm, be warmer. if you are alone, refuse it slowly, the way frost refuses a window when morning explains itself.

always

—A
