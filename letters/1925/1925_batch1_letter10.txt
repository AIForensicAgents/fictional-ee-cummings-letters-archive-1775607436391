midnight inventory (year ending while the kettle sings)

Date: 1925-12-28
Recipient: Mr. Pern, my patient printer
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1925
Themes: craft, printing, gratitude, year-end, labor

Dear Mr. Pern,

the fog has ink on its cuffs tonight. i suspect it has been lingering in your shop, eavesdropping on the compositors. i am taking an inventory of what we borrowed from the year and did not return: one rainstorm, overdue. a hatpin of anger, lost. six brave mornings, still warm. a rumor of glory. three mistakes i would like to keep.

Your fingers, all ten, dance the quadrille of letters. i swear the lead naps when you turn away. then—like any child—pretends innocence when you scold it back into lines. i love the moment when the first proof comes up from the press, shy and breathing, as if a small town just learned to pronounce its own name.

Please make room on the chase for a stanza that refuses to wear shoes. it tracks sand across the margin but promises to wash its feet before the binding. Also, if the italic dreams too loud again, let it. We owe it one opera.

As for payment: i have enclosed money stiff with good intentions, and a blue button from the coat of a woman who taught me the exact difference between almost and yes. Consider it talisman rent until i earn a better luck.

When the kettle sings it lists tiny saints—steam, tin, patience, grip. The window answers with a slow applause. If you are working late, lift your head once every hour and salute the unprinted night: the great white page into which we keep leaning our narrow ladders.

With gratitude and a pocket of vowels,

—S.
