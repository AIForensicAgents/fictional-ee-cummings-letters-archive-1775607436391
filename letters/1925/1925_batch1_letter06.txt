while the world is a gramophone

Date: 1925-07-30
Recipient: B.
Location: Atlantic City, New Jersey
Year: 1925
Themes: summer, music, love, ephemera, sea

B.,

the boardwalk spins us like a shell held to the ear of noon. Everything is recorded in salt—your laugh, the gulls’ grammar, my name mispronounced by the surf (deliciously). i bought a cheap umbrella with a red appetite and we let it eat the sun while we kissed in its mouth.

There is a girl in a striped suit selling postcards of the future. i chose the one where the moon has her hair bobbed and the skyscrapers wink like rascals. We ate peaches that practiced indecency on our wrists. a small boy pointed and announced, marriage! (we thanked him for his prophecy and bought him lemonade).

The bandshell coughed, then gave birth to foxtrot. You taught me a step i will forget by September, but the forgetting will be kind because it leaves a bruise with nice manners. Later, in the rented room with its one stubborn window, the fan made an honest wind of us and we became: two bold commas, pausing in a paragraph of heat.

If winter is a far rumor—let it gossip. Tonight the ocean lies on her side and hums, tired and perfect. i have written your initials on my elbow where the day bends easily. Come morning, the lifeguards will count the red flags and we will pretend the world is safe (long enough to believe it).

keep the gramophone turning. the song owns us but we don’t mind.

—A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
