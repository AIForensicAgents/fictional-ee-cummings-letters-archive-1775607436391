harvest of small silences

Date: 1925-10-14
Recipient: Marjorie (sister)
Location: Cedar Rapids, Iowa
Year: 1925
Themes: family, rural life, change, work, memory

Marge,

the fields have finished their sentences and stand there, satisfied period of stubble and sky. i helped old Mr. Halvorsen stack his shocks and he paid me in apples bruised by honesty. The radio coughs out waltzes at dusk. Mother hums anyway; it fits better than the news.

You asked about the city and i asked about your hands. We trade questions like quilt pieces and meet somewhere warm. Father has been quiet since the doctor left, but his quiet is a useful tool; he sands the evening until it is smooth enough for all of us to sit on. When he smiles there is a nail in it—work to be done, even for joy.

The little schoolhouse smells of chalk and wind. i read a poem to the children about a train that dreams it is a meadow. they laughed the way leaves do when the fence isn’t watching. After, one girl slipped me a note: i believe in the forward of things. i pinned it to my heart like a permission slip.

We will slaughter soon. forgive that word; it holds a blade. The necessary kind. i will hold the lantern and think of every tender thing that has fed us without complaint. Perhaps gratitude is the best knife—sharp and careful.

Come Sunday, bring your boy. i will teach him how to pocket a cricket without grieving it. And when the moon loosens her braid over the corncrib, we’ll count the crickets’ fiddles and call it mathematics.

write.

—R.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
