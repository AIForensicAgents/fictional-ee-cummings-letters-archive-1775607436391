night-train confession, car 7

Date: 1925-12-04
Recipient: Mr. Lionel Hart
Location: Pullman car 7, somewhere west of Newark
Year: 1925
Themes: travel, friendship, hope, performance, night

Lionel,

the conductor has the grave authority of midnight and the mustache of a trustworthy cloud. we are a procession of suitcases and sighs, clicking the country open like a long dark book. i promised myself a clean thought, but the window keeps offering stars and the stars have their own opinions.

we passed a town named for a saint i do not recognize; all its porches were lit as if expecting a carnival. the moon rode beside us a while, then pretended not to know us. i have been thinking of your stage, the curtain’s heavy throat, your voice stepping into a hush and teaching it manners. i have been thinking of the time you laughed in rehearsals and broke three sorrows at once. i should have clapped then. i am clapping now, softly, so as not to wake Ohio.

i write to admit that i want to be braver at joy, less provincial about hope. you made it look possible: courage dressed not in armor but in evening wear, with a tear in the seam and a flower in the pocket.

when we stop at Trenton a woman boards, holding a cake as if it were a passport. i decide, without asking, that it is chocolate and perfect. if there is a moral, it is this: travel with a cake or a song or a letter; border guards of the heart stamp those every time.

may the marquee spell your name correctly, and the applause find you home.

in motion,

S.
