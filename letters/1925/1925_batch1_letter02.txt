telegram heart (unsent)

Date: 1925-02-19
Recipient: Mr. Eli Hart
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1925
Themes: city, jazz, desire, restraint, night

Eli,

i wrote a telegram and then folded it until the words became small coins (spent only in the dark). it said: STOP. come quick my chest is a switchboard and every wire sings your name STOP. but i did not send it.

i walked instead down Wabash where the trains climb the air like brass animals. a trumpet leaked its hot moon into an alley. there is a way a saxophone leans that makes the street forget it is a street; we all become breath and blue. the waiter’s towel, the girl with her hat like a quiet bell, the boy who jokes with the snow—all of us are vowels of night.

you sat across last week under that green lamp. your hands translated the table into a patient geography. i nearly learned your language. then the drummer scattered rain on us and i drank too quickly from the cup of maybe.

forgive me this: the more i think of your mouth the more the alphabet misbehaves. periods turn into comets. commas grow spines. and don’t ask what exclamation points do (they’re obscene acrobats).

this letter will not be delivered by Western Union. it will travel inside the coat you forgot here. on cold mornings i wear it, and your absence grows warmer. should you ever return, i will give you this unsent urgency, neatly folded, and listen while the night sets its cufflinks.

(yours if listening is a kind of answer)

—L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
