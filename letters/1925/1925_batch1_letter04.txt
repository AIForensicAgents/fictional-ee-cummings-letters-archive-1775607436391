postcard from a slow river

Date: 1925-05-23
Recipient: Ruth (who keeps the shop of shadows)
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1925
Themes: travel, nature, friendship, ritual, time

Ruth,

i am writing from an afternoon that is mostly porch. The river turns like someone unbuttoning a story. Spanish moss hangs from the oaks—long sighs, long dresses. Even the flies are courteous here; they tap the air as though it were a door.

Your shop would be happy in this heat. All your mirrors would nap with one eye open. The lace gloves could learn the soft rebellion of magnolias. i walked past a baptism where laughter kept making little ripples on God. The preacher held up a dripping hallelujah and the sun applauded with its thousand gold fingers.

The hotel room smells of fans and patience. i drink sweet tea that tastes like a promise you intend to keep even if it ruins you. A boy sold me a paper boat and told me it never sinks on Wednesdays. Today is Saturday but i believed him anyway. One must keep faith with someone, if not oneself.

At dusk the insects start their glass symphony, and my heart joins as a shy triangle. If i send this to your girls—yes, the hats with their brave brims—tell them to save a space on the shelf for a cotton-white cloud. The sky keeps shedding.

When i return i will bring you a postcard of humidity. it will fog every pocket and we will laugh until your candle gives up pretending to be a star.

with a handkerchief of river folded for you,

—R.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
