the theatre of salt

Date: 1925-11-21
Recipient: Aurelia, stage-door
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1925
Themes: performance, desire, labor, city, intimacy

Aurelia,

after the curtain fell, i watched the dust make tiny actors in the footlights. They bowed to no one and everyone. Your throat still wore the glitter of labor. i wanted to press my mouth there and drink the part of you that believes even the last row can be rescued.

Backstage smelled of powder and nerves. The chorus girls unhooked their song and let it breathe on the chair. You sat, spine a brave exclamation, while someone unstitched your sorrow from the dress so it could be used again tomorrow. i have never loved you more than in that economy of stars.

We walked to the corner in the honest cold. Salt turned the street into a clever ocean. i carried your silence the way one carries a sleeping child—careful of corners, grateful for weight. You said, i am tired of pretending the kiss is in the script. So let us not pretend.

If the city asks who we are, say: two mouths rehearsing a scene with no audience but the radiator. Say: we have ticket stubs for our own hearts and will be seated now, please.

Sleep. i will sit in the wings of your dream and keep the lamps from blinking too fast. Tomorrow the matinee will eat you and spit you out shining. i know the trick. i am not afraid of it anymore.

—D.
