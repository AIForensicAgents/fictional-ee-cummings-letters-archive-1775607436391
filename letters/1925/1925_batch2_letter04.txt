A room with violets that refuse to wilt

Date: 1925-01-29
Recipient: Father
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1925
Themes: family, forgiveness, artistic calling, winter, memory

Father,

the snow has been deliberate all day, as if spelling my name in a patient alphabet. i thought of your handwriting—its measured weather, every letter a sound beam—and wished i could borrow that steadiness. i am writing to say i have chosen a crooked road and find it kind.

you asked last Sunday how one earns bread by courting air. i did not answer well. here is the answer i meant: some days the canvas is stingy and hoards its white; other days it offers a mercy you would call a dividend. i trade in mercies. i deliver them wet and shining and hope the world signs for them.

i keep violets on the sill. they have refused to wilt all winter, perhaps because i water them with questions. do i disappoint you. is disappointment simply love wearing its work boots. when i try to measure myself against your silence, the ruler becomes a ribbon and i cannot read it.

please forgive the way i stand outside the house i came from and ring the bell of it with pictures. i remember your hands catching my bicycle when i believed the street was a rumor. i remember you running alongside until belief learned to balance.

i am running now, beside my own uncertain frame, and sometimes i hear your breath keeping time. keep it.

Your son,

Nathan
