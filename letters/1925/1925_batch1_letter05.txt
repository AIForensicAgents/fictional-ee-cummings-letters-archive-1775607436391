small manifesto for the ordinary miracle

Date: 1925-06-11
Recipient: Self (folded into a pocket)
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1925
Themes: self-creation, modernity, language, permission, joy

dear you,

walk slower. let the cobbles teach your ankles the alphabet of balance. drink your coffee as if the cup had a pulse (it does). carry two languages in your mouth the way a child pockets marbles: not to own them but to listen to their roundness.

spend your afternoons where tables bloom. the waiter is a stern gardener; he trims silence so conversation can flower. when the rain finds you, do not run; stand and be translated. watch how the city puts on her pearls (puddles) and laughs at every taxi.

write without permission. especially from fear. especially from the tidy cousins who iron their sentences. let words find their wrong shoes and go dancing anyway. if a comma refuses to sit still, make room for its sway. we were not born to be furniture.

remember the pharmacist with spectacles like small moons who whispered, love is an experiment in gravity. believe him, but also believe the laundress when she says, love is soap and rinse and hang it out.

send letters to the person you will be on Thursday, the one with the blue ink on their thumb. tell them: do not apologize for joy. also not for sorrow. both are necessary instructions for the heart’s mechanic.

most important—carry tenderness like a contraband fruit in your coat. when the gendarmes of sense demand you surrender it, smile and eat it slowly, the juice naming your chin.

yours in the radical enterprise of breathing,

—me

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
