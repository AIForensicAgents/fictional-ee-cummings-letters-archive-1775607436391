saltwishes in january

Date: 1948-01-07
Recipient: Mira
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts, USA
Year: 1948
Themes: longing, winter, sea, art, distance, hope

dear mira,

winter drills its quiet into the shingles, and the gulls fold like handkerchiefs along the pier. the ocean keeps practicing the same blue syllable until it believes itself. i sit by the little kerosene stove (its breathing is a faithful dog) and paint what the window cannot keep—white dunes, a fence, a horizon exactly the width of our missing.

your last note smelled faintly of lilacs, or maybe i invented that, needing something sweet to hold between the teeth of january. the brushes clatter in their jar like thin bones, and i think of your laugh—how it broke into a room and suddenly every shadow remembered its manners. out here, the postman arrives as if he were the weather, sometimes rain, sometimes sun, sometimes none.

i walked the beach at dusk and found a bottle with nothing inside but delicious emptiness: i considered corking up a promise for you, but promises drown faster than shells. instead, i send a tide. if you place this letter against your ear and lean, you will hear it say: come back when your shoes have learned to fly.

send me a word, any word, and i will dress it in salt, teach it a small song, and let it parade along my windowsill until morning forgets to be cold.

all my unbuttoned january,

—l
