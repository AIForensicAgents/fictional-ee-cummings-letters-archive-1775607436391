switchyard gospel

Date: 1948-02-19
Recipient: August
Location: Chicago, Illinois, USA
Year: 1948
Themes: music, industry, friendship, restlessness, night, work

august—

the yard smokes and sings; even the rails hum like a low choir trying not to wake the snow. i loaded crates till my shoulders forgot my name, and all the while the switchlights winked their patient green secrets. nights here, every whistle is a confession and every boxcar a mouthful of somewhere else.

your letter arrived with smudged notes on the back—was the saxophone really that hot or just your breath making holidays in winter? i like when melody stumbles into honesty, just once, bleeds a little, wipes its nose on the sleeve of rhythm and keeps going. i kept two nickels in my coat to hear them click together, minor planets learning to orbit. that sound takes me to the bar on lake street where they still pour beer like found money and the floorboards tap answers to questions nobody asks out loud.

i dreamt of departure lines curled in a pocket like sleeping snakes. imagine, we are alive in a country of tracks; every morning a map sharpens its teeth. maybe i'll ride out one day without warning, my hat a quiet boat, my suitcase believing in miracles. if i do, remember to feed my philodendron and tell it not to worry about the sun—all the lamps here have good intentions.

play something that forgives me, will you? mend your broken reed with laughter and send me its splinters. i'll make a little altar from them on the sill, and we can pray to yesterday without bowing.

in smoke and friendship,

l.
