humidity psalm

Date: 1948-08-21
Recipient: Aunt Pearl
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana, USA
Year: 1948
Themes: heat, music, family, appetite, cities, ritual

dear aunt pearl,

the air here walks around wearing everyone’s perfume at once. the ceiling fan draws a lazy cross in the heat and pardons us for sweating. i have learned the exact color of trumpet at midnight: a gold that forgives mistakes and asks for more.

you were right about the beignets. i powdered my fingers and for one blessed minute it snowed in august. the river licks the levee with a big cat’s patience; the streetcar clacks through its prayers, beads clicking the rosary of rails. in the market, i bought peaches with bruises that tasted like poems too honest for magazines.

i am writing at a café where the waiter calls me monsieur and means kid. a woman in a red dress explained the weather to me by laughing. later we will walk past doorways that grow music like ivy. i keep thinking: if heaven has a front porch it will look exactly like this, slats feathered with talk, a pitcher sweating like a sinner.

how is your porch swing? does it still complain until you sing to it? kiss the boys for me and tell uncle henry that his hat would be considered jazz here. i send you a handful of heat, wrapped carefully—open it near the stove in january for a memory of now.

sticky and grateful,

l.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
