forge on the fourth

Date: 1948-07-04
Recipient: Sergeant Levin
Location: Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, USA
Year: 1948
Themes: memory, work, resilience, veterans, friendship, healing

levin—

fireworks tonight spoke the old artillery, only softer, and nobody ran when the sky broke open. i stood on the mill’s back steps in my blackened shirt, lunch pail a small planet tugging at my hand. inside, the steel glowed with its private dawn and i remembered what fear looked like when it had a uniform.

you wrote that sleep still arrives in pieces, a convoy of uncertain trucks. here too. i keep a chair beside the bed for whatever comes in the night; sometimes it is a dog with warm eyes, sometimes a boot full of river. we left our war in the attic but the crickets keep saluting.

the foreman says i work like a man building stairs to a place he will never climb, and he is not wrong. there’s music in the rivets if you lean right. the younger men joke loud so their ghosts cannot hear their real names.

i wish you were here—i would buy you a cheap beer and a kinder silence. we would watch the river move like a long apology and believe it. when the whistle goes, and the men stumble toward the future with their pay folded like fragile letters, i think of your steady hands fixing a stranger’s radio, turning chaos into station.

be stubbornly alive, sarge. write when your hands stop shaking or because they shake.

as always,

l.
