midnight inventory

Date: 1948-12-31
Recipient: Self (tucked under pillow)
Location: Detroit, Michigan, USA
Year: 1948
Themes: time, work, rebirth, solitude, resolve, winter

dear you—

the assembly line ticks like a winter watch and the snow tastes faintly of smoke and peppermint. the city has wrapped its engines in scarves and calls it celebration. somewhere a horn mistakes the hour and forgives itself by being loud.

year-end arithmetic: two worn gloves, one stubborn hope, three good books softened by pockets, seven letters unsent (send them), a mirror that believes me only at night. victories: did not become a ghost when invited; learned the first name of the river; remembered to buy oranges when sadness demanded salt.

resolutions (penciled, amendable): 1) breathe with the whole ribcage. 2) write as if grammar were a kindly aunt, not a jailer. 3) forgive the old love without inventing new sins. 4) mail the letters you draft at midnight. 5) be the first embrace, not the last apology. 6) stand in the snow and let it finish its sentence.

i am not brave, but i am stubborn about light. tonight at twelve, when the factories exhale and the church bells try on each other’s names, i will open the window and let the future in, even if it tracks mud on the floor.

sleep now, poor heart. morning has promised to arrive, and for once i choose to believe a promise.

—l

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
