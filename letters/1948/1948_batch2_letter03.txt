paris postscript in the margins of june

Date: 1948-06-10
Recipient: Aurelia
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1948
Themes: love, exile, cityscape, reconstruction, distance

dear aurelia,

paris keeps its promises the way a cat keeps sunlight: by sleeping in it, stretching, forgetting to return it. the seine practices its soft handwriting along the quays and every bridge is a wrist suddenly jeweled with boats. i walk until the map surrenders, until the streets turn to whispers and the whispers turn to crumbs i follow back to you.

in saint-germain the talk is serious and the coffee is too; smoke writes algebra in the air and nobody solves it. i sit with a window and learn your face from the faces passing, collars high, mouths folded against the wind. outside a cart delivers crates painted with distant stars; inside a girl sings a song with no vowels and all heart.

i think of your letters, their margins crowded with small republics of breath. do not worry for me. i have found a room with a clean square of sky and a clock that refuses to shout. the concierge is a mathematician of keys. when i climb the stairs my feet beat out a drum i think you would call perhaps.

send me a photograph of your balcony. i will send you the river in an envelope, folded twice, still wet at the edges. june wears a dress the color of waiting, and yet at night the city unbuttons itself and the moon says well, then. if i close my eyes i can hear you laugh from an ocean away and the distance becomes a bridge that knows our names.

à toi, always in lowercase and light,
r.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
