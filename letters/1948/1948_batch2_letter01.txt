morning pocket of snow, to m.

Date: 1948-01-17
Recipient: M.
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1948
Themes: longing, winter, city, music, intimacy

dear m,

the city shakes its pockets of snow and out falls a nickel of light. i spend it on coffee and courage. the elevated rattles like someone's bones learning the latest tune, and my breath writes fog on the shopwindow which spells your name, shy as a moth.

this morning the typewriter coughs, then sings. between ink and thumb i keep a small republic of hope where vowels are warm bread and every consonant has a door. if you were here i would open the door marked now and let the day come tumbling in, socks unmatched, laughter unshaved, the two of us citizens of a bright disorder.

last night on fifty-second a trumpet unbuttoned midnight; we stood at the edge of the roof and the sky took off its hat. a paper blew across tenth street like an escaped letter, maybe ours. i followed it until the wind remembered some other appointment and i was left with only the discipline of wanting.

send me a postcard with a stamp of your pulse. i will answer with a little calendar of touch, every square a yes. winter keeps licking the fire escapes but i have taught my hands your alphabet and they do not forget.

yours (in the fragile grammar of snow),
a.
