fog catechism

Date: 1948-10-14
Recipient: Noah
Location: San Francisco, California, USA
Year: 1948
Themes: modernity, art, friendship, cities, reinvention, night

noah—

the fog here is a patient animal: it pads in on enormous feet and sits in your lap until you remember to be quiet. the bridge is a red syllable held too long, and every gull writes its name on the air without caring for spelling.

i have found a basement where poems sweat and the chairs know how to listen. the coffee is a new religion; the congregation recites from chipped mugs and the hymns are mostly mistakes that decide to be beautiful. i read three pages and discovered that my voice had elbows. afterward, a stranger pressed a notebook into my hands as if it were bread.

after midnight the cable cars ring themselves back into dreams, and the storefronts breathe like tired saints. if you were here we would walk columbus avenue with our collars turned up against philosophy and discover an alley where someone has painted a door onto a wall. i would knock anyway.

send me news of your north: do the leaves there still quit their jobs in october? i miss the honest grammar of frost. but here the ocean keeps tutoring me in generosity, and i am trying not to be a difficult pupil.

in fog and forward motion,

l

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
