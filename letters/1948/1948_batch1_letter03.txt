seine in borrowed verbs

Date: 1948-03-12
Recipient: Ellen
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1948
Themes: exile, spring, language, healing, cities, yearning

ellen—

march has a way of standing on tiptoe here; every plane tree is an almost, every café cup a small moon rehearsing light. the seine moves like a quiet animal remembering its original country. i walk beside it with pockets full of coins and untranslatable sighs.

i am learning to speak with my hands: american cigarettes traded for verbs, a grin for conjugation, shoulders for punctuation. the bouquinistes open their green boxes—long rows of rescued sentences breathing again. i bought a book whose margins were full of strangers; i love how a city leaves fingerprints on paper and calls it history.

there is less hunger and more color every week. the baker wrapped a loaf as if he were bandaging a wound, and when he smiled the morning grew two sizes. i wanted to send you a slice through the envelope, but the crumbs would arrive as snow and you would call it weather when it is, in fact, a poem about hunger ending.

your name fits into this place with a softness that surprises me. perhaps it is the way your vowels lean against the cold without apology. write soon, and tell me if the elm by your window remembers me (i tied a thought to it, somewhere under the second branch from the left).

i'll come back when i have enough french to say: stay, and enough silence to mean it.

with my pockets lit by little suns,

l
