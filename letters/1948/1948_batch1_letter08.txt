breadcloud letter

Date: 1948-09-28
Recipient: Isolde
Location: Berlin, Germany
Year: 1948
Themes: survival, hope, division, gifts, sky, tenderness

isolde—

a plane stitched a loaf to the day. from my window i saw it fall like a soft decree, and the children ran with their hands already remembering yes. the city wears bandages beneath its coat, but the eyes here do not apologize for looking. when you smiled at the coal line the gray had to make room for teeth.

i keep a tally of your courage in my pocket: chalk marks that refuse to be rained away. last night we counted the seconds between engines as if they were a strange constellation—corridors of cloud where mercy travels. how is it that bread can be an alphabet? i have learned at least the first letter.

i cannot hold your hand across the wire, so i send buttons of words, small and bright, to fasten your coat against the cold that keeps asking for more. i dream of a future where streets learn to pronounce your laugh correctly and fences retire to the country to become harmless.

if you find a crumb in this envelope, keep it. it is not from the bakery but from a thought i broke in half to share. tell me about your window—what it forgives, what it refuses.

from the other side of sky,

l
