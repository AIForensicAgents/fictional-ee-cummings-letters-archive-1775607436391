rooftop republic

Date: 1948-05-30
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Brooklyn, New York, USA
Year: 1948
Themes: love, city life, summer, anticipation, ordinary magic, domesticity

ruth—

the stoop grew warm enough today to sit like saints and let the street anoint our ankles with dust. up on the roof, the laundry made small white politics with the wind; each shirt voted to be a sail, and for once nobody argued. we ate peaches over the tar and our shoes learned to be boats.

the radio coughed up a ballgame, then a jingle about toothpaste rescuing america. i switched it off and we listened to the city’s heartbeat instead: a truck groaning, a neighbor laughing, a baby upstairs rehearsing for opera. i keep a chair for you beside the pigeon coop—a throne of splinters but true. when you sit there, the skyline behaves itself.

your letter last week carried a photograph—your smile trying on the brim of june. i’ve placed it in the sugar bowl; every morning i sweeten my coffee with your almost-arrival. will you come before july melts? i have saved a shadow for us at coney, a cotton candy cloud with our names spelled wrong in blue, a rollercoaster that admits fear but not defeat.

i know you are careful with happiness, the way a seamstress is with light silk. i promise i will hold my laughter with both hands and not spill it. come up the four flights and knock twice; the door sticks when the day is hot.

expecting you the way a window expects evening,

l
