desert telegram to father

Date: 1948-04-25
Recipient: Father
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico, USA
Year: 1948
Themes: family, independence, landscape, forgiveness, artistic calling, expectation

dear father,

the desert has a clock made of shadows; you can tell the hour by where the cactus confesses. i rented a room where the ceiling fan whirls like a thought nearly catching itself. i brought the typewriter, which coughs vowels and hammers consonants like a blacksmith making horseshoes for invisible hooves.

i know the house you built for me: ledger lines, steady tools, a porch with room for duty. there is honor in its lumber. but i have chosen a door with no hinges. the sky here is a lidless eye and i am trying not to blink first. i will be poor as kin and rich as light, which is to say i will eat beans and astonishment and write until my wrists remember another life.

today a storm arrived across the mesa as if god were slowly shredding a dark letter. it passed without spilling anger and left behind the good ache of almost-rain. forgive me for not becoming your blueprint; forgive me for wanting to be your unexpected son instead of your finished one.

send news of the lilac bush and the stubborn lawnmower. kiss mother on the forehead where worry keeps folding its traveling clothes. tell her the mountains say hello in a language we both understand.

from your wayward but listening boy,

l.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
