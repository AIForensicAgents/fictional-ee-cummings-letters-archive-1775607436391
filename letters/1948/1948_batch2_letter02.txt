thin spring letter to my brother

Date: 1948-03-29
Recipient: Eli
Location: Des Moines, Iowa
Year: 1948
Themes: family, spring, homecoming, work, memory

dear eli,

the thaw comes on like an apology and we accept it because we are polite to weather. the field, which yesterday was a question, today is a sentence running on and on. the tractor coughs itself brave, hens begin their slow applause, and i stand at the fence learning the old arithmetic of mud plus boot equals home.

i write you because silence grows weeds when unvisited. since you went west with your boots full of miles, mother saves you a chair at supper and the chair keeps getting better at listening. father pretends the radio is broken, which is to say he does not like the news, which is to say there is too much world for one table.

you asked if the barn still holds thunder. yes. in the loft the hay keeps the shape of last july; a pitchfork leans like a tired soldier; a swallow stitches dusk to the beam with its flicker of needle-wings. i oil the gate and the hinge says amen in that old iron tongue.

we are not who we were before the loud years, but the soil is patient and teaches by repetition. plant. wait. believe. even the scarecrow, button-eyed philosopher, nods in agreement with the wind. i count the seeds like small bright futures and tuck each into its quiet bed.

write soon. send me the color of your mornings and the names of your new streets. i will mail you a jar of the river's smell and a handful of last year's lightning i found asleep under the porch.

your sister,
d.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
