airlift for a single heart

Date: 1948-08-02
Recipient: Captain Haines
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1948
Themes: hope, war aftermath, friendship, solidarity, airlift

dear captain haines,

your last note arrived with a smudge of coal on the edge as if the envelope had flown in formation with engines. i pressed the mark to my cheek and it sounded like courage. across the radio, their voices say corridor and tonnage, but i hear only verbs with wings: bring, carry, keep.

i walk along the charles and practice sending bread by thought. gulls rehearse silver commas above the water and i imagine them stitching the sky where history tore it too quickly. in the newspapers the photographs are black snow, aircraft like quiet questions dropping their unmistakable answers. i cut one out and tape it over the draft that was my fear.

what can a civilian mail to a city underlined by hunger. stamps, yes; prayers, of course; also these: a recipe for dawn involving one window, two hands, and the willingness to open. a blueprint for laughter to be assembled with simple tools. an alphabet with extra vowels for astonishment.

when the engines fall asleep you say the silence is loud as a hangar. i believe you. here, crickets propose impossible treaties and the river signs them anyway. tonight i will place a slice of bread on the sill and tell the stars to carry it east. if they refuse, i will teach them the word please in german and in rain.

hold fast to the small arithmetic of kindness. one loaf plus one landing equals a city breathing. write when you can; i will be awake with the porch light, an amateur lighthouse blinking yes, yes, yes.

with fierce ordinary affection,
m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
