grain moon & other troubles

Date: 1935-02-19
Recipient: Brother Eli
Location: Omaha, Nebraska
Year: 1935
Themes: family, dust bowl, survival, hope, rural life

eli,

the fields have learned a new word for empty. it begins in the throat like a cough and ends as the blind scrape of wind along the porch. i sweep the kitchen and the broom gathers a small brown nation that never stops migrating. our boots subtract the road, mile after mile, and still the sky is the largest silo we cannot fill.

i have stapled prayers to the barn like wanted posters for rain. the radio crackles out the day's electricity of voices; sometimes they speak of far cities where the dust is a story other people tell. i cannot imagine a city that does not taste of grit between teeth. even coffee here has a horizon in it.

as for father, he keeps rearranging fences, as if the wind were a stray we could pen. mother folds and unfolds the same apron into a bird that will not fly. at night she hums and the walls lean in to be mended by her hum.

you asked if i was angry. i am not, or i am, but it is the kind of anger that plows furrows and waits for something to answer. last week a single cloud took the afternoon hostage, and every calf lifted its wet joy. we stood very still, as if movement might frighten weather.

i have hidden one handful of wheat in a jar by the stove. it is a promise i keep on the low shelf where we keep our salt and small courage. write soon. send any spare syllables your city does not need; we will plant them along the fence and teach them to be green.

your sister (stubborn as fencepost),

m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
