blueprint of a summer (with revisions)

Date: 1935-06-21
Recipient: Ruth at the WPA studio
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1935
Themes: art, work, friendship, summer, hope

ruth,

our mural has learned to perspire. the wall sweats a little generosity each noon, and we mop it back in with bristles and patience. the foreman whistles in a square key; i think he is building a scaffolding of sound so we do not forget to climb.

you mix blue as if you were writing a letter to the harbor. i keep misplacing my pencil inside the shadow of a worker's shoulder. today a woman pushing a pram stopped and stared; i watched her lips move as she counted the brick-work we had already invented. perhaps she was measuring if her life could fit inside the picture and rest there a while.

there is talk that the money will thin. we paint faster, not from fear, but from a desire to outrun the ordinary and meet it on the next block, breathless and laughing. when i load my brush it feels like lifting a small, wet vow. i try to keep each stroke polite but not shy.

meet me after, where the trolley sighs and the street vendors pin steam to buns like medals. i will bring the paper cup we stole from the breakroom (the one that says property of nobody) and fill it with lemonade and the end of the longest day. the blueprint says: roofline, ladder, sun. i add, secretly: you.

until the wall learns our names,

d.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
