snow telegram (for your quiet wrists)

Date: 1935-01-07
Recipient: Agnes
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1935
Themes: longing, winter, city, distance, memory

dear agnes,

this morning the city unbuttoned its coat and shook out a million brief white syllables. i thought of you (your wrists the hush of small bells) and typed a telegram i never sent: stop. i am living between breaths of steam. stop. sidewalks bloom with mufflers and soft oaths. stop. your name keeps happening under my tongue.

the el stalls and we sway like church lamps. i count rivets and people not looking at anyone. a boy spits his penny-luck into the drift; it disappears immediately and forever. do you remember how winter taught us to listen? the snow taking the loudness from the streets and handing it back as a secret. i wanted to hand you such a secret today.

i have been painting the window with the heat of my hands, inventing a geography that ends exactly where your shoulder might begin. (geographies, like promises, are truest when smudged.) the typewriter is a black dog at my feet, tired from chasing commas. its paws leave ink on my socks.

when i think of you walking, i imagine the snow clinging to your coat the way vowels cling to the bones of a word. come home to a tea that keeps missing you. to a chair that keeps remembering your shape. if you cannot, then send me the lowercase of your laughter, and i will pin it to this day like a small brave flag.

always (in mittened breath),

i
