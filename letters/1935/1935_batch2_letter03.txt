color writes me before i wake

Date: 1935-06-07
Recipient: Mother
Location: Montparnasse, Paris, France
Year: 1935
Themes: art, exile, longing, modernity, city, family, hunger

dear mother,

the morning opened like an envelope and inside was the seine, folded twice and tied with a barge. i eat bread that cracks like laughter and paint with a thrift that would make you proud: three colors and an argument. paris is a city that keeps rearranging its face in every puddle; i try to memorize one of them and the rain writes another.

the studio is a roofroom with a window that thinks in clouds. when i mix blue with the absence of blue, a coin of summer appears on the palette. i chase it with the brush like a boy after a hat on a windy street.

at the dôme café men speak in rectangles and women in spirals. someone says tradition as if it were a lock; someone else says future like a key made of smoke. i keep quiet and draw their hands—how they argue even when the mouth rests.

i miss your kitchen's geography: stove north, table east, breadbox twenty minutes from patience. do you remember how i used to line up peas into constellations? now i place small squares of color side by side until they begin to hum, a weather only i can hear. the canvas teaches me manners; i bow and it bows back, and suddenly i am allowed to be more honest than speaking permits.

money is a small and particular worry. i sell a sketch to an american lawyer who says he misses baseball more than his wife. i do not know what to say to that, so i draw him a ball that refuses to be caught.

mother, keep the letters coming. the smell of your ink is a road home. when i paint the river at dusk, it looks exactly like your handwriting when you are almost out of light.

your son,
peter
