typebars & trumpets share a breath

Date: 1935-09-28
Recipient: Ruth Halpern
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1935
Themes: work, music, friendship, labor, city, optimism, feminism

ruth,

the office is a tambourine that forgot its music, but my typewriter still plays a stubborn rhythm—click clack, ding, the little carriage kissing the end of the line and turning back for more. the boss counts commas like coins; i hide one now and then just to prove a sentence can breathe.

at noon the street performs: shoeshine boy whistling scandal, a woman selling apples that remember october even in september, a man with a sign reading yes there is work—smiles paid daily. i buy a paper i do not need, fold it into a fan, and tell my heart to behave.

last night we found a basement that invents jazz. the trumpet leaned against the piano the way tired lovers lean at the elevated stop, and the drum kept interrupting—politely. i thought of you every time the clarinet climbed a fire escape and waved to the moon.

the union boys talk like matches, all strike and bright. i keep tea in my desk for them and for myself. small heat counts. you would laugh to see me standing on a soapbox that once held lemons, telling mr. pierce that a woman can carry both a ledger and a cause without dropping either.

write me the shape of your window's light at four p.m. tell me if the lake remembers my name. i am practicing a life that refuses to be afraid of its own shoes. meet me saturday by the bridge with your red scarf—the one that turns wind into a sentence i can finally read.

love,
may
