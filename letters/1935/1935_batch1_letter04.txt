a lamp against the wind

Date: 1935-04-11
Recipient: Mr. Asa Mallory, lighthouse keeper
Location: Marquette, Michigan
Year: 1935
Themes: love, navigation, work, light, devotion

mr mallory,

if anyone asks, tell them the fog is a guest that overstayed its tea. it has set down trunks in the yard and hired the gulls to sing to its laundry. yet, even so, your light keeps chiseling a room out of the weather, a faithful geometry against unfaithful gray.

i have come to love your steps (eleven up, seven along, eleven down) by the way the boards answer our weight. when you wind the lantern it is as if you were telling the dark a bedtime story (and the dark, being a child, pretends not to listen). i am learning the names of the shoals the way i once learned prayers: with my breath caught like a candle awaiting match.

last night the horn spoke in a language of whales and widows. i put my cheek to the glass and felt it say, i am here, i am here, foolish sea, behave. the lake does not behave; it only remembers. your hands smell of oil and patient fire. you lift the lens with a tenderness i recognize from church and kitchens.

do you know what i want? to be the reason a far boat corrects its course by the width of a heartbeat. to place this letter under your plate and have it light your supper. perhaps that is too forward. but then again, the horizon never apologizes for its straightness, does it.

write soon. or stand at the gallery and tip your cap at three o'clock sharp, and i will pretend the gulls are delivering your yes.

with salt in my shoes,

l.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
