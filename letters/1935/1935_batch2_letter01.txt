ink-blue midnight between streetlamps

Date: 1935-01-23
Recipient: Samuel Bloom
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1935
Themes: city, performance, family, longing, survival, winter

dear samuel,

between the el and the moon the street makes a blue sound, like a trumpet learning to whisper. my silver shoes remember thirty-six steps and one brave leap, but my feet remember you and the orchard and how the grass said hush when we fell into it.

tonight the theater coughed light onto us (a hundred warm coins). we danced for the men with collars like fences and for the girls who stitched their courage under their dresses. afterwards, someone passed a glass of legal gin—funny how a thing tastes different once it doesn't have to hide. i only wanted water though; the kind mother kept in the blue pitcher, poured as if it were a song.

the subways breathe like animals. sometimes i close my eyes and let their iron lungs carry me to another self: the brave one who writes her name without blinking. but then a stranger's newspaper folds into a paper knife and reminds me the world is sharp—breadlines, bankers, a city polishing its teeth with nickels.

still, i am learning the bright grammar of survival: rent is a verb, hunger an adjective, and you are the noun that keeps me. send a postcard of the old house if it still stands. send dirt. send a piece of quiet. i will pin it inside my coat.

when the orchestra falls asleep, i walk home under lampposts that hold their breath. the sky has buttons and i undo them one by one until a star looks at me the way we used to look at peaches in june (bless them, soft as fugitives).

i am not lonely; i am just far. tell father that the city is a patient animal and i am learning its language. tell yourself that i keep a seat for you in every empty row.

your sister always,
lila
