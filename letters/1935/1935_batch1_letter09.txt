magnolia & ink

Date: 1935-10-04
Recipient: Miss Ada Finch
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1935
Themes: romance, southern gothic, nature, anticipation, quiet

ada,

i write from a porch that has memorized the afternoon. the magnolia waits like a careful grandmother, loosening one perfume at a time so the day does not faint. the air is a rumor that keeps proving true. my paper curls at the edges as if listening closely.

the postman and the lizard share a leisurely philosophy. i envy them. the city has decided to walk slowly past itself and admire the fit. down the street a gramophone is practicing forgiveness for a scratch it will never heal. i sit here and find that i am not unhappy; i am something else, less known, perhaps a verb.

your last letter made a small thunder in my chest. you said: come when the leaves begin to rehearse their nobler exits. i will. i will bring a suitcase full of almosts and one dress the color of a patient horizon. if you do not like it, you may send me back on the noon train, but i think perhaps you will not.

i dream we walk through the cemetery and read every epitaph aloud so the names can be living a little longer. we will speak softly and carry a thermos of lemonade that believes in reincarnation. if the sky breaks into polite applause, pretend not to notice; magnolias are shy with their joy.

until then, press your palm to this page. i will measure a glove to fit it. the glove will be weather.

affectionately,

s.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
