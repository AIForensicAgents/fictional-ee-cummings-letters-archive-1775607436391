prayer stitched in red thread

Date: 1935-05-09
Recipient: Mamá
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1935
Themes: family, home, memory, culture, tenderness

mamá,

i sewed our afternoon into the hem of my skirt so i would not misplace it. the chile simmered like a small sun with manners, and the courtyard swallowed and returned every word we said, softer, as if the air had lined its pockets with our vowels.

i have begun a habit of talking to the tin saints you keep on the window. each has a dent like a memory. the little one with a horse listens best. i told him i am practicing the art of patience and he flicked a ray of stubborn onto my cheek. thank you for teaching me how to wait without going cold.

the market was a song of apricots and dust. i walked home through it with my shawl arguing sweetly with the wind. in the plaza a boy balanced a drum on his knee and made the afternoon remember its heart. sometimes i think the sky here is a blue that has learned how to kneel.

you asked if i still dream in spanish. i do, but the dreams wear different shoes now. they cross a street full of newspapers and stairways and they still arrive with your voice around their shoulders. i am not sad (or i am, but it is the good kind, like rain that knows your name).

i send you a packet of seeds labeled courage, and another labeled finally-enough. plant them by the door. when they bloom, bring them to the table and we will eat.

con todo mi amor,

rosa

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
