powder-sky prays in my lungs

Date: 1935-04-15
Recipient: Marta Reyes
Location: Cimarron County, Oklahoma
Year: 1935
Themes: dust bowl, love, migration, resilience, radio, weather, hope

marta,

yesterday the afternoon turned to night at three o'clock and the cattle forgot their names. we stood with wet handkerchiefs over mouths as the black rolled in—april teaching us a new alphabet made of grit. the radio said stay inside and i did, but my heart stood out in the yard with the fenceposts, counting seconds between lightning and the windows shaking.

there is dust in my pillow and in my coffee and in the gospel. i sift it out of psalms and find seeds there, stubborn commas that refuse to end the sentence. when i climb the windmill i can hear kansas talking beneath the roar, a woman voice saying hold.

your last letter smells like lilac and train smoke. i read it slow, as if it were a clean cup of water i must make last till harvest. you tell me dodge city has work if a man will sweep the sky from his shoes before entering. i am not proud; i will sweep.

i fixed mr. garza's radio and for a minute we caught chicago—trumpets like lit matches, some fellow making promises to a crowd i could not see. the aerial stretched against the storm like a prayer-rod and i felt foolish and saved at once. if there is a god he must be the thin wire that lets a voice arrive.

please save me a small room with a window that faces anything green. i will bring my harmonica and the tin rooster that still turns in the wind (even when the wind is made of knives).

marta, marry me when the road stops crawling. i have two hands and both of them remember how to be gentle.

elias
