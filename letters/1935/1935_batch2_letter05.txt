the lighthouse keeps remembering you

Date: 1935-12-11
Recipient: Captain Aaron Field
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1935
Themes: grief, sea, memory, duty, hope, winter, light

captain aaron,

the light turns and turns, a slow eyelid keeping watch over the herring and the gossiping waves. december gnaws the railing; the paint answers with flakes like old wedding rice. i sweep it into my pocket as if it were permission to remember.

i polish the lens until it tells the truth. gulls sign their rough names on the weather; the bell-buoy argues with the fog and then forgives it. some nights the sea prints your shadow on my wall and i nod to it like a neighbor.

they said your ship went down like a sentence that chose not to finish. i argue with that ending—quietly, with tea. in the ledger i write: visibility fair, hearts variable. i date the entry and the page warms a little, as if paper believed me.

once, at two in the morning, i saw a tiny light moving east to west, trembling like a boy on his first dance floor. i answered with our own bright circle and felt the lighthouse stand a little taller—as if the tower had bones and they'd remembered joy. i thought of your hands explaining knots to me, the rope behaving like a serpent charmed into usefulness.

if you are living, come by the long way and i'll hang a blue towel from the gallery. if you are elsewhere, let the storms be kind and the kelp refuse to tangle your feet. i will keep the mechanism wound and the wick trimmed. the ocean is a large silence; i fill it with your name until dawn.

yours,
nora
