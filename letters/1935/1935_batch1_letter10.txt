year-end with radio snow

Date: 1935-12-28
Recipient: Vasya
Location: Brooklyn, New York
Year: 1935
Themes: friendship, winter, immigrant life, work, hope

vasya,

the radio is full of weather pretending to be news. it snows in the speaker, and someone far away clears their throat and the whole room becomes a railway platform again. i turn the dial and arrive briefly in warsaw, then cleveland, then a barn where a fiddle has survived a century and is trying to pass it on.

i kept your scarf on my chair all autumn. it learned the curve of my absence. tonight i tied it around the window latch and the wind danced with it until both were out of breath. i think of you on the night shift, counting rivets like constellations, your hands loyal to a furnace no one will ever write a poem for.

we are not poor, or we are, but this poverty keeps a tidy house and tells jokes about its own elbows. the landlord's cat has decided we belong to it. i accept. i have never been properly owned by anything that purrs.

year's end is a kind of polite door. i stand in it and bow to what won't follow me. i bow also to what will. come new year, bring your boots and a stubborn cake. we will listen for midnight on the radio and add our quiet to the nation's. i will shake the snow-globe of my head and wish you visible happiness.

if you cannot come, send a nail from the factory and i will hang the moon on it, over the stove.

with a warm pocket and a spare match,

n.
