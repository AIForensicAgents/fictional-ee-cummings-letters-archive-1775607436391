postcard without stamp (addressed to later)

Date: 1935-09-12
Recipient: Myself, future address unknown
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1935
Themes: time, self, city, melancholy, hope

you,

if this reaches you, then time has not quite learned to keep its hands to itself. the river has perfected a way of pretending to go somewhere while staying. i admire such acting. the cafes breathe their smoke through sentences; people sit with their faces arranged like complicated pastries and bite the morning into manageable proofs.

you will ask: were you happy. i will answer: i learned the geography of a smaller hunger and fed it very well. i bought a pencil that makes a sound like rain when it thinks. i walked home with a loaf tucked under my arm like a warm book not yet read.

i am practicing the delicate cruelty of goodbyes. they are seeds that refuse every soil but memory. i plant them anyway and wake to water. sometimes a bell rings and all the birds in my chest lift, turning the air to quiet confetti.

if you have forgotten me, i forgive you. if you have not, i forgive you louder. there is a corner table at a bistro with our initials cut into it with a butter knife, which is to say: the future might be kind; it carries a small, sharp mercy in its apron.

when you arrive, save the last sip of your coffee. put it on the windowsill. leave it for the sun. send me its shadow.

with a slant of hat and heart,

m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
