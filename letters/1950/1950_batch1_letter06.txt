unsent letter found in a pocket

Date: 1950-07-17
Recipient: The Stranger from Train 47
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1950
Themes: travel, chance, transience, desire, silence

Stranger,

this will never reach you. it rides in my coat like a moth that prefers words to wool. the desert outside is a grammar of patience: sentences of road, commas of yucca, a coyote's long parenthesis. when you fell asleep with your cheek toward the window, the sun moved carefully so as not to wake you and the whole car practiced its best hush.

you wore a hat with a slight rain-dent (though there was no rain), and a suitcase like a hymnbook for a church of elsewhere. i pretended not to watch your hands, how they whispered to your lap as if negotiating peace. at the little station with the green bench, someone sold peaches, and i bought two and did not offer you one because courage is something i keep promising to learn next year.

if i had spoken, i would have said: the world is too wide to carry and too beautiful to set down. i would have said: there's a thin silver thread between your pulse and mine and the train is the needle. i would have said: when we part, let us do it as carefully as people crossing ice.

instead, i let the landscape finish my sentences. now this letter sleeps with ticket stubs and a Saint Christopher medal i do not believe in yet. keep traveling well, unknowable you. may your destinations be slightly kinder than your maps.

anonymous (but real),

a fellow passenger
