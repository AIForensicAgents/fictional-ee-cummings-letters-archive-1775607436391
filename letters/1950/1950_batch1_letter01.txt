ink between snowflakes

Date: 1950-01-12
Recipient: Mara (somewhere warm)
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1950
Themes: longing, winter, distance, urban solitude, memory

Dear Mara,

snow keeps folding itself out of the gray like little excuses and i keep unfolding them (only to find more snow). the radiator hisses in a language i nearly remember from a childhood kettle and the windows keep their frosted eyelids half shut, pretending not to see the cheap miracle of morning.

i work at the desk by the lamp that flickers when the trolley goes past. my typewriter ribbon is the only red left in this city: it stains my fingers with stubborn headlines and soft poems i will not mail. i pretend to be practical, sweeping yesterday into a neat pile, but a draft teases the pile into birds (and there they fly, into the stovepipe sky).

you asked if i am happy—how can i answer with anything but a mitten? i am warm enough to lie, cold enough to tell the truth. the truth: i miss the way you peeled oranges, giving the room a small sun. there are theaters downtown where matinees bloom like secret gardens; i sit among strangers and practice the art of being no one you know.

the calendar has boxed this month in little squares and i color each with a distance-crayon: some days blue, some days the pale of old postcards. write when the heat makes your hair unruly. i will send you a pocket of snow to press to your forehead, and in its brief, melting alphabet, my name.

faithfully (with mittened fingers),

ruth

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
