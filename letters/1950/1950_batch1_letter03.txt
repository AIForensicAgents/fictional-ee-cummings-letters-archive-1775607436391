telegram of a heartbeat

Date: 1950-03-05
Recipient: A.
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1950
Themes: love, city, jazz, yearning, night

A—

this city writes me in dots and dashes. my feet tap platforms like keys: 14TH STREET STOP 23RD STREET STOP your name in my ribs STOP. even the moon looks like a coin ready to slip into the jukebox of midnight (one more song please) and the saxophone on 52nd answers with a breath that almost forgives me.

i saw a boy asleep on a newspaper and i envied him the simple headline of dreams. i am awake in the loudest room—the kind with no walls, only neon punctuation. when the train opens its iron lung i inhale whole crowds. somewhere you are a quiet between words and i keep trying to pronounce you.

i want to tell you: in the telephone booth glass i nearly kissed my own reflection because it wore your outline. i want to not tell you: i am a coward with a brave coat. at the counter on 8th the coffee is black enough to draft a treaty between night and morning; i sign my name, then yours beside it, then tear it up and scatter the pieces like pigeons (they always come back).

if your window is a ship, i am sending this as weather. expect a small storm around your wrist where my fingers remember to be rain. will you let me be a forecast that keeps getting it wrong until we are right?

stop me only if you must.

j.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
