september with lungs of smoke

Date: 1950-09-11
Recipient: Rosa
Location: Chicago, IL
Year: 1950
Themes: labor, city, hope, grief, friendship

rosa—

this morning the factory whistled a weary alphabet. we all lined up to spell our names with oil on our hands. the lake kept its cool coin under the tongue of wind; the pier counted our footsteps like a slow abacus of wanting.

you asked if hope is a real thing or only a word the radio keeps rehearsing. i will answer with what i know: your laugh stains the air the way jam stains a child's shirt, gloriously, without regret. i have seen your wrists lift a crate as if floating were practical. i have watched the window remember your face long after you turned.

i do not have parades in my pockets, only lint and a stubborn match, but i will strike it when your street turns to winter in the middle of a tuesday. we will light the little flame and teach it to spell bread. we will teach it to say no, politely at first, and then in the necessary music.

yesterday i passed the church where we once tuned our quiet to the bell. the door was ajar and the floor smelled of lemon and old prayers. a woman sang hush into the pews and the wood believed her. i stood a moment and thought of the letter you did not send in june. i have written it on your behalf to the river: dear river, carry this grief until it learns to float. the river signed back with a wet thumbprint.

meet me after second shift near the tired diner with the flickering r and the pie that remembers july. we will split a miracle of coffee. we will practice the secret handshake of tomorrow: you take my hand and i take yours and neither lets go when the bus sighs.

in the name of everything ordinary and therefore holy,

luz
