postscript to a red umbrella

Date: 1950-04-23
Recipient: Miss June Petals
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1950
Themes: flirtation, spring, weather, civility, color

Dear Miss June,

it rained with that tidy southern hush today—the kind that tucks the azaleas in and kisses their foreheads. i walked behind your red umbrella because it was a small theater of courage and because the street was a string and you, its only music. forgive the following truths: i loved the tilt of your elbow more than all the afternoon, and when you shook the drops free, the town briefly applauded.

you left a handkerchief (white as a vow) on the café chair. i took it like a telegram from weather to weather, pressed it to my face and smelled lemon and a dare. the waitress (who collects secrets as if they were sugar packets) smiled and told me nothing i didn't already hope. i paid with coins that sounded like rain in a tin roof and thought, this is how a sky makes change.

i am not practiced in such overt rememberings, but may i return your handkerchief under your porch light? i will say only necessary syllables: your name, the day, the word thankyou stitched invisibly between. if you prefer, fold this note into a boat and send it down the storm drain—i will catch it where the street becomes a creek and understand.

sincerely (with a borrowed courage),

noah

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
