year-end map with coffee stains

Date: 1950-12-28
Recipient: Future Me (wherever)
Location: Portland, Oregon
Year: 1950
Themes: reflection, hope, self, time, intention

Future me,

the year is an unrolled map on the diner counter, and the waitress sets the cup down right in Nebraska. coffee blooms into a brown weather that looks like decisions i meant to make in june. outside, rain rehearses the old play about staying indoors; i am a fine audience.

i kept a list of firsts in my pocket until the ink forgot itself: first apartment with a window that whistled, first love who didn't, first train taken without a name to meet, first time the moon refused to be photographed and i thanked it for its boundaries. somewhere between april and now i learned to rest without apologizing to the clock.

you will want plans, so here are two: plant basil in a chipped enamel pot and forgive everyone before breakfast (repeat as needed). also buy more stamps than you require because you will write letters you had not intended, at odd hours, to people whose absence makes noise.

if i fold this map cleanly, i will lose the coffee stains that teach me where my hands were. so i will leave it rumpled and honest and slip it into the cupboard with the matches. when you open it next december, may it smell like rain on wool and accidental courage.

sign here if you arrive: ________

always becoming,

c.
