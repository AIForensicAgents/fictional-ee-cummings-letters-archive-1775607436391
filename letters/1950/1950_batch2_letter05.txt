fog writes the ocean in lowercase

Date: 1950-12-03
Recipient: Captain Hargreaves
Location: San Francisco, CA
Year: 1950
Themes: departure, friendship, sea, time, forgiveness

captain—

the bay has folded itself into a gray handkerchief and tucked into the vest of morning. gulls argue in apostrophes. a man with a broom edits the pier. even the rope has a memory of salt you could braid into a story if your patience were wider than the pacific.

i am late to the matter of farewell because time here moves on rubber soles. the clock clears its throat but nobody listens. the ferry scrapes the throat of the fog and says a word that sounds like stay. you once told me that departure is a grammar: subject, verb, distance. i have learned the conjugations poorly. i keep inventing a tense called almost.

your pocket watch, the one with the hairline crack, ticked in my palm last night like a small, stubborn fish. i wound it twice and thought of that winter off yokohama when the sea combed us with its cold teeth and we laughed as if teeth were jokes. forgiveness, you said then, is a kind of weather. i think i am ready for it: the rain, the afterward.

i have left you a book of maps in the locker beside the life preservers, though you keep better maps behind your ribs. inside the front cover i tucked a photograph of us not looking at the camera, because we were busy surviving. keep it where the compass can hear it.

if the ocean asks after me, tell it i remain on shore practicing my quiet. i am building a boat the size of a sentence, big enough for one breath and the next. when i learn its name, i will send the name to you folded into the corner of a napkin.

may your wake be a single, honest line, and may it lead back when it needs to,

thomas
