how August tastes

Date: 1950-08-29
Recipient: Lydia
Location: Cape Cod, Massachusetts
Year: 1950
Themes: summer, sensuality, youth, sea, promise

Lydia dear,

august tastes like salt on the apple peel and bicycles leaning into their shadows. i keep pockets full of beach glass—poor man's jewels—and your laugh keeps arranging them by color until the sun approves. the air is butter; even the gulls spread it thick over the dunes.

i found a postcard stand with views of the very ocean we were standing in (which felt like cheating). we bought none and wrote our own on napkins with blueberry stains, because a stain remembers the mouth better than any stamp. your hair, today, argued with the wind and won, convincingly.

in the evenings the boardinghouse lobby plays records that were brave enough to skip; i slow-danced with you in a courage that surprised its owner. the ceiling fan translated our breath into a soft verdict: stay. but september waits behind the curtain, tapping a sensible shoe. the nights are already the size of sweaters.

let's promise a small future: next year, same porch, two chipped mugs, stories that end not with morals but with sea-sound. i will bring the peaches that bruise if you look at them kindly and you will bring that book you read out loud the way a match reads its own flame.

remember me when you wash the sand from your knees and it refuses to leave (some things never do, bless them).

yours (still damp at the cuffs),

peter
