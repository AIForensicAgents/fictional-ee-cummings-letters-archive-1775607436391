winter unfolds like a slow telegram

Date: 1950-01-17
Recipient: M.
Location: New York, NY
Year: 1950
Themes: longing, winter, distance, love, city

dear m,

the city wears frost like a thin idea you could breathe through if only the ribs of morning would loosen. i carry your name where the glove refuses to warm my smallest finger; there it sits, stubborn and faithful, a candle cupped against subway wind.

yesterday a pigeon crossed madison square with the authority of a general, and all the businessmen saluted by accident: their hats jumped when the bus sighed. i thought (there goes our courage) and laughed into my scarf, which tasted faintly of pencil lead and last night's cigarettes.

i type your initials and the carriage returns to the beginning of me. the radiator coughs its gray confessions; the walls reply in parentheses. i think of the way you fasten silence to a room, so that nothing escapes but light. how you say my name like a letter opening itself.

please forgive the unruly geometry of distance. i am measuring it with pockets of change and the little calendar the pharmacist pressed into my hand with aspirin. each square is a door. behind january 28 an orange is waiting. you will peel it with your left thumb and the smell will be a small astonishment i put in the mail.

walk carefully across the wet noon. if you see a cloud the size of a handkerchief, tell it i am learning to fold my heart smaller, so it will travel quicker. (soon, soon.)

until then, i remain a certain window looking east, and a letter traveling through snow,

yours,

l.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
