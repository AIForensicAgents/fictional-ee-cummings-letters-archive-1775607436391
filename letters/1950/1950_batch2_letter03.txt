regarding the piano and the hour of moths

Date: 1950-06-22
Recipient: Mrs. Latham
Location: Boston, MA
Year: 1950
Themes: apology, music, tenement, humor, night

dear mrs. latham,

this morning i found a small brown moth asleep inside the piano, like an apology with wings. i mention this by way of confessing to last night's music. i had meant only to sit with the instrument the way a boy sits with a dock, watching dark water argue with the moon. but the keys kept suggesting sentences, and one does not interrupt a good sentence without cruelty.

i am aware of rules, and of your fine sign in the hall: no noise after ten. at nine-fifty-nine i was righteousness itself. at ten-oh-one i was a criminal with both hands. perhaps you heard the fugitive scales creeping up the stairs; perhaps they sounded like polite thieves borrowing silver.

the truth is, there are hours which refuse to be normal, and last night was such a one. a rain came soft as the collarbone of june. the alley took a bath. someone somewhere laughed without permission. i remembered a woman who taught me middle c by tapping my wrist with a pencil. i could not let go of that thin knocking, so i made a door.

i will, of course, make amends. i have placed two quarters and a bright button in an envelope for the electric meter. the button is handsome and blue. if it buys no kilowatts, may it at least purchase a smile. furthermore, i shall restrict any future disobedience to the clock's earlier decencies.

forgive the agitation of the moth; it left of its own accord at dawn, unimpressed by my chopin but kind enough to leave a powder of permission on the keys.

respectfully (and apologetically) yours,

harlan 3b
