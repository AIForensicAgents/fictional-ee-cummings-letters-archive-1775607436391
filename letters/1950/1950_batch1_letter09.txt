nocturne for a busted lamp

Date: 1950-11-13
Recipient: Gus at the Garage
Location: Detroit, Michigan
Year: 1950
Themes: industry, friendship, night, music, repair

Gus,

the streetlamp outside my window died last week and the night got its license back. i can hear engines thinking in their sleep—valves whispering about tomorrow, belts dreaming they are rivers. the radio plays a blues that leaves fingerprints on the dark, and i clean them up with coffee and a pencil.

i walked by your place after closing; the bay doors were tired eyelids. in the corner, a Packard smiled with only two teeth. you have a way with the broken: your hands argue with rust until rust changes its mind. tonight mine tried to argue with a poem and the poem smoked a cigarette and left.

do you remember when we pushed my Chevy three blocks and laughed like villains? the moon was a hubcap and we were poor enough to be rich in jokes. the cold is coming back wearing its good hat; check my heater before it starts asking questions i can't answer. i'll pay you in cash or in stories (there is the one about the girl who sold matches to a furnace and lived happily for an afternoon).

if you see that boy who shines shoes outside the diner, tell him the city keeps its promises but not in order. tell him the busted lamp is a kind of star you can fix from the ground.

friend to wrenches and words,

sam
