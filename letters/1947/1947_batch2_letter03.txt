Stacks whisper when I cannot

Date: 1947-09-23
Recipient: Miss Alta, Librarian of Maple Street
Location: Cedar Junction, Ohio
Year: 1947
Themes: healing, literature, homecoming, quiet, gratitude

Miss Alta,

when the noon whistle coughs us back from the mill, I come to your door like a moth confusing quiet for flame. you stamp dates into spines and somehow into me; the red ink smells a little like October, a little like courage. I do not know the right way to say this (there are many wrong ways and they line up like soldiers) but your reading room is the only place where the ceiling does not lean.

the war sits in my chest like a chair nobody claims. the radio reports futures with a voice too certain and my nights are a stiff suit of rain. but between Biography and History there is a window, and the dust performs a kind of ballet no general could invent. I sit and pages turn me; maps unfold their delicate nerves, poems pry open the stubborn of my mouth until sometimes a laugh escapes and nobody salutes it back into silence.

forgive me for the way I hover by your desk as if the bell were a lighthouse rope. you ask do I need assistance and I ache to say only: yes, but not the kind a catalog can find. you recommend a book about a man looking for home while never leaving his chair; I carry it like a secret animal and read it on the back steps where the laundry trembles like flags that have forgotten which country they belong to.

if interlibrary loan is not too grand a magic, might you conjure a volume on gardens. I want to learn the patience of tomatoes. the world is still loud, but in your aisles the noise becomes translation; even the light knows how to behave. I will bring back the books on time as though time were a promise one could keep.

respectfully and with thanks,
Roy

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
