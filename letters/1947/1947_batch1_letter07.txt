The Letter I Folded Into a Paper Boat

Date: 1947-09-03
Recipient: My Future Self
Location: Rockport, Maine
Year: 1947
Themes: self, time, sea, promise, quiet

dear you
who are also me, wearing tomorrow’s sweater
this harbor has built its language out of ropes and gulls and i am learning to conjugate the word stay

i folded this page into a shape that remembers water
the tide approved, green and complicated; the docks nodded like old men who know the joke but enjoy the telling
lobster traps stack their patience in bright wooden paragraphs

if by the time you read this our hands have become winter, remember the tin pail of September light
remember how the boats chimed lightly against each other as if exchanging recipes for mercy
remember that quiet is not the absence of sound but the moment before the bell

please do not choose the safe chair every time
please keep at least one unruly plant on the windowsill and say its name aloud when no one is home
and when love looks up from its newspaper and asks you the time, tell it the truth and mean it

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
