Sister with the Red Scarf

Date: 1947-02-02
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Davenport, Iowa
Year: 1947
Themes: family, midwest, memory, change, comfort

Ruth—
this morning the river looks like a gray ribbon someone forgot to iron
and the freight whistles practice their lonely arithmetic down by the silos

Mother is mending the tablecloth with that tiny patience she keeps in the sugar tin
Father has coaxed the radio to hush its static long enough for the weather to promise not much
Mr. Greeley next door walks his fence line, a quiet veteran of things his mouth refuses to name

your red scarf is still on the peg by the back door, an obedient flame
sometimes i put it on and the kitchen becomes a Paris i have never seen
(or maybe just the warm exhale of the oven, which is also a kind of Paris)

i may go to Chicago come spring, ride a train that speaks in sparks and steel vowels
learn how city light moves across a face and does not apologize
but i will write, and send a pressed leaf, and keep the ledger of home carefully

if you come for supper Sunday bring the scarf; the snowdrift by the mailbox has opinions
and i would like to hear them in color

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
