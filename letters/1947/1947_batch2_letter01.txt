Night telegram to your quiet

Date: 1947-02-11
Recipient: Elodie M.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1947
Themes: longing, city nights, music, winter, intimacy

Elodie,

tonight the radiator drums a small war with February and I hitch your name to the moon's shoelace. the window, a polite mirror, keeps my breath like a postage stamp (unlicked). I would send you a telegram of blue jazz if Western Union understood how a wrist turns when music smiles.

I walked down Bleecker where the snow performs its slow arithmetic; 52nd Street is a brass comet, and somewhere a trumpet invents a staircase that climbs without moving. if I were brave I would telephone and say only: come. but my coins grow shy in the pocket and the booth light is a tiny confession; instead I practice saying your name to the typewriter—its ribbon keeps the secret.

what are you doing at this exact hum of the night? are you unpinning your hair (every pin a star unhooked), are you arguing with a kettle, or leaning on the window like a lighthouse with no shore to save. I keep my hands open (for snow, for you) and the room understands. all day the newspapers spoke their clever sadness about strikes and ships and men; I folded them to make a boat and set it sailing on the sink. it nearly reached your street before dissolving into yesterday.

if love is a city it has your doorway for an address; the postman of my ribs rings and rings. forgive the hour, the commas that refuse to stand straight, the envelope that will not quite close—each line is only my heart, practicing its handwriting.

until the thaw,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
