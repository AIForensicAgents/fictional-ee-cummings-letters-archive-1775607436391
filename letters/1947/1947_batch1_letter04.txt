Apricot Sun Over a City of Trains

Date: 1947-04-29
Recipient: Leo
Location: Union Station, Chicago
Year: 1947
Themes: travel, friendship, desire, machines, modernity

Leo—
i am writing from a bench that keeps everyone, briefly
porters gliding, soldiers turned civilians carrying their new ordinary like a sleeping child
announcers practice an ancient ritual of destinations; the schedule boards flutter like blackbirds taking notes

this morning the sun cracked open like an apricot and spilled into the glass roof
i have been sticky with it since dawn
in the waiting room a boy with a toy locomotive has already learned how longing sounds in miniature

if you were here we would count hats and stories and decide who is brave by how they hold their paper bag of lunch
i would tell you the truth about the window last night, how it made a mirror and i did not recognize my face
(only the part of me that leans toward your name when nobody is looking)

meet me at the corner under the green clock next month; bring a joke that takes three blocks to finish
we will take the long way across the river and let the bridges do their slow metal prayer above us

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
