Streetlamp Confession

Date: 1947-01-14
Recipient: M.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1947
Themes: longing, city, winter, jazz, intimacy

dear M
night is a pocket full of nickels and i spend them all on your name
sleet braids the avenue into silver ropes; taxis hum their small warm hymns
my windows are two patient eyes, fogged with maybe
and i, a misplaced universe of buttons and breath, lean into the typewriter until it begins to believe me

remember that alley behind the jazz cellar, the horn making blue ladders out of cold
we climbed nothing and everything: your glove fell, i picked it up like a soft comet
and the moon, ridiculous coin, kept buying us seconds we could not afford

war has stepped off the stage, yes, but the applause still trembles in the seats
the city walks home in stockings not yet sure if they are allowed to be silk
and i keep a ration book of kisses, nearly used up, nearly

if you come by, i will leave the door unlocked (the word hello blooming like a shy carnation)
if you do not, i will unlearn my hands slowly and call it winter
meanwhile: the lamp outside performs its blond arithmetic upon the snow, counting me, counting you, not stopping

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
