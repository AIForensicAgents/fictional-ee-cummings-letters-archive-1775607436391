To a Publisher Concerning Small Miracles

Date: 1947-03-18
Recipient: Mr. Alden, Editor, Blue Finch Press
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1947
Themes: art, ambition, publishing, hope, humility

Mr. Alden,
forgive the unruly margins; my typewriter ribbon has learned to smudge like rain on a cheap coat
inside this envelope is a book the size of a matchbox (or perhaps just a handful of breathing)
poems that lean forward the way a streetlamp does when it wants to overhear two lovers
after the war my words refused to stand at attention, so i let them wander into small miracles

if you tilt the manuscript to the light you may see the thumbprints of rooms i have slept in
Chicago depot benches, a boardinghouse with wallpaper like faded telegrams, a room in New York that smells of ink and onions
these pages know the inside of my coat pocket and the ache of my wrist at midnight

i am asking for a slender book with a plain cover
something a stranger might mistake for silence and then keep forever

there is a stamped self-addressed envelope hiding behind this letter like a shy brother
use it to send either yes or the gentlest no you own
in the meantime i will continue to write like someone sweeping a doorstep clean enough for dawn

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
