Bread rations & constellations

Date: 1947-03-29
Recipient: Lucien
Location: Montmartre, Paris
Year: 1947
Themes: scarcity, friendship, resilience, city life, hope

Lucien,

this morning the queue curled like a gray cat along the bakery wall; our tickets fluttered against our fingers (little moths who know their hunger). the woman behind me smelled of coal and violets; we all looked at the same loaf as if it were a small planet cooling. later, I climbed the hill where the wind keeps its pocket watch; the basilica shone in a tired way, handsome as an overworked saint.

at the cinema on Rue Lepic, the projector sneezed its snow, and for ninety minutes everyone forgot to be reasonable. a man laughed with his whole coat; a girl cried and made no apology to anyone. walking home I counted stars like change and saved the brightest for you. did you receive the scarf I sent (the blue remembers the Seine, the fringe is rain learned by heart).

people say help is coming from across oceans; I do not mistrust it, but I keep my hands busy in the meantime. I mend a chair with wire, I teach a neighbor's child to whistle (badly, beautifully). by the canal, cranes build a skeleton of tomorrow and geese argue about philosophy in the reeds. last night the radio coughed then sang—an aria stitched to a weather report—and for a breath the apartment grew taller.

if you were here we would drink something warm and throw shadows onto the ceiling until they grew brave enough to dance without us. until then, I write your name on my ration coupons, purely as superstition; the clerk never notices and the bread is no different, except it tastes more like April.

write soon, with your untidy honesty,
Adele

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
