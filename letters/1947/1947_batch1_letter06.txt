After the Blackout, A Blue Door

Date: 1947-07-22
Recipient: Anya
Location: Notting Hill, London
Year: 1947
Themes: recovery, transatlantic, hunger, romance, resilience

Anya—
London wears its bones openly but ties a ribbon on them just the same
queues like patient rivers; fog that remembers every footstep since 1939
still, this morning a blue door winked at me, as if the future had been painted overnight

your kettle sang, we learned the grammar of steam
biscuits softened in their own history and we laughed until the neighbor upstairs forgave us
later, walking, you pointed at a bombsite garden where beans climbed a memory and did not fall

i wanted to kiss your wrist where the pulse corrects my mistakes; i wanted to tell you how a ship’s horn at dawn is the oldest wish i know
the Thames carried our reflections like unpaid bills and returned them stamped received

ration books fold like origami birds in your drawer; we have taught them to fly into next week
i will sail back soon, pockets full of tea and a postcard of a lion that pretends not to be tired
keep the blue door open a thumb’s width; let the day squeeze in and sit on your windowsill

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
