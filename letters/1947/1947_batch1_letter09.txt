Copper Wire Between Two Silences

Date: 1947-11-09
Recipient: Samuel (over the telephone wires)
Location: Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania
Year: 1947
Themes: friendship, industry, trauma, listening, forgiveness

Sam—
i wanted to say this on the line but the operator might have worried, and the wire already knows too much
mills breathe slower now; the river carries a night’s work on its back and does not complain
in the tavern by the bridge men sip a grammar of smoke and leave their coats to think for them

i have been listening for the sound your breathing makes when the past tries to knock
sometimes i hear the knock instead and stand very still until it forgets the address
if you wake and the room smells like 1944, call me; i will read the phone book aloud until we both laugh at a name

forgiveness is not a trick, it is a small coal carried carefully in both hands
someday we will warm our fingers at it without burning
write me the color of your morning when it arrives; i am trying to learn a palette that does not flinch

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
