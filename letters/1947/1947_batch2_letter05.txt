little boat of tomorrow (to be named)

Date: 1947-12-01
Recipient: my as-yet-unborn child
Location: Santa Monica, California
Year: 1947
Themes: anticipation, family, coast, tenderness, postwar

little boat,

the Pacific keeps licking the edge of us clean and the gulls practice their grammar on the power lines. your room is almost a room—one blue wall like a sky we can take down for washing, a crib that creaks the way hope creaks when it grows. we have folded blankets the color of patient fruit; when I hold them my hands imagine a weight that is all question and all answer.

it is late and the radio stitches warm noises into the cold corners. a dance band gets lost then finds itself again; an announcer tells me weather I could learn by opening a window. I think about names and the world they will call you to; some names are doorways, some are ladders, some are pocket mirrors. we will choose you a doorway that opens on sea and citrus, a ladder that climbs out of anyone's sorrow.

this city has a talent for light. even the streetcar rings like a spoon against a holiday glass. still, the war we just unlaced leaves its shoes by every bed. I will teach you to untie knots (shoelaces, worries). I will teach you the prayer of tying: loop and pull, loop and pull, let it hold without hurting.

when you arrive the calendar will turn its quiet page and all our clocks will agree for once. there is an orange on the table getting sweeter without moving. I think that must be how joy works—staying still until it becomes more itself. come when you are ready, not a minute sooner; the tide knows the moon better than any schedule.

waiting, a little foolishly, a lot completely,
Your parent

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
