Year's End, Window Frost

Date: 1947-12-28
Recipient: Elena
Location: Vancouver, British Columbia
Year: 1947
Themes: winter, renewal, love, distance, light

Elena—
frost has drawn its feather alphabet on the panes and i am trying to read it before the kettle interrupts
streets shine like a thought kept too long inside the mouth
there is a voice on the radio that croons the hour into something you could rest your head upon

i walked by the harbor and every mast wrote a thin letter to the sky
seagulls argued politics and then forgave one another without taking notes
this year has been a drawer full of mismatched gloves and i have learned to leave the house anyway

if you are cold come north by increments
train, then ferry, then the stubborn boots of your brave feet
we will eat soup with the sincerity it deserves and let the clock watch us with polite confusion

stay until January forgets how to be stern
stay until the salt on the sidewalk admits it is glitter
and when the new calendar arrives, we will hang it crooked on purpose so it laughs

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
