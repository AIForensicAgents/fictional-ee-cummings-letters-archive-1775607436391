Mother, About the Small Weather in My Chest

Date: 1947-06-07
Recipient: Mother
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1947
Themes: motherhood, healing, landscape, economy, letters

Mother—
this place is a bowl of sky and someone forgot to stop pouring
adobe and shadow, blue that has learned every vowel by heart
in the evenings the swallows conduct tiny thunderbolts over the plaza and it makes a person feel forgiven

you asked if my cough has improved; the dry air is a kindly aunt, she tucks me in with sunlight
and i sleep without the old knuckles of worry knocking at the ribs
money is thin but honest; i paint signs for a grocer whose melons look like small suns

sometimes the mountains put their hands on my shoulders and say be still
i try; i make coffee slowly; i read your last letter twice and kept the stamp because it smelled of the pantry
war sounds no longer visit at midnight, only the train that arrives once and refuses to explain

i am sending a pressed wildflower tucked in a page about patience
do not be alarmed if it has forgotten some of its purple on the way
kiss the linoleum for me and put an extra blanket over the radio when it misbehaves

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
