small moon for a typewriter

Date: December 2, 1921
Recipient: Mira Delaney
Location: Paris, Rue de la Grande Chaumière
Year: 1921
Themes: exile, letters, art, winter, romance, Paris

dear mira,

paris walks in its own weather, a shawl of fog embroidered with whispers. the seine is a patient wrist; bridges learn their balance from it. i carry a notebook and a tin of matches, though the city lights itself often without my help.

my typewriter wears a new ribbon—ink-dark and shining like a narrow midnight. when i turn the carriage, it is as if a small moon slides across a private sky and the stars rearrange themselves into sentences. i keep forgetting to breathe at the right times.

the cafés practice their heat. cups click, a soft applause. painters argue about the color of silence (i chose a grey with a thoughtful green in it). i tried to describe your laughter and the paper blushed so hard i had to start again.

this morning the post brought a letter from someone who believes in tradition the way cats believe in laps. i am trying not to be wicked about it. after all, even a cathedral remembers once being a dream that was told no. the bells today rang like polished fruit.

i am saving my last franc for a winter apple and a stamp back to you. please send me a small inventory of your window: rain, an echo, perhaps a neighbor who hums when she sews. if the year has a door, i think we have the key; it is small, oddly shaped, and looks a lot like kindness.

put your hand on this page, mira. there. between your palm and these words lives a room we haven’t visited yet—a fire, two chairs, a silence that enjoys our company. when i close my eyes, i hear snow considering us.

with the quietest affection and a very warm coat of it,

— a
