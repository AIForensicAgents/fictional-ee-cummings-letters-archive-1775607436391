stars are modest telegrams

Date: 1921-12-01
Recipient: Dear Future
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1921
Themes: time, reflection, city, winter, resolve

dear Future,

i rented a room facing a hill that forgets itself in fog at least twice a day. the cable cars climb and ring like polite ghosts requesting admission to heaven. i have purchased a coat that agrees with wind.

i am writing at the edge of the year where the calendar hesitates, barefoot. the harbor edits itself with gulls and lanterns; the moon is a round apology for whatever cannot be mended in twelve months. please accept it on our behalf.

i want to send you certain improvements: a habit of unpanicked mornings, a wallet with fewer metaphors and more sensible coins, a way to listen that doesn't rehearse its reply. in exchange, keep me a chair by a window that overlooks something unreasonable (a cliff, a kindness).

it is late and the city scatters its sequins down the streets. strangers unbutton doorways; pianos forgive mistakes; a child names a star after nothing in particular. the small telegrams above say stop in a thousand spark-dialects, but truly they mean go on.

if you must change me, do it like fog—arriving quiet, making even the loudest rooftops considerate. i promise to meet you with warm pockets and a scandalous faith in morning.

until we become each other,

n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
