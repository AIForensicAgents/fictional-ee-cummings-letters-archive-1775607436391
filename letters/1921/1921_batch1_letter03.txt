after the shutter clicks

Date: 1921-03-14
Recipient: Miss O.
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1921
Themes: modernity, intimacy, city, art, observation

dear O,

you stood very still, which is not the same as being captured. the camera tried to swallow you politely, but you dissolved like sugar into its throat and then reassembled (exactly yourself) in the moment between dark and darker. afterward we drank coffee and the spoons argued in their mild silver voices.

the street is a long instrument; some nights i think the elevated train is bowing the city into a bruise-colored song. we walked home through that music. your hat tilted toward the moon (a conspirator), your gloves making paragraphs of the cold.

forgive me for admiring the seam of your left sleeve—it is a small geography where patience and thread made a treaty. perhaps that is all beauty is: the peace we negotiate with time, so that a glance can live there for a while.

i will develop the plates tomorrow. if they fail, i shall not mind; my eyes have already kept what cannot be printed. still, i like the way paper pretends to be truth—flat, crisp, unbreathing—while the truth itself is a heartbeat in a coat pocket, forgetting and remembering on purpose.

bring the red scarf next thursday, and the joke you refused to tell. i promise to laugh with sufficient scandal.

sincerely (which is the only scandal worth mastering),

n.
