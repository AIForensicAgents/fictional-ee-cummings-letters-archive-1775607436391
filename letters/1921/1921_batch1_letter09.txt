autumn with a stitched mouth

Date: 1921-10-18
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Asheville, North Carolina
Year: 1921
Themes: illness, hope, autumn, care, mountains

Ruth,

the mountain sits like a patient thought just behind the town's forehead. i walk up to it daily to practice breathing more deliberately than sorrow allows. the sanatorium windows rehearse politeness; inside, coughs knock faintly like distant visitors.

i brought chrysanthemums and placed them in that chipped blue jar you liked. they stand as if pretending to have roots in this new air. your bed is a boat when you sleep—sheets learning the motion of a patient sea. i sat and watched how your face returned from far places with each inhale, as travelers do, with small careful luggage.

we do not have to talk about pain. it is an excellent listener and understands being ignored. instead i read to you from a book where the hero is perpetually almost-late; you smiled at the nearlys. after, we ate apples that tasted like a decision made correctly.

the nurse says the cold is honest and will not lie about progress. i have pinned that sentence to my sleeve where i can reach it with my teeth when courage needs chewing.

i leave at dusk when the clouds button themselves and the town puts on its lamp-earrings. i walk home with your name steady as a lantern. tomorrow i will bring a scandalously cheerful scarf.

with a fierce gentleness,

n.
