Letter with Moths and Streetlamps

Date: April 21, 1921
Recipient: Clara Hart
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1921
Themes: urban night, intimacy, spring, modernity, longing, music

dear clara,

tonight the streetlamps are small pale moths that forgot their wings and decided to stand still and shine. the sidewalks keep a secret rhythm (a hush of shoe-soles and last-cup-of-coffee steam), and i carry your name in my mouth like a warm match.

i walked past washington square where the arch pretends to be a brave white ear, listening to every unspoken yes in the city. a boy sold newspapers that smelled of thunder. someone’s piano leaked through a thin wall—one key stuck, sorrowful, faithful. i waited for the key to free itself (it didn’t) and still i loved the stubbornness of its trying.

if i could fold this evening, i would slip it into your pocket: a minor rain beginning, the lilac that devours itself with fragrance, the sudden astonishment of a cat stepping out of darkness like a question mark answered. you would find it later, perhaps on a trolley, and smile very quietly toward no one.

i am learning the elastic grammar of our two hands, how they invent new sentences whenever they touch. punctuation becomes a shy thing, commas blush into fingerprints. love, which is not a proper noun and doesn’t want to be, spills over the edge of every polite teacup.

(do you hear it—our small courageous future practicing its heartbeat?)

tonight i promise to keep a window open so the wind can come in and make a mess of everything tidy and correct. this is how happiness enters: uninvited, laughing, with wet shoes.

always (and always) yours,

— a

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
