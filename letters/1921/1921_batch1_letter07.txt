salt-letter, carried by gulls

Date: 1921-07-30
Recipient: A.
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1921
Themes: ocean, longing, summer, creativity, freedom

dear A,

the ocean is an old aunt and she is gossiping in every direction. i went to the rim of her apron and confessed i don't know how to be patient. she slapped my ankles with foam and said, watch.

sails like pale ears are listening to wind; fishermen speak in ropes, and the knots remember storms the way our wrists remember bracelets. i took a room with a window built specifically for blue things. through it i watched two gulls argue who owned the horizon. their lawsuit continues loudly.

when i swim, the water borrows my heartbeat and returns it tidier, as if folded by a laundress who believes in rhythm. thinking of you in the shallows, where light fences in small gold animals. my skin decided to be brave and followed the decision.

i painted your name on a shell and hid it in a dune (latitude of secrecy, longitude of yes). should you come north, i will lend you a hat woven from sea-breeze and improbable outcomes.

don't be surprised if i arrive sunburned with ideas. the sun is a loud teacher, but even it grows tender at the rim of evening where your silhouette proposes itself to my useless grammar.

from your impatient coast,

n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
