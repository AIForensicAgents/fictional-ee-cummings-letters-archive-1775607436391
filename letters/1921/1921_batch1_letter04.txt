april invented green for you

Date: 1921-04-07
Recipient: Elias
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1921
Themes: spring, exile, language, art, longing

Elias—

Paris wears rain the way a violin wears dusk. every corner is practicing a new grammar of wet light. i learned how to say hunger in two languages today and found it tasted the same (your name was the aftertaste).

i send you a map that refuses to behave: the streets fold into swans and the Seine is an unbuttoned ribbon. on the Left Bank a boy sold me a book with the last chapter missing; i read it twice and preferred the absence, which seemed more accurate to us.

april keeps opening like a green umbrella (without asking). i walk under it and discover miracles that can safely be stepped on: moss, cigarette stubs, a puddle remembering sky. if you were here we would argue about which shade of green belongs to forgiveness and then fail happily to agree.

in the café, a woman smoked in hexameter; even her ash fell in meter. i thought of your hands attempting to translate thunder into penstrokes. perhaps letters are our clumsy thunder, stuttering across the ocean with a pocketful of light.

kiss the dog on the nose for me. tell it that Paris smells like bread, rain, and polite disobedience. tell yourself that i am coming back when the tulips forget how to be serious.

—n.
