small hours, enormous heart

Date: 1921-01-11
Recipient: M.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York, NY
Year: 1921
Themes: love, winter, city night, longing, art

dear M,

this night is not a clock it is a black blossom opening on our window (slowly) and my thoughts walk out wearing their thin shoes. how peculiar that the streetlamps make frost seem to glow from the inside, like a letter learning to believe its own light.

i have been practicing the word tomorrow until it becomes a bell. your face arrives between the rings, a sort of soft thunder. i keep a chair for you (it is made of listening). every cushion remembers the rumor of your shoulders.

today the painter downstairs flung his window wide—out spilled the smell of turpentine and mercy. i wanted to borrow a color that could speak you, but colors are stubborn saints. instead i wrote your name on the fog of the glass and watched it vanish into the alley. perhaps vanishing is a kind of staying in the language of winter.

i am not brave except when i imagine your hand, which turns fear into a small polite animal. oh M, if the universe is a coat, then i am very willing to be your pocket where a coin of laughter jingles (lightly, endlessly).

don't hurry the morning. let it arrive like tea, steaming its simple holiness. and should you wake before my knocking—keep a corner of the pillow for my late wandering dreams.

always, and then more,

n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
