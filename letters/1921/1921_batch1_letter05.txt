desert telegram (unsent)

Date: 1921-05-19
Recipient: Father
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1921
Themes: family, journey, landscape, reconciliation, silence

Father,

no wires here, only the pure arithmetic of distance. the mesa counts slowly, subtracting each of my footsteps from the horizon until evening solves for x and the answer is: silence.

i have been rehearsing our conversation with the cottonwoods; they are attentive, greenly judicial. i tell them that your voice is a careful tool, and that i broke a little under it once. they rustle like a jury removing their hats.

the desert keeps an honest book. every lizard writes its name in dust. storms arrive pre-addressed to whoever is foolish enough to believe in roofs. yesterday a blue wind rolled a can down the road until the can found its rhythm and became a drum. i listened, and the listening made a bridge.

please do not worry. i am eating peaches with both hands and letting the juice invent a new alphabet on my wrists. i sleep in a room that smells of wood and unargued moons. at night the coyotes edit the dark, replacing fear with commas.

if you were here i would ask you to teach me how to fix a fence, and i would show you how forgiveness lowers its shoulders and fits through the smallest gate.

not posted, but meant,

your son,

n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
