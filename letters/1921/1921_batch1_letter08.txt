things a train window knows

Date: 1921-09-05
Recipient: Mr. Pike
Location: Baltimore, Maryland
Year: 1921
Themes: travel, ambition, change, mentorship, observation

Mr. Pike,

writing to you from the station where announcements bloom and wilt on the same breath. my ticket is the color of resignation but the engine argues otherwise. have you noticed that steel has a gentle side? the wheels hum as if apologizing for their hurry.

through the glass, America is a patient movie: cornfield, porch, field again, a boy waving his hat so hard he nearly starts weather. i have decided that ambition is a landscape seen quickly—one believes it continuous while the eye edits.

you once said, carry fewer books and more boldness. i am trying. today i packed one shirt, a pencil, and the idea that i might be forgiven for being late to myself. the baggage clerk stamped this idea with a friendly thump; it rode the conveyor like a small indestructible animal.

if i am lucky i will arrive where work and astonishment share a desk. if i am wise i will not ask which one is senior. the window knows this already; it keeps no copy but remembers every reflection.

when i return, i will bring you a timetable annotated with weather reports and improbable metaphors. you may red-pencil them as you please. i remain, irreverently devoted to your corrections.

respectfully,

n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
