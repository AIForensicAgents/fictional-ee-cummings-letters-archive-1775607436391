ragtime confession in blue

Date: 1921-06-22
Recipient: Clara B.
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1921
Themes: music, desire, night, city, heat

Clara—

i walked into night and it was already singing your name (two trumpets, one whisper). the river dragged its hem along the wharf, lace of moon snagging on the pilings. i am unqualified for this heat; it graduates me anyway.

in the hall on Rampart the piano was a weather report: lightning in E-flat, thunder in a grin. a woman with glass bracelets dealt joy from her sleeves; we all took our clumsy share. i thought of your ankle (how it invents a city block each time it turns) and suddenly the floor became a map i was not lost on.

i will not apologize for the blue note i put in your mailbox (midnight, folded). if you open it and a small breeze escapes, keep it—teach it to sit, to beg, to speak. teach it the word stay, which i am learning too slowly.

before dawn a baker opened his door and let out the warm idea of bread. i understood something then about mercy: that it is an aroma first, a crust second.

walk tomorrow past the old church and look up where the steeple forgets its manners and elbows the sky. that is where my mouth keeps your name, like a quiet. like a promise with dancing shoes.

always your inconvenient admirer,

n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
