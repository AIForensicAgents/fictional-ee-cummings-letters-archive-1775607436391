small moon in the postbox

Date: 1946-02-11
Recipient: M.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1946
Themes: longing, winter, letters, distance, hope

dear M,

today the postman slid a small moon into the wooden mouth of our box; i carried it inside like a bird cupped carefully in mittened hands. your handwriting (which is a kind of weather) stammered across the card and suddenly the kitchen had tides. three cents of sky pressed into the corner—i kissed the stamp and tasted salt and glue and a little courage.

i am learning the alphabet of thaw. the river loosens its buttons. icicles step down from the porch in their slow shining shoes. at night the kettle sings its bent little opera and i pretend it is you, unbuttoning a story. we keep the shades half drawn like eyelids; we leave the lamp polite for returning thoughts.

you asked if the bed feels larger alone. it feels longer. i sleep diagonally (northwest to southeast) so that my feet can dream of chicago while my head listens to the snow performing white arithmetic on the sill. some nights i stitch hours together with letters; the thread is thin but holds.

i went to the common to feed sparrows and ended up reading the wind. it said: trust your lungs. it said: the year is a door you do not open, it opens you. forgive me for sounding like a calendar cracked in half, but this is what february does—melts and then insists.

should you find a pocket in your coat lined with noon, please mail it. i will place it over my heart and wait for the clumsy brave spring to remember our names.

always here, always slightly astonished,

r.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
