Notes for a Quiet Factory

Date: 1946-04-29
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Akron, Ohio
Year: 1946
Themes: return, healing, family, industrial town, spring

tom—

i keep hearing your boots even when the stairs are empty. a kind of ghost that smells like machine oil and thunderstorms. i know you say the war is folded away (bottom drawer, right side) but the drawer keeps singing through your pocket. the city hums now—not the siren hum, the ordinary one—the belly hum of rubber and paycheck and noon whistle, and still your hands tap the table as if counting the breath between shell and sky.

this morning i rode the bus with a woman balancing a cake on her lap, a frosted empire. she told me it was for a boy who had learned to be seven again. we both looked out the window when the river showed its teeth. spring is a generous liar; it says nothing ever happened except tulips. still, i bought you oranges. their skins want to be peeled like new names.

i have a spare room and a patient chair. the chair is good at listening, it was built by a man who lost two fingers and learned patience the way rain learns roofs. if nights are loud, bring them. i will line them up on the windowsill with the other jars of sound—train whistle, radio static, my heartbeat when i think of june.

we are all trying to master peacetime like a left hand learning cursive. be clumsy. i will applaud.

in the cupboard you will find the blue cup. it misses you too.

sisterly,

agnes
