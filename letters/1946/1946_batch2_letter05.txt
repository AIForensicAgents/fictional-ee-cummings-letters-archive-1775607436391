Midnight schedule to the coast

Date: 1946-12-21
Recipient: Elias
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1946
Themes: travel, anticipation, winter, reunion, movement

eli—

the timetable is a kind of moonlight folded into columns. tonight the station smells of coal and goodbye. winter leans against the big clock; its hands are gentle and wrong. i have a secondhand suitcase and a first-rate ache. it is the solstice; the dark is practicing endurance and so am i.

remember summer on the ferry when the gulls heckled our sandwiches and you taught me how to read the wind by the way it toyed with our sleeves. i have packed that lesson between two sweaters. the conductor wears his hat like a verdict; still, he smiles when i hand him the little rectangle that says yes.

i will sit in the car where strangers keep their histories politely folded. there is always a woman with knitting, a soldier with sleep like a fragile package, a boy discovering the geography of windows. we pass towns that blink one light at us—hello goodbye hello—and the prairie behaves like an ocean pretending to be flat. sometimes i believe the tracks are writing us, not the other way around.

if the pacific meets me with fog, i will answer in hushes. if it shouts, i will be brave and shout back. find me on market street near the music store that sells harmonicas and winter dreams. i will be the one with a suitcase teaching itself to breathe.

save me the seat by the window of your mouth. say my name like a departure and an arrival.

halfway already,

a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
