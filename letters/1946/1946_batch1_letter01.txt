green windows after winter

Date: 1946-03-17
Recipient: M.
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1946
Themes: longing, city, postwar, spring, intimacy

dear M,

today the city shrugged off a last soot of snow and i saw green in a tenement window like a small flag of april. the el kept coughing its iron lullaby, and i pretended those sparks were letters from you (thin bright birds that cannot keep still). i bought oranges with a nickel i should have saved, and peeled one on the stoop; the peel made a sun around my shoes.

soldiers keep walking home in pieces of uniform—one sleeve, a hat too stiff for the wind. the corner grocer no longer keeps the blackout curtains; he smiles in full daylight, which is another kind of ration finally lifted. i think of your hands learning again to be only hands, not signal flags, not bandages.

at night i put the lamp on the floor and the ceiling becomes a pale lake. i lie back and send a boat across it named after you. it never lands anywhere, which is how i know the voyage is true.

send me the sound of your laugh folded in a napkin. i will hold it to my mouth whenever the subway forgets my name.

yours somewhere between the last snow and the first leaf,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
