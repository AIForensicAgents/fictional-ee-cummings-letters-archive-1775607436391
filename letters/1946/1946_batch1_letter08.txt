paper snow & orange peels

Date: 1946-12-24
Recipient: Anouk
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1946
Themes: winter, love, austerity, city, holiday

chere Anouk,

christmas eve and paris wears its thin silver coat. the grocer wrapped my oranges in yesterday's headlines and the ink made my hands look like i had been reading the skin of a saint. on the bridge a boy sold paper snow that melted in his smile. i bought two pinches and let them vanish on my tongue the way a year does.

your window showed me two candles and the shadow of your wrist pinning laundry to a line of light. i wanted to knock, then didn't, then did, then wrote instead. forgive me. sometimes the doorbell confuses my heart with a bell in the cathedral and i think if i touch it all of paris will kneel.

tonight we will make a feast of hardly: a bottle, a heel of bread, the orange peels made beautiful by their arrangement. we will listen for the bells; they know how to enter a room without taking it. after midnight i will walk you home on streets that remember both boots and bare feet and are kind to each.

keep the second candle for tomorrow. keep the first for now.

avec tendresse plus que courage,
m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
