note left for the mirror

Date: 1946-02-01
Recipient: Self (kept in the bureau drawer)
Location: Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
Year: 1946
Themes: self-forgiveness, renewal, anxiety, ritual, tenderness

dear Self,

i have placed this between shirts that smell faintly of soap and the old life. you will find it when you are looking for a clean collar and instead discover a promise.

here it is: i will not punish you for surviving. the mirror will be a window when possible and a lake when necessary. we will forgive the trembling that happens in grocery lines and at kind voices. we will walk to the station for no reason but the useful lesson of departures.

let us collect small proofs. a penny rescued from the gutter. a neighbor's hello that arrives without debt. the wild calm of tying our shoes slowly. take the saved ticket stubs and press them in a book until they become leaves.

if you wake at four with your chest full of bees, open the bureau. hold the cool spoon of moonlight. drink water from the chipped glass. say our name as if introducing a friend to a room that will, eventually, applaud.

remember: the future is a coat we are still learning to grow into. be gentle with the sleeves.

ever yours (practice makes steady),
s.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
