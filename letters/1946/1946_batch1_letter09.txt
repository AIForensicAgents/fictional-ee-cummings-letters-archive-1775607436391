assembly line of quiet

Date: 1946-05-31
Recipient: Ephraim
Location: Detroit, Michigan
Year: 1946
Themes: labor, solidarity, memory, community, change

dear Ephraim,

the line rolled to life this morning like a hymn missing its middle. we stood with our gloves off to feel if the machines meant it. they did. the new foreman speaks in decimals and the old one in tobacco; between them we find a kind of weather and go to work.

i keep thinking of last autumn when we walked out with signs that spelled our hunger correctly. the sky was gray but the inside of my mouth was bright as a lunch pail. i learned then the weight a word can carry if you put your spine behind it. now the belts whisper again and we are back to the marriage of bolt and nut, spark and eye.

after shift i wash oil from my wrists and it leaves ghost bracelets. my wife says they make me look like i belong to something. maybe i do. there is a dance saturday at the hall, a fiddle borrowed from a cousin, pies cooling on every windowsill in this block like clocks that only tell mercy. come.

if i ever forget why we stood still together, remind me. if i ever forget to sit down with you, do not.

your friend until the whistle unlearns our names,
sam.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
