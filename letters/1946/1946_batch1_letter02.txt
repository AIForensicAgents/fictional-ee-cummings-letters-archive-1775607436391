hammer quiets, heart begins

Date: 1946-01-09
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1946
Themes: family, work, war aftermath, winter, hope

dear Thomas,

since you came home the hammer in the kitchen keeps forgetting to be angry. we use it now to straighten a frame, to hush a loose shutter; no more swearing at crates stamped with the names of far seas. chicago freezes and thaws and freezes again, but you move through it like a man teaching winter to be polite.

i watch your hands relearn coffee, newspapers, the leash of the neighbor's mutt. on sunday we oiled the bicycle that waited for you like a faithful dog all these years; when you rode it down wabash you were a bright tin miracle and the children applauded.

they offered you classes for free because the government believes in mending with books. take them, brother. let a quiet algebra climb your spine. let evenings be soft lamps and the slow popcorn of pages instead of rifles.

if the nights are loud in your head, come knock on my door (three times, the old code). i will sit with you and not speak until the clock is gentle again.

your sister (with pockets full of warm rolls),
e.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
