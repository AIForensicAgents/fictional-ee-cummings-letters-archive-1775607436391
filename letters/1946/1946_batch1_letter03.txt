lesson of breath

Date: 1946-04-28
Recipient: Miss Adelaide Finch
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1946
Themes: art, gratitude, study, healing, language

dear Miss Finch,

i keep your chalk-dusted breath folded inside my notebook, the one bound in blue cloth that looks like the sky trying to remember itself. you once said: a poem is what happens when language forgives us. lately i am very much in need of forgiveness.

in the library after work (my fingers smell of carbon paper and paste) i read the thin spined voices. some nights they feel like careful ladders. other nights i fall through every rung and land in my own hush. i have enrolled in the veterans' class even though i am only veteran of rooftops and fire escapes; the bursar smiled and waved me in as if anyone carrying a heart has earned it.

teach me again how to breathe between lines. how to make a line that breathes someone else. i practice on the way home: the trolley sparks, a child sings to her mitten, a woman loses her heel and keeps walking brave as a metronome. are these not poems trying to be born through me.

if you ever tire of proper letters, send me just a comma. i will place it at the exact moment my lungs most require turning.

gratefully and a little afraid,
n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
