Rain telegram without a stamp

Date: 1946-06-15
Recipient: L.
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1946
Themes: city, music, memory, desire, rain

l—

the city today is written in italics. rain performing on every sheet metal roof, every hat brim, every puddle that believes itself a lake. i carry an umbrella that behaves like a black flower and i keep opening it too late; the water adores me. my shoes invent a small river with every step.

last night i stood on 52nd between a trumpet and a door. the air was all elbows and laughter. there are notes that refuse to walk on the sidewalk; they swing from the fire escapes and catcall the moon. i thought of your wrists teaching cigarettes to glow. i thought of your hair supervising the damp. forgive me—memory is a terrible photographer; it underexposes what hurts and frames too closely what we almost had.

i rented a room with a view of brick and laundry. somewhere on the line a shirt flaps apocalypse, a towel spells out prayer. mornings i buy a paper and underline the weather. the headlines keep promising a future like a movie matinee; we stumble into it, blinking, polite.

you once mailed me a matchbook that said light carefully. i keep it by the sink. some nights i strike one and write letters to your shadow on the wall. we have long conversations about bridges. the shadow is sensible; it agrees to nothing but gravity.

if you should come uptown, carry silence in your pocket. i will spill mine on the table and we will count to nothing together, expertly.

rain-rinsed,

d.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
