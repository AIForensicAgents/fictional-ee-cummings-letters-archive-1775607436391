monsoon in a matchbox

Date: 1946-07-02
Recipient: Mother
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1946
Themes: home, rain, work, memory, belonging

dear Mother,

the sky here keeps matches in its sleeves. it strikes one on the bone of the mountain and the whole afternoon flares into rain. we run to the porch with the tin roof and the drops drum our names in morse neither of us remembers perfectly, though we pretend we do and answer back with our knuckles on the rail.

i bought a string of red chiles for your kitchen doorway; when it dries it will look like a hymn. the woman who sold it wore a silver crescent on her wrist and called me honey as if that could cure everything. perhaps it can. i keep a little honey for the bus station, where the hours are always biting.

do not worry about money. i am painting signs for a grocer who believes peaches should be written in the kind of cursive that makes you taste them. at night i sleep on the daybed covered in father’s old army blanket. it holds the shape of him without the war and i fold myself there like a good letter.

if the storm breaks the telephone again, tie your news to the cottonwood. i will hear it when the leaves learn my name.

your daughter with damp hair and clean feet,
rosa.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
