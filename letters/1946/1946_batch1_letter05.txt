river with a brass throat

Date: 1946-10-13
Recipient: Lena
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1946
Themes: desire, music, night, city, chance

dear Lena,

the river tonight has a brass throat and every boat is a trumpet. i kept thinking of your shoulder blade, how it turns under silk like a small moon looking for its tide. at the club a woman sang so close to the floor her sorrow had knees. we all wanted to kneel with her and instead we ordered another drink.

there is a smell here made of sugar and engines. the streetcar sweats a little. a boy sells newspapers with a grin you could catch fish with and i think, if i kiss you again the city might fog the windows and let us be two warm animals behind the glass.

i send you a napkin full of powdered sugar folded into this envelope. when you open it, a sweet winter will fall on your table. think of us two powdered saints, laughing. think of the bandleader tossing his hair like water. your name in my mouth keeps remaking the night into a room we can afford.

don’t wait for me at the corner with the jealous magnolia. i want to find you by mistake, as the horn finds the exact ache it was born to cure.

love not later but now now now,
j.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
