to the station clock at midnight

Date: 1946-11-06
Recipient: The Station Clock (12th Street)
Location: Kansas City, Missouri
Year: 1946
Themes: time, music, travel, restlessness, night

dear Station Clock,

you have been staring without blinking since the war learned how to end. you wear midnight like a modest suit, not showing off your seconds though they are handsome. i have stood beneath you between trains and saxophones and felt the minutes gather in my coat like stray cats refusing to be shooed.

teach me your patience. men tip their hats to you and women check you with a glance that scolds and forgives at once. you never hurry a late kiss nor scold a brave goodbye. when the last car hums itself small and the platform becomes a bed for cigarette ends, you still hold the note.

i confess i have lied to you (said five when it was nearly six) just to keep a conversation sweet a little longer. forgive me. time is a street i cross without looking sometimes, believing your hands will stop the cars.

keep ticking, kind tyrant. i will meet the train i owe. when the band down on 12th throws its gold into the dark and the dancers forget their names, be the one thing that remembers.

respectfully, a regular loiterer,
p.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
