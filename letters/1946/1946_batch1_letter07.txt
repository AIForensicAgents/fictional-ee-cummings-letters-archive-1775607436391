fog is a slow telegram

Date: 1946-08-20
Recipient: Mr. K
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1946
Themes: art, friendship, transition, ocean, work

dear Mr. K,

the fog wrote me a letter at dawn and i am forwarding it to you: dear painter, it said, i will show you the outlines of absence if you show me their color. the shipyard is sleeping like a dog that has finally chased every ambulance. i walk there mornings and count rivets by listening.

your last postcard smelled of turpentine and clementine peel. in it, the bridge kept one red string for luck and i believed you. come by the studio on mission; i have a wall that wants to be a harbor. we will staple paper to the light and call it sky until the real sky files a complaint.

do you remember the sirens that were work and not war. how we measured days by whistles blown and whistles kept. i want a new calendar sorted by tides and blank canvases, by the exact moment a kettle decides to sing.

if we are poor, let it be with paint under the nails. the cable car will forgive our pockets and carry us anyway. meet me where the street becomes ocean. bring the smallest brush.

yours in salt and primer,
h.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
