mud-sky letter for a soldier i haven’t met enough

Date: 1915-03-30
Recipient: Private Samuel R.
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1915
Themes: war, friendship, rain, hope, distance

sam,
i walked the embankment where the river keeps its own patient war, shouldering the bridges like a weary porter. all day the sky was the color of a bandage and the rain remembered everything. somewhere your boots are practicing the alphabet of mud while the guns write sentences no one should have to read.

in a café a girl sang very softly and the spoons listened with their shining faces. i bought cigarettes (they are small gray prayers that burn) and thought of your hands, the way they carried laughter before they were asked to carry other things. send a map if you can, not of trenches but of the secret countries—where men trade stories for sleep, where stars are smuggled under coats. if there is a ditch that holds your fear, tell it my name; i will keep watch from here with a pen that refuses to salute.

paris tries on spring as if it were an unsure dress: a lilac at a window, a child’s kite unquiet with joy. i put these into the envelope for you. may one petal outshout a shell.

yours in stubborn weather,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
