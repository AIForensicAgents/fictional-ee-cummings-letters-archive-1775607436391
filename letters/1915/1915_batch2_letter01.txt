Steam & Lilacs

Date: 1915-05-09
Recipient: Clara Wentworth
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1915
Themes: longing, spring, memory, trains, becoming

dear clara,

this morning the trains along the charles let loose their pale animals of breath and the river rose to pat them like some old aunt (soft! soft!) while the college trees rehearsed their green vowels. i walked beneath them with my pockets full of the smallest wind and thought how your name loosens a room of lilacs even when the window is stern with weather.

i have been learning to speak more slowly to myself, because the days insist on listening. every page i open answers back with a bird. what a racket of spring! and yet—how precise the way light buttons the sleeves of afternoon, how exact the corner where we once stopped to mend your glove with my impatient laughter.

last night a whistle stitched two towns together and i believed for an instant we were threaded to the same future: your white hat (stubborn sail), my unruly mouth (harbor in no particular country). it is ridiculous to be so convinced by simple machinery, but i confess, all iron persuades me.

i would send you a library card for my chest if the post would deliver such things; you might check out the shy courage, the unreturned fines of blushes, the mysterious volume entitled: the way you stood by the wicket fence when dusk was spelling its first long word.

please write. i promise to read you aloud to the lilacs (who are poor spellers but brilliant listeners).

faithfully (and somewhat scandalously) yours,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
