year’s end with a pocket watch full of weather

Date: 1915-12-31
Recipient: Myself (to be opened later)
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1915
Themes: time, identity, renewal, introspection, hope

dear you-who-are-not-yet,
there is a small snow arguing with the streetlamp and losing beautifully. the year closes its eyelids; behind them, fireworks practice being flowers. i have miscounted the days on purpose so i could arrive surprised. you, who will read this with a different wrist, forgive what i have not yet dared.

i inventory: a handkerchief with someone’s laughter in it; three promises, one cracked; a map of a country where the borders are erased nightly by rain; a heart that refuses to wear uniform. i release: the habit of folding myself smaller than the envelope; the reasonable shoes of fear.

if tomorrow asks an oath, give it your breath—only that. walk toward the face that undoes your practiced names. kiss sturdily. believe in the delicate. if asked what time it is, answer: almost. i will meet you there, at the corner of yes and maybe, where midnight opens a door and calls it morning.

faithfully (in fragments, which is to say wholly),
me
