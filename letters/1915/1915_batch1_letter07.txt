ink rehearses a leap

Date: 1915-07-15
Recipient: Mr. Hargrave
Location: New York City
Year: 1915
Themes: mentorship, craft, risk, art, learning

sir,
i have done as you asked: torn the good pages and kept the dangerous ones. the easy line lies down and purrs, but the true line keeps its claws; i am learning to let it scratch me awake. last night in a little hall with too few chairs, a violinist broke three hairs on his bow and smiled like an outlaw. we applauded the breakage because it told the truth about trying.

you said: jump, then discover what floor will consent to be beneath you. today i leapt from a word i did not understand and landed in a sentence that forgave my ignorance. perhaps style is only courage wearing its best hat; perhaps failures are lanterns we carry backward to see where we almost weren’t.

i send you a sketch of a street heard through rain—look, the lamplight is a throat clearing. please write, even if to tell me that the ink is arrogant and the margins are thieves. i will practice until the clumsy bird becomes a sky.

yours in apprenticeship and mild rebellion,
a.
