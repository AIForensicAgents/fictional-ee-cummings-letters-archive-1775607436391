prayer with locomotives

Date: 1915-05-06
Recipient: Father
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1915
Themes: faith, modernity, family, machinery, wonder

dear father,
i stood on the bridge and watched the train unbutton the horizon. each car was a thought that refused to sit still; the black engine, a sermon that forgot its text but saved everyone anyway. i wanted to tell you that your voice visits when the factories ring their iron bells at dusk. it says, build a tenderness large enough to live in, even if the nails are oddly shaped.

i doubt as properly as any son—measuring heaven with a ruler that keeps losing inches. yet, inside the measurement, something unmeasured flickers: a small cathedral of breath, collapsing and standing, collapsing and standing (which is to say, heart). when i kneel it is often to tie a shoe; i rise walking a little more gently, and perhaps this is also prayer.

bless the narrow streets that widen when two strangers share a grin. bless the engines that drag night across our chests so the stars can practice their shining. bless you for teaching me to mistake mercy for ordinary light, until ordinary light became mercy.

your wayward but listening,
son

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
