Small Prayer in the Stationery Drawer

Date: 1915-12-31
Recipient: Mother
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1915
Themes: family, hope, year's end, exile, prayer

dear mother,

it is the last evening of the year and the city is wearing its careful dark. the boulevards keep a straight face though somewhere a piano drops its gloves and begins to dance. i have learned enough french to order bread and accidentally propose marriage—both have ended well.

the ration lines invent patience i did not know i had. even the pigeons seem to be practicing charity, sharing their sky with brass hats and bare heads alike. the river has not lost its nerve; it carries under each bridge a quiet that could repair most arguments.

tonight i have put my resolutions in the top drawer with the envelopes: be kind before being clever; listen to boots, not rumors; keep an orange for a stranger. i remember how you folded sheets with such calm that the fabric believed in itself. i am trying to fold this year that way, edge to honest edge.

if bells ring at midnight, i will count them as your hands. if they do not, i will hum. please keep the porch lamp arguing with the dark, for my return will be a late train that cannot remember its platform.

i kiss your good sense. i obey when it tells me to eat.

your child,
E.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
