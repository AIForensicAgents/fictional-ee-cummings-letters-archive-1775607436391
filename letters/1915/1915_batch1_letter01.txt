snow writes your name on the window

Date: 1915-01-12
Recipient: Eleanor M.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1915
Themes: love, winter, longing, memory, distance

dear eleanor,
this morning the snow made a hush so complete i almost heard your eyelashes. the panes were white with a breath that did not belong to me. in that milk-white quiet i traced your name with a finger and the window shivered into river-light (how a single letter can melt a room astonishes me).

i walked to the square where the horses stood like thoughts not yet pronounced. their breath climbed into the pale air, little churches of steam, and the bells on their harnesses remembered summer as though summer were a boy who left his cap on a fence.

what is distance but a coat we keep taking off? every street makes a promise it cannot keep, but the corners fold open like envelopes and i pretend some lamp is your lamp, some door, your door. if i press my hand to the snowbank it is because i want the pulse of whiteness to answer in your stead (even no can be a beautiful syllable when you are its echo).

until thaw, keep my foolish warmth beneath your pillow. wear it when the night mistakes itself for forever.

always,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
