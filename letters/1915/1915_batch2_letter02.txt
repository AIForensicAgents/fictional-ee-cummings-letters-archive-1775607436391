Ink that Hums

Date: 1915-01-21
Recipient: Mr. Lionel Ashe
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1915
Themes: ambition, art, city, poverty, noise

lionel—

this room is the kind that mistakes a radiator for a choir. every night the iron sings its red hymn and i learn a verse about stubbornness. beyond the window, broadway practices its blinking alphabet; the snow eats light then gives it back (chewed, sweeter).

i have a desk that collapses if you ask it any serious question. the typewriter makes a small storm of certainty under my wrists—ink becoming weather—words taking off their boots and walking forward barefoot, which is to say honestly. i keep a tin of sardines for supper and a violin case full of drafts for hope. if the postman arrives while i am conducting both symphonies, he merely salutes and the building becomes briefly military.

you asked for pages. i send these in the envelope of my tomorrow (unfranked, but game). the poems insist on being exactly themselves—nervous, a little daring, a little hungry. editors prefer manners. i prefer mouths that attempt the moon and leave teethmarks.

should you find a small place for me in your respectable magazine, do not tell it that i am bringing along a runaway adverb or two. they are gentle creatures once fed.

in gratitude and unslept ambition,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
