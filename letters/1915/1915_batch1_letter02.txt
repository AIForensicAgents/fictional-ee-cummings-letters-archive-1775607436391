brown paper and breathing

Date: 1915-02-08
Recipient: Mother
Location: New York City
Year: 1915
Themes: family, city, art, poverty, comfort

dear mother,
forgive the ink which has decided to be a little storm tonight. the radiator clanks like a stubborn mule and the room smells of turpentine and toast. i have wrapped my sketches in brown paper because brown paper understands hunger and keeps things quiet.

i am learning that a penny is sometimes a prayer, sometimes a coin. when i close my eyes i can hear your kitchen—the calendar ticking, the soft armies of flour—and my heart walks home with muddy shoes, not caring about the rug. i keep well, bundled in laughter and a scarf that pretends to be sunlight. every morning the city puts on its hat of smoke and steps into the day with a broken-but-brave gait (we all do).

you asked whether i eat; yes, i do, greedily—paint, bread, oceans of coffee, and on good days a peach whose perfume is a little orchestra. your letters are quilts i crawl beneath. kiss father’s spectacles for me; tell him i see farther, not clearer, and that is, perhaps, the point.

with both hands around your name,
your child

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
