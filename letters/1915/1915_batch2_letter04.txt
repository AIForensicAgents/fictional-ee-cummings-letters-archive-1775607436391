The House with Rain for a Roof

Date: 1915-07-14
Recipient: Mira Ellery
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1915
Themes: love, coast, theater, rain, freedom

mira—

the town is stitched by ropes and laughter. fishermen talk with their shoulders and the docks are polite to the tide (up, down; up, down). afternoon performed a surprise rehearsal of rain and every porch applauded with tin pails. i carried our borrowed lantern across the boards and felt the ocean finishing a sentence for me.

today we read lines in the little theatre with the roof that leaks whenever a character tells the truth. you were brave enough to be a storm and precise enough to be a teacup. some girls in the second row looked at you as one looks at rescue. afterward we ate chowder so honest it scalded my doubts.

i keep the window unlatched for your letters. gulls arrive with nothing but complaint, which is still a kind of companionship. at night, the lighthouse pencils a circle and erases it, again and again, keeping everyone’s peril neatly corrected.

when the rain slowed, you touched my wrist as if fastening a sail; i have not been still since. if any authority demands my address, may i give your name? the house with rain for a roof is the best I have known.

the kettle is beginning a small opera. come soon and teach me the encore.

yours, as the tide is unwillingly mine,
R.
