solstice ticket

Date: 1915-06-21
Recipient: Miss Adelaide Brooke
Location: Aboard a northbound train
Year: 1915
Themes: travel, summer, anticipation, time, romance

adelaide,
the conductor punched a sun into my ticket and the day obliged. telegraph poles run beside us like polite soldiers who have promised never to overtake. somewhere a meadow blinks and forgets we are strangers. when the car leans into a curve, the whole world tilts its hat and the window empties its pockets—barns, geese, a boy waving so furiously he invents wind.

i am counting stations instead of hours. each name is a mouth learning to kiss. if we change at concord, let us not change too much; let our luggage be mostly laughter, our schedule a rumor. you once said time wears boots; today it goes barefoot, stepping very carefully through the long grass between thens and nows.

on my lap: a violet folded in newspaper (the headline shrugs and becomes perfume). if i arrive before this ink dries, keep the letter anyway; it contains the small thunder of a bell when the door opens and you breathe yes.

northward (which is to say, toward you),
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
