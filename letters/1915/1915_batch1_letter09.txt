chimneys spelling autumn to no one in particular

Date: 1915-10-27
Recipient: Agnes (next-door, sometimes singing)
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1915
Themes: city, loneliness, autumn, connection, industrial

dear agnes,
the factories cough their brown sentences and the wind edits nothing. from my window the street is a long math problem solved by leaves (answer: rust). somewhere a phonograph tries to mend evening with a torn waltz, and i can hear you through the thin wall naming your hair with pins.

i have not said hello because the stairwell keeps forgetting our feet. so i write. today i found a note in my coat that i did not write: be kind, it said, as if posted by a Noticer general. i obeyed by buying an apple and giving its shine to a boy who preferred the bruise (we are odd in our hungers).

if you need a cup of sugar, take the whole cup; if you need a song, take my window—it holds a late sun the color of tea. the city pretends to be a machine, but i saw it blush when a stray dog and a lamplighter bowed to each other. perhaps we could meet when the rain finishes spelling—to see whether two umbrellas make one weather.

yours over the banister,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
