salt pockets for a small voyager

Date: 1915-09-10
Recipient: Little Thomas
Location: Sea Bright, New Jersey
Year: 1915
Themes: childhood, ocean, play, imagination, tenderness

dear tommy,
i am putting the ocean in my pockets so i can tip it into your hands when you visit. today the waves were very busy teaching pebbles to be round. gulls argued important cases against the sky and won, mostly. a crab wrote its name in the wet sand and the tide erased it softly (which is how the sea says, start again).

i built a castle that thought it was a moon. i left a door just your size and a tower for your brave eyebrow. if you bring your red bucket, we shall invent a river and send it on errands. remember: if you listen to a shell with both ears it will whisper secrets about long journeys and small socks.

don’t grow up yet; the anchors are heavy and the maps too sure. for now, be the sort of captain who commands a fleet of soap bubbles into battle with the afternoon. i will keep the shoreline until you arrive, and the horizon will practice being wide.

with a pocketful of yeses,
aunt who is also a pirate

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
