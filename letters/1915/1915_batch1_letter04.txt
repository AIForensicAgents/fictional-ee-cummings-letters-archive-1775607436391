patriots’ day, with lilacs skulking in the stacks

Date: 1915-04-19
Recipient: Miss Marian Larkin, Librarian
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1915
Themes: books, flirtation, spring, silence, ritual

dear miss larkin,
this morning the drums paraded down the street while in your kingdom of whispers a moth rehearsed an aria against the green lamp. patriots shouted; you stamped dates—each thump an obedient thunder. i returned keats (late, as breath is late when one has been running) and borrowed a slim volume that pretends to be asleep until page three.

might i confess: i like the way your spectacles argue with the sun. the dust in the aisles is holy and unafraid; it settles upon revolutions as if they were grandfather clocks. and you, among the ladders, are the tall word that rescues the short ones from drowning. outside, the city waves flags; inside, you wave silence, and both are victories.

permit me a heresy: today i choose a country of sentences where the capital is a semicolon and the anthem is a whisper. if improperly patriotic, forgive—I was born an emigrant from anywhere-but-language. keep my card in the drawer where unruly patrons are remembered kindly.

with gratitude (and the gentle treason of admiration),
—one borrower
