christmas with a pocket of birds

Date: 1930-12-24
Recipient: Mother
Location: Lookout Mountain, Tennessee, United States
Year: 1930
Themes: home, winter, memory, ritual, reconciliation

mother,

the mountain keeps its breath tonight to warm the barns. frost stitches the fenceposts with delicate insistence. i brought oranges from town and their light is a kind of fire in a bowl. the church bell is a patient cook; it calls us to table one note at a time.

i walked the ridge before supper and felt the old boots remember my feet exactly. a hawk unbuttoned the pale sky and folded it again without apology. i thought of the year the river froze and you wrapped my hands in wool and stories until both thawed. a good story is the warmest glove.

i have come home with less noise than usual. the trains did their tall dark work and i obeyed their rhythm. on the journey i counted windows that did not wish to be counted. at every station there was someone pretending not to wait for someone else. i loved them all a little for their brave pretending.

i will mend the shed roof after breakfast, and bring the cedar in, and if it pleases you, sing the carol we both forget halfway through and then find again. keep an orange in your pocket while you sleep, for luck. i have tucked three small birds into mine: hope, forgiveness, and a future we will not rush.

your son, quieter and true,

sam

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
