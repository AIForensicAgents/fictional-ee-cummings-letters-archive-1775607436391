valentine with a jazz heart

Date: 1930-02-14
Recipient: Miss Aurelia Finch
Location: Greenwich Village, New York, New York
Year: 1930
Themes: love, music, city, desire, celebration

miss aurelia finch,

the city is wearing its red mouth tonight. down on sheridan, trumpets make a bright confession and every table forgives them with a glass. a drummer is tap-dancing on my ribs; he has rented the whole apartment of my chest and is very late on the rent, but i do not mind. such music is a fine debtor.

i bought lilacs from a woman whose hands were wintered to the bone. she said flowers remember what kisses forget. i put them in a green bottle and the room learned to breathe sideways. i am practicing your name against the window because the glass understands pressure better than paper.

your letter last week unbuttoned something in me i did not know had buttons. you wrote of a dress the color of unslept sky. i imagined it talking with your shoulders and my head becoming a well-behaved eclipse. believe me i would gladly misbehave.

a valentine is a telegram from the unreasonable. i sign this one with the abandon of a streetcar taking the corner on two wheels and a prayer. if you will meet me after the last set, we shall teach the night to swing without falling. and if you will not, i will go on hearing you anyway, the way a coin hears the pocket long after it is spent.

yours in brave syncopation,

j.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
