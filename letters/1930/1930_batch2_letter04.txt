salt ledger to a captain

Date: 1930-08-19
Recipient: Captain Jonas Hale
Location: Boston Harbor, Massachusetts
Year: 1930
Themes: sea, work, courage, friendship, time

captain hale,

i write from the wharf where ropes learn their prayers by friction. the gulls audit the morning; their white ledgers flutter and nothing quite balances. a boy sells newspapers with wet hands. the headline is a mouthful of worry, but the tide keeps its appointment without reading.

your vessel went out with a shrug of chains and a promise as blue as a bruise. the harbor, wearing its summer, watched like an old aunt who has seen too many weddings to cry. i carried your packet of letters to the clerk, and even the stamp had a salt taste. there is a honesty to the sea’s arithmetic: take, give, take, give—its decimals are made of fish.

you asked me once why i stay ashore. i answered with my pockets: empty as gulls. truth is, i am afraid of that straight horizon, the way it refuses to bend for a man’s small story. but i can hear you laughing now, and the laugh is a rope: i hold, and it holds.

yesterday i mended a net that had forgotten how to catch. i told it your name and it remembered. the sun dropped a gold coin into the water and did not ask for change back. i wish to live like that coin: spent into brightness, not retrieved.

when you write from halifax (or some longitude whose syllables taste of brine), tell me how the stars reckon you after midnight. the city has its clocks, but the ocean keeps its secrets with a louder tick.

in anchor and ash,

nathan

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
