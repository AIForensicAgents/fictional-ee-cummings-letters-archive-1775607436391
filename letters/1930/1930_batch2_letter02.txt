dust grammar of the west

Date: 1930-03-12
Recipient: Brother Eli
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1930
Themes: family, landscape, silence, work, spirituality

eli,

morning unbuttons itself slowly here, one pearl at a time, and the mesa wears the first light like a patient bruise that is learning to be sky. i have discovered new punctuation: the coyote’s pause, the raven’s ellipsis, the wind’s long em-dash that connects nowhere to nobody and makes them kin.

i help an old man mix adobe. mud, straw, sun—the recipe for a wall that remembers rain. our hands come away the same color, which is to say: brothers. he speaks little english and i speak even littler spanish; so we work in a beautiful grammar of nods. there is no exam at the end, only a house that will let shadows sleep inside it.

the train that brought me coughed itself out of the night like a preacher who forgot his own sermon. the conductor tipped his cap at the stars. someone played a harmonica and the desert answered with silence so enormous it felt like mercy.

are you eating well back east? does the stoop still catch the noon like a stray dog? tell ma the sky here is a clean plate. i think i will learn to paint it with a brush made of breath. i think the saints, if they came, would tuck their sandals under these piñons and forget their miracles on purpose.

send me the hymn we liked (but don’t send the notes). i want to hum it wrong and find it new. and when the wind shaves the dust close to the bone, i swear i can hear your laugh—small and stubborn, as if it wore boots.

from a pocketful of sun,

s.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
