postcard that never sits still

Date: 1930-03-21
Recipient: Thomas
Location: Montparnasse, Paris, France
Year: 1930
Themes: art, exile, friendship, travel, rain

thomas,

i am writing from a table that has mistaken itself for a ship. it lists toward the window, sails of cloud, streets like gray sentences everyone is still revising. the waiter is a captain of cups and shrugs. painters here argue in the deep blue language of cigarettes and palette knives. i nod as if understanding, which is a fine way to be understood.

today it rained the color of piano keys. the boulevard smelled of raincoats and bread, the good bread with handsome crusts like old books. i found a small museum of broken umbrellas beneath a bridge. each rib, a tiny cathedral. there is such architecture in collapse.

i send you sketches that are not sketches: a woman balancing a pear on the soft theater of her throat; a bicycle refusing to be ordinary; a cat that has sworn itself to mystery. in their company i am precisely the right size. paris is large but there is room for my small astonishments.

your last letter arrived with seasalt grammar. i held it to my face and inhaled the country where i learned to throw stones across a creek and call it traveling. thomas, if the atlantic is a stubborn wall, we shall be the ivy. write me a map that uses verbs for roads. i will read it out loud to this table-ship until we both arrive.

always raining toward you,

n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
