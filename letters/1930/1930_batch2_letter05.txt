ink between rain and jazz

Date: 1930-11-09
Recipient: Lucien
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1930
Themes: art, music, city, nostalgia, friendship

mon cher lucien,

after the rain, the boulevards wear their hair slicked back, and every streetlamp is a tall gentleman who has decided to glow. i drank a small, brave coffee in a glass as thin as a rumor. a pianist in the corner pulled midnight out of the instrument and folded it, carefully, into our pockets. even my silence kept time.

montparnasse swelled with umbrellas like dark mushrooms. i bought a carnation for my lapel and it spoke in red. you would have laughed at the waiter who conducted our spoons (allegro, always allegro) while a woman sang a song with no words and too many goodbyes.

i wrote a letter to my future and mailed it to my past; one of them will answer, surely. the postbox swallowed it as tenderly as a whale swallows a star. do you remember the room above the bakery we almost rented? the oven’s hush coming up through the floorboards, the shy flour on the doorknob. we decided on hunger and art, which, it turns out, are related.

money is a rumor tonight, and the moon edits the rooftops with a sharp white pencil. i walked home beside a stranger whose sadness chimed like good crystal; we did not speak, but our footsteps rhymed.

write me your messy manifesto about paint and lightning. tell me which color forgives november. send also the sketch of your hand holding nothing—i will frame the nothing and keep it on the wall above the bed, so that something may learn its shape.

je te serre fort,

r.
