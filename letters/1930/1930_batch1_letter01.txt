snow writes us in white ink

Date: 1930-01-07
Recipient: Lena
Location: Beacon Hill, Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1930
Themes: winter, longing, urban, memory, intimacy

dear lena,

the night has unbuttoned itself into snow, soft as the breath a violin keeps secret. i walked up charles street where the lamps tried their golden arithmetic upon flakes that refused to be counted. my boots invented two new countries behind me. i thought of your wrist, how a glove becomes a small theater for its shy bones, how the pulse insists even in winter.

this city is a pocketwatch of houses, brick-ticking polite hours, but the snow is a kind of untime. it stops things from being too useful. a cab, puzzled into slow music. a boy dragging a sled like an unread poem. i wanted to write you immediately, which in snow means now and also soon and also forever.

i am learning to be brave in quiet ways: to let tea steam the window and not correct its fog grammar, to trust the letter will arrive though the night swallows streets whole. my hands, thawed by the thought of your name, hold this pen as if it were a small hot bird.

when the wind stands up sudden as a question, the flakes tilt and go somewhere i cannot, and i want to follow, and i want you. say you will come when the river loosens its iron smile. i will bring a red scarf to teach the thaw its lesson of color.

faithfully (and foolish),

i
