between posters and rain

Date: 1930-11-09
Recipient: Eli
Location: Charlottenburg, Berlin, Germany
Year: 1930
Themes: city, friendship, uncertainty, art, weather

eli,

i write from a cafe whose mirror is practicing honesty badly. outside, the posters argue in colors that have learned to shout. inside, a woman in a hat the size of a patient moon drinks coffee like a verdict. the violinist at the door plays hunger's sister. we tip him with coins that are still deciding their value.

yesterday the train arrived as a long gray sentence and everyone disembarked with commas of breath. the station smelled of coal and questions. i carried your last note folded under my tongue and it tasted of cinnamon and attic dust. i am hoarding such flavors against a winter that already lowers its shoulders.

we went to the cinema and the light made mistakes very beautifully upon smoke. i wanted to be that bright error. later, rain rehearsed its soft manifesto on the pavement. i read it and understood only the part that said stay together. i am trying.

you asked if there is fear. yes, and also cake. we eat both because one makes the other possible. write me of your street, whether the lamplighter forgives the darkness easily. i will send you a scarf the color of defiance and kiss it twice before posting.

in the tender middle of weather,

h.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
