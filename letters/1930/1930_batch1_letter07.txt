letter packed with dust and small prayers

Date: 1930-09-01
Recipient: My sister Ruth
Location: Garden City, Kansas, United States
Year: 1930
Themes: drought, family, perseverance, faith, longing

ruth,

i shake this letter before sealing it and a brown weather falls to the floor. forgive me for sending the plain truth of air. the wind here has learned to unmake everything it loves. we sweep and the house sighs back into dirt. i find soil in my hair like old dreams.

ma keeps the wet cloth over the bread bowl as if it were an altar. she says a loaf can rise against anything but despair. i watch the dough and try to believe. last sunday, pastor preached that the lord remembers we are dust; i wanted to ask if he also remembers we are thirst.

today the sky turned noon into night and my voice became a hand i could not find. the corn has forgotten its own height and leans into nothing, asking. i carry pails, i mend screens, i list the names of rain i have known and cannot summon. sometimes i think of the river two states over and my mouth fills with travel.

write me of the baby, whether her lungs are teaching her gentle weather. tell me if the lilacs dared bloom on your street and whether anyone whistled them a soft road. if a train passes you, lend it my ache. when it reaches me, i will return your miracle with interest.

all my grit and love,

ella
