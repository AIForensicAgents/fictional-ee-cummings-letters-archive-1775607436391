humidity teaches the moon to improvise

Date: 1930-10-18
Recipient: Beloved M.
Location: Tremé, New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1930
Themes: music, desire, night, place, sensuality

beloved m,

the air here is a slow dancer with warm hands, and every street corner keeps a trumpet in its pocket. last night i turned a corner and the moon mispronounced my name; the clarinet corrected her gently. i walked on, forgiven.

i have learned a new arithmetic: one drum plus one porch equals three aunts clapping better than time. a boy shined my shoes until they remembered rain. i tipped him with a story about a northern winter and he laughed like a stove. you would have kissed that laugh; i did it for you in the air where everyone kisses everything eventually.

the river is heavy with the business of remembering ships. barges move as if the water were a stubborn sentence they are almost ready to read aloud. i lean over the rail and think of your shoulder, its brave hush. write me the smell of your hair after walking through apples. i will answer with trumpet and sweat.

when the night grows too bright for sleep, i drink a little and listen to the alley teach a stray cat subtle mathematics. if desire is an instrument, then i am the fool practicing scales for the joy of practicing scales. come soon. there is a balcony that requires our names.

always in the warm key,

d.
