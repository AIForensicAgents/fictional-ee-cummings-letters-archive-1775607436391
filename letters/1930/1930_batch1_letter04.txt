april desert grammar

Date: 1930-04-30
Recipient: Father
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico, United States
Year: 1930
Themes: family, independence, landscape, forgiveness, calling

father,

the desert speaks in a quiet that is not silence. the sagebrush writes its long low sentences across the floor of light and i learn to read without noise. at dusk, the hills blush the way a man does when he finally says what he meant to say three years ago. if i am disobedient, it is only to the clock.

you asked whether i am eating. yes: sun and dust and beans and sometimes a peach that believes in summer before summer comes. the nights here are a cathedral of stars. such architecture never asks for permission. a coyote invented a sermon behind the house and i believed every word because it was not in a book.

i think often of your hands, their square grammar, how they built fences that kept sheep sensible and children safer than we knew. my hands now carry a brush that forgets to measure. forgive them their soft arithmetic. when i paint this sky, the canvas opens its blue mouth and swallows my doubt whole. then returns me like a wiser seed.

i will come home when the wind spells my name on the wheat, or when you write that the porch needs mending and so does your heart. until then, know that your stubborn son kneels to the horizon with all the respect he never said at the table.

your boy,

m.
