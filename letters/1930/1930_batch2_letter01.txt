winter letter with small radiator

Date: 1930-01-27
Recipient: Marta
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1930
Themes: longing, winter, city, distance, intimacy

dear marta,

tonight the city is a white throat, breathing steam and astonishment. snow tries the window with patient fingers; sometimes it remembers to fall. the radiator (that obstinate red animal) clanks like coins in a magician’s fist and, for a moment, i am rich with heat. i keep the lamp low, so the words can grow tall.

i walked down bleecker at dusk. the grocer’s early oranges were lit from the inside (small suns trying to forget their sky). a newsboy sang headlines as if they were lullabies for wolves. some men in their hats were too tired to be men; a woman’s laugh escaped a doorway and vanished upward, as if stairs were clouds.

is there a bridge between our mouths that snow cannot untie? i write you on paper thin as the air between two slow dancers. if i fold it once, i am nearer. if twice, nearer still. i could make a crane of this page and ask it to fly south, but all cranes here are cranes of iron.

last night i typed a poem with my gloves on. the ribbon stuttered, and the letters came out like bruised fruit. i kept them, of course. anything that remembers being touched is worth saving. meanwhile the moon performed its small circus above the el, and the train applauded with sparks.

(i will learn the difficult grammar of waiting. it begins: i will see you when the lilacs forgive january.)

please send a scrap of your morning, folded into an envelope that smells of tea. i promise to hold it carefully. i promise to wear its light.

in the shiver between heartbeats,

— a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
