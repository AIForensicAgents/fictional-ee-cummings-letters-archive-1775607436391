fireworks discover our salt

Date: 1930-07-04
Recipient: Clara
Location: Provincetown Harbor, Massachusetts
Year: 1930
Themes: summer, sea, freedom, romance, celebration

clara,

the harbor tonight is a patient throat and our little boat clears it of silence. gulls invent white laughter against the dusk, and i swear the oars are writing your name on water that refuses to keep secrets. i am sunburnt where i forgot you. there is a stripe on my wrist like a quiet flag.

the town spent all its red and blue in one bright sentence. rockets climbed the dark spine of air and fell back as confetti that did not know where to land. i wanted to pin each spark to your hair, to dress the wind in applause. from shore came a band learning to march in waves. every offbeat was perfect.

i brought peaches wrapped in yesterday's news. we bit them and the day sweetened its mouth. our fingers, sticky diplomats, negotiated peace with salt. you told me the word independence tastes like mint if you say it slowly. i tried. it did.

if this letter finds you landlocked, dip it in the sink and listen. the paper will remember more ocean than i can speak. i will return when the tide tells the moon it is time, or when you write that your porch has discovered stars and needs witnesses.

yours, salt and spark,

p.
