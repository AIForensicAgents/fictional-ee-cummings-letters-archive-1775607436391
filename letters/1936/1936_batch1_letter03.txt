postcard to the wind (unsent)

Date: 1936-03-12
Recipient: Brother Ellis
Location: Oklahoma Panhandle
Year: 1936
Themes: dust, family, survival, hope, drought

ellis,

i have wrapped this in the cleanest dust i could find. it is the soft brown kind that used to be wheat, or a cow's slow afternoon, or sunday. the hard dust (the mean dust) keeps for itself.

ma says we should nail the windows tighter but the wind knows arithmetic better than we do. it adds and adds until the house equals a small cough. yesterday the sun failed its appointment and the chickens practiced the religion of hiding.

i carry water the way a violin carries silence: very close, very careful. sometimes it sings anyway. when i pour into the tin pail, the sound is a promise i am almost brave enough to believe.

you wrote that the city smells like bread. imagine that. here even words dry on the line. still, we plant. the seed is a stubborn shiny thought and the soil, if you listen late, remembers being rain.

do not send money; send a joke. send the one about the traveling hat. i will wear it around the room and we will be elsewhere for a minute. tell the streetcar i said: keep him safe, and bring him home behind a small thunder of steps.

sister m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
