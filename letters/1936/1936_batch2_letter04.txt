Inventory of Little Braveries

Date: 1936-12-03
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Flint, Michigan
Year: 1936
Themes: labor, winter, resolve, tenderness, community

Ruth,

i am making a list so my shaking has somewhere to stand.

thermos (coffee, stubborn).
wool socks (two pairs; the cold repeats itself).
a harmonica with three loyal notes.
sandwiches for trading with strangers who will not be strangers by midnight.
your scarf, which smells like the month of June pretending to be December.
a pencil, for names and promises.
one photograph of your crooked grin (medicine).

the boys at the Fisher plant talk quiet and fast, like men building a boat in a room where water is already ankles-deep. i keep thinking of the way you sew a button: firm, then slack, then firm. that is how a door is held shut against a winter nobody ordered. do not worry if you hear the word strike walking around town with its collar up. sometimes a word has to get wet before it learns to swim.

if anything happens, i will remember the recipe you taught me: breathe in for luck, out for kindness. i will hold my anger like a lantern not a hammer. i will sing the small songs so the big ones can find us.

kiss the baby’s wrists for me; they smell like the honest part of milk. tell your mother her roses are asleep but listening. leave the porch light on in case my feet forget the map. they are learning it, heel first.

yours, warmer for thinking of you,
sam

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
