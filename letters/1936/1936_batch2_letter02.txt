A Telegram That Wouldn't Learn To Rush

Date: 1936-05-14
Recipient: Lionel Graves
Location: Harlem, New York
Year: 1936
Themes: friendship, city, music, art, survival

Lionel,

i went to Western Union with a heart the size of twenty words. the clerk tapped his machine like a tiny god of lightning and said stop whenever i breathed. but what i have to tell you refuses to hurry.

last night the club on 133rd cracked open like a blue egg and out fell trumpets, reckless gold. the drummer kept exact time for all the mistakes i am. a girl in a red dress threw her laughter over the balcony rail and it climbed back up as music. our table was a geography of elbows, cheap gin, and hope. outside, laundry argued with the spring wind in six languages. i walked home beneath clotheslines of prayer.

the WPA boys are painting a wall at the school; color is a relief map of tomorrow. they let me stir the bucket and i felt like a small storm permitted to be kind. i thought of your manuscript sleeping on the radiator, the letters sweating themselves into meaning. do not let the rent collector hold your sentences by the throat. slip them out the back window at night. they know the way here.

this is not a telegram. it is a slow river learning to be a hand. take it. when you open it, listen: an elevator counting the ribs of a building; a clarinet making a door out of air; your name arriving in its Sunday suit.

love to your mother. a chair is waiting for you beside mine.

inez
