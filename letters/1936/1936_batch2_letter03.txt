Unposted, at the edge of the sea

Date: 1936-07-22
Recipient: Mother
Location: Marseille, France
Year: 1936
Themes: departure, sea, courage, family, politics

Mother,

the harbor writes its own letter over and over: gulls for commas, ropes for sentences, salt for ink. i am learning to read it with my hands. the ticket to Barcelona winks from my pocket like a mischievous coin. everyone here speaks a piece of summer i do not yet know, and still i understand the part where a loaf is broken and shared.

do not be afraid. courage is not a shout; it is a spoon. i have packed two shirts, a book with a cracked spine, and your thimble which makes all holes honest. on the quay, men argue softly about maps and women pass cups of black coffee that explain morning better than clocks. somewhere beyond the blue, a city is waking with bandages and banners. i do not intend to be a hero. i intend to wash whatever can be washed and carry whatever is heavy until it is less so.

if the wind lifts my name out of your window tonight, keep the window open. this sea rearranges the sky until it fits in a pocket. i will bring you a sky that remembers our backyard lilacs. i will come back with a handful of foreign silence to plant by the porch.

i may not send this because it already knows the address. kiss my brothers. tell father the moon here looks like a coin the poor have polished with their wishes.

your daughter, with both hands,
l.
