The Moon Rents a Room Above the Barn

Date: 1936-09-10
Recipient: Ephraim Cooley, Rural Route 3
Location: Waldo County, Maine
Year: 1936
Themes: rural life, moonlight, harvest, neighborliness, nostalgia

Ephraim,

evening has put its elbows on the hayloft and is listening to the crickets practice their bright, astonishing grammar. the moon has taken a room above the barn again, payment in silver quiet. from the kitchen the radio hums a hymn that has misplaced its shoes. i like it better that way.

we pulled the last potatoes and the dirt sighed like a gentleman unbuttoning his vest. mother lined the jars on the pantry shelf—amber, ruby, small stained-glass cathedrals for beans and beets. the brook rehearses winter under its breath. i sharpened the scythe until it believed in itself. if you were here we would whistle the way we did at fourteen, and the neighbor’s dog would write an exclamation mark across the field.

i have not forgotten the carving knife you lent me in june; it has learned my hand, so i will return it before it grows homesick. your letter said a city job might take you as far as Bangor. if it does, wear your quiet like a coat when the trolleys get loud. there is a chair for you at our table and a nail in the rafters where your cap remembers you.

tell Aunt May the asters have perfected blue. tell yourself what i am telling the lantern as i trim its wick: be small, be steady, be here.

your neighbor in moonlight and potatoes,
nathan
