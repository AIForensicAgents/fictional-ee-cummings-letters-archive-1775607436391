instructions for an ordinary spring miracle

Date: 1936-04-30
Recipient: Lina
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1936
Themes: art, work, renewal, courage, spring, WPA

lina—

today the lake put a mirror in its window and told the sky to try on april. all afternoon i stood on scaffolding the way a gull stands on a word: lightly, pretending not to fall. the boys from the project whistled the alphabet out of order and passed up buckets like secrets.

i painted a woman carrying oranges through a factory of light. the foreman came by with his hat full of rules and went away empty. color has its own union and today it struck for joy; even the gray signed the petition.

on my break i wrote your name in chalk behind the post office where no one goes. rain will find it and read it aloud to the alley cats. i will not mind. the heart needs allies more than privacy.

tonight my wrists hum like small engines. i picture you buttering bread with the radio talking about someone else's trouble. i want to mail you this wet brush and the smell of linseed and a handful of the afternoon we borrowed from the future.

be fierce at breakfast. save me a seat at the edge of your laugh.

yours in paint,

n.
