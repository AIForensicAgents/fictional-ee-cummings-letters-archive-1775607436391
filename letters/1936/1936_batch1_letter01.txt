ink & snowfall

Date: 1936-01-08
Recipient: M.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1936
Themes: winter, longing, intimacy, letters, snow

m—

tonight the snow is a quiet typewriter and every flake spells your name (slowly, carefully) across the dark. i walked home with my collar full of cold light and thought how your laughter turns corners before you do.

in the library a radiator hiccups and the windows grow their private constellations. i make tea too strong and let it forgive me. ink warms in the nib like a small animal wanting out. so i send you this small weather: my hands, ungloved; my breath, rehearsing; my foolish heart polishing its one coin of yes.

cambridge creaks like an old book. bicycles dream against railings. if you were here i would show you the bridge where the river forgets its manners and runs fast, fast, like a boy late for summer. we would lean too far, and not fall, and call that science.

sleep where you are; i will keep the night open a little longer. should tomorrow ask after you, i will say: she is busy with miracles—buttoning them, unbuttoning them—until even the snow nods and agrees.

—a

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
