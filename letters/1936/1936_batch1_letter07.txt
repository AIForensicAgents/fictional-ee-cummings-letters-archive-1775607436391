cornfield arithmetic (for samuel)

Date: 1936-08-03
Recipient: Sam
Location: Ames, Iowa
Year: 1936
Themes: rural life, friendship, time, youth, summer

sam,

the county fair has packed its tin moons and gone. what remains is the arithmetic of july, carried forward into august: 1 scarecrow, 12 crows, 3 boys pretending to be men, 9 trains we almost caught with our eyes.

i walk the rows counting mistakes the way a banker counts blessings. tassels whisper the old corn psalms. a grasshopper leaps its green equation and lands on my sleeve, solving everything for an instant.

your postcard from des moines says the city delivers newspapers to itself and still wants more. i believe it. here, news arrives on the wind and leaves the same way. we measure time by the thirst of the pump.

mother keeps a jar of buttons and a jar of thunder. she uses one for mending and one for warning; sometimes she confuses them and the house clatters beautifully.

when you return we will climb the water tower and rename the town while no one is looking. bring a pocketknife and that laugh that cuts rope without trying.

in the margin of this day i have written: stay. the sky has counter-signed in blue.

h.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
