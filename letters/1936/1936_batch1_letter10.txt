winter solstice, addressed to the future

Date: 1936-12-21
Recipient: Whoever Finds This
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1936
Themes: time, hope, winter, stars, solitude

whoever finds this,

winter has taken the desert in both hands and shaken it gently until stars fell out. i walk after dusk and my boots learn a new alphabet from the snow. the moon looks like a coin someone meant to spend on music and forgot.

i am leaving this letter in a book inside a station where trains rehearse leaving. perhaps you will find it when the year is tired of pretending to be endless. consider it a receipt for a certain small courage: i was here, and it was cold, and beauty insisted anyway.

if you are lonely, untie the day slowly. let morning spill only as much as you can drink. if you are joyful, spend it wildly; the sky has more where that came from.

i have loved a few people and a great many moments. both have forgiven me. the future is a shy animal; speak in whispers and do not look too directly.

when the sun returns past its thinnest hour, lift your face like a question and accept the answer.

with borrowed warmth,

s.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
