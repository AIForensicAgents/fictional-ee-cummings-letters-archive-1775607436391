edges of a trumpet at midnight

Date: 1936-02-21
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Harlem, New York
Year: 1936
Themes: music, city, desire, night, poverty, jazz

ruth,

midnight keeps spilling its pockets. the trumpet downstairs invents a staircase and i climb it without moving. this is how a city teaches you to dance: by erasing your feet and leaving only the yes.

the rent collects dust and the clock pretends it is important. i cook beans like a fortune teller, stirring for a sign, but the only sign is your sleeve left on my chair, holding one invisible armful of me.

uptown the moon minds everybody's business. it leans into our window like a gossip and i tell it hush; the music is writing your name in smoke and i must learn to read fast.

i sold a poem to a paper that does not pay, which is to say i traded breath for air. still, the saxophone forgives me with a note so blue it makes night look ordinary. come back before the dawn begins lying again. bring your laugh. i will meet you at the corner where the streetlamp blushes and the pavement remembers to be kind.

always,

l.
