small prayer with typewriter ribbon

Date: 1936-09-14
Recipient: Mother
Location: Cleveland, Ohio
Year: 1936
Themes: work, family, faith, newsroom, ambition

dear mother,

the presses downstairs thunder like a benevolent storm and the floor shakes the dust off its long memory. i sit between a window that refuses to close and a calendar that refuses to open and try to make sentences behave.

there is a man who brings coffee that tastes like courage. there is another who sells headlines to his own heart and never makes a profit. at noon the city leans into our door with its raincoat unbuttoned and asks what happened; we say, everything, and it nods as if it already knew.

i have learned the small prayer of the ribbon: turn me, dark to light, light to dark, until words are visible footprints of an invisible animal. some days my hands come home inked like sailors.

wages are thin but laughter is a raise. tell father i still have his fountain pen; it leaks only when i brag. tell the cat the pigeons are writing to her from the rooftops and she must be patient.

if i miss sunday dinner, save me the chair by the window. the weather and i have business.

with love and a pocket of exclamation points,

your daughter m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
