Porchlight and Dust (for M.)

Date: 1936-03-08
Recipient: M.
Location: Boise City, Oklahoma
Year: 1936
Themes: longing, dust bowl, distance, hope, trains

M—

tonight the porchlight is a small brave sun, teaching moths how to be planets. the yard is a grammar of dust; every wind speaks the same impossible sentence and still we breathe it. mother keeps a wet cloth at the sill. father whispers to the radio as if it were a visiting uncle. i write you with my elbows powdered, my lungs learning new maps.

how is chicago unlocking you. i imagine you behind a window of neon rain, your dress full of city rivers, your hair complicated with streetcar bells. do you still pin your laughter to the mirror so it will not wander when the job foreman frowns. i have saved twelve dimes in a jar that once held peaches. when i shake it, a train happens somewhere far inside me.

today the wheatless field looked like a song that forgot its words. yet the calves kneeled to drink noon from the metal trough and i believed in the small authority of thirst. i carry that belief in my pocket with your last postcard and a stub of pencil bitten thin.

if i were there: we would walk past the elevated until the rails became sentences and the stations commas and our hands the grammar nobody teaches. until then, write me your sky. i will wear it.

always (even in the grit between my teeth),
j.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
