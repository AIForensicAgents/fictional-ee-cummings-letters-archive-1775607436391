october letter from the library of leaves

Date: 1936-10-22
Recipient: Anna
Location: Berlin, Germany
Year: 1936
Themes: autumn, uncertainty, books, silence, tenderness

anna,

today the park kept all its gold in the open. leaves were not falling so much as deciding. i walked under their quiet election and thought of you behind the circulation desk, stamping the date onto the patient spines of strangers.

a book taught me how to breathe more slowly; another taught me how to open a door from the inside. i recommend both. outside, a loud certainty marched by and the pigeons considered sudden careers in distance.

i wish to speak softly into your afternoon. to say: make tea. hide nothing from the lamp. let the window correct the room with its cool grammar. if the world becomes too official, i will meet you in the index under hope, see also: lamp, tea, window.

write me which poem you kept in your pocket for emergencies. i keep a small october folded four ways, and when i unfold it the air remembers how to be kind.

until soon,

k.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
