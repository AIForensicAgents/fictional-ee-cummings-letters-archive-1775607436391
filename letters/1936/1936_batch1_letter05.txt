sea-letter with twelve gulls

Date: 1936-05-19
Recipient: Captain Father
Location: Gloucester, Massachusetts
Year: 1936
Themes: sea, memory, forgiveness, family, waiting

father,

i have been practicing the ocean on paper: line after line until the margins grow salt. gulls arrive like noisy commas and correct me. a net hangs in the shed, retired from weather; it dreams of fish the way men dream of being forgiven.

your boots stand by the door as if the door were a captain too. sometimes i wear them to take out the trash and the trash behaves like a storm. mother says i walk heavier when i miss you, that the house lists a degree or two.

do you remember teaching me the names of knots? square, bowline, clove hitch—each a way of telling rope to believe in itself. i have tied a small courage around my day and it holds even in the wind that keeps your voice in its coat.

i will come down to the docks when you return and pretend not to see you first. we will both lose that game, and it will be good to lose. bring stories that smell like diesel and moon.

with all my weather,

e.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
