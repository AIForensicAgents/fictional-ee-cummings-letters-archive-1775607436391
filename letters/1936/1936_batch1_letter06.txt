a telegram i never sent

Date: 1936-06-27
Recipient: Miss Aurelia V.
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1936
Themes: hesitation, love, modernity, language, travel

miss aurelia v.,

the post office blinks all afternoon like a tired semaphore. i stand with a form in my hand that keeps becoming a bird. the clerk says write short, monsieur, as if feeling could be folded into a word the size of stop.

so i practice:
arrived. stop. saw your blue scarf make the seine forget itself. stop. coffee bitter as truth. stop. please forgive the miracle of your hands. stop.

none of it lands. the pen stammers. outside, a boy juggles three oranges and a stare. somewhere a piano falls softly down a staircase and becomes a cafe. i order a second chair for my small loneliness and we discuss your smile as if it were a treaty.

i will not send the telegram. i will send this slower animal, this letter with its pockets full of commas. if it reaches you by autumn, treat it gently. it bruises easily where your name touches it.

yours between pauses,

a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
