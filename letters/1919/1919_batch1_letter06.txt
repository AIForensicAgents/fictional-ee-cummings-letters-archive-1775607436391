a night in june, with trains

Date: 1919-06-12
Recipient: Samuel
Location: Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
Year: 1919
Themes: friendship, travel, music, night

dear samuel,

june loosens every bolt. the city sweats out its polite words and becomes honest around midnight. from my window the rails braid and unbraid themselves—steel serpents conversing in sparks. a whistle is a mouth that knows two languages: departure and promise.

the dance hall on market let its windows bloom open. inside, a trumpet taught the walls to shine while a girl in a red dress argued convincingly for gravity. i tried not to believe her, so i kept my feet ignorant and they were grateful.

i think of you when i watch the switchman lift his lamp, deliberate as a priest with a star in his fist. we were boys when we thought distance was a punishment; now i know it is a tool. what we build with it is our business.

send word of your track repairs and your stubborn roses. my own garden is a tin cup on the sill with a basil sprig rehearsing its green. if you come by, we will lay on the roof and drink the dark until it laughs.

your friend,

—t
