snow teaches the alphabet of hush

Date: 1919-12-09
Recipient: Father
Location: Hartford, Connecticut
Year: 1919
Themes: family, winter, reconciliation, silence

dear father,

the first snow came with a priest’s careful hands, blessing the roofs one by one. it spoke a language of gentle silences; i listened as if i might be forgiven by learning it. the post office smelled of wool and resolve. i watched a boy lick an envelope like a small oath and thought of your steady mouth over the morning paper.

we quarreled in july (i remember the red impatience of those tomatoes). the garden kept growing anyway, which is one of the better jokes the world tells. i would like to place my apology there, among the stubborn vines, where it can ripen into something our knives will understand.

i have taken work where the day is honest and the hands are instructed by tools. in the evenings i read psalms to the radiator, which hisses back an amen. sometimes i practice your signature, not to forge it, but to remember the path your name takes toward itself.

if you are willing, leave the porch lamp on when it storms. i will come by with bread (good bread, with the kind of crust that believes in teeth) and we can talk in the alphabet of hush until the house knows we mean it.

your son,

—d

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
