lantern letter for winter (for M.)

Date: 1919-12-28
Recipient: M.
Location: Portland, Maine
Year: 1919
Themes: winter, hope, intimacy, quiet, new year

Dear M,

snow has been inventing its quiet all afternoon. the harbor fastens its buttons with little clicks of ice. lamps along the street bow to each other and the wind accepts or refuses according to private reasons. i lit the cane-handled lantern and the flame turned the dark into a kind of listening.

soon a new year will climb the staircase with cold feet. everyone will promise it victory and vegetables. i prefer a smaller treaty: that your hand will find my hand across the table where we forgot to put flowers. two hands are a bouquet if they agree to be.

the newspapers rehearse their astonishments about laws and hunger and the science of how far a voice can travel through air without touching. yet in the post office a woman said thank you as if she meant an entire alphabet. this is the news i keep.

i walked past the wharf and heard the ropes arguing gently with the posts about duty and rest. a gull signed its name on the wind and the name was yes. i took off my hat to it. (respect for the maritime civil service.)

will you come before January has finished sharpening its teeth. bring your worst story so we may warm it until it forgets to be sharp. i will place three oranges on the sill, and their round light will politely contradict the winter until the winter laughs and admits we are right to be here.

in case we cannot meet, know this: a lantern is only a modest star that agreed to be held. i am holding it. i am holding you.

with stubborn, excellent affection,

S.
