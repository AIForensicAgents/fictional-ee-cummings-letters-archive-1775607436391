on the matter of courage (or bread)

Date: 1919-05-17
Recipient: Editor Brown
Location: London, England
Year: 1919
Themes: art, publication, hunger, modernism

dear mr. brown,

london wears its fog like a well-cut doubt. even the pigeons are editorial. i submit, for your consideration, a handful of unconventional pieces (enclosed), which insist upon speaking with their shoes untied. they have been walking through my head for months, scuffing the corners, refusing to say sir.

you will notice an economy of punctuation. it seems to me that breath is more trustworthy than grammar, and bread more urgent than both. yesterday the baker wrapped my loaf in yesterday’s newspaper; all afternoon i ate headlines with butter. it felt correct.

if these poems offend the furniture, forgive them. i only ask that you place one (just one) in the window of your august magazine, where it may startle a clerk into remembering that his mother once blew out candles for him.

should you decline, i will continue writing on the backs of receipts, in the margins of timetables, upon the edges of sleep. courage is a small stove: one feeds it scraps and it makes soup of the day.

yours in hunger & mischief,

—r

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
