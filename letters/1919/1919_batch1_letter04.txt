ink of lilacs (a spring wage)

Date: 1919-04-30
Recipient: Addie
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1919
Themes: spring, work, family, hope

dear addie,

lilacs have begun to stud the alleys with their violet buttons, and even the stockyards smell briefly less like work. from the bookshop window i count the streetcars (orange insects with opinions) and practice selling dreams with a straight face. men in hats ask for history that flatters them; women ask for poetry that does not apologize.

i have kept back fifty cents from the week’s wage to buy paper and a bouquet small enough to fit in a teacup. extravagance, i know, but sometimes one must eat a flower to remember sweetness is not a rumor.

mrs. krupa downstairs says her boy came home all elbows and silence. she feeds him pierogi as if they were explanations. i dream of our mother’s kitchen with its buttered certainty and the scrape of her chair (a metronome for kindness).

my hands smell of ink and twine. i tie packages the way you once braided my hair, making the ends brave. please send a scrap of the garden—one stubborn dandelion, a democracy of green—and a photograph of your laugh if the camera can be taught such a trick.

spring has put a soft thumb on my throat. i am learning to sing against it.

love,

—s
