the flood of small happies

Date: 1919-07-29
Recipient: Lydia
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1919
Themes: summer, theater, sea, love

dear lydia,

the dunes tilt like sleeping ships, and the sea keeps making and unmaking its blue decisions. i am painting scenery for a theater that believes in scandalous sincerity—curtains the color of whispered cherries, a door that refuses to open unless wooed by applause.

you should see the gull that scolds us during rehearsals. he knows more about timing than our leading man, and his wings are commas correcting our speeches. i have learned to hammer without hating the nail. i have learned that sand, given patience, becomes a lover of ankles.

a child showed me a bucket full of sky (the simplest trick). an old fisherman tipped his cap to a wave. last night the moon borrowed our streetlamp and wore it down to a reasonable glow. afterwards, walking home, i could smell violets thinking about rain.

i have made a tent of my heart for your name. inside it, every afternoon naps like a clean cat. come when you can, and bring your wildest hat. i will greet it respectfully.

always yours,

—m
