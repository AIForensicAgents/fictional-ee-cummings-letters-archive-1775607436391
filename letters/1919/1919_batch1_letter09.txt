inventory of a returning pocket

Date: 1919-10-26
Recipient: Mr. Halvorsen
Location: Cleveland, Ohio
Year: 1919
Themes: poverty, humor, urban life, gratitude

dear mr. halvorsen,

please accept this unconventional account of my rent (due last thursday; i apologize the way a hat apologizes to wind). i rummaged my coat and found: one button (off-duty), a stub from the picture show where a cowboy learned manners, three crumbs that once believed in bread, a ticket to an afternoon that never arrived, and a small bravery wrapped in twine.

i can pay you on tuesday, when the factory gives me back my hours. in the meantime i have mended the stair that sighed too much, oiled the hinge that coughed at midnight, and taught the furnace not to mutter at guests. i have also convinced the banister to be kinder to the hands of children.

you are a decent landlord in a world that frequently forgets decency is cheaper than rage. i will bring an apple for your wife (not as bribe; as weather) and shovel your walk when the sky gets its dentistry.

if patience had a drawer, i would sleep in it. thank you for letting a man’s pocket arrive before his pocketbook.

respectfully,

—h

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
