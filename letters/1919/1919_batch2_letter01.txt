a little bell in the rain (to A.)

Date: 1919-03-29
Recipient: A.
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1919
Themes: spring, longing, urban quiet, awakening, intimacy

dear A,

morning untied itself like a wet ribbon and the brick houses (solemn as old shoes) breathed. a sparrow rang the sky's small bell and the trolley wires hummed a thread through everything. i walked where puddles kept the world upside down and thought of your wrist (how the pulse hides there like a shy violin).

the baker's window fogged its warm alphabet. i spelled: come. then the clouds (with their secret handwriting) corrected me to: soon. i obey both.

i have been learning the grammar of April: verbs that sprout, commas of rain, a reckless semicolon of sun. still the street carries winter in its pocket, lint and a lone button. i am a foolish umbrella, blooming late.

last night i dreamed a staircase was growing out of my chest. each step smaller, each step lighter, until the last step was a swallow. it flew toward your mouth and vanished into the word yes. when i woke the room was all windows.

write me your day, its small rebellions. press a petal in the envelope of air between us. i will unfold it with both hands. i will wear the fragrance like a brave mistake.

A—i am (without any permission) glad for you in me and me in you, two pockets inside a coat that keeps finding its own warmth while the weather tries to argue.

yours in the bright damp,

l.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
