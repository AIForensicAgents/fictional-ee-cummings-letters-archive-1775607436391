autumn letter with ragtime umbrella

Date: 1919-09-18
Recipient: Clara
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1919
Themes: music, autumn, city, yearning

dear clara,

september has unbuttoned the heat just enough to breathe. on canal street a brass band persuades the clouds to dance; even the streetcars keep time with a secret ankle. rain comes like a polite drummer—firm wrists, tender ears. i bought an umbrella the color of a piano’s smile and walked it home slowly, letting the sidewalks kiss it into local citizenship.

i think of you in your careful northern rooms, where autumn is a teacher with ink on her fingers. here, the river is a long memory pretending to be new every minute. i watch it practice forgiveness on the levee stones.

last night, in a courtyard, a woman sang a lullaby mean enough to wake the dead, sweet enough to make them grateful for the trouble. i wanted to write you immediately, but my pen was too full of listening.

if you were here i would lead you down dauphine at dusk, when shutters argue with the breeze and the sky puts on its violet habit. we would drink something foolish and read palms—our own, because we are unreliable prophets but excellent liars when promising joy.

write soon, and send me a leaf from your street. i will press it in my book of claimed inevitabilities.

affectionately,

—p
