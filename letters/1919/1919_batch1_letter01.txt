rain writes your name (in lampblack)

Date: 1919-01-14
Recipient: Marian
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1919
Themes: love, city, winter, longing

dear marian,

this city is a tall pocket full of clattering nickels & sky. the rain walks with its hands in its coat, touching everything; it smudges streetlamps until they glow like tired peaches. on the el the windows argue with their own reflections, and every bridge is a black spine, dreaming of rivers. i took my paper to a diner that smelled of coffee and sailors, where a window kept breathing my breath back to me. i wrote your name there (lightly) in the fog and it became a small moon until the waitress whisked it away with the rag that erases constellations.

i am working at being brave, which is to say i buy stamps as if they were roses and let the newsboys choreograph my afternoon. the world keeps insisting on its noisy miracles: a horse snorts steam into noon; a newsboy skips two cobblestones and does not fall; a violin groans upstairs like a door taught to sing. but tonight i wanted only your shoulder—an address that would not move. even the fire escapes look like ladders to your window.

if i close my eyes the rain writes your name in lampblack on the lids. i carry a matchbook in my chest just to read it.

always,

—a
