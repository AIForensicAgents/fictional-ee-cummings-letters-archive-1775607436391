telegram hearts & bicycle moons

Date: 1919-03-21
Recipient: Julien
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1919
Themes: friendship, art, modernity, city life

cher julien,

paris is practicing its future on every corner. posters climb the walls like new vines, and the café tables are brass islands where argument & affection dock without passports. i have seen seventeen manifestos since tuesday and each one wore a different hat. the hats are superb.

i send you this letter because telegrams feel like metallic birds that forget how to nest. paper (slow animal) carries warmth in its belly; by the time it arrives it will know the shape of my pocket, the perfume of the man who sorted it, even the sigh of the clerk who stamped its ribs.

last night a rain of bicycles crossed the street. the spokes stitched quick little moons into the puddles; the women wore laughter around their throats like scarves. i thought of your studio: your canvas a storm of possibilities where the paint is learning to pronounce its own name.

i am working on a sequence of small poems that behave like matches—each one bright, brief, leaving a necessary smoke. perhaps it is childish to want the world to be kissed awake by a word, but i have always been fond of childishness.

send me news of your colors. i will send you pockets of my city, wrapped in careful sentences.

à toi,

—e
