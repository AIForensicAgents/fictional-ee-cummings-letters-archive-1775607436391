april unbuttons

Date: 1931-04-17
Recipient: Father
Location: Concord, Massachusetts
Year: 1931
Themes: family, nature, craft, reconciliation, spring

dear father,

april has begun to unbutton the trees, one careful green at a time. the river pretends to be young again and i almost believe it. i walked past the old mill and it coughed me a century of dust; then a robin amended the whole sentence with a single note.

you asked whether i am useful. i practice usefulness by sharpening mornings, by lifting words where beams are missing. sometimes i nail two silences together and make a door. sometimes the hammer strikes my thumb and i laugh, which may be a kind of work also.

i remember your ledger smile, the way numbers behaved for you, neat little soldiers. my battalion refuses parade. they sprawl, they dance, they trip into parentheses. yet even their disorder earns a crust or saves a breath. will this console you? it consoles me when i let it.

come sit with me by the fence that fails politely to keep the meadow out. we will count cows without arriving at an answer. we will drink the good sort of quiet, the kind that returns the cup. i will tell you how i am trying to love the world the way you loved precision.

your son,
still learning the straightest line through a crooked day,
—i
