ink breathes

Date: 1931-01-12
Recipient: M.
Location: New York City
Year: 1931
Themes: love, winter, city, art, longing

dear m,

tonight the city pretends to be a quiet piano; every window a white key that forgets, then remembers, how to sing. my hands smell of ink and stations. if i close my eyes, you step from the platform of my breath and the whole street applauds with steam.

i have been painting hours onto envelopes, addressing them to your heartbeat care of morning. the postman will laugh, but i do not mind; the moon understands such foolish parcels. she drifts above tenements like a silver stamp someone licked too tenderly.

eat something warm. promise. the cold is a small bureaucrat who loves forms, the body a form he approves. we must refuse him politely (with scarves, with laughter in pockets, with the urgent grammar of touch). i will arrive when trolleys remember my name.

until then—be the city i imagine: a staircase unafraid of feet, an alley that turns into a river when no one is watching, a door which opens simply because i believe it.

kiss the margin for me; i am writing there already.

yours in breath and buttons,
—i

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
