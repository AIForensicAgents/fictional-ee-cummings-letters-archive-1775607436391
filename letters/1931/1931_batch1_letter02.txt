smokestacks and other prayers

Date: 1931-02-08
Recipient: Brother Theo
Location: Chicago
Year: 1931
Themes: family, work, faith, city, resilience

dear theo,

the river wears its gray overcoat and the bridges kneel slowly across it, as if to pray. i queued with the men this morning; the line bent around a block like a tired saxophone. we joked anyhow—jokes are small breakfasts you can eat even on an empty pocket.

work arrives like weather: sometimes a sudden sun, sometimes a rumor of rain. yesterday a foreman counted us with his thumb; i became number eleven, which felt humble and astonishing, like being the eleventh note in a ten note song. i lifted crates until my shoulders invented new alphabets.

mother's letter sat in my coat all day, keeping the chest warm. she asks if i am being good; i answer yes in the mirror and try to believe it. i have not gambled except with sleep. coffee is the only reliable miracle, and even it must be persuaded.

tell the little ones that lake michigan still smells of adventure when the wind behaves. tell yourself what i tell myself: that hunger is a tunnel and the mouth at the end belongs to morning. if you send me your laugh in a small envelope, i will pin it in my hat for courage.

give my stubborn love to everyone.

your brother who is practicing hope,
n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
