year's pocket

Date: 1931-12-31
Recipient: Self
Location: Boston
Year: 1931
Themes: time, resolve, solitude, hope, winter

dear you,

the year is a small coin at the bottom of the coat. reach, and you will find lint, a ticket stub to august, three grains of salt from the ocean you promised to remember better. reach further; there is a match that never quite learned fire, and a name someone mispronounced but meant kindly.

tonight the snow practices being soft enough for hooves. streetlamps rehearse halos, fail gracefully, try again. somewhere a clock stutters toward midnight; we forgive it because we do not walk any straighter.

what did we make? a couple of rooms out of breath. a few friends out of strangers. soup from bones and laughter from the toothache of days. what did we lose? enough to keep pockets honest.

resolve, if you must: to speak when silence is a coward and hush when noise is greedy. to hold the door for weather and for widows. to write letters that leave a lamp lit for the reader on the last line.

when morning breaks like a shy plate, pick up the cleanest pieces. arrange them on windowsill and call it a mosaic. add tea. add the first foolish bird. add the old heart, willing as ever to be broken into music.

sign here,
and begin again.

—i
