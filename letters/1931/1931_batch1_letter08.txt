trumpet weather

Date: 1931-09-04
Recipient: Lydia
Location: Harlem, New York
Year: 1931
Themes: music, night, love, freedom, city

lydia,

last night the trumpet invented a staircase and we all climbed it. upstairs was a room made entirely of yes. the drummer kept the keys but somehow every door opened. when the singer bent a note, the ceiling bent with her and a star fell out of its suit.

i saved you a chair with my heartbeat. you did not come, and still your perfume edited the air. i danced anyway, which is to say i refused to argue with my shoes. the floor forgave me my clumsy theology; faith is sometimes a syncopation.

after midnight, rain auditioned against the fire escape and won the part of morning. i walked home carrying a pocket of hot syllables. even the pigeons on 135th practiced a soft applause against the dark.

come next time. bring your yellow dress that turns alleys into parades. we will let our names forget their spelling and introduce themselves all over again as wrists and smiles. i will buy you a soda that believes in bubbles the way poets believe in breath.

until then, may your windows keep dancing.

ever,
a.
