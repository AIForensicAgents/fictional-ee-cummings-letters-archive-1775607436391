Between Station Whispers

Date: 1931-02-14
Recipient: Marion S.
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1931
Themes: longing, city, radio, winter, love

Apologies: this letter is an original work with a lyrical, experimental mood—not an imitation of any single author's style.

Marion—

The radio keeps breathing its small warm snow into the room, and somewhere between Newark and the moon I catch a station that says your name without vowels. I lay my ear against the varnished box, as if a throat could be made of walnut and wires, as if a voice could be persuaded to cross bridges the way February crosses streets—careful, cold, shining where wheels have cut.

Tonight the El hammered its bright nails into the dark. I walked under it and the city dripped a kind of music: rain from steel, shoes from strangers, a saxophone folded up like a silver pocketknife in some upstairs room where a door was left politely ajar. I thought of your hands learning to speak fluent winter, how you hold a teacup and make it confess to summer.

If you were here I would show you the tiny ship that sails my window—only a shadow of laundry on the next building, a shirt and two socks hung like white flags of surrender to the wind. This ship comes and goes as if the Atlantic were a clothesline and we were the pegs. I would say: let us be the cotton and the gale, both, so that nothing can quite drop us.

I am trying to be brave inside my coat. The streets are all piano keys we have yet to press. I keep my coins in one pocket and your last letter in the other; the money warms faster, but the paper burns longer. At midnight I spoke to the ceiling: it answered with a flake of plaster in my hair, a dandruff of old paint, proof of gravity and not of heaven. Still—love is a good liar for the soul, and I am willing to be fooled.

Write me the color of your silence tonight. Is it the blue of coal smoke, the red of your door when the sun gives up, the soft brown of bread waiting on a plate? I will answer with the sound of my footsteps crossing Third Avenue; in them you will hear every yes I have not yet said to you.

With my heart folded small, like a street map that refuses to tear,

A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
