desert grammar

Date: 1931-05-29
Recipient: Rosa
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1931
Themes: love, desert, light, silence, homecoming

rosa,

the desert speaks in very slow verbs. noon is a sentence with no commas; even the lizards must take a breath between syllables. i have learned to spell heat with my shoulders. the sky keeps its pockets turned out, nothing but blue lint and hawks.

your last note arrived folded like a small adobe room. i opened it and stepped inside; the walls were the color of your patience. i hung my hat on a question mark and waited until dusk taught the horizon to blush. then i wrote this reply on the back of my shadow.

bones bloom here, white flowers of what used to be. i touch them as if they might remember snow. there is a river, but mostly it is a parable. when it decides to exist, children chase it like a traveling circus.

i will bring you a pebble that learned music from sun. you will place it on the sill beside your other quiet instruments. we will listen to their unplayed tunes and pretend we are not the ones humming.

be well. drink water the way prayers drink us.

always,
n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
