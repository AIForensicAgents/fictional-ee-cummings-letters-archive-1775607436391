salt telegrams

Date: 1931-03-22
Recipient: Miss Clementine Ward
Location: North Atlantic, aboard S.S. Europa
Year: 1931
Themes: travel, distance, sea, anticipation, modernity

dear miss ward,

the ocean keeps writing the same blue letter and then forgetting it, line after climbing line. i stand at the rail and your name unties itself inside me like a lifeboat. gulls telegram their crooked yes from noon to dusk; every wave stamps it again.

the ship is a patient building that decides to walk. in the smoking room, men compare countries the way tailors compare cloth. i nod and drink clouds. somewhere beneath us, a whale rehearses being a continent. somewhere ahead, the harbor sharpens its green pencils.

forgive me for sending nothing but weather. i wanted to pack certainties, but all i found were soft metaphors and two very determined socks. i bring them anyway. i bring the smell of rope and varnish, the deck's old hymn for feet, the small bravery of tea.

we will choose a street where the lamps bloom early. we will let the door click behind our two shadows and hang our coats like quiet flags. you will speak; your voice will unwrinkle my voyage. i will answer; the room will learn a new horizon.

until that shore,
yours across the giddy miles,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
