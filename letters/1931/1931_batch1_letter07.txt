paper republics

Date: 1931-07-19
Recipient: Anselm
Location: Berlin
Year: 1931
Themes: art, politics, friendship, Europe, uncertainty

anselm,

streetcars ring the hour like impatient monks. posters keep blooming on walls overnight, each with its own thunderous certainty. in cafes we drink theories until our tongues invent banners. then we fold them into paper boats and float them in our beer.

you asked what i see: ink marching, eyes marching, even bread lining up to be believed. there is a seriousness here that steps carefully around laughter, as if laughter were a sleeping dog. i prefer to wake it, offer it a newspaper, and let it chew the headlines.

i read your poem in the park where statues practice not blinking. it tilted the afternoon toward kindness. a child fed pigeons her last crumbs and they didn't know what last meant; perhaps we don't either. perhaps last is only another word for fastened, and we are learning to unfasten.

meet me near the clock that forgets five. bring your ridiculous hat. we will walk into the summer like two small governments negotiating with shade. if danger arrives in its polished boots, we will answer with untranslatable jokes and pockets full of unruly metaphors.

write soon. my courage needs your handwriting.

dein freund,
n.
