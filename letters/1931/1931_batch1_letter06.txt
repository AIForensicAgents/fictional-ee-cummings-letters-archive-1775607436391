rent, and the moon

Date: 1931-06-03
Recipient: Mr. Hollis
Location: Lower East Side, New York
Year: 1931
Themes: poverty, city, humor, dignity, negotiation

dear mr hollis,

forgive the audacity of ink at this late hour. i owe you rent and three apologies. the first is for time, which ran down the stairs ahead of me and refuses to be caught. the second is for my coat, which has grown more pocket than fabric and hides coins like a shy magician. the third is for the moon, who talked me into poetry when i should have been adding.

i do have something: a promise with sturdy shoes. by the fifteenth, your key will feel less embarrassed in its own door. i have accepted two small jobs, one lifting boxes, one lifting spirits. the latter pays in sandwiches and a rumor of luck, which, if not negotiable, is nourishing.

you once told me you came to this city with a loaf, a shirt, and a name you'd borrowed. i admired that inventory, especially the borrowed name (mine keeps trying on accents in the mirror). if kindness can be rented by the week, please let me keep this room of light and steam and neighbors who argue musically.

if you must scold me, do it with that grandfatherly cigar, the one that frowns for you while your mouth forgives. i will sign the ledger like a citizen of calendars.

respectfully stubborn,
—i
