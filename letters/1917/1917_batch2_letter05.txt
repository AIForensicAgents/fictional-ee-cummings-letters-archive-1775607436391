Postscript in star-ink

Date: 1917-12-21
Recipient: Adeline
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1917
Themes: love, letters, Paris, hope, snow, distance

Adeline,

Paris is a pocket watch in the dark, ticking under a glove. after curfew the streets learn to whisper; even the cafés sip their own light. i walk beside the Seine and the river keeps my secrets the way a scarf keeps warmth, casually and completely.

your last envelope arrived sugar-dusted by the long journey, and when i opened it a small weather of home occurred. i read your sentences aloud to the lamp and it blushed. the postmarks made a constellation i pretended to understand.

snow visited this morning, a careful handwriting over roofs and statues. a boy skated on the thin ice of a fountain and the statue of a horse considered applauding. i bought two hot chestnuts and named them after us, then ate us both (forgive me) and was warmed.

soon there will be bells. i have saved a seat in the future with your name written crookedly on it. come occupy it when you can. until then i carry your letter folded like a small just-in-case sky.

with a heart precise and somewhat foolish,
Margot

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
