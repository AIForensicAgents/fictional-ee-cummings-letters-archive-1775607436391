Salt and quinine

Date: 1917-10-02
Recipient: Brother Daniel
Location: Hospital Ship, English Channel
Year: 1917
Themes: healing, sea, brotherhood, patience, recovery

Daniel,

the sea keeps tapping its teaspoon against the hull as if to ask are you awake. i answer by existing, which is the slowest yes. quinine paints my tongue a winter bitterness; the nurses drift like careful snow and take my temperature in syllables.

i am mended by increments, a watchmaker of ribs. at night the porthole invents a small moon expressly for my bedside, and the engines hum a brotherly psalm. pain has manners now; it bows and excuses itself to the corridor, then returns with fewer arguments.

send news of the orchard and the black dog who insists on belonging to no one. tell Mother that the bandage remembers her Sunday hems. i will wear the shore soon and it will fit, though i expect the sleeve called silence to be a little long.

when we dock, the gulls will criticize our posture, and i shall thank them for their honesty. until then, i practice breathing like a beginner, amazed at the ordinary air.

your somewhat salty brother,
Thomas
