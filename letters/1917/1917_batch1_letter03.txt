apartment of quiet clocks

Date: 1917-01-22
Recipient: Mrs. Whitaker
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1917
Themes: poverty, time, art, city, apology

mrs whitaker,

i owe you the courtesy of coins and bring instead a pocket of minutes. please forgive their small denominations. the poem i was writing for bread became a calendar and fell asleep; meanwhile the radiator performs a sonata in steam and every clock in my room practices being a heart.

the narrow window looks upon a sliver of street where a hat blows by at the speed of despair. down in the bakery a kind of dawn rises hourly. i confess to losing time the way some men lose umbrellas; carelessly and always in rain. yet i sweep the hours into a neat pile and promise not to track them onto your good carpet.

i will have the rent by saturday if the newspaper accepts my column about pigeons and other necessary citizens. if not, accept this letter as collateral: i pledge all unwritten mornings, my whiskers, and the single suit that believes in me.

your kindness has kept winter from locking the door. should you find a stray minute under the stair, it is mine; it escaped while i was listening too closely to silence.

respectfully (and almost solvent),
—arthur in room 3b
