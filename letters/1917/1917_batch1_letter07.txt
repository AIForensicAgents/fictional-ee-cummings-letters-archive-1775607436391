two candles, one stubbed

Date: 1917-12-24
Recipient: Father
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1917
Themes: faith, winter, family, war, yearning

father,

the city hangs a quiet wreath upon its many mouths. snow is promised but not delivered; instead there is a cold gentleness that persuades the streets to speak softly. in a little church near the market two candles argue about endurance. one is tall and certain; the other, short and stubborn, refuses to die in the draft.

i stood a long while and tried to believe the world will mend itself by morning. the organ coughed out a carol and the butcher crossed himself with hands that know both mercy and knives. a child touched the nativity donkey as if to wake it; even statues deserve astonishment.

i have not your iron faith but i have your patience with small lights. tonight i will sit in a cafe with soldiers who pretend to be boys again, and we will drink a modest christmas into our chests. i will keep a chair empty for the idea of peace. if it arrives, i promise to let it keep the change.

bless mother for wrapping the socks until they smell like home. bless you for the stern kindness that taught me how to carry what cannot be put down.

with a son's careful love,
—julien

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
