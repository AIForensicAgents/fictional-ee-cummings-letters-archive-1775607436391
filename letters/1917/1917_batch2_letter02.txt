In the quiet between shells

Date: 1917-05-18
Recipient: Father
Location: Near Arras, France
Year: 1917
Themes: war, family, courage, mud, sky, silence

Father,

between two thunder-remarks of the guns there is a peculiar daylight, thin as onion skin, where larks practice being unafraid. in that slit of quiet we remember faces the way a man remembers the taste of Sunday.

the mud has invented careful manners for our boots; we bow to it and it bows back. Jimmy swears the horizon is only a door that forgot its hinges. a shell is a loud clock without patience, but even loud clocks must rest, and when they do i count your stories like rosary beads.

i am not brave in the parade-drum sense. i am brave like a candle set behind blue glass. the sergeant says my pen scratches too much; i tell him it is the only clean knife i own.

should the worst arrive, let my pocket contain the folded map you drew when i was small, the one where the orchard sits like a green ear listening to June. i keep it near my heart where it remembers how to be simple.

your son,
Edward

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
