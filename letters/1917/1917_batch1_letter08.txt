factory-windows become rivers

Date: 1917-05-30
Recipient: Lena
Location: Lowell, Massachusetts
Year: 1917
Themes: work, friendship, freedom, machines, hope

lena,

the mill whistles teach dawn how to whistle back and all day the looms argue with a patience that would astonish god. thread leaps like small obedient lightning from finger to finger. i have learned the mathematics of bobbins and the secret that machines have dreams if you listen at lunch.

today the sun pushed through the factory windows and made rivers on the floor. we walked them with our tired shoes and did not drown. mary says there will be better wages soon; i say there will be lilacs that do not smell of grease. we are both probably right, which is a happiness rare as a quiet spool.

i finished reading the newspaper clipping you left me about women riding bicycles without permission. bless every skirt that becomes a flag. when the bell let us out, i did not go straight home. i went to the canal and practiced not belonging to the foreman.

write and tell me if the streetcar conductor still winks at you like a poorly tied bow. tell me if you have started the evening classes. i will send you a poem the next time the shuttle forgets to be serious.

in thread and thunder,
—rosa
