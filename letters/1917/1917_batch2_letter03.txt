A window on Seventh Avenue

Date: 1917-06-08
Recipient: Miss Eleanor Hart
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1917
Themes: city, art, modernity, friendship, loneliness

Miss Eleanor Hart,

i have rented a thin room above a bakery that kneads dawn into the street. Seventh Avenue looks up with a thousand buttonholes of light and asks to be fastened. newsboys fling syllables that bounce like pennies; somewhere a cornet argues with a piano and both of them win.

this noon i visited a gallery where pictures refused to stand still. chairs became squares surprised by their own shadows; a woman's face discovered its third cheek and looked pleased. i felt my pockets shyly fill with angles. when i left, the sky had been rearranged by scaffolds into an experiment.

you asked what the city is to me. it is a crowded heartbeat that insists i listen. it is also loneliness in a clean collar. i am learning to be both the window and the moth.

if you come in July, we will drink coffee the color of chestnuts and count the blue mornings on the Hudson. bring your green umbrella that refuses to open when it should; stubbornness is a kind of prayer here.

faithfully and somewhat impatiently,
Samuel
