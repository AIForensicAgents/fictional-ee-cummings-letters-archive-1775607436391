zeppelins are only swollen rain

Date: 1917-09-03
Recipient: Captain Barlow
Location: London, England
Year: 1917
Themes: courage, aerial war, duty, city, defiance

captain barlow,

your memorandum instructs us to keep our heads down when the lamps are hooded and the sirens grow teeth. i agree and yet confess that last night, when the warning moaned like an old violin, i looked up. the sky wore an ungainly belly of darkness moving slow as a thought you do not wish to think. we named it zeppelin to make it smaller, which is a type of prayer.

i will continue my duties with the ambulance and with the business of carrying strangers as if they were familiar. a man without a leg can be as light as a child when he is brave enough to faint. london keeps its humor in a tin beside the matches; i borrow from it when the streets smell of soot and milk.

if discipline requires a signature, here is mine, inked with the understanding that courage and disobedience are cousins who share a hat. should you ever need a volunteer to drive where maps refuse to agree with roads, call my name and i will answer twice.

respectfully and with a necessary upward gaze,
—t. harrow
