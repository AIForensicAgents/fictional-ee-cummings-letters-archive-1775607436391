Snowlight, with footsteps

Date: 1917-01-19
Recipient: Iris
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1917
Themes: love, winter, city, longing, anticipation

dear Iris,

snow has made the streets memorize our footsteps; the city hushes its metallic heart and every tram-bell becomes a small moon.
this afternoon i walked past the river where the ice pretends to be brave. a boy whispered to a sled and it obeyed. i thought of your hands teaching my coat its shape.

there is talk everywhere, like a draft under the door, of distant thunder with uniforms. i do not know what courage looks like, but i suspect it has your laughter stitched inside. if the world must turn to stricter colors, let us keep a secret skylight between our ribs.

i bought a paper flower from a girl with chilblains; she called the color winter-sun. i pinned it beside the stubborn button that never learned to close and imagined your name as a small fire in my pocket.

write when you can. write even if the ink freezes. i will warm your sentences against my breath until they wake and run across the page.

always,
A.
