prayer with broken umbrella

Date: 1917-10-11
Recipient: Mother
Location: Le Havre, France
Year: 1917
Themes: mother, weather, hope, travel, faith

mother,

the harbor today is a mouthful of salt and gulls, and my umbrella is a thin black skeleton that still insists upon shelter. the rain makes alphabets upon the cobbles; i cannot read them yet, but i believe they speak kindly of you. i am waiting for orders and therefore for everything. sailors walk by like moving doorways, letting bits of atlantic through.

i found a chapel whose roof leaks with sincerity. a woman lit two candles and carried three wishes between her ribs. i set down your name where the light could find it and the priest, being practical, wiped the wax tears from mary's blue sleeve. outside a man mends nets with hands that have forgiven many storms.

do not worry for my soul; it wears your good stitching and holds. i eat when hungry and write when the sea allows. if the ship is late, it is only because the horizon is wide and refuses to be measured.

kiss father and the quiet house. tell the clock to be brave. i will sail as soon as the rain remembers how to fall somewhere else.

your son, beneath a useful sky,
—p.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
