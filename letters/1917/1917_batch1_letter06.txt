a modest treatise on kissing & cartography

Date: 1917-04-09
Recipient: Agnes
Location: Baltimore, Maryland
Year: 1917
Themes: romance, maps, playfulness, spring, city

agnes, who refuses maps—

you say streets should be discovered the way lips are, by turning the wrong corner on purpose. i agree and offer the following small geography:

1) the city is a mouth that mispronounces us beautifully. we correct it only by getting lost.
2) intersections were invented to teach hesitation the manners of courage.
3) a kiss is a compass that points nowhere useful and therefore everywhere true.

this afternoon i watched a boy fly a paper kite stitched together from failed arithmetic. it knew exactly where to go. the lilacs have found a way to be blue and purple at the same time. may i come by at six with a disagreeable bouquet and an excellent apology.

i intend to lead you down charles street and up into a twilight that keeps spilling coins of birdsong. we will spend extravagantly. when at last we stand before your door, let us forget the key. let us open by laughing.

please reply with either a yes or a better idea.

errantly,
—m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
