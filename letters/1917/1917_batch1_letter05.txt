on the back of a ration ticket

Date: 1917-08-18
Recipient: R.
Location: Somewhere in Picardy, France
Year: 1917
Themes: love, war, immediacy, longing, resilience

r.,

i have borrowed the back of a ration ticket because the present is always feeding on paper. the cook calls it coffee but it is night melted in a tin. i drink it anyway and find your mouth at the bottom, a surprising sweetness, the sort that makes orders hesitate.

we move before dawn when even fear is yawning. fields fold and unfold like weary handkerchiefs. a lark practices the idea of happiness above the mules and, foolish bird, succeeds.

your last letter wore the fragrance of a streetcar and rain. i kept it under my shirt until the ink learned my heartbeat. forgive me for answering with this rough scrap; it has grease at the corners but means no disrespect. this is a world of corners.

if you could see me now, a smudge with ears, you would laugh, and my bones would remember dancing. keep the window unlatched. one day i shall climb through the evening and put down this war carefully on the floor between us like a wet coat.

until then i am
your stubborn yes,
—d.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
