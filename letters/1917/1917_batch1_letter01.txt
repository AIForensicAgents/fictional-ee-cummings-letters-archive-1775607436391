thin-spring telegram (unposted)

Date: 1917-03-14
Recipient: Marian
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1917
Themes: spring, longing, city, anticipation

dear marian—

i walked across the hard glitter of cambridge this afternoon where the trolley bells pretend to be birds and the telegraph wires hum a thin-spring hymn. i meant to post a telegram and discovered my hands making pockets of weather instead; your name kept falling through my gloves and i had to pick it up with both knees.

the river is a mirror the wind keeps breaking. boys skate where yesterday there was only careful water. i leaned against a brick building and listened to your absence like a distant piano no one admits is playing. in the shop windows, hats lean forward as if to overhear our unspoken sentences.

i have been practicing a very small courage: to breathe your possible arrival. i move the furniture of my lungs so your laughter may sit and look out. if you come, bring nothing but that smile which climbs stairs faster than feet. i will meet you at the corner where winter forgets itself and even policemen become almost human.

write soon, or do not; either way my pockets are filling with the blue of your maybe. if i do not send this, forgive me. not every telegram must be electricity; some are merely a whisper finding its address.

yours in patient astonishment,
—h.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
