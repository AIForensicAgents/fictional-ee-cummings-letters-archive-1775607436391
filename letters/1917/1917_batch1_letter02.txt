mud & starlight

Date: 1917-06-01
Recipient: Brother Elias
Location: Near Arras, France
Year: 1917
Themes: war, night, brotherhood, nature, homesickness

brother elias,

tonight the guns sew crooked lightning into the soaked fields and still the sky remembers how to be quiet. i am learning the alphabet of mud; every letter begins with a boot and ends with a star. we carry men as if they are tomorrow's weather, damp and unbelievable. when the stretcher cloth warms with a body, the world reduces to four hands, a breath, and the shiver of wheels over ruts.

i keep a pebble from the charles in my pocket. when it touches my fingers, boston stands up inside me with its brick shoulders and river knees. tell father i drink my coffee without sugar and my fear the same. tell mother i am careful with my heart, though it keeps slipping off toward the skylarks who are foolish enough to sing above mathematics of shrapnel.

we sleep in a dugout whose roof is a dictionary of drips. someone whispers prayers in polish; someone mends a joke with rusty laughter. between barrages a silence drops that is not peace but the possibility of it.

if a star should ask where i belong, give it my name and a small map of our porch. i will follow.

all my clumsy love,
—n.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
