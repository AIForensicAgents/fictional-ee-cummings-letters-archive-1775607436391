postscript to a dream (stitched in blue)

Date: 1917-02-05
Recipient: Sofia
Location: Charleston, South Carolina
Year: 1917
Themes: dreams, sea, craft, friendship, longing

sofia—

i woke with the taste of the ocean on my tongue and found the needle sleeping in the pincushion like a small silver fish. all morning the shop has smelled of starch and salt as if the sea sent us its laundry. i hemmed a sailor s cuffs and thought how hands are small boats that keep not drowning.

the magnolias along meeting street are rehearsing white. a boy on a bicycle delivered a parcel of buttons bright as certain thoughts i decline to have in daylight. every thread today desires to be blue and who am i to argue with longing.

your last card spoke of geese cutting precise opinions into the sky above savannah. i loved them for their certainty. mine is of the vaguer sort, but when i close my eyes i can stitch a horizon to a window and this town becomes a dress that fits.

come when you can. i will sew you a pocket for moonlight and we will keep a quiet contraband there. if not, write me the recipe for the tea that forgives afternoons.

from the corner table, counting thimbles and blessings,
—e.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
