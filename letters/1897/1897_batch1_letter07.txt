Where the Piano Hides its Rain

Date: 1897-07-17
Recipient: Lina (the pianist upstairs)
Location: Vienna, Austria
Year: 1897
Themes: music, neighborhood, love, silence, intimacy

dear lina,
last night you practiced the second movement until even my unmusical shoes understood it. the floor between us is an obedient river; each note walked across and knocked softly at my table. i answered with a candle. it burned so still the wick forgot which way is down.

i am learning the apartment by ear. your window opens, and my books turn their pages as if to bow. a neighbor coughs, and the hallway believes itself an organ. in this fragile republic of rooms, your piano is both constitution and revolt.

when you pause, the silence is salted with possibility. perhaps you drink, perhaps you listen for the horse in the street who wears noon like a medal. perhaps you feel my listening and blush into a different key. forgive the listener who falls in love with the hands he cannot see. they are ten birds and they migrate nightly onto my chest, nesting there until my own heart plays accompaniment (out of tune, but ardent).

if you need an audience composed of a single chair and a man who claps the way rain begins, invite me. i promise not to speak except with breath.

yours two floors below,
jakob

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
