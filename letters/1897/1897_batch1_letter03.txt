A Small Thunder of Lilacs

Date: 1897-03-28
Recipient: Mrs. Josephine Hart (my landlady)
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1897
Themes: art, debt, urban life, gratitude, forgiveness

madame hart,
i owe you francs and an apology, in an order i cannot decide. if money were as easy as perfume, i would distill the breath of these lilacs and settle every account with scent. alas, flowers are extravagant accountants who refuse arithmetic.

forgive last night's piano (it was not the neighbor but the moon misusing my fingers). forgive also the sketch upon your stairwell—charcoal is a restless guest and chooses its own bed. i will wash the wall when the light is brave enough to show me what stain is really truth and what is merely shadow gossiping.

i have been painting the street after rain. each puddle keeps a second city that does not pay rent. i thought, briefly, to move there, where balconies dissolve and the roofs are generous to their reflections. but i remained, because your kettle whistles like a conscience and because you startled me this morning with kindness: two eggs placed beside my bill, as if debt could be poached and eaten.

keep the lilac sprig i left by your ledger. it is a receipt for gratitude.

yours with ink under the nails,
isaac

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
