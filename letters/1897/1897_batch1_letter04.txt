Concerning the Sea That Refuses Maps

Date: 1897-04-15
Recipient: Captain Aurelio Finch
Location: Lisbon, Portugal
Year: 1897
Themes: travel, yearning, nature, freedom, identity

dear captain finch,
from the hill above the white warehouses i watched your ship rub its shoulder against the river, a beast remembering the forest. ropes sang their low work-song and gulls finished the captain's sentences where the wind forgot them.

i brought a folded chart, careful as a sermon. but the sea laughed out of one corner of its blue mouth, saying: your paper is a very fine fiction. i agreed and loved it more for that honesty. do you, when you stand upon the bridged spine of the vessel, feel how distance drinks us like cold water? i would be cargo if it meant arrival, a crate marked fragile which is also the general condition of breath.

if you have room for a passenger who pays in stories and washes his own cup, send me a line. my hands have caught the oars of sleep each night and rowed nowhere, which is tiring work. i promise to keep company with the compass even when it sulks and to salute each star by its proper rumor.

the tide is a handwriting i am learning; be my tutor.

yours on the quay with both shoes untied,
clara
