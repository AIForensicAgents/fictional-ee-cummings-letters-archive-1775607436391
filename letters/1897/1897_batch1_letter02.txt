On Lantern Light and Geese

Date: 1897-02-19
Recipient: Brother Matthias
Location: Norwich, England
Year: 1897
Themes: faith, doubt, nature, friendship, winter

dear brother matthias,
this morning the frosted meadows unbuttoned themselves slowly, one pearl at a time, and a skein of geese stitched the sky with their unruly thread. i walked along the hedge with a lantern still burning from last night's study, the little flame standing upon its ankle as if to say: i will not sleep until you do.

prayer has been a coat too tight for me of late. i try each buttonhole and sigh the seam apart. yet i keep the lantern, whose small courage is not theology but something humbler (a promise in oil). the bell called prime; within, your voice was a matched candle to its iron tongue. i stood outside and answered with my silence, which felt less like refusal than listening to the field itself breathe.

is doubt a feather or a stone? carried in the pocket, both are felt. and yet, when the geese bent south, their music made a staircase even my clumsiest foot might climb.

i will come before lent with the borrowed ladder we laughed over last spring. we will repair the gutter and, if you permit, the leak in certain psalms where my heart drips through.

light the kettle when you can. the lantern and i are coming home.

affectionately,
thomas
