For the Child with Pockets Full of Stars

Date: 1897-08-30
Recipient: My niece, Ruth
Location: Dubuque, Iowa
Year: 1897
Themes: childhood, imagination, encouragement, family, education

little ruth,
i send you a postcard of the river that decided to become a road and then changed its mind. keep it under your pillow so dreams may have a map and disobey it sweetly.

your mother says you collect buttons and keep them like small moons. good. every tailor is a captain, every dress a ship. when you sew a button you command the ocean to stay closed a little longer. remember that ships open, so do dresses, so do hearts. sometimes they open at the wrong weather. that's all right; umbrellas are simply patience wearing a hat.

i enclose a pencil. it is a wand that only performs when you are kind to paper. draw the tallest grass. draw a cricket who refuses to sing until you are fully listening. draw me a picture of your laugh. (i have forgotten the exact blue of it.)

when school begins, do not be afraid of numbers. they are shy animals but they like sugar. offer them pockets in your mind; they will curl there and purr.

if anyone says the world is too big, tell them your pockets disagree.

love from your uncle who trips gladly over clouds,
sam

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
