The Train Whistles Your Name

Date: 1897-01-07
Recipient: Miss Elowen Vale
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1897
Themes: longing, winter, travel, anticipation, memory

dear elowen,
this evening the station keeps breathing; iron lungs of coal and snow. the platform boards remember each footstep as if it were a small and particular storm. i stood where the porter chalks his minutes and thought, your name is a carriage that arrives whether or not i purchase a ticket.

there is soot in the winter dusk that stains letters darker than ink. i held mine for you inside the glove of my left hand until it sweated through the paper—an invention of warmth. the conductor called numbers, and each number was a bell ringing somewhere in my ribs. people hurry, which is a kind of forgetting; i waited, which is a kind of knowing.

i wanted to telegraph you a simple Yes but my tongue is rarely a wire, it is a bird unsure of slate roofs. so i send this slower coil of words: be patient with my clumsy heart. it wears two labels, outbound and return, and the man at the window says both are valid.

i have learned how tracks meet in the distance only to pretend separation up close; we shall perform that trick more tenderly, you and i. keep your lamp turned toward the wind so i can find the house of you.

yours in the steam and hush,
—e.
