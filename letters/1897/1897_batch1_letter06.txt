A Prospectus of Moonlight Investors

Date: 1897-06-21
Recipient: Mr. Percival Brigg, Esq.
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1897
Themes: urban life, imagination, entrepreneurship, nature, joy

sir,
permit me a small and luminous enterprise. i propose to purchase an ordinary roof and persuade it into a garden where the moon may be a silent partner. consider, for modest capital, these returns: tomatoes red as telegrams of joy; peas polite as commas; vines that will sign their names along the brick until the building blushes.

you will ask about figures (the arithmetic of sky). here: one watering can equals a rainfall of sincerity; three chairs yield six conversations; a trellis amortizes loneliness in a single season. the elevator becomes a beanstalk for grown people, and interest compounds nightly in silver deposits on leaves.

shareholders receive invitations to sunset. dividends are paid in mint, basil, and the perfectly unnecessary strawberry at midnight. risk includes bees, who are treasurers with wings; they are scrupulous and sometimes late.

i offer you ten percent of something certain and unledgered: the feeling that June is a verb we can conjugate together upon a city that frequently forgets to breathe.

send word by return post. i have already told the seeds to expect you.

your obedient and hopeful servant,
ellen hart

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
