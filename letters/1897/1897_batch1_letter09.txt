Sermon to a Teacup After Midnight

Date: 1897-09-12
Recipient: Miss S.
Location: Calcutta, British India
Year: 1897
Themes: heartbreak, rain, acceptance, memory, night

s.,
monsoon rehearsed at my window, then performed. the ceiling fan made a tired halo and the mosquito a liturgy of persistence. i brewed tea because the night insisted on being held by the handle. you once said cups teach us how not to burn our fingers on what we love.

i tried to write your name without sorrow but the ink broke into weather. it seems my heart has two alphabets: one for before you answered, one for after you did not. the street below kept its consequence of wheels and hooves, but within this room the only traffic was memory (
it refused to obey the constable).

i will not pretend to be gallant. i keep the kerchief you forgot, and when i press it to my lips i become ridiculous and honest, a pair of qualities that travel often together. you are happy, i hope, with the man whose shadow lengthens beside yours at evening. may he learn your quiet grammar and not interrupt.

i have poured the last of the tea into the plant on the sill. it will grow on our behalf. sleep is a country i must enter with empty hands.

without accusation, with the softest goodbye i own,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
