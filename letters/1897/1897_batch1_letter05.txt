Needlework of the Heart

Date: 1897-05-09
Recipient: Mother
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1897
Themes: family, home, forgiveness, memory, domestic life

dear mother,
all day the porch has been a kind of hymn and the magnolia a choir holding its breath between verses. i repaired my cuffs with the tin of needles you sent—each needle a grandmother of patience. how you taught me: pull the thread as if it were a tiny river needing only one good bank to keep from wandering.

i think of your hands, which are geography. i know where bread rises and where letters sleep; i know the delicate border where worry becomes prayer. when i was small i believed thimbles were bells that protected fingertips with their ringing; i believe it still, though now the ringing is mostly in my chest.

i have not done as i promised (who among us is a perfect map?). but i am working, i am honest as i can be in the weather of this age. if coins grow late this month, forgive me and spend instead the knowledge that i can almost taste your biscuits when the evening turns damp.

keep the door unlatched to summer. i will arrive with a basket of good news the moment i gather it.

your child, always a little crooked at the seam,
—m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
