Gaslight Letter to Mina with Small Galaxies

Date: 1897-11-28
Recipient: Miss Mina Hart
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1897
Themes: love, art, city, night, wonder

Mina,

The boulevard is a river of ankles and sudden conversation. From my window the lamps spill their mild astronomy; each globe keeps a pet planet, glowing in a jar of fog. I write at a café that believes in velvet and serves the moon in cups of milk. Your last letter arrived wearing a violet scent and its words pressed leaves into my pocket where they will stay all winter.

Today I walked through a gallery of strangers and found your shoulder hidden in a portrait of a girl who looked almost unafraid. Paint, when it is honest, does not dry; it keeps being wet behind the eyes. I wanted to buy that gaze and hang it above my sink so that even washing would become a ceremony.

I promised you a city without cruelty. I am a poor prophet. Yet even here, where the carriages mispronounce every corner and the stones rehearse their ache, there are kindnesses: a baker who ties his loaves with red string, a coat left on a chair as if to warm the empty room, a violinist in the alley who can play rain into being.

When I think of you, letters burst their seams and a few stars escape. Not the famous stars—the small ones that hide behind roofs and only come out when someone thinks a tender thought. You are that thought, Mina, without hurry or fence.

If winter arrives like a grand aunt with too many perfumes, let us make a window of our mouths and breathe the season thin. I send my hand across the channel of air, palm first, ready to learn your constellation.

Always nearly and nearer,

L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
