An Inventory of Leaves and Promises

Date: 1897-10-28
Recipient: Mr. Silas Beecham
Location: Edinburgh, Scotland
Year: 1897
Themes: debt, autumn, responsibility, humility, work

mr. beecham,
autumn counts its coins and pays the gutters in copper. i owe you rather more than a season's change and I carry the sum like a stone that has learned my pocket by heart. this is not avoidance but delay, those cousins who look alike in fog.

i have secured a modest position copying invoices at the warehouse by the canal. the ink there is a serious animal that bites when mishandled; it leaves teeth in my cuticle to remind me of accuracy. with these wages i will reduce the arithmetic between us until it becomes a kindness.

forgive my earlier letter in which i described money as a rumor. it is a fact, i find, especially when absent. yet i would not trade certain rumors: that a man may become more exact without becoming less gentle; that a debtor may speak to his creditor as to a fellow traveler caught in the same weather.

i enclose the first installment. it is small as a leaf and almost as earnest. the rest will follow with the regularity of this northern rain.

with respect and a broom for my own doorstep,
robert keene

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
