postcard that refuses to be small

Date: April 9, 1933
Recipient: Armand
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1933
Themes: travel, art, city, intimacy, observation

armand,
paris still spills its vowels on the stones after midnight. this is not a postcard; it is a stubborn window. lean here and you will smell the river keeping secrets from the bridges. a violin argued with a bicycle bell and they shook hands politely in the rain.

i have been sketching gargoyles from the café window. their faces remind me of our first mistakes: too proud to be ugly, too ugly to be angels. the waiter places my coffee like a promise and steals my cigarette like a thief of sadness. i forgive him because his shoes squeak a small opera across the tile.

you wrote that you fear the world is shrinking. perhaps. but the bread grows a patient room inside the loaf, and the wine insists on being a red horizon in a glass. i believe in stubborn enlargements: a blush, a yes, a street corner where two strangers briefly become the same weather.

give my regards to the studio. tell the canvases to breathe. when i return we will teach light to sit without fidgeting.

faithfully extravagant,
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
