Handkerchief with Stars

Date: 1933-07-21
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1933
Themes: sisterhood, dust bowl, hope, homesickness, letters

Dear Ruth,

Chicago steams like laundry and the trains rattle our rented plates. Your last envelope arrived with sand in its elbow; I shook the dry hymn of Kansas onto the kitchen table.

How is the orchard that is not an orchard now? I keep thinking of the peach tree holding out its dead wrists and the small defiant green you said you saw at the base—like a thought stubborn against a headache.

They say relief is coming but it moves as a slow wagon through mud that is not mud. Men here sell apples as if they were apologies. I buy two and eat one carefully for you, as if a sweetness could travel.

When the wind erases fences, please sit with your back to the stove and let your breath fence you. I have stitched a handkerchief with clumsy stars; if you press it to your face perhaps a cool night will occur.

I did a wicked, childish thing: on the El I pretended every farm between stations was our mother's, that I could jump down and plant my name like a seed and your laughter would arrive as rain. The doors clapped and made a sermon of me.

Write me which sounds are left. Does the screen door still speak? Are there crickets in that ocean of dust, playing their thorny violins? If nothing sings, sing me a list of the quiet.

Hold fast to the low green.

Your sister,
M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
