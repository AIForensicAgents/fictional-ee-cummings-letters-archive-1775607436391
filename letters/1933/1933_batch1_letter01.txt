winter with pockets of bells

Date: January 5, 1933
Recipient: Clara
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1933
Themes: winter, yearning, city, art, survival

dear clara,
this morning the radiator coughed like a tired trumpet and i poured the last coin of warmth from my hands. outside, the city lifts its gray sleeves. snow is the thinnest confetti; a newsboy's cry turns into a gull and flies off with the headline. i think of your wrists, those quiet streets where i have never been lost.

i am painting again (on borrowed daylight and a sandwich of hope). the landlord's shoes thump over my ceiling like nervous hearts. if i sell one shy canvas we will eat oranges the size of small suns, peel them with our laughter, let their bright juice argue against winter. i nailed a nail into the day so it would not fall apart and hung a cup of coffee there. it swung modestly and gave off steam that spelled your name.

please save me a corner in your afternoon, near the clean window where your smile teaches dust to waltz. i will bring a poem stitched to my coat. let us be extravagant with breathing.

all my unruly heart,
-n
