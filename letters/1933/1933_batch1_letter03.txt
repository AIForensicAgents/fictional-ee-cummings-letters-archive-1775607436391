red hills listening

Date: March 21, 1933
Recipient: Miriam
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1933
Themes: desert, hope, distance, nature, craft

miriam,
the desert has taught my shoes to be quiet. every path is a vein and the hills are listening for some brave blood. i found a blue door leaning against nobody, sun-scalded, and it opened into sky. you would have laughed that wide laugh of yours that sounds like clay cooling.

they say on the radio there is a new deal of hope. the words ride the mesas, hitch their hems, and dance with the sage. i am not a banker of promises, but i am good at keeping small wild vows: today i bent down and watered a seed with my throat's last sip; tonight i will sleep with the window open so the moon can clean me.

i am carving letters into a pinon stick. it is a walking prayer. each cut says miriam stay bright. when the evening unbuttons itself and the coyotes rehearse their soft crimes, i practice being kind to distances. if you were here, we would make a fire and feed it stories until even our shadows ate.

send me a scrap of your city, a ticket stub, a crumb of your perfume. i will answer with a pocket of dust that remembers rain.

always your barefoot accomplice,
abe

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
