salt & fireworks on the tongue

Date: July 4, 1933
Recipient: Mother
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1933
Themes: family, sea, independence day, memory, art

dear mother,
the sea keeps licking the shore like a child who cannot believe the sugar is free. gulls practice their white astonishment over our heads. i carry a paper sack of cherries and a quiet that stains my fingers red.

tonight the fireworks will try to convince the night to be otherwise. they will write surprise and swallow it back. i wish you could see the small faces turn skyward, lanterns of bone and wish. a boy just offered the ocean his kite and the ocean said yes differently and ate it.

i am painting boats with elbows and laughter. i remember how you kept the house brave with ordinary things: warm bread, a needle that always knew the way home. i keep a button from your coat in my pocket. when the wind grows unpolite i rub it until it is a small, obedient star.

i will return before the tomatoes have finished their red lesson. save me a jar of lightning on the shelf where we keep jam.

your daughter who keeps learning to float,
mae

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
