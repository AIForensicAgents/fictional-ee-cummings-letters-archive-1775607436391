august letter with careful light

Date: August 19, 1933
Recipient: Anneliese
Location: Berlin, Germany
Year: 1933
Themes: uncertainty, love, city, courage, everyday life

dear anneliese,
this morning the street wore its quiet like a fragile coat. posters spoke too loudly to the bricks; boots learned to echo more than feet. a sparrow insisted on its tiny citizenship, signing the air with quick approvals. i copied its courage into my pocket.

i keep the windows polite, curtains like unbitten nails. the bakery still smells like absolution. a woman bought two loaves and carried them as if they were infants. we nodded. there are new salutes people try on, sleeves of lightning. i prefer our old handshake that left laughter on the cuffs.

i am writing by the shallow light that sneaks past the radiator and makes a small slope on the desk. i slide your name down it and it arrives whole. i remember the park where we argued about the moon being a bruise or a promise. we were both correct; we were both forgiven by the sky.

if i must speak softly, i will not speak small. i will keep the alphabet in my mouth like warm coins and spend it on kindness. send word of your roses and whether they still argue with the fence.

all gentleness to your door,
h.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
