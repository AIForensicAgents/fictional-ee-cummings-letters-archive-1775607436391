wind writes our names backwards

Date: October 3, 1933
Recipient: Ruth
Location: Boise City, Oklahoma
Year: 1933
Themes: dust bowl, family, perseverance, nature, hope

ruth,
the wind is a nervous teacher and today it erased the lesson as fast as it gave it. dust came in on its brown horses and galloped through the parlor, turned our chairs into ghosts. i found your ribbon under the stove, a quiet river that forgot its map.

the wheat gave up early this year, bowed to the sky like a gentleman and did not rise. we stack jars of nothing on the shelf and label them patience. the well tastes of old coins and stubbornness. still, last night a thin green flame climbed the horizon where rain is rumored to live, and the frogs practiced the alphabet in their throats.

i have sewn damp cloth to the windows, pinned hope across the cracks. your boy braces his kite against a sky that keeps saying maybe. when i tuck him into whatever sleep remains, he asks whether the earth will forgive us for standing on it so hard. i tell him the earth has wide hips for carrying and a long memory for mercy.

if you can come, bring your quietest smile and that recipe for bread that becomes a Sunday. i will meet you at the road where it used to be.

my dust-covered love,
naomi

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
