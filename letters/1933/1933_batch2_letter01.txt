Small Deposits of Moonlight

Date: 1933-03-10
Recipient: A.
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1933
Themes: love, bank holiday, urban quiet, longing, resilience

Dear A—

The city holds its breath like a jar of pennies. All week the radio whispering about closed doors. I woke before the streetcars and watched the thin March light pour, nickeled and careful, into the cup of our window.

Men queue like bare trees; the teller's window is a sleepwalker's eye. I carry sixty-seven cents and your last letter. Between them I am almost wealthy.

Do you remember the old woman who fed the pigeons on Spring Street? Today they looked like crumpled notes passed during a sermon. Everyone keeps passing quiet to everyone.

I learn a new thrift: to spend my longing in small change. A breath here, your name on the back of a bus ticket, the way steam lifts from the laundress's mouth in the alley and becomes a tiny white flag.

The paper says a holiday, but it is a holiday of keys. Locks resting, pockets sleeping. Yet in the pause I hear the hinge of the world oil itself. Something unjangles. Maybe it is only my heart unpocketing you.

Tonight I will hide a slice of moon in the sugar tin for luck. Come when you can, or write the way rain writes, suddenly, everywhere. I will meet you in the vestibule of tomorrow with my hat off.

Yours in the interval,
L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
