midnight pocketful of oranges

Date: December 24, 1933
Recipient: Lydia
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1933
Themes: winter, love, holidays, tenderness, city

lydia,
all the shops have closed their eyes but the street still blinks in tinsel. i walked home carrying three oranges like small faithful suns. the snow is not fierce tonight; it is a polite handwriting across the stoops. a choir passing under my window forgot the last word and found it again like a coin in the sleeve.

i have placed a chair for you by the radiator's patient breathing. the kettle considers its future and decides to sing. i think of the year—its loud pockets, its mean mirages, its sudden candy—and how you kept finding me in the wrong rooms and making them right. when i speak your name the lamp grows warmer at the edges.

i wrapped no present except this sentence: tomorrow will be softer if we open it together. keep your scarf long and your patience longer. if you cannot come, send a corner of your laughter; i will unfold it slowly and it will become a window.

with both hands and the good half of my heart,
isaac
