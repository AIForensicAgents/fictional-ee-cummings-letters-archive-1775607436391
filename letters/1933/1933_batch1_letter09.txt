fog rehearses a bridge

Date: November 11, 1933
Recipient: Theo
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1933
Themes: construction, memory, peace, awe, friendship

dear theo,
the fog arrived with its pockets full of ladders and practiced climbing the morning. down by the strait men are stringing a red idea from rock to rock. they say it will walk across the water like a careful miracle. a rivet flies, a hammer answers, and the air becomes a factory of yes.

on the radio they remember the other november when guns stopped arguing long enough for silence to sign the treaty. i stood with my hat in my hands and counted backward from sorrow. today the ships wear their horns like low prayers. the gulls oversee without taking notes.

i saw a man bring his daughter to the site. she wore braids and seriousness. when the foreman waved, she saluted him with both hands, which is how i imagine nations should greet courage. i bought a pencil that smells of cedar and drew the first tower as if it were a tall, red violin stringed to the earth.

write soon. tell me whether your new window admits more sky than trouble.

a lamp in the fog for you,
max
