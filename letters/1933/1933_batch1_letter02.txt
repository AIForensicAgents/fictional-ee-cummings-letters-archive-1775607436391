valentine in a breadline

Date: February 14, 1933
Recipient: Brother Daniel
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1933
Themes: poverty, family, resilience, urban life, humor

dear daniel,
this morning the line made a slow river around the block, hats like small boats, pockets full of weather. a man behind me whistled a song with no center; a child counted the cracks that held us together. when the kitchen doors opened and steam came out, it was a valentine—broken, warm, honest—cut from the heart of a kettle.

do you remember when we believed coins were tiny planets, orbiting our palms? now they are comets, rare and quick. yet i keep one stubborn constellation: your letters. i fold them twice and they still shine. i carry them where a wallet should be and pay the afternoon with your jokes.

i have been drawing on torn paper, faces that look back and do not apologize. sometimes i draw our old porch, how the boards hummed when rain came like a laughing cousin. tell mother i have learned to make soup out of adjectives. it tastes like almost and afterward, but it fills the bowl.

don't worry about my ragged coat; it keeps all the important heat: stubbornness, and a very stupid hope.

yours (hungry yet somehow singing),
eli
