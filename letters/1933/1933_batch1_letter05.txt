humid brass and a moon with holes

Date: May 30, 1933
Recipient: Etta
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1933
Themes: music, sensuality, cities, joy, longing

dear etta,
last night the trumpet licked the air clean and left bite marks of gold. my dress stuck to me like a confession; the river leaned in through the screen to eavesdrop. someone danced with grief until it forgot its name. when the drummer lost a stick the floor found it and kept time with our feet.

i think of you when the notes refuse to end, when they are long wet ropes we swing on back toward childhood. you with your nails like small storms, your laugh that kicks the chair out from under sorrow. the bread here tastes of boats, and every alley has a cat that is also a priest.

i am sending you a program with sweat-bent corners, proof that joy is not delicate. the moon had neat holes in it, like it had been buttoned to the night and then unbuttoned in a hurry. come south if you can. the air is a crowded chapel and we are all holy when we breathe.

love enough to fog your mirror,
celie
