A Toast with Legal Rain

Date: 1933-12-06
Recipient: Clara
Location: Cleveland, Ohio
Year: 1933
Themes: celebration, prohibition repeal, friendship, memory, winter

Dear Clara,

Last night the street unbuttoned itself. The law, that tall uncle, went soft in the knees and the bar on Huron Street took off its false name and stood there like a bride.

We lifted glasses that were not secrets anymore and I thought of the cellars we have known: two chairs, three whispers, four knocks, and the clock pretending not to listen. How diligent we were at being careless.

Music spilled without hiding. Someone kissed the mouth of a trumpet and the room learned a new alphabet beginning with yes. I kept the cork as a fossil of hush.

Outside, snow kept its own counsel. A policeman leaned his elbow on a sigh and smiled as if the city were his cousin. I walked home with a legal warmth, astonished that joy could leave receipts.

Now what shall we do with all this daylight that used to crouch under tables? Come tomorrow and we will practice drinking slowly, like citizens. I will make a toast to the version of us that survived on passwords and tin cups.

And to the future which, if it is kind, will keep our recklessness but return our sleep.

Ever yours,
L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
