letter sewn into a coat hem

Date: 1943-04-29
Recipient: Anya
Location: Kraków, General Government
Year: 1943
Themes: secrecy, love, survival, courage, war

anya,

there is a sparrow here who does not respect announcements. he visits the sill, taps out an alphabet of crumbs, and leaves without his hat. i consider this a form of courage.

today the sky rehearsed a brightness and then thought better of it. people queue with the elegance of those who remember dancing. i keep a piece of green string in my pocket; when i touch it the street widens by one heartbeat.

i have sewn this letter into the hem because certain sentences prefer the dark. carry it like a coin that cannot be spent. if anyone asks, it is a seam;
if anyone listens, it is a road.

when the bell tolls for curfew i think of your hands—how they loosen the knot of a day without untying its usefulness. we are not alone. even the pigeons sign their names with neat gray signatures.

i will meet you on thursday at the corner where the statue points nowhere. bring only your smallest yes.

j.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
