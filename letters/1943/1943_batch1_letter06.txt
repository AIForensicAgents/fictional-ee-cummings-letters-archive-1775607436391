letter stitched with rain

Date: 1943-08-13
Recipient: Mother
Location: Guadalcanal, Solomon Islands
Year: 1943
Themes: war, nature, mother-son, endurance, faith

mother,

the rain here is an industry. it manufactures rivers, puddles, a vocabulary of drip that argues politely all night. our boots graduate slowly into small private boats. i have learned to sleep with my shoulders listening.

i won't speak of noise. i will tell you instead about a lizard the color of a difficult word, who keeps his own council beneath my helmet when i write. he approves nothing and everything with the same slow blink.

jack from ohio swears he saw a mailbox in the jungle. i believe him—it would explain how your last letter found me like a sudden clean shirt. you wrote about the peaches. i let the rain read that part aloud twice.

when we boil coffee it smells like a road leading north. sometimes the sky opens and pours a spoon of blue into my hand. i save it for when the air becomes too near.

i keep my rosary in a tobacco tin. it rattles like good news.

all my love (folded carefully),
your son, peter

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
