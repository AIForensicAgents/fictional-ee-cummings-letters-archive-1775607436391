on the troopship, night folds like a map

Date: 1943-07-21
Recipient: Rosa
Location: Somewhere on the Atlantic
Year: 1943
Themes: love, war, sea, separation, courage

rosa,

tonight the ocean rehearses its one enormous word, over and over, until even the moon believes it. we move inside a small invented city—corridors, steel stairs, a chapel with a chair pretending to be a pew. someone plays harmonica and it makes the air remember peaches.

i sleep in a bunk that teaches a man's back to fold. when the ship turns, we become pages; when it stops, we become stones. i try to write but the ink grows sea-legs and my hand sails away.

yesterday i dreamed you wore the blue dress with pockets, and in each pocket a sun. i woke with two warm coins of light and lost both in the corridor.

sometimes the night leans close and you can hear the convoy thinking—deep, careful thoughts made of distance. if fear comes, i give it a spoonful of sky and it quiets like a child.

i keep your last letter in my shirt. when the engines start their prayer i touch the paper and a door opens and you are standing there, alive as bread.

love beyond longitude,
luís
