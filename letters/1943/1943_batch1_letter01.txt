snow-window letter (for ellen)

Date: 1943-01-14
Recipient: Ellen
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1943
Themes: longing, winter, rationing, love, home front

dear ellen,

how do i say winter without saying your wrists? the village puts on its thin black gloves—sirens, curtains, the moon courteous as a streetlamp—and i walk home counting the buttons you forgot. the radio hums someone else's courage. i pour it back into the cup and it cools like a small lake.

i have learned the alphabet of ration cards, all those strict blue vowels and red consonants; but your name is the extravagant letter that refuses to queue. typewriter clacks, ash loosens from a cigarette, i imagine your white shoes discovering corridors of iodine and quiet.

last night a soldier laughed with his whole body in the doorway of the café; the sound made me believe in trains returning. if you were here i'd draw a map of my pockets: one for matches, one for fear, one for the sudden enormous happiness of your postcard—nurse on leave, picture of a palm tree like a green hand waving yes yes.

be careful of sleep. it steals the fingerprints from the world. write soon, so that i can keep a key somewhere under my tongue.

yours (completely),
noah

p.s. when it snows the rooftops practice being letters until the city finally remembers how to say your face.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
