valentine with spare buttons

Date: 1943-02-14
Recipient: Abe
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1943
Themes: love, home front, city, humor, longing

abe—

it is snowing a kind of silence that even the el cannot argue with. i saved two sugar stamps to bribe the kettle into a holiday and brewed a cup that tastes like a radio playing a slow trumpet in another apartment. call this valentine: three buttons i found in the hem of my coat (proof that the city keeps its small change), a paper heart cut from the back of the theater bill, my foolish mouth practicing your name against the cold window.

work is a ladder we carry sidewise; tonight i made the backdrop of stars behave, one pin at a time, while the chorus girls kept hiding their laughter in rouge tins. after, i walked under neon that had learned to whisper. the streets were a chessboard no one was winning. somewhere a sailor kissed the wind and lost.

i think of you in your shop with its oil-clock heart, taking apart the day to see how it ticks. send me a drawing of the wrench that remembers your hand. send me a swear word that fits in a thimble. i will return to you two aspirin and a dream of june that smells like tar on a rooftop and the first peach bobbing in ice.

mama says i ought to look at boys with meat rations, but my eyes have decided to be wartime—blackout except for your light. ridiculous, yes. brave, maybe. anyway, if this city can tape its windows and keep singing, i can be the window and you can be the song.

stay warm. put paper in your shoes the way we did when we were poor and theater was a verb. write soon, my stubborn mechanic.

kisses bundled like newspapers on a stoop—

l.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
