sandclock, heartbeat

Date: 1943-05-20
Recipient: Elias
Location: Near Bizerte, Tunisia
Year: 1943
Themes: war, nursing, love, distance, desert, hope

darling elias—

this country is a bowl of light turned upside down; the sun spills grit into our boots and the clock runs on wind. we carry water like a hymn, and the tin cups remember each mouth. i am supposed to say nothing of where we are, so let me say instead: between an emptied sky and a map of flies, with poppies sealing the edges of bandages.

last night a boy from vermont slept with his hand inside the word home. i wrote the word on the canvas with iodine, small as a freckle. he smiled—do you see how a smile can be both a coin and a wound?—and made it through till morning. sometimes the morphine is a soft bell. sometimes the night is a staircase we climb on our knees.

v-mail arrives thin as onion skin; your sentences are little windows i press my face against. your k has a limp; your g loops like a rope swing. i read them out loud to the kerosene, and it makes a low field of listening. if you could hear the jackals arguing with the moon, if you could taste this bread that remembers stones.

in the afternoons, the wind peels dates from the palms and the flies compose their black paragraphs. i rinse the bowls; i count the pulse in the hollow before the thumb; i gather what the day has broken and tell it to behave. inside that order, i sneak you: your collarbone (sudden white boat), your laugh like a matchhead, our cold january rooms where the radiator sang anger into sweet steam.

do not fear for me. i am small and many, like stitches. if i come back with silence sewn to my sleeve, pull it gently and let the thought spill. if i do not, find me in the copper bottom of your coffee cup, where the last heat keeps its promise.

be kind to your back. plant beans. kiss the neighbor's old dog for practice.

always—your m
