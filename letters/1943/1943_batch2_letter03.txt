teacups under the siren

Date: 1943-04-10
Recipient: My little Robin
Location: Bloomsbury, London
Year: 1943
Themes: motherhood, war, separation, domestic, resilience

my small bird—

this morning the siren wound itself up like the kettle and then forgot why. i set two cups anyway: one for me, one for the idea of you across the hedges and stone fences, where lambs are spelling april in their stumbling script. london has learned to walk softly; even the buses breathe through their sleeves.

i stitched your name into the inside of my glove so that when i reach for coins or courage i touch you first. yesterday the grocer had oranges that looked as if a small sun had been misplaced. i bought one and made it last the afternoon. imagine me putting the peel on the windowsill so the room would remember sweetness when the all-clear comes too late.

schoolchildren passed with their gas masks swinging like quiet clocks. i wanted to stop each one and ask what their dreams do now—do they practice air raid drill, or do they still climb apple trees without looking down? perhaps both. perhaps that is how we grow.

tell mrs. penhaligon i watered her geranium and it forgave me. tell the cat i am learning his unspelled patience. i keep the radio at a polite murmur, and when the news is a stone i drop it in my pocket and walk farther.

at night i think of your breath, the way it once made the curtains move as though the sea had discovered our small room. i keep your toothbrush for a bookmark; it leaves salt where the story needs it. be kind to the ponies. let the wind win sometimes. and when you miss me, take the lane at the top of the field and look toward london: that faint bruise of light is my whisper.

all my love packed neat as sandwiches,

mummy
