telegram never sent (because breath)

Date: 1943-05-11
Recipient: Saori
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1943
Themes: friendship, absence, resilience, home front, yearning

dear saori,

i keep your teacup turned upside down on the shelf as if weather could not find it that way. the house has learned your absence: the door now closes with both hands. fog writes the same careful kanji on the window every morning and i cannot translate it, only listen.

the newspapers have a talent for not saying your name. i walk past the pier and count gulls like unanswered telegrams. there is a radio program that promises victory before the sponsor's jingle; i turn it off and hear only the kettle, brave and exact.

i fold cranes out of grocery lists. they refuse to fly but they do become a flock of quiet in the middle of the table. i hope there is a tree where you are that lets the wind speak without demanding an oath.

if letters are bridges we will not look down. we will cross them carrying our shoes.

when you return (because the word return has both feet), we will drink the tea and you will show me how to say thank you without my mouth apologizing first.

affectionately,
a

p.s. i pressed a sprig of rosemary inside this envelope for luck; forgive the boldness of its smell.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
