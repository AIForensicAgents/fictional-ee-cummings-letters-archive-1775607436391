coal dust psalm

Date: 1943-11-17
Recipient: Sam
Location: Wheeling, West Virginia
Year: 1943
Themes: labor, brotherhood, war, endurance, home

sam,

i come home black as a canceled word, and anna wipes my face with the dishcloth that never forgives. the lamps keep their bright animal on a leash. sometimes the seam sings, sometimes it sulks. i hum a hymn that doesn't know its own address.

the union hall is busy with mittens and news. a boy read your letter aloud last night—his hands shook like windows. you wrote about a hill that would not move. i believe you; some hills are hired for their stubbornness.

i am sending you a sweater that still remembers her shoulders. do not argue with it. let it win.

coal is the night's handwriting and i am learning to read it. when i turn out the light the dark shines back with every face i miss.

put your feet where the earth is kind. if there is no such place, invent it with your next step.

your brother,
eli
