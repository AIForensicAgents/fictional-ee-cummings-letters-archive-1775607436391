dot dot and the moon

Date: 1943-12-05
Recipient: Ruth
Location: North Atlantic, Convoy Duty
Year: 1943
Themes: war, siblinghood, sea, danger, hope

sis—

nights out here are written in taps and hushes. i sit with the headphones like two cold ears borrowed from a whale, and the code crawls through them—dot dot dash, as if the ocean keeps stubbing its toe on the alphabet. we do not write places, only weather; so believe me when i say: the wind is a tall soldier with cracked lips, and he stands watch even when we sleep.

sometimes the sea grows a green handwriting that clings to the bow, phosphorescence spelling our wrong name. then the captain curses gently because light is a kind of brag, and bragging invites teeth. yesterday the depth charges punctuated a rumor until it stopped trying to be true. afterward we made cocoa from a kindness one of the cooks smuggled inside his memory of his mother's kitchen. i have never seen steam so brave.

when i am off watch i lie in my bunk and let the ship count me instead of sheep. one-two, one-two: the engines practicing heart. i think of your garden, the winter cabbages like folded fists learning to pray. tell father i finally learned to splice a line without it sulking. tell the dog the gulls are only dogs with commas for feet.

if anything happens, look for me in the pause between your pulse and the clock on the mantle. i will keep house there—dust, straighten the photographs, put the kettle on for snow. more likely, nothing grand will happen. we will arrive with the grain sacks and the uniforms and the stale jokes, and i will step onto a dock that smells like rope and fish and the inside of an old pocket, and i will look for the letter you have hidden in your glove.

kiss the baby till she protests, then kiss her again. save me the smallest corner of christmas.

love,

matt
