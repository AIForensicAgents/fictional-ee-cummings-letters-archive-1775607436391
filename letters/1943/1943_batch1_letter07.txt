for the child who will read tomorrow

Date: 1943-10-05
Recipient: My unborn daughter
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1943
Themes: parenthood, hope, home front, anticipation, love

dear small tomorrow,

your mother laughs and the apartment arranges itself: chairs choose civility, the kettle remembers mercy. outside, lincoln avenue coughs buses. a man with newspapers sails his armfuls into the hands of history and then goes home for soup.

i am building you a crib out of promises and three boards from mr. comiskey's yard. every nail has an idea. i hit my thumb and the idea blooms red and instructive.

you will be named for a star or for a woman who could not be persuaded by sorrow. we will decide it on a tuesday because tuesdays are honest.

someday you will ask about this year and i will say: people learned how to be bigger on the inside. we carried our good cups through the blackout and drank patience by candlelight. the city practiced listening. even the el train tried to whisper.

come when you are ready. the radiator is already rehearsing winter for you.

your ridiculous father,
abe

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
