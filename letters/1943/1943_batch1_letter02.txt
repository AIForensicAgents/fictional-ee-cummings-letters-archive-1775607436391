fieldpost with lilacs (imagined)

Date: 1943-03-02
Recipient: Private Benjamin Hart
Location: Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania
Year: 1943
Themes: sibling love, war, home front, hope, memory

dear ben,

the mill keeps breathing like an animal too big for its cage; all night the city sweats sparks and the river eats them, polite as a banker. i come home rinsed in iron and hear our mother's clock cough once then behave.

i planted lilacs beside the back steps in dirt that remembers your boots. ridiculous, i know—march is a gray envelope—but i wanted to write you a purple sentence with roots, something the censors wouldn't black out.

neighbors practice victory gardens in their hats. the grocer smiles with his thumb because sugar is a rumor. i stitched your initials into a handkerchief i will never let you use. you will have to come back for it. you will have to come back for everything.

when the whistle empties the sky at shiftchange i count the birds leaving it. if i reach ten before i think of you, i promise to eat another potato.

keep your feet dry. keep your laugh. keep the small tin of matches father slipped into your duffel and swore was luck.

with all my stubbornness,
maggie

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
