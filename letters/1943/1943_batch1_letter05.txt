studio window (and the sudden sun)

Date: 1943-02-26
Recipient: Clara
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1943
Themes: occupation, art, secrecy, friendship, hope

clara,

the city speaks softly now, as if the streets are asleep under the weight of their own shoes. bread arrives at dawn thin as patience; i carry it home like a violin. in my room the violets have learned to be quiet. so have i.

i paint with water because the colors have been called away for duty. still, a small sun sneaks into the glass and invents yellow on the table. a policeman watches the sky as though it might break curfew. swallows edit the air with fast commas.

if you come on thursday, bring nothing but your laugh (it fits into any pocket). we will walk by the river that has never signed a document and count the tin boats pretending to be leaves. perhaps the wind will lift the corners of the day and we will see the secret name stitched inside.

i stitched another name (yours) into the lining of my coat. that way when i pass the mirror i am never entirely alone.

yours in a low voice,
marin

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
