the quiet machine of snow

Date: 1943-12-24
Recipient: Aunt Bea
Location: London, England
Year: 1943
Themes: winter, resilience, family, faith, wartime

dear aunt bea,

i have borrowed a small slice of christmas from the baker's window—one sugared gaze—and carried it home like contraband cheer. the city eats its breath softly under the blackout; even big ben wraps his voice.

mrs. wells on the second floor has knitted a scarf loud enough to remember us. we shared a tin of pears and a hymn. the radio attempted a carol and almost did not stutter.

it began to snow while i was not looking. the flakes were modest engineers, building a white machine for silence. in its hum i could hear mother asking if i had washed behind my ears. answer: not yet; it keeps the warm in.

if the siren speaks tonight, i will light the candle anyway and let its small gold grammar finish the sentence this year could not.

be well, my practical saint. write me the recipe for your lemon biscuits, the one that forgives mistakes.

love,
maeve
