the small thunder of your name

Date: 1945-01-14
Recipient: L.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1945
Themes: longing, winter, homefront, intimacy

dear l,

the sidewalks carry little suitcases of snow and everybody’s breath is a pocket of steam.
the el rattles its silver eyelashes; pigeons sew gray buttons onto the morning.
i buy oranges with the last two ration stamps in my coat and i keep one unpeeled, a small moon for your pocket.

when i say your name in the stairwell the banister remembers your hand.
nights i tilt the lamp until its light spills like milk and i drink enough to keep your shadow fed.
sleep is a narrow bed without you; i learn to fold my bones politely.

if the ocean were a letter i would lick the stamp forever.
come home by whatever verb you can
crawl run sail fly
i am waiting at the kitchen table with the radio turned to warm.

yours (and then some),
m.
