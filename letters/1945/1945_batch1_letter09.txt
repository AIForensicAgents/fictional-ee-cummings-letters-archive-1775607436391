october inventory of small salvations

Date: 1945-10-27
Recipient: Private First Class Meyer (missing address)
Location: Bronx, New York
Year: 1945
Themes: absence, hope, ritual, friendship

meyer,

i am writing to the last place you were not.
perhaps the mail will find you the way a cat finds the one warm tile in a kitchen.

what i have saved for you:
— a chair kept warm by my coat
— the uglier of the two mugs (you said it made coffee taste braver)
— a joke i have refused to tell, so it will be new when it makes you roll your eyes
— the rent paid to the very edge of next week
— your shadow, watered daily on the stoop

what i have not saved:
— fear it kept spilling through the cracks, and i was tired of mopping

if you cannot come, send me a sign extravagant as a watermelon or small as the word okay.
i will answer either with the same mouth.

still your fool,
abe

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
