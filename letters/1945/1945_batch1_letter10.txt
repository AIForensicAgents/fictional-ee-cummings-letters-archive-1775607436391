december teaches the alphabet of quiet

Date: 1945-12-03
Recipient: Anna (by the sea)
Location: Marseille, France
Year: 1945
Themes: winter, love, rebuilding, coast

anna,

the tram bells practice their patience, and fishmongers sing the morning into being.
salt whitens the pier posts like honest gray hair.

i am learning the town’s small languages: how bread says noon, how shutters say weather,
how your name says come back when pronounced against the window.

we mend the nets with borrowed thread and borrowed time, and the sea forgives our mismatched stitches.
there is wine cheap as advice; we drink it and grow briefly wiser.

come when you can by boat by train by rumor.
i have kept a little garret of silence intact for two, with a blanket that remembers your knees.

until then i fold this letter into a gull and let it practice hovering above your door.

je t’embrasse from a stumble of stairs,
marcel

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
