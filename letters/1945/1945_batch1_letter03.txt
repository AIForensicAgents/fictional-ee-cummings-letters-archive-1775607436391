april breaks like bread

Date: 1945-04-12
Recipient: Sister Ruth
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1945
Themes: spring, grief, faith, domestic

ruth,

the river has the good manners of spring today, carrying light the way a hymn lifts a name.
jasmine climbs the fence like a polite trespasser; magnolia opens all its white mouths to pray.

the radio cracked itself open at noon and grief came out, not like thunder but like a handkerchief folded slow.
everybody in the grocer’s stood as if we had been called upon in school, and no one knew the answer we were giving with our silence.

in the afternoon i baked bread without measuring, which is to say i trusted the air.
there is a comfort in yeast, how it remembers to rise after being punched down.
we will learn it too (we always do).

tell the children to keep their nickels where their laughter is.
i will send sugar when sugar remembers us.

love,
marie
