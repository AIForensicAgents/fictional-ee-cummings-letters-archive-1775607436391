apricot moon in a cracked bowl

Date: 1945-09-14
Recipient: Kiyoshi
Location: Kyoto, Japan
Year: 1945
Themes: survival, autumn, rebuilding, absence, tenderness

dear kiyoshi,

tonight the moon sits like an apricot we forgot to finish, ripening in a chipped bowl of cloud. the temple bell counts slowly, as if our hours were heavy peaches lifted with both hands. i patched the shoji with thin paper and patience; the torn parts make new maps where light travels differently.

i watered the pine and it remembered its green. at the market a bicycle rang its small bell (a shy bird) and an old woman wrapped rice with a care that made the air kinder. we stand in the doorway of a century that has learned to breathe through a scarf. the news is ink and wind and also tea; i pour it and watch the steam write a letter to nothing that answers everything.

your sandals waited by the mat until i lined them side by side and bowed. i speak your name to the kettle because names, like water, prefer to be warm. when you return we will glue the broken bowl with gold we do not have, and our lack will shine. we will hang our laundry like white flags that belong to no army.

send me a story about the road you walk. i will fold it into the hem of my sleeve so it does not blow away when the geese attach commas to the sky.

with steady heart,

aiko
