when the guns blinked shut, the sky remembered birds

Date: 1945-05-09
Recipient: Evelyn
Location: Reims, France
Year: 1945
Themes: armistice, sisterhood, silence, spring, memory

dear evelyn,

today the metal giants unclasped their jaws and a strange, clean quiet walked in. even the mud looked unafraid. we stood in the doorway of the ward (white aprons scissored by sunlight) and waited for the roar that did not come, the way one waits for a final heartbeat in a radio and hears only the room breathing.

i have learned the music of small things: the cup that ticks when it cools, the way a match sighs out, the secret conversation between lavender soap and skin. when the announcement came, private allan laughed with his whole ribs; sergeant turner cried into a towel; i washed my hands with a sudden clumsiness, as if touching peace required a new alphabet of fingers.

someone shouted outside and a boy kissed the ground so gently you would think he was apologizing to it. a sparrow tried the rafters (did you ever know that silence is big enough to open a door for a bird?). i hung dressings like thin flags, and the wind read them, kindly.

send me the smell of our kitchen: the starch, the iron, those lilacs that keep their cool even under june. i want to stand by your line again, to watch a white sheet make a sky for the grass.

we are not finished here—quiet is a fragile bowl—but tonight i will sleep with my boots unbuttoned, and under my pillow there will be a small blue corner of tomorrow.

with both hands,

m.
