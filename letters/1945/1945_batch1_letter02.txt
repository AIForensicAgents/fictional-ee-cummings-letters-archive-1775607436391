postcard with snow and gasoline

Date: 1945-02-23
Recipient: Sergeant Eli Navarro
Location: Cleveland, Ohio
Year: 1945
Themes: homefront, labor, hope, kinship

eli,

the factory whistles wake the roofs like alarmed birds, and we march into smoke
with lunch pails that smell of yesterday’s apples.
we punch the clock as if it were a stubborn star; sparks drift like unseasonable snow.

my hands have learned the grammar of rivets (period period exclamation) and i am tired
in the good way a street is tired after a parade.
the girls in blue kerchiefs keep the radio low, as if the news were a fevered child.

mother still salts the soup as if ships could taste it from so far.
i have hidden your old harmonica behind the flour, safe from the dampness of worry.
when i breathe hard enough i hear it answering.

come back wearing your ordinary smile.
i have saved the last dance in the kitchen and two scuffed heels to teach it.

your cousin,
tomas

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
