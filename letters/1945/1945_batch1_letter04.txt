ve day (the sky learned how to breathe)

Date: 1945-05-08
Recipient: Myself (folded neatly)
Location: Piccadilly Circus, London
Year: 1945
Themes: victory, crowds, introspection, resilience

dear you-who-are-also-i,

streets erupted into confetti made of shouting; hats went flying like spare moons.
people climbed statues as if to remind stone it had a heartbeat.
a girl with lipstick the color of sirens kissed a stranger and both of them became the same word: yes.

but inside my ribs, a small quiet sat cross-legged, patient as tea.
it remembered dark stairs and buckets by the door and an alphabet whispered by candle.

learn this: joy is not the opposite of sorrow, only its other shoe.
wear both, walk home.

when the last song spills into the gutters and the lamps take off their makeup,
we will sweep up the glass and keep a bright shard for the window that is still missing.

unfold me when needed.

—a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
