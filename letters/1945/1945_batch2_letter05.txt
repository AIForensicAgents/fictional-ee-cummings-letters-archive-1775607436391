zeros and stars and the radio's blue pulse

Date: 1945-08-15
Recipient: Jack
Location: Okinawa, Ryukyu Islands
Year: 1945
Themes: peace, ocean, signal, exhaustion, brotherhood

dear jack,

the word arrived swimming through static and every face here turned into a window. i kept my hand on the dial as if peace were a fever we needed to lower gently. a man laughed and the laugh broke twice and healed the third time. somebody sang and forgot the second verse and nobody minded.

the ocean kept working, big wrists, small patience. stars salted the blackout and for the first time felt like decorations instead of orders. i could taste battery on my thumb and the sweet ghost of home inside my canteen.

we are coiling the antenna tomorrow like a long idea finally agreed upon. i have decided to keep your last letter untouched, grease stains and all; it smells like baseball and sidewalks and that river whose name we never say right. when i come back, let us go where the map gives up and the minnows translate for us. we will use quiet the way a knife uses light.

i do not know what to do with my hands yet, so i am teaching them to hold a morning. it shakes, but less each hour.

save me a seat on the porch.

sam
