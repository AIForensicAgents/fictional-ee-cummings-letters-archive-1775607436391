to my son, who does not yet know his name

Date: 1945-09-05
Recipient: Little Stranger (crib by the window)
Location: Detroit, Michigan
Year: 1945
Themes: parenthood, peace, home, tenderness

little one,

the factory across the street exhales a new quiet, gears loosening their shoulders.
crows hop along the alley collecting shiny rumors of peacetime.

your mother has wrapped you in a blanket the color of a promise kept.
when you hiccup the whole room politely pauses.

once i tightened bolts for tanks; today i fix the squeak in the rocking chair
and it feels like the bravest work i have learned.

by the time you can walk, there will be more milk, maybe even butter on ordinary days.
i will teach you how to hold a hammer without scaring the nail.

your name is waiting on the table beside the lamp, warm as bread.
we will try it on you soon, and it will fit.

love,
dad

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
