letter to a ruined window

Date: 1945-06-21
Recipient: A house on Zielna Street
Location: Warsaw, Poland
Year: 1945
Themes: ruin, rebuilding, memory, community

dear window (who once wore lace),

i remember how you practiced daylight until it fit like a glove.
children pressed their noses to your clean astonishment and learned the neighborhood by heart.

today your mouth is full of sky.
bricks crouch around you like apologetic dogs.
dust writes the names we can’t pronounce anymore, careful as a priest.

listen: a woman down the lane is boiling water for no reason except the proof of it.
a boy returns with three nails, heroic as a king.
we have found a door that belongs to no one and given it to you.

if you will hold afternoon for us again, even for a minute,
we will forgive these empty pockets.
we will hang a curtain sewn from saved corners of shirts and wedding night slips.

we are not finished.

always,
jan

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
