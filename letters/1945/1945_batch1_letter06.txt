july with one hand bandaged

Date: 1945-07-19
Recipient: Mrs. Keegan (my teacher)
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1945
Themes: healing, education, labor, harbor

mrs keegan,

i am learning to write with my left hand while my right remembers the shape of a wrench.
the shipyard whistles have grown hoarse from so much dawn, and fog keeps rehearsing its entrances.

once you said the alphabet is a small navy, each letter a brave thin boat.
well, i am launching them now into the wide paper pacific, and look—
they do not sink, even when i tremble.

last night the harbor lights braided themselves into something like a lullaby.
a train arrived with somebody’s brother and a woman’s laugh broke in two from relief.

please tell the children that splinters are only the body’s souvenirs of work.
also that ink can be a soft bandage.

sincerely (and crookedly),
frank
