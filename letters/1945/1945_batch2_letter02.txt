neon, unclenching

Date: 1945-07-01
Recipient: Thomas
Location: New York, New York, USA
Year: 1945
Themes: love, city, art, anticipation, healing

dear thomas,

the city loosened one more button and out came july: warm, electric, a little shameless. ration books thinning like old schoolpaper, and the grocer whistled something almost like comfort. i carried paint home in a paper bag that sweated through and laughed when the blue dripped on my shoe. the stain looks like a small atlantic and i keep stepping in it to remind myself of distance.

i am building you a room on canvas. the windows refuse straight lines (bless them), and the bed is a good idea rather than a rectangle. our street has learned to glow differently. neon returns as if it were a shy animal: paw, pause, paw. i drink seltzer and the bubbles practice the alphabet of your name.

on rooftops the future is practicing too; someone tried fireworks yesterday, only a whisper high, as if not to offend the tender ceiling of peace. i stood where the pigeons rehearse their ordinary miracles and promised a cup of coffee to every brave morning we have left.

come home to the chair by the window that looks like a question mark. bring your stubborn laugh and that sweater with the missing elbow (i will darn it with a thread the color of summer rain). we will learn how to say ordinary again, softly, until the word fits.

hurry slow,

lena
