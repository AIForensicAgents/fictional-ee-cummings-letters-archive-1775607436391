august: the hush after lightning

Date: 1945-08-15
Recipient: Akiko (unknown street, Tokyo)
Location: Honolulu, Territory of Hawaii
Year: 1945
Themes: surrender, compassion, distance, future

akiko,

today the harbor kept very still, as if a schoolchild were asked to read the last sentence aloud.
bells did not ring so much as lean their weight into the air.
people looked at one another the way tide looks at shore—known and strange.

i have not seen your face and yet i tuck rice and salt into the corners of this letter.
some things have burned that have no names and i will not name them here.
let us practice the difficult verb mend.

if you are sweeping, keep even the smallest dust: it could be a seed disguised as sorrow.
if you are hungry, take this line like bread and eat it slowly.

when the map remembers we are neighbors
perhaps we will teach our children how to fold paper into boats
and let them float toward each other without fear.

i bow from far away,
naomi

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
