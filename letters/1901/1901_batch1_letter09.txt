The lantern inside your name

Date: 1901-11-15
Recipient: Miss Mina Zhou
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1901
Themes: city, courtship, light, fog, cultural exchange

Mina,

Fog arrived this morning with the deliberation of a queen, and laid her gray fur upon our hills. Lanterns in the alley brightened their small hearts and continued, unafraid. I thought of your handwriting, which is itself a lantern: precise flame; generous light.

At supper I learned to lift chopsticks as one lifts the spine of a delicate book. The rice yielded with a small sigh and the tea asked nothing of me except attention. A merchant laughed and the whole street moved back so that his laughter might have a little more sky.

Do you remember the paper chrysanthemum you tucked behind my ear, then removed because it was too much? I have kept it between pages where I keep the first words of things. Some evenings it opens by itself and the room smells briefly of distant rain.

Cable cars ring and ring; bells like pockets of clear thinking. If you will meet me Saturday on the steps of the library, I will read you my newest paragraph, in which your name carries a lantern through a house that has been asleep for years. It finds a door. It does not knock. The door opens.

With careful regard and an unruly hope,
J.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
