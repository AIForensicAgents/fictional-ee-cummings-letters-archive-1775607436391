Montmartre Night (with a pocketful of sky)

Date: 1901-06-11
Recipient: Mlle. Lucille Carré
Location: Paris, Montmartre
Year: 1901
Themes: city nights, art, longing, music, color

Chère Lucille,

the hill wore its lanterns like freckles, and the wind, which has read every poem before we do, turned the leaves of the plane-trees with invisible fingers. At the Lapin Agile a fiddler braided thunder into silk; I kept your laughter in my pocket as if it were a bright coin (I have been poor of everything except that).

Do not be jealous of the canvas; it does not answer back. I set out my colors and the night kept offering more—ink for shadows, wine for miracles, a little soot for honesty. There was a girl selling violets who arranged them the way some people arrange sentences: daring the passerby to breathe too quickly. I bought none, as if refusing to be charmed were a kind of prayer; nevertheless their purple entered me and refused to leave.

You said once, do not paint the steps, paint the climbing. So I tried (forgive my audacity) to paint an absence: the space you leave as you turn the corner, the tremor left behind by a silk sleeve, the taste of a word that has not yet been spoken but is already true. It looked like a window at midnight in which someone is absolutely alone and completely saved.

Write soon. Your handwriting travels like a swan across the page, sure of its river. I am certain that distance is a staircase and not a wall; see how the stars keep stepping toward us, unashamed of falling.

avec foi (and a foolish bouquet of hope),

Anselme

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
