A garden made of listening

Date: 1901-03-21
Recipient: Madam L.
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1901
Themes: spring, listening, mentorship, desire, Paris

Madam,

Spring has rehearsed itself so carefully that today it opens without a single cough. The chestnuts along the boulevard spill a soft applause. I walked to your doorway and stood as if before a piano, not daring to strike myself into sound.

Your last note instructed me to listen between the blue places. I have been obedient. In the Luxembourg, a child let go a red kite; how it learned at once the language of roofs. A soldier leaned against a statue and studied the invisible. Between these two silences a sparrow translated everything.

I write to admit what a letter cannot carry: your voice has begun to live inside my pocket, where my watch ticks it gently into time. When evening performs its velvet experiment, I rehearse our conversation with the windows. They answer in glass.

You once said that every sentence must have a garden. I am planting commas and watering them with restraint. If they bloom by Sunday, will you walk with me beside the river and name the shades of water as if they were cousins?

Until then, I remain a chair waiting for the body it was born to hold.

Devotedly,
S.
