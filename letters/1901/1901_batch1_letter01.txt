A small hour opens its hands

Date: 1901-01-14
Recipient: Miss Aurelia Finch
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1901
Themes: winter, longing, city, distance, memory

Dear Miss Finch,

this morning the snow has the small bravery of lace and every step of Boston hushes like an apology. My window fogs with the soft grammar of breath; I write upon it with a foolish finger, your name becoming cloud becoming nothing.

Last night the Common was a theater of muffled hooves. Gaslight stitched pale music between trees. I walked perfectly alone except for the unruly crowd of my thoughts, which jostled and clattered like plates in a cupboard when the carriage passes too close.

If I had the courage of a match I would strike it in your letter and set these margins trembling. But I am a quieter flame (a pocket ember), traveling by post. Accept me if you can: a small hour opening its hands.

Do you remember the shell I sent in autumn? Press it to your ear and you will hear how winter learns to imitate the sea. Perhaps this is what we are: distances practicing each other.

Write soon, with ink that smells faintly of violets as yours does. Between your words and my hands a city kneels to tie its boots; then rises, brave again.

Yours faithfully, and more than faithfully,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
