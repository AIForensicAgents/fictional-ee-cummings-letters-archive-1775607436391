An autumn made of letters

Date: 1901-10-03
Recipient: Dr. Octave Mire
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1901
Themes: autumn, city, music, healing, observation

Doctor,

Autumn has loosened its gold into the gutters where the streetcars collect it and spend it uselessly. Brass sidles down the avenue in a sort of parade that has not yet decided its reason. A cornet argues sweetly with the afternoon and is correct.

You asked after my pulse. I placed two fingers upon it and found a river unwilling to keep still. Perhaps this is the influence of the levee, that polite monster leaning against our doors. I wake at night to the creak of its broad shoulder and imagine every house a ship, tied but dreaming.

In your surgery the shelves behaved like librarians. You listened with your head tilted as if the blood knew a secret and you had promised not to interrupt. The prescription you handed me read simply: write daily, and walk where the letters of the city are largest.

So I walk. The letter O is a full moon caught in a bottle of sarsaparilla. The letter S is a river-bend filling its pockets. The letter T is a telegraph pole practicing mercy. If I complete the alphabet by All Saints, will you permit me to declare myself improved?

Ever your apprentice in the anatomy of days,
P.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
