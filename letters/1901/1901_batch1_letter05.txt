When ink becomes sky

Date: 1901-05-17
Recipient: Señorita Isabela Mar
Location: Havana, Cuba
Year: 1901
Themes: desire, tropics, summer, light, flirtation

Isabela,

The afternoon is a hammock full of lemons. Streetcars ring their little bells as if teaching the air to curtsy. I write from a patio where the light combs my hair into honest disorder and the sea keeps taking the city gently by the wrist.

You laughed yesterday and the palms obliged by inventing new hands. That laughter was a blue umbrella under which I forgot my caution. A gentleman in white linen tipped his hat to your shadow; the shadow was unimpressed and kept walking.

Sometimes the fan in your hand becomes a small theology: open close open, as if the world were a creature that must be persuaded to breathe. Will you teach me the patient art of coolness? My blood insists upon the storm even when the sky is a silk glove.

If you answer, please press between your pages the scent of guava, the courage of your handkerchief, a grain of the pink salt from your lip. I will send you in return the north star folded twice, and a promise not to name what need not be named.

Con todo el cuidado del corazón,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
