Of masts and inward weather

Date: 1901-02-03
Recipient: Brother Elias
Location: New Bedford, Massachusetts
Year: 1901
Themes: sea, brotherhood, work, nostalgia, winter

Dear Elias,

The harbor wears February like a gray coat turned inside out. Tar and salt, rope and gull: such is the catechism of mornings here. I stand where the pier thinks slowly about leaving, and listen: the rigging prays in rattles and minor notes.

Men stride past with shoulders built of storms. They speak low; their vowels smell of lamp oil. I try to answer with a steadier jaw than my hands permit. Even so, the sea reaches in and rearranges the furniture of my chest.

Do you remember the toy boat we set in the washtub? It floated with a dignity too large for its bathtub world. Now I watch real hulls glance off moon-shivers and I confess: sometimes a toy truth is braver than an adult fact.

Mother writes that you are mending the fence. Mend a little sky for me also, if you would. I keep a pocketful of small suns (buttons, coins, a brass thimble) to pay whatever tide collects.

Send word when the crocuses blink awake. Until then I will learn the grammar of gulls and the punctuation of oars, and practice being a harbor for those who cannot moor.

Your brother in weather and other seas,
R.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
