Snowmelt Addressed to a Quiet Window

Date: 1901-02-24
Recipient: Elias Harrow
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1901
Themes: grief, early spring, youth, change, friendship

Dear Elias,

tonight the streetlamps tremble like pocket-sized moons and the drifts lose their hard grammar. the gutters learn a softer language, syllables of melt knocking in the dark. since the funeral bells unstitched January, I keep hearing the world relearn its breathing—slow, then sudden.

You asked whether courage has a neighborhood. perhaps it lives where the last snow finds the first mud: that shy border, ankle-deep and unpronounceable. I walked there today with the book you lent me asleep in my coat. each step said yes in a new accent.

I have done a foolish thing and left your glove on my desk, palm open as if it expected a bird. do not scold me; the room has been glad of its gentle company. between lectures I watched the window mist and cleared it with my sleeve to write your name (small as a seed) and then erased it, which felt like planting.

If we are young—which is to say, if we are not yet afraid to be unfinished—come tomorrow and we will drink coffee that tastes like bells and make plans as immodest as April. I am teaching my heart to tie its shoes; it trips, it rises, it laughs at the bruise.

With a clumsy affection that is learning to be otherwise,

R.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
