Snow teaches the moon to write

Date: 1901-12-29
Recipient: Myself (future)
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1901
Themes: reflection, winter, resolution, self, hope

Dear Future,

I write from the end of a year, which is not an end but a careful hinge. Snow rehearses the moon until both are fluent, and my window is an unblinking pupil. I promise to keep lessons from the smaller teachers: a match, a mitten, the steam that braids from a horse.

Do not forget the rooms where kindness happened for no reason. Do not forget the mornings that arrived on tiptoe, and the nights that fixed a loose star rather than complain. When you become busier, become softer also. There is no other arithmetic worth mastering.

Count the books not by their pages but by the amount of listening they accomplished. Count friends by the chairs they kept empty for you whenever the sudden guest called Loneliness ate too fast and needed a walk.

Take these vows: to speak after thinking and to think after loving. To keep an open umbrella under your ribs. To permit the small bird of astonishment to nest, even when rain rehearses war upon the sill.

Send back to me, if you can, a single sentence worth warming both our hands upon. I will be waiting where the kettle hums its work song and the streetlamp remembers our names.

Yours in advance,
The one who began.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
