Dust Psalm from the Veld

Date: 1901-03-17
Recipient: Clara Finch
Location: Pretoria District, Transvaal
Year: 1901
Themes: war, conscience, dust, longing, faith

Dearest Clara,

Today the horizon kept its distance like a shy animal, and the sun rehearsed the same speech until we believed it. I am learning the craft of quiet: how to bind a wound with my own shirt, how to listen for water with the ear at the soil, how to speak to a frightened horse until its breath agrees to return in sentences.

The men here are made of many countries and one weariness. We pretend at bravado around the cook-fire, but it is a poor actor and forgets its lines the moment the night advances. I cleaned the grit from a boy's eyelids and he thanked me with a nod so small it could have been a leaf. Later a shell gnawed the hill and left its intention half-finished; there are mercies even in incompetence.

Do not accuse me of despair. I carry your last ribbon in my pocket, and sometimes the wind makes a little music through its frayed edge as if our future were practicing. When I pray, it is seldom in words. I inventory what is whole: a canteen that does not leak, a fellow with a joke he cannot tell without smiling, a star that pushes through the smoke as if to set an example. I believe in these, and in your hands remembering mine.

If I return in time for the hawthorn to astonish the lane, I will plant my boots by the door and forget them there. You will teach me again the grammar of peacetime—how to hold a spoon, how to listen to rain without counting, how not to startle when a door becomes a bang. Until then write as often as you can. Your letters reach me with the dust still thinking; then they open the day like a book already loved.

I remain your Jonathan

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
