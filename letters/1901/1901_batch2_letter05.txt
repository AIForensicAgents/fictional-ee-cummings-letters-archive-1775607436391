North Atlantic Ledger of Small Braveries

Date: 1901-11-09
Recipient: Mr. Silas Pennington
Location: Aboard the S.S. Asterion, North Atlantic
Year: 1901
Themes: voyage, ambition, reckoning, weather, forgiveness

Sir,

At sea, the arithmetic becomes simpler: gray above, gray below, and between them a thin row of decisions. The ship is a clerk with a splendid pen, underlining the horizon until it remembers itself. I had meant to spend these days with your accounts—indeed I have numbered the bales and recounted the mint crates—but the wind insisted on its own columns: heave, settle, heave, settle, and in the margin a gull that has misread the season.

You asked in your last whether I judged the Liverpool venture sound. It is, provided we remember that profit dislikes applause. We must enter quietly and sit where the ledgers cannot blush; we must leave before the champagne learns our names. I am bringing you several signatures that will make December deliberate, and one refusal that will save us the embarrassment of success we cannot carry.

Forgive this manner. Something about the sea rearranges my sentences. Nightly the engine translates the heart into iron music and the deck answers with a prayer not taught in chapel. I walk until salt salts my patience and a single star volunteers to be sufficient. I think of the boy I was when I first tallied nails in your counting-house, so earnestly improving I could not hear the clock forgive me. I hope to arrive improved in the opposite direction.

If the weather holds we will see a pilot by Sunday. Then the harbor will rise out of its fog like a coin from a conjurer's ear, and men will cheer as if they had not been afraid. I will bring you figs if there are any, and a new steadiness at breakfast that costs nothing.

Respectfully (and with a small, sea-taught audacity),

Edwin Cole
