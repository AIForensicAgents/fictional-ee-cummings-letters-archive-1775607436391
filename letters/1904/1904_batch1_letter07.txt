Lantern Arithmetic

Date: October 1, 1904
Recipient: R.
Location: Tokyo, Japan
Year: 1904
Themes: distance, war-shadow, learning, intimacy

R.,

Paper doors, when they slide, make a sound like snow deciding. Lanterns add their numbers to the dark, and the sum is tender. I learn the shapes that brush makes when it bows, and the ink takes its time the way a thought should. The station boards utter new syllables I am growing ears for.

News arrives with the salt taken out, folded thin over oceans. We read between lines and also inside them. There are drums beyond the hills, but here a woman sells chrysanthemums and a child laughs so hard he loses a shoe. The world is two hands clapping in different rooms.

I say your name into tea steam, and it ribs the air. Letters are slow ships. I put this one into the current and give it three bows for luck.

Keep my place by the window if you can. I am learning patience by copying the moon as it learns to be less round.

Always,
M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
