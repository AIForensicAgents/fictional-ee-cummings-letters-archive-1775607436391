High Country Breathing

Date: August 16, 1904
Recipient: Agnes
Location: Leadville, Colorado
Year: 1904
Themes: longing, mountains, labor, solitude

Agnes,

The altitude rings like glass. When I exhale, the note hangs a moment, surprised to be born this high. Men descend into the mountain as if paying a debt we never counted. The hoist sighs, the ore answers, and my shirt is a book the dust insists on reading.

At night the sky leans so near I can see where the stars have been darned. I put your ribbon in my pocket, a private horizon. The saloon piano is brave and cracked; it knows one waltz and a handful of staggered hymns. On Sundays the preacher scolds the earth for being so generous with gold and so stingy with mercy.

Do not worry if I grow spare in my handwriting. The thin air pares everything to its question. Mine is the same as always: When may I come down the long slope of the world and find you at its green foot.

Your devoted,
Samuel

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
