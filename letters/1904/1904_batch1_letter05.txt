Apricot Dawn at the Atelier

Date: April 14, 1904
Recipient: Mother
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1904
Themes: art, homesickness, city, hope

Dear Mother,

Paris keeps its promises with bread. Each morning, a gold heat breaks open on the counter and says 'Be reasonable, eat me.' I take a crust to the river where the bridges are ribs and the water is a quiet lung. Painters lean over the quay as if the surface were a kindly mirror and they, at last, agreeable.

At the studio they have hung a scrap of sky on the wall and named it Discipline. We stand before it until our eyes make a grammar we never knew we knew. My palette is a small orchard of suns. The model today yawned once and it was the best lesson: everything alive unstitches for an instant and then remembers its buttons.

Do not be alarmed if my letters stray or idle. I am learning to be patient with beginnings. Even the light takes a moment to become itself between shutters. Tell Father the chimneys here puff like old philosophers and the streets are lettered with chestnut leaves.

I send my love, wrapped in the paper of a baguette to keep it warm.

Your son,
Julien
