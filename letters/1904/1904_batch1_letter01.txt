Snowlight and Ink

Date: January 7, 1904
Recipient: Ariadne P.
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1904
Themes: love, winter, longing, modernity

Dear Ariadne,

Snow has a way of taking the city's noise into its sleeve and softening it. Today the sleigh-bells stitched the street from corner to corner, a silver seam. I kept the lamp low, wrote your name until the paper breathed warm, and thought of the brave little pulse that lives at your wrist.

The typewriter stands like a small black piano. When I press its keys, alphabets leap like fish. Some swim toward you. Some turn back, shy of your light. Forgive me if I send too many. Words are bread when distance fasts us.

I walked to the Common and fed the winter birds. They wore the afternoon like a tiny coat. In the falling hush the world remembered how to listen. I remembered you.

If you were here, I'd warm your hands in mine and say nothing for a while. Our quiet would be a new instrument, perfectly tuned.

Yours, with faithful snow,
E.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
