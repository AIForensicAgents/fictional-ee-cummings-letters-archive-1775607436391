A Small Republic of Leaves

Date: November 3, 1904
Recipient: Self (in a mirror)
Location: Brooklyn, New York
Year: 1904
Themes: introspection, city, autumn, resolve

To the self in the glass at dusk,

The neighborhood has been elected to its annual office of leaves. They make law in whispers and pass, unanimously, a motion to fall. On the promenade a small dog outruns its name and returns blushing. The bridge strings a harp across the river and asks a fog to play it.

Today I bought an apple that tasted like a beginning and an end. I put it in the window. The sun leaned in and signed it.

Be kind to the lungs that carry this city up and down the stairs. Be patient with the stubborn door that is your heart; it swells in rainy weather and does not fit its frame. Hang a key on a nail and call it hope. When it rusts, call the rust experience and keep the key.

Tomorrow, sweep the stoop. Smile at the woman who folds daylight into her laundry. Practice being forgiven.

Yours, experimentally sincere,
J.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
