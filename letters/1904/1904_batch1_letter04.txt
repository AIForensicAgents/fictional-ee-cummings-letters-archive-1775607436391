Coal-smoke Envelope

Date: March 22, 1904
Recipient: Mr. Otis Crane, Station Master
Location: Evening train between Dayton and Cincinnati
Year: 1904
Themes: travel, time, mechanization, restlessness

Mr. Otis Crane,

From the evening train between Dayton and Cincinnati, I send you this moving letter, which will reach you only after it comes to a full and courteous stop. The cars speak in iron vowels. Coal smoke writes a soft argument on the dusk and then erases it with the next bend.

Your clocks keep stricter sermons than any deacon. The conductor swears by them. I try to, but my heart insists on a timetable of its own: delays for sunsets, unscheduled stops for a stranger's profile, an express through grief with no chance to disembark.

Tonight a boy counted telegraph poles like beads, and a woman hid a tear in her knitting. Someone somewhere pulled a whistle that startled the moon. I thought of your office, the tidy empire of inches and minutes; then of my own pockets, where lint and stubs of thought congregate like small conspirators.

Should this arrive late, forgive the letter its scenery.

Respectfully,
A traveler with soot on his collar

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
