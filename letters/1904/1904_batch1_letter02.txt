Sea-salted Ledger

Date: January 29, 1904
Recipient: Brother Emil
Location: Galveston, Texas
Year: 1904
Themes: family, recovery, sea, work

Dear Brother Emil,

We raise a house the way the sea raises a wave: board by board, wind by wind. The storm taught us to count nails like prayers. Now, four years on, the piers remember music instead of mourning. I smell tar, salt, coffee, iodine wind; I feel the hammer reckon with the stubborn heart of wood.

Your last note sat in my pocket through three sunsets. I read it by lantern, and the letters shone as if they had been rinsed in tide. Come if you can. There is a room facing east, a shy square of light that wakes earlier than we do. The pelicans are solemn as deacons; they pass with the day's first news folded in their beaks.

We are menders, you and I. Stitch the rope, patch the sail, set a table. Sometimes the water looks back and shows a face I almost know. It is the boy we carried away from ruin, still holding a toy boat without a mast and calling it masterpiece.

Write soon. The gulls practice your name.

Your brother,
Thomas

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
