fairgrounds of lightning

Date: 1904-07-03
Recipient: Clara Mendel
Location: St. Louis, Missouri
Year: 1904
Themes: modernity, wonder, friendship, technology, city nights, music, anticipation

dear clara,

tonight the fair unbuttoned the dark and wore a necklace of ten thousand little suns. they call it electricity; i call it a tame storm on holiday. we walked below palaces without kings, only engineers with ink-bright sleeves and eyes like new coins. my shoes learned a faster grammar, clicking yes yes yes upon wooden boards.

a man poured music out of a box (ragtime, it said on the poster) and my knees forgot sunday. i bought an orange that tasted like a telegram from summer. also there was a wheel that lifted me into a polite dizziness; the city crouched far below like a cat deciding whether to forgive us.

do you remember when we dreamed of a dress with a heartbeat? i found it here: silk flash, waist stubborn as a promise. i did not buy it. instead, i kept its idea the way a pocket keeps a bird's feather: small thunder you can carry.

somewhere a gentleman spoke to the air and the air answered with dots and dashes; he called it wireless, as if freedom could sit on a table and wear headphones. i wanted to write you at once, to invent a wire that curls only toward your hands. until i do, consider this letter a lantern i leave along the path between our houses.

wash your sorrows in lemonade and pin them out where the stars may bleach them. if you were here we would name each light and not run out of names before morning.

from a bench under domesticated constellations,

agnes
