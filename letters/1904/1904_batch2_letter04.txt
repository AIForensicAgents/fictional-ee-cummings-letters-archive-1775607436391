strings between dusk and father

Date: 1904-09-05
Recipient: Señor Ortega
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1904
Themes: reconciliation, immigration, art, identity, urban life, pride, family

padre,

the violin has been my stubborn prayerbook, and every evening i turn its thin pages until they bleed color. forgive the years i have mailed you only silence; stamps cost less than pride, but pride would not stand in line. i am learning to spend it.

tonight in a hall that smells of varnish and brave perfumes, i drew the bow and a small river remembered its source. i thought of your hands (daylight in calluses), how they taught bread to rise and doors to straighten. i was a crooked door, father. i am trying, with music, to swing true.

new york is a cupboard of bright noise. we eat hurry for breakfast and lick steam from our collars. sometimes spanish arrives on the street like a cousin from the harbor and i nearly answer. then the train insists on english and i become two useful halves that do not greet each other.

do not fear for me; even loneliness has a metronome here. i have students—thin boys, fierce girls—who believe a note can carry them across the river of their own rooms. i believe with them. perhaps belief is the boat we never owned.

if you can, write only this: hijo, i hear you. i will put that in my case beside rosin and a handkerchief, and when the night becomes a question i cannot translate, i will take it out, and the question will decide to be music.

con respeto y amor que tardó demasiado,

rafael
