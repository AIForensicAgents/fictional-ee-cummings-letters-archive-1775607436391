steam-heart at evening

Date: 1904-01-22
Recipient: Miss Eliza Hart
Location: North Station, Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1904
Themes: longing, winter, trains, distance, memory, urban, hope

dear eliza—

north station steams like a kettle pressed to the world's lip; i keep my ticket tucked where your wrist once dreamed against my sleeve. (can you hear the coal cough & the rails teaching iron to sing?) the evening unbuttons carefully, snow by snow, until the clocks remember how to be hearts.

today the conductor smiled the way a winter sun pretends it is june. i believed him for a breath. you would have laughed (small fireworks) and the laugh would have stayed to warm the glass.

i have written your name upon the window's fog & watched it melt into something like a road. if you were to follow, please wear the blue hat, the one that startles the drifts into oceans. if you cannot, send me simply the smell of oranges; i will build a city from it, street by bright street.

there are letters we never post, little rooms of thought where the furniture is music. i live in those rooms when the train sleeps. i live in the hollow between leaving & arrival; it is a house with a door that opens only in your direction.

until the timetable learns to speak your voice, be careful with the brittle dark. i am saving a seat made of tomorrow.

yours (with a pocketful of january),

m.
