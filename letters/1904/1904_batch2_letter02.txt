the lamp & the ocean's whisper

Date: 1904-04-10
Recipient: Samuel Pike
Location: Monhegan Island, Maine
Year: 1904
Themes: solitude, duty, sea, family, work, weather, endurance

dear sam,

the lens is a quiet moon i must keep from drowning. nights the fog steps ashore on gray feet and asks our house for bread. i give it kerosene and a wick; it eats, becomes light, and forgets its hunger. (would that i could teach my own shadows to be so useful.)

i walk the spiral, slow as a prayer. the brass complains, the sea agrees. gulls have written their names upon the wind; i cannot read them yet, but the book is good. last week a schooner coughed past the reef and tipped its hat of smoke. i saluted like a small republic.

mother’s thyme still grows in the crooked window box, pretending the salt is snow. when i crush a leaf between thumb & forefinger, there you are—kitchen-loud with laughter, boots like two faithful dogs by the stove. distance is an oar the tide borrows, brother; it returns wet with news i cannot translate.

the government has sent a new oil can, polished to a sermon. the flame loves it and behaves. in storm, the panes click their teeth and the tower becomes a bone the wind tries to swallow. i think of you then, your stubborn shoulders, the way you held a net full of February and called it supper.

should you come, bring that harmonica you abuse. we will teach the beacon a song that keeps ships honest and keeps us less alone. if you cannot, write soon—your hand is an anchor i trust more than charts.

your brother among the turning waters,

jonah

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
