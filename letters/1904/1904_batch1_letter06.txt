The Net of Hours

Date: July 9, 1904
Recipient: Captain Elias
Location: New Bedford, Massachusetts
Year: 1904
Themes: change, sea, memory, ageing

Captain Elias,

The yard smells of tar and something that should have been a voyage. Ropes tumble in their dreams, and the gulls rehearse a play with too much sky in it. The new boys talk of engines as if they are future thunder. I nod, and my hands unthinkingly shape a knot that has no ship to bless.

Do you remember the ice at the edge of the world, how it made a cathedral of our breath? Now there is a museum where a whale hangs like an ellipsis. People stand under it and whisper because that seems like the correct grammar. I tried to be a person among them. The glass was clear as prophecy; still I could not believe it held.

There is no sermon here, only a small rusting bell and a pocket full of tides. If I close my eyes, the deck lifts, and your orders clean the air. If I open them, a cart rattles past with groceries and the afternoon dozes against the wharf like an old dog.

If you are mended enough to walk, come. We will drink coffee boiled to courage and let the harbor tell us a few last, long sentences.

Your former first mate,
Silas

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
