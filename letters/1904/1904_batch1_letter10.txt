Green Ribbon for Pearl

Date: December 24, 1904
Recipient: Little niece, Pearl
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1904
Themes: family, tenderness, holiday, nature

Little Pearl,

Tonight the river wears a ribbon of moonlight so green it must be keeping a secret for spring. We will not tell it. The camellias at Mrs. Burke's gate have opened like careful fingertips. I carried an orange home and the whole street smelled like a ship bound for warm weather.

If you were here, I would tie your hair with the strip of satin you like and tuck a sprig of rosemary behind your ear to tell the year to be gentle. In your stocking I would place a spinning top painted with a patient blue, and a small book of birds who refuse to sing on command.

Your aunt says I write too many sentences that lean. Perhaps I do. But there are nights when words must be ladders. Climb these and kiss your sleeping mother for me. Kiss the cat as well, if she allows it.

From this porch I send you the bell of midnight and a handful of soft air.

Your uncle,
Owen

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
