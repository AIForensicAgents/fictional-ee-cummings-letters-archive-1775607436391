A Map of Electric Dusk

Date: May 1, 1904
Recipient: Miss L.
Location: St. Louis, Missouri
Year: 1904
Themes: wonder, technology, desire, city

Miss L.,

The Exposition threw open its bright ribs this morning, and I walked beneath them like a pilgrim under a mechanical sky. Electric bulbs freckled the white facades, tame stars learning their manners in daylight. By dusk they were fluent, and everyone was a little luminous.

I ate ice cream from a paper cup while a brass band unbuttoned the air. A dirigible shrugged past like a fat cloud that had learned a new trick. Children pointed, and so did I. The machinery halls breathed and sweated as if each engine had a soul it was determined to exhibit.

All that brightness makes a person honest. I saw my hunger for you in every polished surface. The lagoon held me by the lapel and said, Look. So I did. The world's corner lifted, and under it there you were, laughing at the seriousness of statues.

If you come, I will meet you at the Palace of Fine Arts at noon. I will be the one who is all eyes.

Faithfully,
H.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
