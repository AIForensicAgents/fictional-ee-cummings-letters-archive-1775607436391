to the train that carries you west

Date: 1929-05-12
Recipient: Eleanor
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1929
Themes: farewell, travel, longing, midwest, memory

eleanor—
the station peeled you away from me with a whistle i can still feel in my
teeth. there was sunlight trying its best on the steel, all that gleam like a promise
no one could read. your hand went first, then your shoulder, then your hat (small
sailing ship) until only the window kept you, and i kept the window.

someone shouted for newspapers; a boy sold time in thin folded slices. i bought
one and wrapped my heart in it the way fish are wrapped, to keep the smell of
absence from my coat.

i walked home beside a freight car i invented—in my head it was slow enough
for me to keep up, carrying freight i could pronounce: bread, oranges, a scrap of
moon for your hair. every block i wrote your name between the cracks and said
mind the gap to every stranger.

when you sleep somewhere past iowa, imagine my breath ironing the miles.
rail ties are beads; i will say them all with my mouth until distance itself begins
to exhale.

return to me with prairie in your pockets. i will pour it on the floor and call it a
map.

still on the platform,
max

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
