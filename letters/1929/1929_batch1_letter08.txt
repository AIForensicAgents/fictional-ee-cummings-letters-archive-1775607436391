on the anatomy of quiet in a library

Date: 1929-11-12
Recipient: Miss Hart, Librarian
Location: Boston Public Library, Massachusetts
Year: 1929
Themes: books, sanctuary, gratitude, city, study

miss hart—
i return your quiet with interest. it multiplied in my pockets all afternoon while
the reading room officiated at the marriage of dust and sun.

you have a way of shelving a book that feels like forgiveness. spines sigh into
place and the alphabet makes a low contented sound, a cat no one sees.

i came here wickless and left with a small flame folded in a page about whales.
thank you for trusting me with the atlas (heavy, yes, but not as heavy as my
untraveled feet). thank you for the vigilant fern that keeps watch by the chess
manuals. thank you for the little brass bell that declines to be rung unless time
is misbehaving.

the city outside rehearses its horns, but under your ceiling even my breathing
agrees to be polite. i promise to bring the books back more full than i found
them: with the whole of my listening crammed between their lines.

quietly yours,
henry

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
