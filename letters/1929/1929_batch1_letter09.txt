postcard that grew too long

Date: 1929-06-03
Recipient: Nadia
Location: Harlem, New York
Year: 1929
Themes: music, city, desire, nightlife, invitation

nadia—
this was supposed to be three words and a stamp. but lenox avenue kept
walking into my sentence. last night the trumpet turned the corner before the
man who owned it; the piano argued kindly with the floorboards until both of
them were wiser.

your laugh decided on a red dress and wouldn't be talked out of it. i kept
thinking: how many rooms can a single note unlock? there were people opening
doors in their chests and finding balconies, moonlight leaning over like a curious
neighbor.

i wanted to write: wish you were here. instead i write: if you were here the
lamps would sing a little truer and the waiter would bring another bowl just to
see your hands explain the heat to the spoon.

come north, if only to teach my feet a better courage.
from a table sticky with sweetness,
leo
