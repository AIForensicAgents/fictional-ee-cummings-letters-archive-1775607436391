five o'clock sugar in a southern kitchen

Date: 1929-04-18
Recipient: Aunt Lila
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1929
Themes: domestic, southern, nostalgia, food, women

dear aunt lila—
i am writing with my elbows floured. the dough rose like a small miracle and sat
back down as if embarrassed by its own ambition. jasmine pries open the window
and the street drifts in, all magnolia vowels and wheel squeaks.

mrs. jackson sang while she shelled peas; every green moon jumped into the
pan with a pleased sound. she says the weather has a secret it is not telling and i
believe her. we all hold something under the tongue: a grain of salt, a sugar of
sorrow, an unfinished name.

i washed the good teacups and thought of your hands, the patient geography of
bone and vein that taught me to measure by listening. put your ear to the bowl, you said, and you will hear when enough is enough.

i have put by joy in a jar, plainly labeled peaches. when you arrive we will open
it and let july climb out, syrup-ankled and laughing at its own sweetness.

keep your knees from the storm and your hat from the birds.
with a spoon and a promise,
clara
