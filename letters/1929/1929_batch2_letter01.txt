Valentine with Streetcar Bells

Date: 1929-02-14
Recipient: M.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1929
Themes: love, city life, longing, intimacy

Dear M,

This morning the streetcar rang itself awake (tin heart, brazen tongue) and everything in the window shook its light loose. I went out with my coat half-buttoned—sky like an overturned inkpot—thinking only: you. The baker flung warm breath into the street and the bread leaned together like people on a pier in wind, and still: you. I tried to buy roses but the bucket had already invented the sea and my pocket had invented austerity; so instead i borrowed a red pencil and wrote your name inside my glove where the hand gets its quiet from.

Do you remember Orchard Street, the button man who glued his laughter to every price tag, and we, solemn as astronomers, choosing constellations of small pearl circles? Your thumb was cold and marvelous. I escorted it in my palm (the city a blizzard of wheels, a telegram unfurling) until the sun’s last tram clanged away.

Legend says today is for declarations, but the only thing i can declare without ruining it is this: when i walk home, even the bricks tilt their little faces to listen, because my shoes are telling the sidewalk about you. Such excessive civility of air! Such scandalous patience of time! The mailbox kept its red mouth open longer than necessary, as if it expected my whole heart to fit—what a comedian.

I am practicing an alphabet that falls upward (a b c—you), stapling it to the flimsy curtain of hours. If there is any excellence in my foolishness, it is that i would rather be late to every appointment the rest of my life than early to a moment without you. Tonight the radiator stutters its metallic lullaby, and the moon (that coin a careless god left under the bed) keeps rolling toward your side of the river.

Come down Bleecker after the second lamppost, turn left where the cat with one ear keeps vigil, climb the stairs that argue in their old language; the door will be thinking of hinges and i will be thinking of you, which adds up to open.

yours (always in a hurry toward you),
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
