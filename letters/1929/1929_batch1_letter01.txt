umbrella-small, heart-wide

Date: 1929-03-07
Recipient: Marjorie
Location: New York City, New York
Year: 1929
Themes: rain, city, longing, tenderness, night

dear marjorie—
this rain writes its own alphabet on the street, all puddle commas and shining
parentheses around our hurried shoes. under the stubborn umbrella (blue, a little
brave against the sky) i discovered the quiet geography of your shoulder; a
country i will keep visiting whenever taxis hiss and the day forgets its manners.

i keep the typewriter awake past midnight, persuading its clacks to become
your name. sometimes the ribbon refuses (what machine can measure a blush?)
and so i blow on the paper like a lamp about to sleep, whispering: stay. stay.

i believe in small miracles: an elevator that arrives just as longing does; the
steam from a coffee rising into a soft astonishment; and you, who tilt the city
slightly so that my heart slides nearer.

should the weather change its mind, let us be stubborn rain together. walk home
anyway. i will learn each streetlight of your laughter and keep it, match after
match, until morning.

all my wet faith,
—a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
