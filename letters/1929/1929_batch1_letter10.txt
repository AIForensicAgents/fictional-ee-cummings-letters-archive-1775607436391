winter letter with matches

Date: 1929-12-24
Recipient: Mother
Location: Minneapolis, Minnesota
Year: 1929
Themes: winter, family, home, ritual, solitude

mother—
the river has become a long-held breath. i carry matches as if they were small
suns that forgot how to be enormous. the street makes a tidy crunch—someone
has taught the snow its manners.

i found a pine that smells of choir. brought it up the stairs like a polite burglar
and told it we would borrow december for one more night. hung a clock of
oranges in its branches; time tasted better at once.

i wish your hands were here to teach the air its old warmth. i have attempted
your recipe for comfort: milk, cinnamon, the soft insistence of patience. the cup is
not as brave without you, but it does its best.

if you hear a knocking it is only my heart practicing being a door. i will leave a
light at the window even though the window already knows the moon quite well.

grace to you, from the corner where my coat sleeps,
—sam

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
