after the bell (somewhere a bird)

Date: 1929-10-30
Recipient: Brother Daniel
Location: Lower Manhattan, New York
Year: 1929
Themes: uncertainty, resilience, city, work, hope

dan—
yesterday the air came apart like a book whose spine has given up. confetti,
only it wasn't a parade (unless panic is a parade). men stared at their hands as if
those were new inventions, faulty ones. the sidewalks turned into a strange kind
of ocean: everyone learning or forgetting how to swim.

i watched a hat roll away from its owner and imagined it still believing in
safety. the bell quit scolding and the street kept talking. under the talk i heard a
small thing (feathered? breath?) and decided it was a bird, choosing the fragile of
sky over the fragile of us.

for supper: a clean plate and two stubborn laughs, borrowed from ellie across
the hall—she banged on my door with a loaf like a prayer and said: eat. so i did.

we are not numbers on a chalkboard, brother. we are men who can boil water,
sing off-key, and mend buttons by the yellow vulgarity of a single lamp. i will be at
my desk tomorrow at nine. i will choose to believe in the ledger of ordinary
things: the ink, the coat on its peg, the door which (astonishingly) still opens.

write me about the river. tell me it is still moving in your town, and i will send you
back this bird i keep hearing, to build us both a small roof in our chests.

Yours,
abe

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
