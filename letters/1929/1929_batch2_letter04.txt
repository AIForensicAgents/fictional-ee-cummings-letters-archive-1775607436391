After the Falling Numbers

Date: 1929-10-30
Recipient: Sam
Location: Lower Manhattan, New York
Year: 1929
Themes: economic crisis, resilience, friendship, urban life

Sam,

The tickers have eaten their tape and are licking the floor. Yesterday the street sounded like a hundred collars being torn in the same second, and the sky did that pale trick of pretending to watch calmly while the windows discovered how to shout. I stood beneath the stone men with spears and the living men with hats and felt suddenly very unbuttoned by arithmetic.

I will not lie: i was afraid, that thick honest kind of fear that makes the hands try to invent pockets inside pockets. The telephone rang until it grew hoarse; numbers fell and kept falling like the kind of snow that refuses to be pretty. Men who always had the right size of smile walked without it, as if misplacing an organ. Someone kissed a folded telegram as though his lips could correct it.

But then—oh listen—then the small things took off their disguises. A woman I did not know steadied a stranger’s elbow; the bootblack shined without charging; the florist put water like forgiveness into the vases. I carried home a loaf with more crust than courage and, by accident, an extra inch of sky under my hat brim.

I have counted what we have: you, my ridiculous handwriting; the chair with its honorable wobble; the piano that always begins to doubt itself on the high notes; a window the river keeps writing itself upon. Also one bottle of red we were saving for a triumph. Let’s open it for surviving Wednesday. Let’s salute the scaffolding that believes in the building even while the wind is arguing otherwise.

Come by after seven. We will pretend the radiator is a hearth and the calendar a gentleman with manners, and if the future knocks with its brass knuckles we will teach it to knock softer. Bring your old hat; we will mend its brim with stories. I will make a soup that robs the icebox respectfully and returns everything warmer.

In a city busy forgetting, i remember you on purpose.

Yours,
E.
