telegram-length dream for my unborn poem

Date: 1929-01-02
Recipient: Myself
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1929
Themes: art, self-address, exile, creation, europe

dear you—
morning at the table with a coffee dark as any certainty; the window, politely
fogged, refuses to disclose the full sentence of the street. somewhere a violin
practices forgiveness. the waiter achieves an eloquence with his eyebrows i'm not
sure the academy would approve.

remember: what you love is a door slightly ajar. push it with your shoulder of
breath. let the nouns come in dragging water, let the verbs arrive barefoot and
muddy. you are not the mayor of these words; you are their astonished neighbor.

last night i walked along the river until bridges began agreeing with each other.
there were philosophers quarrelling with bread and a hat arguing successfully for
another night of weather. i put my hands in my pockets and found two small
fires. i wrote one on the napkin and kept the other for the way home.

stop waiting for perfect. send the wire. it will find you.

with a stamp and a coin left over,
yours truly

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
