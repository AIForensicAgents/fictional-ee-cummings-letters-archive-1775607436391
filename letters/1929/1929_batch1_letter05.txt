a lighthouse writes to a dancer

Date: 1929-08-16
Recipient: Agnes
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1929
Themes: coast, performance, light, devotion, nature

agnes—
what is a light but a stubborn sentence the sea must read again and again?
tonight the gulls stitched themselves to the wind and the boats answered with
little alphabets of rope. you rehearsed on the sand (bare feet, telegram toes), and
my beam kept forgetting the ocean.

you said hold me, i am learning to fall. the dunes obliged—with their enormous
softness they are the kindest audience. the moon, not to be outdone, pretended
indifference and spilled silver all over the rehearsal.

i have seen storms come to church here. the chapel shakes; the windows make a
music almost holy. remember last winter, when the surf tried to eat the town and
you danced at the end of the pier to teach the waves about grace? i carry that in
my pocket like flint.

i will go on turning (it's my job), but understand: whenever you lift your wrists,
something in me—tall, chalk-white, official—becomes a boy throwing
pebbles, hoping for a ring of water bright enough to be called a yes.

keep the sea in your ankles and the land in your lungs.
from the far end of the shore,
leon
