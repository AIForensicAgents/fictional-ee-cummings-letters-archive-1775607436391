New Year's practice: breathing

Date: 1934-12-31
Recipient: Myself (to be opened in spring)
Location: Baltimore, Maryland
Year: 1934
Themes: reflection, renewal, self compassion, winter

Dear Future Me,
there is a clean shirt folded at the foot of the bed and the radio is learning midnight in a crackling accent. outside, the streetlights are wearing their halos loosely. i have decided the only resolution worth an envelope is breathing. not the automatic kind, but the sort where the ribs make room for the person you are when nobody is catching you.

you will read this in April with shoes wet from a blown-open puddle and pockets full of small failures. keep them. plant them in the leftmost drawer. i've learned that some seeds prefer darkness first.

tonight i lit a match and the room applauded. i wrote three letters and kept none, ate an orange with both hands, let the peel become a little sun on the table. the clock tried to be important and succeeded briefly.

when the bells argue with the whistles and the whistles win (as they always do), inhale, then exhale as though teaching the century a polite bow. remember: kindness is not a luxury item. keep one good spoon for joy and one for sorrow and stir both into your tea.

faithfully and foolishly,
E.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
