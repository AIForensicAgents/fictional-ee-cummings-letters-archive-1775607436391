Dust-psalm for a brother

Date: 1934-05-03
Recipient: Elias
Location: Near Dodge City, Kansas
Year: 1934
Themes: dust bowl, family, perseverance, memory, land

Brother—

today the sky folded up its blue like a worn handkerchief and the wind came preaching its brown gospel. we nailed old calendars over the cracks and called it carpentry, but the dust knows every door by its first name. it sits with us at supper, uninvited and affectionate as a stray dog. mother keeps a wet cloth over the bread; the radio crackles a far voice promising rain in a week that has not yet been born.

i walked the field and could not find the field. the rows are sleeping under a single blanket that won't keep anyone warm. all my pockets are full of yesterday's soil; it waits politely for a seed as if patience were water. a tumbleweed chased me (or i chased it) and we laughed, two fools with no map but motion.

do you remember last june when we went out before dawn, the wheat humming with green music? your hands learned harvest the way a sparrow learns flight: by forgetting earth. i keep that morning folded in my coat like a letter addressed to better weather. sometimes i unfold it under the barn's sore roof, and the light, dusty with its own sorrow, reads over my shoulder.

the schoolteacher says this is a year that writes itself with a blunt pencil. still, i bought a small tin packet of hope (they call it seed) and pushed each into the ground as if tucking a child to sleep. between my fingers were the afterthoughts of rain. i told them your name because names are warm.

if you find work out east, send a postcard that smells of river. i will hold it to my face until even our scarecrow smiles. and when the wind goes hoarse and the sky remembers its handkerchief was meant to wave, come back. we will stand in the doorway like two commas waiting for the sentence to begin again.

yours (stubborn as fence wire),

A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
