Letters invented of salt and sea

Date: 1934-07-18
Recipient: Mother
Location: Aboard S.S. Europa, North Atlantic
Year: 1934
Themes: voyage, mother child, courage, horizon

Mother,
night spreads its careful ink and the ship writes a sentence through it, a long white clause without a period. i have learned to sleep with the engines' big heart under mine; its patient thudding edits my fear into smaller versions.

on deck today a woman let her scarf go—sea-green surrender unrolling toward a country we could not see. a steward laughed, a child cried, and the scarf decided, as all good stories do, to become an ending. gulls practiced their commas.

i stood at the rail until the salt drew its map on my lips. the horizon behaves like a promise: always ahead and never wrong. i am bringing you postcards of clouds and a handful of foreign words that will feel good in the mouth, like clean apples.

if you could see the way dark comes here—no corner to tuck into, no lamppost gentling it—you would understand why i have made friends with a star the size of a pin and an old woman whose knitting is a net that will catch next Tuesday. please water my spider plant and forgive the envelope i will surely send you, blotched with Atlantic and the soft oil of my unpracticed courage.

your wandering child (who keeps both feet and all hope),
Ruth

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
