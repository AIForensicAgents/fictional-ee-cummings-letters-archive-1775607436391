A small cathedral of autumn

Date: 1934-11-02
Recipient: Anna
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico
Year: 1934
Themes: autumn, art, place, invitation

Anna,
piñon smoke threads the morning and the adobe walls keep their ancient laughter under whitewash. i went to the market early and bought a blue bowl that seemed to have swallowed sky and decided to keep it. the woman who sold it to me said, you must give it something round to live beside—so i carried home apples, which thumped like soft bells in the paper bag.

yesterday i hung my watercolors at the co-op: eleven small windows where desert lay down with cloud and pretended to be ocean. a man stood considering a field of chamisa for such a long time that i began to think he was the painting's relative.

the afternoons here are long pews of light. i sit in them. sometimes a lizard rehearses vanishing across my doorstep, which is to say: i practice paying attention.

if you would come, we could open that blue bowl between us and eat the last sweet figs of the season, naming each one for a mistake we no longer need. i could show you the hill where sunset kneels. bring your wool coat; it is a new keen cold, and at night the moon carves our breath into small white sculptures.

with the slowness this place teaches,
Miriam
