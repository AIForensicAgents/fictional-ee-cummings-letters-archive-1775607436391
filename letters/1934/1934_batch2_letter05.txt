Night letter (sung to the river)

Date: 1934-12-29
Recipient: Hector
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1934
Themes: river, printing, music, friendship, night

Hector—

the river is a long black trumpet tonight and every eddy keeps time with a secret wrist. down by the levee, a man in a white hat sells boiled peanuts from a kettle that remembers every hunger since it was forged. winter here is a damp pocket; you reach in and come up with a coin of warm fog.

i set type until my thumbs wore their own quiet alphabets. the press breathes like an animal that has learned patience; every sheet comes away blushing with ink, a shy miracle. i am printing a small book of brief astonishments (yours is the first name on the dedicational wind). soon we will haunt back rooms with our paper ghosts—pages that tremble into persons when held.

jazz escapes through shutters as easily as laughter; prohibition took its exit bow last year and still the glasses sparkle like redeemed sinners. a cornet reached me across the corner and untied a knot in my chest i didn't know i wore. the tune leaned its elbow on the night and told a joke i will be laughing at in january.

your last letter said the city you inhabit is learning to be careful. promise me you won't. promise me your shoes will continue to argue with cobblestones, that your hat will never fit perfectly. send me one wrong-sized glove so i may wear it when i set the title: The Ordinary Becomes Brave At Dusk.

i will mail you the first copy, still warm, and we will both pretend the distance is only a slow river anyone can swim by thinking of the right face. at twelve, the cathedral is a watch that forgets to tick; at twelve-oh-one, a streetcar remembers its bell and we all come back to earth. meet me, then, in the bell between bells.

until your footsteps translate my doorway into meaning,

R.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
