Train-window valentine

Date: 1934-02-14
Recipient: The woman in the green hat
Location: Pennsylvania Railroad, between Newark and Philadelphia
Year: 1934
Themes: chance encounter, travel, observation, ephemera

Madam with the green hat,
i will not hand this to you, because the car rattles and bravery is not yet my language. but i am writing upon the inside of the window and the fog of my own breath, which appears and disappears exactly like a life.

your hat is a small brave meadow. your book has a red cover and the spine is tired the way good things are. we passed a frozen pond with two black skaters (or crows) above it and you looked up at precisely the moment the sun did not.

if i could speak across this corridor of strangers and their newspapers, i'd say: the telegraph poles are practicing their straightness for you. the conductor’s punch is a constellation spelling your name in a language of holes. i would say: when you turned the page, the page forgave the world for weather.

we are slowing. the station is a mouthful of iron and promises. should you step onto the platform, take with you this little invisible bouquet: two parentheses around a breath, and the thought of someone who noticed, simply, how beautifully you are being you.

from a fellow traveler whose stop is always admitting it is temporary,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
