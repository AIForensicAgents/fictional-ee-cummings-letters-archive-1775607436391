Snow-letter with a quiet heart

Date: 1934-01-05
Recipient: Eleanor
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1934
Themes: winter, longing, memory, intimacy

Eleanor,
this morning the river has forgotten how to speak and carries its hush beneath crusted glass. i walked along the trembling edge of it, where the reeds make their delicate handwriting against the wind, thinking of your small mismatched teacups and the way you tilt your head when listening to the radio's thin news.

i am learning the soft grammar of winter: footfalls as commas, breath as a hesitant colon, the pause before your name the longest sentence. the lamp on my desk blooms a yellow moon which keeps me faithful to our unruly certainties—ink on my fingers, your shawl around my shoulders though you are three streets and a bridge away.

do you remember the chocolate we hid in the blue sugar tin last spring? i found one square yesterday, sipping at it like an argument i didn't want to win. the taste of you in that small sweetness (and then the laughter, that brave umbrella).

if it snows harder tonight i will let the windows frost into lace, and i will not scrape them clear. the world may keep its insistence on seeing. i would rather feel you—thin as a match and bright as a match—struck in the tender dark of my chest.

with both my hands, and the clumsy blessing of January,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
