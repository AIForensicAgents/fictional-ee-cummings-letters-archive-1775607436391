Dust to apricots

Date: 1934-04-22
Recipient: Brother Joel
Location: Boise City, Oklahoma
Year: 1934
Themes: dust bowl, family, resilience, hope

Joel,
i keep finding a day's worth of sky inside the coffee tin, which is to say: dust. it rinses our throats with its brown patience, drifts into the Bible, writes its own scripture over every margin. the porch is a ship whose sea has climbed aboard. Mother shakes the rugs like flags of surrender and the wind salutes them with more of itself.

we planted apricot pits last year beside the leaning pump. foolish, you said, and then smiled because hope is sometimes foolish but still green in the mouth. i press two fingers into the yard and come up wearing a continent. if a tree comes of it, i will name its first fruit after your laugh.

at dusk, the well creaks up its little offering. we take turns with the tin cup, saints of a very local thirst. Henry’s boy runs the lane with a kite that looks exactly like a rib. it flies. yes it does. against the unkind sky it makes its yes.

come if the road allows you. bring your harmonica, your good jokes, and any seed that remembers how to remember rain.

sisterly and stubborn,
Mae
