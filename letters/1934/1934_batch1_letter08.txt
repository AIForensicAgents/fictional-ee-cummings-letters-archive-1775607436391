Paris, without the Eiffel

Date: 1934-09-01
Recipient: R.
Location: Montparnasse, Paris
Year: 1934
Themes: art, exile, thrift, observation

R.,
i have discovered the most important museum and it is not a building: it is the light between 4 and 5 when the fruit stalls drowse and every pear rehearses being painted. i cannot afford the official galleries today, but the river keeps its green admission fee free, and yesterday an old man fixed my sleeve with a needle that remembered wars i do not.

i am learning to order coffee in a way that makes the waiter forgive my accent. the word for water here feels like glass in the mouth. i stretch francs the way artists stretch canvas, and sometimes both tear a little. it is all right. air heals.

i set up my easel on a corner where a violin argued with a bicycle bell. people looked, or didn't. a child stood very near and breathed as if he were practicing being a wind. i painted the bakery door not because it is a door but because it is an opening.

no tower today, no postcard proof. just crumbs in my pocket and a streak of blue on my wrist where the sky escaped custody. if you were here i would pretend to be brave and you would pretend to believe me. until then, i will mail you these small translations of a city that keeps remaking my eyes.

tenderly,
Adele
