Harlem nocturne for daylight

Date: 1934-06-09
Recipient: Clara
Location: Harlem, New York
Year: 1934
Themes: music, city life, joy, invitation

Clara,
last night a trumpet unbuttoned the street and every syllable of June climbed out wearing sequins. upstairs, the baby's sleep was a small canoe that kept its river, even when the bass walked heavy down Lenox. Mr. Jenkins waved his newspaper as if swatting the century into good behavior. it did not behave, but it danced.

we found ourselves in a room the size of an argument, ten people and a piano that knew all our names. i wrote you between notes on a scrap of brown paper bag, then the drummer borrowed it to light a cigarette and for a minute you were smoke and rhythm but you did not disappear.

morning now, the kind with laundry singing from every window. i am mending socks with blue thread because what is the point of matching, when the world mismatches itself so gorgeously? three girls practiced steps on the stoop until the sun learned them. i clapped, i spilled coffee, i was forgiven.

come if you can. the corner grocer has peaches that bruise just from loving them. i will buy two, carry them home like holy instruments, and we will eat them over the sink like people entirely alive.

always,
M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
