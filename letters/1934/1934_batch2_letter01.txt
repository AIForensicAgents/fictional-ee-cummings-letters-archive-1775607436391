letter with a pocket of skylight

Date: 1934-02-11
Recipient: M.
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1934
Themes: urban longing, intimacy, modernity, music, anticipation

dear M,

i walked up sixth avenue with my coat refusing to button (the wind a mischievous tailor) and every window of the city tried on a different piece of afternoon. a boy sold violets in a tin cup that remembered being soup; an old woman fed pigeons and they opened like small gray umbrellas against the drizzle. all the telephones in new york must have been breathing at once because my heartbeat learned their rhythm, that click and hush and sudden bright voice: hello?

last night the piano in that basement café (the one with mirrors like patient lakes) invented a staircase out of notes and i climbed it with my eyes closed. i thought of your storm-blue coat, how its hem is a secret map of places we will be brave in. the city has put away its speakeasy mask and smiles with legal teeth; the bartender cops no longer hover in every glass, and even gin seems to have washed its hands. somehow this makes me lonelier (or truer): we never needed contraband to be contraband together.

i bought a roll of cheap paper and wrote your name until the pencil sighed. the e in your name went quietly to sleep but the m danced. maybe letters are just those small rooms where breath becomes furniture. i keep rearranging the chairs until our tomorrow fits.

outside, the el chattered past like a metallic sparrow. one seat was empty (i put your shadow there). it looked out over rooftops that yawned open, brick mouths speaking a grammar of chimneys. i wanted to slip a verb between every lamppost so that walking would finally say what walking means when it is walking toward you.

come down soon; or if not soon, come crookedly, dangerously, with rain in your hair and the kind of smile that unties shoes. bring nothing but your ear so the city can whisper to it, and that postcard of the sea we promised to believe in when neither of us had seen an ocean. i will keep a cup warm on the stove the way a lighthouse keeps a window warm in the storm.

until then, i am the street after your footsteps have visited: shining for no one's reason but yours.

(yes) always,

i
