a quiet rebellion with teacups

Date: 1934-11-07
Recipient: Mother
Location: Cambridge, Massachusetts
Year: 1934
Themes: art, independence, family, domesticity, courage

Mother,

the elms along Brattle have decided to be cathedral arches again, even after a summer that forgot to finish its sentences. the yard is a lecture in russet; leaves recite without a professor. i am writing at a kitchen table that wobbles like a conscience, and the kettle, grateful for the smallest flame, speaks in the voice of a timid choir.

i did a reckless thing that is also the only faithful thing i know: i told the department i would not continue. Latin packed its declensions into a suitcase and left without slamming the door. the chairman's spectacles frowned independently of him; he called it impractical (as if breathing were a career). i nodded my best obedient nod, then came home and painted until the room remembered it had walls.

you raised me to iron handkerchiefs and listen to the weather, to always leave a saucer under the cup for the tremble. today i took the saucer away. the tea did not spill; it stayed where i told it: here, heart. i thought of your wrists—strong ribbons—and wished i could hang my decision from them the way laundry trusts a line.

news travels by radio faster than sorrow. europe hums with a bad dream no one will wake from yet; the announcer's voice has a shine i do not trust. perhaps that is why i want to make small true colors and nail them to canvases until they stop trying to run. perhaps it is only because the light at four o'clock lays its hand on the table just so and refuses to move, and i must learn its name or be lonely forever.

forgive me the unladylike courage; teach me instead the proper way to keep a promise to myself. send the blue scarf i left behind and, if you have it, that small porcelain bird grandmother kept on the mantle. i will put it on the windowsill to practice the art of still flight.

i will visit at Christmas if i can, and if i cannot, i will write letters fat with afternoons so you may drink them from a cup and not burn your tongue.

with a daughter's shameless love,

E.
