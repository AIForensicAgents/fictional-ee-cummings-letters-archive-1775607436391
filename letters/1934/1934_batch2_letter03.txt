Date: 1934-08-19
Recipient: Miss Oona Calvert
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1934
Themes: performance, romance, city, optimism, fair

Oona,

if this were a true telegram i would swallow adjectives and spit only bones STOP but you know me (unthrifty with wonder) and the day refuses to be rationed STOP. lake michigan is a polite mirror with a scandalous heart; even the gulls are rehearsing.

the fair keeps blinking its carnival eyelashes—century of progress, they call it, as if time were a circus trick that finally learned to land on one foot. we rode the Sky Ride and were taller than money. in the aerial silence i could hear your ankle speak fluent applause; i could hear the city hold its breath between steel syllables.

i found a rehearsal room with a window too honest to lie about light. the pianist has ten fingers and one secret. he played a fox-trot that bent into prayer and i thought (ridiculously, gloriously) of the way your shoulder invents air. audition monday. my shoes are practicing without me; the floorboards have memorized your name and won't stop saying it whenever anyone waltzes by.

they sell lemonade without permission now; even the policemen are learning to smile with their hats off. nights, we cut our laughter into thin slices and feed it to the dark until the dark is full of us. yesterday i bought a carnation and taught it your step; it bobbed like a small polite moon pinned to my coat, entirely convinced by rhythm.

come see me fall upward, and catch me by not catching me. bring that blue ribbon you pretend not to be proud of, and the coin you toss before every entrance. i'll bring the world a chair so it can sit down and watch you become the stage's favorite rumor.
