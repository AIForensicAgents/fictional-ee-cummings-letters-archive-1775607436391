Dust and Violin

Date: 1926-05-03
Recipient: My Sister Ruth
Location: Santa Fe, NM
Year: 1926
Themes: family, music, desert, memory, resilience

Dear Ruth,

The wind here is a broom with ideas. It sweeps the canyon, then stands me up and shakes the sand from my pockets as if to see what I have been hoarding. These days it finds only rosin dust and a folded measure of Schubert.

I tune in the courtyard at dusk, string against string until the air brightens at the touch. The adobe keeps its cool like a kept secret; the sun steps back, embarrassed by all it has said.

Mother's shawl still smells faintly of cedar. When I pull it around my shoulders the moths in my chest quiet, and Father—do you hear it too?—goes on hammering soft nails in the next room of memory. We were children once, with a map of the sky cut from newspaper, and look at us now: you with your fast city patience, me with a violin that is not sorry for singing even when no one is listening.

Last night coyotes wrote their jagged alphabet along the ridge. I answered with scales that wandered like a horse off its tether. In the pauses, crickets stitched the torn places.

Send word of your window, what flower the neighbor grows, whether the sidewalk remembers your shoes. I will send back a handful of saffron dust, and a note that says: the desert is not empty. It is a cup, and sound is the water I pour into it.

Your sister,
Miriam

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
