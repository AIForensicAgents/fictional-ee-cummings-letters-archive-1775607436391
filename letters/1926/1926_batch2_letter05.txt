Timetable in the Snow

Date: 1926-12-21
Recipient: My Future Self
Location: On the Union Pacific, Nebraska
Year: 1926
Themes: time, travel, self-address, winter, hope

Dear Future Me,

The car rocks like a cradle promised a moon it cannot keep. Outside, snow decides itself into fields and then undoes the decision with a shrug of wind. Telegraph poles beat out their sure little fences of music; I hear them through the window glass, those square notes marching to Omaha.

The porter polished the aisle until my reflection learned to walk. I have set the typewriter across my knees and it hums like a patient insect. Words arrive with their hats on crooked, nod, and sit.

It is nearly the year's last turn. What have we carried that was too heavy for its own good? What did we drop that became a flower when we were not looking? I think of the rooms we have borrowed from cities, the names written in chalk on our palms, the way a kiss can rearrange a timetable.

When you open this—later, in a room that smells of soap and rain—remember that once I watched a farmhouse move through the dark like a ship with one lantern and believed I was blessed by passing it. Remember that even now a stranger is sleeping with his hat on his chest and looks forgiven.

I would like you to be kind to our knees and extravagant with sleep. I would like you to allow a foolishness daily, like drinking the dawn before it is quite done.

If you have forgotten how the rails sew distance into a coat that fits, press the paper to your ear. Hear it? That is the world clicking its bright needles, mending what it can.

Yours in transit,
E.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
