snow learns my name (or tries)

Date: 1926-12-23
Recipient: Ivy
Location: Brooklyn, NY
Year: 1926
Themes: winter, love, city, hope, solitude

ivy,

the borough puts on its white gloves and suddenly even the alleys remember their manners. i walked home with snow practicing my name in small careful letters on my coat. the elevated rattled like a pocket of tin soldiers on leave.

i have bought oranges and stamps and a string of unsentences for your tree. if you were here we would peel the oranges with exaggerated patience and make halos of their spirals and hang them in the window to teach the dawn about color. the kettle approves this plan.

the typewriter and i are at peace tonight; it forgives my commas and i forgive its thunder. sometimes i wonder if i am merely a secretary taking dictation from that persistent clerk named hope. he speaks in brief memos: eat, write, forgive, walk, call her.

there are carolers below who know only half of every song, which seems exactly right. i like a holiday with ragged edges; it catches the light better. if loneliness knocks, i will ask it to remove its boots and we will share a blanket politely until your letter arrives to dismiss it from duty.

should the storm delay you, send a one-word telegram: breathe. i will.

my heart (a small stove) is lit for you.

always,
—
