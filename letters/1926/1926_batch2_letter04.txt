Window with Brass Moon

Date: 1926-09-30
Recipient: Miss Lenore Blake
Location: Harlem, NY
Year: 1926
Themes: jazz, desire, nightlife, art, freedom

Dear Miss Lenore Blake,

I found the moon beaten thin into a brass button tonight and fastened it to the sky over Lenox. It held. Perhaps you felt the streetlight tug at your sleeve.

Inside the club the trumpet burned its syllables to ash and we breathed them gladly. Your laugh arrived before you did, a bright spill, and then you—satin and thunder—stepped through the smoke like a story that refuses to behave. I tell you this as if I were bold; in truth I have been practicing courage in a teacup, stirring until the spoon stands on its own.

Between numbers you leaned out the window for a sip of October and the city obliged with its peppery night. Down on the corner, a boy sold papers that crackled like kindling. Someone called for the blues and they arrived in a dress with torn hem, unashamed.

I have carried you home in my pocket, a folded map that keeps unfolding. If I open it here at the table, the streets climb out and do their dance—your shoulders keep time, your jaw makes its quiet beautiful argument. If I close it, the room dims as if a theater curtain had behaved badly.

If you find this too forward, call it only a postcard from the bright side of midnight. If you do not, meet me Sunday where Adam Clayton meets 135th, and we will walk until our feet forget their names. Bring your gloves. I will bring a thermos of city sky.

Respectfully loud with admiration,
M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
