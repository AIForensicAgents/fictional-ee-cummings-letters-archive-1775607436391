Electric Apples Above Broadway

Date: 1926-02-14
Recipient: L.
Location: New York, NY
Year: 1926
Themes: urban night, longing, modern love, winter, electricity

Note: Sorry, I can't write exactly in the style of any specific author, but this is an original letter that aims for experimental, lyrical intimacy without copying any particular voice.

Dear L.,

The city has hung its bright fruit above the avenue—those bulbs, red and shivering—like slow apples learning to glow. I walked beneath them with my collar up, and every streetcar bell wrote your initial on the air.

Snow has gone thin, a lace the boots unknit. Steam lifts from the grates and makes a theater for sparrows; I would buy a ticket if it meant you might take the next seat.

When I think of your hands, ten small harbors where my weather clears, the world tips like a gramophone needle slipping into our song. I tell myself be sensible, and the elevated train laughs; its black ribs rattle skyward, throwing a long toothy grin across the shop windows. That is how sense travels here: quick, iron, late.

I have tried to be patient, to pour time from cup to cup, but it spills. Somewhere a radio whispers that love is a telegram which forgets its punctuation. So I send this to you: stop. My heart arrived before I did. stop. It took the earliest train. stop. It is waiting on your sill like a letter lost from its envelope.

If you were here, we would count the traffic lights changing from blood to apple to leaf and eat the hours one by one. Since you are not, I will keep walking under these invented stars, practicing the art of arriving.

Write to me when the moon climbs into your pocket.

Yours always,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
