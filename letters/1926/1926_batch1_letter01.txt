night folds like a paper swan

Date: 1926-01-12
Recipient: Lydia
Location: New York, NY
Year: 1926
Themes: love, winter, city, longing, jazz

dear lydia

midnights lean their elbows on the rooftops and listen to the el hum a tired lullaby. i imagined your mouth learning my name sideways, as the snow tried to keep the streetlamps warm, and a blue note escaped the sax from the corner cellar where boys in thin ties teach morning how to be late.

i have taken to writing with the window open (cold breath gentling the page), because the world behaves better when it is allowed to enter. tonight a cab coughed, a girl laughed her pearls loose, and some brave shoe tapped the sidewalk into a small, obedient planet.

do you know how i keep you, sometimes? between parentheses of glove and pocket, (small bright secret), or folded into the ticket-stub kingdom of my coat. you are the straightest line in a city of corners; your handwriting loosens the knot behind my ribs.

there are stars that prefer brick to sky. they fall and stay—tiny stubborns in puddles and windows, all whispering lydia. and if my heart is a shy elevator, forgive it for stalling between floors when you say hello. the operator has wandered off to practice being bold.

i send this letter in my thinnest handwriting, so the envelope travels faster. meet me where houston forgets its consonants and the snow pretends to be quiet. i will bring two coffees, the cheap kind that tastes like courage.

yours (until the morning teaches me manners),
me
