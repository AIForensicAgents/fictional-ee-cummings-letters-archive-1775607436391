mechanical sunrise for a human heart

Date: 1926-04-05
Recipient: Mr. Kline, Clockmaker
Location: Philadelphia, PA
Year: 1926
Themes: time, technology, hope, modernity

dear mr. kline,

i bring you a foolish machine. it is small as a pocket and large as a room without a door. when it keeps time the minute-hand bites gently at my ribs; when it stops, birds exit the window of my nerves in a disagreeable hurry.

i have been thinking: perhaps the hour is not a circle but a ladder some invisible boy keeps moving while we climb. perhaps the spring inside your clocks is a secret alphabet, and we are all stuttering toward noon. if you open the case and find a leaf instead of a gear, do not be alarmed; i have been letting trees into my chest for air.

outside, market street shakes its coins and argues with the trolley. inside your shop the ticks behave like polite guests. teach my little machine to forgive my running, my sudden standing still, the way i measure love with a carpenter s tape that keeps snapping back.

i have soldered a wish to the escapement, loosely, so it can swing and not break. repair as necessary. my payment is this: when you set the hands, set them to a sunrise nobody else is using. i would like a private dawn to wear under my coat.

with thanks for steadying the skittish second,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
