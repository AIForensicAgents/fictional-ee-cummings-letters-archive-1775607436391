letter carried by a migrating moon

Date: 1926-11-14
Recipient: Mother
Location: Cambridge, MA
Year: 1926
Themes: family, seasons, aging, gratitude

mother,

autumn sweeps the yard with a broom that sounds like a quiet choir. the maples have unbuttoned; the walk to the post is a confetti of patience. i have been keeping good company with the library, which smells like elder light and wool. there is a chair by the window that forgives my weight and remembers my father s cough.

your pie tin arrived packed in newspaper and love, both dented but faithful. it sits on the shelf like a silver moon retired from night duty. i am eating more carefully, sleeping more deeply, and worrying with the steady thrift of a seamstress: enough to make the stitches hold, not enough to ruin the dress.

on sundays i attend the small church where the organist is also the janitor and the janitor is also a saint. the sermon is ordinary, which is what i require; holiness has always lived, to my mind, in boiled potatoes and unfrayed dishcloths. i light a candle for your knees.

i am not lonely exactly; i am accompanied by my future, which is a shy animal. when it sits closer i do not move, and sometimes it purrs.

i send this under the wide wing of the year, almost flown. kiss the kitchen clock for me and tell it i am trying to be on time.

your child,
—
