fourteen windows, one river

Date: 1926-05-30
Recipient: Aunt Ruth
Location: Cincinnati, OH
Year: 1926
Themes: family, home, river, identity

dear aunt ruth,

from my boarding room there are fourteen obedient squares of glass and beyond them the river busy with remembering. a steamboat unbuttons the afternoon, slow and lordly, while on shore boys trade marbles with the grave intensity of senators.

you asked if i am well. yes, with qualifications: i eat eggs that make the pan sing, i carry nickels like lucky planets, and i sleep in sentences. the landlady talks to her ferns in a german that feels like velvet. the stairwell smells like someone s old violin.

i know the family would prefer a practical vocation (accounting, sermons, barrels, the respectable arrangements), and i am trying to say i am practicing a kind of account, aunt—i count the way light moves on water and i give numbers to my silences so they add up to a voice. if this letter is a boat, it leaks; but look how bravely it floats.

tonight the cicadas will drill their patient gold into the trees and i will write beside the window where the city kneels to drink. your recipe for lemon cake survived three apartments and my clumsy pockets; i made it and the whole hallway smelled like a Sunday with its tie loosened.

please keep me in the place behind your kettle, where prayers hang to dry.

your foolish, grateful niece,
—
