after the dance, the little mercy

Date: 1926-10-02
Recipient: Rafael
Location: New Orleans, LA
Year: 1926
Themes: music, desire, regret, nightlife

rafael,

i am writing from a Sunday that overslept. last night the horn player convinced midnight to take off its coat and stay awhile; even the floorboards learned to speak in hips. you said my name like a river forgetting it was once rain.

the city was perfumed with hot sugar and oil, and every corner promised a reckless education. in the hallway a girl pinned a gardenia to a rumor and danced it into truth. i lay my cheek against your shoulder and heard a kindness i had not earned.

forgive the way i left—like a coin falling out of a pocket that did not notice until the street had already spent it. shame walks fast. but there is a small mercy this morning: the café cup warming my hands understands everything and demands nothing. the waiter trimmed the world to a clean plate and said cher like a blessing.

if desire is a trumpet then let regret be a soft drum behind it, keeping time, reminding the music where it learned to listen. i will come back with the better part of my honesty and we will sit on a stoop where the air conducts us and the leaves applaud.

until then i keep you inside my left sleeve, nearest the pulse that argues for joy.

—
