desert grammar with thunder

Date: 1926-08-21
Recipient: Nell
Location: Santa Fe, NM
Year: 1926
Themes: nature, art, storm, self-discovery

nell,

the desert does not whisper; it edits. whole paragraphs of sky are struck through with light and left to dry on the mesa. my paintbox sweats. afternoons i study adobe, which is the local word for the color of old hands.

today a storm rehearsed its consonants behind the mountains and arrived with the seriousness of a new schoolteacher. the lightning had opinions. i pinned paper to a board and the rain pinned everything else to me. when it finished, the world smelled like stone remembering water.

i thought of your laugh, which moves across a room as easily as a faithful dog. oh stay, it says to loneliness. the pueblo women pass with baskets, a slow procession of necessary grace. i keep mispronouncing blue and the sky forgives me by becoming more of it.

my room is simple: a bed the size of honesty, a table kind enough to receive my elbows, a window that frames the unacceptable beauty of dusk. at night coyotes type their letters to the moon and i try not to read over their shoulders.

do not worry if i vanish sometimes from the post. i am learning the desert s grammar: fewer commas, more horizon. send me your wildest rumor and i will trade you an ocotillo in bloom.

i miss the way your name solves the problem of my mouth.

always,
—
