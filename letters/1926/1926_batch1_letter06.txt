telegram that refuses to be small

Date: 1926-07-04
Recipient: Elias
Location: Chicago, IL
Year: 1926
Themes: friendship, nation, noise, modernity, freedom

eli—

the city is a brass lung tonight. fireworks swallow the dark and cough up astonishment in red syllables. i tried to send you a proper telegram but the clerk could not fit my noise inside stop. independence seems to prefer verbs without uniforms.

your last letter smelled of engine oil and river wind. i have been learning to love the elevated s grammar—how it curves like a rumor, stutters at corners, then believes in destination again. a boy on the platform sold papers with headlines that kept trying to climb off the page. the century is a fast horse; i do not know if we are riding or being dragged, but we grin.

remember when we swore to put our names on something bigger than the two of us? perhaps it is enough to sign our breath to a July night and call it even. the bands in the park hammered a tune into shape and the crowd wore it with the brave sloppiness of a first suit.

if the world is a telegram it should read: breathe stop i am with you stop not across miles but under your ribs stop. i am the exclamation you keep in your pocket for when the whistle sounds.

meet me by the bridge tomorrow at eight if the sky is still wearing its sequins.

—
