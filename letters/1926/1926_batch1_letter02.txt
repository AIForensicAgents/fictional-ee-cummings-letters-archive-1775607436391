postscript with rain in its shoes

Date: 1926-02-28
Recipient: Brother Samuel
Location: Providence, RI
Year: 1926
Themes: family, memory, rain, forgiveness

sam,

the factory whistle bent the afternoon into a paper boat and set it sailing down benefit street. i walked behind the rain (which had the slow tired gait of a coat too heavy) and passed the window where mother once kept her teacups, five stubborn moons for cloudy days. strange how the hinges remember the sound of our arguments better than i can remember the words.

i am writing you without capitals because i am not feeling tall. forgive me (and forgive the rain which kept stepping in puddles it had made itself). we were boys when we sharpened our voices on each other, and men when we pretended we had not bled. yesterday a girl with crimson ribbon sold me stamps and said careful like a prayer. i thought of the night we traded pocketknives and futures—yours steady as bread, mine frayed as kite-string.

let us begin again with something small: a spoon, a porch, a joke about the neighbor s rooster who believes daybreak is a personal calling. send me word of your hands, if they ache after barrels, if the barrel smell is still sweet like wheat had a secret. i will send you a map of the city drawn entirely in lamplight and apology.

the rain has untied itself. i leave this on the post counter, a damp confession. tell mother my voice is learning the shape of home.

your brother,
—

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
