to the woman in the red hat (whom i keep losing)

Date: 1926-03-19
Recipient: M.
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1926
Themes: love, travel, art, yearning

m.

the bridges here practice the art of keeping. i practice the art of losing and call it walking. your red hat performed an opera across la concorde today and i, cheap ticket in the last row of morning, applauded with my pockets.

i keep a notebook of paris sounds: pigeons rehearsing their gray vowels; waiters stitching the air with silver thread; the seine saying yes, but slowly. when i turn the page you step out wearing sunlight as if it were a borrowed book, due back never.

last night a poet (all kneecaps and cigarette) told me to marry the blankness. i said i am already engaged to your silence, which wears perfume and arrives precisely late. the café windows shiver with everyone's ambitions. i drink an ink-thick coffee and count how many times your hat becomes a pronunciation of desire.

if you ever need me i will be under the third lamp after the bakery that refuses dawn, rehearsing what the mouth can say without frightening the heart. there is a grammar particular to your wrist when you lift a menu: it makes verbs of everything i thought was noun.

i bought a postcard of the tower and wrote nothing on it because everything towered.

sometime soon meet me where the river bends like a wrist and let us be clumsy together (two reds, one city).

with the cautious extravagance of someone learning how to be brave,
me
