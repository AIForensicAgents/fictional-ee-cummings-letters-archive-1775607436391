Ink & Salt

Date: 1899-02-23
Recipient: Mr. Thomas Hale
Location: New Bedford, Massachusetts
Year: 1899
Themes: sea, family, homesickness, courage, memory

dear thomas,

i folded this paper with tar on my fingers (a souvenir from the dock piles that refuse to forget storms). the harbor today is a dark mouth, full of gull-grammar and ropes that rhyme with themselves. i imagine you reading this where the sky is stitched to the water with a moving needle, and i am the knot at the end of the thread, holding.

mother keeps your chair as a small harbor. she polishes it with salt tears and smiles afterward, as if the shine were your doing. i mend your old scarf, though it travels less than rumors. your knife sleeps under the book of psalms; even that steel has a weather, a hush, a readiness.

when the wind barges up the street, it smells of your boots—of places a map cannot finish naming. i stand into it like a stubborn candle. you once told me the compass is not a boss but a patient friend; i keep mine near, so the needle may comfort me with its faithful trembling.

if you see whales, greet them like old aunts. if you see a storm, talk to it in our father’s voice (he always convinced the mule). if you see a horizon, remember: home is a moving star i pin to the inside of my sleeve.

write when you can, even if all you write is salt.

your brother, ink-stained and waiting,

edwin.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
