Midnight Account of the First Frost

Date: 1899-11-08
Recipient: Mother
Location: Helena, Montana
Year: 1899
Themes: home, grief, winter, resilience, rural

dearest mother,

tonight the pump handle winced and the pail sang small when the water first thought of crystal. the yard wrote itself in a white handwriting even i could read. i covered the turnips, dragged in the blue china from the porch, and told the geraniums a kind lie. in the barn, dinah lifted her breath and it went into the rafters like a warm astonishment. the lantern made a shy chapel of every shadow.

i felt father walk past in the cold as if it were a corridor and he a remembered coat brushing my shoulder. this is not to trouble you, only to say that grief has learned its manners and knocks before entering. there is a rugged peace at this edge of the year. the stars make a brittle music and the stove keeps the low, faithful note beneath it all.

i put an extra stick on the fire for you though you are six states away; in that orange square i saw your hands shelling peas as if summer could be coaxed back by such gentle arithmetic. if you find the green scarf behind the linen press, send it. i will wear it like a sentence from you. i promise to mend my boots and to keep my hope from fraying, and to write next after the first proper snowfall puts its soft seal on the hills.

your son, and the smoke from our roof,

albert
