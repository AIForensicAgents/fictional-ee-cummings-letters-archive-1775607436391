Music with Bees

Date: 1899-08-18
Recipient: Sister Agatha
Location: Ashland, Oregon
Year: 1899
Themes: music, prayer, nature, growth, reflection

dear sister agatha,

this afternoon the bees kept time while i practiced in the orchard. the old violin, contrary by nature, found kindness under my jaw, and the bow discovered a path the wind had rehearsed. when i held a note long enough, the bees accepted it (as if i had finally translated clover).

you asked whether prayer is more like melody or rhythm. i answered then with a shrug i did not mean. today i know: prayer is the rest one dares to take between notes, trusting the next will come and be welcomed. the hive teaches this, and so does the creek where it widens without apology.

mother baked bread and set it on the sill so the steam could learn to fly. i watched a girl chase a yellow butterfly as if chasing a word just out of reach. my hands smelled of rosin and peaches and something older (perhaps patience wearing an apron).

i have mended the hem of my courage with green thread. small fears still escape like moths, but fewer. when the evening star started its small grammar lesson, i put the fiddle away and listened with my whole face. saints must do this: make a cathedral of their listening.

write when the convent’s bell forgives you the minutes. send a petal from your nasturtiums if they are preaching. i will tuck it under the string that always squeaks, and perhaps we will teach each other softness.

your wayward pupil, nearly in tune,

beatrice.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
