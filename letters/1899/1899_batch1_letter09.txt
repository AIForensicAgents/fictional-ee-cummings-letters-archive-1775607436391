Coal Dust on the Moon

Date: 1899-10-03
Recipient: Mr. Jabez Pike
Location: Scranton, Pennsylvania
Year: 1899
Themes: work, solidarity, poverty, hope, family

dear jabez,

last night the moon rose from the slag heaps looking honest and tired, as if she had taken a shift herself. the children drew circles on their slates and called them planets, but their palms were black from the day’s errands and every orbit left fingerprints on the air.

your wife says you cough less when she sings the hymn about ladders and angels. i prefer the one about rivers, because it keeps moving even when we forget the words. i visited the company store and practiced not buying anything; it is a stern schoolmaster with candy in his pocket.

i will not preach work to a man who knows the names of stones. only this: let the lamp wear the fear, not you. when the timber complains, answer it with your boot and with your good sense. return to us in the coat that smells of earth’s pocket, and we will wash it with laughter.

i enclose a sprig of pine (if the postmaster allows). it knows how to keep green in a difficult vocabulary. plant it behind your house when nobody watches, so they cannot say which hand made the shade.

should you need me, send a boy with a cap too large for his wishes. i will come with soup and a story that belongs to both of us.

your friend in daylight,

maria.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
