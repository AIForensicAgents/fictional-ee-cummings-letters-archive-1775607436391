A Quiet Lamp in Snow

Date: 1899-01-17
Recipient: Miss Ada Bell
Location: Concord, Massachusetts
Year: 1899
Themes: longing, snow, patience, love, winter

dear ada,

tonight the snow remembers how to fall—softly, syllable by syllable—upon the eaves and fenceposts and my foolish boots. the lamp on my desk trembles with the window's breath, and in that little trembling, i hear your name.

do you know how a clock holds its breath between ticks? i live there. between the kettle’s first rumor and its singing; between the match and the candle; between the yes i have not said and the world that waits to bloom from it.

if i step outside, the road is a white letter i cannot read without your hand. the pines keep their dark cursive. the river is asleep, but under the skin of ice it keeps writing (a secret grammar of spring).

i would post you a piece of this night if paper could carry temperature. i would send the chill that wants only your wrist to warm it; the hush that widens when i speak aloud to no one and someone answers (it is you, even here).

i have stitched your initials to the inside of my coat, where the heart hurries. snow finds the hem, kisses it, vanishes.

come when you can, even if only as a thought with wet eyelashes. the lamp is brave enough for both of us. and i—i am learning patience in a white language.

yours, in the soft alphabet,

m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
