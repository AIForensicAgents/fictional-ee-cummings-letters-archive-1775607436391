A Harbor Spelled With Lanterns

Date: 1899-03-03
Recipient: Mr. Thomas Vane
Location: Cape Ann, Massachusetts
Year: 1899
Themes: sea, friendship, duty, isolation, light

dear thomas,

your message came ashore in the pocket of a boy who smelled of rope and salt, his hair combed with the wind's unstudied hand. tonight the fog lay down like a tame animal and purred against every sill; i thought of your lantern writing its alphabet across the wet air, lesson by lesson, for ships that have forgotten their names. between the flashes the world remembers to breathe.

in town they argue about coal prices and municipal lamps and who will pay the bill for spring, but the sea calculates its own tariffs: rust, sleep, gulls, and a great patience. do you sleep with one ear open for the bell buoy's iron syllable, the way a mother listens for an infant? i imagine your boots drying by the stove, the lens turning like a careful eyelid, refusing to close upon a drowning.

i will visit saturday with a loaf still warm and the newspaper folded to the tragedies you prefer not to read. we will keep them unopened until the kettle makes a little sun on the table. i will stand where the stairs grow narrow and watch you tend the wick, that delicate surgery that stitches the horizon to its light. if the storm promises mischief, write me a single word and i will come sooner, the way a gull arrives with its body already an answer.

should the north window shine strangely some clear night, consider it my stubborn heart signaling from shore.

your friend of the rocks and errands,

lydia
