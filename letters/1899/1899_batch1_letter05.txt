A Field Writes Back

Date: 1899-05-11
Recipient: Cousin Ephraim
Location: Council Bluffs, Iowa
Year: 1899
Themes: labor, nature, harvest, patience, kinship

dear ephraim,

i drove the plow until the handles remembered my father's hands. the field, which pretends deafness all winter, finally answered—not with words, but with that clean fracture of soil that smells like a good secret told honestly. crows supervised (black punctuation), approving each furrow with their awkward exclamation.

the seed corn is a polite army. it sleeps in sacks and dreams of thunder. i touched each kernel as i poured, as if greeting travelers soon to change their names. there is grammar to this: you open the earth as you would a book, you place a letter, you close the page and wait for the sentence to grow.

rain visited with the authority of a midwife. after, the fenceposts steamed like horses, and the creek rehearsed a story the river already knows by heart. i repaired the gate with wire that remembered being lightning. it held.

when you come, bring your harmonica. the barn has a halo of late flies and needs music to forgive them. aunt says the price of nails has more zeroes than manners; i say the red wheel of the sunset pays its debts with interest (yes, i preach sometimes to nobody, and am answered).

if you cannot come soon, send a scrap of your handwriting. i will plant it in the far corner, to see what word grows. perhaps patience. perhaps mischief. perhaps simply your name, standing green and shy, waving whenever the wind passes.

until harvest,

your cousin,

abel.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
