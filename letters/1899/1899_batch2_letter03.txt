On the Timetable of My Heart (Third Class)

Date: 1899-06-19
Recipient: My Brother, Edwin
Location: On the 10:15 from Chicago to Omaha
Year: 1899
Themes: travel, family, work, westward, uncertainty

dear edwin,

the conductor keeps a little leather book and in it our miles become obedient as columns of numbers. coal smoke looks for spare pockets in the sky and finds them, and the telegraph poles step across the prairie like very thin giants who are afraid to wake the wheat. the carriage sways as if the earth were learning a gentle dance it will never quite master.

your last letter was a good older-brother sermon, and i have it beside me with the corner turned up like an eyebrow. yes, i will save money. yes, i will keep my socks dry. yes, i will distrust men with too many rings. the man sitting opposite carries a birdcage hooded by a blanket, and sometimes the cage shivers like a secret remembering its feathers.

between stations the water towers rise like prayers no one finishes. a child counts them until she sleeps, and when she wakes the numbers have built a small city under her eyelids. we stop for nothing visible, then start again; even the prairie must blink sometimes. they say omaha has work that is honest and then another word for honest that means there will be pay. i am bringing father's watch, which ticks like a restrained horse wishing to run.

tell ma that i will write upon arrival, and that i keep her thimble in the little inside pocket where it presses like a punctuation mark against my ribs. i mean to be a sentence worth that punctuation.

your brother (moving forward even while sitting still),

samuel
