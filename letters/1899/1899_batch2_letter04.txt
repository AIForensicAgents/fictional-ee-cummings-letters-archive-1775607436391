Small Hymn for a Music Box

Date: 1899-09-28
Recipient: Madame Aurelia Duvall
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1899
Themes: music, desire, study, summer, river

madame duvall,

your salon last evening unlatched a door in the thick september air. the chandelier hummed like a courteous swarm, and the pianist set the room upon a bright little river, his ragtime stepping from stone to stone while the cicadas beyond the shutters wound their tireless metronome. i was delivered for an hour to the kind of childhood where i could fit both hands in the snow and come up shining, though here the snow was only the quick of ivory under lamplight.

when you named the encore your voice made an orchard happen in my chest. forgiveness fell from it and also a few green, beautifully sour possibilities. i confess a thing my face is too cowardly to say aloud in company: i wish to study with you. afternoons, if you permit, when the sun has folded itself into the narrow alleys and the river wears its brown sleeve and passes by with a deliberate grace. i bring more hunger than talent but i have learned this month the exact posture of attention, which is to say prayer without knees.

i can pay in honest coin and in the small patience of copying music by lamplight. i will not waste your time with scales that refuse to bloom. only say when to come and which door to knock, the one with jasmine or the one that opens on the quiet stair.

with respect which flares at the edges into something nearly dangerous,

julien

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
