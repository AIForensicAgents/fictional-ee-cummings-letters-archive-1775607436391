Blue Thread for a Suitcase

Date: 1899-07-29
Recipient: Miss Mireille Laurent
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1899
Themes: elopement, travel, rebellion, love, anticipation

dear mireille,

i sew blue thread into the hem of my suitcase so even the sea will know whose tremble it carries. the port smells of oranges and brass; a trumpet practices an argument with evening, and loses politely. you said, meet me when the gulls unbutton the noon—i will come before they finish the second button.

the ticket agent wore a mustache ambitious as a river. he stamped our passage with a sleepy thunder, as if voyages were merely naps that lasted longer than most. i asked him whether paris is a person or a place; he shrugged like a french verb with too many moods.

i have packed: two dresses (one that lies, one that forgives), the comb with three teeth absent, your letter folded into itself until it resembles a tiny boat. i leave behind the balcony that taught me to lean without falling, the alleys that keep secrets in a musical key.

do you think scandal is a candle that burns faster when watched? let them watch. love is a cart that refuses its own horse and runs ahead. i am tired of permission wearing gentlemen's boots. tonight i wear rivers.

wait at the end of the platform nearest the moon. do not bring flowers; bring your true mouth. we will step into the long courage together, and if the ship doubts us, we will teach it our names until it understands weather.

with a heart like brass polished thin,

solange.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
