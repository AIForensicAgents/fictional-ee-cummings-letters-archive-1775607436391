Streetlamp Psalm

Date: 1899-06-07
Recipient: Mr. Lionel Crane
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1899
Themes: city, modernity, friendship, electricity, awe

dear lionel,

new york tonight is a lantern hung in a larger dark. the elevated rattles like bones learning a hymn, and each streetlamp keeps a small Sabbath under its hat. i walked from battery to a place that calls itself by numbers (streets behaving like soldiers), and still the city kept unfolding its metal prayer.

steam climbed out of the pavement to look for a moon. boys sold papers with mouths like trumpets, and a woman in a violet dress turned a corner and became an answer to somebody's impossible question. i confess i stared until the sky blinked.

your last letter smelled faintly of machine oil and bergamot. you wrote of gears with tempers, of belts that prefer certain weather. i read it on a stoop while a cat tried to rename me with his tail. friend, there is holiness in a bolt properly tightened.

we are all apprentices here: to electricity (that domesticated lightning wearing a clerk's visor), to the ocean of windows with their separate climates, to the bridges that teach us how to lean and not fall. when the trolley cracked a joke with sparks, i laughed out loud, and an old man blessed me for the noise.

write me of your heart without shyness. the city is not only thunder; it is also a fingertip on a wrist, counting what survives the noise. i keep your photograph in my glove as if warmth could be posted back to you.

ever your companion in the lit rain,

clara.
