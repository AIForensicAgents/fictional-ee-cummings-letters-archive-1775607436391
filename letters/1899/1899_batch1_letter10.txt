Letter to a Future Child (sealed with leaf)

Date: 1899-12-31
Recipient: To my not-yet-born
Location: Burlington, Vermont
Year: 1899
Themes: hope, future, time, kindness, advice

dear child whose name i do not know,

tonight a year is a door with its hand on the knob. the lamp makes a small sun on the table; the snow invents a white hush outside; i am writing you because beginnings prefer witnesses.

i do not own much: a copper kettle that believes in mornings, the blue plate chipped where laughter struck it, a book with a pressed fern from a day that asked nothing. i would give you these if they knew how to travel ahead of their owner. what i can send is practice: how to tie shoes in the dark, how to choose the riper peach by listening, how to forgive the mirror.

someone told me the new century will arrive like a brass band. i hope it also arrives like a robin, unassuming, with a throat full of ordinary gold. there will be inventions with proud voices. listen to quieter machines too: the spine’s hinge, the eyelid, the small engine of kindness.

if your feet are the sort that dance, find a floor; if they are the sort that root, find a good rain. i have learned that love prefers verbs to nouns. i have also learned that fear is punctual—arrives early and often—and yet cannot read a calendar. we may let it wait on the porch while we eat.

when at last we meet (if paper can manage such miracles), i will know you by the way you open a window.

with deliberate hope,

aunt evelyn.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
