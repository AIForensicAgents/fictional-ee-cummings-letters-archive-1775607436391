Cypress Letters Behind the Shutters

Date: August 27, 1910
Recipient: Miss Evangeline Rousseau
Location: New Orleans, Louisiana
Year: 1910
Themes: friendship, heat, music, secrecy, desire

Note: This letter adopts an experimental, lyrical voice without emulating any specific author's style.

Dear Evangeline,
the heat today leaned its whole astonished weight against my window and whistled, low, as if to ask entrance. i set a bowl of lemons on the sill, and their yellow argued with the afternoon until both were satisfied.

Spanish moss does not hang here; it listens. each thread an ear for gossiping breezes, each shade-pulse an apostrophe. last night, on Chartres, a cornet peeled the dusk like a ripe fig—ragtime in its good Sunday shirt—while a cart rolled past with ice that chimed its own pure arithmetic.

i wrote a lesson on the board: adjectives that sweat. the children suggested “glorious,” “sticky,” and “breathing.” we kept “breathing.” in the faculty closet i have hidden your last note behind the copybooks; i open it only when the fan is tired and the clock is brave. my fingertips memorize the paper as if it were Braille (it is not; it is better; it is your voice folded thin).

remember the cypress alley behind Madame Thibodeaux’s, where we learned the art of slow walking. the leaves sighed like pages being turned, and i thought: here we are, two sentences that briefly believe in the same book. forgive me my shyness; it is only a silk veil i wear until my skin understands.

if the rain comes at last (as it must), i will let it enter the parlor, civilly, and introduce it to the piano. perhaps we will grow a new tune between the shutters. write soon, and tell me whether the river has kept its promises.

yours, under the listening trees,

—A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
