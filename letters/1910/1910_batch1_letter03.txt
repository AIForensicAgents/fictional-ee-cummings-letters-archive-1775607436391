Letter Folded with a Train Ticket

Date: March 21, 1910
Recipient: Mr. Ezra Pike
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1910
Themes: choice, urban life, independence, love, journey

Ezra—

Consider this ticket a small, rectangular intention. I found it shivering under the brass clock at Grand Central, a brave slip of paper promising departure without apology. I have tucked it inside because our conversations always required an engine, even when we were only walking along the river and pretending the night was not following.

You ask if I will stay. The city has placed its hand over my mouth and I find I like the pressure; it keeps the useless answers in. There is a theater on 23rd where laughter climbs the dull chandeliers like ivy. There is a grocer who believes oranges are holy. There is a window in my boardinghouse that insists on opening itself at three in the morning, as if to let the moon borrow my breath.

I am trying to become the kind of person who can sit at a table and say, This is my life, and mean it as both question and feast. If you come, we will drink coffee that stares the day down. If you do not, I will keep my pockets full of small departures until I learn to arrive alone.

With a stubborn fondness,
M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
