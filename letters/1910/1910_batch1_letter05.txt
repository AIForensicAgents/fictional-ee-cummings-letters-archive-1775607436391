Seaside with Kerosene Lantern

Date: June 8, 1910
Recipient: Myself (to be opened midsummer)
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1910
Themes: memory, self, nature, healing, summer

Dear Future Me,

You must remember the way the gulls stitched the horizon, wide white signatures on a blue that never learned to behave. The lantern, stubborn little moon, salted the dunes with its patient thumbprint; and the tide, like a thoughtful tailor, let out the hem of evening until it fit us both.

Do not forget the boy who whistled a Portuguese song and the boat that leaned into it, as if sound itself were a wind. Do not forget that our hunger tasted of peaches and of wanting to be forgiven for wanting. At noon I swallowed a spoonful of sky and at dusk it fluttered in my chest—something with wings, maybe courage.

By the time you read this, your hands will be the color of cherries and work. If sorrow returns, as it likes to, lead it down to the wharf and let it listen to the ropes practicing their knots. Let the salt name you back to yourself.

Keep the lantern. Keep the small ache. Keep writing the sea inside the margins until the margins surrender.

Always yours,
A person who remembers.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
