The Factory Whistles Twice

Date: July 12, 1910
Recipient: Mr. Thomas Brooks
Location: Lowell, Massachusetts
Year: 1910
Themes: labor, friendship, aspiration, city, dignity

Tom,

I write at the window while the mill coughs the last of its daylight. My fingers keep the machine’s measure even now, dear ridiculous metronomes. You used to say our lives were bent spoons, still worth holding. Today I saw a girl thread six needles at once and grin as if she had married the sun.

The floor boss carries a stop-watch like a small, obedient thundercloud. We bargain with seconds, we hide them in our aprons, we spend them on gulps of August air. There is a tenderness among us that no one sees: how we share pins, and pears, and stories about the kind of mornings we would invent if God took suggestions.

I am saving for a red ribbon and for a train whose windows will teach me a new geography. Do not worry. I am not running—only walking fast toward a door that is wider than this whistle.

When the bell cries twice I will put this letter in my boot and let it warm my ankle like a vow.

Yours in lint and stubborn laughter,
N.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
