A Map Drawn With Teacups

Date: August 2, 1910
Recipient: Mother
Location: Cincinnati, Ohio
Year: 1910
Themes: family, home, travel, memory, care

Mother dear,

I arranged the teacups on the kitchen table until I could see every place I have not yet been. The chipped white saucer is St. Louis, the blue willow with its flying lovers is New Orleans, and the small cracked cup—you guessed it—is home. Father’s chair still keeps the curve of his back, as if the wood remembers better than we do.

I found the recipe you wrote when I was ten, the one that begins with patience and ends with butter. I have learned at last that both must be kept warm, or they harden into argument. Outside, a boy sells newspapers with a voice so certain it could convince the river to turn around.

If I leave in September, I will write each Sunday, even if only to list the names of streets I meet. I will send you a thimbleful of every city’s sky, carefully packed in words.

Please take more rest. Ask Mrs. Halpern to fetch the coal. Let the tomatoes stay on the vine one more day; ripeness is not a race.

All my love, folded neatly as a napkin,
Daughter.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
