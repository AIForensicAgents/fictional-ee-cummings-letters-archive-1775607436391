Lamplight on Charles Street

Date: January 14, 1910
Recipient: Miss Lillian Harrow
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1910
Themes: city, longing, winter, art, intimacy

Dearest Lillian,

Tonight the lamp makes a small, warm circle in my room and the snow, unruly as a child, presses its face to the window. I walked back along Charles Street just as the last horse car complained its way home, the bells talking like nervous teeth. I wanted to save the sound for you, to pin it inside this envelope like a moth, but paper keeps only shadows and salt.

You asked after my painting. I have begun the city again, not its brick and smug edges, but its breath: the sigh that happens at doorways when someone is late; the astonished hush before a letter is opened; the shy blue that windows keep for themselves after midnight. I am learning the grammar of roofs and telegraph wires, the commas of stoops, the exclamation of a barn cat.

I know you prefer the countryside, but there is a tenderness to this wintered city that would fit even the smallest of your gloves. If you were here we would share a single scarf and let the Charles tell us a secret under its glass skin. Until then, I send you my heartbeat, carefully wrapped in old news.

Be well and warm. Write soon, if you can, with ink the color of your patience.

Yours ever,
A.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
