Room With Red Curtain

Date: October 28, 1910
Recipient: Miss Beatrice Vale
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1910
Themes: friendship, self-discovery, urban life, change, honesty

Beatrice,

The room is a moth’s theater: a single red curtain breathes in the draft like a practiced actress, and the gaslight gives the wallpaper an expression that could be mistaken for worry. I set your photograph on the trunk; it tilts forward as if to say, Tell me everything twice.

Do you remember the bridge where the river carried mirrors to market? Every time I cross now, I choose a reflection and walk beside it until we part like decent strangers. It is a way of keeping company without quarreling.

I played the pianola this evening—waltzes that insisted on my crooked grace. The landlady laughed until her apron found religion. For dessert I ate an orange very slowly, the way one reads a letter from a far city: peel, pause, peel.

I ought to speak plainly. The city has widened me. I cannot be the quiet cupboard you once admired. If you write, send not promises but weather. I prefer truth as brisk as November.

Always your complicated friend,
S.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
