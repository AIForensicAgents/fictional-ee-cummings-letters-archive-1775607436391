Orchard in the Pocket

Date: September 17, 1910
Recipient: Mrs. Adelaide Morrow
Location: Amherst, Massachusetts
Year: 1910
Themes: teaching, patience, autumn, children, hope

Mrs. Morrow,

Your kind note asked what becomes of a teacher in September, when the chalk is still innocent and the desks blink like coltish calves. I confess I carry an orchard in my pocket: apples not yet picked, lessons not yet bruised, children whose names are seeds in my mouth.

Today, Ruth pronounced geography as if it were a friendly animal. Harold, fierce with caution, refused to sing unless the tune apologized first. We practiced writing the date, 1910, as if it were a door politely opened. Outside, the hills stood up straight in their good jackets.

Teaching, I find, is a series of small doorways. You cannot force a child through; you must coax the hinges with patience and honey. I do not always succeed. Sometimes my temper growls, sometimes my fear unbuttons its collar. But when a hesitant hand lifts like a dawn and says, I know, then the room brightens to the bone.

If you visit, bring a story about failure with a mischievous ending. Bring also your neighborly smile; it teaches without trying.

Faithfully,
M. Ellery

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
