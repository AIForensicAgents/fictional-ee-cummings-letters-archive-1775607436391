Electric Evening on Fourteenth Street

Date: October 2, 1910
Recipient: Mr. Samuel Hart
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1910
Themes: city life, ambition, family, modernity, noise

Note: This letter adopts an experimental, lyrical voice without emulating any specific author's style.

Dear Sam,
this city is a hive whose honey is light. i came up from the subway with the taste of iron and newsprint on my tongue, and there stood Fourteenth Street like a row of lit pears—electric, humming, a thousand throats of filament singing.

my typewriter keys have blackened my fingers so that my hands look like they've shaken hands with night itself. it is a respectable superstition to believe ink breeds luck; i carry my ribbon in a tin, talisman against dullness. at noon, the boss chewed his cigar and said i was quick as a telegraph—i took it as a blessing disguised as smoke.

horses clatter their patient music, but above them the elevated rattles a second sky. i watch a woman balance violets in a white paper cone, a gentleman’s hat chasing him downwind like a small, obedient moon. on the corner, a girl with a sandwich-board adverts dance lessons—her shoes speak soft alphabets to the pavement.

in our boardinghouse the radiators gossip (wetly) and the hall smells of cabbage and brave perfume. i am learning to sleep through horns, sermons, lullabies, and the wild punctuation of rain on the fire escape. nights i stand at the window and rehearse my name in lights—not for fame, but so i might be legible to myself.

how are the maples at home. do they still keep their red pockets for last-minute secrets. kiss mother’s cheek for me and tell her i am eating properly (which is almost true). send word of the river’s mood; i will trade you the exact color of the Hudson at six o’clock—coal becoming velvet becoming something i cannot name.

brother, do not worry if the city forgets to blink. i have learned to blink for it.

yours in a hurry and with care,

—M.
