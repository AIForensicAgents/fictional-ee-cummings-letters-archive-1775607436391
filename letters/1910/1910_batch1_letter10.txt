Winter Postscript with Coal Soot

Date: December 5, 1910
Recipient: Dr. Harold Finch
Location: Baltimore, Maryland
Year: 1910
Themes: illness, gratitude, winter, observation, mortality

Doctor Finch,

Your prescription was sensible: broth, rest, and a book that refuses to hurry. I have followed it stubbornly and still the cough keeps a small empire in my chest. Yet evenings are a consolation. Coal soot freckles the window and the streetlamp outside performs its one trick, bravely.

I watch the neighbor lad feed the cat half his sandwich, an economy of tenderness I wish to practice. And I think often of our last conversation—the one about endings. You said a body closes itself like a ledger, line by line. I disagreed then, but now I see how the day balances: debit of chill, credit of tea; debt of fear, interest of a letter arriving exactly when one needed a witness.

If the lungs permit, I will come in the new year and let you scold me kindly. Until then, please accept this awkward gratitude. Your attention was a warm coin passed from palm to palm without ceremony, and it bought me one more unremarkable afternoon, which turned out to be radiant when properly observed.

With respect and a cautious hope,
J. L.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
