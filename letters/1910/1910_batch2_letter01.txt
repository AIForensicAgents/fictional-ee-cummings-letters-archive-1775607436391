Tidal Margins and Matchboxes

Date: June 10, 1910
Recipient: Miss Ada L—
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1910
Themes: longing, sea, memory, distance, intimacy

Note: This letter adopts an experimental, lyrical voice without emulating any specific author's style.

Dear Ada,
this morning the shore unbuttoned itself—one glimmering shell at a time—and the gulls spoke in quicksilver syllables i could almost pocket. the lighthouse kept winking (a patient conspirator) while i struck a match inside my coat against the damp.

have you ever noticed: the sea pretends to go away but always returns with new handwriting. it writes you here, in damp cursive, as if your name were made of salt and a little sky. i keep finding your initials in the foam, the way a child finds ships in cloud-pockets. and somewhere a trawler coughs out smoke like a thought too large to swallow.

we are a pair of matchsticks in a box that smells of pine: rattle us and the future finds a spark. i am not afraid of the rainy quarrel of wind on shingle; i am only afraid of silence where your breath should be. the neighbors mended nets and hummed a tune that might have been a prayer (or its opposite). even the laundress hung blouses like bright, surrendering flags.

when dusk leaned its blue elbow on the roofline, i stood at the edge where sand decides to become water and said your name—quietly—so as not to frighten the brave little waves. they answered by polishing my boots with their delicate tongues, ceremonious as officials in a small, dearly improbable kingdom.

write soon. include the weather from your window, the color of your evening, the rumor your clock is telling. i keep your last letter folded under my pillow; sometimes at night i listen to the paper creak and pretend it is your shoulder turning toward me.

until you return (or i learn the art of tides),

—C.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
