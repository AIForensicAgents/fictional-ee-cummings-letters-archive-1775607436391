The Fifth Stair Creaks like a Violin

Date: 1894-04-26
Recipient: Mrs. Penelope Hart, Landlady
Location: Brooklyn, New York
Year: 1894
Themes: urban life, humor, poverty, music, negotiation

Madam Hart,

i write to confess three things:

1) the fifth stair sings. each ascent is a tiny concert, all resin and apology. i try to step around the note, but it insists. perhaps it likes me (perhaps i like being noticed by wood).

2) the wallpaper, with its faint regiment of tulips, grows kinder at dusk. under certain lights the petals appear to be listening. i only tell you this to explain why my rent arrives shyly, as if it, too, must be coaxed from a bouquet.

3) i am poor in the way a clock is poor (rich in seconds, bankrupt in bells). by monday no later than monday i will put into your hand the sum i owe, warm from my pocket's practice of longing.

if the tenants object to my midnight tea, inform them that i am educating water to behave. when it boils over i call it a sermon and take notes.

respectfully and with the humble audacity of hope,

J. M.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
