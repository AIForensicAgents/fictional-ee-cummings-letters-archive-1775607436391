Salt Lantern at Year's Edge

Date: 1894-12-27
Recipient: A Stranger by the Window of Car No. 7
Location: On the New York Central Line, somewhere between Albany and Utica
Year: 1894
Themes: travel, transience, winter, strangers, hope

Sir or Madam,

your reflection and mine negotiated the glass with such courtesy that i felt compelled to write, though you will never read this unless the wind is a skilled postman. beyond our traveling house of iron the fields wore their delicate bones; fences counted themselves aloud in frost.

you held a paper like a shield against dreams. i pretended not to sleep but failed and, in that brief republic, i saw a lantern made of salt. it burned without permission, and every tear it birthed became another light. surely this is how a year ends: illumination learning to dissolve.

when the conductor stamped our time with his neat empire of holes, i almost laughed. how brave we are, agreeing to be perforated so we may continue.

may your station be kind. may a small dog find you if sorrow does. should our trains meet again in some other arithmetic of rails, let us nod as if we had always been friends.

in fellow passage,

a fellow passenger
