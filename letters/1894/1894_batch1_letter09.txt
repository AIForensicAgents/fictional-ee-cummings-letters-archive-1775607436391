Parlor Eclipse

Date: 1894-10-31
Recipient: Mr. Horace Blythe, Editor
Location: Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
Year: 1894
Themes: art, publication, autumn, playfulness, uncertainty

Mr. Blythe,

i send you three poems sewn together with the black thread of october. consider them small windows in a room where the furniture remembers dancing. i have corrected only what refused to be wild; the rest i leave in their useful astonishments.

tonight the parlor performed an eclipse: the lamp shade slid like an obedient moon and the carpet found its own constellations. i read aloud until the shadows recognized their names. if you deem the poems excessive in punctuation, attribute it to the season; commas hang from trees like minor ghosts.

your magazine needs less certainty and more weather. allow a poem to knock over a chair; people will forgive the noise if the dark learns something by it.

if you decline them, decline with a gentle shoe, for the poems are skittish and may refuse to be caught again. should you accept, i will send an author photograph of my left ear (the honest one).

awaiting your particular moon,

S.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
