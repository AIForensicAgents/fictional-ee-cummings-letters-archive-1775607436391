Lilac Arithmetic

Date: 1894-05-14
Recipient: Miss Emmeline Ward
Location: Providence, Rhode Island
Year: 1894
Themes: courtship, spring, nature, anticipation, music

Emmeline—

today the lilacs performed addition in the yard: purple plus wind equaled you. i stood and pretended not to see, the way a courteous man overlooks a woman's secret radiance so as not to startle it away. nevertheless, the air wrote your name across my chest in perfume that refused to fade.

may is a startled bird, always almost flying. i have folded my foolishness into an envelope and addressed it to our future; forgive the postage, it is mostly blushes. last week i tried to photograph your laughter with my eyes—failed—settled instead for counting how many times the sun chose your hair as its cathedral. the number exceeded my arithmetic.

if your aunt approves, if the rain communes gently with the road, if courage arrives in a jacket that fits—walk with me to the green where the brass band tells the truth about afternoons. we shall stand terribly still and listen until stillness becomes a dance.

consider this the earliest ring, made of morning.

forever presently,

Alden

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
