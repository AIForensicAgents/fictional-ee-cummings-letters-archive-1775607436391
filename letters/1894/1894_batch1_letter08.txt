Harvest with a Broken Watch

Date: 1894-09-23
Recipient: Cousin Beatrice
Location: Omaha, Nebraska
Year: 1894
Themes: harvest, time, family, rural life, contentment

Bea,

the watch surrendered noon yesterday and has since been bravely honest about being wrong. perhaps time prefers corn, which keeps its own calendar of silk and whisper. we brought in the last of the ears and stacked them like yellow hymns. even the scarecrow seemed to remember a childhood.

i write with chaff on my wrists. the wind steals everything it can pronounce; it knows my name only in fragments and so i am safe. father says the weather has perfected its arithmetic—one cloud plus one cloud equals rain unless the prairie says otherwise.

and you? send word of your city, how the pavements manage their sky. i imagine horses arguing with photographs in every storefront window. when you visit, we will shell the stubborn kernels of silence between us and feed them to a generous pan.

my watch will not heal. good. i would rather measure the afternoon by your footsteps from the lane to the door.

with a yawning contentment,

D.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
