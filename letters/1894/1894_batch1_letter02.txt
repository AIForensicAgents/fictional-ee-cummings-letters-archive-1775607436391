Ink and Snow at Vespers

Date: 1894-02-03
Recipient: Brother Nathaniel
Location: Concord, New Hampshire
Year: 1894
Themes: faith, family, winter, doubt, ritual

Nathaniel,

the chapel bell counted its coins of evening and left them on my tongue. i tasted iron and prayer, and somewhere between the two i remembered how we once wrestled on the parsonage lawn, beards of frost upon our knuckles. i have grown less certain of the borders between yes and amen. snow makes a plain of all roofs—surely, this is the mercy of weather.

i have copied by lamplight the psalm about green things, though all is whiteness now. the page refuses to thaw. every letter becomes a house for a careful breath. forgive me: i punctuate my doubts with commas so they may return safely.

mother writes that father has taken to polishing the doorknob each morning, as if an immaculate threshold might usher spring. i hope his slight rituals keep him from the ache of stairs.

i send you this: a sliver of my stubbornness, wrapped in the rumor of birds. if faith is a coat too large, let us grow into its sleeves together, or roll them and laugh.

yours ever in the awkward radiance of trying,

E.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
