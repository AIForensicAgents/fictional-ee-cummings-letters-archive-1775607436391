Dust-prayer with blue ribbon

Date: 1894-04-09
Recipient: Brother Thomas
Location: Abilene, Kansas
Year: 1894
Themes: family, illness, prairie, faith, spring

Dear Brother Thomas,

The wind combs the wheat so thoroughly I expect to see partings down the middle of the field, neat as a sermon. Father sleeps this afternoon with his mouth open to catch the hymn Mother hums without knowing. His breath is a small wheeled thing: sometimes it rolls, sometimes it forgets the road.

I tied a blue ribbon to the pump-handle to remember hope is not a theory but a practical color. The pump groans like an old mule; it still gives. When the evening train breathes its iron sigh and goes, the house keeps listening long after the sound has walked away. In that hush, cups look braver on their hooks than I feel.

You write that the city is a thousand windows opening at once. Here is one window: above the stove the plaster has cracked into a river-map and I intend to travel it with my finger until we reach a kind place. Send news of your apprenticeship, your boots, your breakfast. Send your laughter, folded; we will open it at the table and pass it round.

If you can come before the harvest, the north pasture will surprise you with violets pretending not to be small. Father wakes sometimes clear as a bell and asks for you by your childhood name. I do not correct him. There are moments that do not require truth so much as mercy.

Your sister who waits,

Eliza

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
