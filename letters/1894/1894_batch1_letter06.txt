A Silence Packed in Trunks

Date: 1894-06-09
Recipient: Mother
Location: Baltimore, Maryland
Year: 1894
Themes: departure, family, home, ritual, growth

Mother dear,

the trunk stares at me with its brass eyelids; i keep placing silence inside it, then closing the lid so the hush will not escape during travel. i have rolled my shirts into small obedient scrolls of departure. your thimble rides in my pocket, a moon for the sewing of rough hours.

i leave by the early train when even the birds doubt themselves. please do not come to the station; the platform is crowded with goodbyes performing acrobatics and i am a poor audience. instead, stand at the kitchen sink and tell the kettle a story in which i become (despite all evidence) steady.

your letter about the peonies—how they gave in to their own abundance until every ant knew his way—has become my map. i shall be excessive in kindness where possible and, where not, i shall be quiet.

i will write at each new address until addresses grow weary of being new. kiss the porch for me; it has held my shoes through a hundred unimportant storms.

as ever and always,

your son

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
