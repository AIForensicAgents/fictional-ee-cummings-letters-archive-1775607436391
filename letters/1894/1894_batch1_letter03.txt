Compass of Salts

Date: 1894-03-17
Recipient: Captain Elias Roe
Location: New Bedford, Massachusetts
Year: 1894
Themes: sea, guidance, friendship, homesickness, ritual

Captain Roe,

a letter like a rope—i throw it and hope it finds your hand. the sea has been quarreling with the moon again (i heard it in the cough of the wharf). nets hung to dry look like sentences forgetting their subjects. i walk among barrels that remember whales and think: every voyage leans its shadow against a wall and calls it home.

you taught me once to spit into the wind so it would know my name. i did and it returned with salt, a small, surprising forgiveness on the lip. i keep the compass you gave me pressed to my ribs and it insists on north (even in bed, even during rain). perhaps north is merely where the heart is most undecided.

if your chart fails you, follow the gull that limps (all the perfect ones lie). nights when the fog arranges the harbor into rumor, i strike a match and believe it a lighthouse; i keep it until it burns my future thumb.

bring back a pocket of far-off blue. i will trade you a loaf of bread shaped like an island.

in faithful land-tide,

P.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
