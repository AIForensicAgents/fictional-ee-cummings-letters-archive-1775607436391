A Small Thunder in the Teacup

Date: 1894-01-12
Recipient: Miss Aurelia Finch
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1894
Themes: winter, longing, domesticity, time, memory

dearest aurelia,

the morning breaks like a careful cup (thin porcelain of sun) and i, trembling spoon that i am, stir the quiet until it believes in sound. there is a little storm living in my teacup—(tiny hooves of steam, bright, invisible)—and it remembers your breath on the windowpane when we counted carriages and forgot the numbers on purpose.

last night the streetlamp braided its hair with snow. i watched it become a saint of ordinary light and thought, if i stand very still, my heart might learn the patience of sidewalks. meanwhile, your letter arrived with the soft authority of sparrows. i read it, then stitched it shut with a pin of sleep, and dreamed that your name was a door left slightly open.

what news? only that the clock has discovered a new way to be late, that republics of dust vote themselves into corners, and our neighbor coughs his apologies to the staircase. i make biscuits that rise like shy moons. your ribbon is still around my wrist. it has taught my pulse a better grammar.

if you come next tuesday (or any tuesday wearing its best silence), we will boil two oceans down to sugar and tell each other everything.

with unabashed astonishment,

a.
