Ferns write green on my wrists

Date: 1894-11-30
Recipient: Miss Beatrice Wynne
Location: Adirondack Camp near Saranac Lake, New York
Year: 1894
Themes: nature, solitude, science, friendship, autumn

Dear Beatrice,

The first frost wrote its silver alphabet on the canvas of the tent and I woke reading. Ferns have a way of whispering accuracy; their green is not opinion but vow. I press them between the pages of the survey ledger and they flatten obediently, all while keeping the secrets of steep places.

I have found a hillside that leans its shoulder into the sun. Lichens assemble there with the patience of old women at quilting, piecing stone to stone with a thread you cannot see. Yesterday a bear put down a thought in the mud and then took it back with the next rain. I admired the revision.

The lake bears seven stars when the wind stands still. I have learned to pour the kettle so quietly that the minnows do not flinch. They are growing bold; or I am. When I rinse the nib, the water takes a little of my black certainty and makes it wearable. Perhaps that is all the forest asks of us—to be less sure and more present.

You write that the city gnaws. Come north for a week and let the moss name you something kinder. Bring your small notebook and the good boots. I will meet you at the narrow dock with matches and a story about the spruce that survived lightning and kept the shape of fire in its rings. We are both smaller and braver than the maps imagine.

Yours in spores and starlight,

Horace Quill, M.D.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
