On the Anatomy of Twilight

Date: 1894-08-01
Recipient: Dr. Julius Crane
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1894
Themes: modernity, science, urban life, twilight, longing

Dr. Crane,

i have dissected the evening and found beneath the skin a modest blue, then another, then a bruise of gold that refused to name itself. is there a nerve where day confesses to night? if so, i have touched it with a match and heard the softest cry.

this city hums like a cathedral built of engines. under the viaduct two boys negotiate the price of marvels; above them, a woman strings laundry between parallel hopes. i keep notes, though sometimes the page blushes and will not consent to be precise.

your last paper on the voluntary ache interested me deeply. surely, longing is a muscle: flexed in secret at windows, sore by morning. advise me, if you please, how to prescribe gentle weather to a heart that strikes sparks on ordinary stones.

when you visit, bring your microscope. we shall examine the dust motes that officiate over stairwells. perhaps under glass they will reveal themselves as courteous planets.

until then, consider this letter a small, usefully inaccurate map of dusk.

with empirical affection,

M. H.
