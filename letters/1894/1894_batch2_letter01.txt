salt-threaded hush

Date: 1894-01-27
Recipient: Mr. Rowan Pike
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1894
Themes: longing, sea, winter, sewing, distance

Dear Rowan,

This morning the harbor is a quiet bruise of slate and silver. Snow falls like folded paper instructions from a patient sky. I sit by the north window where the needle obeys and the cloth remembers your shoulders. Each stitch is a small boat; each knot is a harbor I cannot enter.

The telegraph in Dock Square rattled once—thin as a bird's leg—and then refused me. So I write instead, foolish and fearless, because ink never asks the cost. You have taken the map of me and sailed it past its edges. I hang your name to dry beside the kettle, and steam warms its vowels into a home.

At night the tenement breathes (slow, chimney-lunged) and I count the boards between me and the street as if distance were arithmetic. There is salt on my sleeves where I lean to watch the water bruise and heal under moonlight. I think of your hands turning rope into order, of gulls who do not understand return and make a profession of it anyway.

Come back with pockets full of stories and a single pebble you will swear is the color of my gaze. I will mend your cuffs with summer, I will unspool the snow.

Your faithful,

Aurelia Finch
