night-lantern with gulls

Date: 1906-11-10
Recipient: My Dear Leda
Location: Owls Head Light, Rockland, Maine
Year: 1906
Themes: parenthood, solitude, sea, light, memory, homecoming

dear leda,

the lighthouse is a patient throat that clears itself every twelve seconds. this is how your father learns faithful. the lens is a cathedral of glass, precise as a prayer and kinder. kerosene keeps its small sun in a can, awaiting midnight as if it were a polite guest.

i speak to the fog the way a man speaks to a horse that might spook. steady. steady now. it lays its wool against the horn until even the sea cannot remember its own name. then the gulls scribble their fast unpleasant jokes and the tide laughs without courtesy.

how is your school. does brooklyn teach you the science of sidewalks and the art of avoiding puddles with grace. i imagine your hair refusing to stay braided because the wind has hired it as an employee. i have saved for you a shell that promises an ocean if you press it correctly; i will teach you where to listen when you come for christmas.

at night the keeper is a sort of priest without congregation. i light this tower and marry ships to the notion of arrival. i confess to the stairs that i miss your small hand in my coat pocket. i absolve myself by scrubbing the brass until even the moon is embarrassed.

there is a fox that writes its autobiography in prints across the snow and a kettle that mistakes loneliness for a whistle. i allow both their delusions. your mother once said every sorrow deserved a saucer to sit in; i have set out three, in case i am clumsy.

send me the drawing of the bridge you promised, the one with the people walking across it like cautious commas. i will pin it to the wall above my bunk so that silence must learn to share.

love is a lamp and a reckoning, and i keep both trimmed. be warm. tuck yourself into the city and let it purr you to sleep. the beam swings its arm into the dark, as if to hail you from this elbow of earth.

your father who counts waves as if they were sheep,

amos
