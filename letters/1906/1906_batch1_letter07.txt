desert letter (train breathing)

Date: 1906-09-09
Recipient: Miss Pilar Romero
Location: Santa Fe, New Mexico Territory
Year: 1906
Themes: travel, landscape, longing, language, patience

Señorita Pilar,

after so much east, i have arrived where space is the first language and sound is translated very carefully. the desert rehearses its silence all afternoon. at dusk the horizon snaps a silver thread and suddenly the world wears purple with a thin belt of gold. i think you would like how the wind keeps its hands visible.

the train that brought me here sighed with its whole iron chest, then fell asleep under a quilt of stars sewn by coyotes and distant bells. i left the platform with two valises and an unreasonable tenderness for dust. in the market a woman sold chilies like strings of punctuation, red commas for the mouth to breathe through. a child drew a horse without lifting the charcoal once.

i learn new patience. water is not a guarantee but a covenant; the well answers only after my palms remember how to cup. i think of your garden behind the school, how tomatoes waited for your particular voice before blushing. perhaps distance is just a long road that wants to shake hands.

if you write, address me care of the Plaza boardinghouse. send me a corner of your summer folded in paper. i will send in return a sunset i caught in my hat.

warmly (with dust in my boots),
Eduardo
