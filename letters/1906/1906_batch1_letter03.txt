light breaks itself (and spills color)

Date: 1906-03-05
Recipient: Mademoiselle Sabine Chevalier
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1906
Themes: art, light, Paris, desire, imagination

Chère Sabine,

the Seine has decided to be a ribbon today, and every bridge is a patient hand holding it in place. i went to the atelier with a crust of bread and a stubborn heart; the models were bored, the air tasted like turpentine and daydreams. i am learning what the brush refuses, which is to lie. it demands a living truth, even if that truth is a crooked pear or the melancholy of a cracked jug. the palette becomes a small parliament where colors argue and then suddenly vote yes.

you asked me what i see when i close my eyes. i see light breaking itself in order to fit inside the room. it throws tiny weddings on each surface, marries tone to tone. i see your hair like a blackbird in a tree of fingers. i see the way you drink coffee: as if teaching your mouth a new alphabet.

forgive my absurdities; Paris invites them. there is a violinist on the corner of Rue de Seine who plays a song that is also a staircase; it says going and it says return. stay well. keep your breaths wrapped in lavender. do not forget how your laugh invents afternoons.

affectionately (with paint under my nails),
Lucien
