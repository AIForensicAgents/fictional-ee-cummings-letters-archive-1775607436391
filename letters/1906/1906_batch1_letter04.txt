after the shaking (we counted the skies)

Date: 1906-04-20
Recipient: Cousin Ruth
Location: Oakland, California
Year: 1906
Themes: earthquake, resilience, loss, community, memory

Ruth dear,

forgive the ash. forgive the smell of smoke that insists on traveling even inside folded paper. when the earth gritted its teeth the other morning, we were little buttons on a great coat being shaken out. i do not know what i stepped on, what i called a wall became a prayer, what i called a street became a mouth. there is so much breaking when the world decides to bend.

i crossed the bay at last light and looked back. San Francisco was a book with half its pages torn and thrown to wind, yet the sentences still made sense: people carrying water, doors unscrewed to become stretchers, a woman with a blue scarf singing to calm a dog. we learn our names again in these hours; mine has more edges than before. i keep it close as a cup.

if Uncle asks, tell him i saved the photograph of Grandmother; it rode in my pocket all day, a small anchor. hunger wears a new face but so does kindness. strangers make ladders of their arms. we count the skies with buckets. i am tired in a way sleep cannot unspool, but i am awake to the fact of each other.

write when you can. i will be where the smoke thins, mending a fence of days.

with a heart that trembles and holds,
Elias
