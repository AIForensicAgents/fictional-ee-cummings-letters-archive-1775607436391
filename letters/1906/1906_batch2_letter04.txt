resignation during thunder

Date: 1906-07-03
Recipient: Mr. Whittemore, Headmaster
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1906
Themes: independence, teaching, storm, change, women's agency, renewal

sir,

forgive the weather in my pen. the afternoon has opened its mouth and is teaching the street a drumming lesson. spanish moss swings like an old hymn and the lightning writes its fast pale letters. i have chosen to join the storm.

i tender here my resignation, effective upon the reading, though i hope you will allow me to finish this week's spelling list, as the children have only just found the courage to spell the word hurricane without trembling. they have also learned to divide nine by three and to multiply laughter by ten. i am not sure what subject that last one belongs to, but i graded it with a gold star.

the chalk dust has been kind to me; it has made my dress older and my hands honest. yet there is a bell somewhere beyond the yard calling my name with a throat full of rivers. i have carried my own patience the way a woman carries oranges in a market basket, bright and bruising each other, and today i choose to eat one in the rain.

this is not rebellion so much as a seam rip where the garment always wanted to breathe. i will go north first, and then quite possibly nowhere, which is to say i may end up exactly where i am most alive. please give my regard to the magnolia that scratches its perfumes on the schoolhouse brick. tell it i have not betrayed it; i have borrowed its courage for a while.

if any parent complains, permit them my apology: i could not teach stillness any longer without becoming a liar. what i most wish the children to learn is that the alphabet is a door and sometimes the handle is on the outside, but sometimes it waits inside your own ribcage.

i leave the keys in the top drawer, beneath the attendance book that knows our ghosts by name. let the new teacher keep my erasers; they have been working too hard forgetting us.

lightly and with thunder,

miss evangeline ward
