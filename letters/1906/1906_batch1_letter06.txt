fourth of july (salt and fireworks)

Date: 1906-07-04
Recipient: Brother Samuel
Location: Bar Harbor, Maine
Year: 1906
Themes: family, sea, holiday, work, nature

Sam,

the ocean keeps inventing the same miracle and it does not tire of itself. i watched the gulls scribble their white errands above the pier and thought of you, stern as a mast and kind as a pocketknife. we ate clams with our sleeves rolled and the sun fumbled all over our wrists like a new puppy.

tonight the fireworks volunteered to be temporary stars. each burst was a loud blossom, a bragging flower the dark pretended not to love. small children pronounced their astonishment in syllables that tasted like sugar. i preferred the moment after, when the sky remembers itself and pretends solemnity, though you can see red and green blushes lingering the way secrets do.

i am working with Mr. Dawes scraping paint from the hotel veranda, and i have discovered there are seven kinds of white if you look long enough. the best one smells faintly of salt and wind. i send you a coin polished by my thumb; buy tobacco or keep it as a round promise that i will visit by harvest.

may your nets bring home an honest weight. may the boat treat your name gently. write me about the river and the old dock’s patience.

with brother-noise and quiet both,
Nate

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
