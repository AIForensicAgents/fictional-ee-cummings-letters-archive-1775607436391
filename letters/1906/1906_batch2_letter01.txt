letter of smoke and bread

Date: 1906-05-02
Recipient: Dearest Ruth
Location: Oakland, California
Year: 1906
Themes: earthquake, survival, family, hope, rebuilding, urban life

dearest ruth,

the earth buckled its own hymn and for several terrible minutes every wall in the world forgot how to stay. even the clocks unlearned their circles. i write to you with dust still behaving like winter in my lungs, but listen: last night we found bread, and this morning milk, and the mouth remembers gentleness very quickly.

i crossed the bay where the water wore the sky with more patience than we did. san francisco is a page with its letters shaken off, a house whose name fell out of its mouth. yet in alleyways a small republic of brave kettles is already beginning to boil. a woman passed me with apricots in her pocket and you would have wept at the brightness. how a small, round sun can insist upon sweetness where ash has lived.

we sleep on church steps, under quilts that once were curtains. i never knew how kind wood could be until a pew held my whole body and did not ask one question. some men swear the ground will twitch again, but my bones make a different treaty with the night: to wake means we are not finished.

please send paper, my fingers are greedy for clean margins. and a shawl, if you can spare the blue one that smells faintly of soap and your laughter. i think of our porch with the geraniums arguing politely with the wind. i practice the word home the way a pianist practices scales, each syllable a small hammer teaching silence to sing.

if father asks, tell him i am learning the names of nails and beams and how to bind a loaf with gratitude. tell him i pull my sleeves to my elbows and carry buckets as if they were language. tell him the city may have stumbled, but its heart is a stubborn lantern and i have seen it lifted.

kiss mother for me. tuck a spoon of mercy under her pillow. and when evening lowers itself like a kind eyelid, breathe once for me in our kitchen, where the light is never afraid.

yours in soot and stubborn hope,

m.
