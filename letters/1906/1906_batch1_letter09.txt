fog rehearses the bridges

Date: 1906-11-02
Recipient: Dearest M
Location: London, England
Year: 1906
Themes: distance, city life, yearning, performance, weather

My dear,

London is a theater where weather plays itself to a full house. today the fog tried on every coat at once and found each flattering. i crossed the river as if paging through a thick book, each span a chapter on patience. the cab horses wore their breath like lace. i confess i spoke aloud to a pigeon who looked more experienced than i.

the music hall last night was eighty percent feathers and twenty percent longing. a comic tripped over a joke and bowed to his own mistake; i loved him instantly. after, i walked home along a street that smelled of oranges and coal and someone’s stubborn hope. your letter in my pocket read me like a map; each curve of your handwriting a road i would repeat.

i have not grown used to five o’clock arriving hungry for tea. i obey it like a minor god. the city is kind but distracted; i feel like a note pinned to a sleeve. still, there are mornings when the chimneys salute the pale sun and i swear the rooftops practice waltz steps.

come when you can. or write your absence so full of breath it resembles you.

always (and then more),
Julian
