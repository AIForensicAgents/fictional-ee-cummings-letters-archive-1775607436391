midnight bell with evergreen

Date: 1906-12-24
Recipient: Future Reader (whoever finds this)
Location: Hartford, Connecticut
Year: 1906
Themes: time, winter, reflection, hope, unknown reader

Dear Stranger,

if your hands open this, they have done so after mine let it go. perhaps the ink is tired by now. it is Christmas Eve and the clock tells midnight with a voice of copper. the tree blinks in the parlor, not with electricity but with a patient army of candles, each learning how to be a star without burning the house.

i am writing to practice tenderness toward the unknown. tonight the street is the quietest instrument. a sleigh passed earlier and stitched a silver seam down the snow. i will leave this note inside a book no one borrows, between two pages that argue softly about summer. if you are reading, it means arguments resolve themselves eventually.

tell me, how did our small century treat you when it grew old. did the factories learn to nap. did the rivers forgive us. i kiss the foreheads of questions and send them out into the dark with mittens on. i am neither brave nor certain, but i am here, with ink, with a chair that creaks like an honest uncle, with an orange whose skin breaks open to tell the room it is possible to be sweet and be peeled.

i wish you a gentle astonishment, whenever you are.

in faith that words cross winters,
Annalise

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
