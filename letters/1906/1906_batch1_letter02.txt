a pocket of soot and courage

Date: 1906-02-23
Recipient: Mother
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1906
Themes: ambition, family, city, work, hope

Dearest Mother,

forgive the grime spelling itself along the edge of this paper; the trains here breathe coal like giant horses and i have been riding in their lungs. Chicago is a cathedral of iron, all ribs and clamor. i go to the shop at dawn, and the bell has a temper; it scolds, it forgives, it rings us open as if we were drawers in a bureau of work.

i am careful with my coins. there is a girl who sings for a nickel on State Street, and she could buy a kingdom with the way her voice devours the fog. i thought of you yesterday when the bread came out of the oven at Mrs. Quinlan’s, the crust so proud of itself; i nearly burned my name into it, the way you do when something is too good to wait for plates.

sometimes, at night, i study by the window and the sky hangs its dark coat on the hook of the tallest building. i have ideas, Mother, the small ferocious kind that chew through rope. when i close my eyes, the blueprints of future days begin to glow like coal beneath ash. pray i do not mistake smoke for sky. pray i learn the difference gently.

i kiss your dear work-worn hands in my thoughts.

Your son,
Benjamin
