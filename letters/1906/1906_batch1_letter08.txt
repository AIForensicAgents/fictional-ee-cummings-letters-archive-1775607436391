ink and the small brave envelope

Date: 1906-10-17
Recipient: Mrs. Winifred Hale, Editor
Location: St. Louis, Missouri
Year: 1906
Themes: art, publication, humility, ambition, honesty

Madam,

this letter is a partner to the thin sheaf of poems you will find folded with it. forgive the audacity of their knock. they have walked with me through tenements and under viaducts, through green rooms where actors powdered their worries, through alleys that smelled of cabbage and of luck. they have learned to keep their collars straight even when rain thinks the day should be barefoot.

i do not promise they are good. only that they are honest. each line holds a small nail from my pocket, used to affix weather to memory. if you find a cracked meter or an impatient rhyme, know that i argued with it but left it wild on purpose. some words refuse polishing; they prefer to show their work.

your magazine makes a corridor in the month where new sunlight enters. if there is space for one of mine, even a word-length corner on page last, i will send you gratitude with both sleeves rolled. if not, kindly return them and i will feed them butter and let them grow another year.

thank you for reading with both eyes and whatever heart is not otherwise occupied.

respectfully and hopefully,
Agnes Rowan
