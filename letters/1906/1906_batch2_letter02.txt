valentine with ink-stained thumbs

Date: 1906-02-14
Recipient: Miss Adelaide Pierce
Location: Greenwich Village, New York
Year: 1906
Themes: love, urban romance, intimacy, winter, youth, defiance

adelaide,

the city is a bouquet of elbows and umbrellas and still, on this particular day, it wears a foolish red mouth. i brought you a paper heart that kept folding itself into a swan. perhaps being foolish is the only honest way to be brave.

i have sew marks on my fingers, small constellations pricked into skin. the thimble is a planet that knows how to forgive. when i press cloth to your shoulder the seams become a grammar of nearness, and we speak by button and thread. i swear the elevated rattles our names in its tincan throat and does not drop them.

walk with me after supper where the lamplight puddles and the snow is tired of being pure. i will show you my secret map of the village: that stoop where the violinist tunes his hunger, the bakery that exclaims sugar joy, the alley that keeps a whisper in its pocket for your laughter. your laughter which is both coin and hymn.

it is not scandal to be alive exactly in this minute. we may be reduced, later, to gossip or shadow, but now we are twenty and invisible to the past. i would like to press this moment like a leaf between dictionary pages, so that every time the word kiss is searched for, our throats rustle.

if there is a permission slip for happiness i have lost it. if there is a law against your wrist finding my wrist under the table, i promise to study it only after midnight. adelaide, the doorjamb is learning our names by heart; even the kettle blushes when you enter the room.

bring nothing when you come except how you breathe. i will bring matches and the way i cannot stop saying your name under my breath, which embarrasses even the wallpaper.

with both hands (ink and all),

clara
