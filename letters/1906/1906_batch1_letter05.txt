magnolia and the ache of bells

Date: 1906-05-30
Recipient: Reverend Abner Cole
Location: Savannah, Georgia
Year: 1906
Themes: faith, doubt, southern life, ritual, forgiveness

Reverend Cole,

the magnolias have become soft moons on the trees, and every evening the air is a hymn that forgot its words but kept the tune. i sit on the porch with my doubts folded like handkerchiefs in my pocket. your sermon on Sunday made a good noise inside me, like someone opening shutters in an old house, but still there are rooms i cannot enter without my shadow arguing first.

i believe in the small sacraments: buttons sewn back on; a neighbor’s lost dog returned; butter turned out of its mold and the knife made clean. i believe in a cobbler who knows the names of each nail he drives. what i do not know is how to hold both sorrow and laughter without spilling one on the other. perhaps that is what faith is, the tray steadying itself in our palms while we learn to walk.

the bells rung for a wedding this afternoon, and my bones remembered funerals. i suppose everything in us is a chorus that alternates verse and echo. if i were brave i would tell you the mistake i made last winter, but courage is a slow tool. for now i offer this letter as a low candle.

yours in plain sunlight and awkward hope,
Marian
