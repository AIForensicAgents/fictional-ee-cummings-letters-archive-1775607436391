snow (with small fires of breath)

Date: 1906-01-11
Recipient: Miss Ada Everly
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1906
Themes: love, winter, longing, urban quiet, intimacy

Dear Ada,

snow taught the streets to whisper this morning, and every hoofprint became a soft pocket of quiet. i walked through the milk of it, my coat gathering small white astonishments, thinking how your name makes a warmth beneath my tongue. the lamps along Beacon were commas in a sentence the day kept trying to finish, but could not, because somewhere you were reading (or sipping honey tea) and that secret delayed the period.

i do ordinary things: wind the watch, count the stamps, hold the rail on the Common steps. but each ordinary thing has a pulse today, and i am almost afraid to touch it. if you had been here, we would have measured our breath like children do, those ghosts of ourselves making shy theater before our faces. i would have told you how the river waits patient as a button in a mother’s box, and how the ice is its clear silence.

do not think me extravagant. i am only the simplest hinge, and you are the door’s idea of music. write when the afternoon turns blue about the edges; tell me if your lilies have opened; tell me if you still keep the letter i sent in November locked between the recipe pages.

my hands remember yours even in gloves.

yours (plainly, gladly),
Arthur
