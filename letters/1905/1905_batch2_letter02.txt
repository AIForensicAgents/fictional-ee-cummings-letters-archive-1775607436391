ledger of lilacs

Date: 1905-04-30
Recipient: Mr. August Fielding
Location: St. Louis, Missouri
Year: 1905
Themes: family, grief, domestic life, spring, work

dear august,

today i balanced the books against the sky and found them nearly even. receipts like brittle moths; expenditures like obedient geese marching into the river. in the margin i drew a sprig of lilac to keep the numbers from starving.

since mother left the April of our house ajar, all the dust has been practicing to be light. i sweep it into small continents then let the broom be weather. it is an honest geography, brother: longitude of chairs, latitude of hats, a climate made entirely of your last letter.

mr. greeley paid his account (two dollars seventy) with the face he uses for church. i forgave mrs. bell her penny and she forgave me the world. forgiveness, noted in blue ink, is the only profitable line.

do not worry that your handwriting leans—so does the evening, and it does not fall. send news of your experiments with seedlings; i will report on the uprightness of the till. when i close the shutters the street peels like an orange, and i keep the rind for courage.

kiss the shovel. kiss the rain. keep your pockets full of dirt that remembers.

your sister,
clara.
