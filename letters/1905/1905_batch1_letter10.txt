locomotive with wings

Date: 1905-12-02
Recipient: Mother
Location: Nairobi, British East Africa
Year: 1905
Themes: travel, nature, home, awe, modernity

mother,

the rail here is a straight thought drawn across a dreaming body. we ride a whistle into the morning and the savanna opens and closes like an eye. acacia trees stand about with their green umbrellas furled; a giraffe scribbles the horizon in long careful letters.

yesterday the sun rose twice: once from the east and once from a lion’s back. the first time it was solemn; the second time it was a joke between beasts and light. the men speak a dozen tongues and the tea, miraculously, understands them all. i keep a small notebook of colors: dust-of-elephants; sky-asleep; water-when-it-remembers.

i am well. the only fevers i keep are for the idea of home and for the way the track hums long after the train has passed, as if some iron ghost continued on ahead, clearing the thorns from our thoughts. if you send letters, seal them with bread crumbs; the weaverbirds here prefer their reading with a snack.

i will return with a pocket full of distances and a story tame enough for our kitchen.

your son,
thomas
