sparrows vote for morning

Date: 1905-10-03
Recipient: Miss Beatrice Lane
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Year: 1905
Themes: activism, friendship, hope, city, birds

dear beatrice,

this afternoon a meeting of women became a weather. chairs, hats, opinions—all rustled toward a thunder we did not fear. someone read aloud a sentence that had never been asked to stand so straight, and even the wallpaper leaned closer to listen.

i have discovered that courage arrives disguised as small chores: pin the notice, fold the leaflet, warm the timid hand inside your own. yet in the sum of these quiets a world re-buttons itself, higher by one hope.

outside, the sparrows held their own convention on the telegraph wire, unanimous for morning. i took their vote into my pocket and felt it tick, tick, like the inside of an egg. we are hatching, b., and anyone who calls it improper has never been new.

father says the law is a locked door; i say the knob is within reach. perhaps i am merely a key with a woman attached. i will turn. with you.

if you cannot attend Thursday, send your shadow—it is already braver than most men i know.

yours in bright ink,
nell.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
