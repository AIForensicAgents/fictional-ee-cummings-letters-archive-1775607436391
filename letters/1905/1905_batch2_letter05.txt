autumn with a piano in it

Date: 1905-11-21
Recipient: Doctor Leena Varkas
Location: Helsinki, Grand Duchy of Finland
Year: 1905
Themes: change, city, music, politics, friendship, winter

dear leena,

today the city paused the way a piano pauses between notes—hands up, air full of unheard music—and then the streets began to play us. shoes striking time, banners the color of breath, faces opened like violins. i walked through the chord and came out tuned.

someone said strike and the word turned into a thousand doors opening inward. we learned how much a whisper weighs; how snow, falling, counts people and does not forget. i saw a child spell future with a stub of chalk on the tram rail; the letters melted and still were true.

your room misses your bootprints. i straighten the desk and the desk straightens me. if i touch your pen it remembers your pressure and becomes a small, brave bone. do you find sleep where you are? our nights rattle like a political poster on a fence—fastened, but asking.

when you return we shall take tea that tastes of streets, and we will add sugar not for sweetness but for steadiness. bring your clever anger; mine has grown thoughtful and would like company.

my coat smells of smoke and frost and piano. this is to say: of you.

always,
s.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
