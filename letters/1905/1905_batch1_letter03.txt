ink of the dawn

Date: 1905-03-21
Recipient: Mademoiselle R.
Location: Paris, France
Year: 1905
Themes: art, dawn, city, sensory, playfulness

mademoiselle—

i rose before the pigeons (they were still turning in their sleep like soft coins) and climbed the hill where montmartre keeps its pocketknife of wind. the sky was a pale envelope; i wrote upon it with a very thin brush. a line, and then another—blue learning to say itself. the houses wore yesterday as a shawl and shrugged it off just in time.

you asked what color is the hour before coffee. i have discovered it is not a color but a listening. the paint waits while the light tells it its small scandal: we all come from a secret yellow. in the café, the sugar made a white snowfall into my cup and the violin at the opposite table smiled without being seen. i wanted to hand you that smile on a plate, with a spoon, a napkin, and two crumbs of the moon.

it is not that i am in love (that old coin) but that everything suddenly has a pulse—chair hat chimney, even the cobbles rehearse their hearts—and i cannot help but learn the steps.

bring your blackest ribbon. we will tie a bow on noon and loosen it when the bells are not looking.

a bientôt,
—r.
