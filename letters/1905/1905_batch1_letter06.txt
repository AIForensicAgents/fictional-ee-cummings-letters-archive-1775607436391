papyrus wind

Date: 1905-06-17
Recipient: Aunt Beatrice
Location: Cairo, Egypt
Year: 1905
Themes: travel, ancient, family, wonder, nature

dearest aunt b.,

the sun here is a brass thought: bright, unapologetic, and it does not blink. yet the nile has invented a cool that creeps into the wrists. men in white sail the river with triangles of cloth, and the wind is a quiet scribe, writing on our cheeks with reed pens.

you would love the market: pomegranates argue politely with dates, spices laugh in red and yellow, and a cat conducts the afternoon from atop a basket of onions. i touched a carved falcon today—stone older than breath—and it felt as if it were politely pretending to be asleep. our guide read me a few hieroglyphs; they are birds and hands and small square mouths, gossiping across the centuries about bread and kings and love.

i have begun to collect shadows. at noon they are brief notes; by late day they become letters to themselves. i will bring you the one that fell from a palm tree and landed with a sigh on my shoulder. wear it on sunday afternoons.

please keep my plants alive. talk to the geranium (she believes herself a queen). i will return before she stages a coup.

with sand in my shoes and you in my small sun,
marian
