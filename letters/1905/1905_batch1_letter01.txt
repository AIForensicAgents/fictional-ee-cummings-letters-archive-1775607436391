the winter breath between us

Date: 1905-01-12
Recipient: Clara Whitlow
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1905
Themes: love, winter, distance, longing, memory

dear clara,

tonight the window writes your name upon itself in frost—shivering little alphabets—while the streetcar rings a distant silver circle and goes on. i have put my hands to the glass and the cold shook them like a polite stranger (how-do-you-do) but would not let me pass. how near your cheek is to my cheek when i remember it. i walk across this room—three steps, four—and between the fourth and the rug's edge i find the whole atlantic, and cross it, breath by breath.

the kettle sighs a moon of steam. i drink an hour slowly. it tastes of penny candy and the red mitten you forgot in my coat. i am trying to be brave (which is another word for quiet) but every knob and hinge in this house insists on singing you—softly badly beautifully. forgive the letters i do not write: they are sleeping under my tongue, pale birds, waiting for the thaw.

i keep your last line folded in my vest: "soon." that small word grows fur and warmth and purrs, and when the snow decides to be a river again, i will carry it like a lantern through the dripping streets, until your door learns my knock.

always (until always is not enough),
—m.
