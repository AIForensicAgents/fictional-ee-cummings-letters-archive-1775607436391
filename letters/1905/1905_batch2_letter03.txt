harborclock (midnight letter)

Date: 1905-07-12
Recipient: Captain Elias Wren
Location: Provincetown, Massachusetts
Year: 1905
Themes: love, sea, night, waiting, time

dear elias,

the harbor wears midnight like a wet sleeve, and i am the small coin it loses to the tide. lanterns make their stitched, ridiculous moons along the pier; gulls keep the book of silence open to the same page.

i have wound the clock of your chair again—twelve wooden ticks toward morning—so the room remembers the way you sit, that salt-mapped listening. the bed keeps your shape as a schooner keeps a wind; do not be kind to it. return and teach it turbulence.

the sea reports itself constantly; it is a correspondent without envelopes. today it wrote in foam: come home, come home, but spelling is not the ocean's gift. in the broken grammar of waves i am always the noun you left, the verb you keep.

bring me the breath of a whale if you find it, bottled, unspilled. bring the long spine of horizon folded in your pocket. bring nothing—i will unfold you.

i send, in this letter, a knot i tied with one thought of you and one thought of sleep. pull it and the night will fall apart like a net releasing stars.

ever your shore,
m.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
