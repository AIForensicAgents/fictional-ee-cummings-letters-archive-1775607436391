telegraph sunset

Date: 1905-05-09
Recipient: Mr. Hartley
Location: San Francisco, California
Year: 1905
Themes: work, city, modernity, nature, restlessness

dear mr. hartley,

at the end of market street the water eats the day—bite by bite—and leaves the bones: violet, copper, a last crisp blue. from our office window the wires cross and re-cross like a thousand taught questions; every question hums. i type the city into little metal squares until the squares are full of footsteps and steam.

the fog arrived this morning wearing galoshes. it stepped carefully over the hilltops, sat on the ferry building, and sighed a bell out of its pocket. i wanted to send you that bell with my report, but the envelope is too honest for such smuggling. instead, i enclose figures that ring in their poor way. the men below shout as if to frighten the ocean into behaving. a gull drops a sentence from its mouth; it breaks and becomes weather.

i remain industrious (with occasional rebellions: a late lunch, a window opened too widely, a thought about poetry smuggled inside a ledger). should you require an extra pair of ears, mine have learned to listen to telegraphs as to rain.

yours professionally and a little otherwise,
lenore
