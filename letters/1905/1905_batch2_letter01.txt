between trains (snow learning your name)

Date: 1905-01-17
Recipient: Odette Harrow
Location: Boston, Massachusetts
Year: 1905
Themes: longing, winter, trains, separation, urban hush

dear O—

the station makes a clock of breath. men stamp their feet like small cold engines; women wrap the color of silence around their throats. i stood where your goodbye was still warm on the rail and i learned the alphabet of snow: its soft s, its rushing o, its nevermind.

i think a train is only a very long question. one end asks where, the other asks who, and between them the cars answer with trembling. you, you, you.

i keep in my glove the ticket i did not use; in my mouth, the promise i was too brave to speak. tonight i am a window and every flake is handwriting—that bright, that brief.

if you should wake before morning, touch the lamp twice and i will feel it. the world rings like a teacup when you set it down, and i would drink the sound until the cup is gone.

be careful of corners; the wind collects there like stray cats. send me the smallest thing you can—one button, one sigh—and i will sew a winter to it.

faithfully (and somewhat foolishly),
a.

[This is a fictional creative work inspired by the style of E.E. Cummings and is not an authentic historical letter.]
