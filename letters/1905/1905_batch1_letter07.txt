bamboo grammar

Date: 1905-04-10
Recipient: Haruko
Location: Kyoto, Japan
Year: 1905
Themes: travel, ritual, peace, war, nature

haruko-san,

the temple bell pronounced morning and then kept the echo as punctuation. we crossed a bridge thin as a promise; below, the stream rehearsed its silvers. everywhere, plum and cherry—blossoms like whispered syllables repeating themselves until the air learned the language.

i watched an apprentice tie his obi (carefully bravely) and thought how even knots can be a way of saying: now. my shoes, rude foreigners, tried to argue with the tatami; the room forgave them. an old woman painted a poem on rice paper—seven brushstrokes—and there was suddenly a mountain, a bird, and something i will not dare to name.

in the tea-room time kneels. the steam bows. we drink green silence. outside, the newspaper shouts about war in letters that look like fishbones, and the world momentarily remembers its teeth. i prefer your garden's sentence: moss comma stone comma bamboo, and then that delightful period: a carp's small kiss on the pond.

if i do not return soon, blame the lanterns for their soft conspiracies. they have promised me a night where even the dark is gentle.

with palms together,
ai
