snow that learns a shout

Date: 1905-01-27
Recipient: Sofia
Location: St. Petersburg, Russia
Year: 1905
Themes: unrest, courage, winter, love, hope

sofia—

today the snow pretended to be clean silence and then remembered its voice. boots wrote heavy poems down nevsky while the sky kept losing buttons. i held my scarf against the wind and it held back (like a stubborn aunt). we said very little, and said it with our bones.

i know you will say: do not go. but the street has a mouth and it is hungry; it eats fear and spits out courage like hard seeds. we are not brave: we are simply tired of our lids being sewn shut with gray thread. in the bakery a loaf cracked, laughing. the old woman behind me crossed herself as if to kneel inside her own heart. we all want bread and a window that opens without argument.

if i do not write tomorrow, it is only because tomorrow has become too wide to fit on paper. i will tuck a piece of it in my glove—warm as a secret—and keep it for you. listen: when i say your name, the snow stops falling long enough to think.

hold a candle to the north side of your house. i will follow the flame's breath home.

—m.
