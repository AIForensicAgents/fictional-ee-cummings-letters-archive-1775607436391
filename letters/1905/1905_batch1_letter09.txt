the street learns a heartbeat

Date: 1905-11-14
Recipient: Isabel
Location: Buenos Aires, Argentina
Year: 1905
Themes: dance, night, love, city, music

isabel—

tonight the bandoneón opened and the room unfolded itself, all corners turning into curves. someone struck a match and suddenly the floor had blood warm in it. we were a handful of coins tossed into music; we spun and clinked and did not think of pockets.

the women wore shadows stitched to their ankles; the men kept their sadness in their hats (took it off only to bow). i learned the step that says: forgive me. and the step that says: if we die, it will be beautifully. outside, the street swept its own hair and the moon leaned down like a gossiping aunt. we danced until the baker began to breathe his first bread into the dark; even the dogs said amen.

do you remember the photograph of us on the ferry? i keep it in my shoe, close to the part of me that never pretends. when i am lost, i take a step and there you are.

bring your red ribbon when you come. we will tie it to a lamppost and teach the night to listen.

a besos y baile,
julio
