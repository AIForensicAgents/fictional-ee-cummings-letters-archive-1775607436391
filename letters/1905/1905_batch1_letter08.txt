gaslight rehearsal

Date: 1905-10-05
Recipient: Seamus
Location: Dublin, Ireland
Year: 1905
Themes: theatre, city, art, humor, melancholy

seamus,

the stageboards remember every step; when i walk them, the dust rises like old applause. we rehearse by gaslight, which flatters no one and forgives everyone. our leading lady keeps misplacing her sorrow and finding it again, brighter. i think sorrow is like that—always where you last smiled.

we are performing a new piece whose furniture argues with the characters; the table steals a line, the kettle flirts with a monologue. i hold a broom and a speech and (sometimes) my breath. the director is a man of commas: he scatters them on the floor for us to collect with our feet. outside, the rain smokes against the windows as if the city were learning to disappear.

the audience will be poor and splendid. they will come in with cold ears and leave with warm ones, which is, i suppose, all we can give. last night i dreamt the curtain rose on the liffey and we all took bows to a river. it applauded us with boats.

send tobacco (the cheap heroic kind) and your luck.

a flood of ink to you,
frank
