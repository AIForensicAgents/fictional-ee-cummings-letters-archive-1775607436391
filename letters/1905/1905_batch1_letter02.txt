harbor with tin stars

Date: 1905-02-03
Recipient: Elias (brother)
Location: New York, New York
Year: 1905
Themes: family, sea, work, yearning, city

eli—

night at the docks is a pocketful of bright nails: lanterns hammered into dark wood so the river will not swallow the world. the tugboats cough (old men) and coal climbs into the sky with black elbows. i stand on Pier 17 and practice your whistle—two notes—that sharp knife you use to cut distance. it almost works; even the gulls look over their shoulders as if your hat might come walking up the gangplank by itself.

i am apprenticed to a counting-house by day, which is to say i measure the hours and return them to their boxes. the ledger is a great quiet whale and i am a pin. and yet there are those minutes (between tea and ink) when a mast passes the window like a spine and the ocean inside my ribs remembers its salt.

send me your weather in a bottle. cork it with your laugh. i will trade you a city of tin stars for one honest ship's morning—the kind that smells of rope and bread and the small brave prayers men hang in their boots.

keep your hands, brother. the sea will take what it can—nails buttons the names of towns—but not our stubborn whistle.

yours as a dock to rope,
j.
