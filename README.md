# Letters from the Margins

**A Creative Archive of Fictional Letters Inspired by the Style and Era of E.E. Cummings**

> *"since feeling is first / who pays any attention / to the syntax of things"*
> — E.E. Cummings, *is 5* (1926)

---

## ⚠️ Important Notice

**Every letter in this archive is an original fictional creation.** These are not authentic historical documents, recovered manuscripts, or transcriptions of real correspondence by E.E. Cummings or any other historical figure. They are imaginative literary works composed in the spirit, voice, and stylistic sensibility of a particular era and aesthetic tradition. Please see the [Ethical Note](#ethical-note) and [Disclaimer](#disclaimer) sections below for full context.

---

## Overview

*Letters from the Margins* is a curated collection of **915 fictional letters** spanning the years **1894–1954** — a period that encompasses the life, artistic development, and cultural milieu of Edward Estlin Cummings (1894–1962). The letters are original creative works that explore the textures of early-to-mid twentieth century correspondence: the intimacies, the formal courtesies, the experimental impulses, the private rebellions, and the quiet observations that characterized a remarkable period in American literary life.

The collection draws inspiration from Cummings' distinctive approach to language — his playful disregard for conventional punctuation and capitalization, his painterly eye for image, his philosophical tenderness, and his defiant individualism — while inhabiting a broader fictional world of invented correspondents, relationships, and circumstances.

These letters are not imitations or pastiches of specific real letters. They are new compositions imagining what the interior world of such correspondence *might have felt like* — letters between fictional poets and painters, editors and expatriates, lovers and estranged friends, parents and children — all situated within the genuine historical and cultural landscape of those six decades.
